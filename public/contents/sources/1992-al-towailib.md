# A New Correlation for Two-Phase Flow Through Chokes

**Summary**: The thesis develops a new empirical correlation to determine the optimum choke size for two-phase flow in oil wells. It incorporates mixture density as a key parameter and validates the model against multiple datasets, including Middle Eastern data, showing superior accuracy compared to existing correlations like Fortunati, Gilbert, and Surbey.

---

## Research Problem

Existing correlations for determining choke size and predicting oil flow rates in multiphase flow environments often exhibit high error margins or lack consistency across different flow regimes. A more accurate and robust correlation is needed for practical application in the oil industry to improve production estimation.

## Objectives

- Develop a new empirical correlation for multiphase flow through chokes relating choke size, flow rate, upstream and downstream tubing pressures, gas-oil ratio, and other pertinent variables.
- Perform statistical error analysis to validate the new correlation against existing ones.
- Identify and select independent variables influencing fluid flow mechanisms in chokes.
- Develop a new non-linear multiple regression model for choke size calculation.
- Perform statistical analysis (T-test, error distribution, sensitivity) on the new correlation.
- Compare the performance of the new correlation against established industry correlations.

## Methods

The study utilizes non-linear multiple regression to develop a correlation where choke size is a function of oil flow rate, upstream wellhead pressure, and mixture relative density. The methodology includes: 1) Selection of independent variables based on literature review; 2) Formulation of the model using least squares method; 3) Statistical validation including T-statistics (at 99% confidence), error distribution analysis, and sensitivity analysis; 4) Graphical evaluation via crossplots and incremental range analysis to compare the new correlation against Gilbert, Ros, Omana, Fortunati, Osman, and Surbey models.

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes a dataset of at least 1784 data points (as implied by the error elimination analysis) consisting of field measurements for oil flow rate (STBD), upstream wellhead pressure (psi), mixture relative density, and choke size (64th of inch).

## Key Findings

- The new correlation outperformed all published correlations in literature for the data used in this study. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 20]])
- A total of 52 tests were removed due to single phase flow (upstream tubing pressure higher than bubble point). ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 61]])
- 335 tests were excluded because they did not meet the critical flow criteria (downstream pressure < 55% of upstream pressure). ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 61]])
- The study utilized a final dataset of 3,554 points for developing the new correlation. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 61]])
- Oil flow rates in the data ranged from 172 to 33,847 STBD. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 62]])
- Gas-oil ratios (GOR) in the dataset ranged from 12 to 5,026 SCF/STB. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 62]])
- Upstream tubing pressures ranged from 97 to 1,880 psia. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 62]])
- Choke sizes in the dataset ranged from 16 to 160 (in units of 1/64th of an inch). ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 62]])
- The study evaluated several existing correlations including Gilbert, Ros, Omana, Fortunati, and others. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 13]])
- The new correlation developed in this study resulted in an average absolute relative error of 8% for choke size and 18% for flow rate. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 99]])
- The T-statistics for all coefficients in the new correlation were significantly different from zero at the 99 percent confidence level, confirming that variables like oil flow rate (T=167.98) and tubing pressure (T=-57.41) are meaningful predictors. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 85]])
- The error distribution analysis for the new correlation showed a normal distribution with a mean of zero, indicating that errors were distributed evenly on both sides of the zero. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 83]])
- Sensitivity analysis revealed that oil flow rate has a strong effect in all choke ranges, particularly at lower choke sizes. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 83]])
- The average absolute relative errors for existing correlations were significantly higher: Omana (165% for choke size), Fortunati (24%), and Osman (77%). ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 98]])
- The new correlation showed a lower root mean square error (RMS) of 10.78 compared to the Gilbert correlation (16.46) and Surbey (25.06). ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 103]])
