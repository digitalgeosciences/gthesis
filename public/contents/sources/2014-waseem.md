# SEDIMENTOLOGICAL AND PETROPHYSICAL CHARACTERIZATION OF SARAH FORMATION WITH THE AID OF GIS, CENTRAL SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the sedimentological and petrophysical heterogeneity of the Late Ordovician Sarah Formation within the Al-Ilb paleochannel in Central Saudi Arabia. It utilizes field investigations, thin-section petrography, XRD, SEM-EDS, and CT scans to characterize porosity and permeability across proximal, medial, and distal zones, ultimately integrating this data into a GIS-based smart map.

---

## Research Problem

The Sarah Formation is an unconventional tight gas reservoir characterized by significant sedimentological heterogeneity and complex architecture in both horizontal and vertical dimensions. Limited subsurface information makes it difficult to predict reservoir behavior without high-resolution outcrop models.

## Methods

The research involved field investigation followed by facies analysis and construction of vertical/lateral profiles. Laboratory studies included thin-section petrography to identify mineralogy and textures, SEM-EDS for detailed mineral identification, XRD for semi-quantitative mineral phase determination, and CT-scan for non-destructive internal feature imaging. Petrophysical measurements were conducted using the gas expansion and saturation method for porosity and the gas permeability method for permeability. Statistical analysis (mean, median, skewness, kurtosis) was applied to characterize the distribution of petrophysical properties across three zones: proximal, medial, and distal.

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes samples from the Al-Ilb paleochannel in the Baqa area, including various lithofacies such as massive gravels (Gm), trough cross-bedded sandstones, and planed/horizontally cross-bedded sandstones. Data includes porosity measurements, permeability measurements (horizontal and vertical), and mineralogical data from XRD and SEM-EDX.

## Key Findings

- The Sarah Formation at the distal part (S1, S2) contains poorly sorted to moderately sorted sandstone with monocrystalline quartz and iron cement. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 13]])
- Proximal parts (S3, S4) are characterized by more consistent mineralogy including monocrystalline quartz and some polycrystalline components. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 13]])
- XRD analysis identified major minerals as Quartz (Q), Kaolinite (K), Iron oxide (Fe), and Chlorite (C). ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 14]])
- Petrophysical parameters like porosity and permeability are influenced by composition, texture, and diagenesis. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 106]])
- Architectural elements in the Al-Ilb paleochannel include sandy bedforms, channel bodies, and lateral accretion. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 59]])
- The Sarah Formation shows six lithofacies in a predominantly glacio-fluvial and braided river environment. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 22]])
- Sandstone is classified as quartz arenites (98% pure quartz on QFL classification) with very low feldspar content (~1%). ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 23]])
- Kaolinite and smectite are found in most samples, while iron oxide and clay minerals are predominantly present in the pores. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 23]])
- Petrophysical studies revealed high-quality porosity across all parts of the formation. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 23]])
- Permeability values are high, ranging from 53 mD to 5 D. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 23]])
- Porosity follows a normal distribution in all parts of the paleochannel, while permeability follows a log-normal distribution. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 23]])
- Scatter plots of porosity vs. horizontal and vertical permeability show very poor correlation for each part of the paleochannel. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 23]])
- The main controlling factors for petrophysical properties are clays, iron oxide, grain size, pore size, pore volume, and connection of pore throats. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 23]])
- Evidence of glacial retreat is indicated by the presence of massive gravels (Gm) facies at different parts of the channel. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 22]])
- The formation is characterized as a tight gas sandstone reservoir with significant heterogeneity in both horizontal and vertical directions. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 34]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Use CT-scan applications for detailed study of heterogeneity.
