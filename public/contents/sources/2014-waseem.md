# SEDIMENTOLOGICAL AND PETROPHYSICAL CHARACTERIZATION OF SARAH FORMATION WITH THE AID OF GIS, CENTRAL SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the sedimentological and petrophysical heterogeneity of the Sarah Formation within the Al-Ilb paleochannel in Central Saudi Arabia. Using a combination of field mapping, thin-section petrography, SEM-EDX, XRD, and CT scanning, it characterizes lithofacies and identifies kaolinite and iron oxide as primary controls on porosity and permeability.

---

## Research Problem

The Sarah Formation is characterized by high sedimentological heterogeneity and complex architecture in its glacio-fluvial deposits, making subsurface exploration for unconventional tight gas resources difficult. There is a lack of detailed information regarding the spatial distribution of facies and petrophysical properties across different parts of the paleochannel.

## Objectives

- Characterize the facies type, architectural element, and paleoenvironment of the Sarah Formation.
- Determine sedimentological characteristics and heterogeneity.
- Investigate the porosity and permeability distribution within the formation.
- Reconstruct GIS-based smart maps for the glacio-fluvial Sarah Formation.
- Characterize the sedimentological and petrophysical heterogeneity of the Late Ordovician Sarah Formation at proximal, medial, and distal parts of the Al-Ilb paleochannel.
- Identify lithofacies and sedimentary structures to determine depositional environments.

## Methods

The methodology includes field investigation (sedimentological mapping) followed by lithofacies analysis and construction of vertical/lateral profiles. Laboratory work included thin-section petrography on 27 samples, SEM-EDX on 10 samples to determine mineralogy and texture, XRD for semi-quantitative mineral phase identification, and CT scanning for non-destructive internal feature visualization. Petrophysical measurements were conducted using gas expansion and saturation methods for porosity and the gas permeability method for core plugs (150 selected from 226 total). Statistical analysis included univariate analysis of mean, median, standard deviation, skewness, and kurtosis to evaluate heterogeneity.

## Data and Materials

140 core plugs (selected from 226) representing proximal, medial, and distal parts of the Al-Ilb paleochannel; field samples for thin-section petrography; SEM-EDX, XRD, and CT scan data; georeferenced maps of Baqa quadrangle.

## Key Findings

- The Sarah Formation is characterized by six lithofacies in a predominantly glacio-fluvial and braided river environment. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 22]])
- Sandstone in the formation is classified as quartz arenites (98% pure quartz) with very low feldspar content (1%). ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 23]])
- Petrophysical studies revealed high-quality porosity across all parts of the study area, while permeability values ranged from 5 to 53 mD. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 23]])
- Porosity follows a normal distribution in proximal, medial, and distal parts, while permeability follows a log-normal distribution. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 23]])
- The primary controls on porosity and permeability are clay minerals (kaolinite, smectite), iron oxide, grain size, pore size, and pore throat connectivity. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 23]])
- Kaolinite and smectite were found in most samples, with kaolinite often filling pores and reducing permeability. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 23]])
- The presence of massive gravels (Gm) facies provides evidence of glacial retreat. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 22]])
- Petrophysical results showed very poor correlation between porosity and horizontal/vertical permeability for each part of the paleochannel. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 23]])
- The formation is significantly affected by shallow burial conditions during diagenesis. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 23]])
- Proximal parts are dominated by medium to coarse grained sandstones with trough cross-bedded and planar cross-bedded structures (St, Sp). ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 72]])
- The medial part represents a fluvial braided stream facies characterized by horizontally stratified sandstone (Sh) in a low to upper flow regime. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 73]])
- The Sarah Formation is characterized as mature quartz arenites with an average of 97% monocrystalline quartz. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 86]])
- Lithofacies include Glacial Outwash (St), Sand bedforms (Sp, Sh), and Glacial facies (Gm). ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 77]])
- The depositional model is identified as 'low sinuosity glacial outwash braided river deposits with linguoid bars'. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 81]])
- Kaolinite, chlorite, and palygorskite are the primary authigenic clays; kaolinite acts as a pore-filling/lining material that significantly reduces permeability. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 91]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

Future work should include: 1) Detailed outcrop studies of proximal/medial parts to integrate with South Rub Al-Khali basin data; 2) Correlation of Al-Ilb paleochannel with other paleovalleys; 3) Geostatistical modeling for reservoir heterogeneity; 4) Mapping of glacial deposits along the Arabian Shield; 5) 2D modeling in GIS for horizontal/vertical heterogeneity; 6) Further CT-scan applications.
