# DEGRADATION OF METHYL TERTIARY BUTYL ETHER USING UV/TITANIUM DIOXIDE AND CARBON NANOTUBES

**Summary**: The thesis investigated the photocatalytic degradation of MTBE and its by-products (acetone, TBA, and TBF) in water using titanium dioxide (TiO2), multi-walled carbon nanotubes (MWCNT), and a TiO2-MWCNT composite under UV light. The study concluded that TiO2 was the most effective photocatalyst for MTBE removal, while MWCNTs showed better performance than the TiO2-MWCNT composite.

---

## Research Problem

MTBE is a persistent fuel additive in groundwater with high water solubility and resistance to biodegradation, making it difficult to treat using conventional methods like air stripping or activated carbon. There is a need for efficient, cost-effective advanced oxidation processes (AOPs) to remediate MTBE-contaminated water.

## Objectives

- To demonstrate the removal of MTBE as well as its degradation by-products from MTBE-spiked water using TiO2, MWCNT, and TiO2-MWCNT.
- To examine MTBE removal efficiency under varying conditions of TiO2 and MWCNT dosage and UV light intensity.
- To identify the most efficient treatment condition in removing MTBE and its degradation by-products (acetone, TBA and TBF) from contaminated water.
- Develop a water treatment technique that efficiently removes MTBE and its degradation by-products from water.
- Combine the mechanisms of TiO2-photocatalysis and adsorption by multi-walled carbon nanotubes (MWCNTs).
- Synthesize and characterize 5% TiO2-MWCNT composites.

## Methods

The study utilized a modified sol-gel synthesis method to create 5% TiO2-MWCNT composites. Characterization of materials was performed using FE-SEM (Tescan Lyra 3), XRD (Rigaku Ultima IV MPD), and EDX (Oxford INCA Penta FETx3). MTBE-spiked water samples were prepared at a concentration of ~1 ppm and buffered to pH 7. Treatment was conducted in a Luzchem LZC-4X photoreactor using UV-A lamps with varying intensities (0.98, 1.12, and 2.17 mW/cm^2) and different catalyst dosages (1 mg, 10 mg, and 20 mg). Analysis of MTBE and by-products was performed via GC-MS using a Thermo Scientific Trace GC ultra with an ISQ mass spectrometer.

## Data and Materials

Samples included: 99.5% MTBE solution; ≥ 99.5% TBA; 1000 µg/mL TBF in methanol; analytical grade acetone; 69-71% HNO3; and NaOH pellets. Materials used for treatment were TiO2 (anatase), MWCNTs (8-15 nm diameter, 10-50 µm length), and a synthesized 5% TiO2-MWCNT composite.

## Key Findings

- TiO2 showed the highest removal rates for MTBE across all UV intensities, with removal reaching up to 95% at certain conditions (e.g., 10 mg dosage under 0.98 mW/cm^2). ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 49]])
- MWCNTs demonstrated higher removal rates than the TiO2-MWCNT composite, with removal rates up to 68.21% in some trials. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 10]])
- The concentration of MTBE was found to be optimal at pH 7; therefore, all subsequent experiments were conducted at this value. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 45]])
- At a dosage of 10 mg and UV intensity of 1.12 mW/cm^2, TiO2 achieved a removal rate of 96% (residual of 4%). ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 66]])
- TBF concentrations were significantly reduced to below detectable levels (<5 ppb) when using higher dosages or specific combinations that favored degradation. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 73]])
- The XRD patterns for anatase TiO2 showed peaks at 25.46, 37.94, 48.18, 55.207, and 70.44 degrees. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 35]])
- EDX analysis of TiO2 powder confirmed it was composed of 46.29 wt% Ti and 53.71 wt% O. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 34]])
- The XRD spectrum for MWCNT showed peaks at 25.62, 43.78, and 77.15 degrees corresponding to hexagonal graphite. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 39]])
- EDX analysis of the 5% TiO2-MWCNT composite confirmed the presence of Ti and O on the surface of MWCNTs. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 40]])
- At a dosage of 20 mg, MTBE residual after half an hour was 80% for 5% TiO2-MWCNT, 18% for TiO2, and 75% for MWCNT. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 83]])
- TiO2 showed a lower residual of 10% and 6% after one hour and ninety minutes respectively at a dosage of 20 mg. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 83]])
- At a dosage of 10 mg, acetone concentration was 30 ppb after an hour of treatment with 10 mg TiO2. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 86]])
- For TBA analysis, concentrations less than 5 ppb were recorded for all three materials (5% TiO2-MWCNT, TiO2, and MWCNT) at various dosages. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 89]])
- TBF concentration of 223 ppb was recorded after half an hour treatment with 1 mg TiO2. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 92]])
- UV-A lamps at intensity of 1.12 mW/cm2 resulted in better removal of MTBE and a consequent increase in by-product formation compared to other intensities. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 95]])

## Limitations

The study noted that some UV-A intensities (0.98 and 1.12 mW/cm2) were not optimal for certain results, and the mechanism of photo-induced-electron absorption in MWCNTs is not fully understood.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Synthesize TiO2-MWCNT composites with higher TiO2 loadings to improve photodegradation capabilities.
- Perform a detailed analysis of by-products to ensure no harmful contaminants are generated.
- Test the procedure on other contaminants such as benzene.
- Develop techniques to overcome the problem of settling of the materials.
