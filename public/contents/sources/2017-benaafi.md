# Nature and Characteristics of Fracture System within Wajid Sandstone Group, SW Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the fracture systems within the Wajid Sandstone Group in southwest Saudi Arabia, focusing on their distribution, geomechanical properties, and relationship with petrophysical characteristics. It utilizes a combination of remote sensing, field mapping, and laboratory analyses (XRD, SEM, Schmidt Hammer) to characterize these fractures as potential impacts on groundwater and hydrocarbon reservoirs.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of detailed studies on the natural fracture systems within the Wajid Group sandstone, which is a critical aquifer in the Wadi Al-Dawasir/Najran areas and a potential hydrocarbon reservoir in the Rub' Al-Khali Basin. Understanding these fractures is essential for improving fluid flow modeling and hydraulic fracturing design.

## Methods

A multi-scale approach was used: 1. Remote Sensing: Landsat-8 OLI/TIRS, Spot-5 images, and SRTM DEM were processed using Erdas Imagine and ArcMap to identify lineaments via radiometric and spatial enhancements (including edge enhancement and low-pass filtering). 2. Field Investigation: Direct characterization of fracture orientation, type, infilling, spacing, height, termination, and cross-cutting relationships at 15 outcrop stations in Wadi Al-Dawasir. 3. Laboratory Analysis: Petrographic analysis via thin sections; XRD (20 samples) for mineralogy; SEM-EDX (10 samples) for cement/clay identification. Petrophysical measurements included gas porosity, grain density, and permeability using a helium-porosimeter and mini-permeameter. Geomechanical tests included Schmidt Hammer field readings and uniaxial compression tests to determine Young's Modulus and UCS.

## Data and Materials

The study utilized satellite imagery (Landsat-8, Spot-5), SRTM DEM data, and aeromagnetic anomaly maps from the Saudi Geological Survey. Lineaments were mapped across multiple scales (1:1,500,000 to 1:6,000) in several sub-regions including Wadi Al-Dawasir, Tathlith, Najran, and Abha.

## Key Findings

- Two primary fracture sets (N165° and N075°) were identified persisting along the vertical outcrop of the Sanamah Formation. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 13]])
- Fracture infilling materials include iron oxides, calcite, reworked sandstone, and siltstone. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 13]])
- Geomechanical properties (UCS, Young's Modulus) were analyzed for 82 sandstone samples from the Wajid Group. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 10]])
- A correlation was established between grain size and geomechanical properties like Schmidt Hammer Rebound Number (RN) and Uniaxial Compressive Strength (UCS). ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 14]])
- Note: Specific quantitative values for UCS or Young's Modulus are listed in Table 5.1 but the text provided is limited to the table of contents/introductory pages. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017]])
- The regional lineaments are oriented in northerly (N-S, NNW-SSE, NNE-SSW) and NW-SE directions in eastern outcrops, while western outcrops are dominated by NE-SW trending lineaments. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 18]])
- Three main fracture sets were identified at the outcrop scale: NNW-SSE, NNE-SSW, and ENE-WSW; NW-SE and NE-SW were also observed but in smaller numbers. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 18]])
- Fractures within the Wajid Group are primarily vertical to sub-vertical, Mode 1 type (joints), and are often open or filled with calcite or iron oxide. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 18]])
- Four main geological factors control fracture distribution (average spacing): bed thickness, diagenetic features (cementation/dissolution), lithofacies type, and matrix porosity. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 18]])
- Geomechanical characteristics of the Wajid Group are primarily controlled by sandstone texture, cement content and type, matrix porosity, and matrix permeability. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 18]])
- XRD analysis of Khusayyayn formation samples showed quartz and microcline minerals. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 120]])
- XRD analysis of Sanamah formation samples (S21) showed quartz and Kaolinite minerals. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 121]])
- XRD analysis of Juwayl formation sample (J3.5) showed quartz and calcite minerals. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 121]])
- The NW-SE trending fracture set strongly controls the fluid flow behavior in the Wajid sandstone aquifer. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 34]])
- Two major lineament trends, NNE-SSW (0150) and NW-SE (1450), were dominant across the entire outcrop of the Wajid Group. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 56]])
