# Nature and Characteristics of Fracture System within Wajid Sandstone Group, SW Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the multi-scale fracture systems within the Wajid Sandstone Group in southwest Saudi Arabia, analyzing how lithological, stratigraphic, and petrophysical properties influence fracture spacing. It concludes that while regional lineaments are largely independent of local geology, outcrop-scale fractures are significantly controlled by bed thickness, cementation type/degree, and lithofacies.

---

## Research Problem

The need to understand the role of fractures in enhancing reservoir permeability and the specific geological/petrophysical factors that control fracture distribution within the Wajid Sandstone Group for groundwater and hydrocarbon potential.

## Objectives

- Map and characterize the regional and outcrop-scale fracture system within the Wajid Group outcrops.
- Determine the possible tectonic origin of the fracture system.
- Characterize the fracture-hosting sandstone properties (sedimentological, stratigraphical, and petrophysical) to identify factors controlling geomechanical and fracture characteristics.
- Quantify fracture parameters including orientation, spacing, height, and termination.
- Characterize geomechanical properties (Schmidt reading number, UCS, Young's Modulus) of the sandstones.
- Establish relationships between geomechanical properties and petrophysical/petrographic properties.

## Methods

The study utilized a multi-scale approach: 1) Regional scale: Integration of satellite imagery (Landsat-8 OLI/TIRS, Spot-5), SRTM Digital Elevation Models (DEM), and magnetic anomaly maps to identify lineament trends. 2) Outcrop scale: Direct measurement of fracture spacing and orientation from field samples. 3) Petrophysical analysis: Porosity measured via porosimeter; grain density, porosity, and permeability analyzed for 63 samples. 4) Mineralogical analysis: XRD and SEM used to determine mineral composition and cement types. 5) Geomechanical testing: Determination of Uniaxial Compressive Strength (UCS) and Young's Modulus (E). 6) Statistical analysis: Linear regression and correlation analyses between fracture spacing and various rock properties.

## Data and Materials

Data from 15 outcrop stations; 82 sandstone samples for laboratory testing (UCS, Young's Modulus); detailed measurements of fracture orientation, spacing, height, and termination across multiple sets (N015o, N165o, N075o, N135o, N035o).

## Key Findings

- The regional lineament trends are dominated by NW-SE (1450) and NNE-SSW (0150), with the latter being particularly prominent in the eastern outcrops. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 57]])
- At the outcrop scale, three main fracture sets were identified as predominant: NNW-SSE, NNE-SSW, and ENE-WSW; however, NW-SE and NE-SW were also observed. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 18]])
- Fractures within the Wajid Group are mainly vertical to sub-vertical, Mode 1 type (joints), and many are open but some are infilled with calcite or iron oxide. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 18]])
- Four main geological factors control fracture distribution (average spacing): bed thickness, diagenetic features (cementation and dissolution), lithofacies type, and matrix porosity. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 18]])
- Geomechanical characteristics are primarily controlled by sandstone texture, cement content/type, matrix porosity, and matrix permeability. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 18]])
- Lineament trace lengths follow a power-law distribution with R2=0.956. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 56]])
- Large-scale lineaments (length > 5 km) are predominantly associated with three trends: northerly (N-S, NNE-SSW, NNW-SSE), NE-SW, and NW-SE. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 57]])
- The western outcrops in the Abha area are dominated by NE-SW trending lineaments (0350). ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 72]])
- Three primary fracture sets were identified as predominant: N165o, N015o, and N075o; the N165o and N075o are dominant in the northern part, while N015o is more dominant in the southern part. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 82]])
- Extensional fractures (mode 1) are the dominant type of fracture forming the system in Wajid Group outcrops, often appearing as open joints. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 82]])
- Fracture spacing for N165o varies significantly by formation: 1.9m (average) in lower Dibsiyah, 9.3m in upper Dibsiyah, 7.9m in Sanamah, 5.8m in Khusayyayn, and 5.3m in Juwayl. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 86]])
- Fracture swarms (e.g., N015o) exhibit a density approximately ten times higher than the normal fracture pattern, averaging around 10 m^-1. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 94]])
- Juwayl Formation shows the highest average Schmidt reading numbers (31.40), UCS (7.97 MPa), and Young's Modulus (48.28 GPa). ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 97]])
- Sanamah Formation exhibits high variability in geomechanical properties, with a CV of 1.96 for UCS. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 98]])
- A strong negative correlation exists between mean grain size and geomechanical properties (RN: -0.79, UCS: -0.82, E: -0.83) in the Juwayl Formation. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 104]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

The identified controlling factors (bed thickness, porosity, degree of cementation, and lithofacies) can be used to predict the relative average fracture spacing within subsurface equivalent sandstone hosts.
