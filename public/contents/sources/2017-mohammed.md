# CHARACTERIZATION OF THE CARBONATE MUD ROCKS OF THE MIDDLE JURASSIC TUWAIQ MOUNTAIN FORMATION, JAFURAH SUB-BASIN, SAUDI ARABIA; IMPLICATION FOR UNCONVENTIONAL RESERVOIR QUALITY PREDICTION

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the carbonate mud rocks of the Tuwaiq Mountain Formation in the Jafurah Sub-Basin to evaluate their potential as unconventional reservoir source rocks. Using a combination of rock-eval pyrolysis, bitumen reflectance, and mud gas isotope logging (MGIL), the study establishes maturity models and identifies distinct hydrocarbon signatures for different formations.

---

## Research Problem

The Tuwaiq Mountain Formation is a known source rock for the Mesozoic Petroleum System in Saudi Arabia, but there is a gap in understanding its specific potential as an unconventional reservoir due to lack of detailed petrophysical and geochemical characterization in the Jafurah Sub-Basin.

## Methods

The study utilized a multi-scale approach: 1) Field work and core analysis for lithofacies identification across six types. 2) Mineralogical characterization using XRD. 3) Petrophysical evaluation of well logs and core data to determine porosity, permeability, and saturation. 4) Geochemical analysis including TOC determination and mud gas isotope logging (MGIL) for maturity assessment. 5) Basin modeling (1D and 2D) to evaluate burial history and thermal maturation. 6) Integration of microseismic and hydraulic fracturing simulation models to determine fracture half-lengths (ranging from 400 to 800 ft) and drainage strategies.

## Data and Materials

Data includes core samples from the Tuwaiq Mountain Formation across different maturity windows, well logs from three exploration wells (A, B, C), microseismic data from 7 monitored stages in a horizontal well, and production logging (PLT) data for flow analysis.

## Key Findings

- The TQMN is characterized by carbonate mud rocks with distinct lithofacies including 'none-reservoir' (organic lean) and 'reservoir' (organic rich) facies. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 11]])
- Mineralogical composition of the Tuwaiq Mountain includes significant clay content, which influences anisotropy in mechanical properties. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 160]])
- TOC values were successfully predicted from seismic-inverted attributes like acoustic impedance (AI). ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 171]])
- page_number_not_specified_in_source ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017]])
- Six distinct lithofacies were identified in the Tuwaiq Mountain Formation: 1. Shallow marine bioturbated (oxic), 2. Deep marine bioturbated (oxic), 3. Horizontal bioturbated (dysoxic), 4. Laminated with storm ripples (anoxic), 5. Laminated without ripples (anoxic), and 6. Massive anoxic. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 23]])
- The formation was divided into three tiers; Tier 1 (bottom) consists of lime mudstone with high TOC (up to 10%), low clay content (3-5%), and high porosity, making it the most promising for shale gas. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 17]])
- Mud gas isotope logging (MGIL) successfully distinguished between Middle and Upper Jurassic source rocks based on their unique isotopic signatures. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 17]])
- A new methane carbon isotopic maturity correlation was calibrated for the Tuwaiq Mountain source rock to provide accurate maturity assessments. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 20]])
- Basin modeling showed that TQMN source rocks began generating oil at ~100 Ma, reached peak oil generation at ~70 Ma, and are currently in a wet gas window in warmer parts of the basin (≥28 °C/km). ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 25]])
- Higher temperature gradient areas require higher basement heat flow input, which was linked to an increase in granitic basement and radiogenic heat generation. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 17]])
- Simulation models for hydraulic fracturing suggest that fracture half-lengths range between 400 ft and 800 ft. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 27]])
- The Jafurah Sub-Basin is a new frontier unconventional exploration play equivalent in size to the Eagle Ford play in south Texas. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 16]])
- The Tuwaiq Mountain Formation contains six distinct lithofacies based on rock composition, bioturbation, sedimentary structures, and organic richness. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 28]])
- Lithofacies 1 and 2 are non-reservoir facies with low TOC (< 1%). ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 31]])
- Lithofacies 3, 4, 5, and 6 are potential unconventional reservoir targets due to low clay content (< 5%) and high organic matter (> 3%). ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 32]])

## Limitations

Data on subsurface depth and temperature could not be provided due to company confidentiality. The study notes that standard basin modeling parameters for oil migration can lead to overestimations of expelled oil and underestimations of retained gas if not properly adjusted.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The study recommends using integrated multi-discipline data to inform completion optimization and drainage strategies, specifically highlighting the need for tailored drilling, stimulation, and perforation designs based on specific reservoir parameters.
