# Sequence Stratigraphy of Arab C Carbonates in Khursaniyah Field, Eastern Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the sequence stratigraphy of the Arab-C carbonate reservoir in the Khursaniyah Field, characterizing it as a fining-upward composite sequence. It establishes a high-resolution stratigraphic framework by identifying six high-frequency sequences and analyzing their associated depositional models.

---

## Research Problem

The need for a higher resolution sequence stratigraphic framework than previously available (Exxon 1982) to better resolve the scale of correlation, map reservoir facies, and determine the geological factors controlling reservoir quality in the Arab-C carbonate and underlying/overlying evaporites.

## Objectives

- To build a high-resolution sequence stratigraphic framework for the Arab-C and sub-C carbonates and the underlying and overlying evaporites.
- To generate the associated depositional models within the sequence stratigraphic framework of Khursaniyah Field.
- Perform geological characterization of core including mineralogy, porosity, texture, and sedimentary structures.
- Define the carbonate/evaporate facies suite for the Arab-C in Khursaniyah Field.
- Determine the vertical cyclic organization of facies in each core.
- Establish the stacking pattern and hierarchy of defined cycles.
- Identify fundamental surfaces and their hierarchy within the stacking pattern.
- Construct 2-D sequence stratigraphic cross sections to map sequences and systems tracts.
- Develop a depositional facies model based on core logs and correlation framework.
- Establish a high-resolution sequence stratigraphic framework for the Arab-C carbonates.
- Identify and characterize the high-frequency sequences (HFS) within the Arab-C and underlying Arab-D units.
- Develop depositional models based on the analysis of facies changes and sea-level fluctuations.

## Methods

The study utilized nine wells with 465 meters of core. Core characterization included detailed bed-by-bed descriptions (mineralogy, porosity, texture, grain types, sedimentary structures, surfaces, grain size, and fossils) using a hand lens and binocular microscope. Log-to-core calibration was performed by matching core gamma-ray logs to well logs (gamma-ray, caliper, density, neutron, sonic). Thin section petrography was conducted on 100 thin sections to investigate grain and pore types. A depositional model was constructed based on the identified facies and their spatial/temporal distribution.

## Data and Materials

Core samples from the Khursaniyah field, including those from wells such as KRSN-F, KRSN-G, KRSN-H, and KRSN-I; thin sections of various carbonate types (mollusk-rich ooid grainstones, thrombolites, etc.); 3D seismic data for structural mapping.

## Key Findings

- The Arab-C carbonate is an overall fining-ward composite sequence that can be subdivided into six high-frequency sequences. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 12]])
- Each of these high-frequency sequences can be further subdivided into multiple fining-upward small-scale cycles. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 12]])
- The Arab-C reservoir ranges from 120 to 160 feet in thickness. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 16]])
- The underlying 'Sub-C' reservoir is a carbonate unit that is time-equivalent to the evaporites between the Arab-C and Arab-D, ranging from 25 to 40 feet in thickness. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 16]])
- Water depths for the Arab-C carbonate are estimated to have fluctuated in the range of 0-15m based on fossil assemblages and sedimentary structures. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 22]])
- The Khursaniyah structure is a NE-SW, doubly-plunging near domal anticline with maximum dips of 9 to 10 degrees. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 31]])
- The Arab-C reservoir is an overall fining-upward composite sequence that can be subdivided into six high-frequency sequences. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 33]])
- The Arab-C reservoir consists of an average 120 ft of packstone to grainstone, overlain by 15-30 ft of anhydrite. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 34]])
- Nine distinct depositional facies were identified: Mollusk-rich Ooid Grainstones, Ooid Grainstones, Intraclast-Peloid Grainstone/Grain-dominated Packstone, Thrombolites, Nodular to Massive Anhydrite, Muddy/Grainy (Stromatolites), and Dolomitic Breccia. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 45]])
- The sedimentary environment for the Arab-C transitioned from low energy (sabkha/tidal flat) to high energy (ooid-grainstone/rudstone). ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 54]])

- The Arab-C carbonate is an overall fining-upward composite sequence that can be subdivided into six high-frequency sequences. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 68]])

- The first composite sequence (AD) is made up of two high-frequency sequences (HFS) defined by transgressive grainstone shoals and anhydrite interbedded with mudstones. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 71]])

## Limitations

The study was performed over a very small area, which may limit the generalizability of the depositional model to larger regions.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Integrate geological data with engineering data (pressure, petrophysical) for further reservoir characterization.
- Perform isotope studies for the Arab-C reservoir to understand diagenesis, climate, and stratigraphy.
- Acquire new core in areas of the field that currently lack good core samples.
- Expand the study regionally to improve understanding of the depositional environment.
