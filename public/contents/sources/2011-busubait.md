# Sequence Stratigraphy of Arab C Carbonates in Khursaniyah Field, Eastern Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the sequence stratigraphy of the Arab-C carbonate reservoir in the Khursaniyah Field, characterizing it as a fining-upward composite sequence. It establishes a high-resolution stratigraphic framework and identifies depositional models based on facies shifts rather than significant water depth changes.

---

## Research Problem

The need for a higher resolution sequence stratigraphic framework for the Arab-C carbonates (150 ft thick) and adjacent evaporites to better resolve the scale of correlation, map reservoir facies, and identify geological factors controlling reservoir quality in the Khursaniyah field.

## Objectives

- To build a high-resolution sequence stratigraphic framework for the Arab-C and sub-C carbonates and the underlying and overlying evaporites.
- To generate the associated depositional models within the sequence stratigraphic framework of Khursaniyah Field.
- Identify and characterize the high-frequency sequences within the Arab-C composite sequence.
- Establish depositional models based on the relationship between facies, sea-level changes, and accommodation space.

## Methods

The study utilized core analysis (465 meters of core), thin-section petrography (100 thin sections), and well-log analysis. Core characterization included detailed bed-by-bed sedimentology, mineralogy, porosity, carbonate texture (modified Dunham), grain types, sedimentary structures, and identification of surfaces. Log-to-core calibration involved matching gamma-ray, caliper, density, neutron, and sonic logs to core data. A 2D sequence stratigraphic cross-section was constructed to map cycles, high-frequency sequences, and long-term sequences.

## Data and Materials

Core samples from the Khursaniyah Field; petrographic thin sections of various lithofacies including peloidal grainstone and oolitic skeletal grainstone; stratigraphic data for the Arab-C (150 ft thick) and underlying/overlying evaporites.

## Key Findings

- The Arab-C carbonate is an overall fining-upward composite sequence that can be subdivided into six high-frequency sequences. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 12]])
- Each of these high-frequency sequences can be further subdivided into multiple fining-upward small-scale cycles. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 12]])
- The Arab-C reservoir is divided into Arab-C and sub-C, where the sub-C is equivalent to the Upper Arab-D Sequence in surrounding fields. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 71]])
- The upper part of the Arab-D sequence (AD) is made up of two high-frequency sequences: AD HFS 1 and AD HFS 2. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 74]])
- The Arab-C sequence (AC) is made of four high-frequency cycles (HFS). ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 71]])
- Mollusk-rich ooid grainstones are characterized by grains ranging from coarse to pebble and were deposited in a high-energy setting. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 45]])
- Ooid grainstone facies show well-winnowed, often well-sorted textures with sedimentary structures like planar horizontal lamination and cross bedding. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 47]])
- Intraclast-peloid grainstones contain grapestone intraclasts resulting from early cementation/binding of ooids and other grains. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 49]])
- Thrombolites are characterized by a clotted rather than laminated texture, indicating a low energy environment. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 50]])
- Anhydrite occurs as a 15+ ft thick layer sealing the Arab-C reservoir and is consistent with a sabkha/salina depositional-diagenetic origin. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 51]])
- The sedimentary facies of the Arab-C represent an energy transition from low-energy sabkha/tidal flat to high-energy ooid-grainstone/rudstone facies. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 54]])
- Five depositional environments were identified: Sabkha, Tidal flat (stromatolites), Lagoon, Shoal, and Foreshoal. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 56]])
- Arab-C High-Frequency Sequence 1 (AC-HFS-1) is up to 6 meters (15-20 feet) thick and defined by a retrogradational set of oolitic skeletal grainstone to crinkly laminated grainstone. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 77]])
- Arab-C High-Frequency Sequence 2 (AC-HFS-2) is up to 25 meters (70-80 feet) thick and defined by a fining-upward sequence starting with partly dolomitic oolitic skeletal grainstone. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 79]])
- Arab-C High-Frequency Sequence 4 (AC-HFS-4) is up to 9 meters (20-25 feet) thick, part of a regressive set of cross-bedded skeletal ooid grainstones. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 80]])

## Limitations

The study was performed over a very small area; some facies like peloidal grainstone have limited thin section availability due to cementation.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Integrate geological data with engineering data (pressure, petrophysics) for further reservoir characterization.
- Perform isotope studies to understand diagenesis, climate, and stratigraphy.
- Acquire new core in areas with poor core quality.
- Expand the study regionally to improve understanding of depositional environments.
