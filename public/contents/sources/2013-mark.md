# SEISMIC ATTRIBUTES AND ADVANCED COMPUTER ALGORITHM TO PREDICT FORMATION PORE PRESSURE: QALIBAH FORMATION OF NORTHWEST SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis explores the use of Support Vector Machine (SVM) algorithms and seismic attributes to predict pore pressure in the Qalibah Formation of Northwest Saudi Arabia. By training an SVM model on mud-weight data from three wells and eight seismic attribute volumes, the study successfully predicted pore pressure at a blind test well (Well-4), demonstrating that SVM can effectively identify high-pressure zones obscured by lithological changes.

---

## Research Problem

Predicting pre-drill pore pressure is critical for safety and drilling efficiency; however, traditional methods are often masked by lithology and pore-space content. The study addresses the need for a more robust method to identify abnormal pressure in the Qalibah Formation where high-pressure zones (e.g., the 'hot' Qusaiba shale member) are difficult to distinguish from surrounding formations using standard seismic velocity models.

## Objectives

- Investigate the utility of Support Vector Machine (SVM) to learn patterns of high pore pressure regimes.
- Utilize specific seismic attributes (Instantaneous Phase, t*Attenuation, Cosine of Phase, Vp/Vs ratio, P-Impedance, Reflection Acoustic Impedance, Dominant frequency) and well data as a training datasets.
- Identify the best combination of well and seismic attributes to reliably predict formation pore pressure in the Qalibah Formation.
- Utilize SVM algorithm as a pattern recognition tool to learn and predict the seismic signature of sediments under abnormal pore pressure.
- Evaluate the effectiveness of various seismic attributes (e.g., Vp/Vs, P-Impedance) in predicting mud-weight requirements.
- Validate the prediction model using a blind test well (Well-4) located 7 km from the training wells.

## Methods

The study utilized an SVM algorithm to integrate eight seismic attribute volumes with well data. The input attributes included Vp/Vs, P-Impedance, Attenuation, Reflection Intensity, Cosine of Phase, Reflection Acoustic Impedance, Dominant Frequency, and Instantaneous Frequency. A parameter file (parameter.txt) was used to configure the SVM, including parameters like Nsubset, Attribute_correlation_threshhold, and Gamma1. The model's output was a mud-weight seismic attribute volume, which was then converted to pore pressure using the formula: Pore Pressure (psi) = mud weight (ppg) * 0.052 (psi) * 19.25.

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes 3D seismic data from the Qalibah Formation in Northwest Saudi Arabia. Training data includes mud-weight curves from three wells (Well-1, Well-2, Well-3) and a blind test well (Well-4). Eight specific seismic attributes were extracted from the 3D volume for analysis.

## Key Findings

- The study found that pore pressure trends in the Qalibah formation can be predicted using SVM with both seismic and well attributes as the learning dataset. ([[2013-mark|Mark, 2013, p. 12]])
- The results showed subtle variations of pressure within the thick succession of shale units of the Qalibah formation. ([[2013-mark|Mark, 2013, p. 12]])
- Mud-weight curves were identified as a good discriminator between formations under normal pressure and those under high pore pressure. ([[2013-mark|Mark, 2013, p. 81]])
- The Qalibah formation is characterized by overpressure conditions likely resulting from hydrocarbon generation within the formation and surrounding beds, forming a bounding seal. ([[2013-mark|Mark, 2013, p. 50]])
- The study identified eight specific seismic attributes (Instantaneous Frequency, Average Frequency, Reflection Intensity, t*Attenuation, Cosine of Phase, Vs/Vp, P-Impedance, and Reflective Acoustic Impedance) for use in the SVM algorithm. ([[2013-mark|Mark, 2013, p. 74]])
- Vp/Vs (correlation index 0.431) and P-Impedance (0.429) were identified as the most influential seismic attributes for predicting pore pressure. ([[2013-mark|Mark, 2013, p. 86]])
- The SVM prediction showed a high degree of correlation with actual well mud-weight at Well-4, successfully identifying the transition from higher pressured Qalibah Formation to lower pressured Sarah Formation. ([[2013-mark|Mark, 2013, p. 98]])
- The average correlation indices for the eight attributes were: Vp/Vs (0.431), P-Impedance (0.292), Attenuation (0.226), Reflection Intensity (0.058), Cosine of Phase (0.042), Reflection Acoustic Impedance (0.038), Dominant Frequency (0.036), and Instantaneous Frequency (0.001). ([[2013-mark|Mark, 2013, p. 86]])
- The SVM algorithm was found to be faster than traditional seismic velocity modeling for pore pressure prediction while requiring less complex infrastructure. ([[2013-mark|Mark, 2013, p. 104]])

## Limitations

The author noted that the current results are indicative of borehole pressure; a direct, satisfactory empirical equation for converting mud-weight values directly to pore pressure without intermediate steps was not found to their satisfaction.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Future research should focus on developing well data that continuously measure pore pressure and finding better methods to convert predicted mud-weight volumes directly into pore pressure volumes.
