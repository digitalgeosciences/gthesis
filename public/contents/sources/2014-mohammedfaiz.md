# Sedimentological and stratigraphic studies of the Cambro-Ordovician succession in northwest Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the sedimentology and stratigraphy of the Cambro-Ordovician clastic succession in northwest Saudi Arabia, specifically focusing on the Siq, Quweira, and Saq Sandstones. Through field mapping, paleocurrent analysis, and thin-section petrography, it identifies 14 sedimentary facies to characterize depositional environments ranging from fluvial to tidal systems.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of detailed stratigraphic and sedimentological characterization of the Cambro-Ordovician succession in northwest Saudi Arabia, specifically regarding the transition from continental to marine environments in the Saq Sandstone.

## Objectives

- Integrate sedimentological descriptions, facies associations, and distributions to interpret the depositional setting.
- Perform petrographic analysis to identify mineralogical changes across stratigraphic boundaries.
- Analyze paleocurrent directions to determine shifts in flow patterns.
- Evaluate the lithostratigraphic relationships between the Siq, Quweira, and Saq Sandstones.
- Describe the lithostratigraphy and sedimentary structures of the Saq, Siq, and Quweira Sandstones.
- Analyze the paleoenvironmental evolution of the Cambrian-Ordovician succession.

## Methods

The study involved field investigation of 20 measured sections across two primary areas (Al Ula and Tabuk). Sedimentological descriptions included grain size, sedimentary structures, lithology, and color-coded facies. Paleocurrent data were collected from cross-bedded sets using a Brunton compass. Thin section petrography was performed on samples from all studied facies; some were stained with sodium-cobaltinitrite to identify K-feldspars. Point counting (300 points per thin section) was used to quantify detrital and authigenic components.

## Data and Materials

Data includes multiple measured sections in Al Ula (e.g., U101, U305) and Tabuk (e.g., T101, T102). Petrographic analysis was performed on a selection of thin sections (e.g., samples A: U102-S4-1; B: U302-S5). Paleocurrent data were compiled into rose diagrams for various sandstone units.

## Key Findings

- The Al Ula area is dominated by high-energy, braided-fluvial, in-channel deposition with minor tidal influence. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 25]])
- The Tabuk area shows a significantly increased tidal dominance compared to the Al Ula area. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 25]])
- A major stratigraphic break exists at the base of the Upper Siq Sandstone, marked by a rejuvenation of fluvial facies. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 25]])
- Petrographic analysis indicates a petrographic break at the same boundary (base of Upper Siq), marked by the complete loss of feldspars above that level. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 25]])
- Paleocurrent directions show a major shift from NW to NE at the base of the Upper Siq Sandstone. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 25]])
- The Siq Sandstone is divided into three units: Lower, Middle, and Upper Siq Sandstones. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 28]])
- Facies association III is characterized by fining-upward mixed facies including braided fluvial and high/low energy tidally-influenced deposits. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 140]])
- Facies association II includes braided fluvial channel deposits that transition into tidally-influenced facies. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 138]])
- Facies association IV represents high-energy tidally-dominated deposition of tidal sand flats, possibly in an estuary. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 141]])
- Evidence of significant role of tectonic fluctuations during the Cambro-Ordovician time is suggested by overall characteristics. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 26]])
- The Saq Sandstone in central Arabia is interpreted as a transition from fluviatile environments at the base (Risha member) to tidal and shallow marine environments at the top (Sajir member). ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 51]])
- The Siq Sandstone is characterized by robust cross-bedding, being interpreted as a non-marine sandstone in some areas but showing evidence of rapid deposition in a shallow marine basin in others. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 63]])
- The Quweira Sandstone shows a transition from alluvial braided to straight-river systems (lower part) to deltaic and shoreface environments (upper part). ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 67]])
- The Sajir member of the Saq Sandstone in the Tayma quadrangle is divided into three units, showing a progression from distal alluvial braided plains to intertidal distributary plains. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 70]])
- The Wajid Sandstone includes several members (Dibsiyah, Sanamah, Khussayyayn, Juwayl) representing various fluviatile and marine-influenced environments. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 74]])

## Limitations

The study was limited to selected study areas due to time and logistics concerns related to field work; petrography was not a full characterization of the entire succession but focused on representative samples from all facies.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The study suggests a need for revisiting the stratigraphic nomenclature and redefining the sandstone units based on current findings.
