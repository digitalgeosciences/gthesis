# Sedimentological and stratigraphic studies of the Cambro-Ordovician succession in northwest Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the sedimentological and stratigraphic characteristics of the Cambro-Ordovician succession in northwest Saudi Arabia, specifically focusing on the Saq Sandstone (Risha and Sajir members) and the Wajid Sandstone. It integrates petrographic analysis, paleocurrent data, and facies studies to interpret depositional environments ranging from fluvial to marine settings.

---

## Methods

The research involved field mapping and measurement of sedimentary structures, including cross-bedding. Petrographic analysis (thin-section petrography) was used to characterize lithology. Facies were categorized into 14 types based on sedimentological features. These were grouped into seven facies associations (FA). Paleocurrent data (270 dip-direction measurements) were analyzed using rose diagrams to determine mean directions and identify changes in sedimentary source areas.

## Data and Materials

Petrographic data from multiple samples (S1 through S12) across several sections (U102, U103, U301, U302, U401, U501-a, T101, T102). Data includes mineral counts (Quartz, Feldspars, Lithics), clay minerals (Muscovite, Kaolinite), and porosity measurements (Intergranular and Intragranular).

## Key Findings

- The Siq Sandstone in the Al Ula area is characterized by high-energy sedimentary structures including trough cross-bedded sets and mud clasts. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 12]])
- The Quweira Sandstone shows evidence of significant deformation, such as oversteepened and recumbent-folded foresets and fluid-escape structures. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 17]])
- Facies association I is characterized by fining-upward braided fluvial channel deposits. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 18]])
- Facies association II includes fining-upward braided fluvial channel deposits that transition into tidally-influenced facies. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 18]])
- Facies association III is characterized by fining-upward mixed facies including braided fluvial and tidally-influenced deposits. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 18]])
- High-energy tidally-dominated deposition of tidal sand flats was identified in the Quweira Sandstone (e.g., section T101). ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 18]])
- The Saq Sandstone in the Tabuk area contains planar tabular cross-bedded sets. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 13]])
- Double-clay drapes were observed in several sandstone units, including the Middle Siq Sandstone. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 16]])
- Syneresis cracks were identified in massive sandstone beds as indicators of fluctuating salinities. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 16]])
- The Al Ula area is dominated by high-energy, braided-fluvial, in-channel deposition with minor tidal influence. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 25]])
- The Tabuk area shows a significantly increased tidal dominance compared to the Al Ula area. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 25]])
- variation in facies associations and distributions between Al Ula and Tabuk provide evidence of paleogeographic and proximality implications. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014]])

- A major stratigraphic break exists at the base of the Upper Siq Sandstone, marked by a rejuvenation of fluvial facies. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 25]])
- Petrographic analysis shows a distinct break at the same boundary (base of Upper Siq), characterized by the complete loss of feldspars. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 25]])

## Limitations

The study was limited to selected study areas due to time and logistics concerns related to the field study. Petrography work was not intended for a full petrographic characterization of the succession but rather to provide initial insight into lithostratigraphically debated features.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The study suggests the need for revisiting stratigraphic nomenclature and redefining the sandstone units based on the identified lithostratigraphic and petrographic breaks.
