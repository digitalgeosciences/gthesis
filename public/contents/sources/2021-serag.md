# THE EFFECT OF DRILLING FLUID TYPE AND EXPOSURE TIME ON GEOMECHANICAL PROPERTIES OF SANDSTONE FORMATIONS

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the impact of different drilling fluid types (barite, hematite, micromax, and ilmenite) and exposure times on the geomechanical properties and pore systems of Berea Buff and other sandstone formations. Using a combination of NMR, SEM, and ultrasonic testing, it quantifies how mud-rock interaction leads to significant reductions in rock strength, permeability, and changes in elastic moduli.

---

## Research Problem

Wellbore instability in horizontal drilling sections caused by chemical and mechanical interactions between drilling fluids and the formation. Specifically, how exposure time and fluid type affect geomechanical parameters like Young's modulus, Poisson's ratio, and UCS in sandstone formations.

## Objectives

- Study the effect of the exposure time of rock-fluid interactions on rock mechanics.
- Investigate the effect of the mud filtrate on the rock geomechanical properties.
- Investigate the effect of the higher or extreme overbalanced pressure on the fluid-rock interactions and rock geomechanical properties.
- Study the rock geomechanics changes due to the different fluid saturation for the sandstone core samples.
- Evaluate the effect of different weighting materials (barite, hematite, micromax, ilmenite) on the rock pore system and geomechanical properties.
- Quantify the impact of mud-rock interaction time on rock strength (UCS and TS) and dynamic elastic moduli.

## Methods

The study utilized Berea Buff, Berea Spider, Bandera Brown, and Parker sandstone samples. Core samples were characterized using XRD for mineralogy, XRF for weighting material composition, and NMR to determine porosity, permeability, and pore size distribution. Ultrasonic pulse velocity (UPV) was used to measure Vp and Vs to calculate dynamic elastic moduli. A scratch-test machine was employed to evaluate rock strength (UCS and TS). Mud-rock interaction was simulated in a customized aging cell under high pressure (300 psi) and temperature (200°F) for durations of 30 minutes, 1 day, 3 days, and 5 days. For the second study phase, mud filtrate from barite-weighted WBM was applied to four sandstone types for 10 days.

## Data and Materials

Samples included Berea Buff (91% quartz), Berea Spider (82.2% quartz), Bandera Brown (59% quartz), and Parker (87% quartz). Four drilling fluid types were tested: Barite-WBM, Hematite-WBM, Micromax-WBM, and Ilmenite-WBM. NMR data provided T2 distribution profiles; ultrasonic measurements recorded Vp and Vs in km/s.

## Key Findings

- Exposure time is the most critical parameter; rock strength decreased by 19% for five days of exposure in certain conditions. ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 18]])
- Young's modulus increased from 7.72 GPa (saturated) to 9.52 GPa after 5 days of mud interaction, representing a 23% increase. ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 55]])
- Poisson's ratio increased from 0.24 (saturated) to 0.29 at 30 minutes and stabilized at 0.3 after one day of interaction. ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 55]])
- Bulk modulus (K) increased by 60% (from 4.87 to 7.78 GPa) after five days of mud interaction. ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 56]])
- finding_page ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021]])
- Rock strength (UCS) decreased from 50.25 MPa to 40.51 MPa (a 19% reduction) after 5 days of interaction with barite-weighted mud. ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 57]])
- Tensile strength (TS) reduced from 3.71 MPa to 3.04 MPa over a 5-day exposure period. ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 58]])
- Porosity of the Berea Buff sample decreased from 21% to 12.4% after 5 days of mud interaction, with significant reduction occurring in the first 24 hours. ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 63]])
- Permeability damage reached 98% after 3 days of exposure for the Berea Buff sample. ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 64]])
- Micromax-WBM resulted in the highest porosity reduction (9.9%) compared to Ilmenite-WBM (4.2%). ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 79]])
- Ilmenite-WBM produced the maximum filtrate volume (8.3 cm3) and filter cake thickness (7.6 mm). ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 78]])
- Compressional wave velocity (Vp) for Micromax-WBM increased by 4.5% to 2.56 km/s. ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 86]])
- Shear wave velocity (Vs) showed a reduction of up to 6.6% for the Micromax-WBM sample. ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 86]])
- Young's modulus (Ed) decreased by 40% for Berea Buff and 39% for Bandera Brown after mud filtrate exposure. ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 97]])
- Poisson's ratio increased significantly, reaching a 155% increase for the Parker sample after filtration. ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 98]])

## Limitations

The study is limited by the specific pressure and temperature conditions (300 psi and 200°F for 10 days), the specific sandstone rock types used (Berea Buff, Berea Spider, Bandera Brown, and Parker), and the use of barite-weighted water-based mud.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Extend the proposed approach to carbonate rocks and provide technical solutions for field applications to preserve rock geomechanics and protect drilled formations from damage.
