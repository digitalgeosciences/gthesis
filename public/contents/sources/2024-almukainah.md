# The impact of CO2 storage on the rock integrity.

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the impact of CO2 storage on the integrity of carbonate rocks by analyzing changes in porosity, wormhole volume, and tortuosity after 60 days of exposure to live brine. The study concludes that while wormholes expand and increase complexity, these changes are minor enough that they do not significantly compromise the rock's overall structural framework or mechanical stability.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the lack of understanding regarding the long-term effects of CO2 sequestration on the petrophysical properties (porosity, pore structure) and structural integrity (wormhole volume, tortuosity) of carbonate rocks like limestone when exposed to carbonic acid formed from CO2 and brine.

## Objectives

- Conduct a comprehensive investigation into how CO2 sequestration impacts the porosity, pore structure, and preexisting wormholes in carbonate rocks over a 60 Day period.
- Compare measurements taken before and after CO2 exposure to evaluate changes in petrophysical properties.
- Measure the change in porosity using Helium Porosimeter and NMR before and after 60 days of aging in live brine.
- Assess the impact of aging on wormhole geometry and tortuosity using micro-CT scanning.
- Evaluate the relationship between specific surface area and changes in wormhole volume.
- Determine if increased wormhole volume and geometric tortuosity compromise the rock's structural integrity.

## Methods

The study utilized four Indiana limestone core samples (1.5" diameter, 3" length) aged for 60 days in live brine at 60°C and 2000 psi. Petrophysical analysis included: 1) NMR using an Oxford Geospec 0.05 T system with a CPMG pulse sequence to determine pore structure; 2) Helium Porosimetry using a He-Porosimeter to measure porosity via the gas-expansion method; 3) Micro-CT imaging using a Heliscan MicroCT (FEI ThermoScientific) for 3D visualization of internal structures. Wormhole analysis involved image registration and 'difference mapping' in PerGeos software, followed by watershed algorithm segmentation to quantify wormhole characteristics.

## Data and Materials

4 Indiana limestone core samples; 1 liter of synthetic formation brine (120,000 ppm); live brine prepared with CO2 at 700 psi; 60-day aging period.

## Key Findings

- A uniform increase in porosity was observed across all four limestone samples after 60 days of aging. ([[2024-almukainah|Almukainah, 2024, p. 14]])
- Wormhole formation showed a slight increase but remained minor enough to have minimal impact on overall rock integrity. ([[2024-almukainah|Almukainah, 2024, p. 14]])
- The geometric tortuosity of the wormholes increased, adding complexity to pathways without significantly compromising structural stability. ([[2024-almukainah|Almukainah, 2024, p. 14]])
- A water-wet core exhibited a larger increase in porosity (46.7%) compared to an oil-wet core (19%) during CO2 flooding. ([[2024-almukainah|Almukainah, 2024, p. 35]])
- Note: This finding is from a cited study (Al-Yaseri et al., 2022) used for context. ([[2024-almukainah|Almukainah, 2024]])
- All samples showed a modest increase in porosity after 60 days of aging (e.g., IL-1 increased from 15.1% to 17%, IL-2 from 16.2% to 17.7%). ([[2024-almukainah|Almukainah, 2024, p. 55]])
- NMR porosity measurements showed a consistent increase across all samples, with average relative increases ranging from 4.4% (IL-4) to 12.6% (IL-1). ([[2024-almukainah|Almukainah, 2024, p. 61]])
- Wormhole volumes increased by 4.1% (IL-4), 8.7% (IL-3), 8.1% (IL-2), and 10.2% (IL-1) after aging. ([[2024-almukainah|Almukainah, 2024, p. 67]])
- A positive correlation was found between the specific surface area of wormholes and changes in their volume (R² = 0.9036). ([[2024-almukainah|Almukainah, 2024, p. 68]])
- tortuosity increased by 5% to 9.5% across samples, but did not significantly compromise structural integrity. ([[2024-almukainah|Almukainah, 2024]])

## Recommendations and Future Work
