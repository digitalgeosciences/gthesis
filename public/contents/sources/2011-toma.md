# Resolving the Cycle Skip Introduced by the Multi-Layer Static Model using a Hybrid Approach

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the problem of artificial cycle skips in seismic data caused by multi-layer static models and proposes a hybrid approach. By combining high-frequency statics from single-layer models with low-frequency statics from multi-layer models, the study successfully resolved cycle skips in 3D seismic data from the Haba Field.

---

## Research Problem

Artificial cycle skips (data breaks) are often introduced into seismic data when using multi-layer static models due to issues like buried anomalies, velocity inversions, and complex refractors. These skips can lead to misleading interpretations of subsurface structures.

## Objectives

- Identify the causes of cycle skips in seismic data related to different static model applications.
- Develop a hybrid approach that combines high-frequency components from single-layer models with low-frequency components from multi-layer models.
- Validate the proposed hybrid method using 3D seismic data from Haba Field.
- Identify and characterize cycle skips introduced by multi-layer static models.
- Evaluate the effectiveness of conventional residual static techniques in resolving these specific issues.
- Develop a hybrid approach combining single-layer high-frequency statics and multi-layer low-frequency statics to create an external pilot for robust residual static correction.

## Methods

The study utilizes 3D seismic data. The pre-stack processing workflow includes transcription, noise attenuation (50/60 Hz), geometric spreading compensation, multi-layer datum static correction (high frequency), velocity analysis, and amplitude scaling. Post-stack processing involves surface-consistent residual statics, second pass of velocity analysis, stacking, automatic gain control (1000 ms gate), and multi-layer datum static correction (low frequency). The proposed hybrid method uses a single-layer high-frequency component to build an external pilot, which is then used in conjunction with the multi-layer model's low-frequency components to calculate residual statics.

## Data and Materials

One 3D seismic data set was selected for study. The analysis included pre-stack and post-stack data volumes, including cross-line (A) and in-line (B) sections, and time slices at 700 ms and 1400 ms.

## Key Findings

- Multi-layer models are used in over 90% of Saudi Arabian seismic projects to provide better static solutions than single-layer models. ([[2011-toma|Toma, 2011, p. 13]])
- The primary cause of cycle skips is that multi-layer models use horizontal ray paths (refracted energy), while single-layer models use near-vertical ray paths (direct arrivals). ([[2011-toma|Toma, 2011, p. 22]])
- Buried anomalies are often missed by upholes but can be incorrectly mapped as lateral shifts in multi-layer models, leading to cycle skips. ([[2011-toma|Toma, 2011, p. 25]])
- Velocity inversions cause the multi-layer model to create an 'invisible' layer that results in a significant time delay and subsequent cycle skip. ([[2011-toma|Toma, 2011, p. 32]])
- Refractor complexities, such as discontinuities, can lead to data breaks when using multi-layer models but are often bypassed by the vertical paths of single-layer models. ([[2011-toma|Toma, 2011, p. 37]])
- The hybrid approach successfully resolved a major cycle skip in the Haba Field data, resulting in better image of the subsurface structure. ([[2011-toma|Toma, 2011, p. 10]])
- The difference between multi-layer and single-layer low frequency statics in the study area ranged from -11 to +120 ms. ([[2011-toma|Toma, 2011, p. 41]])
- Conventional residual statics applied on top of a multi-layer model developed cycle skips rather than fixing the static break. ([[2011-toma|Toma, 2011, p. 47]])
- In-line data showed only a discontinuity, while cross-line data showed a clear cycle skip when using conventional methods. ([[2011-toma|Toma, 2011, p. 47]])
- The hybrid approach (single-layer high frequency + multi-layer low frequency) produced a skip-free data volume by utilizing an external pilot. ([[2011-toma|Toma, 2011, p. 55]])
- The proposed solution resulted in reasonable data continuity with no cycle skips in both cross-line and in-line directions. ([[2011-toma|Toma, 2011, p. 64]])
- Multi-layer models are more realistic than single-layer models but can introduce artifacts due to horizontal ray paths, especially in areas of velocity inversion or shallow buried anomalies. ([[2011-toma|Toma, 2011, p. 66]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

The single-layer static model should always be used in addition to the multi-layer model as a quality control step. In-line, cross-line, and time slices must be examined for both models to check for artificial discontinuities or cycle skips before final processing.
