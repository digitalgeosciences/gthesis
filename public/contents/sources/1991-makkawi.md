# Geostatistical modelling of Zarghat magnesite deposit Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the geostatistical modeling of the Zarghat magnesite deposit in the northern Arabian Shield. It evaluates the effectiveness of kriging and co-kriging techniques for estimating MgO content, concluding that co-kriging provides more accurate results due to the strong correlation between CaO and MgO.

---

## Research Problem

Accurately estimating the mineral resources and characterizing the spatial distribution of magnesium oxide (MgO) and calcium oxide (CaO) in the Zarghat magnesite deposit using geostatistical methods to account for non-linear distributions and spatial correlation.

## Objectives

- Review the geology of the Zarghat area.
- Describe available data sets and methods of data preparation.
- Perform statistical analysis to determine types of distributions and correlation coefficients between MgO% and CaO%.
- Compute and model semivariograms and cross-semivariogram functions for geostatistical parameters.
- Apply simple linear kriging to estimate block and point values.
- Apply co-kriging to estimate point values and compare results with standard kriging.

## Methods

The study utilized 1,239 samples from 51 vertical drill-holes. Data preparation involved coordinate rotation (53° counter-clockwise) to align with a grid and logarithmic transformation of CaO% and MgO% values to account for lognormal distribution. Semivariogram analysis was used to determine spatial continuity and anisotropy. Estimation techniques included block kriging and co-kriging (due to the strong correlation between CaO and MgO). Software packages such as GEOSTAT and SAS were utilized for data processing and modeling.

## Data and Materials

Data from 51 vertical drill-holes in the Central Hill deposit; 1,714 kriged blocks of 25m x 25m x 1m dimensions; calculation of tonnage for a 3.2 million tonne reserve estimate.

## Key Findings

- The sampling distributions of CaO% and MgO% values are characterized by lognormal behavior, necessitating the use of logarithmic transformations before semivariogram calculations. ([[1991-makkawi|Makkawi, 1991, p. 56]])
- Compound spherical models were found to fit the experimental semivariograms for both CaO% and MgO% logarithmic values. ([[1991-makkawi|Makkawi, 1991, p. 19]])
- The kriged estimates and corresponding relative kriging standard deviations of the logarithms of MgO% were determined for blocks of 25m x 25m x 1m dimensions. ([[1991-makkawi|Makkawi, 1991, p. 19]])
- The grade-tonnage relationship, based on kriged estimates, indicates the existence of 3.2 million tonnes of magnesite. ([[1991-makkawi|Makkawi, 1991, p. 19]])
- A strong linear correlation between CaO% and MgO% justifies the use of co-kriging to estimate known sample values. ([[1991-makkawi|Makkawi, 1991, p. 19]])
- Drill-holes located near the boundary of the deposit showed higher variations in CaO% and lower MgO% mean values, indicating influence from dolomite veins. ([[1991-makkawi|Makkawi, 1991, p. 47]])
- The Central Hill contains approximately 3 million tons of magnesite with a grade ranging from 38% to 48% MgO. ([[1991-makkawi|Makkawi, 1991, p. 20]])
- A strong inverse relationship exists between MgO% and CaO% (Pearson's coefficients: -0.89 for crude; -0.85 for calcined), indicating the enrichment of Mg at the expense of Ca during diagenesis. ([[1991-makkawi|Makkawi, 1991, p. 58]])
- A high positive correlation (+0.97) was observed between calcined and crude values of individual variables, confirming similarity between raw and processed samples. ([[1991-makkawi|Makkawi, 1991, p. 61]])
- The average vertical semivariograms for CaO (log %) and MgO (log %) showed a compound spherical model with short ranges of about 7m and long ranges of about 22m, indicating the presence of nested structures. ([[1991-makkawi|Makkawi, 1991, p. 83]])
- The horizontal semivariograms for both CaO (log %) and MgO (log %) showed a range of 25m and 100m respectively, with similar sill values in both directions. ([[1991-makkawi|Makkawi, 1991, p. 89]])
- Kriging standard deviations for the 1714 blocks were generally low, ranging from 0.003 to 0.008, indicating high reliability of the kriged estimates. ([[1991-makkawi|Makkawi, 1991, p. 104]])
- The total tonnage in the Central Hill was found to be approximately 3.2 million tonnes with a mean kriged block estimate of 1.665 (log %) and standard deviation of 0.011 (log %). ([[1991-makkawi|Makkawi, 1991, p. 106]])
- The grade-tonnage relationship confirmed total reserves of 3.2 x 10^8 tons above the cut-off grade of 1.633 (log %). ([[1991-makkawi|Makkawi, 1991, p. 108]])
- The sampling distributions of CaO% and MgO% values showed lognormal behavior, suggesting that more accurate evaluation can be achieved using logarithmic values. ([[1991-makkawi|Makkawi, 1991, p. 105]])

## Limitations

Limited number of samples in some areas and the fact that in-fill drill-holes were terminated at a depth of 6m limited the information derived from those specific semivariograms.
