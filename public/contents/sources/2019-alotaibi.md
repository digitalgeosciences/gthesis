# STUDYING LONG AND SHORT-TERM AIRBORNE PARTICULATE MATTER OVER THE ARABIAN PENINSULA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the distribution, morphology, and chemical composition of long and short-term airborne particulate matter (APM) over the Arabian Peninsula using a combination of ground-based sensors (AERONET), satellite data (Terra/MODIS), and laboratory analyses (SEM, XRD, XRF, FTIR, ICP-MS). It concludes that while natural factors drive increases in the East and Center, man-made activities are primary drivers for increased particulate matter in the West and South.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the impact of air pollution on the environment by analyzing the trends and distribution of particulate matter over the Arabian Peninsula, specifically focusing on how human activities influence aerosol characteristics like AOD and Angstrom exponents across different regions and seasons.

## Objectives

- Assess the trends and levels of PM10 using satellite data (AOD and AE).
- Determine the morphological and chemical characteristics of PM10 samples.
- Predict future trends of particulate matter over the Arabian Peninsula using ARIMA models.
- Determine physical, optical and seasonal Particulate Matter characteristics over several geographical locations in the Arabian Peninsula based on long-term space-borne and ground-based observations.
- Assess the morphological and mineralogical characteristics of the Particulate Matter over two different cities within the Arabian Peninsula.
- Predict the future trend of the Particulate Matter using historical data gathered from space-borne and ground-based stations.
- To understand the trends, physical, optical, and seasonal characteristics of airborne particulate matter (PM10) using satellite data over the South, North, East, West, and Center of the Arabian Peninsula.
- To assess the morphological and chemical characteristics of the airborne particulate matter (PM10) through sample collection in industrial areas (Jubail and Ras Tanura).
- Characterize the morphology and mineralogy of PM10 samples from Jubail and Ras Tanura.
- Determine trace metal levels in particulate matter samples.
- Analyze long-term trends of aerosol optical depth (AOD) and Angstrom Exponents across the Arabian Peninsula using ground-based stations and satellite data.
- Predict future trends of particulate matter using time-series models like ARIMA and Neural Network Autoregression.
- Determine physical, optical and seasonal Airborne Particulate Matter characteristics over several geographical locations in the Arabian Peninsula based on long-term space-borne and ground-based observations.
- Investigate the distribution of long and short-term airborne particulate matter over the Arabian Peninsula.
- Analyze seasonal variations in PM10 concentration and size distribution.
- Evaluate the impact of human activity on air quality using AOD and Angstrom exponent data.
- Analyze seasonal variations of AOD (tau 500) and Angstrom exponents at various wavelengths.
- Compare particulate matter trends between northern and southern regions of the Arabian Peninsula.
- Identify the influence of anthropogenic emissions on atmospheric aerosol concentrations.
- Assess morphological and mineralogical characteristics of the Airborne Particulate Matter over two different cities within the Arabian Peninsula (Jubail and Ras Tanura).
- Predict the future trend of Airborne Particulate Matter using historical data gathered from Space-Borne and Ground-Based Stations.
- Analyze the trend of particulate matter concentration using AERONET and satellite data.
- Determine the mineralogical composition of particulate matter in industrial zones.
- Assess the chemical elements present in particulate matter to determine their source and impact.

## Methods

Ground-based monitoring was conducted using a Synchronized Hybrid Ambient Real-time Particulate Monitor (Thermo Scientific 5030i) to record hourly PM10 levels. Morphological characterization of samples from Jubail and Ras Tanura was performed using Field-Emission Scanning Electron Microscopy (FESEM). Elemental composition was determined via X-ray Fluorescence (XRF), and mineralogical analysis was conducted using X-ray Diffraction (XRD). Trace metal concentrations were analyzed using Inductively Coupled Plasma Mass Spectrometry (ICP-MS). Satellite data from the MODIS sensor were integrated to correlate ground observations with regional trends. Time-series forecasting of PM10 levels across different regions of the Arabian Peninsula (Central, North, and South) was performed using ARIMA modeling in MATLAB.

## Data and Materials

Data includes 15.288 years of operation from the Solar Village AERONET station, 5.079 years from the KAUST AERONET station, and satellite data (MODIS, CALIPSO) covering periods between 2000-2015. Physical samples were collected over a two-month period (October–December 2015) in Jubail and Ras Tanura.

## Key Findings

- AOD and AE values were used to determine the types and levels of PM10 across various regions, including Central, Eastern, Western, Northern, and Southern regions. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 53]])
- Morphological characteristics of PM10 samples from Jubail and Ras Tanura were analyzed to identify shape and size distributions. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 130]])
- XRF analysis was used to determine the elemental composition of PM10 samples collected from Jubail and Ras Tanura. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 133]])
- XRD analysis identified mineralogical characteristics and categorized minerals into three distinct clusters for samples in Jubail and Ras Tanura. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 134]])
- Trace metal concentrations (e.g., heavy metals) were quantified in PM10 samples from Jubail and Ras Tanura to assess pollution levels. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 144]])
- ARIMA models were developed to predict future trends of particulate matter across the Central, North, and South regions of the Arabian Peninsula. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 149]])
- Analysis of particulate matter (PM) concentration showed an increase in the East and Center of the Arabian Peninsula attributed to natural particles, while the West and South were dominated by man-made particles. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 21]])
- The percentage of growth for PM concentrations over five regions was: Central (58%), Western (15%), Southern (13%), Northern (8%), and Eastern (6%). ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 21]])
- Trends for man-made particulate matter decreased by 29% in the North, 28% in the Center, and 21% in the East, while increasing significantly in the West (72%) and South (18%). ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 21]])
- SEM analysis showed average particle sizes of 1.4-2.8 µm in Jubail city, which are smaller than those in Ras Tanura (5-7 µm). ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 22]])
- XRF results indicated higher concentrations of zinc, barium, sulfur, sodium, and potassium in Jubail compared to Ras Tanura. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 22]])
- Ras Tanura samples showed higher concentrations of calcium, chlorine, aluminum, iron, and magnesium than Jubail samples. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 22]])
- XRD analysis identified the composition of APM as silica, koktaite, mascagnite, and calcium carbonate. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 22]])
- ICP-MS analysis confirmed that trace metals (lithium, cobalt, vanadium, arsenic, silver, manganese, nickel, molybdenum, copper, chromium, cadmium, and lead) were within regulatory limits. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 22]])
- ARIMA models predicted a rise in PM concentration in the Central region followed by a decrease until late 2030. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 22]])

## Limitations

The study notes that there was not enough data to investigate the fall season over the specific regional area (KAUST_Campus) during the initial analysis. Additionally, some results for the northern region were not statistically significant at the 95% confidence level.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Study size distribution over the south and north of the Arabian Peninsula.
- Establish cooperation at the GCC level to reduce emissions of man-made particles.
- Conduct more research in West and South areas to understand reasons behind increased man-made particles.
- Perform chemical characterization of long-term particulate matter across more areas of the Arabian Peninsula.
