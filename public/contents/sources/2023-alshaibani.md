# THE USE OF INTEGRATED GEOPHYSICAL METHODS TO CHARACTERIZE THE SHALLOW INHOMOGENEITY ON DAMMAM DOME, SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates a 30-meter wide deformation zone at an outcrop on the Dammam Dome using integrated geophysical methods (2D/3D seismic and ERT) and mineralogical analysis. It aims to characterize the subsurface extent of this inhomogeneity and understand its geological origins in relation to the Dammam Formation.

---

## Research Problem

A large deformation zone was recently exposed near KFUPM, and its underlying cause and extent need to be characterized using geophysical methods to understand the nature of the local geomorphology and structural geology.

## Objectives

- To explore and identify the geological processes caused the anomaly observed at the study area.
- To use multi geophysical methods and generate one geological interpretation explain the nature of the anomaly.
- Explore and identify the geological reasons for the deformation zone observed in the study area.
- Use multi-geophysical methods (seismic and ERT) to generate a comprehensive geological interpretation.
- Characterize the mineralogical and chemical composition of the formation using XRD and XRF.
- Explore and identify the geological reasons for the deformation zone observed in the study area
- Use multi-geophysical methods to generate a comprehensive geological interpretation that explains the nature of the deformation zone
- Investigate the relationship between the near-surface structure and the Dammam Dome

## Methods

The study employs a multi-method approach: 1) Traveltime Tomography including SVI (Signal Variation Improvement) for data enhancement and smoothing filters; 2) Electrical Resistivity Tomography (ERT) using a Syscal Pro multichannel system with a 96-channel dipole-dipole array; 3) 2D and 3D seismic surveys (2D: 120 receivers/120 shots; 3D: 108 receivers/385 shots); 4) Mineralogical analysis using XRD and XRF on six rock samples collected from the deformation zone.

## Data and Materials

6 rock samples for mineralogy, 120 receivers and 120 shots for 2D seismic survey, 108 receivers and 385 shots for 3D seismic survey, and a 96-channel ERT system. The study area is located at coordinates 26°21'32.3"N 50°06'09.9"E.

## Key Findings

- XRD analysis of sample 1 (left side of deformation zone) showed 73.4% dolomite, 22.4% quartz, and 4.2% calcite. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 10]])
- XRD analysis of sample 2 (right side of deformation zone) showed 96.8% calcite and 3.2% dolomite. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 10]])
- Sample 3 (host rock within the deformation zone) was characterized by roughly equal parts quartz (25%), dolomite (36.1%), and calcite (33.6%). ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 10]])
- XRD analysis of sample 4 (small fracture within the deformation zone) showed 74.7% evaporites, 11.3% quartz, 10.2% dolomite, and 2.6% feldspar. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 10]])
- XRD analysis of sample 5 (bottom of the deformation zone) was rich in dolomite (93.9%) with 6.1% quartz. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 10]])
- XRF results confirmed that magnesium and calcium are the most common elements in most samples along the deformation zone. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 11]])
- The 2D seismic tomogram showed a low velocity area (650-800 m/s) below the surface, corresponding to the deformation zone. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 11]])
- The ERT survey identified a high resistance anomaly of 76.5 ohms.m in the first 20 meters of the subsurface. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 11]])
- High velocity values (1210 to 1665 m/s) were found in the first 20 meters, coinciding with the deformation zone location. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 11]])
- The filling material of the fracture is from the same sedimentary unit as the host rock, indicating a closed system. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 12]])
- Sample 1 (left side of deformation zone) contains 73.4% dolomite, 22.4% quartz, and 4.2% calcite. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 36]])
- Sample 2 (right side of deformation zone) shows a very high percentage of calcite (96.8%) and only 3.2% dolomite. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 36]])
- Sample 3 contains approximately equal parts quartz (25%), dolomite (36.1%), and calcite (33.6%). ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 37]])
- Sample 4 is dominated by evaporites (74.7%) with minor amounts of quartz (11.3%) and dolomite (10.2%). ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 37]])
- Sample 5 is rich in dolomite (93.9%) with only 6.1% quartz. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 38]])

## Limitations

ERT is limited by the assumption of a homogeneous subsurface and sensitivity to electrode placement/noise; seismic data can be affected by low SNR at large source-receiver offsets.
