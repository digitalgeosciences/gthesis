# THE USE OF INTEGRATED GEOPHYSICAL METHODS TO CHARACTERIZE THE SHALLOW INHOMOGENEITY ON DAMMAM DOME, SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates a 30-meter wide deformation zone on the Dammam Dome using integrated geophysical methods (2D/3D seismic and ERT) and mineralogical analysis. The study concludes that the deformation zone is a shear zone with a consistent internal composition, confirmed by high-velocity seismic zones and high-resistivity anomalies.

---

## Research Problem

The presence of a large, 30-meter wide deformation zone at an outcrop on the Dammam Dome and the need to characterize its subsurface extent and geological nature using integrated geophysical methods.

## Objectives

- To explore and identify the geological processes caused the anomaly observed at the study area.
- To use multi geophysical methods and generate one geological interpretation explain the nature of the anomaly.

## Methods

The study employed a combination of mineralogical analysis (XRD, XRF) on six rock samples, 2D seismic reflection survey (120 receivers/shots), 3D seismic survey (108 receivers, 385 shots), and Electrical Resistivity Tomography (ERT) with a 96-electrode dipole-dipole array. Data processing included first arrival picking using 'DataPick' software, reciprocity testing for quality control, Super-Virtual Interferometry (SVI) to improve signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) in noisy environments, and traveltime tomography to generate velocity models.

## Data and Materials

6 rock samples from the deformation zone; 2D seismic survey with 120 receivers/shots over a 360m profile; 3D seismic survey with 108 receivers and 385 shots across 6 lines; ERT survey using 96 electrodes over a 480m profile.

## Key Findings

- The deformation zone is approximately 30 meters wide at the surface outcrop. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 12]])
- XRD analysis of samples 1, 2, and 4 showed filling materials composed of gypsum, quartz, dolomite, and clay minerals. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 38]])
- Sample 5 (deformation zone) was found to be dominated by dolomite (93.9%), while sample 6 (outside) was characterized by the prevalence of clay minerals. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 37]])
- XRF results confirmed XRD findings, showing magnesium and calcium as common elements in most samples. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 40]])
- The 2D seismic tomogram identified a high-velocity zone (1210 to 1665 m/s) within the first 20 meters, coinciding with the deformation zone location. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 53]])
- A high-resistivity anomaly of approximately 76.5 ohm.m was identified in the ERT tomogram at the deformation site. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 54]])
- The 3D seismic model showed a velocity range of 650 to 800 m/s within the highlighted deformation zone area. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 59]])
- Mineralogical analysis confirmed that filling materials in the fault cement are from the same sedimentary unit, indicating a closed system. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 56]])

## Limitations

The proximity of the Dammam-Jubail highway caused significant random and coherent noise in the seismic data.
