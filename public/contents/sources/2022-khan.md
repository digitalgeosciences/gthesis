# DIAGENETIC, PETROPHYSICAL, GEOCHEMICAL AND MICRO-PALEONTOLOGICAL ANALYSIS OF PLEISTOCENE-HOLOCENE CORES ALONG THE KING FAHD CAUSEWAY BETWEEN SAUDI ARABIA AND BAHRAIN

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the diagenetic, petrophysical, and micropaleontological characteristics of Pleistocene-Holocene core samples collected along the King Fahd Causeway. It identifies two distinct lithofacies—a lower dolomitic interval and an upper non-dolomitic interval—and correlates their depositional environments with sea-level fluctuations in the Arabian Gulf.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the characterization of Pleistocene-Holocene sedimentary sequences in a semi-restricted marine area (Gulf of Salwa) to understand how sea-level changes and diagenetic processes influenced lithology, porosity, and permeability.

## Methods

The study involved core descriptions based on Dunham (1962) for texture and Flügel (2014) for grain size. Petrographic analysis was performed on 62 thin sections to identify minerals, textures, and diagenetic features. XRD was used to determine bulk mineralogy of powdered samples using an EMPYREAN PAN system. XRF (M4-Tornado micro XRF) was used for elemental chemical composition. Petrophysical measurements (porosity/permeability) were conducted on 16 core plugs using an Ap-608 automated permeameter. SEM-EDS (JEOL JSM-B6610LV) provided high-resolution mineral morphology and semi-quantitative elemental analysis. QEMSCAN (FEI 650F) was used for detailed mineralogical composition of thin sections.

## Data and Materials

Analysis of three core sections (BR1, BR2, BR3) from the King Fahd Causeway area. Data includes 8 distinct lithofacies types, petrophysical measurements (porosity/permeability), and mineralogical data from XRD and XRF for multiple samples.

## Key Findings

- Two distinct lithofacies were identified: a dolomitic lithofacies (subdivided into oolitic grainstone and other types) and a non-dolomitic lithofacies. ([[2022-khan|Khan, 2022, p. 2]])
- Petrophysical analysis revealed porosity and permeability values for the core sections, with specific data points plotted in Figure 63. ([[2022-khan|Khan, 2022, p. 103]])
- XRD analysis identified mineral percentages including dolomite, quartz, halite, and gypsum across different lithofacies. ([[2022-khan|Khan, 2022, p. 88]])
- XRF analysis provided elemental weight percentages (e.g., for samples BR1 B14, BR2 B24, and BR3 B44.3). ([[2022-khan|Khan, 2022, p. 95]])
- The study identified several lithofacies including Loose Sand, Oolitic Sand, Dolomitic Breccia, Bioclastic Dolomitized Limestone, Quartz Sandstone, Chalk, and Argillaceous Limestone. ([[2022-khan|Khan, 2022, p. 51]])
- Stable isotope analysis was used to characterize the geochemical signatures of the carbonate minerals. ([[2022-khan|Khan, 2022, p. 49]])
- Two distinct lithofacies were identified: a dolomitic lithofacies and a non-dolomitic lithofacies. ([[2022-khan|Khan, 2022, p. 19]])
- The lower dolomite lithofacies can be subdivided into oolitic grainstone, skeletal packstone and wackestone-packestone, pelletal skeletal packstone-grainstone, and wackestone-mudstone. ([[2022-khan|Khan, 2022, p. 19]])
- The upper non-dolomitic unit is comprised of interbedded sandstone with quartz, calcitic skeletal grainstone, quartz sand, aragonitic skeletal grainstone, and aragonitic oolitic sand and grainstone. ([[2022-khan|Khan, 2022, p. 19]])
- Thirteen lithofacies were identified based on lithology, color, texture, grain size, visible porosity, and sedimentary structure. ([[2022-khan|Khan, 2022, p. 19]])
- The dolomitic interval shows several shallowing upward depositional cycles consisting of skeletal packstone, wackestone-mudstone, pelletal packstone, and green mudstone. ([[2022-khan|Khan, 2022, p. 19]])
- Dolomitic lithofacies were most likely deposited at a somewhat lower sea level than present (mid-Pleistocene). ([[2022-khan|Khan, 2022, p. 20]])
- Aragonitic components dissolved and calcite cement precipitated during the late Pleistocene pluvial periods. ([[2022-khan|Khan, 2022, p. 20]])
- The non-dolomitic units lithostratigraphy and diagenesis match to the sea level changes profile. ([[2022-khan|Khan, 2022, p. 22]])
- Two distinct lithofacies were identified: a lower dolomitic unit (approx. 40 m thick) and an upper non-dolomitic unit (3-9 m thick). ([[2022-khan|Khan, 2022, p. 48]])

## Limitations

Many samples were very soft and fine, making it difficult to extract core plugs for petrophysical measurements; additionally, some samples developed cracks during porosity measurement.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Conduct oxygen isotope studies to relate to sea level changes.
- Perform oxygen and carbon stable isotope analysis to clarify the nature of dolomitization.
- Perform detailed micropaleontological analysis on other boreholes to confirm Holocene foraminiferal assemblages.
