# SEISMIC ATTRIBUTES AND PETROPHYSICAL MODELLING OF THE ARADEIBA-D MEMBER, MUGLAD RIFT BASIN, SUDAN

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the Aradeiba-D member in the Muglad Rift Basin, Sudan, to improve reservoir characterization between sparse well data. It utilizes a multidisciplinary approach combining petrophysical analysis (ANN for facies) and seismic attribute modeling via multivariate regression and convolutional operators to map porosity, permeability, and water saturation.

---

## Research Problem

The Aradeiba Formation in the Muglad basin provides significant seal and reservoir potential (specifically the Aradeiba-D member), but its heterogeneity and geometry are complex due to varying depositional environments. Traditional single-attribute correlations often fail to capture these complexities effectively.

## Objectives

- Derive lithological and petrophysical reservoir models of the Aradeiba-D member to enhance characterization between sparse well data.
- Improve the integration process to predict and map less understood reservoir rock properties using geostatistical and seismic attributes analysis.
- Apply multivariate regression analysis to integrate multi-attribute transforms with petrophysical data.
- Estimation of petrophysical parameters (shale content, porosity, permeability, fluid saturation).
- Characterization of facies types and assign to each facies its petrophysical properties (i.e. make a geological model).
- Combining well logs (calculated petrophysical parameters) and seismic data for predicting well log properties through the seismic cube by employing multi attribute transforms to predict the rock properties between wells.
- Evaluate petrophysical parameters (Vsh, porosity, permeability, and water saturation) for the Aradeiba-D Member using well log data.
- Apply neural networks for facies classification within the Aradeiba-D Member.
- Perform multi-attribute seismic analysis to model the reservoir properties.
- Classify lithological facies using Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) based on well log data.
- Perform univariate statistical analysis on porosity, permeability, and water saturation for the Aradeiba-D zone.
- Utilize multi-attribute seismic analysis to generate pseudo-log cubes of facies, porosity, permeability, and water saturation.
- Map the distribution of reservoir properties across the Aradeiba-D Member using 3D seismic data.
- Evaluate petrophysical parameters (Shale Volume, porosity, permeability, and water saturation) for the Arediba-D Member using well log data.
- Integrate 3D seismic attributes with well logs to predict reservoir properties across the field.
- Develop models for facies classification, porosity, permeability, and water saturation using multivariate regression and neural networks.
- Evaluate the petrophysical properties of the Aradeiba-D member using well log data.
- Analyze seismic attributes to characterize the lithology and porosity of the formation.
- Develop a 3D petrophysical model to improve understanding of reservoir geometry and distribution.

## Methods

The study involved a two-stage approach: 1) Petrophysical and Facies Analysis: Shale volume was determined from various indicators; porosity was calculated using sonic, density, and neutron combinations; permeability was estimated from core-derived relationships; and water saturation was calculated using the 'Indonesia' equation. Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) were used for facies classification based on GR, DT, NPHI, RHOZ, and RLA5 logs. 2) Multi-attribute Transforms: Seismic attributes were selected as sample-based transforms. Multivariate linear regression and a five-point convolutional operator were applied to the seismic data. Cross-validation was performed to optimize the number of attributes and the length of the convolution. The final models (facies, porosity, permeability, and water saturation) were then mapped across 3D seismic volumes using time slices and cross-sections.

## Data and Materials

The study utilized 3D post-stack time-migrated seismic data covering an area of approximately 40.24 km² with a bin size of 12.5m x 25.0m. Well log data (including GR, DT, NPHI, RHOZ, and RLA5) were obtained from 18 wells within the Aradeiba-D Member.

## Key Findings

- The Aradeiba-D member is an oil-bearing sandstone reservoir with sand bodies of small size, where the thickest part reaches nearly 20 meters and the average thickness is approximately 7 meters. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 14]])
- Sand bodies are distributed as belts from Northwest to Southeast. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 14]])
- The Aradeiba-D reservoir contains sand bodies that, based on multi-attribute analysis, suggest a composite of single sand bodies. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 14]])
- Maximum usable frequency for the seismic data was determined to be between 60 and 70 Hz. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 10]])
- The Aradeiba-D member provides a seal to stratigraphic and structural traps of mudstone in the Upper Cretaceous. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 13]])
- The Aradeiba-D Member is characterized by small sandstone bodies with a total thickness of approximately 20 meters (maximum) and an average thickness of about 7 meters, distributed in the form of bands trending NW to SE. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 16]])
- The Aradeiba Formation acts as both a major reservoir horizon and a seal for the underlying units. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 37]])
- The study area includes a sedimentary sequence of approximately 110,000 km² in the Muglad rift basin. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 17]])
- Shale volume (Vsh) in the Aradeiba-D sand zone varies significantly, reaching a maximum of 43.5% at well X-13 and a minimum of 13.3% at well X-18. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 51]])
- Porosity in the Aradeiba-D sand zone ranges from a minimum of 24% (wells X-17, X-11) to a maximum of 38.1% at well X-5. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 56]])
- Permeability in the Aradeiba-D sand zone ranges from a minimum of 0.216 Darcy at well X-7 to a maximum of 11 Darcy at well X-16. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 58]])
- Water saturation (Sw) in the Aradeiba-D sand zone ranges from a minimum of 5% at well X-14 to a maximum of 51% at well X-3. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 61]])
- The study identified three distinct rifting phases in the Muglad basin: first (Jurassic-Early Cretaceous), second (Turonian-late Senonian), and third (Late Eocene-Oligocene). ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 42]])
- Three main lithological facies were identified: (i) thick massive grey to dark grey and purplish red shale; (ii) fine- to medium grained sandstone of mainly feldspathic and subfeldspathic quartz with minor argillaceous matrix; and (iii) a second type of sandstone with similar mineralogy. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 63]])
- The ANN model for facies classification showed a very good match when compared to well cutting descriptions from master logs. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 64]])

## Limitations

The study notes that standard cross-plot regression with single attributes is limited because it ignores differences in frequency content between logs and seismic data; this was addressed by using convolutional operators to include neighboring samples.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Extend the study using multi-attribute neural networks to improve correlation; conduct a thorough study on how specific clay minerals affect reservoir quality to refine petrophysical calculations.
