# SEISMIC ATTRIBUTES AND PETROPHYSICAL MODELLING OF THE ARADEIBA-D MEMBER, MUGLAD RIFT BASIN, SUDAN

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the Aradeiba-D member in the Muglad Rift Basin, Sudan, to improve reservoir characterization between sparse well data. It utilizes a multidisciplinary approach combining neural networks for facies classification and multivariate regression of seismic attributes to model porosity, permeability, and water saturation.

---

## Research Problem

The Aradeiba-D Member is a major oil-bearing sandstone reservoir in the Muglad Rift Basin, but it exhibits significant heterogeneity and complex geometry due to varying depositional environments. Sparse well data make it difficult to accurately map and predict petrophysical properties across the field.

## Objectives

- Estimation of petrophysical parameters (shale content, porosity, permeability, fluid saturation).
- Characterization of facies types and assignment of petrophysical properties to each facies to create a geological model.
- Combining well logs and seismic data for predicting well log properties through the seismic cube by employing multi-attribute transforms.
- Evaluate petrophysical parameters (shale volume, porosity, permeability, water saturation) for the Arediba-D Member.
- Apply Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) for lithofacies classification based on well logs.
- Utilize multi-attribute seismic analysis to predict and map reservoir properties in 3D space.

## Methods

The study follows a two-part workflow: 1) Petrophysical and Facies Analysis: Shale volume (Vsh) was calculated using both Gamma Ray (Larionov, 1969) and Density-Neutron methods. Porosity was determined from sonic, neutron, and density logs, while water saturation was calculated using the 'Indonesia' equation. Neural networks were applied to classify facies based on GR, DT, NPHI, RHOZ, and RLA5 logs. 2) Multi-attribute Transforms: Well logs were tied to seismic data (converted to time domain). Multivariate linear regression was used to establish statistical relationships between seismic attributes and reservoir properties. These models were validated using cross-validation analysis before being applied to the 3D seismic volume.

## Data and Materials

The study utilized a 3D Post Stack Time Migration (PSTM) seismic cube covering approximately 40.24 km² in the Fula depression and data from 18 wells including logs for RLA5, RLA3, RXOZ, DT, RHOZ, NPHI, GR, SP, and HCAL.

## Key Findings

- The Aradeiba-D member is characterized by small sand bodies with a maximum thickness of nearly 20 meters and an average thickness of approximately 7 meters, distributed as belts from Northwest to Southeast. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 14]])
- Shale content in the Aradeiba-D zone varies significantly, ranging from 13% at well X-18 to a maximum of 43.5% at well X-13. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 51]])
- Porosity values in the Aradeiba-D sand range from a minimum of 24% (at wells X-17 and X-11) to a maximum of 38.1% (at well X-5). ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 56]])
- Permeability values for the Aradeiba-D sand vary from as low as 0.217 Darcy (well X-7) to a maximum of 10.871 Darcy (well X-8). ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 52]])
- Water saturation values in the Aradeiba-D sand range from 4.9% (well X-14) to 51.4% (well X-3). ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 52]])
- The Aradeiba-D sand shows permeability values ranging from 0.216 Darcy at well X-7 to a maximum of 11 Darcy at well X-16. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 58]])
- The ANN successfully classified lithofacies into three groups (shale, shaly sand, and sandstone) with a high correlation between results and physical well cuttings. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 64]])
- Porosity values in the Aradeiba-D Member range from 23% to 38% for interpreted logs (Table 5.1). ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 107]])
- Permeability values were found to be between 200 mD and 12,000 mD in the Aradeiba-D zone. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013]])

## Limitations

The study notes that high-frequency components and the discrete nature of facies logs can lead to lower correlation coefficients in some models; additionally, very thin beds may not be resolved by standard seismic data.
