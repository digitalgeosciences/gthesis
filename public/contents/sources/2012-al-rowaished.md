# Quantitative Assessments of Land Reclamation along the East Coast of Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis quantifies land reclamation activities along the eastern coast of Saudi Arabia, specifically within Tarut Bay, from 1973 to 2007 using remote sensing and GIS. It concludes that significant reclamation occurred before 1995, primarily for residential use, resulting in a substantial loss of mangrove habitats.

---

## Research Problem

The rapid expansion of infrastructure and population in the Eastern Province since the 1930s has led to extensive land reclamation from the Arabian Gulf. These activities pose significant threats to coastal ecosystems, particularly the rich mangrove habitats in Tarut Bay, which require quantification and assessment of their impact.

## Objectives

- To quantify the reclaimed land within Tarut Bay from Arabian Gulf toward the shore line of Eastern coast of Saudi Arabia from 1973 until 2007.
- To identify and classify the land uses of the reclaimed land, and effected marine habitats in the studied area.
- Identify land reclamation activities in the eastern coast of Saudi Arabia
- Quantify reclaimed lands within Tarut Bay from 1973 to 2007
- Determine the environmental impact on marine habitat (mangroves) due to these activities
- Quantify reclaimed lands within Tarut Bay from 1970 until 2007

## Methods

The study utilized remote sensing technologies to obtain satellite imagery (Landsat MSS, Landsat TM, and SPOT) from different time periods (1973, 1995, and 2007). The 1973 Landsat MSS images were geo-corrected using the ER Mapper software by aligning them with 1995 TM imagery. All processed images were then integrated into ArcView GIS to create coastal line mosaic images for analysis. These tools were used to quantify reclaimed areas and identify land use types.

## Data and Materials

The study utilized Landsat MSS data from 1973 (provided by KFUPM), Landsat TM and SPOT imagery from 1995 and 2007 (obtained from KACST). The analysis focused specifically on the Tarut Bay area within the Eastern Province of Saudi Arabia.

## Key Findings

- The total land reclaimed within Tarut Bay area since 1973 up to November 2007 was found to be 84.42 square kilometers (Km²). ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 10]])
- Most of the reclaimed land in Tarut Bay was utilized for residential purposes. ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 10]])
- The majority of land reclamation activities were carried out before November 1995, with 83.68 km² reclaimed between 1973 and 1995. ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 10]])
- Only a small amount of land (0.74 km²) was reclaimed after November 1995 up to 2007. ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 10]])
- The superimposition of environmental biotype maps with reclamation areas showed a clear and destructive impact on mangrove habitats in Tarut Bay. ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 10]])
- Mangrove habitat in the study area decreased by approximately 35% between 1990 and 1999 (based on literature review of similar studies). ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 24]])
- The total land reclaimed within Tarur Bay from 1973 to 2007 was found to be 84.42 square kilometers (km²). ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 54]])
- Most of the reclaimed land (83.68 km²) occurred between 1973 and 1995. ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 54]])
- Only a small amount of land, approximately 0.74 km², was reclaimed after November 1995 up to 2007. ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 54]])
- The majority of the reclaimed land was utilized for residential purposes. ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 54]])
- Reclamation activities resulted in a clear and destructive impact on mangrove habitats in Tarut Bay. ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 54]])
- Approximately 90% of the original mangrove ecosystems along the Gulf coast have been lost due to coastal urban development. ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 55]])
- The total land that has been reclaimed within Tarut Bay area since 1973 up to November 2007 were found to be 84.42 square kilometers (Km²). ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 10]])
- Most of land reclamation activities were carried before November, 1995. ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 10]])

## Limitations

The study was limited to the Tarut Bay area because of significant reclamation activities in that specific zone and the availability of a local environmental biotype map.
