# Quantitative Assessments of Land Reclamation along the East Coast of Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis quantifies land reclamation activities in Tarut Bay, Eastern Province, Saudi Arabia, from 1973 to 2007 using remote sensing and GIS. It concludes that the majority of reclaimed land (83.68 km²) was added before 1995 and resulted in significant destruction of local mangrove habitats.

---

## Research Problem

The rapid expansion of urban infrastructure in the Eastern Province of Saudi Arabia since the 1930s has led to extensive land reclamation from the Arabian Gulf, which poses a threat to marine ecosystems like mangroves. There is a lack of specific quantification and assessment of these activities in Tarut Bay.

## Objectives

- To quantify the reclaimed land within Tarut Bay from Arabian Gulf toward the shore line of Eastern coast of Saudi Arabia from 1973 until 2007.
- To identify and classify the land uses of the reclaimed land, and effected marine habitats in the studied area.

## Methods

The study utilized remote sensing data (Landsat MSS, Landsat TM, and SPOT satellite imagery) from 1973, 1995, and 2007. Images were processed using ER Mapper for georectification and enhancement of the 1973 datasets. All images were then integrated into ArcView GIS to create coastal line mosaics. The area of interest was defined based on a biotype map of Tarut Bay. Reclaimed areas were quantified by calculating the polygon areas of different time periods (1973, 1995, and 2007) and determining the difference between them.

## Data and Materials

The study used 29 main imagery datasets including Landsat MSS (6 scenes), Landsat TM (5 scenes), and SPOT (18 scenes). Additionally, an environmental biotype map of marine habitat for Tarut Bay provided by the Center for Environment and Water at KFUPM was used to identify affected habitats.

## Key Findings

- The total land reclaimed within Tarut Bay area since 1973 up to November 2007 was found to be 84.42 square kilometers (km²). ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 10]])
- Most of the reclaimed land was utilized for residential purposes. ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 10]])
- The majority of land reclamation activities occurred before November 1995, with 83.68 km² reclaimed between 1973 and 1995. ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 10]])
- Reclamation activities after November 1995 were minimal, totaling only 0.74 km² up to 2007. ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 10]])
- Superimposition of the environmental biotype map with reclaimed areas showed a clear and destructive impact on mangrove habitats in Tarut Bay. ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 10]])
- The area of the polygon for 1973 was 917.29 km² and for 2007 was 1001.71 km², resulting in a difference of 84.42 km². ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 49]])
- Approximately 90% of the original mangrove ecosystems along the Gulf coast have been lost due to coastal urban development. ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 55]])
