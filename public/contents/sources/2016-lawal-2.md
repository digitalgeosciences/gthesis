# LEVELS AND CHARACTERISTICS OF AIRBORNE PARTICULATE MATTER IN DAMMAM, DHAHRAN AND KHOBAR, SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the levels, morphology, and elemental composition of airborne particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10) in three major cities in Saudi Arabia's Eastern Province: Dammam, Dhahran, and Khobar. It utilizes SEM-EDX and other analytical techniques to characterize these particles and identify their potential sources such as industrial, traffic, or natural origins.

---

## Research Problem

There is limited information regarding the assessment of particulate matter (PM) with respect to its geochemical and morphological characteristics in Saudi Arabia, particularly in the Eastern Province which is a major hub for petroleum and petrochemical industries.

## Objectives

- To determine the concentration levels of airborne particulate matters (PM) in the selected sites.
- To assess the morphological and mineralogical characteristics of the airborne particulates in the selected sites.
- To assess the spatial distribution of the particulate matter in the selected sites.
- To assess the levels of airborne particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10) in Dammam, Dhahran, and Khobar.
- To characterize the morphology and elemental composition of these particles.
- To identify the potential sources of the particulate matter.
- Determine the concentration levels of PM10 in Dhahran, Khobar, and Dammam.
- Characterize the morphology of airborne particulate matter samples.
- Analyze the elemental composition of the sampled particulate matter.
- Identify the mineralogical composition of the airborne particulate matter.
- Determine the concentration levels of PM10 in Dammam, Dhahran, and Khobar.
- Analyze the morphological characteristics of collected PM10 using SEM.
- Identify the elemental composition of PM samples using EDX and XRF.
- Determine the mineralogical composition of the particulate matter using XRD.
- Determine the concentration of PM10 in Dhahran, Khobar, and Dammam.
- Analyze the mineralogical composition of the collected airborne particulate matter using XRD.
- Identify the morphology and size of particles through SEM imaging.
- Quantify trace metal concentrations (e.g., As, Ba, Cd, Co, Cr, Cu, Mn, Mo, Ni, V, Zn, Ti, Se, Pb) in the samples.

## Methods

PM10 samples were collected using an Ecotech HiVol-3000 sampler with a PM10 size selective inlet over 7 days at three locations in each city. Gravimetric analysis was performed by weighing membrane filters before and after sampling to determine concentration in µg/m3. Morphological characterization was conducted using Lyra3 FESEM coupled with EDX, involving gold sputtering for sample preparation. Elemental composition was determined via XRF (using a JSX-3400RII instrument) and ICP-OES (using an Optima 8000). Mineralogical analysis was performed using Rigaku MiniFlex600 XRD.

## Data and Materials

Samples were collected from 3 specific locations in each of the three cities: Dammam, Dhahran, and Khobar. Blank filters were used as controls for SEM analysis. Elemental data was recorded as weight percentages across multiple elements (C, O, F, Na, Mg, Al, Si, K, Ca, Fe, Zn, Ba, Cl, S, Ti).

## Key Findings

- Average concentrations of PM10 were 126.00 µg/m3 in Dammam, 177.00 µg/m3 in Dhahran, and 379.85 µg/m3 in Khobar. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 14]])
- Morphological analysis showed Dhahran samples were predominantly rod-like and platy (2-6 µm), Khobar samples were mostly irregular (2-8 µm), and Dammam samples were nearly spherical (1-3 µm). ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 15]])
- Elemental composition included C, O, F, Na, Mg, Al, Si, K, and Ca in all sampling locations. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 15]])
- XRD analysis identified calcite and montmorillonite as the main mineral constituents of the PM. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 15]])
- Trace metal analysis showed Mn, V, Zn, and Ni at levels higher than those reported in literature. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 15]])
- The study concluded that most PM in the area originates from crustal materials with biogenic or natural origins. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 15]])
- PM2.5 has a higher tendency of penetrating deeper into the human lung compared to particles larger than 2.5 µm, potentially causing respiratory and cardiovascular issues. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 23]])
- SEM-EDX is identified as a convenient method for identifying the source of emission of PM by analyzing morphology and elemental composition. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 26]])
- The study notes that air pollution in the Eastern province was reported to be higher than other areas studied in previous research due to its industrial status. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016]])

- In Dhahran location 1, PM10 concentration ranged from 137.93 to 303.08 µg/m3 with an average of 203.76 µg/m3. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 46]])
- In Dh1, the mean daily gravimetric measurement exceeded USEPA and WHO standards but was lower than PME standards. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 46]])

- The overall mean PM10 concentration in Dhahran was approximately 177 µg/m3. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 47]])

## Limitations

EDX was limited to spot analysis of small areas, necessitating XRF to provide a more representative sample of the entire filter area; however, XRF cannot detect elements with atomic numbers less than 11.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Establish national airborne heavy metal guidelines for Saudi Arabia.
- Conduct continuous monitoring to compare trace elements in air with those in plants and soil.
- Perform year-long studies to assess seasonal variations of PM concentration.
- Extend study to smaller particulate sizes (PM2.5 and PM1.0).
