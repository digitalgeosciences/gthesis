# LEVELS AND CHARACTERISTICS OF AIRBORNE PARTICULATE MATTER IN DAMMAM, DHAHRAN AND KHOBAR, SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the concentration, morphology, and mineralogical composition of airborne particulate matter (PM10) in three cities—Dammam, Dhahran, and Khobar—in the Eastern Province of Saudi Arabia. Using techniques like SEM-EDX, XRD, XRF, and ICP-OES, it concludes that most PM is derived from crustal materials with natural or biogenic origins.

---

## Research Problem

There is limited information regarding the assessment of particulate matter (PM) in terms of its geochemical and morphological characteristics in Saudi Arabia, specifically within the industrial hub of the Eastern Province.

## Objectives

- To determine the concentration levels of airborne particulate matters (PM) in the selected sites.
- To assess the morphological and mineralogical characteristics of the airborne particulates in the selected sites.
- To assess the spatial distribution of the particulate matter in the selected sites
- Determine the concentration levels of PM10 in Dhahran, Khobar, and Dammam.
- Analyze the morphological characteristics (size and shape) of collected PM10 samples.
- Identify the elemental composition of the particulate matter using EDX and XRF.

## Methods

The study utilized gravimetric analysis to measure PM10 concentrations. Morphological characterization was performed using Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) to observe particle shapes and sizes. Elemental composition was determined via Energy Dispersive X-ray (EDX) for localized analysis and X-ray Fluorescence (XRF) for broader area representation of the filter. Mineralogical identification was conducted using X-ray Diffraction (XRD) on 2mm^2 samples. Data processing and graphing were performed using Excel, Origin Pro 8.6, and Sigma Plot 11.0.

## Data and Materials

Samples were collected from 9 total locations across 3 cities. A total of 27 days of samples were selected for detailed characterization (9 per city). Analysis included gravimetric measurements, SEM-EDX imaging/analysis, XRF elemental quantification, and ICP-OES trace metal analysis.

## Key Findings

- Average concentrations of PM10 were 126.00 µg/m3 in Dammam, 177.00 µg/m3 in Dhahran, and 379.85 µg/m3 in Khobar. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 14]])
- Morphological analysis showed Dhahran samples were predominantly rod-like and platy (2–6 µm), Khobar samples were mostly irregular (2–8 µm), and Dammam samples were nearly spherical (1–3 µm). ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 15]])
- Elemental composition included C, O, F, Na, Mg, Al, Si, K, and Ca in all sampling locations. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 15]])
- Mineralogical analysis via XRD identified calcite and montmorillonite as the main mineral constituents of the PM. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 15]])
- Trace metal analysis revealed Mn, V, Zn, and Ni at levels higher than those reported in existing literature. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 15]])
- The study concluded that most of the PM in the study area is from crustal materials with biogenic or natural origin. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 15]])
- The average PM10 concentration in Dhahran was 177 µg/m3, with values ranging from 66.98 to 303.08 µg/m3 across three locations. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 47]])
- Khobar recorded the highest average PM10 concentration of 379.85 µg/m3, with a peak of 1575.46 µg/m3 attributed to heavy sand storms. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 50]])
- Dammam's average PM10 concentration was 126.69 µg/m3, with a range of 56.93 to 232.60 µg/m3. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 52]])
- SEM analysis revealed particles as small as 1 µm in size, with shapes ranging from angular and flaky to spherical and rod-like. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 88]])
- EDX results confirmed the presence of aluminosilicates (feldspar/clay), calcium sulfate, and various metals like Ti, Fe, and Zn across all cities. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 79]])
- XRD analysis identified calcite (Calcite-Magnesian) and montmorillonite as the primary mineral components in all sampled locations. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 98]])
- Barium (Ba) was found at high levels in all three cities, with the highest concentration of 10.13 µg/m3 recorded in Dammam. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 100]])
- Zinc (Zn) showed very high levels across all sites, peaking at 12.64 µg/m3 in Khobar. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 101]])
- Chromium (Cr) was found at concentrations exceeding the allowable limits for urban and industrial areas in all three cities. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 101]])

## Limitations

EDX is limited to spot analysis of small areas, whereas XRF provides a more representative sample of the entire filter area; however, XRF cannot detect elements with atomic numbers less than 11.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Establish national heavy metal guidelines for Saudi Arabia.
- Conduct continuous monitoring of trace elements in air compared to levels in plants and soil.
- Perform year-long studies to assess seasonal variations.
- Extend study to smaller particle sizes (PM2.5 and PM1.0).
