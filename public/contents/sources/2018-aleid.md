# Reservoir and Completion Parametric Study on a Shale Gas Reservoir

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the impact of completion and reservoir parameters on well performance in a shale gas reservoir using three case wells (A, B, and C). Through sensitivity analysis and trilinear composite modeling, it concludes that completion parameters primarily affect short-term performance while reservoir parameters influence long-term production.

---

## Research Problem

Determining the specific impacts of various completion parameters (fracture length, height, number, conductivity) and reservoir properties (porosity, permeability, adsorption) on flow regimes and cumulative production in a shale gas reservoir.

## Objectives

- Evaluate the reservoir and completion key parameters that affect short-term shale gas production in single, multi-fractured, horizontal well.
- Quantify the impact of uncertainty in reservoir and completion data on production forecasts.
- Perform sensitivity analysis to identify which specific parameters most significantly influence production.
- Analyze initial production performance for three different wells (Well A, Well B, and Well C).
- Perform sensitivity analysis on completion parameters to determine their impact on short-term well performance.
- Evaluate the influence of reservoir parameters on long-term well performance.

## Methods

The study utilizes a trilinear composite model for production modeling. Well data (petrophysical logs, completion summaries, surface rates, and pressure) were analyzed using straight-line analysis to identify flow regimes (bilinear, linear, and BDF). A probabilistic evaluation was conducted to account for uncertainty in reservoir parameters. Sensitivity analyses were performed by varying parameters like fracture half-length, height, number, conductivity, well length, spacing, porosity, permeability, adsorption, and initial pressure over a 60-year period.

## Data and Materials

Data from three multi-fractured horizontal wells (Well A, B, and C) located in a shale gas basin. Parameters included: Gross Thickness (80-160 ft), Porosity (2-8%), Matrix Permeability (100-800 nD), Water Saturation (10-40%), Reservoir Pressure (6,000-9,000 psi).

## Key Findings

- Well A showed two distinct flow regimes: bilinear and linear; the bilinear period lasted approximately 2 months and recovered about 13.5% of fracturing fluid. ([[2018-aleid|Aleid, 2018, p. 60]])
- The trilinear composite model successfully achieved an excellent history match for Well A production. ([[2018-aleid|Aleid, 2018, p. 63]])
- Production is directly proportional to fracture half-length, with a 1:2 ratio in production increase relative to the increase in length. ([[2018-aleid|Aleid, 2018, p. 66]])
- Production is directly proportional to fracture number; however, the initial rate increase only lasts for at most one year. ([[2018-aleid|Aleid, 2018, p. 67]])
- Well length impacts production, but the effect fades on longer well cases after approximately one year of production. ([[2018-aleid|Aleid, 2018, p. 69]])
- Fracture conductivity has a negligible impact on long-term production, only appearing in the first few months. ([[2018-aleid|Aleid, 2018, p. 70]])
- Well spacing impacts production; short spacing that is very close to fracture length significantly reduces well life and total production. ([[2018-aleid|Aleid, 2018, p. 72]])
- Completion parameters (fracture length, height, number) primarily affect short-term well performance, while reservoir parameters (porosity, permeability, adsorption) impact long-term production. ([[2018-aleid|Aleid, 2018, p. 127]])
- Bilinear flow was confirmed to last for several weeks in all cases, potentially due to highly de-hydrated conditions where water is quickly soaked into the formation. ([[2018-aleid|Aleid, 2018, p. 127]])
- Linear flow lasted for a few months at most, indicating high permeability or intensive stimulation treatment. ([[2018-aleid|Aleid, 2018, p. 127]])
- For Well A, production is directly proportional to fracture height with a ratio of 1:1; higher conductivity had negligible impact on long-term performance. ([[2018-aleid|Aleid, 2018, p. 73]])
- For Well B, the ratio of production increase due to fracture half-length was 1:2, and the ratio for fracture number was 3:5 over 60 years. ([[2018-aleid|Aleid, 2018, p. 88]])
- Well spacing impacts only the length of the BDF (Basin Drainage Factor) but does not impact well performance or flow regime duration. ([[2018-aleid|Aleid, 2018, p. 129]])
- Adsorption affects performance during BDF flow and delays depletion, with a ratio of 2:1 for production change due to adsorption change. ([[2018-aleid|Aleid, 2018, p. 102]])
- Over 50 percent of EUR (Estimated Ultimate Recovery) is produced in the first 5 years of production across all cases. ([[2018-aleid|Aleid, 2018, p. 128]])

## Limitations

The study notes that some parameters like fracture height are difficult to measure directly in horizontal wells, requiring assumptions (e.g., assuming maximum height equals pay thickness). Additionally, RTA results can be non-unique when boundary dominated flow is not reached.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Optimization of fracture half-length and height is recommended to improve EUR, as results indicated that doubling the fracture half-length could double the EUR in cases with limited fracture size.
