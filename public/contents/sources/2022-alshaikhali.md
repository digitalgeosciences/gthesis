# MICROPLASTIC CONTAMINANTS IN THE SEDIMENT OF THE EAST COAST OF SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates microplastic contamination in the sediment of the East coast of Saudi Arabia, specifically at four beach locations: Khafji, Jubail, Dammam, and Salwa. Using density separation and ATR-FTIR analysis, it establishes baseline data on abundance, size, shape, color, and polymer type.

---

## Research Problem

Microplastic contamination in the sediment of the East coast of Saudi Arabia has not been previously addressed or quantified, necessitating a baseline study to understand the extent of pollution in these coastal environments.

## Objectives

- Characterize microplastic abundance (particle/kg and particle/m2) at four locations: Khafji, Jubail, Dammam, and Salwa Bay.
- Determine microplastic size distribution (from 5 mm down to 1 mm).
- Classify microplastic shapes into fragments, foam, fiber, and pellet.
- Identify the polymer types of the collected microplastics using ATR-FTIR analysis.
- Compare microplastic parameters between urban areas (Dammam) and rural/less populated areas (Salwa).
- Collect baseline data on microplastic abundance at four different beaches (Khafji, Jubail, Dammam, and Salwa).

## Methods

Sediment samples were collected from high tide (HTZ) and low tide (LTZ) zones at four sites. Samples were weighed, filtered through 5 mm and 0.5 mm mesh sieves, and subjected to density separation using a saturated NaCl solution (1.2 g/ml). The resulting material was filtered through Whatman glass microfiber filters. Particles were analyzed under an Olympus stereo microscope (450x) and a Leica CME compound microscope (1000x) for identification based on size, shape, and color. Polymer types were identified using Bruker ATR-FTIR coupled with OPUS-Spectroscopy software.

## Data and Materials

24 sediment samples collected from 4 locations (Khafji, Jubail, Dammam, Salwa). A total of 586 microplastic particles were extracted and analyzed. Samples included both high tide and low tide zones with a 0.5 m x 0.5 m sampling area.

## Key Findings

- A total of 586 microplastic particles were collected from all sites, with an average particle size of 1.55 ± 0.94 SD mm. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 11]])
- 77% of the microplastic particles were less than 2 mm in size. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 11]])
- Microplastic abundance ranged from 5.5 ± 1.55 to 21.2 ± 0.68 SD particle/kg (51.1 ± 14.71 to 152.8 ± 21.32 SD particle/m2) in the low tide region. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 11]])
- Microplastic abundance ranged from 6.3 ± 4.05 to 16.5 ± 4.98 SD particle/kg (50.6 ± 31.21 to 204.5 ± 64.15 SD particle/m2) in the high tide region. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 11]])
- Transparent and blue colors were the most common, followed by black; these are more likely to be ingested by organisms as prey. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 61]])
- Fiber was the most common shape, accounting for 96% of the collected particles. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 11]])
- Polyethylene terephthalates (PET) were the common polymer type in fibers, while polyethylene (PE) and high-density polyethylene (HDPE) were common in fragments and filaments. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 11]])
- No significant differences were found between overall low tide average abundance (12 ± 5.94 particle/kg) and high tide average abundance (11 ± 3.85 particle/kg). ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 38]])
- Microplastics with size ≤ 2 mm were the most common, accounting for 77% of the samples. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 60]])
- ATR-FTIR identified four polymer types: PET, PE, HDPE, and PP. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 38]])
- The average abundance of microplastics was 112.5 particles/m2 for the low tide zone and 106.3 particles/m2 for the high tide zone. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 57]])
- Approximately 96% of the collected microplastic particles were identified as fibers. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 60]])
- ATR-FTIR analysis identified Polyethylene terephthalate (PET) as the primary polymer type for fibers, which is commonly used in ropes and drinking bottles. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 61]])
- Fragments and filaments were primarily identified as Polyethylene (PE) and high-density polyethylene (HDPE). ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 62]])
- Khafji, Jubail, and Dammam showed higher abundance in low tide zones compared to Salwa. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 59]])

## Limitations

The study area of 800 km cannot be fully represented by the four selected sites; however, it provides a baseline for future research. The assumption of equal vertical distribution along the 5 cm depth was required to calculate particles/m2.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Identify the effect of sampling time on microplastic quantity.
- Measure contamination in both water and sediment simultaneously to find correlations.
- Identify specific organisms as bioindicators (e.g., clams).
- Use multiple brine solutions with different densities for extraction.
- Measure abundance relative to depth.
- Provide results in multiple units for easier comparison with international studies.
