# MICROPLASTIC CONTAMINANTS IN THE SEDIMENT OF THE EAST COAST OF SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates microplastic contamination in the sediment of four beaches (Khafji, Jubail, Dammam, and Salwa) on the east coast of Saudi Arabia. It utilizes density separation methods to extract particles and ATR-FTIR spectroscopy to identify polymer types, providing baseline data for the Arabian Gulf.

---

## Research Problem

Microplastic contamination in the sediment of the East coast of Saudi Arabia has not been addressed before this study, despite the region's high traffic from transport ships and its role as a major source for seafood and desalination.

## Objectives

- Collect baseline data on microplastic abundance at four different beaches on the east coast of the Gulf waters of Saudi Arabia (Khafji, Jubail, Dammam, and Salwa).
- Determine the physical characteristics (color, size, shape) of collected microplastics.
- Identify the polymer types of the microplastics using ATR-FTIR spectroscopy.
- Characterize microplastic abundance at four locations (Khafji, Jubail, Dammam, and Salwa Bay) reported as particle/kg of sand and particle/m2.
- Determine microplastic size distribution from 5 mm down to 1 mm.
- Classify microplastic shapes into categories: fragment, foam, fiber, and pellet.
- Identify microplastics polymer types using ATR-FTIR.
- Compare microplastic parameters between the four distinct locations.
- Collect and characterize microplastic abundance at four different beaches on the east coast of the Gulf waters of Saudi Arabia (Khafji, Jubail, Dammam, and Salwa).
- Quantify microplastics in high tide zone (HTZ) and low tide zone (LTZ) to compare environmental impact.
- Identify polymer types using ATR-FTIR analysis.
- Collect baseline data on microplastic abundance at four different beaches on the east coast of the Gulf waters of Saudi Arabia.

## Methods

Sediment samples were collected from 24 locations across four beaches using a 0.5 m x 0.5 m quadrat and a metal shovel, focusing on the top 5 cm of sediment. Samples were filtered through 5 mm and 0.5 mm stainless steel sieves. Density separation was performed using NaCl (1.2 g/ml) to float microplastics. The resulting material was filtered through 47 mm Whatman glass microfiber filters. Analysis included visual identification under a stereo microscope (Olympus, 450x) and compound microscope (Leica CME, 1000x). Particle dimensions were measured using IC Measure software. Polymer types were identified via ATR-FTIR (Bruker) with spectra matched in OPUS-Spectroscopy software. Statistical analysis included Kolmogorov-Smirnov tests for distribution and one-way ANOVA for significance testing.

## Data and Materials

24 sediment samples collected from 4 locations (Khafji, Jubail, Dammam, Salwa). Samples were analyzed for size, shape, color, and polymer type. Data included counts per kg and count per m² in both high tide (HTZ) and low tide (LTZ) zones.

## Key Findings

- A total of 586 microplastic particles were collected from all sites with an average particle size of 1.55 ± 0.94 SD mm. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 11]])
- The majority of microplastic particles (77%) were less than 2 mm in size. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 11]])
- Microplastic abundance ranged from 5.5 ± 1.55 to 21.2 ± 0.68 SD particle/kg (51.1 ± 14.71 to 152.8 ± 21.32 SD particle/m2) in low tide region. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 11]])
- Microplastic abundance ranged from 6.3 ± 4.05 to 16.5 ± 4.98 SD particle/kg (50.6 ± 31.21 to 204.5 ± 64.15 SD particle/m2) in high tide region. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 11]])
- The most dominant colors were transparent (34%) and blue (30%). ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 11]])
- Fiber was the most common shape (96%). ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 11]])
- Polyethylene terephthalates (PET) were the common polymer type in fibers, while polyethylene (PE) and high-density polyethylene (HDPE) were common in fragments and filaments. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 11]])
- The study establishes baseline data for microplastic contamination in the Gulf waters of Saudi Arabia across four specific beaches (Khafji, Jubail, Dammam, and Salwa). ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 22]])
- Previous studies in the Arabian Gulf indicate that fiber is the most dominant shape (44.1%) and PE/PP are the most common polymers (76%). ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 31]])
- In some regions of the Arabian Gulf, microplastic abundance was reported as high as 1.8 x 10^4 particles/km2. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 31]])
- A total of 586 microplastic particles were extracted from the 24 samples with an average size of 1.55 ± 0.94 mm and an overall average abundance of 11.5 ± 5.02 particles/kg (109.4 ± 50.26 particles/m²). ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 49]])
- The highest abundance in the low tide zone was found in Dammam (21.2 ± 0.68 particles/kg), while the lowest was in Jubail (5.5 ± 1.56 particles/kg). ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 49]])
- The highest abundance in the high tide zone was found in Salwa (16.5 ± 4.98 particles/kg), while Jubail had the lowest (6.3 ± 4.06 particles/kg). ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 49]])
- No significant differences were reported between overall low tide average abundance (12 ± 5.94 particles/kg) and high tide average abundance (11 ± 3.85 particles/kg). ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 49]])
- Microplastics with a size range of 1 mm - 2 mm were the most common (44%), followed by <1 mm (33%). ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 49]])

## Limitations

The study notes that the sample size (4 locations) does not represent the entire 800 km coastline of the East Coast, and some smaller particles (<0.5 mm) may have passed through the sieve.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Identify the effect of sampling time on microplastic quantity.
- Measure microplastic contamination in both water and study sediment simultaneously to find correlations.
- Identify a biological organism as a bioindicator (e.g., clams).
- Use multiple brine solutions with different densities for extraction.
- Measure microplastic abundance relative to depth.
- Provide results in various units for easier comparison with international studies.
- Study protected areas like mangroves and islands.
- Utilize acid digestion of organic material (with caution regarding color/shape impact).
- Use a finer mesh size (300 µm) for filtering the supernatant.
