# HUMAN HEALTH RISK ASSESSMENT OF INDUSTRIAL AIR POLLUTANT EMISSIONS UNDER DIFFERENT MITIGATION SCENARIOS USING DISPERSION MODELING

**Summary**: The thesis evaluates the impact of industrial air pollutant emissions (CO, SO2, NO2, PM2.5, and PM10) in Saudi Arabian industrial regions using CALPUFF dispersion modeling. It compares two mitigation scenarios—increasing stack height and switching to cleaner fuels—concluding that fuel shifting is significantly more effective than increasing stack height for reducing hazardous concentrations of SO2 and NO2.

---

## Research Problem

The rapid industrialization in Saudi Arabia has led to increased consumption of fuels like diesel and gas, resulting in the dispersal of ambient air pollutants that pose significant risks to human health. There is a need for precise estimates of pollutant dispersion patterns and an assessment of human health risks under various mitigation scenarios.

## Objectives

- To obtain dispersion model input parameters comprising meteorological and stack emission data.
- To predict of atmospheric dispersion of air pollutants, including NO2, PM2.5, PM10, SO2, and CO being emitted from an industrial city, using the CALPUFF model.
- To generate pollutant concentration and air quality index (AQI) distribution maps over a selected domain based on the results of CALPUFF model.
- To assess human health risk for the receptor domain by use of the CALPUFF model results of the current emission conditions and two emission mitigation scenarios.
- Assess the human health risks associated with air pollutant emissions from an industrial zone.
- Simulate pollutant dispersion using the CALPUFF modeling system under different mitigation scenarios.
- Evaluate the effectiveness of specific mitigation strategies (increasing stack height, fuel shifting) on local air quality.
- Generate Air Quality Index (AQI) maps to visualize and assess air quality across the study area.
- Assess air pollutant emissions and their impact on local residents in industrial regions of Saudi Arabia.
- Evaluate the effectiveness of increasing stack heights as a mitigation strategy for pollutant dispersion.
- Evaluate the effectiveness of transitioning to cleaner fuels (fuel shifting) as a mitigation strategy.
- Generate AQI maps to visualize air quality across different scenarios.
- Assess the impact of industrial emissions (NO2, SO2, PM2.5, PM10, and CO) using CALPUFF dispersion modeling.
- Evaluate the effectiveness of two mitigation scenarios: increasing stack height and fuel shifting.
- Determine the reduction in air quality index (AQI) and hazard indices for various pollutants under different scenarios.

## Methods

The research employs a multi-step methodology: 1. Site Selection: Identifying an industrial zone in Saudi Arabia with a 150 km radius for analysis. 2. Modeling Software selection: Choosing CALPUFF due to its ability to handle non-steady-state conditions and long-range transport (>50km). 3. Data Collection: Obtaining emission data from over 90 stacks (2016-2021) and meteorological data from three regional stations via Wunderground Database and NOAA/ESRL Radiosonde Database. 4. Processing: Using CALMET to process ground and upper-air data into surface friction velocity, sensible heat flux, etc. 5. Simulation: Running four scenarios (Current conditions, Q1 2021, increased stack height, fuel shifting) using the CALPUFF system. 6. Assessment: Applying US EPA human health risk assessment methodology to evaluate impacts.

## Data and Materials

The study covers a large geographic domain in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia involving multiple industrial sources. Data includes concentrations of five key pollutants: NO2, SO2, PM2.5, PM10, and CO, along with meteorological data used for CALPUFF modeling.

## Key Findings

- CO average concentrations were reduced by 68.6% under the stack height scenario. ([[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025, p. 12]])
- NO2 concentrations showed reductions of 36.4% under increased stack height and 46.9% under fuel change. ([[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025, p. 12]])
- SO2 concentrations significantly reduced by 97.2% with fuel change from diesel to natural gas. ([[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025, p. 12]])
- Increasing the stack height led to a reduction of 59.3% and 60.7% of PM2.5 and PM10 concentrations respectively. ([[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025, p. 12]])
- The study demonstrated higher reductions in pollutant concentrations and lower human health risks when changing the fuel to natural gas compared to increasing stack height. ([[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025, p. 12]])
- The study identified five main pollutants for AQI assessment: ground-level ozone (O3), Particulate Matter (PM2.5), sulfur dioxide (SO2), carbon monoxide (CO), and nitrogen dioxide (NO2). ([[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025, p. 20]])
- The study highlights that the CALPUFF model is superior to steady-state models like AERMOD for domains exceeding a 50 km radius or those with complex terrain. ([[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025, p. 28]])
- Data from 2016 to 2021 showed that regulatory measures led to the lowest recorded emissions in 2021. ([[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025, p. 30]])
- Wind analysis showed a consistent prevailing wind direction towards the southeast across all three monitoring stations in 2021, with peak frequencies occurring between 3.6 m/s and 5.7 m/s (29.4-30.1%). ([[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025, p. 30]])
- Compared to the full year of 2021, the first quarter (Q1) showed significant reductions in pollutants: NO2 by ~50% for 1-hour periods, SO2 by ~53% for 1-hour periods, and PM10/PM2.5 by 57% and 56.6% respectively for 24-hour periods. ([[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025, p. 31]])
- Scenario 1 (increasing stack height to 50m) resulted in moderate reductions: NO2 by ~15% (1-hr), SO2 by ~9% (1-hr), and CO by 68% for both 1-hour and 24-hour periods. ([[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025, p. 32]])
- Scenario 1 showed significant reductions in particulate matter, with PM10 and PM2.5 reducing by 81% and 82% respectively for 1-hour periods. ([[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025, p. 32]])
- Scenario 2 (fuel shifting) resulted in a dramatic reduction of SO2 concentrations, reaching approximately 97% across all averaging periods (1-hr, 24-hr, and 3-month). ([[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025, p. 35]])
- Scenario 2 showed significant NO2 reductions: ~47% for 3-month average, ~55% for 24-hour, and ~39% for 1-hour periods. ([[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025, p. 35]])
- AQI maps for CO showed 'Good' conditions (values < 50) in all scenarios, with maximum values of 8.79 and 8.58 respectively for the first quarter and Scenario 1. ([[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025, p. 38]])

## Limitations

The study lacked model validation due to the unavailability of Air Quality Monitoring Station (AQMS) data. Additionally, CALPUFF does not support parallel computing, which limited the ability to include seasonal variations in the analysis. The effects of greenhouse gases and temperature-related impacts were also not fully integrated.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Prioritize fuel shifting (e.g., transitioning to natural gas) as the primary mitigation strategy. Use increased stack height where fuel switching is not feasible. Establish a centralized real-time industrial emissions database, utilize higher-resolution meteorological data instead of satellite data, and integrate building data into future models to account for downwash effects.
