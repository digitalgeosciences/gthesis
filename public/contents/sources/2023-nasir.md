# Assessment of the environmental quality status and defining reference conditions in naturally stressed coastal lagoons with the help of foraminiferal and pollution proxies

**Summary**: The study evaluates the environmental quality of two coastal sites in the Arabian Gulf (Uqair Beach and Eco-Park Ras Tanura) using benthic foraminifera as bioindicators alongside heavy metal and organic pollutant proxies. It concludes that Uqair Beach shows a high ecological status due to recent improvements, while Eco-Park reflects moderate conditions influenced by historical industrial activity.

---

## Research Problem

The Arabian Gulf is subject to natural stresses (high salinity/temperature) and anthropogenic pressures from over a century of oil production and industrial history. There is a need to establish reference conditions and assess the current ecological quality status using both chemical proxies and biological indicators in these coastal environments.

## Objectives

- Estimate the benthic foraminiferal response to a salinity gradient in a hypersaline lagoon at the western Arabian Gulf
- Estimate the ecological quality status and reference conditions in the western Arabian Gulf (Saudi Arabia), using foraminiferal and pollution proxies
- Assess the environmental quality status of two distinct coastal sites (Uqair Beach and Eco-Park Ras Tanura) in the Arabian Gulf.
- Identify heavy metal pollution levels and organic pollutant concentrations in sediment cores.
- Utilize benthic foraminiferal assemblages as bioindicators to monitor marine ecosystem health.
- Establish a reference condition site for environmental studies in the region.

## Methods

The study analyzed two sediment cores (Uqair Core 25 and Eco-Park Core 12) collected from coastal sites. Chemical analysis included calculating metal enrichment factors (EF), Sediment Pollution Index (SPI), and Pollution Load Index (PLI). Organic pollutants including TPH, TOC, and PAHs were measured to identify sources of pollution via diagnostic ratios. Foraminiferal samples were processed using a 63-micron sieve, washed with Na2CO3-based detergent, and dried at 75°C. Analysis included counting species under a Nikon SMZ-18 microscope and creating SEM images. Statistical methods included CONISS for assemblage zonation, DCA to determine gradient lengths, RDA to assess correlations between biota and pollutants, and several biodiversity indices (H'log2, ES100, etc.). Ecological quality was quantified using the nEQR metric.

## Data and Materials

Two sediment cores: Core 25 from Uqair Beach (minimized impact site) and Core 12 from Eco-Park Ras Tanura (historically industrial area). Data included heavy metal concentrations for several elements, organic pollutant data (TPH, TOC, PAHs), and counts of approximately 300 foraminiferal specimens per sample.

## Key Findings

- Quinqueloculina and Ammonia were identified as the most salt-tolerant species, with Quinqueloculina showing high deformities in hypersaline environments. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 14]])
- Uqair locality was determined to be in a pristine condition status and is recommended as a reference for contemporary marine environmental studies. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 15]])
- The average foraminiferal density ranged from 32 to 8720 individuals per cubic centimeter across the study sites. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 34]])
- The Ammonia-Elphidium (A-E) index, used as a hypoxia indicator, showed high values in the inner and middle lagoons (68.55% and 69.51%), indicating lower water circulation. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 39]])
- The Foraminiferal Abnormality Index (FAI) was highest in the inner lagoon (5.88%) compared to Uqair Beach (1.74%), reflecting salinity stress. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 40]])
- Quinqueloculina showed the highest percentage of deformities (mean 2.70%) among the studied taxa. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 40]])
- The Uqair area has been in a high Ecological Quality Status (EcoQS) category since the late 1950s. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 54]])
- Eco Park showed moderate EcoQS throughout the core, with an improving trend in the top samples. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 54]])
- Eco-Park Core 12 showed a mean PLI of 1.37 (range 0.95–1.76) and an SPI of 2.90 (range 2.26–4.12). ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 50]])
- In Uqair Core 25, metals exceeding the reference criteria (>2) included Zn (max 5.38), Pb (max 3.73), As (max 3.59), and Sr (max 3.11). ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 49]])
- In Eco-Park Core 12, metals exceeding the reference criteria (>2) included Pb (6.14), Hg (5.36), As (4.85), Cu (2.35), Sr (2.33), and Zn (2.18). ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 49]])
- Uqair Core 25 showed a mean TPH of 47.99 mg/kg, TOC of 21241.67 mg/kg, and ΣPAHs of 11.34 µg kg-1. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 50]])
- Eco-Park Core 12 showed a mean TPH of 49.36 mg/kg, TOC of 18768.57 mg/kg, and ΣPAHs of 12.61 µg kg-1. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 50]])
- The foraminiferal count in Core 25 ranged from 139 to 760 individuals per gram, while Core 12 ranged from 90 to 1291 individuals per gram. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 52]])
- The BFAR (Benthic Foraminiferal Accumulation Rate) in Core 25 was higher than in Core 12, ranging from 111.4 to 608 n/cm2/yr vs. 35.8 to 516.5 n/cm2/yr. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 53]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

The Uqair locality is recommended as a reference for contemporary marine environmental studies due to its pristine condition status despite high natural salinity.
