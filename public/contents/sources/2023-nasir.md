# Assessment of the environmental quality status and defining reference conditions in naturally stressed coastal lagoons with the help of foraminiferal and pollution proxies

**Summary**: The thesis evaluates the environmental quality status of two sites in the Western Arabian Gulf—Uqair Lagoon (a naturally stressed but relatively pristine area) and Eco Park (Ras Tanura, a historically industrial site)—using foraminiferal biomonitoring and chemical proxies. It utilizes Pb210-dated sediment cores to analyze heavy metals, organic pollutants (PAH, TPH), and foraminiferal assemblages to establish reference conditions and track historical pollution.

---

## Research Problem

The Arabian Gulf is a naturally stressed marine environment due to high salinity and temperature, which complicates the use of bioindicators. There is a need to distinguish between natural stress and anthropogenic impacts (heavy metals, hydrocarbons) in coastal areas like Uqair Lagoon and Ras Tanura to establish baseline conditions for environmental monitoring.

## Methods

Water temperature was monitored using HOBO TidbiT-400 sensors; salinity was measured via EUTECH PCD650, refractometer, and electronic thermometer. Sediment samples were collected from four transects (Lake-3, Lake-2, Lake-1, and Uqair Beach) and processed following the FOMBIMO protocol: wet sieving (63 µm and 125 µm), drying at 70°C, and micropaleontological sorting under a binocular microscope. Statistical analysis included alpha diversity indices (Richness_S, Dominance_D, Shannon_H, Evenness_E), Foraminiferal Abnormality Index (FAI), Foraminiferal Monitoring Index (FMI), and Ammonia-Elphidium index (AEI). Multivariate analyses included Q/R mode cluster analysis, SIMPER test, indicator species analysis, and nonmetric multidimensional scaling (nMDS) using PAST software. Visualization of relative abundance was performed via the ampvis2 R-package.

## Data and Materials

Two sediment cores (UQSGC3 from Uqair Beach, RTSGC2 from Eco-Park Ras Tanura) sampled at 1cm intervals. Data included heavy metal concentrations, PAH (16 types), TPH, and TOC. Foraminiferal data included counts of live/dead ratios, size distributions, and diversity indices across multiple sites in the Uqair Lagoon.

## Key Findings

- Quinqueloculina and Ammonia were identified as the most salt-tolerant species in the hypersaline environment of Uqair Lagoon. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 14]])
- Foraminiferal deformity indices are not recommended for pollution indication in hypersaline environments due to high natural stress levels. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 14]])
- Uqair Lagoon was determined to be in a pristine condition and is suitable as a reference site for marine environmental studies. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 14]])
- Eco Park, Ras Tanura, showed moderate ecological conditions throughout its history since at least 1827. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 15]])
- The study is the first in the region to estimate historical marine ecological conditions using foraminifera. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 15]])
- The foraminiferal density varied between 32 to 8720 individuals per cubic centimeter across the study area. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 19]])
- The most abundant taxa were Peneroplis (max. 51.4%), Ammonia (max. 46.4%), Quinqueloculina (max. 41.8%), and Elphidium (max. 26.3%). ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 19]])
- The average live/dead ratio for foraminifera was 29.1% (range: 11.17% to 54.35%), with higher values observed in the outer lagoon, potentially due to higher organic matter or sedimentation rates. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 38]])
- The mean Ammonia-Elphidium (A-E) index was 65.12% (range: 42.1–82.50%), with significantly higher values in the inner and middle lagoons, indicating lower circulation. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 39]])
- The Foraminiferal Monitoring Index (FMI) ranged from 0 to 62.5% with an average of 28.8%, showing no significant difference among lagoonal transects but a significant reduction at Uqair Beach. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 40]])
- The Foraminiferal Abnormality Index (FAI) ranged from 0% to 13.3% with a mean of 4.15%, being highest in the inner lagoon (5.88%) due to salinity stress. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 40]])
- Quinqueloculina was identified as a high-tolerance indicator for hypersaline conditions, while it showed lower tolerance to pollutants compared to other taxa. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 53]])
- The Uqair area has been in the 'high' category of EcoQS since the late 1950s due to its distance from industrial centers, while the Eco Park site shows a trend of improving conditions in the top core samples. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 54]])
- The average dissimilarity for the SIMPER analysis was 26.17%, with Peneroplis, Lachlanella, and Quinqueloculina being the primary contributors to differentiation between sites. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 52]])
- Uqair Beach (Core 25) showed high ecological quality status from 1960 onwards, with a mean f-AMBI value of 0.61 and NQI_f of 0.75. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 71]])
