# Characterization of Minjur Aquifer using Geophysical Methods for Future CO2 storage, North of Riyadh, KSA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the subsurface characterization of the Minjur Aquifer north of Riyadh using integrated transient electromagnetic (TEM) and audio magnetotelluric (AMT) geophysical methods. The study aims to evaluate the geological structure and potential for future CO2 storage by mapping lithological units and identifying hydrogeological features.

---

## Research Problem

The need for accurate subsurface characterization of the Minjur Aquifer, specifically identifying lithological boundaries and structural features, to assess its suitability for future carbon capture and storage (CCS) projects in the Riyadh region.

## Objectives

- Analyze and characterize a section of the Minjur Aquifer, specifically the lower saline region, as a potential CO2 storage reservoir.
- Map and characterize the thickness, geometry, and other key properties of the Minjur Aquifer using advanced geophysical techniques.
- Integrate TEM and AMT methods to improve stratigraphic and structural interpretation.
- Construct a 2D deep geophysical section of the study area to provide a clear depiction of the subsurface structure and aquifer characteristics.
- Identify and delineate lateral subsurface geophysical and geological variations influenced by tectonic activity.
- Develop a conceptual model of the study area integrating geophysical and geological data for future research.
- Characterize the subsurface geology of the study area using TEM and AMT methods.
- Evaluate the lithological and geoelectric characteristics of the Minjur Sandstone.
- Assess the suitability of the Min1ur aquifer for future CO2 storage.
- Utilize transient electromagnetic (TEM) and audio magnetotelluric (AMT) methods to characterize the subsurface geology of the study area.
- Process TEM data using Aarhus SPIA software to generate high-resolution geoelectric models.
- Perform 1D, 2D, and 3D inversions to delineate lithological features and determine aquifer suitability for CO2 storage.
- Characterize the subsurface geology of the study area using electromagnetic geophysical methods.
- Utilize TEM data to provide high-resolution insight into geoelectric characteristics.
- Develop 1D, 2D, and 3D resistivity models to delineate lithological features and potential for CO2 storage.
- Identify lithological units such as Dhruma, Tuwaiq, Hanifa, Jubaila, and Sulaiy formations.
- Delineate the stratigraphic succession and structural trends in the Minjur Aquifer region.
- Evaluate the potential of the aquifer for future CO2 storage based on geoelectric properties.

## Methods

The study employs transient electromagnetic (TEM) and audio magnetotelluric (AMT) geophysical methods. TEM data are processed using Aarhus SPIA software with the AarhusInv inversion method, applying various smoothing levels (low, normal, high). The methodology includes 1D, 2D, and 3D inversions to create depth-slice models. Data acquisition involves a large transmitter loop (approx. 400m) and two receiver loops (RC-200 and RC-5) to capture both shallow and deep strata. Measurement parameters include multiple measurement rounds per location for improved SNR, with current intensities ranging from 2A (shallow) to 13A (deep).

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes geophysical data collected via ABEM-WalkTEM equipment. Data includes measurements of the primary and secondary magnetic fields, geoelectric properties, and noise levels for depth of investigation (DOI) estimation. The analysis involves multiple measurement rounds at each location to enhance signal-to-noise ratio.

## Key Findings

- The study identified a lower saline aquifer (Minjur) at depths exceeding 800 meters, which meets the requirement for safe CO2 storage under high-pressure conditions. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 17]])
- TEM data processed via AarhusInv yielded high-resolution insights into geoelectric characteristics of formations including Dhruma, Tuwaiq, Hanifa, Jubaila, Sulaiy, Aruma, and Umm er Radhuma. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 15]])
- A low resistivity zone at the SW end of the profile was identified as a saline aquifer, while high resistivity zones were interpreted as carbonate-deposited or potential freshwater zones. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 15]])
- The integration of TEM and AMT methods successfully delineated substantial resistivity differences and improved stratigraphic/structural interpretation compared to using either method alone. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 15]])
- The Minjur Aquifer is characterized by a sedimentary system where the proportion of sandstone is between 60% and 90%, and shale content ranges from 10% to 40%. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 10]])
- The Minjur Sandstone is a quartz-dominated sandstone with grain sizes ranging from 0.55 to 0.74 mm, indicating coarse sand. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 20]])
- Sandstone constitutes approximately 60-90% of the Minjur formation, while shale accounts for 10-40%. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 21]])
- Permeability values in the Minjur Formation reached up to 3000 millidarcies (md), with porosity reaching as high as 34%. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 23]])
- The aquifer's thickness varies between 74 and 138 m within the 40,000 km2 research area. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 24]])
- Transmissivity values for the Minjur Aquifer range from 0.001 to 0.0062 m2/sec. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 24]])
- The average permeability of the Upper Minjur Aquifer was observed at 7.7 x 10^-5 meters per second, with a range of 2.5 x 10^-5 to 1.6 x 10^-4 m/s. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 25]])
- Sandstones in the formation exhibit porosity ranging from 7% to 20% and possess substantial intergranular and moldic macropores. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 25]])
- A minimum depth of 800 meters is required for CO2 to remain in a supercritical state, while a minimum temperature of 100°C is required for geothermal CCUS. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 28]])
- The Minjur Sandstone is characterized by a fluvial-deltaic system with nine depositional sequences, where sequences 1-4 (Upper Minjur Member) are well-connected sandstone bodies with significant potential for reservoir use. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 35]])
- The upper Minjur in the Rub' Al-Khali basin contains muddy sandstones with porosity ranging from 7 to 20 percent and well-connected intergranular and moldic macropores. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 36]])

## Limitations

1D models are subject to 'geological noise' when representing 3D realities; 2D models are only beneficial if they can accurately portray 3D situations. Interpretation of results is limited by the accuracy of input parameters like timing (requiring precision of 100 nanoseconds) and receiver transfer functions.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The work underscores the necessity for further research, including prospect-level evaluations, to enhance comprehension of oilfield and trap properties, assess injectivity efficiency, and elucidate CO2 behavior.
