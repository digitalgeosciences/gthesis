# Characterization of Minjur Aquifer using Geophysical Methods for Future CO2 storage, North of Riyadh, KSA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the subsurface geology of a region north of Riyadh using integrated transient electromagnetic (TEM) and audio magnetotelluric (AMT) geophysical methods. The study aims to characterize the Minjur aquifer and evaluate its potential for future CO2 storage by mapping resistivity variations and lithological units.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of detailed information regarding the actual thickness and subsurface geometry of the Minjur Aquifer makes it difficult to assess its suitability for CO2 storage. While some data exists from outcrops and remote boreholes, a comprehensive geophysical characterization is needed to map the aquifer's extent and identify potential risks like leakage or structural complexity.

## Objectives

- Construct a 2D deep geophysical section of the study area to provide a clear depiction of the subsurface structure and aquifer characteristics.
- Identify and delineate lateral subsurface geophysical and geological variations influenced by tectonic activity.
- Develop a conceptual model of the study area integrating geophysical and geological data for future research.
- Characterize the subsurface geology of the study area using TEM and AMT methods.
- Develop 1D, 2D, and 3D resistivity models to provide high-resolution insight into geoelectric characteristics.
- Evaluate the suitability of the Minjur Aquifer for future CO2 storage.

## Methods

The study utilized Transient Electromagnetic (TEM) methods with a dual-moment setup (low moment for shallow strata, high moment for deep strata) and Audio Magnetotelluric (AMT) methods to capture both resistive and conductive layers. Data were processed using the Aarhus SPIA software and inverted via the AarhusInv method at various smoothing levels. AMT data was analyzed using the KMSPro program with specific filtering parameters (40 Hz sampling rate, 16-64 sample FFT windows). The study also included a detailed analysis of signal-to-noise ratios, depth of penetration calculations, and interpretation of 2D geoelectric sections to identify lithological boundaries and potential tectonic features.

## Data and Materials

The survey covered a 136 km long profile (SW-NE) with 30 TEM stations spaced at 5 km intervals. AMT measurements were conducted at specific locations near boreholes for calibration. The study included data from 29 TEM soundings and 2 AMT sites.

## Key Findings

- The study area contains several key stratigraphic units including Dhruma, Tuwaiq, Hanifa, Jubaila, Sulaiy, Aruma, and Umm er Radhuma Formations. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 15]])
- A low resistivity zone at the SW end of the profile is delineated by saline aquifers, while higher resistivity zones indicate carbonate deposits or potential freshwater aquifer zones. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 15]])
- The Minjur Aquifer's thickness varies between 74 and 138 m within a 40,000 km2 research area. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 24]])
- Transmissivity values for the Minjur aquifer range from 0.001 to 0.0062 m2/sec, with a vertical conductivity of the overlying confining layer modeled as 0.2 m/sec. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 24]])
- The average permeability for the Upper Minjur Aquifer is 7.7 x 10^-5 m/s, ranging from 2.5 x 10^-5 to 1.6 x 10^-4 m/s. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 25]])
- Sandstones in the Minjur formation exhibit porosity between 7% and 20%, with some areas reaching up to 34%. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 23]])
- The study identifies a potential for CO2 storage in several Saudi Arabian basins, including the Eastern Arabian Basin and Interior Homocline-Central Arch. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 41]])
- The 1D, 2D, and 3D resistivity models successfully delineated primary lithological features and geoelectric characteristics of the subsurface. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 60]])
- A dual-moment setup in TEM allowed for simultaneous detection of shallow (low moment) and deep (high moment) strata. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 64]])
- The 2D geoelectric section revealed a 136 km long profile with distinct resistivity contrasts, potentially indicating tectonic features like faults or fractures containing saline water. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 66]])
- A depth of investigation (DOI) of 180m was identified in the 2D model results. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 66]])
- The study confirmed that a combination of TEM and AMT is optimal for characterizing the Minjur aquifer, as they are sensitive to both resistive (freshwater) and conductive (saline) layers. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 75]])
- The Dhruma formation shows two distinct zones: a highly conductive zone (< 5 Ohm.m) interpreted as a caving or karstic zone, and a second zone with resistivity of approximately 13 Ohm.m. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 83]])
- The Tuwaiq, Hanifa, Jubaila, and Sulaiy Formations are carbonate compositions exhibiting resistivity values of 100 Ohm.m. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 83]])
- The Hith Formation predominantly consists of substantial anhydrite with high resistance over 100 Ohm.m (TEM 16, 17, and 18). ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 83]])

## Limitations

Geological noise is unavoidable when representing three-dimensional reality with a one-dimensional model; however, high spatial density data helps mitigate this. Interpretation of results is subject to the accuracy of signal-to-noise ratios and the impact of near-surface resistivity variations on AMT data.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The study advocates for the enhanced integration of geophysical techniques to improve resolution and depth in future studies. It highlights the need for more prospect-level evaluations to understand oilfield/trap properties and CO2 behavior.
