# NANOSCALE ASSESSMENT OF SHALES MECHANICAL INTEGRITY UPON INTERACTIONS WITH SUPERCRITICAL CO2

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the mechanical properties of Eagle Ford shale at both micro and macro scales, specifically focusing on how interaction with supercritical CO2 (sc-CO2) affects these properties. By combining nanoindentation with SEM imaging and XRD/XRF analysis, the study identifies a dual peak distribution in nanoscale measurements, indicating distinct soft and hard constituents within the rock matrix.

---

## Research Problem

The mechanical response of nanoscale constituents in shales is poorly understood due to difficulties in isolating them without compromising structural integrity. This lack of knowledge complicates the assessment of sc-CO2 for waterless fracturing and CO2 storage in shale formations like the Eagle Ford.

## Objectives

- Investigating the mechanical heterogeneity of organic-rich shale formations through integrating nanoindentation and Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM), specifically focusing on analyzing the influence of long term sc-CO2 treatment on the mechanical characteristics of shale.
- Providing a macro to nanoscale mechanical properties crosslinking.
- Conducting nanoscale mapping of the mechanical integrity of organic-rich shales upon exposure to sc-CO2 utilizing nanoindentation techniques.
- Investigate the mechanical heterogeneity of organic-rich shales at both nano and macro scales.
- Establish a statistical alignment between nano-scale and macro-scale mechanical properties.
- Evaluate the impact of nanoscale mineral variations on macroscopic fracture propagation in hydraulic fracturing.

## Methods

The study utilized a multi-step workflow: 1) Sample Preparation: Core samples were cut, mechanically polished (600/1200 grit), and ion beam polished (EM TIC 3X) to achieve <100nm surface roughness. 2) Characterization: XRD (Panalytical Empyrean), XRF (M4 TORNADO), and Rock-Eval (Rock-Eval 6) were used for mineralogy, elemental analysis, and TOC determination. 3) Nanoindentation: A Hysitron TI 980 system performed a grid of 20x20 indentations on samples before and after sc-CO2 treatment (13.79 MPa, 75°C). 4) Macroscale Testing: AutoScan instrumentation was used to measure Young's modulus at 30 points per sample. 5. Profilometry: Kruss SRA was used for surface roughness measurement.

## Data and Materials

6 Eagle Ford shale core samples; 18 distinct locations for nanoindentation; 7,200 total individual indentations; 30 macroscale data points per sample; TOC measured at ~2.7% average.

## Key Findings

- The mineralogical composition of the shale included 40.5% Pyrite, 15.2% Dickite, 14.3% Muscovite, 11.8% Quartz, and 11% Kaolinite. ([[2024-alvayed|Alvayed, 2024, p. 45]])
- The average Total Organic Carbon (TOC) content of the shale samples was approximately 2.7 wt%. ([[2024-alvayed|Alvayed, 2024, p. 36]])
- Nano-scale Young's modulus values exhibited a wide range of mechanical heterogeneity, varying between 7 to 85 GPa. ([[2024-alvayed|Alvayed, 2024, p. 31]])
- Nano-scale hardness values showed significant variation, ranging from 0.3 to 4.9 GPa. ([[2024-alvayed|Alvayed, 2024, p. 31]])
- Macroscale Young's modulus values followed a normal distribution between 25 and 60 GPa. ([[2024-alvayed|Alvayed, 2024, p. 31]])
- A dual peak distribution in nanoscale measurements for both Young's modulus and hardness was identified, indicating a comparable frequency of soft and hard constituents. ([[2024-alvayed|Alvayed, 2024, p. 31]])
- Surface roughness (RMS) values were measured at 69.1 nm (Sample C) and 50.3 nm (Sample D), meeting the <100 nm requirement for nanoindentation. ([[2024-alvayed|Alvayed, 2024, p. 47]])
- The study confirmed that sc-CO2 exposure leads to a gradual weakening of the shale matrix over time. ([[2024-alvayed|Alvayed, 2024, p. 13]])
- Nanoscale Young's modulus shows a bimodal distribution with Phase 1 (soft) between 7-40 GPa and Phase 2 (hard) between 40-85 GPa. ([[2024-alvayed|Alvayed, 2024, p. 52]])
- Nanoscale measurements captured a wider range of values (7-85 GPa) compared to the more constrained macroscale distribution (25-60 GPa). ([[2024-alvayed|Alvayed, 2024, p. 61]])
- A positive correlation exists between Young's modulus and hardness at the nanoscale, where both increase together. ([[2024-alvayed|Alvayed, 2024, p. 54]])
- page_number_reference_note_for_finding_2_and_3_are_on_pages_52-54 ([[2024-alvayed|Alvayed, 2024]])
- SEM imaging identified darker areas as organic-rich/softer materials and lighter areas as harder minerals like pyrite. ([[2024-alvayed|Alvayed, 2024, p. 56]])
- Macroscale Young's modulus values for the six samples ranged from 40.8 to 45.61 GPa, following a normal distribution. ([[2024-alvayed|Alvayed, 2024, p. 57]])
- The mean Young's modulus at the nanoscale (44 GPa) aligned closely with the macroscale value (42.6 GPa). ([[2024-alvayed|Alvayed, 2024, p. 60]])

## Limitations

Traditional macroscopic characterization methods are limited in capturing the intricacies of shale heterogeneity; however, this study's cross-scale indentation technique aims to bypass the uncertainties inherent in standard upscaling models.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The findings suggest that integrating nano-indentation and macroscale testing can improve the accuracy of predicting mechanical behavior for hydraulic fracturing design in unconventional shale plays.
