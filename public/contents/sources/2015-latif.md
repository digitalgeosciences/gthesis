# GROUND MOTION PREDICTION EQUATIONS (GMPES) FOR SUBDUCTION-ZONE EARTHQUAKES IN JAVA ISLAND, INDONESIA

**Summary**: The thesis develops empirical Ground Motion Prediction Equations (GMPEs) for peak ground acceleration (PGA) and response spectral acceleration (PSA) at frequencies of 1, 2, and 5 Hz specifically for subduction-zone earthquakes in Java Island, Indonesia. The study utilizes a dataset of 1,574 strong-motion recordings from 118 subduction-zone earthquakes occurring between 2008 and 2013.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of reliable, region-specific ground-motion prediction equations (GMPEs) for subduction-zone earthquakes in Java Island. Existing global models (Youngs et al., 1997 and Atkinson and Boore, 2003) were found to be unreliable for predicting moderate subduction events (M ≤ 6.0) in the region.

## Objectives

- Develop predictive relations for peak ground acceleration (PGA)
- Develop response spectral acceleration (PSA) at frequencies of 1, 2, and 5 Hz
- Create models specifically tailored for both interface and in-slab events on NEHRP class B sites
- Perform regression analyses using the least square method to develop GMPEs for peak ground acceleration (PGA) and 5% damped response spectral acceleration (SA) at frequencies of 1, 2, and 5 Hz on rock (NEHRP B).
- Develop these GMPEs specifically for subduction zone earthquakes in Java Island.
- Develop predictive relations for peak ground acceleration (PGA) and response spectral acceleration (PSA) at frequencies of 1, 2, and 5 Hz.
- Differentiate between interface and in-slab earthquake events based on depth and mechanism.
- Evaluate the performance of developed GMPEs against existing global models (Youngs et al., 1997; Atkinson and Boore, 2003).
- Provide region-specific data to improve the Indonesian Earthquake Resistant Building Code (SNI 1726-2012).
- Establish GMPEs for both interface and in-slab events in the subduction zone

## Methods

The methodology involves: 1. Collecting a database from two horizontal-components of BMKG's accelerographic network recordings from subduction zone earthquakes in south Java Island (2008-2013). 2. Determining local site conditions using 30-m shear-wave velocity (VS30) from BMKG and USGS data. 3. Applying ASCE amplification factors to convert records to PSA on rock (NEHRP B). 4. Converting various magnitude scales (mL, mb, mB) into moment magnitude (M) using equations by Abe (1981), Scordilis (2006), and Irsyam et al. (2010). 5. Determining the closest distance to the fault rupture plane (Rrup) using the Wells and Coppersmith (1994) equation. 6. Classifying events into interface and in-slab subduction types. 7. Fitting the database using the least squares method for different GMPE forms, including determining geometric spreading coefficients via preliminary regression.

## Data and Materials

A dataset of 1,574 strong-motion recordings from 118 subduction-zone earthquakes (moment magnitude M 4.4 to 7.0) occurring between 2008 and 2013 in the south of Java Island. Source parameters were compiled from GCMT, ISC, and NEIC catalogs.

## Key Findings

- The study developed predictive relations for PGA and PSA at frequencies of 1, 2, and 5 Hz specifically for subduction-zone earthquakes in Java Island. ([[2015-latif|Latif, 2015, p. 21]])
- Existing models (Youngs et al., 1997) were found to be higher than the new results, while Atkinson and Boore (2003) were significantly lower for moderate events (M ≤ 6.0). ([[2015-latif|Latif, 2015, p. 21]])
- The dataset included 1,574 strong-motion recordings from 118 subduction-zone earthquakes. ([[2015-latif|Latif, 2015, p. 21]])
- The study identified 118 subduction zone earthquakes with moment magnitudes ranging from 4.4 to 7.0 occurring between 2008 and 2013 in the south of Java Island. ([[2015-latif|Latif, 2015, p. 43]])
- The dataset includes 1,574 strong-motion recordings from two horizontal components for analysis. ([[2015-latif|Latif, 2015, p. 27]])
- The study utilizes three specific frequencies (1, 2, and 5 Hz) to determine response spectral acceleration (PSA). ([[2015-latif|Latif, 2015, p. 27]])
- Conversion of mb from ISC to NEIC was found to be practically equivalent using the relation: mb,ISC = 1.02mb,NEIC - 0.18. ([[2015-latif|Latif, 2015, p. 53]])
- The study identified 1,810 records from interface earthquakes and 1,338 from in-slab earthquakes within the dataset. ([[2015-latif|Latif, 2015, p. 61]])
- For interface events, the regression coefficients for PGA at 1 Hz were C1=-1.3866, C2=0.2466, C3=0.0046, C4=-0.0011, C5=2.6891, and C6=-0.6804. ([[2015-latif|Latif, 2015, p. 71]])
- For in-slab events, the regression coefficients for PGA at 1 Hz were C1=-2.6888, C2=0.5618, C3=-0.0024, C4=-0.0018, C5=-1.5296, and C6=-0.0297. ([[2015-latif|Latif, 2015, p. 71]])
- The standard deviation (sigma_logY) for PGA at 1 Hz in interface events was 0.5195, while it was 0.5683 for in-slab events. ([[2015-latif|Latif, 2015, p. 71]])
- The majority of average residuals were near zero for the distance range between 100 and 700 km from the fault, indicating good prediction accuracy at these distances. ([[2015-latif|Latif, 2015, p. 72]])
- Predicted PGA values using the new relations were significantly lower than those predicted by Youngs et al. (1997) but higher than those of Atkinson and Boore (2003). ([[2015-latif|Latif, 2015, p. 80]])
- The study successfully developed predictive relations for peak ground acceleration (PGA) and response spectral acceleration (PSA) at frequencies of 1, 2, and 5 Hz. ([[2015-latif|Latif, 2015, p. 21]])
- The dataset used for the models included 1,574 strong-motion recordings from 118 subduction-zone earthquakes. ([[2015-latif|Latif, 2015, p. 21]])

## Limitations

The study is a preliminary case study; it notes that more ground motion data from the Java subduction zone, especially from larger events, are needed to refine the models. Additionally, the use of the least square method instead of maximum likelihood may not fully resolve issues with data distribution.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Refine site amplification factors throughout the region.
- Test relations with a richer dataset from neighboring countries including more strong-motion recordings.
- Develop new seismic hazard maps for Java Island using these specific GMPEs.
- Revise the Indonesian earthquake resistant building code (SNI 1726-2012) with region-specific GMPEs.
