# Mapping Environmental Sensitivity in the Western Arabian Gulf using Geographic Information System (GIS) for Oil Spill Response

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the environmental sensitivity of the Western Arabian Gulf coast to oil spills by integrating GIS analysis with remote sensing and biotope mapping. It develops an Environmental Sensitivity Index (ESI) based on shoreline types, biological resources, and socio-economic features to identify priority areas for oil spill response planning.

---

## Research Problem

The Western Arabian Gulf contains sensitive ecosystems (coral reefs, seagrass beds, salt marshes, mangroves) and critical economic infrastructure. Oil spills from offshore platforms or human activities pose significant risks to these marine and terrestrial environments, requiring an accurate Environmental Sensitivity Index (ESI) for effective response planning.

- Define the index of environmental sensitivity from different parameters that are suitable for the Gulf, based on an Environmental Sensitivity Index (ESI) ranking method and analysis.
- Prepare thematic maps of priority sensitive areas for oil spill response planning in the Gulf.
- Develop an Environmental Sensitivity Index (ESI) map for the Western Arabian Gulf coastline.
- Identify and categorize shoreline types, biological resources,1 and socio-economic features using GIS.
- Create a priority ranking map to guide oil spill response strategies.
- Map the shoreline types in the Western Arabian Gulf using satellite imagery and GIS.
- Identify and map key biological resources (mangroves, salt marshes, seagrass beds, coral reefs).
- Identify socio-economic features that would be impacted by or are critical for oil spill response.
- Develop an Environmental Sensitivity Index (ESI) to prioritize protection areas.
- Map environmental sensitivity using GIS for oil spill response planning.
- Identify high-priority areas for protection based on shoreline type, biological resources, and socio-economic features.
- Develop a decision support system (DSS) framework by integrating ESI maps with oil spill modeling.
- Identify and map environmentally sensitive areas (ESAs) in the Western Arabian Gulf.
- Assess the impact of oil spills on coastal ecosystems like coral reefs, seagrass beds, and mangroves.

## Methods

The study used Landsat 7 ETM+ satellite imagery processed via ENVI 5.0 (dark object subtraction and radiometric correction). GIS analysis was performed in ArcGIS 10.2, involving the conversion of vector data to raster format with a 5m resolution for overlay analysis. Shoreline types were classified based on NOAA (2004) and IPICEA (2012) guidelines. Biological resources were identified using supervised and unsupervised classification. Socio-economic features were mapped as points and polygons from Google Maps and local data. A final ranking was produced by overlaying the three components (shoreline, biological, socio-economic) into a 3-priority system: 1st priority (high sensitivity), 2nd priority (medium), and 3rd priority (low).

## Data and Materials

The study uses remote sensing imagery for shoreline classification, biotope maps for biological resource identification, and point data/spatial data for socio-economic features. It includes specific locations such as Safaniyah, Khursaniyah-Jubail, Tarut Bay, and offshore islands (Karan, Kurayn, Jana, and Jurayd).

## Key Findings

- The study identified three main components for oil spill contingency planning: shoreline type, biological resources, and socio-economic features. ([[2016-sudibyo|Sudibyo, 2016, p. 18]])
- The resulting environmental sensitivity map includes a compilation of analyses about shoreline type, biological resources, and socio-economic features with different Environmental Sensitivity Index (ESI) scoring. ([[2016-sudibyo|Sudibyo, 2016, p. 14]])
- Based on the results, the first rated priority areas that should be protected in case of oil spills are: Safaniyah, Khursaniyah-Jubail, Tarut Bay and the offshore islands viz., Karan, Kurayn, Jana and Jurayd Islands. ([[2016-sudibyo|Sudibyo, 2016, p. 14]])
- The study identifies shoreline types as a critical factor for determining how oil sticks to habitats like mangrove stands or salt marshes. ([[2016-sudibyo|Sudibyo, 2016, p. 18]])
- GIS was used to integrate different spatial data with standard tools and symbols to identify and describe spatial associations and predict conditions. ([[2016-sudibyo|Sudibyo, 2016, p. 19]])
- The study area covers a coastline of approximately 452.209 km from Khafji to Dammam. ([[2016-sudibyo|Sudibyo, 2016, p. 38]])
- Tidal flats in the Gulf are characterized by a series of well-defined zones including mangroves, salt marshes, and sabkhas. ([[2016-sudibyo|Sudibyo, 2016, p. 28]])
- The study area is divided into 20 grids, each consisting of approximately 25x25 km square for assessment and zoning. ([[2016-sudibyo|Sudibyo, 2016, p. 40]])
- Shoreline types are categorized based on their sensitivity to oil spills and ease of clean-up. ([[2016-sudibyo|Sudibyo, 2016, p. 35]])
- Socio-economic features include resource extraction sites, management/protected areas, archaeological locations, and high-use recreational areas. ([[2016-sudibyo|Sudibyo, 2016, p. 31]])
- Sandy shoreline is the dominant type in the study area, accounting for approximately 49% of the total length. ([[2016-sudibyo|Sudibyo, 2016, p. 58]])
- Muddy-shore types account for 23% of the coastline, while rocky-shores represent 13%. ([[2016-sudibyo|Sudibyo, 2016, p. 58]])
- Vegetated shorelines (salt marshes and mangroves) constitute 8% of the area, and solid man-made structures account for 8%. ([[2016-sudibyo|Sudibyo, 2016, p. 58]])
- The study identified high sensitivity in areas from Safaniyah to Manifa due to dense mangrove and salt marsh growth. ([[2016-sudibyo|Sudibyo, 2016, p. 60]])
- High-sensitivity socio-economic features include oil and gas facilities, refineries, and fish landing sites across the Gulf. ([[2016-sudibyo|Sudibyo, 2016, p. 61]])

## Limitations

The study covers a large area (Khafji to Dammam) providing general information; it is not a full risk assessment analysis but rather an ESI mapping tool. It does not account for real-time oil spill tracking or specific local socio-economic changes.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Conduct more detailed studies on high-priority areas (Safaniyah, Khursaniyah-Jubail, Tarut Bay, and offshore islands).
- Improve remote sensing data analysis using band ratios with water correction to better identify submerged biological resources.
- Develop oil-spill response applications that integrate ESI maps with real-time modeling for field use.
- Assess the specific impact of oil on mangroves, salt-marshes, coral reefs, and seagrasses using ecological modeling.
