# Data integration in 3-D geostatistical porosity modeling of Hanifa Reservoir in Berri field, Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the impact of integrating seismic impedance and depositional facies data into 3D geostatistical models to improve porosity estimation in the Hanifa Reservoir, Berri field. It compares four modeling techniques—well-only, seismic-controlled (sGs_Cok), facies-based (sGs_FB), and fully integrated (sGs_Cok_FB)—concluding that data integration significantly improves accuracy, especially in low well-density areas.

---

## Research Problem

The primary challenge is accurately representing the spatial distribution of porosity in the Hanifa Reservoir, where well data alone are insufficient due to limited sampling density and lack of geological context. The study addresses how integrating multi-scale data (facies and seismic impedance) can improve 3-D geostatistical models.

## Objectives

- Evaluate the added value of integrating different data types such as facies and seismic impedance in 3-D geostatistical porosity models.
- Build four distinct porosity models: one based on well data only, and three using combinations of well logs, facies, and seismic impedance.
- Compare these models using qualitative and quantitative methods to determine the most accurate representation of reservoir properties.
- Evaluate different geostatistical modeling techniques for 3-D porosity estimation.
- Integrate seismic impedance as soft data to improve the spatial resolution of porosity models.
- Validate the accuracy of various modeling methods using a set of independent wells.

## Methods

The study utilized geostatistical workflows including Sequential Indicator Simulation (SIS) for facies modeling. Four porosity modeling methods were compared: 1) Sequential Gaussian Simulation (sGs) using only well logs; 2) Sequential Gaussian Simulation with Collocated Cokriging (sGs_Cok) integrating seismic impedance as soft data; 3) Sequential Gaussian Simulation Facies Based (sGs_FB) using a facies model to constrain porosity distribution; and 4) Sequential Gaussian Simulation with Collocated Cokriging Facies Based (sGs_Cok_FB), which integrated both seismic impedance and facies. Validation was performed by comparing input data histograms, visualizing cross-sections/slices, and calculating correlation coefficients between true and simulated porosity traces for ten wells.

## Data and Materials

The study area in the Hanifa Reservoir, Berri Field, included 97 wells (mixture of vertical, deviated, and horizontal). All wells had porosity logs; 22 wells provided impedance data derived from sonic and density logs. All wells were accompanied by facies logs prepared by Saudi Aramco geologists.

## Key Findings

- Facies-based porosity models yielded better definition of porosity in both vertical and lateral directions compared to other models due to the use of the facies model as a constraint for distribution. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 17]])
- The seismic controlled model showed higher accuracy than all other models because seismic data provides a more dense spatial sampling density than well logs. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 17]])
- The wells-only model had the lowest accuracy, highlighting the necessity of incorporating additional data types (facies and seismic) in porosity modeling. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 17]])
- The Hanifa Reservoir is characterized by a large-scale coarsening- and shallowing-upward carbonate platform sequence approximately 500 feet thick. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 35]])
- Of the eleven identified facies in the Hanifa Reservoir, nine are classified as reservoir facies (e.g., skeletal grainstones) and two are non-reservoir facies (mudstones). ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 36]])
- Porosity in the Hanifa Reservoir is primarily intergranular with minor moldic and intragranular components; moldic porosity is the only significant secondary porosity. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 36]])
- A strong inverse correlation between porosity and seismic impedance was established, with a correlation coefficient of -0.96, confirming that seismic impedance is a reliable indicator for porosity in the Hanifa Reservoir. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 48]])
- The study identified two distinct porosity distributions: higher mean values in zones with good reservoir facies (e.g., Zone 1) and skewed, lower mean values in mudstone-dominated zones (e.g., Zone 12). ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 41]])
- The inclusion of seismic impedance as soft data via Collocated Cokriging was found to be a more effective method for improving spatial resolution than well-only models. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 94]])
- Validation results showed that the accuracy of models based on wells only is highly dependent on sample density, with correlation coefficients ranging from 18% to 85%. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 75]])
- The model successfully captured a coarsening-upward sequence in the Hanifa Reservoir, where grainstone-rich facies (Groups 2 and 3) dominate the upper sections. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 68]])
- Porosity distribution is closely related to depositional facies; grainstone-rich facies correspond to high porosity while mudstones show very low porosity. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 118]])
- The sGs_Cok (seismic-controlled) model showed higher accuracy than the well-only model, particularly in areas with low well density. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 120]])
- The sGs_FB (facies-based) model provided better vertical layering compared to the sGs and sGs_Cok models due to the facies constraint. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 120]])
- The fully integrated model (sGs_Cok_FB) showed high accuracy but was slightly less than the seismic-controlled model because of potential errors in facies estimation. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 116]])

## Limitations

The accuracy of facies-based models is limited by the potential errors inherent in facies estimation; if a face is incorrectly identified, it leads to an inappropriate porosity distribution.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Use streamline simulation methods to evaluate which model best matches pressure and flow rates.
- Conduct 3D seismic surveys during early field development when wells are widely spaced.
- Utilize available seismic impedance data to condition porosity models in future work.
- Employ facies maps to improve the accuracy of facies models.
