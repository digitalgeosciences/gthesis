# ESTIMATION OF WATER SATURATION IN SAND DUNES USING SOURCE-RECEIVER OFFSET

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the use of source-receiver offset to estimate water saturation in sand dunes. By integrating Hertz-Mindlin and Gassmann models into a P-wave velocity depth function, the author developed an inversion method that successfully identified a dry sand dune with average water saturation of 0.1035.

---

## Research Problem

Determining partial water saturation (Sw < 1.0) in unconsolidated sediments like sand dunes is challenging using only P-wave velocity because the difference between high and full saturation is often less than the variation caused by other factors; however, utilizing source-receiver offset provides a more robust measurement for inversion.

## Objectives

- Develop a function that relates seismic P-wave velocity to water saturation $V_p(S_w)$ through the Digby's equation, Hertz-Mindlin equation, and Gassmann's fluid substitution models to calculate the offset (X) of the DDW.
- Estimate the water saturation at the maximum depths of rays corresponding to the offsets at which the DDW is received in the field.
- Compare the results to water saturation values measured in the lab in sand samples obtained from the study area at the depths where the water saturation was inverted.
- Perform sensitivity analysis to provide insights on how water saturation derived from my inversion is affected by changes in the mineralogical composition of the dunes, dune's porosity, and the sphericity of the sand grains.
- Develop an inversion procedure for determining the degree of saturation in a barchan dune.
- Establish a relationship between P-wave velocity, water saturation, and maximum depth of the direct diving wave (DDW).

## Methods

The study utilizes a rock physics-based approach where Hertz-Mindlin contact mechanics are used to model dry frame properties (bulk and shear moduli) and Gassmann’s equations are used for fluid substitution. A P-wave velocity depth function $V_p(z, S_w)$ was derived by combining these models with mineralogy data. The inversion process involves calculating the maximum penetration depth of diving direct waves (DDW) from observed source-receiver offsets using a specific geometric formula. The water saturation ($S_w$) is then determined by minimizing the normalized squared error (NSE) between the observed offset and the calculated offset derived from the $V_p$ function.

## Data and Materials

The study used data from a barchan dune in the eastern province of Saudi Arabia, including 10 sand samples collected at various depths. It utilized 68 total offset measurements from two seismic experiments (conventional and high-resolution). Mineralogical analysis was performed on the 10 sample sets.

## Key Findings

- The inverted water saturation values fell within the range of (0.0882 - 0.1514) with a mean of 0.1035, indicating a dry sand dune. ([[2025-oyekan|Oyekan, 2025, p. 12]])
- The mineral composition of the sand dunes in the study area consists of mainly quartz (83%), feldspar (4%), and carbonate minerals (3%). ([[2025-oyekan|Oyekan, 2025, p. 52]])
- A partially saturated zone was identified up to 7.6 m deep, mostly within the dune, while the deeper 2.4 m is a fully salt-water saturated zone within the sabkha. ([[2025-oyekan|Oyekan, 2025, p. 54]])
- The P-wave velocity of highly saturated sand (92% to 100%) increases by about 2.5 times compared to its value at 92% saturation due to the sharp increase in bulk modulus. ([[2025-oyekan|Oyekan, 2025, p. 47]])
- The sand density increases by approximately 20% as saturation increases from 0% to 99%, while the shear modulus remains unchanged. ([[2025-oyekan|Oyekan, 2025, p. 46]])
- The measured water saturation in the dune was found to be low, with values ranging from 0.0139 to 0.1120 and a mean of 0.0513. ([[2025-oyekan|Oyekan, 2025, p. 58]])
- Inverted water saturation values ranged from 0.0882 to 0.1514 with a mean of 0.1035, indicating low saturation consistent with the measured data. ([[2025-oyekan|Oyekan, 2025, p. 63]])
- Note: Both sets of values (measured and inverted) had means within 10% of each other. ([[2025-oyekan|Oyekan, 2025]])

## Limitations

The use of travel times for $S_w$ inversion is prone to errors due to subjective picking; however, using source-receiver offsets provides higher accuracy. The model assumes a constant porosity of 0.35 and specific sphericity factors.
