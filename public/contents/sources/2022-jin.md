# Prediction of Well Production Rates for Multiphase Flow Using Machine Learning Techniques

**Summary**: The thesis evaluates and optimizes machine learning models, specifically Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, to predict oil, gas, and water production rates in a multiphase flow system. It compares various algorithms like ANN, SVM, and Decision Trees against traditional methods and concludes that optimized LSTM models significantly outperform others in predicting well performance including shut-in periods.

---

## Research Problem

Traditional well production forecasting methods like numerical simulation are time-consuming, while others like decline curve analysis are oversimplified. There is a need for rapid and accurate proxy models to predict multiphase flow rates, especially during complex periods such as shut-in intervals.

## Objectives

- Compare different machine learning algorithms (ANN, Decision Tree, SVM, RNN, LSTM) for predicting oil, gas, and water production rates.
- Identify the impact of shut-in periods on well performance forecasting.
- Optimize the hyperparameters of the LSTM model (neuron count, activation functions, optimizers, batch size, etc.) to improve prediction accuracy.
- Develop a hybrid model combining LSTM and ANN to account for historical performance and daily operation conditions.
- Develop and optimize LSTM models to predict oil, gas, and water production rates.
- Perform hyperparameter tuning (hidden layers, neurons, activation functions, optimizers, batch sizes, and epochs) for the LSTM models.

## Methods

The study utilizes a dataset from the Volve field containing 880 days of production data. Data preprocessing included cleaning (removing missing values/irrelevant features) and correlation analysis (Pearson and Spearman) to select input features like cumulative hours, wellhead temperature, and choke size. Five models were compared: ANN, Decision Tree, SVM, RNN, and LSTM. A systematic hyperparameter optimization for the LSTM model was conducted by testing various neuron counts, activation functions (e.g., Sigmoid, Softmax, Softplus), optimizers (e.g., Adamax, RmsProp), and batch sizes to minimize the Relative Mean Absolute Error (RMAE).

## Data and Materials

Production data from 7 wells in the Volve field (North Sea) covering a period of 880 days (February 2014 to July 2016). The dataset included features such as cumulative hours, average downhole pressure, wellhead temperature, and choke size percentage.

## Key Findings

- LSTM outperformed other models for all three phases; oil RMAE was 17.51%, gas 17.31%, and water 18.86%. ([[2022-jin|Jin, 2022, p. 52]])
- The hybrid model (LSTM + ANN) improved prediction performance for oil and gas, reaching RMAE values of 12.08% and 12.14% respectively. ([[2022-jin|Jin, 2022, p. 15]])
- Optimal LSTM parameters were identified as: 41 neurons with Sigmoid activation for oil; 45 neurons with Softmax for gas; and 7 neurons with Softplus for water. ([[2022-jin|Jin, 2022, p. 59]])
- The optimal optimizer for the three models was determined to be Adamax (oil), Adam (gas), and RmsProp (water). ([[2022-jin|Jin, 2022, p. 61]])
- Optimal batch sizes were selected as 20 for oil, 18 for gas, and 6 for water. ([[2022-jin|Jin, 2022, p. 63]])
- Correlation analysis showed that oil and gas production rates have a very high correlation (close to 1.0), while water production has a weaker relationship with the other two phases. ([[2022-jin|Jin, 2022, p. 45]])
- LSTM performed best among the five compared machine learning algorithms (ANN, Decision Tree, SVM, RNN, and LSTM) in predicting three phases of well production rates. ([[2022-jin|Jin, 2022, p. 74]])
- The optimal LSTM model for oil prediction had an RMAE of 15.52%, while the gas model was 16.60% and the water model was 13.32%. ([[2022-jin|Jin, 2022, p. 75]])
- The hybrid model improved accuracy for oil production from 15.92% (LSTM) to 12.08% RMAE. ([[2022-jin|Jin, 2022, p. 73]])
- The hybrid model's accuracy for oil and gas was similar because most reservoir gas is solution gas. ([[2022-jin|Jin, 2022, p. 72]])

## Limitations

The study did not explore optimization algorithms like genetic algorithms or particle swarm. The dataset only included single well production data. The hybrid model still showed fluctuations (e.g., negative values) during shut-in periods due to the ANN component.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Future work could include using different optimization algorithms for hyperparameter tuning, implementing a modification layer with a ReLU function or decision trees to eliminate negative predictions in shut-in periods, and applying the hybrid model to different wells and reservoirs (transfer learning).
