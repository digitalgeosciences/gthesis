# Paleoecology and Paleoenvironmental Reconstruction of the Lower Silurian Qusaiba Shale in the Qasim Region

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the paleoecology and paleoenvironment of the Lower Silurian Qusaiba Formation in the Qasim Region using an integrated approach of foraminiferal morphogroup analysis and geochemistry. It identifies four distinct siliciclastic lithofacies and uses mineralogy, graptolite biozonation, and trace element ratios to reconstruct environmental conditions.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the need to characterize the paleoenvironmental conditions (detrital flux, redox state, and productivity) of the Lower Silurian Qusaiba Shale, a significant source rock in the Middle East, using integrated biostratigraphy and geochemistry.

## Objectives

- Investigate benthic foraminifera in the Gondwana region in the Lower Silurian Qusaiba formation.
- Reconstruct paleoenvironmental conditions using foraminiferal biofacies and geochemical proxies.
- Analyze lithofacies and sedimentology of the Qusaiba Shale.
- Identification of the foraminiferal species and biofacies in the gray-warm shale unit of the Qusaiba and Sharawra formations of the Qalibah Group in outcrop
- Analysis of foraminiferal morphotypes and inferred life style (i.e., epifaunal, shallow infaunal, deep infaunal) based on morphogroup interpretations.
- Determining the temporal succession of paleo-oxygenation based on major and trace elements.
- Reconstructing the paleoecology & paleoenvironment of the middle to upper Qusaiba Formation in the Qasim Region.
- Perform foraminiferal morphogroup analysis to interpret paleoecology
- Characterize foraminiferal wall textures to determine physical and environmental conditions
- Reconstruct the paleoenvironment of the Qusaiba shale during the Early Silurian
- Identify competition among opportunistic taxa in the succession
- Identify foraminiferal assemblages to determine faunal provinces and distribution.
- Establish graptolite biozonation for relative dating of the Lower Silurian succession.
- Quantify mineral content (clay minerals) via XRD to assess detrital input and weathering.
- Analyze major and trace elements via XRF to interpret paleoenvironmental proxies.
- Evaluate paleoredox, paleoproductivity, and sediment flux using specific geochemical ratios.
- Characterize the foraminiferal assemblages of the Qusaiba Formation.
- Reconstruct paleoenvironmental conditions using geochemical indicators (V/Cr, Co/Al, Fe/Al) and mineralogical data.
- Identify the impact of detrital influx on foraminiferal habitats.
- Identify and describe agglutinated foraminiferal assemblages within the Qusaiba Formation.
- Determine the stratigraphic range of specific foraminiferal species.
- Reconstruct paleoenvironmental conditions based on foraminiferal morphogroups and diversity.
- Identify and describe new species of foraminifera within the Qusaiba Shale.
- Determine the stratigraphic range and geographic distribution of identified taxa.
- Reconstruct the paleoenvironmental conditions of the Lower Silurian Qusaiba Formation.
- Identify and describe new species of agglutinated foraminifera from the Qusaiba Shale.
- Determine the stratigraphic range of various foraminiferal genera and species.
- Use foraminiferal biofacies as proxies for paleo-oxygenation and paleoenvironmental reconstruction.

## Methods

The study utilized field work for sedimentological and stratigraphical measurements across two sections (a reference section and a complementary section) near Old Qusaiba Village. Laboratory analysis included micropaleontological preparation involving sieving (63 μm), binocular microscopy, and stop-counting to identify foraminifera and graptolites. Geochemical characterization was performed using XRD (PANanalytical Empyrean) with glycol treatment to differentiate clay minerals, XRF (Energy Dispersive) with Omnian software for major/trace elements, and SEM-EDS (JSM 6610LV) to validate mineralogy.

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes samples from the Qusaiba Formation (Qusaiba Shale) in the Qasim Region. Specific counts include: 242 specimens of Amphitremoida citroniforma from 13 samples; 113 specimens of Stegnammina elongata from 12 samples; and various other species like Bathysiphon sp. 1 (26 specimens), Ceratammina sp. 1 (1 specimen), and others.

## Key Findings

- The first discovery of agglutinated foraminiferal assemblages in the Middle East, consisting of 24 genera and 77 species. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 13]])
- Lower part of the succession shows low detrital influx (quartz, Ti/Al, Zr/Al), high clay content, and high V/Cr, Co/Al, Fe/Al, P/Ti, Ba/Al ratios, indicating dysoxic to suboxic conditions with high paleoproductivity in an offshore environment. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 14]])
- The middle part of the section shows a high diversity of foraminiferal assemblages (monothalamids, tubothalamids) reflecting better paleo-oxygenation conditions. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 14]])
- The uppermost part features monothalamid assemblages with medium-grained wall texture, indicating oxic conditions in a lower shoreface paleoenvironment. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 14]])
- Lithofacies include Gray to dark gray Mudstone (Mdg), Light gray Mudstone (Mlg), and Alternation of greenish-gray mudstone and reddish siltstone (Mzr). ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 28]])
- The Qusaiba Formation is characterized as a pyritic, organic-rich 'hot shale' with gamma ray values exceeding 150 API. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 23]])
- The study identified four siliciclastic lithofacies in the Qusaiba outcrop: Mdg (gray to dark gray mudstone), Mlg (light gray mudstone), and others not fully detailed in text. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 44]])
- Lithofacies Mdg is composed of quartz (36–55%), clay minerals (30–48% dominated by kaolinite), and potassium feldspar (9–18%). ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 45]])
- Lithofacies Mlg contains higher concentrations of quartz (47–59%), clay minerals (21–37%), and K-feldspar (11–22%). ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 46]])
- Graptolite biozonation in the Qusaiba Formation was identified as belonging to the Rhuddanian–Telychian stages. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 27]])
- The presence of graptolites like Normalograptus scalaris and Pristiograptus reckardsi in lithofacies Mlg indicates a portion of the Lituograptus convolutus biozone. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 46]])
- Lithofacies Mzr contains 3-10% gypsum as confirmed by XRD analysis. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 48]])
- Lithofacies Mzr is characterized by high quartz content (48-64%) and kaolinite (10-30%). ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 48]])
- Foraminiferal assemblages from the upper Qusaiba and lower Sharawra formations consist exclusively of agglutinated foraminifera. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 54]])
- Monothalamous forms of Astrorhizida comprise 95.58% of the total assemblage. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 54]])

## Limitations

The lack of comparable foraminiferal studies in Gondwana limits the ability to assess the full stratigraphic utility of specific species from this section.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Conduct TOC, Th, and U analysis to complement foraminiferal and elemental data.
- Extend foraminiferal investigation to both outcrops and the subsurface to establish a Silurian foraminiferal biozonation in Saudi Arabia.
- Apply foraminiferal biofacies for biosteering purposes.
