# Assessment of hydrogeological parameters from thin-section study of middle cretaceous sandstones, Wasia Formation, Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis evaluates the use of petrographic techniques to predict hydrogeological parameters in the Wasia Formation, a Middle Cretaceous clastic aquifer in central Saudi Arabia. By comparing data from thin-section point counting (grain and pore size) with laboratory measurements (helium porosity and gas permeability), the study validates the accuracy of using microscopic analysis for characterizing clastic aquifers.

---

## Research Problem

The challenge of accurately determining hydrogeological parameters like hydraulic conductivity and intrinsic permeability in clastic rocks, where standard lab methods may be limited by sample fragility or high clay content. The study specifically addresses whether thin-section petrography can provide a reliable alternative for characterizing the Wasia Formation aquifer.

## Methods

The study utilized two primary workflows: 1) Petrographic analysis involving the preparation of 35 thin sections from field samples, followed by point counting to determine grain size distribution (GSD), pore size distribution (PSD), and mineral volume percentages. 2) Laboratory testing where core plugs were subjected to helium porosity measurements (using a Helium Porosimeter) and gas permeability tests (using a Gas Permeameter). Empirical equations (Hazen's rule, Younger's approximation, and others based on grain/pore size) were used to convert petrographic data into hydraulic conductivity and intrinsic permeability. Results from both methods were compared using Sigma Plot software.

## Data and Materials

Samples collected from the Wasia Formation outcrop sections in central Saudi Arabia (Al-Kharj area and Riyadh-Dammam highway). The study includes thin-section petrographic data, helium porosity measurements, and gas permeability measurements for selected sandstone samples.

## Key Findings

- The Transmissivity of the Wasia aquifer in the Al-Kharj area was reported by earlier studies (Sogreah, 1968) to range between 0.03 and 0.01 m2/sec. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 27]])
- Petrographic point-counting methods provide a viable shortcut for obtaining rapid estimates of hydrogeologic inputs (porosity, permeability) to aid groundwater development programs. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 17]])
- The study confirms that the Wasia Formation is a significant clastic unit in the Middle East, serving as both an important hydrocarbon reservoir and a prolific aquifer for fresh water supply. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 24]])
- Transmissivities for the Wasia aquifer ranged from 0.007 to 0.07 m2/sec in a study of 16 wells. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 28]])
- The Wasia formation is characterized by a cyclic succession of friable, reddish to brown, purple, strongly ferruginated sandstone with 'salt and pepper' texture. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 46]])
- A linear relationship exists between thin-section grain size (in phi units) and sieve size: D_phi = 0.32 + (0.95 * D_ts). ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 38]])
- The conversion equation for thin section measured in mm is M_sigma = 0.02 + (0.78 * M_ts). ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 38]])
- Younger's coefficients for determining hydraulic conductivity from thin-section derived porosity values are: A=1669, B=5.137 for fine; A=275, B=3.312 for medium; A=7.898, B=1.287 for coarse. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 37]])
- The average annual precipitation in the study area (Riyadh Station) ranges from 1.05 mm to 14.72 mm between 1964 and 1980. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 44]])
- Average monthly Pan Evaporation in the study area ranges from 68 mm to 528 mm. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 44]])
- The average effective porosity measured by the helium porosimeter was 0.35, while the saturation method yielded a mean of 0.33, resulting in a ~6% difference attributed to gas adsorption or incomplete liquid saturation. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 72]])
- The correlation between porosity measured by helium and that measured by the saturation method showed a moderate linear relationship (r^2 = 0.558, r = 0.75). ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 72]])
- Point counting of thin sections was used to determine grain size distribution and pore size distribution for the Wasia sandstone. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 63]])
- The study confirmed that petrographic techniques provide a viable alternative for evaluating hydrogeologic parameters in clastic aquifers when standard lab methods are limited by sample fragility or high clay content. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 66]])
- The average framework composition of the Wasia sandstone is 94.04% quartz, 4.42% feldspar, and 1.55% rock fragments including chert and hematite. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 80]])

## Limitations

The author notes that while petrographic methods provide quick estimates, further research is needed to define standard conditions for deriving hydrogeologic parameters from petrographic studies, and wider sampling is required to accurately assess the entire Wasia clastic aquifer.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Further research is needed to define the standard conditions for deriving hydrogeologic parameters from petrographic studies; a wider sampling is required to accurately assess estimates of such parameters for the entire Wasia clastic aquifer.
