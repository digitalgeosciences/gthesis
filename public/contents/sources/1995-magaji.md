# Assessment of hydrogeological parameters from thin-section study of middle cretaceous sandstones

**Summary**: The thesis evaluates the use of petrographic techniques to estimate hydrogeological parameters like porosity, permeability, and hydraulic conductivity in the Wasia Formation. By comparing thin-section derived data with laboratory measurements (helium porosimetry and gas permeametry), it validates the reliability of using point counting and grain/pore size analysis for aquifer characterization.

---

## Research Problem

Determining accurate hydrogeological parameters (transmissivity, storativity, and specific yield) in the Wasia aquifer is often costly due to the requirement of extensive pump testing. The study addresses how petrographic data from thin sections can serve as a viable proxy for these values.

## Objectives

- Assess the practical utilization of thin section analysis in hydrogeological studies by determining estimates of aquifer hydrologic parameters and comparing them with values obtained by conventional methods.
- Generate petrographic data from the studied sections of Wasia sandstone to assess natural recharge and the feasibility of artificial recharge or other uses.
- Evaluate the use of petrographic techniques to predict permeability and hydraulic conductivity from thin-section data.
- Determine porosity using both helium gas expansion and liquid saturation methods for comparison.
- Quantify grain size and pore size distribution through point counting in thin sections.
- Correlate laboratory-measured hydrogeological parameters with those derived from petrographic analysis to validate the methodology.

## Methods

The study involved field sampling of Wasia Formation outcrops, followed by core preparation (cutting into 4-5 cm x 2.5 cm plugs) and thin-section preparation (35 sections prepared, 28 used). Laboratory measurements included gas porosity via a Helium Porosimeter (Boyle's Law method), liquid porosity via the gravimetric/resaturation method using a saline solution (2000 ppm NaCl), and absolute permeability using both gas permeameters (Klinkenberg correction applied) and direct liquid flow measurement. Petrographic analysis involved point counting to determine mineral volume percentages, grain size distribution, and pore size distribution under a polarizing microscope.

## Data and Materials

40 large field samples were collected from the Wasia outcrop; 35 thin-sections were prepared (28 used for study); measurements included gas porosity, liquid porosity, gas permeability, and liquid permeability on core plugs; point counting was performed on a minimum of 400 to 530 points per section.

## Key Findings

- The study demonstrates that thin-section analysis can provide a viable shortcut to obtain rapid estimates of hydrogeologic parameters (hydraulic conductivity, transmissivity) by serving as an alternative to more expensive conventional methods. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 17]])
- Transmissivity values for the Wasia aquifer in the Eastern Province were reported in previous studies to range between 0.03 and 0.01 m2/sec (Note: likely a typo in source, intended as 0.01-0.3 or similar). ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 27]])
- The Wasia Formation is characterized by a cyclic succession of friable, reddish to brown, purple, strongly ferruginated sandstone with 'salt and pepper' texture. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 46]])
- Kaolinite beds in the formation range from a few centimeters to approximately 1.2 meters thick. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 48]])
- The study confirms that petrophysical parameters like hydraulic conductivity can be estimated from grain size and pore size distributions obtained via point-counting. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 36]])
- The average porosity measured by the helium porosimeter (mean value 0.35) was approximately 6% higher than that determined by the saturation method (mean value 0.33). ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 73]])
- A moderate linear correlation (r^2 = 0.558, r = 0.75) was found between gas and liquid porosity measurements. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 73]])
- The core samples were predominantly quartz arenites to subarkoses with an average framework composition of 94.04% quartz, 4.42% feldspar, and 1.55% rock fragments. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 81]])
- Note: The text mentions that the primary diagenetic products were compaction, cementation, quartz overgrowth, and detrital grain dissolution. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995]])

- Correlation coefficients between measured hydraulic conductivity and grain radius derived values were 0.98 in the medium to coarse range. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 104]])
- The average thin section porosity was determined to be approximately 30% (0.2955). ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 108]])

- Transmissivity derived from thin section porosity showed a percentage error of only 4.5% compared to values determined by Aramco north of Abqaiq. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 112]])

## Limitations

The author notes the limitations of the petrographic method, stating that further research is needed to define standard conditions for deriving hydrogeologic parameters from petrographic studies and a wider sampling is required to accurately assess estimates for the entire Wasia clastic aquifer.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Further research is needed to define standard conditions for deriving hydrogeologic parameters from petrographic studies; a wider sampling is required to accurately assess estimates of such parameters for the entire Wasia clastic aquifer.
