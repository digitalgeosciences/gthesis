# MODELING CLIMATE CHANGE IMPACT ON SEAWATER INTRUSION IN THE COASTAL AQUIFER OF THE EASTERN PROVINCE OF SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the impact of climate change, specifically sea level rise and altered precipitation patterns, on seawater intrusion in coastal aquifers in the Eastern Province of Saudi Arabia. Using numerical modeling (SEAWAT/MT3DMS), it evaluates three climate scenarios (SC1, SC2, SC3) and proposes mitigation strategies like freshwater injection to push back the saltwater-freshwater interface.

---

## Research Problem

The rapid growth of coastal populations and industrial activities leads to high pumping rates, while climate change causes sea level rise, both of which exacerbate seawater intrusion into the Eastern Province's coastal aquifers. There is a lack of specific research on how these factors interact in this region.

## Objectives

- Understand the impact of climate change on coastal aquifers in the Eastern Province of Saudi Arabia.
- Model the extent of saltwater encroachment under different climate change scenarios up to 2100.
- Propose management strategies to mitigate the impacts of seawater intrusion.
- Interpret the coastal aquifers of the Eastern Province of Saudi Arabia through developing the conceptual and numerical groundwater flow models.
- Study the movement of saltwater encroachment into the coastal aquifers in the study area by building a seawater intrusion model for the Eastern Province aquifer.
- Determine the climate change impacts (sea level rise and accurately calculate recharge rates) on seawater intrusion in the aquifer, using a density-dependent flow model.
- Test various scenarios of climate change impact using the developed model to produce actionable recommendations for the Eastern Province aquifer.
- Model the impact of different climate change scenarios on seawater intrusion in the coastal aquifer of the Eastern Province.
- Evaluate the extent of saltwater encroachment under various conditions (SC1, SC2, SC3).
- Assess the effectiveness of mitigation strategies, specifically freshwater injection wells, to manage and push back the saltwater-freshwater interface.
- To model the impact of climate change on seawater intrusion in the coastal aquifer of the Eastern Province.
- To evaluate the effects of sea-level rise and precipitation changes on groundwater salinity.

## Methods

The study utilizes a multi-step modeling approach: 1. Data collection from reports and maps; 2. Development of a conceptual model incorporating geology and boundary conditions (assuming homogeneity and 1:10 vertical/horizontal anisotropy); 3. Numerical modeling using MODFLOW for groundwater flow, MT3DMS for solute transport, and SEAWAT to integrate both into a density-dependent flow model (DDFM). The simulation includes four periods: steady state (1 year), historical development (1900-2023), and three future scenarios (SC1, SC2, SC3) up to 2100. Parameters include hydraulic conductivity (2.592 m/d), total porosity (0.3), specific storage (0.000011 m^-1), and specific yield (0.01).

## Data and Materials

The study uses data on three specific aquifers: Neogene, Alat, and Khobar. Parameters include transmissivity (e.g., 0.9 x 10^-2 m^2/s to 0.29 m^2/s for the Khobar aquifer), porosity (30-53% in some regions), and thickness (80m for Khobar). Simulation periods are modeled up to the year 2100.

## Key Findings

- The study identifies three climate change scenarios with sea level rise values of 0.58, 0.70, and 0.91m for SC1, SC2, and SC3 respectively. ([[2024-ahmad|Ahmad, 2024, p. 12]])
- Recharge rates in the climate models varied from 4.5 to 5.89 mm/yr depending on the model scenario. ([[2024-ahmad|Ahmad, 2024, p. 12]])
- The Khobar aquifer is characterized by fractured limestone with an 80m thickness and a depth of around 130m, with a transmissivity ranging from 0.9 x 10^-2 m^2/s to 0.29 m^2/s. ([[2024-ahmad|Ahmad, 2024, p. 20]])
- The average hydraulic conductivity for the Neogene and Dammam Aquifers is assumed at 3 x 10^-5 m^2/s for modeling purposes. ([[2024-ahmad|Ahmad, 2024, p. 20]])
- Implementation of management measures (reduced extraction and injection wells) showed a reduction in impacted saline area and shifted the interface back by 12.64% towards the shoreline. ([[2024-ahmad|Ahmad, 2024, p. 13]])
- The current extent of saltwater intrusion in the Eastern Province is approximately 980 meters from the shoreline. ([[2024-ahmad|Ahmad, 2024, p. 42]])
- In scenario SC1 (minimal impact), seawater encroachment reaches 1,400 meters horizontally, a 27% increase over current conditions. ([[2024-ahmad|Ahmad, 2024, p. 42]])
- In scenario SC2 (intermediate impact), seawater encroachment reaches 1,450 meters horizontally, a 32% increase over current conditions. ([[2024-ahmad|Ahmad, 2024, p. 42]])
- In scenario SC3 (maximum impact), seawater encroachment reaches 1,500 meters horizontally, a 37% increase over current conditions. ([[2024-ahmad|Ahmad, 2024, p. 42]])
- The average annual precipitation in the Eastern Province reached nearly 90 mm/year, with a recharge rate of approximately 4.5 mm/yr used for modeling. ([[2024-ahmad|Ahmad, 2024, p. 36]])
- Scenario SC1 showed a 27% increase in horizontal saltwater intrusion compared to current conditions. ([[2024-ahmad|Ahmad, 2024, p. 50]])
- Scenarios SC2 and SC3 showed further encroachment, with salt water reaching 32% and 37% (or 36% as per Table 8) more inland than current conditions. ([[2024-ahmad|Ahmad, 2024, p. 50]])
- Saltwater intrusion may reach up to 1,500 meters inland along the coastal line of the Eastern Province under certain scenarios. ([[2024-ahmad|Ahmad, 2024, p. 50]])
- The S2 injection well solution reduced the affected saline area in the aquifer to a level close to current conditions, with only a 3.7% increase. ([[2024-ahmad|Ahmad, 2024, p. 51]])
- The S1 injection well solution pushed back the saltwater-freshwater interface and reduced the current condition of saline-area by 12.64%. ([[2024-ahmad|Ahmad, 2024, p. 51]])

## Limitations

Assumptions include: constant pumping rates; constant lateral flow; current sea level as zero baseline; linear variation of sea level over time; and a homogeneous aquifer with a 1:10 vertical-to-horizontal anisotropy factor.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Engineering-based recommendations include the injection of fresh water into the aquifer or reducing the extraction of groundwater to push back the saltwater-freshwater interface. Management strategies should include promoting water conservation, optimizing irrigation practices, and investing in alternative water sources.
