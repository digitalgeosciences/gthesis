# MICROPALEONTOLOGY AND SEQUENCE STRATIGRAPHY OF MIDDLE JURASSIC D4-D5 MEMBERS OF DHRUMA FORMATION, CENTRAL SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the D4 and D5 members of the Middle Jurassic Dhruma Formation in Central Saudi Arabia using micropaleontology and sequence stratigraphy. It utilizes sedimentological data from outcrop descriptions and thin sections, combined with mineralogical (XRD) and elemental (XRF) analyses to characterize lithofacies and determine depositional environments.

---

## Research Problem

The Dhruma Formation's shale and marl units have not been extensively studied for micropaleontology, leading to a lack of high-resolution sequence stratigraphy for these facies. Most existing data are from subsurface cores rather than surface outcrops, and only thin sections have been used for microfossil studies instead of physical extraction.

## Methods

The study utilized a dual approach: sedimentological analysis and micropaleontological data. Sedimentology involved outcrop descriptions, thin-section petrography (using an optical microscope), XRD for mineralogy, XRF for elemental concentration, and SEM for high-magnification texture analysis. Micropaleontology employed two extraction methods based on sample type: soap-based disaggregation for muddy samples and acetic acid leaching for hard limestone samples. Samples were prepared as slabs or thin sections to determine lithofacies (Dunham classification) and microfacies.

## Data and Materials

Samples were collected from the D4 and D5 members of the Dhruma Formation in Central Saudi Arabia. The study includes analysis of 10 distinct lithofacies (e.g., Cgdp, Esp, Og, Bw-p, Fp-g, Em-w, Bog, Fw, Spgdp, Ms) and identification of numerous foraminifera species across multiple samples.

## Key Findings

- XRD results showed calcite as the major mineral element with no traces of dolomite in several lithofacies (e.g., Cgdp, Esp, Og). ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 10]])
- XRF data indicated a distribution of elements across various facies, with specific results for each of the 10 identified lithofacies. ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 10]])
- Ten distinct lithofacies were identified: Cgdp (Coral bearing grain dominated packstone), Esp (Echinoderm bearing spicule rich packstone), Og (Oolitic grainstone), Bw-p (Bioclastic wackestone/packstone), Fp-g (Foraminiferal packstone/grainstone), Em-w (Echinoderm rich mudstone/wackestone), Bog (Bioclastic oolitic grainstone), Fw (Foraminiferal wackestone), Spgdp (Spicule rich pelloidal grain dominated packstone), and Ms (Mudstone). ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 7]])
- The Dhruma Formation is part of the Jurassic Shaqra group, ranging from Bajocian to Bathonian in age. ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 1]])
- Foraminifera biozones were established based on first and last occurrences of species within the outcrop. ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 111]])
- The study identified 10 lithofacies in the outcrop, ranging from deep water muds to shallow water oolitic grainstones. ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 18]])
- 35 species belonging to 19 different genera were identified and documented for micropaleontology. ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 18]])
- The D5 member is interpreted as an open marine subtidal environment deposited in the middle portion of a vast ramp. ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016]])

- The Dhruma Formation contains calcareous nannofossils in the lower part (Ellipsageloshaera britannica zone) and various foraminifera zones including Haurania, Pfenderina trochoidea, and Praekurnubia crusei. ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 38]])
- The D5 unit was interpreted as a maximum flooding surface in the lower Bathonian, while the upper part of D5 is a regressive system tract. ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 40]])
- Three distinct sequence units were identified in the Dhruma Formation: Lower Dhruma (Bajocian), Dhruma Sequence B (Late Bajocian to Mid Bathonian), and Dhruma Sequence A (Late Bathonian to Early-mid Callovian). ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 42]])
- Ten distinct lithofacies were identified, including Cgdp (Coral bearing grain dominated packstone), Esp (Echinoderm bearing spicule rich packstone), and Og (Oolitic grainstone). ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 55]])
- The Cgdp facies shows a porosity of 5-10% with moldic, vuggy, and intragranular types. ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 57]])
- The Esp facies is characterized by mud content of 55 to 60% in thin sections. ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 60]])

## Limitations

The study noted limitations in the lower portion of the outcrop due to non-availability of ammonite data and poor recovery of microfossils (especially foraminifera) from highly friable and weathered units.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Extend the study to lower unstudied members of the Dhruma Formation.
- Conduct reservoir characterization studies to determine potential in the subsurface.
- Perform water depth calibrations for studied microfossils to determine exact depths for facies.
- Refine and use identified biozones for petroleum exploration (bio-steering and directional drilling).
