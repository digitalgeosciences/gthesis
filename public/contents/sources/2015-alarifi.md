# EVALUATION OF WETTABILITY ALTERATION IN SANDSTONE ROCKS BY DIELECTRIC MEASUREMENTS

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the impact of chelating agents (DTPA-K5) and different brine types (seawater, low salinity water) on the surface charge (zeta potential) and dielectric properties of sandstone rocks. It concludes that while zeta potential is a useful indicator of mineralogical changes and wettability, high-frequency dielectric measurements are primarily sensitive to bulk fluid properties rather than surface charge alterations.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the challenge of accurately monitoring wettability changes in sandstone reservoirs during chemical treatments (like chelating agent pre-flushes) using laboratory measurement techniques. Specifically, it evaluates whether zeta potential and high-frequency dielectric measurements can effectively distinguish between different fluid types and treatment effects on rock surfaces.

## Objectives

- Evaluate the wettability alteration of chelating agent and different brines on different sandstone rocks by measuring oil recovery after core flooding.
- Assess the effect of DTPA-K5 chelating agent on sandstone rock's ion content (mineralogy).
- Measure changes in zeta potential values of sandstone rocks with high and low salinity water due to mineralogy change.
- Propose the use of laboratory dielectric measurement setup as an evaluation technique, relating the effect of seawater, low salinity water, deionized (DI) water, and chelating agents on certain cations with dielectric and conductivity measurements.
- Evaluate the effectiveness of DTPA-K5 in enhancing oil recovery in Berea sandstone.
- Track changes in mineralogy of sandstone rocks due to conditioning with DTPA-K5 using ICP-MS.

## Methods

The study involved preparing three types of fluids: Arabian Gulf seawater, low salinity water (10x diluted seawater), and DI water. DTPA-K5 chelating agent was added at 5 wt% in seawater. Sandstone rocks (Berea, Bandera, Kentucky, Scioto) were crushed into powder and conditioned with the chelating agent for 2, 6, 12, and 24 hours. ICP-MS was used to analyze ion concentrations (Fe, Ca, Mg, Al) in the filtrate to track mineralogy changes. Zeta potential measurements were conducted using a Zetapals instrument to assess surface charge and wettability. Dielectric properties were measured using a Keysight 85070E Dielectric Probe Kit with a network analyzer across a frequency range of 500 MHz to 20 GHz. Coreflooding experiments were performed on Berea sandstone cores under high pressure (1000 psi) and temperature (100 C).

## Data and Materials

Four types of sandstone rocks: Berea (20% porosity, 100 mD permeability), Bandera (15%, 8 mD), Kentucky (10%, 0.01 mD), and Scioto (10%, 0.2 mD). Samples were prepared as powders for zeta potential and dielectric measurements. Fluids included De-ionized (DI) water, seawater, low salinity (LS) water, and DTPA-K5 chelating agent solutions.

## Key Findings

- Chelating agents (DTPA-K5) were shown to increase oil recovery by 30% in Berea sandstone cores due to the rock dissolution process. ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 28]])
- Zeta potential values for Berea, Scioto, and Kaolinite were highly negative in fresh water at pH greater than 6. ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 26]])
- Note: The study found that adding 1000 ppm of iron (Fe+3) to DI water changed the zeta potential from -12 mV to +6.5 mV, while DTPA-K5 was shown to have a higher potential in seizing Ferric ions (Fe+3) than other ions. ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015]])
- page_number_reference_not_found_in_text_but_contextual_on_page_28 ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015]])
- The addition of DTPA-K5 as a secondary recovery method increased oil recovery in Berea sandstone from 55.9% to 75.1%, an enhancement of 19.2%. ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 48]])
- Permeability in the Berea core increased by 10% (from 76 md to 83.6 md) after treatment with DTPA-K5. ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 48]])
- ICP-MS analysis showed that Fe+3 was significantly absorbed from the rock surface, reaching up to 100% absorption in Berea sandstone after 6 hours of conditioning. ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 55]])
- Al+3 concentration increased up to 350 ppm in the effluent during coreflooding, indicating its removal from the rock surface by the chelating agent. ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 50]])
- Zeta potential values for Berea sandstone became more negative after conditioning with DTPA-K5 (e.g., from -26.75 mV to -53.3 mV in DI water), indicating a shift toward a more water-wet state. ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 57]])
- The concentration of Mg+2 in the effluent increased by approximately 590 ppm after DTPA-K5 addition, confirming its removal from the rock mineralogy. ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 50]])
- Ca+2 concentration remained stable at 2,150 ppm after DTPA-K5 addition, suggesting that the chelating agent became saturated with Ca+2 ions from the rock. ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 50]])
- Dielectric measurements showed that the presence of ions significantly impacts conductivity readings rather than the relative dielectric constant (e'). ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 47]])
- The zeta potential of unconditioned Kentucky powder was already negative due to its low ferric ion content in its mineralogy. ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 73]])
- Pre-flushing with DTPA-K5 in seawater before a subsequent seawater flood resulted in more negative zeta potentials compared to direct seawater flooding (e.g., Berea moved from +11.67 to -16.95 mV). ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 73]])
- The addition of DTPA-K5 to any brine increased the imaginary dielectric constant (e'') and conductivity, while decreasing the real part (e'). ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 85]])

## Limitations

High-frequency dielectric measurements (above 500 MHz) are not suitable for observing changes in surface charge as they primarily measure bulk fluid properties.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Perform further coreflooding and effluent analysis to observe effects on mineralogy and oil recovery.
- Investigate Zeta potential changes due to mineralogy shifts more deeply.
- Relate Zeta potential measurements to other wettibility quantification techniques.
- Use dielectric laboratory measurements to investigate various ion exchange phenomena in reservoirs.
- Study the physics of high-frequency conductivity and relate it to resistivity or dielectric logging tools.
