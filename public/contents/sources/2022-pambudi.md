# IDENTIFICATION OF AQUIFER STORAGE AND RECOVERY POTENTIAL SITES IN CENTRAL – EAST REGION OF SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis identifies potential sites for Aquifer Storage and Recovery (ASR) in the central-east region of Saudi Arabia by integrating GIS, remote sensing, and Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA). It evaluates ten thematic layers—including aquifer system, geomorphology, soil texture, and groundwater salinity—to categorize ASR suitability into four levels: Good, Moderate, Low, and Very low.

---

## Research Problem

Rapidly increasing demand for water in arid regions like Saudi Arabia leads to significant groundwater depletion. There is a need for innovative management strategies, such as Aquifer Storage & Recovery (ASR), but selecting optimal sites for these projects is critical due to the complexity of hydrogeological and environmental factors.

## Objectives

- Identify potential sites for Aquifer Storage and Recovery (ASR) in the central-east region of Saudi Arabia.
- To help decision-makers and water planners to explore alternative sustainable ways for enhancing and managing the groundwater resources.
- Evaluate various parameters including geomorphology, soil texture, aquifer depth/thickness, salinity, and transmissivity to determine suitability for ASR.
- Perform proximity analysis to identify sites near water injection sources like desalination and sewage treatment plants.

## Methods

The study utilized a multi-criteria approach integrating GIS, remote sensing (RS), and Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA) using Weighted Overlay Analysis (WOA). Data sources included ASTER DEM (30m resolution) for geomorphology/lineaments, Sentinel-2 imagery for land use/cover, and various hydrogeological datasets from MEWA. Ten thematic layers were developed: Aquifer system, Geomorphology, Slope, Soil Textures, Lineaments, Land cover, Aquifer depth, Aquifer thickness, Aquifer transmissivity, and Groundwater salinity. Each layer was assigned a weight (totaling 100%) and features within each layer were ranked on a scale of 1-5 based on their suitability for ASR. These were combined using the WOA formula in ArcGIS 10.3 to produce a final suitability map.

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes spatial data for the central-east region of Saudi Arabia, including maps for: 1) Geomorphology (slope and lineament density), 2) Soil texture types, 3) Neogene aquifer depth/thickness, 4) Dammam aquifer depth/thickness, 5) UER aquifer depth/thickness, 6) Groundwater salinity levels, 7) Aquifer transmissivity values, and 8) Locations of desalination and sewage treatment plants.

## Key Findings

- The study identified four categories of ASR potential: Good, Moderate, Low, and Very low; 'Good' sites were prioritized based on proximity to desalination and sewage treatment plants. ([[2022-pambudi|Pambudi, 2022, p. 14]])
- The Saq Aquifer is characterized by high permeability and effective porosity from 12% to 30%, favoring infiltration and storage. ([[2022-pambudi|Pambudi, 2022, p. 67]])
- Geomorphology was classified into three groups: Najd Plateau, Eastern Plateau, and Coastal plain; the latter two were prioritized for recharge. ([[2022-pambudi|Pambudi, 2022, p. 46]])
- Lineament density map reclassified areas into 'close to lineaments' (high rank) and 'far from lineaments'. ([[2022-pambudi|Pambudi, 2022, p. 47]])
- Soil textures like Sandy, Sandy-Loamy, and Sandy-Skeletal were assigned high ranks due to coarse texture and high infiltration rates. ([[2022-pambudi|Pambudi, 2022, p. 61]])
- Slope values ranging from 0 to 15% were divided into five groups; lower slopes (0-2% and 2-4%) were more suitable for recharge. ([[2022-pambudi|Pambudi, 2022, p. 49]])
- Land cover analysis showed that 51% of the area was bare ground and 45% was scrub/shrub, both being favorable for ASR. ([[2022-pambudi|Pambudi, 2022, p. 50]])
- Neogene aquifer depth ranges from <4 to 30 m; Dammam aquifer depth varies from <9 to 80 m; UER aquifer depth ranges from <100 to 700 m. ([[2022-pambudi|Pambudi, 2022, p. 52]])
- Neogene aquifer transmissivity ranges from <1604 to 3455 m^2/day; Dammam aquifer transmissivity varies from <35 to 2497 m^2/day; UER aquifer transmissivity varies from <138 to 950 m^2/day. ([[2022-pambudi|Pambudi, 2022, p. 56]])
- Groundwater salinity was categorized into six levels, with values <1 g/L (fresh) and 1-3 g/L (slightly brackish) receiving the highest ranks. ([[2022-pambudi|Pambudi, 2022, p. 64]])
- The Eastern plateau (28% of the study area) and Coastal plain (12.5%) are considered suitable for groundwater recharge. ([[2022-pambudi|Pambudi, 2022, p. 68]])
- High lineament density, particularly in the Najd plateau area, is a good predictor of groundwater storage potential. ([[2022-pambudi|Pambudi, 2022, p. 68]])
- Sandy and sandy-loamy soil textures are the most appropriate for artificial recharge due to coarse texture and low clay content (covering 5% of the study area). ([[2022-pambudi|Pambudi, 2022, p. 68]])
