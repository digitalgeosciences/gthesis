# REMOVAL OF PHENOL AND CADMIUM FROM CONTAMINATED WATER BY POLYMERIC MODIFIED GRAPHENE NANOMATERIALS

**Summary**: The thesis investigated the removal of phenol and cadmium from contaminated water using two graphene-based polymeric nanomaterials: GPDADMAC (functionalized with polydially dimethyl ammonium chloride) and GPHM (functionalized with 2-hydroxyethyl methacrylate). The study concluded that both materials are effective adsorbents, with GPDADMAC showing high efficiency for phenol and GPHM showing significant capacity for cadmium.

---

## Research Problem

Phenol and cadmium are priority pollutants in water due to their toxic, carcinogenic, and persistent nature. Existing treatment methods like ozonation or ion exchange have limitations such as high costs, sludge production, or long processing times, necessitating the development of more efficient nanoadsorbents.

## Objectives

- Characterize the synthesized polymeric modified graphene nanoparticles before the adsorption experiment.
- Use the optimum treatment conditions to assess phenol and cadmium ions removal capacity of the synthesized GPDADMAC and GPHM in real groundwater.
- Optimize treatment conditions such as temperature, adsorbent dosage, initial adsorbate concentration, agitation speed, pH as well as contact time.
- Develop isotherms and evaluate adsorption kinetics under the optimum treatment conditions and compare with the adsorption with a standard activated carbon removal capacity.
- Investigate the regeneration ability of the adsorbents
- Synthesize and characterize graphene-based polymeric nanomaterials (GPDADMAC and GPHM).

## Methods

The study involved synthesizing graphene oxide via Hummer's method, followed by functionalization with polymers (GPDADMAC and GPHM). Characterization included SEM-EDX for morphology/composition, BET nitrogen physisorption for surface area/pore size, and FTIR for functional group identification. Batch adsorption experiments were conducted to evaluate the effects of pH, temperature, dosage, and time. Analytical measurements used UV-VIS spectrophotometry (271nm) for phenol and ICP-MS for cadmium. Kinetic models (pseudo-first order, pseudo-second order, intraparticle diffusion) and isotherm models (Langmuir, Freundlich, Temkin) were applied to analyze the data.

## Data and Materials

Two types of synthesized nanomaterials: GPDADMAC and GPHM. Analytical data included BET surface area measurements, SEM images, EDX spectra, FTIR spectra, and concentration measurements for phenol (up to 20 mg/L) and cadmium (up to 2 ppm). Real groundwater samples were analyzed for ions including Na, K, Mg, Ca, Cl, SO4, etc., with a conductivity of 3436 µS/cm.

## Key Findings

- GPDADMAC achieved approximately 80% (1.7 mg/g) removal of 10 ppm phenol within 10 minutes under optimal conditions (4 g/L dosage, 298 K, pH 6, 150 rpm). ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 15]])
- GPHM removed more than 60% (0.8 mg/g) of 0.5 ppm cadmium within 10 minutes using an adsorbent dosage of 20 mg, pH 7, agitation speed of 200 rpm, and temperature of 298 K. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 15]])
- Adsorption processes for both phenol (GPDADMAC) and cadmium (GPHM) were best described by the pseudo-second order kinetic model. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 15]])
- Langmuir and Temkin models fitted the phenol removal by GPDADMAC, while only the Temkin model fit the cadmium removal by GPHM. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 15]])
- GPHM can be reused for at least three cycles without significant reduction in removal ability, whereas GPDADMAC cannot be used for more than one cycle. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 16]])
- In real groundwater samples, adsorption efficiency dropped from 80% to 49% for phenol and from 60% to 48% for cadmium. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 16]])
- GPDADMAC exhibited a Type II isotherm and a Type H3 hysteresis loop, indicating a pore system of micropores, mesopores, and macropores. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 50]])
- GPDADMAC exhibited a BET surface area of 357.4 m²/g and a pore size distribution between 2-50 nm. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 51]])
- EDX analysis of GPDADMAC showed carbon (40.98%), nitrogen (6.35%), and oxygen (6.30%) as the main elements. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 52]])

## Limitations

The removal efficiency of both GPDADMAC and GPHM decreased significantly when tested in real groundwater compared to synthetic water (49% vs 80% for phenol; 48% vs 96% for cadmium), attributed to the presence of competing ions in the groundwater.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Conduct economic analysis to compare costs with other materials.
- Investigate different mixture ratios of polymeric material to graphene.
- Study simultaneous removal of phenol and cadmium.
- Explore adsorption capacity for other organic and trace metal pollutants.
