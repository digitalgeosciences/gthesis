# REMOVAL OF PHENOL AND CADMIUM FROM CONTAMINATED WATER BY POLYMERIC MODIFIED GRAPHENE NANOMATERIALS

**Summary**: The thesis investigated the removal of phenol and cadmium from contaminated water using two synthesized graphene-based polymeric nanomaterials: GPDADMAC (for phenol) and GPHM (for cadmium). The study concluded that both materials were successfully functionalized and showed significant adsorption capacities, with GPDADMAC achieving 80% removal of phenol and GPHM removing over 60% of cadmium under optimized conditions.

---

## Research Problem

Phenol and cadmium are toxic, carcinogenic, and persistent pollutants in water. Standard removal methods like coagulation, membrane filtration, and ion exchange have significant drawbacks (e.g., high cost, sludge production, or fouling). Graphene-based materials offer a solution but suffer from aggregation and weak affinity unless modified with polymers to improve stability and adsorption capacity.

## Methods

The study involved synthesizing two graphene-based nanomaterials: GPDADMAC (graphene functionalized with polydially dimethyl ammonium chloride) and GPHM (graphene functionalized with 2-hydroxyethyl methacrylate). Characterization was performed using Nitrogen physisorption (BET analysis), SEM, EDX, and FTIR. Adsorption experiments for phenol were conducted by varying agitation speed, temperature, dosage, initial concentration, and contact time. Kinetic models (pseudo-first order, pseudo-second order, and intra-particle diffusion) and isotherm models (Langmuir, Freundlich, and Temkin) were used to describe the adsorption process. For cadmium removal, parameters such as pH, agitation, temperature, dosage, and concentration were varied. Real groundwater was spiked with phenol and analyzed via spectrophotometry and ICP-MS.

## Data and Materials

Two types of synthesized graphene nanomaterials (GPDADMAC and GPHM). Experimental data included various concentrations of phenol (5, 10, 20 mg/L) and cadmium (0.05, 0.1, 0.5 ppm), multiple adsorbent dosages (up to 200 mg for GPDADMAC; up to 25 mg for GPHM), and a range of temperatures (288 K to 318 K). Real groundwater samples were analyzed for ions including Na, F, K, Cl, Mg, Br, Ca, NO3, and SO4.

## Key Findings

- GPDADMAC achieved approximately 80% removal of 10 mg/l phenol in 10 minutes under optimal conditions (4 g/L dosage, 298 K temperature, pH 6, and 150 rpm). ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 15]])
- GPHM removed more than 60% of 0.5 ppm cadmium within 10 minutes using an adsorbent dosage of 20 mg at pH 7, 200 rpm, and 298 K. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 15]])
- Adsorption processes for both phenol and cadmium were best described by the pseudo-second order kinetic model. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 15]])
- Langmuir and Temkin models fitted the phenol removal by GPDADMAC, while only the Temkin model fit the cadmium removal by GPHM. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 15]])
- GPHM could be reused for at least three cycles without significant reduction in removal ability. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 16]])
- GPDADMAC could not be used for more than one cycle, showing limited regeneration capability compared to GPHM. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 16]])
- In spiked groundwater samples, phenol removal efficiency dropped from 80% to 49%, and cadmium removal dropped from 60% to 48%. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 16]])
- Graphene-based materials are advantageous due to high specific surface area and easy synthesis from graphite via chemical oxidation, exfoliation, and reduction. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 23]])
- Polymer functionalization of graphene improves its dispersion stability and prevents aggregation into graphite, which enhances adsorption capacity. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 35]])
- Graphene oxide synthesized from amorphous graphite showed a significantly higher cadmium adsorption capacity (1792.6 mg/g) compared to that from flaky graphite (260.9 mg/g). ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 32]])
- A 3D sulfonated reduced graphene oxide achieved an adsorption capacity of 234.8 mg/g for cadmium at pH 6.0 and temperature 298 K. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 34]])
- Graphene oxide-based materials showed a higher adsorption capacity for cadmium (106.3 mg/g) compared to cobalt (68.2 mg/g). ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 33]])
- GPDADMAC exhibited a BET surface area of 357.4 m²/g, which is higher than that of GO and other similar nanoadsorbents like CNT. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 34]])
- The pore size distribution for GPDADMAC showed it possesses a microporous structure with a mean diameter of 51.25 Å, and a mesoporous structure (pore sizes between 2-50 nm). ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 34]])
- EDX analysis confirmed the successful functionalization of GO by PDADMAC through the detection of nitrogen (6.35% apparent concentration) and oxygen (6.30%). ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 35]])

## Limitations

The reduction in removal efficiency in real groundwater samples compared to synthetic water indicates the interference of other ions competing for binding sites.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Economic analysis to compare costs with other materials.
- Study the effect of different mixture ratios of polymeric material to graphene.
- Conduct studies on the simultaneous removal of phenol and cadmium.
- Explore adsorption capacity for other similar organic and trace metal pollutants.
