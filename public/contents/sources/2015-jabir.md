# HIGH-RESOLUTION 3D GEOCELLULAR FACIES AND PROPERTY MODELING OF BENTIU-1 RESERVOIR INTERVAL, JACK OILFIELD, FULA SUB-BASIN, SUDAN

**Summary**: The thesis presents a high-resolution 3D geocellular modeling of the Bentiu-1 reservoir interval (Upper Bentiu Member) in Sudan's Muglad Rift Basin. It integrates lithofacies and petrophysical properties (porosity, permeability, water saturation) using stochastic simulation techniques like SIS and SGS to characterize complex structural and stratigraphic settings.

---

## Research Problem

The heterogeneity of lithofacies coupled with complex stratigraphic settings from rifting/subsidence processes in the Bentu-1 reservoir leads to highly variable petrophysical properties, necessitating a detailed 3D geocellular model for better understanding and prediction of reservoir behavior.

## Methods

The study follows a three-part workflow: (1) Structural Analysis: A 3-D structural model was built from 3D seismic data integrated with well logs to define faults, horizons, and isochores. (2) Facies Classification and Petrophysical Analysis: Lithofacies were identified using 'cluster analysis' in Interactive Petrophysics (IP) software and validated against core/log reports. Petrophysical properties were derived from logs; shale content was calculated via the Larinove equation, porosity from density/neutron/sonic logs, water saturation via the Indonesia Equation, and permeability through a porosity-permeability relationship. (3) Geostatistics: Data underwent descriptive statistics (histograms, PDF, CDF) and transformations (logarithmic for permeability, normal score for porosity/saturation). A 3D grid was constructed from seismic volumes, and properties were upscaled. Modeling included ordinary kriging for property estimation and Sequential Indicator Simulation (SIS) for lithofacies, followed by Sequential Gaussian Simulation (SGS) for petrophysical parameters.

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes a 3-D post-stack migrated seismic cube (181 Inlines x 261 Xlines), well logs (GR, DT, mud logging/master log), and core data to characterize the Bentiu-1 interval. The average thickness of the studied interval is approximately 170m.

## Key Findings

- Lithofacies classification revealed three distinct lithofacies: sand, shaly-sand, and shale. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 13]])
- Petrophysical analysis of porosity, permeability, and water saturation showed these properties occur over specific zones correlated with identified lithofacies. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 13]])
- Four locally fining upward lithological cycles were observed from the 3-D stochastic simulation of the lithofacies, each dominated by sand at the bottom and capped with shale at the top. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 13]])
- Four petrophysical units were firmly identified by the presence of three marker-beds characterized by low porosity, low permeability, and high water saturation (shale caps). ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 13]])
- The Bentiu-1 reservoir interval is characterized by gravel sandstone and sandstone facies, with thicknesses ranging between 90 m and 250 m across more than ten productive zones. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 16]])
- Lithofacies analysis identified three primary types: sandstone, silty sand, and clay (shale). ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 15]])
- Petrophysical analysis confirmed that porosity, permeability, and water saturation are distributed in specific ranges corresponding to the defined lithofacies. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 15]])
- Four distinct rock cycles were identified, where lower sections are dominated by sandstone and upper sections are capped by clay (shale). ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 15]])
- Petrophysical models confirmed the lithofacies modeling results, identifying four petrophysical units with three distinct layers characterized by low porosity/permeability and high water saturation in the upper sections. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 15]])
- The Bentiu Formation consists of thick sandstone sequences with a maximum thickness of approximately 5000 feet, showing good reservoir qualities. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 35]])
- The Abo Gabra Formation (source rock) has a burial depth exceeding 5000 meters with maximum Ro values exceeding 1.3%. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 43]])
- Reservoir zones in the Abo Gabra Formation exhibit porosity ranging from 10% to 28% and permeability from 2.325 mD to 3 Darcy. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 44]])
- The Bentiu Formation reservoir zone features individual beds of 8 to 10 meters thickness, with porosity values ranging from 20% to 35% and permeability between 100 mD and 5 Darcy. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 44]])
- The first rifting phase (Sharaf and Abo Gabra Formations) occurred approximately 140 to 95 Ma in the Early Cretaceous. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 41]])
- The second rifting phase took place in the Late Cretaceous between 95-65 Ma. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 41]])

## Limitations

The study noted that permeability was calculated based on a pre-defined relationship from core reports, which might over/underestimate values as it is only a function of porosity. Additionally, lack of core coverage necessitated the use of electro-facies for log interpretation.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Acquire more core data to reduce uncertainty in permeability calculations.
- Subdivide vertical intervals into stratigraphic subzones to better account for periodic behavior in semivariograms.
- Utilize co-kriging to link lithofacies and petrophysical parameters to seismic attributes like acoustic impedance.
- Implement Object-Based Modeling (OBM) or Multi-Point Geostatistics (MPS) with training images from similar depositional environments.
