# Regional Sequence Stratigraphic Model of the Miocene Dam Formation and Its Impact on Lithofacies Architecture and Heterogeneity, An Outcrop Approach, Eastern Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis develops a regional sequence stratigraphic model for the Miocene Dam Formation in eastern Saudi Arabia using an outcrop approach. It identifies 17 sedimentary lithofacies and characterizes depositional environments ranging from mid-ramp to inner ramp, highlighting the impact of clastic input on lithofacies heterogeneity.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of a regional stratigraphic study for the Miocene Dam Formation outcrops in the eastern province of Saudi Arabia, which limits understanding of its lithofacies architecture and heterogeneity during the Middle Miocene tectonic period.

## Objectives

- To characterize the lithofacies and paleoenvironments and depositional settings.
- To establish regional high resolution sequence stratigraphic model for Dam Formation.
- To determine and evaluate the impact on lithofacies heterogeneity, quality and architecture.
- Test the chemostratigraphic response in mixed carbonate-siliciclastic sequence using SGR and XRF.
- Present a new regional sedimentologic and stratigraphic architectural framework for the Dam Formation
- Define sequence hierarchies and stacking patterns within the formation

## Methods

The study utilized a regional traverse of five outcrop locations (Lidam, Um Rabiyah, Hanith, ElSarrar, and ElNuairiyah). Field methods included logging stratigraphic sections to identify lithofacies based on texture, color, fossils, and sedimentary structures. Laboratory analysis involved thin-section petrography for microfacies identification, XRD for mineralogical investigation (identifying dolomite and gypsum), SEM for detailed mineralogy/texture, and XRF for major/minor element analysis. Spectral Gamma Ray (SGR) was used to measure K, U, and Th concentrations to evaluate chemostratigraphic responses in mixed carbonate-siliciclastic sequences.

## Data and Materials

144 samples for geochemical analysis; 5 distinct outcrop locations (Lidam, Um Rabiyah, Hanith, ElSarrar, ElNuairiyah) with varying thicknesses of the Dam Formation (ranging from 15m to 65m); detailed lithofacies descriptions and SGR profiles.

## Key Findings

- Seventeen lithofacies were identified, ranging from mid-ramp to inner ramp settings (shoal, lagoon, and peritidal zones), tidal, and estuarine channels. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 15]])
- The TST is characterized by quartz skeletal wackestone with dasycladean algae, microbial/skeletal banks, and tidal/estuarine channels (including channelized cross-bedded calcareous sandstone). ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 16]])
- The HST is dominated by the presence of quartz oolitic and skeletal grainstone channels. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 16]])
- Clastic content increases toward the North-West direction from Lidam, Um Rabiyah, Hanith, ElSarrar to ElNuairiyah area. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 16]])
- XRD analysis of lithofacies D1 (Planar cross-bedded skeletal packstone) confirmed it is mainly composed of calcite and quartz minerals. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 53]])
- Lithofacies D8 (Evaporites-Carbonates Interbedding) contains gypsum-filled desiccation cracks, indicating arid depositional conditions in supratidal sabkha settings. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 62]])
- XRD analysis of lithofacies D10 (Planar Cross-Bedded Quartz Peloidal Skeletal Grainstone) showed it is composed mainly of dolomite and quartz. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 66]])
- The Dam Formation was divided into three composite sequences in Lidam, Um Rabiyah, and ElNuairiyah; four in Hanith; and five in ElSarrar. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 12]])
- The Dam Formation was deposited on an inner-ramp and mid-ramp, characterized by shoal, lagoon, and tidal flat sediments. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 79]])
- Lithofacies diversity includes 17 distinct types, including oolitic grainstones (D12), cross-bedded skeletal sandstones (D14), and massive sandstones (D15). ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 70]])
- The clastic content increases toward the northwest; Lidam is dominated by carbonate lithofacies (71.2%), while ElNuairiyah has higher siliciclastic content. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 81]])
- SGR analysis identified two types of sequence boundaries: Type I (wide incisions/subaerial exposure) and Type II (no incision, transition to tidal flat). ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 123]])
- High U concentrations in the Dam Formation are associated with organic-rich lithofacies like stromatolites or mudstones. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 94]])
- The formation is characterized by a prograding, shoaling-upward architecture across three to four composite sequences depending on the location. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 123]])
- Lithofacies D12 (oolitic grainstone) indicates high energy environments with constant wave agitation. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 80]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Expand study area to NW and SE directions of the Dam Formation.
- Study siliciclastic Hadrukh and Hofuf Formations to complete the Miocene sequence.
- Build Burdigilian species distribution models for better correlation.
- Construct regional petrophysical controls and use isotope analysis for higher resolution stratigraphy.
- Acquire LiDAR images for improved geometry modeling.
