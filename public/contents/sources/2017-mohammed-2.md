# Regional Sequence Stratigraphic Model of the Miocene Dam Formation and Its Impact on Lithofacies Architecture and Heterogeneity, An Outcrop Approach, Eastern Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis develops a regional sequence stratigraphic model for the Miocene Dam Formation in Eastern Saudi Arabia by integrating lithofacies analysis, spectral gamma-ray (SGR) data, and geochemical signatures. It identifies distinct system tracts and sequence boundaries across five outcrop areas to evaluate their impact on lithofacies heterogeneity.

---

## Research Problem

The high degree of lateral and vertical heterogeneity in carbonate reservoirs like the Dam Formation is difficult to characterize from subsurface data alone, necessitating an outcrop-based approach to understand facies distribution and architectural elements.

## Objectives

- Present a new regional sedimentologic and stratigraphic architectural framework for the Dam Formation
- Evaluate the impact of this framework on lithofacies heterogeneity and architecture
- Define sequence hierarchies and stacking patterns
- Characterize depositional models, paleoenvironments, and formation lithofacies
- Test Spectral Gamma Ray (SGR) and chemostratigraphic response in mixed carbonate-siliciclastic sequences
- To characterize the lithofacies and paleoenvironments and depositional settings.
- To establish regional high resolution sequence stratigraphic model for Dam Formation.
- To determine and evaluate the impact on lithofacies heterogeneity, quality and architecture.
- Test the chemostratigraphic response in mixed carbonate-siliclastic sequence using SGR and XRF.
- Define sequence hierarchies and stacking patterns within the formation
- Define sequence hierarchies and stacking patterns in the Dam Formation

## Methods

The study involved field work across five outcrops (Lidam, Um Rabiyah, Hanith, ElSarrar, and ElNuairiyah) to perform sedimentological analysis including lithology, texture, rock color, fossils, sedimentary structures, diagenetic features, bedding, biogenic features, macro fossils, and paleocurrent measurements. Stratigraphic analysis included profiling sections and developing high-frequency sequences. Spectral Gamma Ray (SGR) was used to measure K, U, and Th radioactivities at a 2-minute sampling rate. Laboratory work included slabbing samples for sedimentological description, preparing thin sections for microfacies analysis, mineralogy, and grain size analysis using Alizarin Red staining, X-ray diffraction (XRD), Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM), and X-ray fluorescence (XRF).

## Data and Materials

Data from five outcrop locations: Lidam, Um Rabiyah, Hanith, ElSarrar, and ElNuairiyah; 17 identified lithofacies; mineralogical data from XRD/SEM; chemical data from XRF; SGR logs for each location.

## Key Findings

- Seventeen distinct lithofacies were identified across the regional traverse (D1-D17). ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 15]])
- Lithofacies were interpreted as mid-ramp to inner ramp settings, including shoal, lagoon, and peritidal zones. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 16]])
- No outer ramp depositional setting was identified within the studied outcrops. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 16]])
- The TST is characterized by quartz skeletal wackestone with dasycladean algae, microbial/skeletal banks, and tidal/estuarine channels. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 16]])
- The HST is dominated by the presence of quartz oolitic and skeletal grainstones and channels. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 16]])
- Clastic content increases toward the North-West direction from Lidam to ElNuairiyah. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 16]])
- XRF data showed a fair to good coefficient of determination between SGR (K%) and XRF (K2O%). ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 11]])
- The Dam Formation is characterized by a variety of lithofacies including packstones, wackestones, and limestone conglomerates, with some units showing significant clastic content. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 43]])
- Lithofacies D1 (Planar cross-bedded quartz skeletal intraclastic packstone) is 60-90 cm thick and contains bivalves, gastropods, and ooids with varying degrees of boring. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 44]])
- Lithofacies D2 (Quartz intraclastic peloidal wackestone) is 20-50 cm thick and contains approximately 15-20% detrital quartz grains. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 44]])
- Lithofacies D3 (Intraformational limestone conglomerate) features rounded-subrounded pebbles and boulders, indicating a high-energy environment. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 45]])
- The Dam Formation represents a regional Burdigilian transgressive event, with carbonates at its base correlated to MFS Ng20. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 37]])
- Lithofacies D4 (Quartz skeletal intraclastic packstone) is characterized by 20% sand content and indicates inner ramp to midramp environments. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 52]])
- Lithofacies D5 (Quartz skeletal peloidal wackestone with dasyclads) suggests deposition in open inner ramp to lagoonal settings. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 52]])
- XRD analysis of lithofacies D10 confirmed a composition mainly of dolomite and quartz. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 66]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Expand study area to cover NW and SE directions of the Dam Formation.
- Study siliciclastic Hadrukh and Hofuf Formations to complete the Miocene sequence.
- Build Burdigilian species distribution models for higher correlation confidence.
- Construct regional petrophysical controls for the Dam Formation.
- Apply isotope analyses for better prediction of paleoenvironments and higher resolution stratigraphy.
- Acquire LiDAR images for improved geometry modeling.
- Construct a regional structural framework for fracture systems.
- Correlate facies and stacking patterns with subsurface data.
