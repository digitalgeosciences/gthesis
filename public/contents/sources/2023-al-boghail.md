# CHARACTERIZATION OF NMR DIFFUSION COUPLING PHENOMENA IN CARBONATE FORMATIONS UNDER THE PRESENCE OF MULTIPLE RESERVOIR FLUIDS

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the 'diffusion coupling' phenomenon in carbonate formations, where the movement of fluid molecules between different pore bodies leads to inaccurate petrophysical interpretations. Using a combination of Silica Gel samples, NMR simulations, and field core plugs, the study evaluates how pore structure, fluid diffusion coefficients, and bulk relaxation impact this effect.

---

## Research Problem

In interconnected dual-porosity systems, 'diffusion coupling' can cause NMR T2 measurements to reflect an average relaxation time rather than individual pore bodies. This leads to inaccurate petrophysical evaluations (e.g., permeability and irreducible water saturation) when multiple fluids are present, as standard interpretation assumes T2 is solely proportional to surface relaxivity.

## Objectives

- Investigate the effect of NMR fluid properties (diffusion coefficient & bulk relaxation) on diffusion coupling in both controlled medium (synthesized gel & simulation) and core plug samples.
- Evaluate diffusion coupling under the presence of multiple reservoir fluids.
- Evaluate the impact of pore structure and grain size on the observation of diffusion coupling.
- Validate findings from controlled samples and simulations using field core plug samples.

## Methods

The study employs a dual-track approach: 1. Controlled experiments using synthetic Silica Gel samples with varying grain sizes and pore sizes to isolate the effects of geometry. 2. Core analysis on carbonate rock plugs (1.5 inch diameter) obtained from Saudi Aramco. Techniques include NMR T2 measurements, 2D T2 measurement for diffusion tracking, Pulsed Field Gradient (PFG) for diffusion coefficients, and Micro-CT/MICP to characterize pore structure. Fluid properties like viscosity were measured using an Anton Paar SVM 3001 Stabinger viscometer.

## Data and Materials

The study used multiple synthetic Silica Gel samples (6 types with varying grain sizes from 20-3000 µm and pore sizes of 60-500 Ång). It also utilized 10 carbonate core plug samples (1.5 inch diameter) with measured porosities ranging from 11% to 17% and permeabilities from 0.13 to 117 mD. Fluids included deionized water, saline water, hydrofluoric acids (5%, 10%, 15%), and four crude oil samples (Extra Light, Light, Medium, Heavy) plus Diesel.

## Key Findings

- Lower contrast between the two pore bodies resulted in stronger diffusion coupling. ([[2023-al-boghail|AL-BOGHAIL, 2023, p. 24]])
- Both NMR fluid properties (bulk relaxation and diffusion coefficient) have significant impact on diffusion coupling; specifically, higher diffusion coefficients are positively correlated with coupling strength. ([[2023-al-boghail|AL-BOGHAIL, 2023, p. 24]])
- Faster bulk relaxation reduces the strength of diffusion coupling. ([[2023-al-boghail|AL-BOGHAIL, 2023, p. 24]])
- Diffusion coupling strength in single-phase saturation may be completely different than in multi-phase due to the significance of wettibility effects. ([[2023-al-boghail|AL-BOGHAIL, 2023, p. 24]])
- Sample 10 showed a high degree of diffusion coupling, with 2D T2 showing strong exchange between protons and MICP indicating porosity bimodality not captured in standard T2. ([[2023-al-boghail|AL-BOGHAIL, 2023, p. 84]])
- The study confirmed that the inclusion of fluid properties (diffusion coefficient and bulk relaxation) is critical for accurate petrophysical interpretation in dual-porosity systems. ([[2023-al-boghail|AL-BOGHAIL, 2023, p. 24]])
- Diffusion coupling is more likely to be observed when there is lower contrast between macro and micro pore systems. ([[2023-al-boghail|AL-BOGHAIL, 2023, p. 49]])
- The relationship between the diffusion coefficient and diffusion coupling strength is non-linear; higher values lead to stronger coupling, but only after a specific threshold. ([[2023-al-boghail|AL-BOGHAIL, 2023, p. 97]])
- A faster fluid's free bulk relaxation (e.g., due to wettability) allows for the observation of diffusion coupling at lower diffusion coefficient values. ([[2023-al-boghail|AL-BOGHAIL, 2023, p. 98]])
- 15% HCl showed significantly stronger diffusion coupling than water, even when both were in the same order of magnitude for diffusion coefficients. ([[2023-al-boghail|AL-BOGHAIL, 2023, p. 55]])
- Sample 2 and Sample 4 from field cores demonstrated high degrees of diffusion coupling as evidenced by single peaks in T2 measurements despite MICP showing bimodal porosity. ([[2023-al-boghail|AL-BOGHAIL, 2023, p. 106]])

## Limitations

Standard T2 interpretation assumes a single pore system; however, this assumption fails in interconnected bimodal systems where diffusion coupling occurs. Additionally, some techniques (like certain wettibility models) are not functional for field evaluation with oil-based mud.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Any study on diffusion coupling must take both the fluid's diffusion coefficient and its bulk relaxation into consideration as they are interdependent.
