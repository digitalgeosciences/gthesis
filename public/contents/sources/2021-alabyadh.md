# MICROFACIES AND DIAGENESIS OF LATE JURASSIC - EARLY CRETACEOUS INNER SHELF - OOLITIC SHOAL DEPOSITS OF MONTE SACRO SEQUENCE (GARGANO PROMONTORY, ITALY)

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the microfacies and diagenetic evolution of Late Jurassic to Early Cretaceous carbonate rocks in the Monte Sacro Sequence, specifically within the Gargano Promontory of Italy. It utilizes petrographic analysis, XRD, and QEMSCAN to characterize oolitic shoal deposits and identify various diagenetic stages including dolomitization and dedolomitization.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the characterization of carbonate rock microfacies and the identification of complex diagenetic processes (such as dolomitization, dedolomitization, and cementation) in a Mesozoic carbonate succession transitioning from inner shelf to platform margin.

## Objectives

- Characterize the microfacies of oolitic shoal deposits in the Monte Sacro Sequence.
- Identify and interpret diagenetic features including microbial micritization, dolomitization, and dedolomitization.
- Determine the paragenetic sequence of carbonate minerals.
- Estimate porosity based on different mineralogical components.
- Identify, describe, and interpret different lithofacies along the transition between Sannicandro and Monte Spigno formations
- Use thin section petrographic analysis to identify microfacies
- Integrate mineralogical and geochemical data to explain the effects of diagenetic and depositional processes on various lithofacies
- Construct a paragenetic sequence to reveal the timing of each diagenetic process
- Characterize the porosity modification processes in the facies
- Construct a lithostratigraphic column for the study area.
- Characterize microfacies and diagenetic processes using petrographic studies (thin sections), XRD, and SEM.
- Determine the mineralogical composition of carbonate rocks.
- Quantify grain size distribution and 2-D porosity of ooids.
- Establish a geological model linking diagenetic processes to reservoir properties like porosity and permeability.
- Identify and characterize microfacies within the Costa Callese stratigraphic section.
- Analyze the carbonate factory and modes of precipitation for various components.
- Quantify ooid grain size distribution and morphology.
- Investigate diagenetic features including microbial micritization, cementation, and dedolomitization.
- Determine the impact of diagenetic phases on porosity.
- Identify and classify microfacies within the Costa Callese stratigraphic section.
- Determine the mineralogical composition of carbonates using XRD and QEMSCAN.
- Analyze the diagenetic history (early, middle, and late stages) of the carbonate sequence.
- Evaluate the impact of diagenesis on porosity and reservoir quality.

## Methods

Fieldwork was conducted in July–August 2018 at the Costa Callese locality. Petrographic analysis involved preparing 76 thin sections (polished and non-polished) for examination under PPL and XPL using an Axio Scan Slide Scanner. XRD (Empyrean, Malvern Panalytical) was used on 76 powdered samples to identify minerals like calcite, dolomite, and anhydrite. QEMSCAN was performed on 7 thin sections for mineralogical mapping and porosity estimation. ImageJ software was utilized for ooid grain size distribution analysis and 2-D porosity calculation from thin sections. R software was employed for statistical analysis of the grain size data.

## Data and Materials

The study analyzes carbonate rocks from the Monte Sacro sequence in the Gargano Promontory, Italy. Specific units include the Sannicandro Formation (inner shelf) and the Monte Spigno Formation (oolitic shoal). The transition zone between these units extends approximately 40 km.

## Key Findings

- The study identified several distinct microfacies, including oolitic grainstones (MF9, MF10), peloidal packstones (MF1, MF2, MF4, MF5), and dasyclad algae grainstones (MF7). ([[2021-alabyadh|Alabyadh, 2021, p. 83]])
- Diagenetic features identified include microbial micritization of grains, isopachous fibrous/bladed cement, disseminated rhombic dolomite, and blocky calcite cement. ([[2021-alabyadh|Alabyadh, 2021, p. 102]])
- Dedolomitization was observed as a significant diagenetic process, characterized by the replacement of dolomite rhombs with calcite. ([[2021-alabyadh|Alabyadh, 2021, p. 110]])
- QEMSCAN analysis revealed specific percentages of calcite and porosity across different samples (e.g., KF-CC-39.6 to 81.5). ([[2021-alabyadh|Alabyadh, 2021, p. 122]])
- Ooids were found with varying sizes, including those larger than 1000um which were often irregularly shaped and broken. ([[2021-alabyadh|Alabyadh, 2021, p. 84]])
- The paragenetic sequence established the relative timing of diagenetic phases from early to late stages. ([[2021-alabyadh|Alabyadh, 2021, p. 127]])
- Porosity was found to be influenced by various factors, including cementation and dissolution processes. ([[2021-alabyadh|Alabyadh, 2021, p. 129]])
- Eleven microfacies were identified, including mudstone, skeletal wackestone, packstone, and grainstone fabrics with components ranging from peloids and dasyclad algae to ooids. ([[2021-alabyadh|Alabyadh, 2021, p. 13]])
- Several diagenetic alterations were recognized: microbial micritization, early marine circumferential fibrous to bladed cement, blocky calcite cement, dedolomite, and rhombohedral molds. ([[2021-alabyadh|Alabyadh, 2021, p. 13]])
- Blocky calcite cement was specifically identified as a primary factor in reducing porosity within the carbonate rocks. ([[2021-alabyadh|Alabyadh, 2021, p. 13]])
- The Sannicandro Formation is characterized by peritidal parasequences (1-5m thick) representing lagoonal to supratidal environments, with an estimated thickness of 200-250 m. ([[2021-alabyadh|Alabyadh, 2021, p. 36]])
- The Monte Spigno Formation consists of oolitic and oncolitic grainstones, with an estimated thickness of 300-400 m. ([[2021-alabyadh|Alabyadh, 2021, p. 37]])
- The transition zone between the Sannicandro (inner shelf) and Monte Spigno (oolitic shoal) formations extends in a belt of approximately 40 km. ([[2021-alabyadh|Alabyadh, 2021, p. 18]])
- The measured stratigraphic thickness along the studied section at the Costa Callese locality is 90 m. ([[2021-alabyadh|Alabyadh, 2021, p. 54]])
- XRD analysis identified key minerals including calcite, dolomite, anhydrite, quartz, and illite in the carbonate rocks. ([[2021-alabyadh|Alabyadh, 2021, p. 50]])
