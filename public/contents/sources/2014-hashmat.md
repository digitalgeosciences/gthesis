# STUDY OF CROSS-LINKED POLYMER GEL SYSTEMS FOR POTENTIAL USE AS LOSS PREVENTION AGENTS

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the development and characterization of cross-linked polymer gel systems (HPAM/PEI, A130/PEI, and PAM/PF) for use as Loss Circulation Materials (LCM). The study evaluates their rheological properties under varying temperatures and salinities, determines gelation kinetics via DSC, and tests their effectiveness in blocking high-permeability zones using a 'see-through' core setup.

---

## Research Problem

Loss of drilling fluids into weak, fractured, or high-permeability zones (thief zones) during drilling operations leads to increased costs and operational delays. There is a need for robust, thermally stable cross-linked polymer gels that can effectively seal these zones.

## Objectives

- To synthesize various cross-linked polymer gel systems using different cross-linking agents.
- To determine the effectiveness of these gels as loss prevention agents in simulated environments.
- To evaluate the rheological properties (viscosity, yield stress) of the prepared gels.
- To study the chemical stability and degradation of the polymers under specific conditions.
- To study the rheological properties of cross-linked polymers
- To study the effect of permeability on loss performance of the cross-linked polymer samples

## Methods

The research involved preparing several polymer systems (HPAM/PEI, Alcomer130/PEI, and K20/PEI) and testing them using a Discovery Hybrid Rheometer with various geometries (concentric cylinder for fluids, cone and plate for gels). Specific tests included: 1. Oscillating Amplitude Test to determine the Linear Viscoelastic Region (LVR); 2. Oscillating Frequency Test (frequency sweep) to study complex moduli; 3. Flow Sweep Test to evaluate shear-thinning behavior; 4. Flow Temperature Ramp to assess thermal stability; and 5. Oscillation Temperature Ramp to observe the effect of temperature on complex moduli.

## Data and Materials

The study utilized several polymer systems: K20 (0.75 wt%), A130, and HPAM. Crosslinkers included PEI and phenol-formaldehyde (PF). Additives included nano-clays (15A), attapulgite (ATP), and calcium bentonite (Ca-bent). Three core types were characterized: k1 (permeability 306 D, porosity 36%), k2 (permeability 432 D, porosity 38%), and k3 (permeability 2965 D, porosity 44%).

## Key Findings

- Cross-linked polymer gels are evaluated as potential agents for preventing fluid loss during well construction. ([[2014-hashmat|Hashmat, 2014, p. 1]])
- Cross-linked polymers exhibited significant increases in viscosity compared to their base polymer forms. ([[2014-hashmat|Hashmat, 2014, p. 3]])
- The addition of specific cross-linking agents resulted in stable gel networks capable of maintaining structure under shear. ([[2014-hashmat|Hashmat, 2014, p. 3]])
- Different chemical formulations showed varying degrees of stability and degradation over time. ([[2014-hashmat|Hashmat, 2014, p. 3]])
- HPAM/PEI (3/1.2) systems showed a 2935% increase in storage modulus compared to the base polymer when aged at 120°C for 1 day. ([[2014-hashmat|Hashmat, 2014, p. 15]])
- Both HPAM/PEI and A130/PEI systems were found to be able to prevent mud losses until a pressure difference of 150 psi across a 300 D porous media. ([[2014-hashmat|Hashmat, 2014, p. 15]])
- HPAM (3 wt%) showed a viscosity reduction from 622 Pa.s at 0.01 s^-1 to 0.1945 Pa.s at 1000 s^-1, confirming shear-thinning behavior. ([[2014-hashmat|Hashmat, 2014, p. 48]])
- Alcomer130 (2 wt%) showed a viscosity reduction from 1561 Pa.s to 0.1325 Pa.s between 0.01 and 1000 s^-1. ([[2014-hashmat|Hashmat, 2014, p. 54]])
- The HPAM/PEI (3/1.2) system aged at 120°C for 1 day showed a storage modulus of 3564 Pa, which is approximately 35 times higher than the base polymer. ([[2014-hashmat|Hashmat, 2014, p. 58]])
- The A130/PEI (2/1.2) system aged at 120°C for 1 day showed a storage modulus of 578 Pa, representing a six-fold increase over the base polymer. ([[2014-hashmat|Hashmat, 2014, p. 58]])
- The thermoviscosifying polymer (K20) exhibited viscosity reduction with increasing temperature, contrary to typical TVP behavior under specific test conditions. ([[2014-hashmat|Hashmat, 2014, p. 47]])
- K20/PEI systems showed lower viscosity profiles than the base polymer at high frequencies; specifically, K20/15A showed improved G' values up to 150 rad/s. ([[2014-hashmat|Hashmat, 2014, p. 71]])
- Thermo-thickening behavior in K20 (0.75 wt%) was only observed in the formation water (FW) case, where viscosity increased significantly after 72°C, reaching 0.67 Pa.s at 90°C. ([[2014-hashmat|Hashmat, 2014, p. 79]])
- The addition of PEI to A130 did not result in significant improvement in complex moduli compared to the base polymer. ([[2014-hashmat|Hashmat, 2014, p. 85]])
- Calcium bentonite (Ca-bent) as an additive for Alcomer130 did not prove to be a rheology enhancing additive and resulted in a thermothinning profile. ([[2014-hashmat|Hashmat, 2014, p. 97]])

## Limitations

The current see-through system cannot be used for high temperature applications or for conventional LCMs with large components; a dedicated HPHT flow loop is recommended for future field-scale simulation.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Future work should include obtaining field formulations of commercial LCMs to establish benchmarks, studying environmentally friendly polymers like chitosan and cellulose, and developing an HPHT flow loop to simulate high temperature/high pressure conditions.
