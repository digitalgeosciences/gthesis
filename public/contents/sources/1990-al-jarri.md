# Oil Compressibility Correlations

**Summary**: The thesis develops and evaluates new empirical correlations for calculating oil isothermal compressibility at and below bubblepoint pressure, as well as gas pseudo-critical properties (pressure and temperature) for hydrocarbon mixtures. The study compares these new models against established correlations by McCain et al., Standing, and Thomas et al., finding the newly developed ones to be more accurate across various ranges of density and pressure.

---

## Research Problem

The need for accurate empirical correlations to determine oil isothermal compressibility at/below bubblepoint and gas pseudo-critical properties in hydrocarbon mixtures, as existing models (McCain, Standing, Thomas) show significant errors or limitations in specific ranges of gas relative density and pressure.

## Objectives

- Develop an empirical correlation to calculate the oil isothermal compressibility values at and below bubblepoint pressures.
- Develop correlations to calculate gas pseudo-critical pressure and temperature for hydrocarbon gas mixtures.
- Compare the accuracy of the existing correlations and the new ones developed in this study.
- Evaluate the accuracy of newly developed correlations against existing models (McCain et al., Standing, Thomas et al.) across various ranges of gas relative density and pressure.

## Methods

The thesis utilizes multiple regression analysis (both linear and non-linear) to develop correlation equations. Non-linear relationships were linearized using logarithmic transformations. Numerical differentiation via Lagrangian interpolation was used to estimate derivatives for oil compressibility calculations. Statistical tools including T-statistics, standard deviation, and correlation coefficients were employed to validate the accuracy of the new models against existing ones.

## Data and Materials

145 sets of gas analysis (for pseudo-critical properties) and 2635 data points from 372 gas differential liberation tests (for oil isothermal compressibility). Data included reservoir temperature, bubblepoint pressure, solution gas-oil ratio, gas relative density, and oil gravity.

## Key Findings

- The new oil isothermal compressibility correlation outperformed existing correlations even in selected ranges of pressure for oil compressibility. ([[1990-al-jarri|Al-Jarri, 1990, p. 18]])
- New pseudo-critical properties correlations are more accurate than the existing ones. ([[1990-al-jarri|Al-Jarri, 1990, p. 18]])
- Oil relative density does not contribute significantly to the calculation of oil compressibility in the presence of other variables (P, Pb, Rg, T). ([[1990-al-jarri|Al-Jarri, 1990, p. 68]])
- The existing correlation is not accurate at pressures below 500 psia or bubblepoint pressures below 763 psia. ([[1990-al-jarri|Al-Jarri, 1990, p. 21]])
- Gas pseudo-critical pressure was found to be a function of gas relative density, with regression coefficients for T-statistics showing high values (e.g., 22.043 and -50.924). ([[1990-al-jarri|Al-Jarri, 1990, p. 64]])
- The average absolute percent error for the natural log of compressibility is 4.5% with a maximum absolute error of 9.2%. ([[1990-al-jarri|Al-Jarri, 1990, p. 30]])
- The study developed a new oil isothermal compressibility correlation that showed an average absolute percent relative error of 3.49% and a maximum of 9.88%, significantly outperforming the McCain et al. correlation (13.10% avg, 117.67% max). ([[1990-al-jarri|Al-Jarri, 1990, p. 91]])
- The study developed gas pseudo-critical pressure correlations with high degrees of correlation (0.9813 and 0.9988 for pressure and temperature, respectively). ([[1990-al-jarri|Al-Jarri, 1990, p. 72]])
- Standing's and Thomas et al.'s correlations were found to underestimate gas pseudo-critical properties compared to the study's results. ([[1990-al-jarri|Al-Jarri, 1990, p. 72]])
- The new correlation for oil compressibility is the most accurate across all ranges of pressure, whereas McCain et al. shows high errors (>20%) below 500 psia and above 3500 psia. ([[1990-al-jarri|Al-Jarri, 1990]])

- The study's gas pseudo-critical pressure crossplots for the current study were found to be very close to the perfect correlation 45° line, while Standing and Thomas et al. fell below it. ([[1990-al-jarri|Al-Jarri, 1990, p. 75]])

## Limitations

Existing correlations are noted to be inaccurate at low pressures (below 500 psia) and low bubblepoint pressures (below 763 psia).
