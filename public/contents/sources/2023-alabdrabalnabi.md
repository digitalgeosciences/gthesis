# INVESTIGATION OF CHELATING AGENT AS ACID FRACTURING FLUIDS

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the use of chelating agents (GLDA, EDTA, and DTPA) as environmentally friendly alternatives to conventional acid fracturing fluids in carbonate reservoirs. Experimental results indicate that GLDA significantly improves fracture conductivity and creates distinct channel patterns compared to other acids or chelating agents.

---

## Research Problem

Hydrochloric acid (HCl) is highly effective at creating fracture conductivity but poses significant risks of corrosion and rapid reaction in high-temperature carbonate reservoirs. There is a need for safer, less corrosive alternatives like chelating agents that can maintain fracture conductivity without compromising the rock's mechanical properties.

## Objectives

- Examine the effectiveness of environmentally friendly chelating agents as acid fracturing fluids in a carbonate formation containing layered or mixed minerals (calcite and dolomite).
- Test the permeability impairment or enhancement on the fracture face.
- Evaluate the impact of the tested fluids on the mechanical properties of the rock and surface roughness.
- Perform a performance comparison with conventional HCl acid.
- Evaluate the feasibility of chelating agents (GLDA, EDTA, DTPA) as alternatives to HCl in acid fracturing.
- Assess the impact of different chelating agents on rock strength and surface roughness.

## Methods

The study was divided into two phases: layered samples (cast from carbonate rocks and cementitious materials) and mixed mineral samples (actual reservoir plugs from the Arab-D formation). Coreflooding was used to measure fracture conductivity under four closure stresses (1000, 1500, 2000, and 2500 psi) at four flow rates. Laser profilometry (Kruss SRA) measured surface roughness. An impulse hammer (NER AutoScan) assessed rock hardness/modulus. ICP-OES (Optima 8000) analyzed effluent to quantify calcium and magnesium dissolution.

## Data and Materials

Two types of samples: cast layered blocks (6x6 inch) and reservoir plugs from the Arab-D formation (2x1.5 inches). Five distinct fluids were tested: 15% HCl, 20% GLDA, 20% EDTA, and 20% DTPA.

## Key Findings

- GLDA treatment resulted in a fracture conductivity approximately 20 times higher than the pre-stimulation state. ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023, p. 43]])
- HCl caused the greatest change in surface roughness and highest dissolution of calcium (approx. 4,000 mg/L) and magnesium (approx. 1,000 mg/L). ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023, p. 48]])
- HCl reduced the initial average hardness of the sample by 70%, from 26 GPa to 8 GPa. ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023, p. 45]])
- EDTA treatment showed only minor improvements in fracture conductivity compared to GLDA and HCl. ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023, p. 43]])
- DTPA showed more than 50% reduction in Young's modulus (YM) but resulted in lower fracture conductivity than GLDA due to its high pH level. ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023, p. 55]])
- GLDA produced a sufficient channel for improved conductivity while being less damaging to rock strength compared to HCl. ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023, p. 47]])
- DTPA showed a unique behavior with fluctuating pressure during stimulation and lower fracture conductivity than GLDA. ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023, p. 59]])
- Chelating agents GLDA and EDTA provided sufficient improvement in fracture conductivity compared to HCl under varying closure stresses. ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023, p. 61]])
- GLDA showed a clear evolution in roughness and rock strength, generating a channel pattern over the fracture surface. ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023, p. 61]])
- EDTA produced an uniform unclear pattern on the fracture surface. ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023, p. 61]])
- In downhole carbonate samples, the sample treated with GLDA showed significantly improved fracture conductivity compared to DTPA. ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023, p. 61]])
- ICP analysis confirmed the feasibility of chelating agents by quantifying calcium and magnesium ions in the carbonate samples. ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023, p. 61]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

Future work should include: determining optimal contact times, identifying optimum pH for each agent, modeling fracture conductivity improvement with more parameters, performing CT imaging to quantify dissolution volume, and using Molecular Dynamic Simulation (MDS).
