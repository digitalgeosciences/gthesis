# TURONIAN TO SANTONIAN AGGLUTINATED BENTHIC FORAMINIFERA FROM A MARGINAL MARINE HIGH SOUTHERN LATITUDE BASIN: IODP SITE U1512 (GREAT AUSTRALIAN BIGHT)

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the micropaleontology of agglutinated benthic foraminifera from Turonian to Santonian ages in a marginal marine basin at IODP Site U1512. Analysis revealed 59 foraminiferal taxa, including seven newly identified species, and established two useful biozones for correlation with northern hemisphere records.

---

## Research Problem

The micropaleontology of southern high-latitude Cretaceous strata is inadequately studied compared to the northern hemisphere, specifically regarding agglutinated benthic foraminifera in marginal marine settings like the Great Australian Bight.

## Objectives

- Identify agglutinated foraminifera from the Turonian–Santonian sediments
- Provide taxonomic descriptions of these microfossils
- Determine the age range of newly identified species through biostratigraphic analysis
- Examine the use of agglutinated benthic foraminifera as a means of reconstructing paleoecological conditions
- Identify and characterize agglutinated benthic foraminiferal taxa from IODP Site U1512.
- Establish a biostratigraphic framework for the Turonian to Santonian sections.

## Methods

The study involved the analysis of 29 samples from 73 cores collected at IODP Site U11512. Analysis included micropaleontological identification, morphological characterization of agglutinated tests (wall texture, shape, and size), and biostratigraphic correlation using both planktonic foraminifera and agglutinated benthic foraminifera.

## Data and Materials

29 core samples from IODP Site U1512; 73 total cores cut at the site; identification of 59 foraminiferal taxa including 42 species-level identifications.

## Key Findings

- A total of fifty-nine foraminiferal taxa were identified, and forty-two of these types were recognized to a formal species level. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 33]])
- Seven newly discovered species were identified: Sculptobaculites sp. 1, Bulbobaculites sp. 1, Bulbobaculites sp. 2, Bulbobaculites sp. 3, Gerochammina sp. 1, Rectogerochammina sp. 1, and Praedorothia sp. 1. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 13]])
- Two Turonian biozones were recognized: the first containing planktonic foraminifera (Whiteinella brittonensis, Whiteinella aumalensis, etc.) and the second based on agglutinated foraminifera (Bulbobaculites problematicus, Spiroplectammina navarroana, etc.). ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 14]])
- The first planktonic biozone is correlated with northern hemisphere Turonian biozones. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 14]])
- A poorly known foraminiferal province referred to as 'Austral' from the Late Cretaceous is reported. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 14]])
- Planktonic foraminifera in the section show a transition from high hothouse to greenhouse states during the Turonian and Santonian. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 16]])
- The sedimentary record of the Great Australian Bight basin encompasses a temporal range from the Turonian to the Santonian. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 16]])
- Calcined benthic foraminifera showed a significant increase in abundance during the middle Santonian, constituting nearly 63% of the sample at a depth of 59 meters. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 27]])
- The study confirmed that both Whiteinella brittonensis and Bulbobaculites problematicus overlap in the middle Turonian. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 151]])
- Haplophragmoides abundance throughout early and middle Turonian supports marginal marine environments including deltaic and brackish water conditions. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 152]])
- The presence of Psammosiphonella discreta and Gyroidinoides umbilicata indicates an extensive open marine depth range spanning outer neritic to middle bathyal conditions during a higher order marine transgression in the middle Turonian. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 153]])
- A major turnover occurred in the Coniacian, where thin tubular species (Bathysiphon sp., Rhizammina sp. 2) were replaced by thicker forms (Rhizammina sp. 3). ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 154]])
- The size of agglutinated species can be used to depict bathymetric range, with smaller tubular fauna in the Santonian corresponding to marine transgression. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 154]])
- Evidence for an 'Austral' faunal province was found, characterized by Bulbobaculites spp., Textulariopsis texhomensis, and Textulariopsis rioensis, and the absence of Tethyan fauna like Caudammina spp. and Hormosinella spp. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 155]])
- Seven new species were recognized in the current samples. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 157]])

## Limitations

Not studied due to inaccessibility of samples spanning 200-380m; some agglutinated tests were deformed or fragmented due to compaction and sedimentation during rifting.
