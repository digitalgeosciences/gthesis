# TURONIAN TO SANTONIAN AGGLUTINATED BENTHIC FORAMINIFERA FROM A MARGINAL MARINE HIGH SOUTHERN LATITUDE BASIN: IODP SITE U1512 (GREAT AUSTRALIAN BIGHT)

**Summary**: The thesis investigates agglutinated benthic foraminifera from the Turonian to Santonian ages in a marginal marine basin at high southern latitudes (IODP Site U1512). Analysis of 29 samples using SEM and light microscopy revealed 59 taxa, leading to the identification of seven new species and the establishment of two useful biozones.

---

## Research Problem

Micropaleontological studies of Cretaceous successions are often concentrated in the Tethyan and Atlantic Oceans, leaving southern high-latitude (SHL) strata like those at IODP Site U1512 inadequately studied. Specifically, many agglutinated foraminifera in these regions remain partially characterized or undescribed.

## Objectives

- Review the taxonomy of agglutinated foraminifera within the Turonian-Santonian sediments at IODP Site U1512.
- Identify and describe new species of agglutinated benthic foraminifera.
- Establish biozones based on both planktonic and agglutinated foraminiferal assemblages to correlate with northern hemisphere records.
- Find and identify the benthic foraminifera from the Turonian to the Santonian.
- Give taxonomic descriptions of these microfossils.
- Determine how they relate to paleoclimate proxies.
- Assess the use of agglutinated benthic foraminifera as a means of reconstructing paleoecological conditions.
- Identify and describe agglutinated benthic foraminifera from Turonian to Santonian ages at IODP Site U1152.
- Provide taxonomic clarification for specific species found in the Great Australian Bight.
- Identify and describe agglutinated benthic foraminifera from Turonian to Santonian ages.
- Analyze the diversity and morphology of these foraminifera in a marginal marine, high southern latitude basin.
- Analyze and characterize agglutinated benthic foraminifera from IODP Site U1512
- Establish a biostratigraphic framework for the Turonian to Santorian section
- Identify new species and faunal provinces in the high southern latitude basin
- Analyze 29 samples from IODP Site U1512 for foraminifera
- Identify and describe agglutinated benthic foraminifera from the Turonian to Santonian stages
- Evaluate the micropaleontological characteristics of the Great Australian Bight region
- Analyze and characterize agglutinated benthic foraminifera from Turonian to Santonian ages.
- Evaluate the biostratigraphy of the Great Australian Bight region.
- Investigate paleoenvironmental conditions in a high-latitude marginal marine basin.

## Methods

Twenty-nine core samples from IODP Site U1512 were processed by boiling in water and industrial soap, followed by sieving through 63-micron and 125-micron meshes to remove silt/clay and isolate adult foraminifera. Approximately 300 specimens per sample were sorted into taxonomic groups. Scanning electron microscopy (SEM) was used to illustrate key taxa and their morphological features. Identification was based on the classifications of Loeblich and Tappan (1987) for calcareous benthic foraminifera and Kaminski (2014) for agglutinated foraminifera.

## Data and Materials

29 samples prepared and analyzed from 73 cores at IODP Site U1512 in the Great Australian Bight; analysis included various benthic foraminifera (agglutinated and calcareous) and planktonic foraminifera.

## Key Findings

- A total of 59 foraminiferal taxa were identified, with 42 recognized to a formal species level. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 13]])
- Seven new species were identified: Sculptobaculites sp. 1, Bulbobaculites sp. 1, Bulbobaculites sp. 2, Bulbobaculites sp. 3, Gerochammina sp. 1, Rectogerochammina sp. 1, and Praedorothia sp. 1. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 13]])
- The first planktonic foraminiferal biozone contains Whiteinella brittonensis, Whiteinella aumalensis, Whiteinella baltica, Muricohedbergella delrioensis, and rare Planohedbergella prairiehillensis. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 14]])
- The second biozone is based on agglutinated foraminifera, containing Bulbobaculites problematicus, Spiroplectammina navarroana, Eobigenerina variabilis, Textulariopsis rioensis, Textulariopsis texhomensis, and Gerochammina lenis. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 14]])
- Southern-latitude taxa exhibited only slight morphological variations compared to those in the northern hemisphere. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 13]])
- A total of 59 distinct foraminiferal taxa were identified in the study section, including both calcareous and agglutinated types. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 18]])
- 42 of the identified taxa were successfully classified and described at the species level. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 18]])
- Planktonic foraminifera in the Turonian interval (489.58 to 603.85 meters) included Whiteinella brittonensis, Planoheterohelix planata, and Globigerinelloides. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 25]])
- The Santonian section showed a high abundance of calcareous benthic organisms, reaching approximately 63% of the sample at a depth of 59 meters. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 12]])
- Agglutinated foraminifera in the Turonian (692 to 423 meters) were characterized by species such as Bulbobaculites problematicus, Eobigenerina variabilis, and Glomospira charoides. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 13]])
- The interval from 279 to 20 meters is designated as late Turoninan to Santonian based on the presence of Uvigerinammina jankoi. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 13]])
- Whiteinella aumalensis was identified in Cores 46R to 60R at Site U1512, characterized by a very coarse perforate wall texture and a distinct aperture shape. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 47]])
- Planohedbergella prairiehillensis was observed in Cores 49R and 51R at Site U1512, showing fine perforations in the wall texture. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 49]])
- Prodentalina oligostegia is a member of the Ichthyolariidae family found in the upper Cretaceous at site U1512. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 52]])
- Hemirobulina arcuatula was identified as having a smooth, elongate calcareous hyaline test with a rounded side view and radiate terminal apertures. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 53]])

## Limitations

Not much discussion in the literature about detailed sedimentation rates nor tectonic activities during the Coniacian-Santonian to explain specific faunal changes; some samples (200-380m) were inaccessible.
