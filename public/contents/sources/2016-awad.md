# Sedimentology and Sequence Stratigraphy of Paleocene Samma, Yabus and Adar Formations in Rawat basin, White Nile state, Sudan

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the sedimentology and sequence stratigraphy of the Samma, Yabus, and Adar Formations in the Rawat Basin, Sudan. By integrating 3D seismic data, well logs (GR, Density-Neutron), and FMI images from five exploration wells, the study identifies nine lithofacies associations and establishes a stratigraphic framework for the third rift phase of the basin.

---

## Research Problem

The lithofacies distribution, stratigraphic framework, reservoir geometry, and continuity of the Samma, Yabus, and Adar Formations in the Rawat Basin are not well understood due to complex stratigraphic settings and heterogeneous sedimentary facies typical of rift basins.

## Objectives

- Establish the sequence stratigraphic framework for the Samma, Yabus, and Adar Formations.
- Understand the basin evolution of the third rift phase in the Rawat Basin.
- Identify lithofacies associations based on GR log motifs and FMI data.
- Interpret depositional environments for each formation.
- Identify major lithology (sandstones, shales) facies, facies association, and depositional environments.
- Construct a depositional model considering structural controls and strata geometry.
- Perform regional correlation for the third rift phase (Samma, Yabus, and Adar).
- Build a sequence stratigraphic framework to understand basin evolution.
- Understand the interplay of tectonics, sediment supply, and climate in controlling large-scale stratigraphy.
- Establish the sequence stratigraphic framework for the Samma, Yabus, and Sudan formations.
- Understand the basin evolution of the Rawat Basin during the third rift phase.
- Identify lithofacies associations based on well log motifs (GR) and FMI data.
- Determine depositional environments for each formation.
- Understand the basin evolution of the Rawat Basin in southeast Sudan.
- Integrate 3D seismic data with well logs and FMI to recognize stratigraphic sequences.
- Determine depositional environments for the identified lithofacies.

## Methods

The study utilizes a multi-method approach: 1. Well Log Analysis: Gamma Ray (GR), Density-Neutron, and Spontaneous Potential (SP) logs were used to identify lithology and log motifs (funnel, bell, boxcar, bow). 2. Formation Micro-Imager (FMI): Used to identify sedimentary structures like bedding dip, fractures, and paleocurrents. 3. Mud Logs: Utilized for cutting descriptions and lithology correlation across five wells (C-1, M-1, C-2, K-1, W-1). 4. Software Integration: Petrel was used to integrate log data, calculate lithology profiles, and perform quality control (e.g., GR vs. SP, Caliper vs. Bit size). 5. Stratigraphic Correlation: Correlating formation tops across the basin using a combination of well logs and physical samples.

## Data and Materials

Data from 5 exploration wells in the Rawat Basin, including Gamma Ray (GR), Spontaneous Potential (SP), Density-Neutron logs, Formation Micro Imager (FMI) data, and 3D seismic volumes. The study covers three specific formations: Samma, Yabus, and Adar.

## Key Findings

- Nine lithofacies associations were identified across the Samma, Yabus, and Adar Formations based on GR log motifs and FMI data. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 17]])
- Samma Formation depositional environments include alluvial fan, fluvial, shoreface, and shallow lacustrine. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 17]])
- Yabus Formation is interpreted as being deposited in fluvial and deltaic environments. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 17]])
- Adar Formation was deposited in marginal and open lacustrine environments. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 17]])
- The low stand system tract (LST) in the second order super sequence is dominated by alluvial fan sandstone. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 17]])
- The Transgressive system tract (TST) consists of lacustrine shoreface, shallow lacustrine, and fluvio-deltaic deposits. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 17]])
- The high stand systems tract (HST) is characterized by semi-deep lacustrine and shallow lacustrine deposits. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 17]])
- Interpretation of 3D seismic data revealed four continuous reflectors corresponding to the bottom Samma sequence boundary, top Samma Transgressive surface, maximum flooding surface (MFS), and top Adar formation. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 17]])
- The basin evolved from an over-filled basin in the early Paleocene (Samma Formation) to an under-filled basin by the early Oligocene (Adar Formation). ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 18]])
- Four seismic facies were identified through seismic facies analysis supported by various seismic attributes. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 18]])
- The most favorable sandstone reservoirs are developed in a fluviodeltaic environment in the Yabus Formation, capped by high-stand semi-deep lacustrine environments in the Adar Formation. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 18]])
- Nine lithofacies associations were identified across the three formations based on GR log motifs and FMI data. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 19]])
- The primary depositional environment for the Samma Formation includes alluvial fans, fluvial systems, beach environments, and lakes. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 19]])
- The Yabus Formation is characterized by fluvial and deltaic depositional environments. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 19]])
- The Adar Formation was deposited in open lake and beach environments. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 19]])

## Limitations

The interpretation of depositional environment and facies analysis using GR log motif and FMI have a large uncertainty; therefore, conventional core and cutting descriptions should be used to calibrate and confirm the interpretation.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Use conventional core and cutting descriptions to calibrate and confirm interpretations from GR logs and FMI.
- Increase the number of wells, especially in the deepocentre, for better facies analysis.
- Extend the results of this thesis to the first and second rifting cycles in Rawat basin.
