# Sedimentology and Sequence Stratigraphy of Paleocene Samma, Yabus and Adar Formations in Rawat basin, White Nile state, Sudan

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the sedimentology and sequence stratigraphy of the Paleocene Samma, Yabus, and Adar Formations in the Rawat Basin, Sudan. By integrating 3D seismic data, well logs (GR, Density-Neutron, SP), and FMI images from five exploration wells, the study identifies nine lithofacies associations to characterize the basin's evolution during the third rift phase.

---

## Research Problem

The need to establish a clear sequence stratigraphic framework and understand the depositional environments of the Samma, Yabus, and Adar Formations (Tertiary age) in the Rawat Basin, Sudan, specifically characterizing the transition from syn-rift to post-rift settings.

## Objectives

- Identify major lithology (sandstones, shales), facies associations, and depositional environments.
- Construct a depositional model considering structural controls and strata geometry.
- Perform regional correlation for the third rift phase (Samma, Yabus, and Adar formations).
- Build a sequence stratigraphic framework to understand basin evolution.
- Understand the interplay of tectonics, sediment supply, and climate in controlling large-scale stratigraphy.
- Establish the sequence stratigraphic framework for the Samma, Y-abus, and Adar Formations.

## Methods

The study utilizes a multi-method approach for lithology and facies analysis: 1) Well log analysis including Gamma Ray (GR), Density-Neutron, and Spontaneous Potential (SP) to identify log motifs (e.g., funnel, bell, boxcar). 2) Formation Micro Imager (FMI) to identify sedimentary structures like cross-bedding and fractures. 3) Mud logs for core/cutting analysis to provide physical lithology data. 4) Integration of these datasets in Petrel software to construct lithofacies associations. 5) Sequence stratigraphy principles (Posamentier & Vail model) were applied to define systems tracts and parasequences.

## Data and Materials

Data includes 3D seismic volumes, well logs (GR, Density-Neutron), FMI logs, and cutting descriptions from 5 exploration wells in the Rawat Basin. The study focuses on three specific formations: Samma, Yabus, and Adar.

## Key Findings

- Nine lithofacies associations were identified across the Samma, Yabus, and Adar formations based on GR log motifs and FMI data. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 17]])
- Samma Formation depositional environments include alluvial fan, fluvial, shoreface, and shallow lacustrine. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 17]])
- Yabus Formation is interpreted as being deposited in fluvial and deltaic environments. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 17]])
- Adar Formation was deposited in marginal and open lacustrine environments. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 17]])
- The study identified four continuous reflectors corresponding to the bottom Samma sequence boundary, top Samma Transgressive surface, maximum flooding surface of Adar, and the sequence boundary of Adar formation. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 18]])
- The basin was an over-filled basin in the early Paleocene (Samma Formation) and evolved into an under-filled basin by the early Oligocene (Adar Formation). ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 18]])
- The most favorable sandstone reservoirs are developed in a fluvio-deltaic environment in the Yabus Formation, capped by high stand semi-deep lacustrine environments in the Adar Formation. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 18]])
- Lithofacies FS1 is characterized as thick amalgamated sandstone of alluvial fan deposit; FS2 as thick claystone of floodplain with thin sandstone of crevasse splay. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 52]])
- The Samma formation shows a transition from an over-filled basin to the Yabus and Adar formations which represent different rift phases. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 18]])
- The Samma Formation contains five lithofacies associations: FS1 (alluvial-fan), FS2 (overbank/crevasse splay), FS3 (fluvial channel/bar), FS4 (lacustrine shoreface), and FS5 (marginal lacustrine). ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 74]])
- The Yabus Formation contains three lithofacies associations: FY1 (fluvial environment) and FY2 (deltaic environment, including middle and distal delta components). ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 83]])
- The Adar Formation contains two main lithofacies: FA1 (shallow lake/lacustrine) and FA2 (semi-deep open lacustrine). ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 87]])
- A clear transition from syn-rift to post-rift is marked by the contact between the Adar and Jimidi formations. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 87]])
- The Samma formation's FS1 lithofacies is characterized by thick, poorly sorted, medium-to-coarse grained sandstones (11-50m thick) in an alluvial-fan setting. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 74]])
- The Yabus formation shows a transition from fluvial to deltaic environments, with FY2 showing a coarsening-upward trend indicating progradation. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 85]])

## Limitations

The interpretation of depositional environments and facies analysis using GR log motif and FMI data has a large uncertainty, necessitating the use of conventional core and cutting descriptions for calibration.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Use conventional core and cutting descriptions to calibrate and confirm interpretations from GR logs and FMI.
- Extend the study's results to cover the first and second rifting cycles in the Rawat basin.
- Increase the number of wells, especially in the deepocentre, to improve facies analysis.
