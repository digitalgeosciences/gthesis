# LONG-TERM INVESTIGATION OF NANO-SILICA GEL PERFORMANCE FOR WATER SHUT-OFF IN FRACTURED RESERVOIRS

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the long-term durability and performance of nano-silica gel compared to PAM/PEI gel for water shut-off in fractured and induced channel samples. Using coreflooding, CT scans, and NMR measurements, it concludes that nano-silica gel maintains 100% plugging efficiency over 6 months while PAM/PEI gel shows signs of degradation.

---

## Research Problem

The management of excessive water production in oil and gas wells is a significant challenge that increases costs and reduces productivity. While various chemical treatments like polymer gels and silicate gels exist, there is a lack of thorough assessment regarding the long-term durability and performance of nano-silica based systems compared to traditional organic polymers.

## Objectives

- Evaluate the effectiveness of nano silica-based gel as a water shut-off agent in a matrix characterized by high permeability and fractures, focusing on injectivity, and plugging efficiency.
- Investigate the durability of nano-silica gel over 6 months under reservoir conditions in both permeable matrix and fractures.
- Conduct a comprehensive comparison between the plugging performance of nano-silica gel and PAM/PEI gel over 6 months.
- Evaluate the long-term durability of nano-silica gel within fractures and channels over a period of six months.
- Investigate the performance of nano-silica gel over a 6-month period in fractured and induced channel samples.
- Assess the plugging efficiency and degradation rates of both gel systems under high pressure and temperature conditions.

## Methods

The study utilized two core samples (A1 and A2) to test two different gel systems. Coreflooding experiments were conducted at 200°F to assess initial permeability, gel injection, and subsequent endurance tests up to 1000 psi. CT scans were used to visualize the distribution of the gel and changes in pore density. NMR T2 measurements were performed to analyze the pore system characteristics and fluid distribution before and after treatment. The samples were aged for up to 6 months at 167°F, with durability tests conducted at intervals (1, 3, and 6 months). Particle size analysis was used to characterize the nano-silica particles.

## Data and Materials

The study used limestone core samples with dimensions of approximately 1.5-inch diameter and 3-inch length. Two gel systems were tested: a nano-silica gel (75% silica, 25% activator) and a PAM/PEI gel (90% PAM, 10% PEI). Brine solutions (0.1 M/L) were used for pre-treatment and post-treatment. Formation water (120,000 ppm) was used during the aging process.

## Key Findings

- Nano-silica gel achieved better long-term performance than PAM/PEI gel for 6 months. ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 20]])
- After 6 months, nano-silica gel was capable of reducing the permeability of the permeable sample to nearly zero with plugging efficiency not less than 99.98%. ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 20]])
- The efficiency of PAM/PEI gel reduced to 99.85% in the permeable sample after 6 months. ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 20]])
- The nano-silica gel maintained a consistent 100% plugging efficiency over a 6-month aging period in formation water at 167°F. ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 151]])
- PAM/PEI gel showed degradation over time, with its plugging efficiency dropping from 99.99% to 99.15% after 6 months in fractured samples. ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 157]])
- CT scan results indicate minimum degradation occurred to both gels with less significant impact on nano-silica gel, illustrating its durability after 6 months. ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 21]])
- Nano-silica gel achieved a reduction in permeability from 144.85 to 0.001 mD initially, demonstrating high plugging efficiency. ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 66]])
- The maximum leak-off rate for the nano-silica gel was recorded at 0.240 cc/min after 6 months, compared to 2.165 cc/min for the PAM/PEI gel. ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 66]])
- CT scan analysis confirmed a significant increase in average CT numbers from 2084.91 to 2273.00 after nano-silica treatment, indicating successful distribution of the gel. ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 77]])
- NMR T2 measurements showed that the T2 peak shifted toward the bulk-free nano-silica value (approx. 32 ms) after injection, indicating successful displacement of brine from macropores. ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 120]])
- The average particle size of the nano-silica was determined to be 14 nm using a FPIA-3000 analyzer. ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 69]])
- A residual resistance factor (RRF) of 106,251.01 was achieved for the PAM/PEI gel treatment. ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 84]])
- The nano-silica gel showed a higher durability at high differential pressure compared to the PAM/PEI system over the 6-month period. ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 80]])
- The nano-silica gel achieved a high plugging efficiency of 99.99% under high differential pressure (1000 psi). ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 92]])
- The average permeability of the fractured sample increased from 70 mD to 184.73 mD before treatment, confirming the presence of an open fracture. ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 116]])

## Limitations

Existing coupled numerical models for water shutoff often lack a wellbore model that can handle non-Newtonian flow behavior of gels and do not fully integrate dynamic models between the wellbore and the reservoir.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Further research is needed to explore a wider range of nanomaterials (e.g., Al2O3, TiO2, ZnO) for EOR applications and investigate the compatibility with different polymers and compositions. More studies are required to understand the long-term behavior of these gels over extended periods in various reservoir conditions.
