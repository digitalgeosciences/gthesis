# ASSESSMENT OF ORGANOTINS IN SEDIMENTS AND BIOTA COLLECTED FROM THE SAUDI COAST OF THE ARABIAN GULF

**Summary**: The thesis investigated six species of organotin compounds (MBT, MPhT, DBT, DPhT, TBT, and TPhT) in sediment and biota samples collected from four locations along the Saudi coast of the Arabian Gulf. The study found that while most concentrations were low, some areas exceeded safety limits, and a significant correlation was observed between organotin concentration and distance from the shoreline.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of baseline information on organotin contamination in the marine environment along the Arabian Gulf coast of Saudi Arabia, despite the known toxicity of these compounds to marine life and humans.

## Objectives

- study the distribution and concentration of organotin compounds in sediment samples collected from selected locations
- study the level of organotins contamination in biota samples collected from selected locations
- study the effect of environmental and water quality parameters on the concentration of organotin compounds in sediments and biota

## Methods

The study utilized GC-MS (Agilent 6890N System) for the detection and quantification of six organotin species. Sediment samples were prepared by air drying, grinding, and sieving before extraction using an Accelerated solvent extractor or dichloromethane. Biota samples (bivalves) were washed, freeze-dried, and ground before solid phase extraction. Water quality parameters (temperature, dissolved oxygen, pH, salinity) were measured in situ using the Eutech CyberScan PC 650 and Atago Hand refractometer. Total Organic Carbon (TOC) was determined using a Shimadzu TOC-V CPN analyzer. Grain size analysis was performed via dry and wet sieving. Statistical analyses including ANOVA, Gamma regression, and correlation/regression analysis were conducted using R and Microsoft Excel.

## Data and Materials

Sediment samples from 10 stations in each of the four locations (Jubail, Khobar, Qatif, Half Moon Bay) at distances of 0m, 250m, 500m, and 1000m from the shoreline. Biota samples consisting of bivalves (Meretrix meretrix and Chama species). Antifouling paint samples collected from Jubail harbour.

## Key Findings

- The concentration ranges of MBT, MPhT, DBT, and DPhT in sediment samples ranged from not detected to 345.2 ng/g, 655.2 ng/g, 126.16 ng/g, and 673.3 ng/g respectively. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 11]])
- In the biota samples, only TBT (3.4 – 12.92 ng/g) and TPhT (14 – 39.9 ng/g) were detected. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 11]])
- Samples from Jubail harbour were significantly different from samples from the control location (p<0.01). ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 11]])
- The distance from the shoreline was significantly related to the organotin concentrations, with an exponential decrease in concentration as distance increased. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 42]])
- MPhT was the most abundant of all six organotin species in the sampled locations (1530.10 ± 243.17 ng/g). ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 41]])
- DPhT was not found at all in Qatif harbour and Half Moon Bay. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 41]])
- The average concentration of the six organotin species in sediment samples was 423.89 ± 331.3 ng/g. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 41]])
- TBT and TPhT were below detection limits in all four sampling locations for sediment, but were the only species found in biota samples. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 48]])
- The average concentration of TPhT in Khobar was 20.07 ± 3.27 ng/g and in Qatif was 24.13 ± 7.88 ng/g. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 48]])
- Several sediment samples from Jubail, Khobar, and Qatif exceeded the 5 ng/g Australian environmental quality guidelines for TBT. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 43]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

- monitor organotin levels in sediment and biota for developing baseline information for the western Arabian Gulf
- develop environmental guidelines and standards for organotin level in sediment and biota samples from the Arabian Gulf
- Regularly monitor organotin contaminants to assess persistence in the coastal environment.
