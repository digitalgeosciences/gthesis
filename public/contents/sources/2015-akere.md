# ASSESSMENTS OF ORGANOTINS IN SEDIMENTS AND BIOTA COLLECTED FROM THE SAUDI COAST OF THE ARABIAN GULF

**Summary**: The thesis assesses the concentration and distribution of six organotin compounds (MBT, MPhT, DBT, DPhT, TBT, and TPhT) in sediment and biota samples collected from four locations along the Saudi coast of the Arabian Gulf. The study concludes that while most organotins were low or not detected in sediments, specific concentrations were found in biota, and a significant correlation was observed between distance from the shoreline and organotin levels.

---

## Research Problem

The accumulation of toxic organotin compounds (e.g., TBT) from antifouling paints in marine environments and the subsequent risk to benthic organisms and local biota along the Saudi coast of the Arabian Gulf.

## Objectives

- Assess the distribution and concentration of organtin compounds in sediment samples collected from selected locations
- Determine the level of organotins contamination in biota samples collected from selected locations
- Evaluate the effect of environmental and water quality parameters on the concentration of organotin compounds in sediments and just biota
- Determine the concentration of six organotin species (MBT, DBT, TBT, MPhT, DPhT, TPhT) in sediment samples from Jubail, Khobar, Qatif, and Half Moon Bay.
- Assess the concentration of organotins in biota (bivalves) collected from Khobar and Qatif harbors.
- Analyze the impact of distance from the shoreline on organotin concentrations in sediments.
- Evaluate the presence of organotins in local antifouling paint samples.
- Investigate six species of organotin compounds in sediment and biota samples from Jubail, Khobar, Qatif, and Half Moon Bay.
- Determine the relationship between total organotins and sampling locations.
- Evaluate the impact of distance from the shoreline on organotin concentration.
- Analyze the correlation between organotins and physico-chemical parameters (temperature, pH, salinity, dissolved oxygen, and TOC).
- Investigate six species of organotin compounds in sediment samples from Jubail, Khobar, Qatif, and Half Moon Bay.
- Assess the concentration of organotins in biota (clams and oysters) collected from these locations.
- Determine the relationship between total organotins, sampling locations, and distances from the shoreline.
- Evaluate the impact of maritime activities like ship painting on local marine environments.

## Methods

Sediment samples were collected at 10 stations across four locations (Jubail, Khobar, Qatif, Half Moon Bay) and analyzed for six organotin species using GC-MS. Biota (Meretrix meretrix and Chama species) were collected from Khobar and Qatif harbors. Sediment analysis included sieve-analysis for grain size, TOC determination via furnace combustion, and extraction/derivation of organotins using dichloromethane and methyl magnesium bromide. Antifouling paint samples were analyzed to confirm the source of contamination. Statistical analyses (ANOVA, Gamma regression) were performed in R and Excel.

## Data and Materials

Sediment samples from 40 locations across 4 sites (10 per site: Jubail, Khobar, Qatif, Half Moon Bay). Biota samples collected from the same areas. Analytical data for six organotins (MBT, MPhT, DBT, DPhT, TBT, TPhT) and environmental parameters (Temp, pH, Salinity, DO, TOC).

## Key Findings

- Concentration ranges of MBT, MPhT, DBT, and DPhT in sediment samples ranged from not detected to 345.2 ng/g, 655.2 ng/g, 126.16 ng/g, and 673.3 ng/g respectively. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 11]])
- In biota samples, only TBT (3.4 – 12.92 ng/g) and TPhT (14 – 39.9 ng/g) were detected. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 11]])
- Samples from Jubail harbour were significantly different from samples from the control location (p<0.01). ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 11]])
- No relationship was observed between physico-chemical parameters and the concentrations of organotins. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 11]])
- The distance from the shoreline was significantly related to the organotin concentrations. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 11]])
- The average concentration of the six organotin species in sediment samples was 423.89±331.3 ng/g. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 30]])
- MPhT was the most abundant organotin species in sediments, with a total concentration of 1530.10±243.17 ng/g. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 31]])
- DPhT concentrations were 138.79±70.04 ng/g in Jubail and 2.15±2.15 ng/g in Khobar. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015]])

- Organotin concentrations in sediment samples from Jubail harbour were significantly higher than those in Khobar and Qatif. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 58]])
- Some stations in the study area exceeded the 5 ng/g allowable limit for sediment set by Australian environmental quality guidelines. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 58]])
- Relatively high levels of organotins were observed close to the shoreline (0-250m). ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 58]])
- Note: The study found that environmental parameters (temperature, pH, salinity) did not show a significant influence or correlation with organotin concentrations. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015]])
- Organotin levels in biota samples from Khobar and Qatif were very low compared to other parts of the world. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 58]])
- Organotin levels showed an increasing trend with the increase in size of the bivalves collected from the study area. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 58]])
