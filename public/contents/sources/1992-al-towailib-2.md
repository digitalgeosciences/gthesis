# A New Correlation for Two-Phase Flow Through Chokes

**Summary**: The thesis develops a new empirical correlation for determining choke size in two-phase flow systems based on 3,554 production test data points from ten Middle East fields. The study focuses on critical flow conditions and utilizes non-linear multiple regression to evaluate several independent variables including oil flow rate, upstream tubing pressure, and mixture relative density.

---

## Research Problem

The difficulty in accurately selecting optimal choke sizes for multi-phase flow (oil/gas) due to the complexity of fluid behavior at high velocities and the limitations or inaccuracies of existing empirical correlations in various field conditions.

## Objectives

- Develop a new empirical correlation for multiphase flow through chokes relating choke size, flow rate, upstream and downstream tubing pressures, gas-oil ratio, and other pertinent variables.
- Perform statistical error analysis to validate the new correlation against existing ones.
- Develop a new empirical correlation for determining optimum choke size for design purposes.
- Incorporate mixture relative density as a key parameter in the flow mechanism study.
- Predict oil flow rates when direct measurement is difficult or unavailable.
- Validate the new correlation against existing models using diverse datasets.

## Methods

The study utilized non-linear multiple regression to develop a formulation for choke size. Data screening was performed to remove single-phase flow cases (where upstream pressure exceeded bubble point) and subcritical flow cases (where downstream pressure was >55% of upstream). Statistical analysis included T-distribution tests, error distribution analysis, error elimination analysis, and sensitivity analysis. The final correlation was refined by fixing regression coefficients to the nearest rounded values to ensure stability.

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes field data from the Middle East including three distinct validation sets: Poetmann & Beck (1963), Majeed-Ghassan, and a collection of 300 production test points from Middle East fields not used in the initial development phase.

## Key Findings

- The new correlation (S = 20.696 * q_o^a * P_us^b * Y_m^c) outperformed all published correlations in literature for the specific data set used. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 20]])
- The average absolute percent relative error dropped from 8.4% to 3.41% after 50% of the data was eliminated during the error elimination analysis. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 82]])
- T-statistics for all coefficients were significantly different from zero at the 99 percent confidence level, confirming that explanatory variables are meaningful predictors. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 82]])
- The error distribution of the correlation showed a normal distribution with a mean of zero error. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 82]])
- The new correlation achieved average absolute relative errors of 8% for choke size and 18% for flow rate, outperforming existing models like Omana (165% and 81%) and Fortunati (24% and 71%). ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 98]])
- Mixture relative density was identified as a critical parameter that significantly influences the correlation across all choke size ranges. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 83]])
- The new correlation showed consistent superiority over existing models in both statistical and graphical analyses for multiple flow conditions. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 102]])
- The study determined that the influence of wellhead pressure on the correlation is significantly reduced at high values, suggesting less need for precise measurement in those ranges. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 83]])
- The new correlation was validated against three independent datasets (Poetmann & Beck, Majeed-Ghassan, and Middle East data), consistently outperforming or matching the best existing correlations. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 131]])

## Limitations

The study notes that some existing correlations (e.g., Omana) are limited by specific choke sizes, flow rates, and pressures; however, the new correlation was designed to cover wider ranges.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The new correlation is recommended for use in cases where measurement of flow rates is not handy or difficult to perform, and it is applicable to wide ranges of flow conditions including high-pressure systems common in Saudi Arabia.
