# EVALUATING THE REMOVAL EFFICIENCY OF SEWAGE SLUDGE-DERIVED ACTIVATED CARBON FOR METHYL-TERT BUTYL ETHER CONTAMINATED - WATER.

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the synthesis and characterization of sewage sludge-derived activated carbon (SDAC) for the removal of Methyl Tert-Butyl Ether (MTBE) from contaminated water. The study concludes that SDAC, specifically sample 2Z-700-90, is an effective adsorbent with a surface area of 385.08 m2/g and follows pseudo-second-order kinetics.

---

## Research Problem

The accumulation of sewage sludge poses environmental risks, while the high cost and limited availability of commercial activated carbon (CAC) present challenges in treating water contaminated with MTBE, a persistent and potentially carcinogenic groundwater pollutant.

## Objectives

- To synthesize and characterize the activated carbon derived from the sewage sludge.
- To demonstrate the removal of MTBE from contaminated water using the optimized SDAC.
- To study the effect of solution conditions such as pH, SDAC dosage, contact time, and MTBE concentration on the adsorption efficiency.
- To evaluate the adsorption isotherms and kinetics under the optimum treatment conditions.
- Assess the removal efficiency of SDAC for MTBE in contaminated aqueous solutions.
- Evaluate the impact of various treatment parameters (pH, contact time, dosage, concentration, agitation speed, temperature) on MTBE adsorption.

## Methods

SDAC was synthesized by chemically activating sewage sludge with zinc chloride and pyrolyzing it. Characterization included BET surface area analysis, TGA for thermal stability/composition, FTIR to identify functional groups (OH, COOH, C=O), SEM-EDX for morphology and elemental composition, and GC-MS for quantifying MTBE concentrations in aqueous solutions. Batch adsorption experiments were conducted varying pH (3-9), contact time (15-180 min), dosage (0.5-4 g/L), initial concentration (500-4000 μg/L), agitation speed (50-200 rpm), and temperature (15-40 C).

## Data and Materials

Four types of SDAC samples (3K-700-30, 2Z-700-90, 2K-500-90, 2Z-500-30), raw sewage sludge (RAW), and commercial activated carbon (CAC). Analysis included GC-MS of MTBE concentrations in 10 samples over various time intervals.

## Key Findings

- The surface area values for the four SDAC samples after acid washing were 451 m2/g, 385 m2/g, 53 m2/g, and 38 m2/g respectively. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 12]])
- SEM-EDX analysis showed that minerals (Fe, Al, Mg, Mn, Ca, Na) and biological components were removed by higher pyrolytic temperatures and acid-water washing. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 12]])
- FTIR identified hydroxyl groups, carboxylates, and aliphatic -CH3 as the primary functional groups of the adsorbent. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 12]])
- Adsorbent 2Z-700-90 was selected for its superior properties: yield of 70%, thermal stability of 50%, and BET surface area of 385 m2/g. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 12]])
- Adsorbent 2Z-700-90 achieved a 100% removal efficiency (stated as 70% in abstract summary) for MTBE at pH 6, 150 rpm agitation, 60 min contact time, and 2g/L dosage. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 12]])
- The adsorption behavior of SDAC followed pseudo-second-order kinetics. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 12]])
- Raw sludge analysis showed moisture content of 84.65% for fresh samples and 15.58% for dry samples. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 35]])
- TOC in raw sludge was measured at 4523 mg/kg (fresh) and 27325 mg/kg (dry). ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 35]])
- The SDAC sample 3K-700-30 exhibited the highest BET surface area at 450.99 m2/g. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 61]])
- All adsorbents (except RAW) were identified as mesoporous with pore sizes between 20 and 500 Å. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 64]])
- FTIR analysis confirmed the presence of hydroxyl functional groups (-OH) in SDAC, which enhance adsorption capacity. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 66]])
- TGA results showed significant weight losses (>30%) between 300-850 C, attributed to the decomposition of organic and inorganic substances. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 68]])
- EDX confirmed that acid-washing successfully removed most metal impurities (K, Ca, Al, Mg, Zn, Fe) from the SDAC. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 75]])
- The surface zero point of charge (pHzpc) for the SDAC was determined to be 7.16. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 82]])
- MTBE removal efficiency peaked at pH 6, reaching 45% for SDAC and 65% for CAC. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 83]])

## Limitations

The study notes that the adsorption capacity of SDAC (specifically 2Z-700-90) was lower than some other materials like non-polar porous polymers, suggesting a need for further improvement in adsorbent preparation.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Recommendations include: performing continuous packed column studies; conducting pilot-scale experiments; exploring other adsorbents; investigating combined treatment methods (oxidation-adsorption or bio-degradation-adsorption); and experimenting with different activation reagents like H3PO4 or H2SO4.
