# DEVELOPMENT OF A NEW FRACUTURING FLUID SYSTEM FOR TIGHT RESERVOIRS

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the development of a new fracturing fluid system for tight reservoirs using a combination of GLDA and AMPS polymers. It evaluates the chemical stability, viscosity thickening, and degradation behavior of these fluids in both fresh water and seawater environments through FTIR analysis and core flooding experiments.

---

## Research Problem

Tight gas reservoirs are highly susceptible to 'aqueous phase trapping' (water block) due to low permeability and high capillary pressure, which significantly impairs production when water-based fracturing fluids are used. Traditional fracturing fluids require many additives, making them complex and costly to manage.

## Objectives

- Develop a new smart fracturing fluid system using GLDA as a cross-linker, breaker, biocide, and clay stabilizer.
- Evaluate the performance of the new fluid in terms of rheology, fluid loss, and filter cake integrity.
- Validate the effectiveness of the fluid in core flooding experiments on both low (Scioto) and high (Berea) permeability sandstones.
- Assess the ability of GLDA to reduce interfacial tension (IFT) and eliminate water blockage.
- Develop a simple-fluid system for Hydraulic-Fracturing (at high pH) purposes using Chelating agents as an acid, cross-linker, breaker, and biocide.
- Evaluate the developed fluid in terms of stability under HPHT conditions, breaking/cross-linking efficiency, and rheological properties.

## Methods

The study involved synthesizing fracturing fluids with various concentrations of GLDA and different polymer types. Rheological profiles were measured under specific conditions (300°F, pH 12, 511 s^-1). FTIR was used to identify chemical functional groups (Amide and Carboxyl) responsible for fluid behavior. TGA was performed to assess the thermal degradation of polymers. Core flooding experiments were conducted on Scioto Sandstone (low permeability) and Berea Sandstone (high permeability) to measure initial and return permeability after fluid treatment.

## Data and Materials

The research utilizes Scioto and Berea sandstone core samples (low and high permeability). Experimental parameters include testing at temperatures up to 300°F and varying concentrations of GLDA or polymers. The study evaluates multiple chemical components including cross-linkers, breakers, biocides, and clay stabilizers.

## Key Findings

- The new fluid system using GLDA can be used for both proppant fracturing (high pH) and acid fracturing (low pH) in sandstone formations. ([[2017-mohammed|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 16]])
- GLDA acts as a cross-linker, breaker, biocide, and clay stabilizer, replacing multiple components of traditional fluids into one system. ([[2017-mohammed|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 16]])
- The new fluid system is stable in high temperature reservoirs up to 300°F and remains stable for about six hours under high shearing rates (511 s^-1). ([[2017-mohammed|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 17]])
- GLDA is a low IFT fluid, which reduces the interfacial tension to eliminate the Aqueous Phase Trapping (APT) effect. ([[2017-mohammed|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 21]])
- The new formulation breaks on its own; the delay time can be controlled by adjusting concentrations of GLDA or polymer. ([[2017-mohammed|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 17]])
- Coreflooding experiments showed that the fluid provides high return permeability in both Scioto and Berea sandstone cores. ([[2017-mohammed|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 17]])
- Tight gas reservoirs are defined as those with permeability less than 0.5 mD for oil and 0.01 mD for gas. ([[2017-mohammed|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 20]])
- Water-based fluids can cause the reservoir to entrap water, reducing the relative permeability of gas in the flushed zone. ([[2017-mohammed|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 39]])
- Viscoelastic surfactants (VES) are effective for proppant transportation and have a return permeability approaching 100% due to wettability altering characteristics. ([[2017-mohammed|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 40]])
- Methanol-based fluids can be used to reduce APT in formations with sub-irreducible water saturation. ([[2017-mohammed|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 41]])
- GLDA successfully functions as an acid, cross-linker, breaker, and biocide for polymers at high pH (12) and low pH (4). ([[2017-mohammed|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 61]])
- GLDA is a low IFT fluid; at high pH, it reacts with salts to create a lower IFT than the initial, reducing aqueous phase trapping. ([[2017-mohammed|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 62]])
- The proposed fluid system can replace up to 8 additives typically required in conventional fracturing fluids (Buffer, Acid, Biocide, Clay Stabilizer, Gel Stabilizer, Cross-linker, Breaker, Surfactant). ([[2017-mohammed|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 63]])
- Berea sandstone core samples exhibited permeability between 150 and 200 mD. ([[2017-mohammed|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 74]])
- TGA analysis showed HPG had the lowest mass loss (11.63%), followed by XC (12.83%), AMPS (13.3%), HPAM (13.8%), and TVP (18.5%) under conditions up to 350°F. ([[2017-mohammed|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 83]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Use 20 wt.% GLDA (at pH 12) mixed with 45 pptg AMPS in fresh water for acid diversion stimulation.
- Use 5 wt.% GLDA (pH 12) mixed with 45 pptg AMPS in seawater as a candidate to replace slick water in shale and tight gas reservoirs.
- Use 20 wt.% GLDA (at pH 12) mixed with 70 pptg XC polymer for stimulating conventional sandstones efficiently.
