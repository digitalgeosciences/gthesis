# INTEGRATIVE CHARACTERIZATION AND MODELING OF THE PERMO-TRIASSIC CARBONATES, SOUTHCENTRAL SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the characterization and modeling of Permo-Triassic carbonate reservoir microfacies in the Rub’ Al-Khali Basin, Saudi Arabia. It integrates core analysis (XRD, SEM, XRF) with well-log data to evaluate mineralogy, porosity, and acoustic properties, ultimately using geostatistics and artificial neural networks (ANN) to model spatial variability.

---

## Research Problem

The Permo-Triassic carbonates in the Rub\u2019 Al-Khali Basin are tight, unconventional reservoirs with complex microfacies and mineralogy. There is a need for integrated modeling techniques (geostatistics and ANN) to predict reservoir properties like elemental composition and lithology efficiently during early exploration stages.

## Objectives

- Detailed carbonate microfacies analysis using available cores and well-logs.
- Study the porosity patterns of these carbonates qualitatively and quantitatively.
- Investigate the acoustic properties as a crucial tool for tight carbonates, using available cores and well-logs.
- Explore the elemental geochemistry and mineralogy of the Permo-Triassic carbonates using available cores and well-logs.
- Investigate the potential of variability modeling and ANN in regional correlation and prediction purposes, in the light of studied reservoir properties.
- Characterize and model late Permian-Early Triassic carbonate reservoir microfacies using core and well-log data.

## Methods

The study utilized a 30 ft (9.14 m) core section from Well-X in the Rub’ Al-Khali Basin. Core analysis included: 1) Petrography and SEM to characterize microfacies, pore types, and microporosity; 2) XRD (X'Pert3 Malvern Panalytical) for mineralogy; 3) XRF (M4 Tornado Bruker) for elemental geochemistry as a proxy for mineralogy; 4) TOC analysis for organic content. Well-log data (GR, NPHI, RHOB, DT) were used to validate core findings and for modeling. Geostatistical modeling involved directional variogram analysis to assess spatial continuity of geochemical properties. Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) were implemented using three different architectures to predict elemental composition and member identification based on a combination of mineralogy, geochemistry, and well-log data.

## Data and Materials

The study utilized core samples from Well-X in the Southcentral Saudi Arabia region, covering a section of 30 ft. Data included 11 identified microfacies (MF1-MF11), XRD mineralogy data for multiple samples, XRF elemental maps, and acoustic velocity measurements taken at high resolution.

## Key Findings

- Core samples were characterized by dominant SEM intercrystalline microporosity of 3-18% and extremely low permeability, indicating a tight, unconventional reservoir setting. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 14]])
- Ten carbonate microfacies were identified, covering various carbonate textures ranging from mudstone to grainstone and floatstone. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 14]])
- XRD mineralogy analysis showed dolomite-rich upper parts with better reservoir quality (intercrystalline microporosity and fractures) and calcite-dominant lower parts. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 14]])
- Quartz and illite were specifically associated with organic-rich mudstone microfacies. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 14]])
- The presence of fractures, stylolites, poor sorting, and organic matter reduced and distorted acoustic velocities for given microporosity regardless of carbonate texture. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 14]])
- Directional variograms showed spatial continuity in geochemical composition with minimal variation and negligible nugget effects for Ca and S. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 15]])
- PCA showed that the input training data could be grouped into three major components capturing more than 80% of the variance. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 15]])
- The simplest ANN model (Architecture_1) performed best, balancing complexity with predictive accuracy for elemental composition and member identification. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 15]])
- The study identified 11 distinct carbonate microfacies, including evaporites (MF1), dolomitic mudstone (MF2), and microbialite (MF11). ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 57]])
- Dolomitic mudstone (MF2) was the most porous unit among the studied carbonate microfacies, with a range of 9.79-18.3% microporosity. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 63]])
- Nitrogen permeability measurements showed extremely low values in all microfacies (around 0.1 mD), except for areas with significant fractures which reached 10s or 100s of mD. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 77]])
- Acoustic velocity was found to have a weak to moderate inverse relationship with microporosity across most microfacies. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 92]])
- Dolomite content in the upper part of the section (0-11.25 ft) reached up to 90% in some samples, while the lower part (14-30 ft) was dominated by calcite. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 89]])
- The presence of organic matter (TOC) and minerals like illite were found to reduce acoustic velocity significantly in microfacies MF4 and MF11. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 91]])
- S-content was identified as a proxy for dolomitization in a hypersaline setting, showing a strong direct relationship with Mg content. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 72]])

## Limitations

The model's prediction weakness was noted in members M-II and M-III because they did not hold distinct enough diagnostic characteristics compared to M-I and M-IV. Additionally, the distance between Well-M1 and other wells contributed to some limitations in predictive accuracy for certain units.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The author suggests using simpler architectures like Architecture_1 for exploration tasks due to their balance of complexity and performance. They also recommend exploring ensemble machine learning (tree-based algorithms) as an alternative to neural networks for predicting elements like TOC or other mineral components.
