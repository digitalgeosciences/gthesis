# INTEGRATIVE CHARACTERIZATION AND MODELING OF THE PERMO-TRIASSIC CARBONATES, SOUTHCENTRAL SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the Permo-Triassic carbonate reservoir microfacies in Southcentral Saudi Arabia using a combination of core analysis, well-log data, and advanced imaging techniques. It characterizes various microfacies (MF1-MF11) to understand their petrophysical properties, specifically focusing on intercrystalline microporosity and its relationship with crystal size and mineralogy.

---

## Research Problem

The integration of elemental composition with well-log data using AI/ML is under-explored for predicting lithology in Permo-Triassic carbonate successions. Traditional methods often fail to utilize 30-40% of available data, and manual laboratory analysis for mineralogy and element determination is costly and time-consuming.

## Methods

The study utilized a multi-step workflow: 1. Data Acquisition: Well-logs from six vertical wells were collected, including conventional logs (GR, RHOB, NPHI, and acoustic slowness) and Elemental Capture Spectroscopy (ECS) for major elements (Ca, Si, Al, Fe, S). 2. Statistical Analysis: Principal Component Analysis (PCA) was used to reduce dimensionality of the input data; Hierarchical Clustering was performed to identify natural groupings in the dataset; Correlation Coefficient analysis determined relationships between minerals and elements. 3. Machine Learning Modeling: Three Artificial Neural Network (ANN) architectures were designed: Architecture_1 (two dense layers with dropout), Architecture_2 (four dense layers with dropout), and Architecture_3 (two dense layers with batch normalization). These models were trained, validated, and tested to predict both mineralogy/lithology and elemental concentrations.

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes core samples from a section of 30 ft in the Permo-Triassic carbonate sequence. It includes 11 distinct microfacies types (MF1-MF11). Data include XRD mineralogy results, micro-XRF elemental maps, SEM images for pore/crystal size analysis, and wireline log data (Density, Neutron) for petrophysical correlation.

## Key Findings

- Core samples exhibited intercrystalline microporosity ranging from 3% to 18% with extremely low permeability. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 14]])
- Ten carbonate microfacies were identified, covering textures from mudstone to grainstone and floatstone. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 14]])
- XRD mineralogy showed dolomite-rich upper sections with better reservoir quality (microporosity/fractures) and calcite-dominant lower sections. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 14]])
- XRF elemental geochemistry served as a good proxy for mineral composition, aligning with XRD results. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 14]])
- Fractures, stylolites, poor sorting, and organic matter were found to reduce and distort acoustic velocities regardless of carbonate texture. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 14]])
- Directional variograms showed spatial continuity in geochemical composition with minimal variation and negligible nugget effects for Ca and S. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 15]])
- PCA revealed that input training data could be grouped into three major components capturing over 80% of the variance. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 15]])
- The simplest ANN model (Architecture_1) performed best, balancing complexity and accuracy for predicting elemental composition and members. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 15]])
- The Khuff Formation is a major reservoir for non-associated gas with approximately 750 TCF of gas in the region. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 36]])
- The carbonate-evaporite units (Khuff-A, B, and C) are characterized by transgressive porous and tight limestones and dolomites. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 34]])
- Mudstones, mud-supported packstones, and wackestone have low porosity (about 2.5%) and permeability (around 1md) in deeply buried reservoirs. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 36]])
- The Khuff Formation contains 5-10% evaporites with individual bed thickness up to several meters. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 33]])
- A sharp negative shift in carbon isotope (delta13C) at the basal part of the Khuff-B reservoir indicates a Permian-Triassic extinction event. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 35]])
- The study identified five main microfacies categories: evaporites (MF1), mudstone/mud-dominated (MF2, MF4, MF5, MF6), grainstone/grain-supported (MF3, MF10, MF7, and MF9), mixed microfacies (microbialites) (MF11), and floatstone (MF8). ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 54]])
- Evaporites (MF1) are composed of anhydrite and occur as separated thin layers (6 cm and 7 cm) within the carbonate sequence. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 54]])

## Limitations

The application of models to an entirely unseen well (well-M1) resulted in lower overall accuracy (<50%) for lithology identification compared to the training set, highlighting challenges in cross-well generalization.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Future work should focus on refining model parameters and potentially using more complex architectures or hybrid models to improve consistency across all elements, particularly where Architecture_3 showed promise but lacked precision.
