# Evaluation of New Flowback Formulation for Hydraulically Fractured Tight Gas Wells

**Summary**: The thesis evaluates the performance of environmentally friendly ionic liquid flowback additives (X1, X2, and X3) in high salinity and temperature conditions typical of hydraulic fracturing. Experimental results showed that longer carbon chains and the presence of divalent ions significantly improved surface tension reduction and interfacial tension, while core flood tests demonstrated a 25% increase in regained permeability compared to standard formulations.

---

## Research Problem

The loss of fracturing fluids in tight gas formations leads to water blockage and formation damage due to high salinity and temperature conditions that often render conventional flowback additives ineffective.

## Objectives

- Assess the performance and effectiveness of new environmentally friendly flowback additives at high salinities and temperatures.
- Compare ionic liquid-based formulations with commercially used flowback additives.
- Evaluate the impact of carbon chain length on surface tension reduction.
- Determine the effect of different salt compositions (e.g., divalent ions) on the performance of ionic liquids.

## Methods

The study utilized a variety of experimental techniques: Surface tension was measured using a KRUSS K100 tensiometer to determine Critical Micelle Concentration (CMC). Interfacial Tension (IFT) was measured using a KRUSS spinning drop tensiometer at temperatures up to 90°C. Wettability was assessed via contact angle measurements on quartz slides using the sessile drop method. Core flood experiments were conducted on Kentucky sandstone cores with permeability of 0.1 md, involving three stages: initial formation brine injection, first drainage (N2), and flowback (N2) after fracturing fluid injection. NMR was used to quantify water volume and distribution within the core samples.

## Data and Materials

The study analyzed three ionic liquids with different carbon chain lengths (X1, X2, X3). Two sets of Kentucky sandstone cores were used for coreflood experiments (Core_1 without additives, Core_2 with 0.5 gpt of X3). Experimental fluids included a 5 wt% KCl brine and a complex fracturing base fluid with high salinity (TDS ~58,857 ppm) and temperatures up to 90°C.

## Key Findings

- Surface tension values for the four chemical formulations ranged from 30 to 45 mN/m with CMC values between 0.5 to 1 gpt. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 14]])
- The lowest IFT value of 3 mN/m was achieved in the case of formulation x3. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 14]])
- Surface tension values for X1, X2, and X3 were observed to be 47 mN/m, 44 mN/m, and 39 mN/m respectively in 5 wt% KCl brine. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 31]])
- CMC values were quantified at 700 ppm for X1, 500 ppm for X2, and 300 ppm for X3 in 5 wt% KCl brine. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 32]])
- In the presence of divalent ions (fracturing fluid), surface tension values were recorded at 42.5 mN/m for x1, 41.3 mN/m for x2, and 39 mN/m for x3. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 33]])
- CMC values in the fracturing fluid base were quantified at 1000 ppm for X1, 600 ppm for X2, and 400 ppm for X3. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 35]])
- IFT of x3 in a 5 wt% KCl solution stabilized at 14.5 mN/m across temperatures from 50°C to 90°C. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 36]])
- In the fracturing-based fluid, IFT for x3 stabilized at 6.2 mN/m at 50°C, 7 mN/m at 70°C, and 8.2 mN/m at 90°C. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 36]])
- Contact angle measurements showed a reduction from 45° (base fluid) to 33° (with ionic liquid), indicating a shift toward a more water-wet surface. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 39]])
- The use of ionic liquids improved regained permeability by 25% compared to the base case, enhancing fracturing fluid recovery to approximately 56% of the injected volume. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 48]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

The findings suggest that ionic liquids are a viable and environmentally friendly alternative for flowback additives, especially in high temperature and salinity environments where conventional additives may fail.
