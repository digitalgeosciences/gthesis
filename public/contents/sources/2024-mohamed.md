# Evaluation of New Flowback Formulation for Hydraulically Fractured Tight Gas Wells

**Summary**: The thesis evaluates the performance of new environmentally friendly ionic liquid-based flowback additives (x1, x2, x3) for shale gas wells under harsh conditions. The study uses surface tension, interfacial tension (IFT), and wettability measurements on Kentucky sandstone cores to compare these additives against conventional surfactants.

---

## Research Problem

The primary problem is the loss of injected fluids during hydraulic fracturing in unconventional reservoirs due to high capillary forces, which leads to poor flowback and formation damage. Existing surfactants often fail under extreme conditions like high salinity (57,000 ppm) and high temperatures (up to 90°C).

## Objectives

- Assess the performance and effectiveness of new environmentally friendly flowback additives at high salinities and temperatures.
- Compare ionic liquid-based formulations with commercially used flowback additives.
- Determine Critical Micelle Concentration (CMC) for different chemical formulations.
- Measure interfacial tension (IFT) between crude oil and various flowback additives.
- Evaluate changes in rock wettability after treatment with flowback additives.
- Evaluate the surface tension reduction properties of different ionic liquids (X1, X2, X3) in various brine solutions.
- Measure the impact of ionic liquid concentration and carbon chain length on surface tension and Critical Micelle Concentration (CMC).
- Determine the interfacial tension (IFT) between fracturing fluids and hydrocarbons using different salt compositions and temperatures.
- Assess the effect of ionic liquids on rock wettability through contact angle measurements.
- Evaluate the effectiveness of flowback additives in regaining formation permeability and recovering fractured fluid volume.
- Evaluate environmentally friendly flowback additives for shale gas wells
- Mitigate formation damage with hydraulic fracturing operations
- Assess the impact of surfactants on well deliverability under harsh conditions (high salinity, high temperature)

## Methods

Surface tension was measured using a KRUSS K100 tensiometer. Interfacial tension (IFT) was measured using a KRUSS spinning drop tensiometer at temperatures up to 90°C. Wettability was assessed via contact angle measurements using the sessile drop method and a drop shape analyzer on quartz slides pretreated with formation brine. Coreflood experiments were conducted on tight Kentucky sandstone cores; core properties (porosity, permeability) were determined using gas porosimetry and NMR. The flowback process involved N2 injection in both forward and reverse directions to evaluate permeability recovery and fluid displacement.

## Data and Materials

The study used three types of methylimidazolium chloride ionic liquids (x1, x2, x3) with varying carbon chain lengths. Samples included Kentucky sandstone cores with a permeability of 0.1 md (dimensions: 1.5 in. diameter, 3 in. length). Fluids included formation brine (TDS 213,734 ppm), fracture base fluid (TDS 58,857 ppm), and light crude oil.

## Key Findings

- Surface tension values for the four tested formulations ranged from 30 to 45 mN/m. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 14]])
- CMC values for the different chemical formulations were found to be between 0.5 and 1 gpt. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 14]])
- The lowest interfacial tension (IFT) value of 3 mN/m was achieved with formulation x3. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 14]])
- Contact angle measurements confirmed that treatment with flowback additives changed the rock surface to be water-wet. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 14]])
- Kentucky sandstone cores used in experiments contained 66% Quartz, 14% Illite, 3% K-Feldspar, and 17% Plagioclase. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 24]])
- Ionic liquid X3 showed the best performance, with surface tension values of 47 mN/m (in 5wt% KCl), 44 mN/m (for X2), and 39 mN/m (for X3) as carbon chain length increased. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 31]])
- CMC values were quantified at 700 ppm for X1, 500 ppm for X2, and 300 ppm for X3 in 5wt% KCl brine. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 32]])
- In a fracturing fluid base, surface tension values were recorded at 42.5 mN/m for x1, 41.3 mN/m for x2, and 39 mN/m for x3. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 33]])
- CMC values in fracturing fluid base were quantified at 1000 ppm for X1, 600 ppm for X2, and 400 ppm for X3. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 35]])
- IFT of X3 in a 5 wt% KCl solution stabilized at 14.5 mN/m, while in fracturing base fluid it stabilized at 6.2 mN/m at 50°C. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 36]])
- IFT in the fracturing-based fluid was lower than in the 5 wt% KCl brine, confirming that divalent salts improved ionic liquid performance. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 36]])
- Contact angle for 5 wt% KCl in N2 was 45°; with the addition of ionic liquid X3, it decreased to 33°, indicating a shift toward more water-wet conditions. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 39]])
- The use of ionic liquids improved regained permeability by 25% compared to the base case. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 47]])
- Flowback additives (X3) enhanced fracturing fluid recovery to approximately 56% of the injected volume. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 48]])
- Ionic liquids are identified as promising compounds for sustainable chemical processes and applications in the oilfield. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 51]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

The study suggests that environmentally friendly formulations, such as the investigated ionic liquids, can mitigate water blockage and reduce formation damage in hydraulic fracturing operations under high temperature and salinity.
