# Digital Outcrop Modeling of The Early Triassic Upper Khartam Member of Khuff Formation; Implications for Reservoir Heterogeneity and Quality, Central Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the reservoir heterogeneity and quality of the Upper Khartam Member of the Khuff Formation in Central Saudi Arabia using a combination of field sedimentology, stratigraphy, and digital outcrop modeling. By integrating LiDAR data with petrophysical properties (SGR) and laboratory analyses (SEM, XRD), the study aims to provide a better understanding of reservoir architecture and its implications for subsurface production.

---

## Research Problem

The Khuff Formation exhibits significant vertical and lateral heterogeneities that are difficult to capture fully using only subsurface data due to limited well spacing. The Upper Khartam Member serves as an excellent analog for the upper parts of the Khuff-A reservoirs, requiring detailed outcrop study to understand reservoir quality.

## Objectives

- Perform Digital Outcrop Modeling (DOM) of the Upper Khartam Member to visualize and quantify bed thicknesses and geometries.
- Analyze sedimentological and stratigraphical features including facies analysis and high-resolution stratigraphy.
- Evaluate reservoir heterogeneity and quality by comparing field data with petrophysical measurements from multiple vertical sections.
- Establish high resolution stratigraphy for Upper Khartam Member based on the field geological data.
- Characterize the reservoir heterogeneity at outcrop scale of lithofacies, cyclicity and facies continuity; and at micro scale by determining microfacies and petrophysical properties.
- Establish high resolution digital outcrop analog model for Upper Khartam Member using LiDAR, field and laboratory data.
- Determine the best outcrops of the Khartam Member using satellite imagery and maps.
- Conduct sedimentological and stratigraphical studies on vertical and horizontal sections.
- Perform petrophysical analysis (porosity, permeability) and mineralogical characterization (SEM, XRD).
- Utilize terrestrial laser scanning (LiDAR) to create a Digital Outcrop Models (DOM) for detailed heterogeneity assessment.
- Develop a digital outcrop model of the Upper Khartam Member.
- Characterize lithofacies and depositional environments of the Upper Khartam Member.
- Evaluate reservoir heterogeneity and quality based on integrated field, lab, and digital data.
- Produce a digital outcrop model (DOM) for the Upper Khartam Member using LiDAR technology.
- Identify and characterize high-frequency sequences (HFSs) within the Upper Khartam Member.
- Interpret the depositional environment and reservoir quality of the Upper Khartam Member based on lithofacies analysis.
- Integrate digital model data with traditional field sedimentology to evaluate reservoir heterogeneity.
- Develop a digital outcrop model of the Upper Khartam Member using LiDAR data.
- Integrate lithofacies, stratigraphy, and petrophysical measurements into the 3D model.
- Quantify reservoir heterogeneity and quality for three specific units (A, B, and C).
- Compare the accuracy of digital outcrop modeling against traditional field measurements.
- Study four vertical stratigraphic outcrop sections of the Upper Khartam Member in Central Saudi Arabia.
- Construct a composite sequence for the Upper Khartam Member bounded by the Permian-Triassic boundary and a sequence boundary.
- Identify lithofacies associations and depositional environments within the member.
- Apply LiDAR scanning to create digital models integrated with sedimentology and stratigraphy data.
- Evaluate reservoir heterogeneity and quality across different units (A, B, and C).

## Methods

The study employed a multi-step workflow: 1) Field mapping and sample collection for thin-section petrography. 2) Spectral Gamma Ray (SGR) analysis using the SGR Gamma GF Surveyor II tool to determine U, Th, K content. 3) Digital data acquisition using LiDAR (Optech system) and a Riegl VZ-4000 laser scanner. 4) Data processing of point clouds using RiSCAN Pro for visualization and IMAlign for triangulation. 5) Model editing in IMEdit to fill holes and refine surfaces. 6) Integration into ArcGIS (ArcScene) for bed surface delineation and thickness calculation. 7) Petrophysical analysis including porosity and permeability measurements from four vertical sections (KM-1 to KM-4).

## Data and Materials

Data includes: 1) Outcrop samples of the Upper Khartam Member in the Buraydah quadrangle; 2) SGR data for U, Th, K; 3) LiDAR point cloud data from a Riegl VZ-4000 scanner; 4) Petrophysical measurements (porosity and permeability) from four vertical sections (KM-1, KM-2, KM-3, and KM-4).

## Key Findings

- The Upper Khartam Member contains several distinct lithofacies: poorly sorted intraclasts packstone, graded mudstones/wackestones to packstones, shoal complex association, and thin marl limestone alternating with grainstones. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 70]])
- The study identified a ramp complex depositional model for the Upper Khartam Member, where HFS-1 and -2 represent the deepest parts of the model. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 87]])
- SGR analysis revealed distinct variations in U, Th, and K concentrations across different lithofacies within the Upper Khartam Member. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 57]])
- The average thickness of beds was calculated for both field observations and ArcMap-derived models to validate the DOM process. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 131]])
- Reservoir heterogeneity was evaluated using a specific key involving measurements from three distinct reservoir units (A, B, and C). ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 138]])
- The study identified six lithofacies associations deposited in three distinct depositional settings: marine transgression, fore shoal, and shoal complex. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 17]])
- High-resolution stratigraphy revealed that the Upper Khartam Member consists of three high-frequency sequences (HFS-1, HFS-2, and HFS-3). ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 18]])
- Reservoir heterogeneity was found to be extremely heterogeneous in unit C, while units A and B were characterized as very heterogeneous. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 18]])
- The primary controls on reservoir heterogeneity are identified as depositional processes and post-depositional diagenesis at the outcrop scale. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 18]])
- The Khuff Formation is estimated to contain approximately 43% of the world's natural gas reserves. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 21]])
- The Upper Khartam Member is characterized by oolitic and peloidal limestone with calcitized and stromatolitic construction, dolomitic coquina limestone, and clayey dolomite. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 45]])
- Khuff reservoir properties are primarily dependent on stratigraphic position and the depositional/post-depositional controls on facies distribution. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 49]])
- The Khuff formation is a carbonate system where porosity can exceed 30% and permeability can reach approximately 300 mD in major gas reservoirs. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 49]])
- Anhydrite content significantly impacts reservoir quality; an increase in anhydrite leads to a decrease in reservoir porosity. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 49]])
- The Riegl VZ-4000 scanner provides high resolution and precision for data collection even in poor visibility conditions (dust, haze). ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 46]])

## Limitations

LiDAR systems have limitations including high cost, heavy equipment weight, short battery life, and the need for large storage capacity for high-resolution data.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The author suggests creating a digital outcrop models library for reservoir analogs in Saudi Arabia by integrating digital modeling with traditional field investigations and combining it with subsurface data to increase confidence.
