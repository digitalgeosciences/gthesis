# Quantitative Assessment of Disinfection By-products Formed by Chlorine, Chlorine Dioxide and Ozone in Saudi Arabia Drinking Water

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the formation of disinfection by-products (DBPs) such as trihalomethanes (THMs), chlorite, and bromate in blended water samples (desalinated seawater mixed with raw groundwater). It evaluates how different disinfectants (chlorine, chlorine dioxide, and ozone) and variables like dosage, temperature, and contact time affect these byproduct concentrations.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the risk of hazardous disinfection by-products (DBPs) forming during the treatment of blended water in Saudi Arabia. Specifically, it examines how different disinfectants and environmental factors (temperature, contact time, and blending ratios) influence the concentration of trihalomethanes (TTHMs), chlorite, and bromate.

## Objectives

- Study the effect of source water quality on the formation of by-products.
- Study the effect of blending ratios on the levels of disinfection by-products.
- Determine the optimum disinfection condition to achieve the minimum DBP.
- Investigate the fate of DBPs formed when using three disinfectants (Cl2, ClO2, and O3) in blended water.
- Quantify byproduct concentrations under different treatment conditions: disinfectant dosage, temperature, and reaction time.
- Determine the correlation between the type of by-products formed and the quality of the source water (desalinated seawater and groundwater).

## Methods

The study involved preparing blended water samples from a Jubail desalination plant (MSF process) and local brackish groundwater (Dammam formation). Three experimental parts were conducted: 1. Chlorination, where TTHMs were quantified using GC-MS with a Purge and Trap technique; 2. Chlorine Dioxide treatment, where chlorite was measured via Ion Chromatography (Dionex ICS-3000 RFIC system); 3. Ozonation, where bromate and bromide levels were analyzed by Ion Chromatography. TOC was measured using a Shimadzu TOC 5000A analyzer, and chlorine residuals were monitored using the DPD method with a Hach spectrophotometer.

## Data and Materials

Samples included: 15 chlorination experiments (varying blending ratios of 1:2, 1:5, 1:10; dosages of 1-5 ppm; temperatures of 25, 35, 45°C); 15 chlorine dioxide experiments (similar variables); and 18 ozonation experiments (varying doses from 0.5 to 5.0 ppm). Water sources included desalination product water and raw brackish groundwater from the Dammam formation.

## Key Findings

- Bromoform represented more than 80% of the total THMs formed in all water formulations disinfected by chlorination, regardless of mixing ratios or chlorine dosage. ([[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011, p. 52]])
- Chloroform was not detected in most samples during chlorination due to low TOC levels (0.056 to 0.098 ppm) and high bromide concentrations (0.25 to 0.98 ppm). ([[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011, p. 52]])
- As the proportion of product water increased from 1:2 to 1:10, bromoform levels decreased while chloroform levels increased. ([[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011, p. 53]])
- Chlorite formation was found to be more active at lower temperatures (59.0% and 60.5% at 10 and 20°C) but reached 80.2% at 30°C. ([[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011, p. 31]])
- Bromate formation was observed in ozonation of water with bromide concentrations as low as 50 μg/l, exceeding regulatory thresholds. ([[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011, p. 33]])
- Chlorine dioxide dosage should not exceed 1.64 mg/l to avoid exceeding maximum contaminant levels for chlorite in samples with DOC of 2.91 mg/l. ([[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011, p. 30]])
- TTHMs formation increased from 0 to 247 ppb as chlorine dosages increased from 1 to 5 ppm at 25°C. ([[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011, p. 66]])
- Bromoform (CHBr3) and dibromochloromethane (CHBr2Cl) were the dominant components of THMs, with bromoform representing more than 80% of total TTHMs in all water formulations. ([[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011, p. 66]])
- chloroform was not detected in most samples due to high initial bromide levels (0.25 to 0.98 ppm) in the brackish water. ([[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011]])
