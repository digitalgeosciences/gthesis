# Hydrogeochemistry of Alat and Khobar aquifers in Eastern Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the hydrogeochemistry of the Alat and Khobar aquifers in the Dammam Dome region of Eastern Saudi Arabia. It analyzes major ion distributions, identifies three distinct facies in the Alat aquifer and four in the Khobar aquifer, and determines that both aquifers are over-saturated with calcite and dolomite while being under-saturated with gypsum.

---

## Research Problem

Characterizing the hydrogeochemical properties and evolution of groundwater within the Alat and Khobar aquifers (Eocene age) to understand their chemical composition, facies distribution, and interaction with surrounding geological units in the Eastern Province of Saudi Arabia.

## Methods

Hydrogeochemical data from 347 wells were analyzed. The study utilized PCWATEQ (a modification of WATEQ) for thermodynamic equilibrium modeling to calculate ion activities and saturation indices for ~200 mineral phases. Data was processed using Datagen for input preparation. SURFER was used for contouring TDS, SI, and SAR values using the Kriging technique. Plotchem was used for generating Piper's trilinear diagrams and Stiff diagrams. Analytical calculations were performed using WF77SYS and ICUSYS.

## Data and Materials

Water samples from multiple wells in the Alat and Khobar aquifers located across the Dammam Dome, Qatif, Ras Tanura, and Al Hasa areas. Geological data included stratigraphic descriptions of the Rus, Midra, Saila, and Alveolina formations as aquitards.

## Key Findings

- Both the Alat and Khobar aquifers are oversaturated with calcite and dolomite, and undersaturated with gypsum. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 19]])
- Piper's trilinear plots show three different facies in the Alat aquifer and four facies in the Khobar aquifer. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 19]])
- The groundwater evolutionary pattern in both aquifers generally follows Chebotarev's sequence. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992]])
- page ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992]])
- The Piper trilinear plots show three different facies in the Alat aquifer (Na-Cl-Ca-SO4, Na-Ca-Cl, and Na-Cl) and four facies in the Khobar aquifer. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 58]])
- In the Alat aquifer, Calcium concentrations vary from 100 mg/l to 1300 mg/l; Magnesium varies from 80 mg/l to 480 mg/l; Sodium ranges from 400 mg/l to 5800 mg/l. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 45]])
- In the Alat aquifer, Chloride concentrations range from 500 mg/l to over 5000 mg/l; Sulfate varies from 400 mg/l to 1800 mg/l. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 49]])
- The Alat aquifer shows a strong linear relationship between TDS and EC with a correlation coefficient (r') of 0.93, following the equation TDS = 0.64 * EC. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 56]])
- Khobar aquifer average transmissivity is 2.9 x 10^3 m/s and average storativity is 2.1 x 10^-3, indicating a confined nature. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 43]])
- The Alat aquifer shows a concentration of Sodium reaching 5800 mg/l near Al-Jubayl and Chloride exceeding 5000 mg/l in the same area. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 50]])
- The Khobar aquifer is oversaturated with calcite (SI 0.2 to 0.4) and dolomite, while it is undersaturated with gypsum. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 87]])
- In the Khobar aquifer, sulfate concentrations range from 400 to 1600 mg/l, increasing seaward. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 68]])
- Bicarbonate in the Khobar aquifer is anomalously high near Hufuf (480 mg/l). ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 71]])
- TDS in the Khobar aquifer varies from less than 1000 mg/l in the south to more than 7000 mg/l in the north. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 72]])
- The TDS-EC relationship in the Khobar aquifer is well-approximated by a linear regression with a correlation factor of 0.87. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 74]])

## Limitations

Due to the limited nature and uneven distribution of existing data, it is difficult to give a precise picture of the facies distribution of groundwaters in the Alat aquifer; therefore, some patterns are oversimplified and generalized.
