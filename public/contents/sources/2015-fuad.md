# LOG POROSITY AND LITHOLOGY PREDICTION FROM SEISMIC DATA: FRONTIER AND TENSLEEP FORMATIONS, WYOMING

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the conversion of seismic amplitudes into rock characterization (porosity and lithology) for the Frontier and Tensleep formations in Wyoming. It utilizes a workflow involving multi-attribute linear regression followed by Radial Basis Function Neural Networks to predict porosity and fractional lithology from 3D seismic data.

---

## Research Problem

The challenge of accurately predicting non-linear and inhomogeneous reservoir properties (porosity and lithology) from seismic volumes, specifically in the Frontier and Tensleep formations within the Teapot basin.

## Objectives

- To correct the neutron porosity fractional values to compute accurate porosity values.
- To correlate multi-attribute transforms with available porosity logs to predict pseudo log porosity over the entire zone of interest (Frontier, Second Wall Creek, and Tensleep reservoirs) from full stack seismic volume.
- To use the proposed new log (fractional lithology log) guided with seismic attributes to estimate the lithological fraction over the Frontier, Second Wall Creek, and Tensleep reservoirs.
- Determine the most appropriate seismic attributes for predicting lithology and porosity.
- Evaluate the performance of multi-attribute linear regression versus RBF neural networks.
- Develop a method to predict fractional lithology (sandstone, limestone, dolomite) from 3D seismic data.

## Methods

The methodology involves several stages: 1. Well Log Pre-processing: Correction of neutron porosity for different lithologies (sandstone, limestone, dolomite), calculation of fractional lithology components using density/neutron logs, and shale_correction based on Gamma Ray data. 2. Seismic Attribute Derivation: Calculation of instantaneous attributes (amplitude, phase, frequency) and derived attributes like amplitude-weighted cosine phase, average frequency, etc., using the EMERGE software. 3. Seismic Inversion: Application of a 'Colored Inversion' approach to obtain acoustic impedance and density from seismic data. 4. Well-to-Seismic Tie: Conversion of well logs to time domain using P-wave sonic and density logs to create synthetic seismograms. 5. Prediction Modeling: Implementation of multi-linear regression (including stepwise regression) to select optimal attribute combinations, followed by training Radial Basis Function Neural Networks (RBFN) for non-linear mapping between seismic attributes and target properties.

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes a dataset from SEG including 12 well logs (gamma ray, caliper, bulk density, neutron porosity, P-wave sonic) and a 3D seismic cube covering the Teapot basin area. The survey includes 345 inlines and 188 crosslines with a 2ms sample rate.

## Key Findings

- The inclusion of multiple attributes (e.g., using four or five attributes) improved correlation and reduced error in lithology prediction compared to single attribute models. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 60]])
- Radial Basis Function Neural Networks (RBFN) showed superior performance over multi-linear regression for predicting porosity and lithology due to their ability to model non-linear relationships. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 64]])
- The 'fractional lithology' approach, which quantifies the percentage of sand, lime, and dolomite, provides a more nuanced representation than simple lithofacies classification. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 23]])
- Stepwise regression was successfully used to identify the optimal subset of seismic attributes (e.g., identifying that four attributes were sufficient for some cases) while reducing redundancy. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 59]])
- The 'Colored Inversion' approach provided a robust method for obtaining acoustic impedance from seismic data as an input for subsequent prediction models. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 24]])
- Post-stack inversion showed high correlation (0.99) between predicted and actual acoustic impedance across multiple wells (e.g., well 1-10, 62-11). ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 50]])
- Single seismic attributes showed poor correlation for lithology prediction in the Second Wall Creek reservoir (max 0.45) and Tensleep reservoir (max 0.40). ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 52]])
- A combination of four attributes (Filter 35/40-45/50, Cosine instantaneous phase, Filter 25/30-35/40, and Average frequency) yielded a correlation of 0.84 for lithology in the Second Wall Creek reservoir. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 74]])
- RBF neural networks improved lithology prediction correlation to 0.87 for the Second Wall Creek reservoir compared to multi-linear regression. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 83]])
- RBF neural networks achieved a high correlation of over 0.92 for lithology in the Tensleep reservoir with a small training error of approximately 0.14. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 83]])
- For porosity prediction, RBF neural networks yielded a correlation of 0.83 for Second Wall Creek and over 0.92 for Tensleep. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 98]])
- RBF-predicted lithology maps showed higher frequency content and better resolution of thin layers compared to acoustic impedance inversion results. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 104]])

## Limitations

The study notes that some wells (e.g., 62-11) exhibited high error in prediction, which could be attributed to inaccurate well-to-seismic ties or local geological heterogeneity.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Future improvements are expected if core data becomes available to validate the results of the predicted fractional lithology.
