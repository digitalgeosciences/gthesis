# LOG POROSITY AND LITHOLOGY PREDICTION FROM SEISMIC DATA: FRONTIER AND TENSLEEP FORMATIONS, WYOMING

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the conversion of seismic amplitudes into rock characterization parameters, specifically porosity and lithology, for the Frontier and Tensleep formations in the Teapot basin. It evaluates multiple methodologies including multi-attribute linear regression, feed-forward backpropagation artificial neural networks (ANN), and Radial Basis Function (RBF) networks to predict petrophysical properties from 3D seismic data.

---

## Research Problem

The challenge of accurately predicting petrophysical properties (porosity and lithology) from 3D seismic data in the Frontier and Tensleep formations due to the limitations of individual seismic attributes and the need for robust, non-linear modeling techniques.

## Objectives

- Convert seismic amplitudes into rock characterization using integrated petrophysical measurements and 3D seismic data.
- Develop an application to obtain porosity and fractional lithology constituent 2D maps from post-stack 3D seismic data.
- Evaluate the credibility of attributes for log property transformation through cross-well validation.
- To correct the neutron porosity fractional values to compute accurate porosity values.
- To correlate multi-attribute transforms with available porosity logs to predict pseudo log porosity over the entire zone of interest (Frontier Second Wall Creek and Tensleep).
- To use a proposed new lithology fraction log, guided by seismic attributes, to estimate lithological fractions over the Frontier and Tensleep reservoirs.
- Develop a method to obtain porosity and fractional lithology constituent 2D maps from post-stack 3D seismic data.
- Evaluate different mathematical models including multi-attribute linear regression, ANN, and RBF networks for petrophysical prediction.
- Integrate petrophysical measurements (density and neutron logs) with 3D seismic data to improve reservoir characterization.
- Determine the optimal combination of seismic attributes for lithology prediction using stepwise regression.
- Evaluate the performance of Radial Basis Function (RBF) neural networks in predicting lithology and porosity.
- Identify the most accurate multi-attribute combinations to overcome the limitations of single-attribute analysis.
- Evaluate the performance of single and multi-attribute regression for porosity prediction.
- Implement a Radial Basis Function (RBF) neural network to predict porosity and lithology from seismic attributes.
- Compare RBF results with standard acoustic impedance inversion to evaluate reservoir heterogeneity and extension.

## Methods

The study involved: 1. Wavelet estimation using both statistical (from seismic data) and deterministic (from well logs) approaches to ensure proper well-to-seismic tie. 2. Post-stack inversion to derive acoustic impedance from seismic and well log data. 3. Multi-attribute analysis where various attributes (e.g., Filter, Average Frequency, Instantaneous Phase) were cross-correlated with lithology and porosity logs. 4. Stepwise regression to select the optimal number of attributes for prediction based on minimizing validation error. 5. Training Radial Basis Function (RBF) neural networks using Gaussian transfer functions to model non-linear relationships between seismic attributes and petrophysical properties.

## Data and Materials

The study utilized a 3D seismic cube, density logs, and neutron logs from two target reservoirs in the Teapot Basin: the Second Wall Creek member of the Frontier Formation (depths 2900-3300ft) and the Tensleep Formation (depths 5500-5900ft). A total of 11 wells were used for training and validation across both reservoirs.

## Key Findings

- The RBF Neural Network showed improved correlation and lower error compared to multi-linear regression for both lithology and porosity prediction. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 64]])
- The optimal number of attributes for lithology estimation in the Second Wall Creek (Frontier) reservoir was determined to be four. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 59]])
- The optimal number of attributes for lithology estimation in the Tensleep reservoir was five. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 59]])
- Cross-well validation showed small differences between estimation and validation errors, indicating high accuracy in the RBF model. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 19]])
- The inclusion of multiple attributes via stepwise regression improved the prediction of lithology compared to single attribute analysis. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 58]])
- The Frontier Formation's porosity varies from near zero to 20 percent, with hydrocarbon-producing sandstones having porosities between 10 to 20 percent. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 25]])
- The Tensleep formation's porosity values vary from near zero to 10 percent, with hydrocarbon-producing sandstones having porosities around 10 percent. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 26]])
- A lithology fraction log was developed where '1' indicates pure sand, '2' represents lime, and '3' represents dolomite; values between these indicate mixed lithologies (e.g., 1.2 is 80% sand and 20% lime). ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 37]])
- The use of multi-linear regression and ANN was found to be effective for correlating log and seismic attributes, with ANN showing potential to overcome limitations of standard statistical tools in non-linear systems. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 30]])
- The inclusion of convolution filters in multi-attribute linear regression allows for the consideration of neighboring sample points to account for the difference in resolution between well logs and seismic attributes. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 49]])
- Stepwise regression is used as a fast method to select the best combination of seismic attributes by iteratively adding attributes that minimize prediction error while monitoring validation error. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 52]])
- Feed-forward backpropagation ANNs can utilize sigmoid-shaped functions (hyperbolic tangent or log-sigmoid) to create non-linear mappings between input data and target outputs. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 54]])
- RBF networks are noted for learning faster and being less sensitive to non-stationary inputs compared to standard backpropagation ANNs. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 60]])
- The RBF network mapping from hidden unit space to output space is linear, while the mapping from input space to hidden unit space is non-linear via Gaussian basis functions. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 62]])
- The post-stack inversion showed high correlation between predicted and actual acoustic impedance, with correlation coefficients of 0.99 across all tested wells (e.g., well 1-10, 56-10). ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 49]])

## Limitations

The study noted that while adding more attributes can reduce training error, it may increase validation error; therefore, a specific number of attributes was selected to minimize validation error. Some data points were excluded due to high error contribution or poor well-to-seismic ties.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The fractional lithology constituent approach can be used for automated interpretations and as an extension of conventional interpretation techniques.
