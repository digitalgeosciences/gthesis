# RESERVOIR HETEROGENEITY AND QUALITY ASSESSMENT OF LATE ORDOVICIAN PALEOVALLEYS, SARAH FORMATION, NW SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the reservoir heterogeneity and quality of the Late Ordovician Sarah Formation in the Rahal Dhab Paleovalley, NW Saudi Arabia. It utilizes a combination of field sedimentological mapping, petrographic analysis (thin sections), geochemical analysis (XRF), and petrophysical measurements (NMR, porosity/permeability) to evaluate how depositional, mineralogical, and diagenetic factors influence reservoir properties.

---

## Research Problem

The Sarah Formation is a complex, heterogeneous tight gas reservoir where rapid vertical and lateral facies variations in the glacial paleovalleys create challenges for development. Understanding these complexities through integrated sedimentological and petrophysical analysis is necessary to assess its potential as an unconventional resource.

## Methods

The study employed a multi-scale approach: 1. Field investigation included sedimentological mapping and logging of three sections in the Rahal Dhab Paleovalley to identify lithofacies and architectural elements. 2. Laboratory analysis included thin-section petrography for mineralogy, grain morphology, and diagenetic features; XRD and QEMSCAN for mineral quantification; XRF for geochemical elemental distribution; and NMR for pore size distribution (PSD). 3. Petrophysical measurements were conducted to determine porosity and permeability, followed by statistical analyses including Coefficient of Variation (CV), Dykstra-Parsons coefficient, and Lorenz plots to quantify heterogeneity.

## Data and Materials

The study analyzed samples from three specific sections in the Rahal Dhab Paleovalley: Section-1 (20m height), Section-2 (26m height), and Section-3 (14m height). Data included thin section petrography, XRD/QEMSCAN mineralogy, XRF geochemistry, and NMR parameters for porosity and permeability.

## Key Findings

- The study identified two primary lithofacies associations: FA1 (Subglacial Diamictite) and FA2 (Glacial Outwash to fluvial braided channel). ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 34]])
- Sandstones in the Sarah Formation are classified as quartz-arenite to subarkose types based on QFL classification. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 51]])
- Quartz overgrowth was identified as a significant factor that reduces porosity and permeability (microheterogeneity). ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 46]])
- The study quantified heterogeneity using the Dykstra-Parsons coefficient and Lorenz plots to compare different sections of the paleovalley. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 89]])
- NMR analysis provided pore size distribution (PSD) categorized into small (D3), medium (D2), and large (D1) pores based on T2 relaxation times. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 111]])
- The measured porosity ranges from 13.03% to 25% (average 17.06%) while permeability ranges from 7.3 mD to 714.61 mD (average 135.50 mD). ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 19]])
- The coefficient of variation (CV) of the permeability ranges from 0.53 to 0.84, Lorentz coefficient from 0.227 to 0.293 and Dykstra Parsons coefficient from 0.456 to 0.577. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 19]])
- The pore size distribution (PSD) revealed trimodal, bimodal with very few are unimodal distribution. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 19]])
- Kaolinite filling pores with low amount of Fe-material, grain compaction, cementation, and overgrowth are main controlling factors for reducing the reservoir quality. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 19]])
- Feldspar dissolution is a main factor for enhancing the reservoir quality. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 19]])
- The study showed a strong positive correlation (R=81%) between quartz content recorded by QEMSCAN and XRD. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 14]])
- Most of the samples fall in T2-peak range 50-100 ms, representing medium sizes of effective pores. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 110]])
- The reservoir is interpreted as glaciofluvial and subglacial deposits (quartz-arenite to sub-arkose). ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 18]])
- The Sarah Formation is characterized by high heterogeneity in both vertical and lateral dimensions due to rapid facies variations within the glacial paleovalleys. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 37]])
- The reservoir quality was highest in fluvial facies (FA2) and lowest in subglacial facies (FA3). ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 38]])

## Limitations

The upper contact between the Sarah and Qasim formations was not found, potentially due to weathering or erosion.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The author recommends further studies on pore size distribution and the integration of more outcrop and subsurface data to build porosity-permeability models for better understanding of reservoir heterogeneity.
