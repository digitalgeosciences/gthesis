# RESERVOIR HETEROGENEITY AND QUALITY ASSESSMENT OF LATE ORDOVICIAN PALEOVALLEYS, SARAH FORMATION, NW SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the macro- to micro-scale reservoir heterogeneity and quality of the Late Ordovician Sarah Formation in the Rahal Dhab Paleovalley, NW Saudi Arabia. By integrating sedimentological field investigations, thin-section petrography, XRD, QEMSCAN, XRF, NMR, and statistical analysis, it concludes that the formation is highly heterogeneous due to complex glaciofluvial and subglacial depositional environments.

---

## Research Problem

The Sarah Formation is a target tight gas sand reservoir in the Arabian Peninsula; however, its complex heterogeneity and quality are not fully understood due were it is influenced by diverse factors like facies variation, mineralogical composition, and diagenetic processes (e.g., kaolinite formation, dissolution).

## Objectives

- Identify, describe lithofacies, Depositional environment and model
- Characterize petrographical, geochemical, and petrophysical properties and their relationship
- Apply statistical techniques to analyze the reservoir heterogeneity properties
- Interpret all analyzed result in terms of determining reservoir heterogeneity, quality and architecture
- Characterize the lithofacies and depositional environment of the Sarah Formation paleovalleys.
- Evaluate the mineralogical composition and petrographic characteristics of the sandstone units.

## Methods

The study employed a multi-scale approach: (1) Field investigation of three outcrop sections in Rahal Dhab Paleovalley to identify lithofacies and sedimentary structures; (2) Laboratory analysis including thin-section petrography (using an Olympus microscope), XRD (EMPYREAN PANalytical X-ray diffractometer), QEMSCAN (FEI QEMSCAN 650F), SEM with EDX (Jeol JSM-B610LV), and XRF for geochemical characterization; (3) Petrophysical measurements of porosity and permeability using an automated permeameter/porosimeter (AP608); (4) NMR analysis (OXFORD MARN DRX HF spectrometer) to determine pore size distribution; (5) Statistical analysis using Coefficient of Variation (CV), Dykstra-Parsons coefficient, and Lorenz coefficient to quantify reservoir heterogeneity.

## Data and Materials

3 outcrop sections in Rahal Dhab Paleovalley (Section 1: ~20m thick; Section 2: ~25m thick; Section 3: ~14m thick); 50 representative samples for laboratory analysis; 18 rock fragments for SEM/EDX; 18 plug samples for NMR.

## Key Findings

- The reservoir is characterized as highly heterogeneous to very heterogeneous, with a Coefficient of Variation (CV) of permeability ranging from 0.53 to 0.84. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 19]])
- Measured porosity ranges from 13.03% to 25% (average 17.06%) and permeability ranges from 7.3 mD to 714.61 mD (average 135.50 mD). ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 19]])
- The Dykstra-Parsons coefficient for the reservoir ranges from 0.227 to 0.293, and the Lorenz coefficient ranges from 0.456 to 0.577. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 19]])
- Pore size distribution (PSD) revealed trimodal and bimodal distributions, with very few unimodal distributions. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 19]])
- Kaolinite filling pores with low amounts of Fe-material, grain compaction, cementation, and overgrowth are the primary factors reducing reservoir quality. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 19]])
- Feldspar dissolution is identified as a major factor for enhancing reservoir quality. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 19]])
- The lithofacies are composed of fine to very coarse grained, poorly to moderately sorted, massive, cross bedded and stratified sandstone with conglomeratic beds and diamictites. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 18]])
- Two main facies associations were identified: (FA1) subglacial/diamictite facies association and (FA2) glacial outwash to fluvial braided channel facies association. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 56]])
- The study confirmed a strong positive correlation (R=81%) between quartz content measured by QEMSCAN and XRD. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 14]])
- The reservoir is classified as a tight gas reservoir, with average porosity of 17.06% and average permeability of 135.50 mD. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 19]])
- The study identified two main facies associations: FA1 (subglacial) containing clast-supported gravels and diamictites, and FA2 (glaciofluvial to fluvial) featuring massive to trough cross-bedded sandstones. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 64]])
- The lower contact between the Sarah and Qasim formations is marked by a transition from fine laminated silty shale (Fl) to massive sandstone (Sm). ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 63]])
- Quartz content was found to be high, ranging from 93% to 97% in thin sections and 69-97.8% in XRD analysis. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 66]])
- Kaolinite was identified as the dominant pore-filling material, appearing in various morphologies (book-stacked, hexagonal, platy). ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 80]])
- feldspar dissolution significantly contributed to secondary porosity, while quartz overgrowth and kaolinite cementation reduced primary porosity. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020]])
