# RESERVOIR CHARACTERIZATION OF UNCONVENTIONAL CALCAREOUS MUDSTONES: KIMMERIDGIAN JUBAILA FORMATION, JAFURAH SUB-BASIN, SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the organic-rich calcareous mudstones of the Jubaila Formation in the Jafurah sub-basin as a potential unconventional resource. It integrates sedimentology, chemostratigraphy, and sequence stratigraphy with advanced analytical techniques like XRD, XRF, and ANN to characterize lithofacies, determine depositional environments, and develop a predictive model for Total Organic Carbon (TOC).

---

## Research Problem

The complexity of unconventional mudstone systems in the Jubaila Formation requires an integrated multidisciplinary approach (mineralogy, geochemistry, and micropaleontology) to understand sediment source, depositional processes, and sea-level fluctuations for better reservoir characterization.

## Objectives

- Characterize a lithostratigraphic framework based on lithofacies identification (vertical and lateral heterogeneity)
- Identify depositional processes based on texture, sedimentologic features, and bioturbation index
- characterize mineralogical and geochemical (elements and elemental ratios) data to explain how they relate to changes in sediment provenance, paleoproductivity, and paleoredox conditions
- Identify micro- and macrofaunal compositions and relationships between fossils abundance and geochemical parameters
- Combine lithostratigraphic, geochemical, and biostratigraphic data into a sequence stratigraphic framework to provide an integrated model for the formation
- Define a new empirical correlation using Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) to generate continuous TOC profiles from well-log data and redox-sensitive elements

## Methods

The study utilized five subsurface cores (J1-J5) totaling ~210m. Core description included lithology, color, texture, bedding style, sedimentary features, and bioturbation index (BI). Laboratory analysis included Micro X-ray fluorescence (μ-XRF) for elemental distribution, thin-section petrography to identify mineralogy/texture, XRD for bulk mineralogy quantification, QEMSCAN for high-resolution mineral mapping, and Rock-Eval pyrolysis for TOC. Geochemical data were processed using Principal Component Analysis (PCA) and Hierarchical Clustering on Principal Components (HCPC) in R software.

## Data and Materials

The study analyzed 100 samples across five wells (J1, J2, J3, J4, J5) in the Jubaila Formation. Data included nannofossil counts (>100 specimens per slide), μ-XRF elemental concentrations for major elements (Ca, Mg, Si, Al, Ti, Fe, S, K, P) and trace elements (Mo, Ni, V, Cr, Cu, Mn, Zr, Zn, Sr), and TOC weight percentages.

## Key Findings

- Twelve distinct lithofacies were identified, ranging from anhydrite to highly bioturbated siliceous mudstones. ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 23]])
- Six distinct geochemical clusters (CG 1–6) were identified via PCA/HCPC, representing categories such as carbonate-rich, oxygen-depleted, and high terrigenous input lithofacies. ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 85]])
- variation in mineralogy and geochemistry is systematically controlled by paleoenvironments (proximal vs distal), sea-level fluctuations, and a pycnocline dividing the water column into anoxic, dysoxic, and oxic zones. ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022]])
- page": 24 ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022]])
- Organic-rich lithofacies (LF 4, LF 7, LF 9) correspond to anoxic/dysoxic conditions with high paleoproductivity. ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 24]])
- Lithofacies with low organic content and high bioturbation indices (LF 2, 3, 5, 6, 10, 11, 12) reflect higher oxygen levels and higher energy conditions. ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 25]])
- The organic-rich interval is primarily associated with the Transgressive System Tract (TST), particularly in distal settings, and decreases within the Highstand System Tract (HST). ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 25]])
- Strong correlation exists between TOC and redox-sensitive elements such as Mo, Cu, and Ni across all lithofacies. ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 156]])
- Well J2 and J4 (proximal) are characterized by higher silica content compared to the more argillaceous J1, J3, and J5 (distal). ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 63]])
- The J1 unit of the Jubaila Formation contains calcite (75%), total clay (12%), quartz (8%), and minor dolomite (0.2%) as determined by XRD. ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 51]])
- TOC values in the Lower Jubaila range from 0.5% to 5%, with an average of 1.5%. ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 51]])
- Average total porosity is 7.4% with an effective permeability of 1.39E-05 mD in the J1 unit. ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 51]])
- Twelve lithofacies were identified, categorized into two associations: those with high TOC and low bioturbation (LF 4, 7, 9) and those with lower TOC and higher bioturbation (LF 2, 3, 5, 6, 10, 11, 12). ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 56]])
- Lithofacies 4 (LF 4) is characterized by high to excellent TOC values and a mineralogy of 83% calcite and 7% dolomite. ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 72]])
- A strong negative correlation was observed between Ca and terrigenous-associated elements (Si, Al, Ti, K, Zr) with R² values ranging from -0.78 to -0.86. ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 82]])

## Limitations

The diversity of calcareous nannofossils was very poor due to potential dissolution and unfavorable environmental conditions during anoxic events.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The study provides a high-resolution sequence stratigraphy and an ANN model for TOC prediction to assist in identifying sweet spots for future drilling and completion strategies.
