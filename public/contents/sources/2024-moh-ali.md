# Improving Hydrocarbon Productivity of Organic Rich Shale Using Thermochemicals

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the use of thermochemical fluids (TCF) to enhance hydrocarbon productivity in organic-rich shales and mitigate liquid loading in gas wells. It employs a dual approach: laboratory core flooding experiments to evaluate petrophysical, geochemical, and geomechanical changes in shale samples, and PIPESIM simulations to model TCF's effectiveness in lifting liquids from wellbores.

---

## Research Problem

Organic-rich shales often suffer from low permeability and poor connectivity, while gas wells frequently experience liquid loading which hinders production. The study addresses how thermochemical fluids (TCF) can simultaneously promote kerogen maturation in shale and provide a heat/pressure source to lift liquids in wellbores.

## Objectives

- Studying thermochemical fluids effect on accelerating the kerogen maturation.
- Capturing the impacts of thermochemical fluids assisted maturation on the petrophysical, geomechanical, and geochemical properties.
- Tackling the issue of liquid loading in gas production wells.
- Assessing the improvements in hydrocarbon productivity after the chemical treatment.
- Evaluate the impact of TCF on petrophysical, geochemical, and geomechanical properties of organic-rich shales.
- Assess the effectiveness of TCF for liquid loading removal in gas wells through experimental and simulation methods.

## Methods

The study utilized a two-phase methodology. Phase I involved characterizing four organic-rich shale samples (intact, artificially fractured, and naturally fractured) using micro CT-scan, XRD, XRF, Rock-Eval pyrolysis, and helium porosity measurements. TCF (NH4Cl + NaNO2) was injected into these cores under high pressure (3000-3500 psi) and temperature (100-250°C). Phase II involved a laboratory flow system to simulate liquid loading where TCF was injected into a line containing gas condensate, followed by scaling the results using PIPESIM software for nodal analysis of VLP and IPR curves.

## Data and Materials

The study utilized shale samples with varying mineralogy and total organic carbon (TOC) content. Experimental conditions included high pressure (3000-3500 psi) and high temperature (100°C).

## Key Findings

- Thermochemical-assisted maturation resulted in a decrease in TOC by 18-42% in the shale samples, indicating hydrocarbon generation. ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024, p. 13]])
- The free hydrocarbon volume (S1) increased by 1.3 folds after treatment, indicating an increase in hydrocarbon reserves. ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024, p. 13]])
- Injection of TCF in gas wells suffering from liquid loading showed a promising result by removing more than 090% of the liquid holdup. ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024, p. 13]])
- Thermochemical treatment resulted in an increase in pore size and the generation of microfractures. ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024, p. 43]])
- Note: The study confirmed that TCF can lower capillary pressure (Pc) and improve production index. ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024]])
- page_number_not_found ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024]])
- TCF reaction produced a temperature increase from 160°F to 600°F and a pressure build-up from 100 psi to 3100 psi in a closed system. ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024, p. 48]])
- Sample 3 (moderately fractured) showed a 70% increase in fracture volume, from 16.3% to 27.7%, after TCF treatment. ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024, p. 51]])
- Porosity increased significantly in fractured samples: Sample 3 rose from 11.3% to 13.1%; Sample 4 rose from 15.2% to 18.2%. ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024, p. 55]])
- TOC reduction was highest in the most fractured sample (Sample 4), decreasing from 15.74 to 9.17 wt.%. ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024, p. 58]])
- S1 values (free hydrocarbons) increased by 106.8% for Sample 3 and 129.8% for Sample 4 after TCF treatment. ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024, p. 60]])
- S2 values (potential hydrocarbons) decreased by 20% and 27% respectively for Samples 3 and 4, indicating successful kerogen conversion. ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024, p. 61]])
- XRD and XRF analysis showed negligible changes in mineralogy or elemental composition post-treatment, confirming rock integrity was maintained. ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024, p. 64]])
- The Vp/Vs ratio increased significantly for fractured samples (e.g., Sample 4 from 2.07 to 2.68) due to microfracture development and thermal stress. ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024, p. 66]])
- Poisson's Ratio increased post-treatment in responsive samples, with Sample 3 increasing from 0.34 to 0.42. ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024, p. 68]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

Future work should investigate the influence of different TCF concentrations on shale rock response, evaluate TCF with a wider range of fluids beyond gas condensate, and explore the integration of TCF with other reservoir stimulation techniques.
