# OPTIMIZATION OF SAGD AND VAPEX PROCESSES WITH MINIMUM WELL SPACING CONSTRAINTS

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the optimization of well placement and control for Steam Assisted Gravity Drainage (SAGD) and Solvent Vapor Extraction (VAPEX) processes in heavy oil reservoirs. It specifically addresses the challenge of enforcing minimum well spacing constraints using a Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) algorithm to ensure drilling safety while maximizing Net Present Value (NPV).

---

## Research Problem

The optimization of well placement for heavy oil recovery methods like SAGD and VAPEX often fails to incorporate mandatory minimum well spacing constraints, which are essential to avoid drilling hazards. Additionally, the high complexity and non-linearity of these systems make manual or simple sensitivity analysis insufficient for determining optimal parameters.

## Objectives

- Develop a methodology to solve the horizontal well placement optimization problem constrained by any user-defined minimum well spacing.
- Implement a penalty approach to handle inequality constraints for well spacing in the optimization process.
- Determine optimal parameters (well location, control settings, and vertical separation) for both SAGD and VAPEX processes.
- Compare the performance of SAGD and VAPEX processes under different optimization cases.
- To enforce the minimum horizontal well spacing constraints in WPO of SAGD and VAPEX processes.
- To optimize injection and production rates in SAGD and VAPEX.

## Methods

The study utilizes a multi-step optimization workflow. First, it defines the well placement problem using coordinates (x, y, z), length, and orientation. It then implements the Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) algorithm to search for optimal solutions in a high-dimensional space. A penalty method is used to handle constraints; specifically, minimum spacing is enforced by defining an elliptical drainage area around each well. If a well's trajectory enters another's ellipse, a penalty is added to the objective function (Net Present Value - NPV). The reservoir simulation is performed using GeoQuest’s ECLIPSE software. A custom-developed code handles the translation of optimization parameters into simulator input files and calculates the Well Index (WI) for non-conventional well geometries.

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes numerical models of Athabasca and Cold Lake heavy oil reservoirs. It incorporates a set of cost parameters including capital expenditure (CAPEX), operating expenses (OPEX), and revenue calculations based on oil and gas prices to determine the Net Present Value (NPV).

## Key Findings

- The proposed methodology successfully determined optimal parameters while satisfying user-imposed well spacing constraints. ([[2015-khan|KHAN, 2015, p. 15]])
- SAGD showed better performance than VAPEX in Case 2 and Case 3 of the optimization study. ([[2015-khan|KHAN, 2015, p. 17]])
- VAPEX demonstrated good results specifically in Case 1 of the optimization comparison. ([[2015-khan|KHAN, 2015, p. 17]])
- Well spacing constraints are critical to avoid drilling hazards, yet they are rarely enforced in standard optimization tools. ([[2015-khan|KHAN, 2015, p. 14]])
- The study identifies that well placement is critical for economic value, as drilling horizontal wells involves high capital investment where errors in selection are costly to correct. ([[2015-khan|KHAN, 2015, p. 51]])
- A minimum spacing constraint can be modeled by defining an ellipse around a well; if any point of another well falls within this ellipse, it is considered a violation of the spacing. ([[2015-khan|KHAN, 2015, p. 69]])
- The Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) algorithm was tested with specific parameters: c1 = 1.494, c2 = 1.494, and omega = 0.729 to balance local exploration and global exploitation. ([[2015-khan|KHAN, 2015, p. 58]])
- The well index (WI) calculation accounts for orientation, permeability of the grid block, percentage of perforated area, and effective wellbore diameter in three dimensions. ([[2015-khan|KHAN, 2015, p. 66]])
- A penalty method was used to transform the constrained optimization problem into an unconstrained one by adding a term that increases as the iteration count increases if constraints are violated. ([[2015-khan|KHAN, 2015, p. 74]])
- Case 2 in VAPEX yielded a best NPV of 120 MM$ in the fourth realization. ([[2015-khan|KHAN, 2015, p. 117]])
- Case 2 in SAGD outperformed Case 1, with a best NPV of 276 MM$ in the third realization. ([[2015-khan|KHAN, 2015, p. 94]])
- In Case 3, the optimized vertical separation was found to be 8.2 ft for each well pair. ([[2015-khan|KHAN, 2015, p. 104]])
- The optimized vertical separation for VAPEX was found to be 8.2 and 41 ft for different well pairs. ([[2015-khan|KHAN, 2015, p. 112]])
- Simultaneous optimization of well control and location (Case 2) performed better than optimizing location alone (Case 1). ([[2015-khan|KHAN, 2015, p. 103]])
- A penalty-based method using circles and ellipses successfully enforced minimum well spacing constraints in all tested cases for both SAGD and VAPEX. ([[2015-khan|KHAN, 2015, p. 131]])

## Limitations

The study notes that for very small values of the penalty parameter (k), the algorithm may spend too much time in infeasible regions, while very large values might lead to sub-optimal solutions by moving too quickly into feasible space.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Implement optimization for different well configurations (e.g., curved, snaky, or deviated wells).
- Apply 3D well spacing constraints for horizontal well placement.
- Utilize local grid refinements around horizontal sections combined with proxy methods to reduce simulation costs.
