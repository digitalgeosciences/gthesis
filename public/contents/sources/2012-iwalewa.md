# Groundwater Management Approach at KFUPM Campus Using Numerical Simulation

**Summary**: The thesis evaluates the sustainability of the Umm Er Radhuma (UER) aquifer at the KFUPM campus by developing and calibrating groundwater flow and solute transport models. It assesses three pumping scenarios to predict future water levels and Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) concentrations, concluding that conservative management measures are necessary to preserve water quality.

---

## Research Problem

Rapid population growth and increased demand for domestic use and irrigation at KFUPM have led to significant groundwater extraction from the UER aquifer, resulting in rising salinity and declining water levels. Existing studies are regional, leaving a lack of specific data on the local hydrogeological impact within the campus.

## Objectives

- Quantitatively and qualitatively assess the groundwater resources at the KFUPM campus.
- Evaluate the sustainability of the aquifer system under three long-term pumping alternatives.
- Develop and calibrate a groundwater flow model to predict future potentiometric levels.
- Develop and calibrate a solute transport model to predict future Total Dissolved Solid (TDS) levels.
- To define the geological and hydrogeological settings of Umm Er Radhuma (UER) aquifer in the study area.
- To investigate the groundwater quality in terms of major ionic compositions.
- To investigate the historical changes in groundwater levels of Umm Er Radhuma aquifer system in the area.
- To develop a groundwater flow model and transport model for the aquifer system in the area, and to use this model to predict the future changes in groundwater levels and quality under three long-term pumping scenarios.
- To investigate possible environmental and health problems with respect to the hydrochemistry of the groundwater in the study area.
- Characterize the hydrogeological properties (lithology, thickness, hydraulic conductivity, and transmissivity) of the Umm Er Radhuma and Rus formations.
- Develop a conceptual model of the groundwater system in the study area.
- Construct and calibrate a numerical groundwater flow model using Visual MODFLOW to simulate head distribution and flow patterns.
- Develop a steady-state numerical model of the Umm Er Radhuma (UER) aquifer to establish baseline conditions.
- Perform transient-state simulation to evaluate the impact of historical pumping on the aquifer system.
- Validate the model by comparing simulated head values with observed field data from multiple wells.
- Evaluate alternative development schemes for future water management based on projected population growth and irrigation needs.
- Simulate groundwater flow and head levels under different pumping scenarios (Alternative Schemes I, II, and III).
- Model solute transport to predict future Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) concentrations in the Umm er Radhuma aquifer.
- Evaluate the impact of various management strategies on water level drawdown and salinity increase.
- Assess the hydrochemical quality of groundwater in the study area.
- Evaluate the suitability of groundwater for drinking and irrigation purposes based on international and local standards.
- Model the future response of the aquifer to different management scenarios (Alternative Schemes I, II, and III) for the period 2011-2030.
- Evaluate current groundwater usage and demand at the KFUPM campus
- Model the hydrogeological behavior of the Umm Er Radhuma aquifer system using numerical simulation
- Develop a management approach for sustainable groundwater use on campus
- Provide specific recommendations for water conservation and infrastructure planning

## Methods

The study utilized a finite difference grid (non-uniform) to discretize the Umm Er Radhuma aquifer. Steady-state conditions were modeled using constant head boundaries, while transient states used variable head boundaries to simulate pumping effects over time. Parameters such as hydraulic conductivity (K), specific yield (Sy), and transmissivity were derived from historical data and regional studies. The model was calibrated manually by comparing simulated heads with observed values, calculating Root Mean Square Error (RMS) and Mean Absolute Error (MAE). Alternative scenarios were modeled for the 2011-2030 period based on projected population growth rates of 8.3% per year.

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes data from 8 active groundwater wells within the KFUPM campus, including physical parameters (pH, EC, TDS), chemical concentrations (major ions like Ca, K, Mg, Na, Cl, SO4), and biological indicators (coliform counts). The model grid covers an area of 2814 x 1691 m2 with a non-uniform mesh.

## Key Findings

- Groundwater extraction increased from 2.4 MCM in 1967 to 13.9 MCM in 2010, representing a 480% increase. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 15]])
- The average decline in water level due to pumping between 1967 and 2010 was approximately 8.5 m. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 15]])
- Groundwater samples were classified as alkaline water with a prevailing sulphate-chloride signature, where Na+ and K+ are the dominant cations. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 16]])
- anion abundance followed the order Cl- > SO4^2- > HCO3-. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012]])

- The UER aquifer is the only source of groundwater in the study area as the Dammam aquifer has been eroded. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 22]])
- The Umm Er Radhuma (UER) formation has a mean thickness of approximately 400 m, reaching up to 650 m within the KFUPM area. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 38]])
- The Rus Formation is represented by only residual carbonates in the study area with a maximum thickness of 65 m and mean thickness of 45 m. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 39]])
- The Dammam Formation has a maximum thickness of 450 m but is significantly reduced by erosion near the 'Pre-Neogene Unconformity'. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 40]])
- The average annual rainfall in the study area is approximately 62 mm. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 21]])
- Sulphate content in the Umm Er Radhuma formation averages 9%, with values reaching up to 23%. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 35]])
- The Umm Er Radhuma (UER) aquifer is characterized as a karstified fractured bedrock aquifer with hydraulic conductivity values ranging from 1 x 10^-7 m/s to 1 x 10^-2 m/s, averaging 1.6 x 10^-5 m/s. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 48]])
- Transmissivity (T) of the UER aquifer ranges from 1 x 10^-4 m^2/s to 1 x 10^1 m^2/s, with an average value of 2.0 x 10^-3 m^2/s. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 48]])
- The UER aquifer is considered a leaky unconfined/confined aquifer; however, due to extensive pumping at KFUPM, it currently functions as an unconfined aquifer in the study area. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 48]])

## Limitations

The study notes that some observation wells were limited for only 1 to 4 stress periods, and the initial assessment of parameters was subject to minor inconsistencies in provided data.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Implementation of specific water conservation measures (e.g., fixing leaks, using buckets for washing cars/floors, and restricting irrigation) and strategic placement of new wells in the southern portion of the campus.
