# Study of Flow Characteristics of Reservoir Rock Using Digital Rock Physics

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the use of digital rock physics to characterize porosity and permeability in sandstone and carbonate reservoir rocks. It utilizes X-ray micro-CT imaging, PerGeos software for segmentation, and the Lattice-Boltzmann Method (LBM) for flow simulation, concluding that these methods provide a reliable complement to traditional laboratory measurements.

---

## Research Problem

The challenge of accurately predicting macroscopic rock properties (porosity and permeability) from 3D digital images, particularly in heterogeneous carbonate systems where representative elementary volume (REV) selection is difficult.

## Objectives

- To study flow characteristics of rock using DRP technology by obtaining digital images of rock using imaging techniques.
- Processing digital images to estimate the connected porosity using PerGeos software.
- Estimating absolute permeability of rocks using Lattice-Boltzmann Method (LM).
- Validating the flow characteristics of the rock by comparing simulated results with experimental results.
- Evaluate the effectiveness of micro-CT for estimating petrophysical properties of rock samples.
- Compare estimated porosity and permeability from digital rock physics with laboratory measurements.

## Methods

The study follows a three-phase workflow: 1) Laboratory measurements of porosity and permeability using an automated Gas Porosimeter/Permeameter. 2) Multi-scale imaging including medical CT for core homogeneity, micro-CT (Zeiss VersaXRM-500 and HeliScan) for high-resolution pore structure, and QEMSCAN for mineralogy. 3) Digital processing using PerGeos software involving non-local means filtering and cubic porosity segmentation. 4) Simulation of fluid flow using the Lattice-Boltzmann Method (LBM) to calculate absolute permeability from 3D segmented models.

## Data and Materials

The study included 6 sandstone samples (Berea Puff, Crab Orchid, Bandera Grey, Bandera Brown, Colton, Berea Grey) and 5 carbonate samples (9137-1, 9058-3, 9135-5, 9137-7, 9136-8). Samples were scanned at a resolution of 4 microns with sub-volumes of 600x600x600 voxels.

## Key Findings

- DRP techniques were shown to be a reliable complement for routine laboratory experiments. ([[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019, p. 19]])
- Results from conventional laboratory work with image-based simulations showed good agreement. ([[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019, p. 19]])
- The most important factors influencing the results were image resolution, representative sample size, and proper simulation set-up. ([[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019, p. 19]])
- X-ray micro-CT is used to scan sub-plugs at a resolution of 4 µm, where pore connectivity is revealed from top to bottom. ([[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019, p. 19]])
- A multi-scale imaging approach (X-ray CT, micro-CT, and XRD) was used to capture the full range of pore structures in carbonate samples. ([[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019, p. 19]])
- Sandstone core plugs showed porosity values ranging from 11% to 22% and permeability values from 0.28 mD to 210.07 mD. ([[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019, p. 103]])
- Carbonate samples exhibited lower permeability (0.11 to 2.83 mD) and porosity ranging from 7% to 14%. ([[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019, p. 103]])
- The Colton sandstone sample showed the lowest permeability (0.28 mD) and lowest porosity among sandstones. ([[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019, p. 103]])
- Bandera Grey showed the highest permeability (210.07 mD) among the sandstone samples. ([[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019, p. 103]])
- QEMSCAN analysis revealed that Colton had the highest clay content (16.5% kaolinite and albite). ([[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019, p. 108]])
- Sample 9058-3 was found to be 100% calcite, indicating high homogeneity. ([[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019, p. 112]])
- Non-local means filtering was shown to reduce noise while preserving edges between grains and pores in the Bandera Grey sample. ([[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019, p. 124]])
- Porosity estimates for sandstone samples using PerGeos software showed good agreement with lab measurements (e.g., Bandera Grey: Lab 20.23%, Estimated 20.44%). ([[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019, p. 128]])
- QEMSCAN results for Bandera Brown showed 65% Quartz, 3% Albite, 3% Illite, and 3% Biotite, with 21% void space. ([[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019, p. 136]])
- LBM simulations for sandstone samples showed good agreement with helium-gas permeameter measurements (e.g., Bandera Grey: Lab 210.07 mD, Estimated 250.44 mD). ([[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019, p. 138]])

## Limitations

The thesis notes that segmentation is a non-unique representation of pore space, which can lead to different results based on the chosen algorithm. Additionally, it mentions that some high-resolution images (e.g., 4μm) may still contain artifacts requiring pre-processing.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Further investigation into the impact of clay micropores on permeability; use of SEM images for low/ultra-low permeability samples; inclusion of multi-phase flow simulations; study of clay distribution and anisotropy effects on P-wave and S-wave velocities; comparison of pore size distributions using Micro-CT and NMR.
