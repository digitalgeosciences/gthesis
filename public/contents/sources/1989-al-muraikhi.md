# Evaluation of Vertical Multiphase Flow Correlations for Saudi Arabian Field Conditions

**Summary**: The thesis evaluates several standard vertical multiphase flow correlations (Aziz and Govier, Hagedorn and Brown, Duns and Ros, Orkiszewski, and Beggs and Brill) against field data from Saudi Arabian fields. It concludes that while Beggs and Brill is the best overall performer, specific correlations perform better under certain conditions such as high water cut or high liquid rates.

---

## Research Problem

The selection of an appropriate multiphase flow correlation is critical for accurately predicting pressure drops in wellbores; existing correlations often perform inconsistently across different production conditions such as varying tubing diameters, water cuts (WC), and gas-liquid ratios (GLR).

## Objectives

- Test available vertical multiphase flow correlations against Saudi Arabian field conditions.
- Identify and recommend the best correlation for predicting pressure drop in the specific conditions of the studied Saudi Arabian fields.
- Improve certain vertical multiphase flow correlations by employing appropriate methods for predicting flow pattern transitions.
- Evaluate the performance of six vertical multiphase flow correlations using field data from five Saudi Arabian fields.
- Compare the accuracy of these correlations based on parameters like tubing size, water cut, and gas-liquid ratio.
- Evaluate the performance of six vertical multiphase flow correlations for prediction of pressure drop.

## Methods

The study involved developing computer programs to implement six multiphase flow correlations: Beggs and Brill, Hagedorn and Brown, Duns and Ros, Kabir and Hasan, Orkiszewski, and Aziz and Govier. These were evaluated against 414 data points from Saudi Arabian oil fields. Statistical analysis (Average Absolute Percent Error - AAPE, Standard Deviation - SD, Coefficient of Variation - CV, and correlation coefficient - r) was used to quantify accuracy across different tubing sizes (2.375, 2.875, 3.5, 4.5, and 7 inches), water cuts, gas-liquid ratios, and production rates.

## Data and Materials

A total of 414 field data points were used, covering pipe sizes from 2.375 in. to 7.0 in., oil flow rates from 280 to 23,200 BPD, water cut up to 95%, and gas-oil ratio up to 927 SCF/STB.

## Key Findings

- The standard Beggs and Brill correlation provided the best predictions considering all data combined. ([[1989-al-muraikhi|Al-Muraikhi, 1989, p. 19]])
- Hagedorn and Brown correlation provided better predictions for water cuts above 80%. ([[1989-al-muraikhi|Al-Muraikhi, 1989, p. 19]])
- Kabir and Hasan correlation provided better predictions for total liquid rate higher than 20,000 BPD. ([[1989-al-muraikhi|Al-Muraikhi, 1989, p. 19]])
- The study identified that the accuracy of correlations varies significantly based on tubing size and flow rates due to their original development parameters being smaller than those in Saudi Arabian fields. ([[1989-al-muraikhi|Al-Muraikhi, 1989, p. 22]])
- The Hagedorn and Brown correlation provides good pressure prediction for all tubing sizes but performs better in wells with high water cuts (e.g., 87% of data points with WC > 20% were within ±10% error) and lower gas-liquid ratios. ([[1989-al-muraikhi|Al-Muraikhi, 1989, p. 90]])
- The Aziz and Govier correlation tends to underpredict bottomhole pressure for all tubing sizes and shows a significant shift of the mean error toward the positive side (approx. 10%). ([[1989-al-muraikhi|Al-Muraikhi, 1989, p. 100]])
- The Duns and Ros correlation provides good predictions for most tubing sizes, with 70% of data points for 2.375 and 2.875 inch tubings predicted within ±10% error. ([[1989-al-muraikhi|Al-Muraikhi, 1989, p. 111]])
- The Kabir and Hasan correlation tends to underpredict the pressure drop for small tubings, low water cuts, and high gas-liquid ratios. ([[1989-al-muraikhi|Al-Muraikhi, 1989, p. 118]])
- For 7 inch tubing, the Hagedorn and Brown correlation shows a coefficient of variation of 14.0 and a correlation coefficient of 0.66 for wells with water cut greater than zero. ([[1989-al-muraikhi|Al-Muraikhi, 1989, p. 117]])
- The Duns and Ros correlation's accuracy improves as the gas-liquid ratio decreases, with 80% of data points with GLR < 350 SCF/STB predicted within ±10% error. ([[1989-al-muraikhi|Al-Muraikhi, 1989, p. 111]])
- The Kabir and Hasan correlation shows a mean error of approximately 4%, indicating a good correlation for the specific conditions tested. ([[1989-al-muraikhi|Al-Muraikhi, 1989, p. 118]])
- The Beggs and Brill correlation was found to be the best overall for predicting pressure drop across all data points, with an average absolute percent error (AAPE) of 6.72% and a correlation coefficient of 0.78. ([[1989-al-muraikhi|Al-Muraikhi, 1989, p. 173]])
- The Hagedorn and Brown correlation was the best for predicting pressure drop in wells with more than 80% water cut. ([[1989-al-muraikhi|Al-Muraikhi, 1989, p. 185]])
- The Kabir and Hasan correlation was the best for wells producing more than 20,000 STB/day total fluid. ([[1989-al-muraikhi|Al-Muraikhi, 1989, p. 185]])
- For 3.5 in. tubing, the Orkiszewski correlation showed a significant improvement when using Duns and Ros or Orkiszewski flow pattern transition criteria compared to its original form. ([[1989-al-muraikhi|Al-Muraikhi, 1989, p. 177]])

## Limitations

No single correlation was found to predict the pressure drop with good accuracy for all ranges of variables.
