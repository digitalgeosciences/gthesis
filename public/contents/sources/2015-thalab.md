# Performance of Saudi Bentonite and Nano clay Admixed Cement for HPHT Conditions

**Summary**: The thesis evaluates the mechanical and rheological properties of cement slurries containing untreated Saudi bentonite, Nano clay, and treated Saudi bentonite under high pressure and high temperature (HPHT) conditions. It concludes that both Saudi bentonite and Nano clay enhance compressive strength and improve slurry performance, while treated Saudi bentonite serves as a viable alternative to commercial bentonite for low-density applications.

---

## Research Problem

The use of raw Saudi bentonite in cement systems often results in poor performance due to high iron content and low sodium-to-calcium ratios compared to commercial standards. This study addresses the need for an effective treatment process (soda ash and thermal) to upgrade local bentonite and evaluate its effectiveness when combined with nano clay under HPHT conditions.

## Objectives

- To study the effect of untreated Saudi bentonite admixed with Saudi cement class G oil well cementing under HPHT Conditions.
- To study the influence of Nano-clay on the cement slurry composed of Saudi cement class G and untreated Saudi bentonite for the optimum mix.
- To compare the performance of cement slurry with 1.9% treated Saudi bentonite and 1.9% commercial bentonite for cement slurry with a density of 101 PCF.
- Evaluate the effect of Saudi bentonite on the physical and chemical properties of Portland cement.
- Investigate the performance of Nano clay as an additive for improving cement strength and durability under HPHT conditions.
- Compare the performance of local Saudi bentonite with standard commercial additives in a slurry design.

## Methods

The study utilized a variety-range of laboratory tests to evaluate cement performance. Thickening time was measured for base mixes and mixtures with 1%, 2%, and 3% Saudi bentonite (SB). Fluid loss was measured in ml/cc per 30 minutes. Free water separation was tested by aging samples for two hours at room temperature. Density was measured using a pressurized cement balance. Rheology was determined by measuring plastic viscosity (cp) and yield point (lb/100 ft2). Gel strength was evaluated using an Ofite and Fann Viscometer. Compressive strength was measured via crushing tests after 24 and 48 hours of curing, and also monitored in real-time using an ultrasonic cement analyzer (UCA) under high pressure (4666 psi) and high temperature (290°F).

## Data and Materials

The study uses Class G cement with various additives including 35% silica flour, dispersants, and expanding agents. Samples include untreated Saudi bented (analyzed via XRD/SEM-EDS) and Nano clay (analyzed via XRF). A typical Saudi well profile is modeled with a depth of 14,000 ft, BHST of 290°F, and BHCT of 228°F.

## Key Findings

- Untreated Saudi bentonite and Nano clay exhibit pozzolanic activity under HPHT conditions, resulting in significant enhancement of compressive strength. ([[2015-thalab|Thalab, 2015, p. 21]])
- The addition of untreated Saudi bentonite and Nano clay reduces porosity and permeability while allowing control on thickening time. ([[2015-thalab|Thalab, 2015, p. 21]])
- Untreated Saudi bentonite and Nano clay reduce fluid loss with no free water and prevent settling under HPHT conditions. ([[2015-thalab|Thalab, 2015, p. 21]])
- Treated Saudi bentonite (at 1.9% bwoc) provides good dispersion and suspension of particles, similar to commercial bentonite. ([[2015-thalab|Thalab, 2015, p. 21]])
- Treated Saudi bentonite fulfills all requirements for an extender in oil well cementing applications, including reducing cement density. ([[2015-thalab|Thalab, 2015, p. 21]])
- Saudi bentonite is a calcium-based type containing high percentages of silica and lower amounts of sodium compared to commercial varieties. ([[2015-thalab|Thalab, 2015, p. 27]])
- Saudi bentonite contains higher percentages of quartz and lower amounts of kaolinite compared to commercial bentonite. ([[2015-thalab|Thalab, 2015, p. 45]])
- Adding 5% Nano-clay resulted in an increase in the elastic modulus by 143% at 28 days. ([[2015-thalab|Thalab, 2015, p. 61]])
- Mixing 7% of Nano-alumina (related to nano-clay study) led to a 30% rise in compressive strength after 7 days. ([[2015-thalab|Thalab, 2015, p. 61]])
- The addition of Nano-silica caused an increase in air content by 79% and a reduction in density by 2.4%. ([[2015-thalab|Thalab, 2015, p. 62]])
- Sauradi bentonite is a calcium-based clay with poor swelling properties but can be used as a cost-effective extender. ([[2015-thalab|Thalab, 2015, p. 49]])
- The optimal concentration of Nano-silica for improving mechanical and rheological properties in Saudi cement Class G was found to be 1%. ([[2015-thalab|Thalab, 2015, p. 63]])
- The optimal concentration of Nano-clay for enhancing properties under HPHT conditions was determined to be 1%. ([[2015-thalab|Thalab, 2015, p. 63]])
- Adding silica flour (35%) to cement prevents strength retrogression at temperatures above 230°F (110°C). ([[2015-thalab|Thalab, 2015, p. 45]])
- Nano clay is a layered material with a thickness of 10 A° and width up to 1000 nm, which can enhance cement strength, stiffness, and heat resistance. ([[2015-thalab|Thalab, 2015, p. 71]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Remove silica products from the cement design to potentially improve performance.
- Test systems for shallow wells with lower pressure and temperature at various water-cement ratios.
- Develop analytical models to study the effects of additives on cement rheology and mechanical properties.
