# Investigating Different Types of Shale Inhibitors Across Qusaiba Shale Formation

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the effectiveness of various shale inhibitors (Latexes, Amines, Nano-Silica, and KCl) on drilling fluid properties and their ability to inhibit swelling and dispersion in the Qusaiba Shale formation. The study utilizes NMR, FT-IR, TGA for chemical characterization, alongside rheological testing and linear swelling analysis to determine the most effective inhibitor combinations.

---

## Research Problem

The selection of appropriate shale inhibitors is critical to prevent clay hydration and swelling in formations like the Qusaiba Shale, which can lead to wellbore instability and poor drilling performance. The study addresses the need to identify an inhibitor that maintains optimal rheological properties while providing high inhibition efficiency.

## Objectives

- Investigate different types of shale inhibitor mechanisms (Hydrogen Bonding, Ion Exchange, Coating) across the Qusaiba Shale formation.
- Characterize selected shale inhibitors using NMR and FT-IR to identify functional groups.
- Evaluate drilling fluid rheology and performance using API standard tests (HPHT filtration, hot rolling).
- Assess the performance of various inhibitors in preventing the dispersion and swelling of Qusaiba shale samples through hot rolling and linear swelling tests.
- Characterize the chemical properties of different types of shale inhibitors using NMR, FT-IR, and TGA.
- Evaluate the impact of these inhibitors on the rheological properties (PV, YP, Gel Strength) and filtration characteristics of drilling fluids.

## Methods

The study utilizes a multi-step laboratory approach: 1) Characterization of inhibitors (Latex, Amines, Nano-silica) via NMR to identify functional groups and FT-IR to determine chemical composition. 2) Preparation of drilling fluid formulations with varying concentrations of inhibitors (e.g., Latex at 10ml, Amines at 1-3%, KCl at 7%). 3) API standard testing including Hot Rolling (16 hours at 200°F and 500 psi), HPHT Filtration to measure fluid loss, and Viscometry for rheology. 4) Shale interaction tests: Linear Swelling Test using powdered Qusaiba shale and bentonite as a reference, and Dispersion Test to calculate the percentage of recovered shale after hot rolling.

## Data and Materials

Samples included Qusaiba shale outcrops (mineralogy determined by XRD), bentonite for comparison, and several commercial inhibitors: Latex 2000, Max-Shield, PoroSeal, D 600, IDISIL SI 4545, and Max-Guard. Experimental parameters included specific concentrations of additives and standard API testing conditions (16 hours at 200°F).

## Key Findings

- Qusaiba shale mineralogy consists of Quartz (21%), Microcline (9%), Pyrite (1%), Kaolinite (39%), Chlorite (7%), Illite (14%), and I/S (9%). ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 52]])
- Smectite clay, specifically montmorillonite, is highly sensitive to water due to high Cation Exchange Capacity (80-150 meq/100g) and large swelling potential. ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 29]])
- Chlorite, illite, and kaolinite show lower hydration but can still be subject to sloughing and dispersion. ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 28]])
- Potassium (K+) is identified as a highly effective ion for inhibiting swelling due to its small hydrated radius and ability to fit into the clay lattice. ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 33]])
- Poly-amidoamine was identified as an effective inhibitor using the hydrogen bonding mechanism. ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 37]])
- Latex and Nano-silica (e.g., Max-Shield, PoroSeal) provide coating/physical plugging mechanisms to seal pore throats and prevent fluid invasion. ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 56]])
- FT-IR analysis revealed that the dominant functional group in most of the samples was O-H, with other groups like C=O, N-H, C=C, C-O, and Si-O-Si also detected. ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 95]])
- TGA analysis showed that all inhibitors except Nano-Silica were primarily organic/water-based materials with a Total Weight Loss (TWL) of over 95% and residual values under 5%. ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 96]])
- Nano-Silica was identified as the only inhibitor containing a significant inorganic component, with a residue of approximately 47% after TGA analysis. ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 96]])
- The reference drilling formulation (without inhibitors) showed the lowest Plastic Viscosity (PV) of 15 cP. ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 74]])
- Latex and Nano-silica formulations exhibited a wide range of PV between 18 cP and 34 cP at different concentrations. ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 74]])
- The reference formulation showed a Yield Point (YP) of 30 lbf/100ft^2, while formulations with amines showed values between 21 and 35 lbf/100ft^2. ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 75]])
- KLA-STOP at a 3% concentration exceeded the maximum acceptable filtration limit of 12 ml, recording 15 ml of filtrate loss. ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 83]])
- Amines inhibitors demonstrated superior performance in hot rolling dispersion tests, with recovery rates exceeding 95% for some formulations (e.g., KLA-Stop at 3% reached 95.1%). ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 87]])
- Latex 2000 showed the lowest swelling percentage on Qusaiba shale among all tested inhibitors, with a value of 23.2%. ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 92]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

The study recommends mixing two types of shale inhibitors (e.g., Latex and Amines) to improve inhibition efficiency while maintaining optimal rheological properties and ensuring resistance to water flow.
