# Reservoir quality of the Unayzah formation in Waqr, Tinat, and Haradh fields

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the reservoir quality of the Unayzah Formation in the Waqr, Tinat, and Haradh fields by integrating grain size analysis, factor analysis, and wireline log interpretation. It concludes that reservoir quality is closely correlated with depositional environments and proposes a neural network approach for lithofacies classification and porosity prediction.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the challenge of characterizing reservoir heterogeneity and predicting porosity in the Unayzah Formation (a Permo-Carboniferous siliciclastic sequence) across three different fields, where traditional methods may struggle to capture complex lithofacies relationships.

## Objectives

- Identify and understand the effect of diagenesis on the quality of the Unayzah Reservoir in the Waqr, Tinat, and Haradh fields.
- Interpret the depositional environment of the Unayzah Formation at the study area.
- Predict reservoir porosity using well log data through neural network analysis.
- Study the diagenetic and depositional controls on reservoir quality.
- Evaluate the depositional environment using grain size analysis and wireline logs.
- Predict the reservoir porosity and lithofacies classification using neural networks.
- To characterize the lithology and mineralogy of the Unayzah Formation.
- To identify and quantify the primary and secondary porosity-forming processes (compaction, cementation, dissolution).
- To determine the paragenetic sequence of cementation phases.
- To evaluate the impact of mineral replacement and alteration on reservoir quality.
- To interpret the depositional environment using grain size analysis.
- Determine depositional environments for the Unayzah Formation using grain size analysis and factor analysis.
- Identify electrofacies from wireline logs (Gamma ray, Sonic, Bulk Density) in the Waqr, Tinat, and Haradh fields.
- Classify lithofacies based on petrophysical properties and textural features.
- Develop a neural network model to predict reservoir porosity using well log data.
- Classify lithofacies using neural networks based on well log data and core information.
- Predict porosity of the Unayzah Reservoir using both genetic (lithofacies-informed) and non-genetic approaches.
- Evaluate the effectiveness of neural network models in predicting reservoir properties compared to standard logs.
- Characterize the lithology and mineralogy of the Unayzah Formation.
- Identify the main diagenetic processes affecting porosity and permeability.
- Determine the impact of clay minerals (illite, kaolinite, smectite, chlorite) on reservoir quality.
- Evaluate grain size distribution as a predictor for reservoir properties.
- Apply neural networks to classify lithofacies and predict porosity from well log data.

## Methods

The study employed mechanical sieving for grain size analysis of core samples from the HRDH-A well. Q-mode factor analysis was performed on 24 core samples across three fields, followed by varimax rotation and ternary plot visualization to identify end-member lithofacies. Wireline log interpretation (Gamma ray, Sonic, Bulk Density) was used to identify electrofacies and depositional environments in the Waqr, Tinat, and Haradh fields. A neural network approach was proposed for supervised lithofacies classification and non-linear regression to predict porosity.

## Data and Materials

Petrographic and XRD analyses were conducted on samples from the Unayzah Formation in the Waqr (WAQR-A), Tinat (TINT-A), and Haradh (HRDH-A) fields. Grain size data for multiple samples (e.g., 18 samples across three fields) were analyzed to correlate with log-derived properties.

## Key Findings

- The Unayzah Formation consists of sandstones, shale, and siltstone; the sandstones are primarily fine to medium-grained, moderately well-sorted quartz arenites. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 23]])
- Calcite, clay, and quartz overgrowths are the major authigenic cements in the Unayzah Formation. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 23]])
- Major diagenetic processes include compaction, phases of cementation (early calcite, authigenic clay coatings, quartz overgrowth, and late calcite), and extensive grain/cement dissolution. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 23]])
- The development of secondary porosity due to extensive grain and cement dissolution is an important diagenetic event in the Unayzah sandstone. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 23]])
- Depositional environment was interpreted as a shallow or marginal marine setting using probability cumulative curves, factor analysis, and wireline logs. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 23]])
- Neural network-based lithofacies classification results were found to be in agreement with the well log profiles. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 23]])
- Genetically derived porosity was found to be in better agreement with neutron porosity than non-genetic methods. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 23]])
- In the Haradh field (HRDH-A), Unit 1 (quartz wacke) has a porosity of 10-15% (average 12%) and is considered good to moderate quality, while Unit 2 (argillaceous wacke) has 2-6% porosity (average 5%) and is poor quality. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 20]])
- In the Haradh field, detrital quartz represents 50-90% of total detrital components; feldspars constitute less than 0.5%. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 21]])
- Calcite is a major cement in the Haradh field, representing 10 to 65% of total cement, and its dissolution is a primary cause for secondary porosity. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 24]])
- In the Tinat field (TINT-A), Group 1 (quartz arenite) has 12-20% porosity (average 15%) and good to moderate quality; Group 2 (quartz wacke) has 2-5% porosity (average 3%) and poor quality. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 27]])
- In the Tinat field, detrital quartz accounts for 50-95% of components; feldspars are ~1%; lithic fragments are <0.1%. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 31]])
- In the Waqr field (WAQR-A), Group 1 (quartz wacke) has 5-15% porosity (average 10%) and moderate to poor quality; Group 2 (sandy mudstone) has 1-3% porosity (average 2%) and is poor quality. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 33]])
- In the Waqr field, detrital quartz accounts for 50-95% of components; heavy minerals represent 3% of detrital components. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 37]])
- Porosity in all fields is significantly reduced by matrix content: samples with high matrix content (e.g., >40%) show porosity as low as 2%, while low-matrix samples reach up to 15%. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 34]])

## Limitations

The non-genetic approach for porosity prediction was found to be less accurate than the genetic approach because it lacked the additional separation of patterns provided by lithofacies information.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Use studied wells as a model to increase understanding of the Unayzah Formation by adding data from more wells in Haradh, Tinat, and Waar fields.
- Apply similar neural network approaches to study deeper reservoir intervals of the Unayzah Formation.
- Utilize FMI and geochemical logs to better understand the depositional history of the formation.
