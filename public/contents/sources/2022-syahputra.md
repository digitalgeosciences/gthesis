# SPATIAL PATTERN OF A BURROWED CARBONATE STRATUM (RAMP CARBONATES), HANIFA FORMATION, CENTRAL SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the spatial patterns and petrophysical properties of a specific burrowed carbonate stratum within the Ulayyah Member of the Hanifa Formation in central Saudi Arabia. Using integrated methods including core flooding, CT scanning, and thin-section petrography, the study characterizes the relationship between burrow morphology and reservoir quality across three outcrop locations.

---

## Research Problem

The spatial patterns of burrowed carbonate strata in ramp systems are poorly understood despite their significance as flow zones in hydrocarbon reservoirs and water aquifers. Specifically, the lack of data on how lateral/vertical variations in burrowing styles and lithofacies impact petrophysical properties in the Hanifa Formation (Ulayyah Member) limits reservoir modeling.

## Objectives

- Illustrate how lithofacies change vertically and laterally in the studied stratum by characterizing rock texture, sedimentary structures, biocomponents, and burrow attributes.
- Demonstrate how the petrophysical properties of the stratum change vertically and laterally by characterizing porosity and permeability variation.
- Interpret how depositional processes and sedimentological patterns are related to the vertically and laterally change in lithofacies and petrophysical properties of the studied stratum.
- Observe possible implications for the key findings of the study to understand carbonate reservoirs with burrows (ramp carbonate) elsewhere.
- Trace and characterize the spatial pattern of a burrowed carbonate stratum in the Ulayyah Member of the Hanifa Formation.
- Quantify the burrow percentage (BP) and morphology using CT scanning and image analysis.
- Evaluate the petrophysical properties (porosity and permeability) of both the host rock matrix and the burrow fills.
- Correlate the sedimentological, stratigraphic, and ichnological patterns across three distinct outcrop locations.
- Trace a specific stratum in the upper Hanifa Formation to understand its depositional environment.
- Analyze the spatial variation of rock texture and burrowing styles (bio-retexturing vs. pipe framework).
- Evaluate the impact of burrowing style and intensity on petrophysical properties (porosity and permeability) across different sections.
- Trace a specific burrowed stratum in the upper Hanifa Formation.
- Characterize the sedimentological and ichnological processes affecting host rock and burrow attributes.
- Evaluate the lateral and vertical variation of permeability and porosity influenced by burrowing styles.

## Methods

The study utilized a multi-scale approach: 1) Fieldwork involved tracing the stratum over ~30 km and collecting 4-inch core samples at Oyeynah (OY), Jabel Al-Abbaqayn (JA), and Sadous (SD). 2) Laboratory analysis included thin-section petrography of 40 sections to distinguish matrix from burrow fills. 3) CT scanning was performed using a Toshiba Alexion TSX-032A scanner, with images processed in OsiriX and Fiji (using Trainable Weka Segmentation) to calculate burrow percentages. 4) Petrophysical analysis included core flooding of 9.4 cm cores to measure bulk permeability, AutoScan system spot-permeability on slabs to map distribution, and gas porosity/permeability measurements on 1.5-inch core plugs.

## Data and Materials

Data includes three outcrop locations (Oyeynah, Jabal Al-Abakkayn, Sadous) in the Hanifa Formation. Core samples were analyzed for petrophysical properties; specifically, 6 core samples were used for core flooding and spot permeability measurements to compare with gas porosity/permeability data.

## Key Findings

- The studied stratum shows a consistent spatial stratigraphic pattern (sharp contacts with underlying/overlying strata), suggesting it is the product of a single depositional cycle. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 13]])
- The lower interval of the stratum consists of wackestone and packstone, interpreted as a result of bio-retexturing during the initial flooding stage. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 13]])
- The middle and upper intervals contain mudstone and wackestone host rock penetrated by Thalassinoides burrow networks (pipe framework) with passive infills. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 13]])
- The stratum exhibits both vertical and lateral variations in burrow percentage and burrowing styles, interpreted as being controlled by water bathymetry variation due to level changes and seafloor topography. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 14]])
- The Hanifa Formation (Ulayyah Member) shows excellent porosity ranging from 5-32% in major fields like Abqaiq and Berri. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 17]])
- Petrophysical properties are influenced by the distinction between burrowed rock textures (e.g., pipe framework) and host rock matrix. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 14]])
- The studied stratum is a burrowed foraminiferal wackestone/mud-dominated packstone with a thickness of 140-150 cm, showing consistent spatial patterns over ~30 km. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 31]])
- Burrow percentage (BP) in the Oyeyyan section varies from 5% to 30%, with a distinct peak of 20-30% in the upper interval. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 43]])
- The Jabel Al-Abakkayn (JA) section shows extensive burrowing with BP ranging from 10-40%, dominated by Thalassinoides and Ophiomorha. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 43]])
- The Sadous (SD) section features a burrow percentage of 5-40% and is characterized by the presence of cherts (5-40 cm long) parallel to bedding. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 47]])
- Host rock matrix in all locations shows mud-dominated texture with abundant sponge spicules (monaxons and triaxons). ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 50]])
- The burrow infills consist of a grain-dominated texture with corroded peloids (0.2–1.6 mm) and coated grains (1–5.4 mm). ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 51]])
- Burrow intensity (BP) varies vertically: lower interval (30–40 cm thick, 10–20% BP), middle interval (30–80 cm thick, 5–10% BP), and upper interval (30–80 cm thick, 20–40% BP). ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 53]])
- The lower interval is dominated by bio-retexturing style; the middle and upper intervals are dominated by pipe framework style. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 54]])
- CT scans of 6 cores revealed average burrow percentages (BP) ranging from 1.45% to 19.06%. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 57]])

## Recommendations and Future Work
