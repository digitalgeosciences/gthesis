# SPATIAL PATTERN OF A BURROWED CARBONATE STRATUM (RAMP CARBONATES), HANIFA FORMATION, CENTRAL SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the spatial patterns of a burrowed carbonate stratum within the Ulayyah Member of the Hanifa Formation in central Saudi Arabia. Using petrography, CT scanning, and integrated petrophysical measurements (core flooding, spot permeability), it concludes that while the stratigraphic pattern is consistent over 30 km, rock texture and burrow attributes vary due to paleo-environmental factors like water bathymetry.

---

## Research Problem

The spatial patterns of burrowed carbonate strata in ramp settings are poorly understood despite their significance as flow zones in hydrocarbon reservoirs and aquifers. Specifically, the lack of data on how lateral/vertical variations in burrow morphology and rock texture impact petrophysical properties in the Hanifa Formation's Ulayyah Member.

## Objectives

- Illustrate how lithofacies change vertically and laterally in the studied stratum by characterizing rock texture, sedimentary structures, biocomponents, and burrow attributes.
- Demonstrate how the petrophysical properties of the stratum change vertically and laterally by characterizing porosity and permeability variation.
- Interpret how depositional processes and sedimentological patterns are related to the vertically and laterally change in lithofacies and petrophysical properties of the studied stratum.
- Observe possible implications for the key findings of the study to understand carbonate reservoirs with burrows (ramp carbonate) elsewhere.
- Trace a specific burrowed stratum in the upper Hanifa Formation to understand its spatial patterns.
- Identify and characterize different burrowing styles (bio-retexturing vs. pipe building).

## Methods

The study utilized a multi-scale approach: 1) Fieldwork involved tracing the stratum over 30 km and collecting 4-inch core samples at three locations (Oyeynah, Jabel Al-Abakkayn, Sadous). 2) Laboratory petrography used stained thin sections to differentiate matrix from burrow fills. 3) CT scanning (Toshiba Alexion TSX-032A) and image analysis (OsiriX, Fiji, Trainable Weka Segmentation) were used to quantify burrow percentage (BP) and connectivity. 4) Petrophysical measurements included core flooding for bulk permeability of 9.4 cm cores, AutoScan system for high-resolution spot permeability on slabs, and gas permeameter/gas porosity for 1.5-inch core plugs.

## Data and Materials

30 km of outcropping stratum; 6 core samples (9.4 cm diameter) from three locations (Oyeynah, Jabel Al-Abakkayn, Sadous); 40 thin sections; 16 slabs for spot permeability measurements; and various sets of 1.5-inch core plugs.

## Key Findings

- The studied stratum shows a consistent spatial stratigraphic pattern over the study area (sharp contacts with underlying/overlying strata), suggesting it is a product of one depositional cycle. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 13]])
- The lower interval of the stratum comprises wackestone and packstone rock texture, interpreted as a product of bio-retexturing during initial flooding. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 13]])
- The stratum features a mudstone/wackestone matrix penetrated by Thalassinoides burrow networks (pipe framework) with passive infills in the middle and upper intervals. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 13]])
- Variation in burrow percentage and styles is interpreted to be controlled by water bathymetry changes due to level change and seafloor topography. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 14]])
- The Oyeynah (OY) section shows a thickness of 150 cm with burrow percentages ranging from 5-30% and prominent concretion features. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 42]])
- The Jabel Al-Abakkayn (JA) section shows a thickness of 140 cm with extensive burrowing (Thalassinoides and Ophiomorpha) and higher burrow percentages (up to 40%). ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 43]])
- The Sadous (SD) section features a thickness of 150 cm, including chert nodules (5-40 cm) parallel to bedding. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 47]])
- Burrow connectivity was only observed in the pipe framework style; bio-retexturing styles showed no connectivity as burrow shafts could not be recognized in CT data. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 57]])
- Bulk permeability values for the upper part of the stratum ranged from 0.676 to 3.823 mD, with a lateral increase toward the Oyeynah section. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 61]])
- Permeability contrast between matrix and burrow fills reached three orders of magnitude in Sadous and Oyeynah sections, while only two orders were observed in Jabel Al-Abakkayn. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 64]])
- The harmonic mean of spot permeability measurements provided the closest correlation to bulk permeability from core flooding tests. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 65]])
- The burrowed stratum shows a laterally similar spatial stratigraphic pattern for ~30 km with sharp contacts and constant thickness, but varies in host rock composition and burrow attributes. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 81]])
- Host rock was deposited in low-energy, quiet water below wave base (middle ramp), while burrow infills are interpreted as high-energy tempestites. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 81]])
- Two distinct burrowing styles were identified: bio-retexturing (mixing grain-dominated infills with mud-dominated host) and pipe building (Glossifungites ichnofacies of Thalassinoides). ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 81]])
- The middle interval shows a burrow percentage (BP) of 10-15%, while the upper interval shows 30-40%. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 76]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

The findings suggest that understanding burrowing styles and intensity is crucial for predicting petrophysical properties (especially permeability) during reservoir characterization and horizontal well placement.
