# POROSITY CLASSIFICATION AND RESERVOIR FLOW UNITS RANKING IN THE KHARTAM MEMBER, KHUFF FORMATION (PERMO-TRIASSIC): OUTCROP APPROACH, CENTRAL SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the porosity and flow unit characteristics of the Upper Khartam Member of the Khuff Formation in central Saudi Arabia using an outcrop approach. It identifies eight distinct pore types based on geometry, establishes a porosity-permeability relationship, and ranks flow units from 1 to 9 based on petrophysical properties and diagenetic influence.

---

## Research Problem

The complexity of carbonate reservoirs like the Khuff Formation, caused by diverse sedimentary processes, biological activities, and diagenetic changes, makes predicting porosity and permeability challenging. A systematic classification of pore types and identification of flow units are necessary for accurate reservoir characterization.

## Methods

The study involved field work in the At Tarafiyah area to map outcrop sections and identify reservoir units. Laboratory analysis included core plug measurements for porosity and permeability using the water saturation method, thin-section petrography under an optical microscope, and SEM imaging for microporosity. Petrographic Image Analysis (PIA) was performed using JMicroVision 1.2.7 software on digitized thin section images to quantify pore area, perimeter, length, and width, allowing for the calculation of aspect ratio and roundness factor to determine pore shape.

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes outcrop data from the Upper Khartam Member in the Tarafiyah area, including three distinct stratigraphic sections (S-1, S-2, S-3). Data includes thin section analysis of oolitic grainstones and mudstone/wackestone facies, core plug measurements for porosity and permeability, and satellite imagery for site mapping.

## Key Findings

- Three main lithofacies were identified: Well-Sorted Oolitic Grainstone, Cross-bedded Oolitic Skeletal Grainstone, and Graded Mudstone to Packstone. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 41]])
- Moldic and vuggy porosities are the primary pore types in the oolitic grainstone facies. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 45]])
- Intercrystalline porosity is primarily associated with dolomite crystals in the graded mudstone to packstone facies. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 46]])
- Dolomitization enhances overall porosity in the graded mudstone to packstone facies. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 46]])
- Micro-fractures are commonly filled with calcite cement but can remain open in association with dolomitization. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 44]])
- The study revealed two distinguished geometries of the moldic porosity (oomoldic and skelmoldic) and two different geometrical-sizes of the vuggy porosity (vuggy-1 and vuggy-2). ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 17]])
- Oomoldic and vuggy-1 porosities are ranked to be the best porosity types in terms of size and their contribution in the overall porosity. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 18]])
- The combination of oomoldic and vuggy-1 form the optimum reservoir quality. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 18]])
- Coefficient of variance indicated major heterogeneity in the porosity and permeability, which can be attributed to variation within individual pore types, presence of micropore systems, and mixture of pore types within individual facies. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 18]])
- Correlation between porosity and permeability was poor when lithofacies were combined but improved significantly when plotted based on individual facies groups. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 18]])
- Cross-bedded oolitic skeletal grainstone facies has the highest flow units quality where moldic and vuggy porosities are dominant. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 18]])
- Flow unit boundaries in vertical sections largely coincide with the facies boundaries, indicating that flow units are primarily controlled by depositional facies and diagenetic alterations (leaching/cementation). ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 20]])
- The Khuff Formation is characterized by complex reservoir quality due to sedimentary formation, biological activities, mineralogy, diagenetic changes, and stratigraphy. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 37]])
- The Khuff Formation in the subsurface is subdivided into five units (Khuff A, B, C, D, and E) representing cyclic deposition of sub-tidal carbonates and intertidal/Sabkha deposits. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 38]])
