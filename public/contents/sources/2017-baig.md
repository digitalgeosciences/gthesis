# Evaluation of Nano Zeolite as an Additive for Oil-Well Cementing

**Summary**: The thesis evaluates the impact of Nano zeolite and micro zeolite as additives in Class G cement for oil-well cementing under high pressure and temperature (HPHT) conditions. The study concludes that Nano zeolite acts as a nano-filler, significantly reducing permeability and porosity while enhancing compressive strength and providing a denser microstructure.

---

## Research Problem

The challenge of maintaining the integrity of oil-well cement in HPHT environments where standard cements may suffer from strength retrogression or gas migration. There is currently no literature specifically documenting the use of Nano zeolite as an additive for this purpose.

## Objectives

- Study various properties of Oil-well cement (OWC) with Nano zeolite as an additive.
- Evaluate the performance of Nano zeolite at severe temperature and pressure conditions (HPHT).
- Compare the effects of Nano zeolite versus micro zeolite on cement slurry properties.
- Determine if Nano zeolite can serve as an effective additive to improve durability and strength in HPHT wells.
- Assess the impact of Nano zeolite on the compressive strength development of cement over 24 hours.
- Evaluate the effect of Nano and micro zeolite on the physical properties of Class G cement slurry (density, rheology, free water content).

## Methods

The study follows API RP 65-2 and API Specifications 10B-2 for cement testing. Slurries are prepared using API Class G cement with various additives (SSA-1, MBHT, HR-12, CFR-3, Halad-344, Halad-413, DA-3000). Testing includes: Thickening time via HPHT consistometer; Density via pressurized mud balance; Fluid loss at 190°F and 1000 psi; Rheology (plastic viscosity, yield point) using a FANN rheometer; Compressive strength via both non-destructive sonic method (Ultra Cement Analyzer) and destructive Unconfined Compressive Strength (UCS) tests. Nano Zeolite characterization includes XRD, SEM, TEM, and XRF.

## Data and Materials

The study analyzed five cement slurry systems: BM (base mix), NZ1 (1% Nano zeolite), NZ2 (2% Nano zeolite), NZ3 (3% Nano zeolite), and MZ1 (1% micro zeolite). Samples were cured at 290°F for 24 hours. SEM/EDS analysis was performed on samples from these mixtures.

## Key Findings

- Rheological properties, including plastic viscosity, yield point, and gel strength, were increased with the addition of Nano zeolite. ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 20]])
- Hydration products were modified with the addition of Nano zeolite as indicated by SEM. ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 20]])
- Nano zeolite retarded the thickening time of cement slurry, while micro zeolite acted as an accelerator. ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 20]])
- No significant effect on density of cement slurry was observed with the addition of Nano and micro zeolite. ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 20]])
- Compressive strength was accelerated with both Nano and micro zeolite. ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 20]])
- Porosity and permeability were reduced significantly with Nano zeolite, resulting in a denser microstructure observed via SEM. ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 20]])
- Destructive compressive strength and tensile strength were shown to be increased with Nano zeolite addition. ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 20]])
- No significant effect on fluid loss of cement slurries was observed with Nano zeolite. ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 20]])
- Nano Zeolite is a pozzolanic material with reactivity between silica fume and fly ash; it reduces porosity but can increase it if replacement exceeds 25%. ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 62]])
- Nano Zeolite particles smaller than 1 micrometer (specifically <100 nm) show significant agglomeration in SEM/TEM images. ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 74]])
- XRF analysis of Nano Zeolite confirmed a composition of 61.30% SiO2, 30.33% Al2O3, 1.24% Fe2O3, and 4.27% CaO. ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 73]])
- Adding silica flour (30-40%) is required to prevent strength retrogression in high temperature wells (>230°F). ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 61]])
- Nanosilica as an accelerator for low-temperature wells (59°F) with API Class H cement showed significant enhancement of hydration and strength compared to CaCl2. ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 64]])
- Nanoclay improved the ultimate compressive strength and early hydration of cement, while also increasing plastic viscosity and acting as a retarder. ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 65]])
- Carbon nanotubes (CNTs) increased final compressive strength and allowed for an early strength of 2000 psi in less time than control mixtures. ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 65]])

## Limitations

The results are restricted to the specific well conditions, cement type (Class G), chemical additives, and concentrations of zeolites used in the study. The lack of reactivity in Nano zeolite is attributed to its crystalline nature.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Investigate the impact of zeolites for shallow applications as accelerators under low pressure and temperature.
- Test Nano zeolite with different water-to-cement ratios and varying T/P conditions.
- Test micro zeolite in a neat cement slurry without additives.
- Investigate calcination processes to convert crystalline zeolite into an amorphous form to increase reactivity.
