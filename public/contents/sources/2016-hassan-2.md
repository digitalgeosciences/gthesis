# Porosity and Permeability Characterization within the Lithofacies Framework of A Miocene Dam Formation Outcrop, Eastern Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the petrophysical properties of the Miocene Dam Formation in Eastern Saudi Arabia by analyzing four specific lithofacies (skeletal oolitic grainstone, sandy fossiliferous packstone, burrowed wackestone, and stromatolite grainstone). Using XRD, petrography, and core plug analysis, it establishes a link between sedimentological characteristics and porosity/permeability distributions.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the challenge of understanding reservoir heterogeneity (porosity and permeability) in the Dam Formation, where subsurface data is limited by sampling density. It specifically investigates how these properties vary across different sedimentary lithofacies within a 230m long outcrop.

## Methods

The study involved field sedimentology description using the Dunham classification for carbonate textures. Laboratory work included sample slabbing, slab imaging, thin section preparation, and core plug preparation (254 plugs). Petrographic analysis was conducted via point counting under a plain-polarized microscope. Porosity was measured using gas expansion (helium) with a TPI-219 device. Permeability was measured using the TKA-209 Gas Permeameter system. Geostatistical modeling included semivariogram analysis to determine spatial correlation, followed by ordinary kriging for interpolation and Sequential Gaussian Simulation (SGS) to generate multiple realizations of porosity and permeability.

## Data and Materials

The study utilized an outcrop of the Miocene Dam Formation in Eastern Saudi Arabia. Data included 15 measured sections across two faces of the outcrop, providing measurements for porosity and permeability at multiple points within various lithofacies (e.g., stromatolite grainstone, burrowed wackestone, sandy fossiliferous packstone).

## Key Findings

- The Dam Formation is composed of mixed carbonate-siliciclastic deposits in a tidal flat environment with a dominance of carbonate deposits. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 13]])
- The porosity-permeability relationship exhibits poor correlation for the selected lithofacies (skeletal oolitic grainstone, sandy fossiliferous packstone, burrowed wackestone, and stromatolite grainstone). ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 13]])
- Geostatistical models showed small scale heterogeneity in porosity and permeability even within the same lithofacies. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 14]])
- Heterogeneities in property distribution were attributed to both depositional and diagenetic controls on reservoir quality. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 14]])
- The Dam Formation is a Middle Miocene formation composed of mixed carbonate-siliciclastic sediments (marls, limestones, sandstones). ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 27]])
- Jeribe Formation (equivalent to the Dam Formation) shows reservoir porosity ranging from 14% to 21% and permeability from 1 to 1146 mD. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 32]])
- The study identifies that high-resolution outcrop data can reveal small-scale heterogeneity (1-2m) that is often missed by standard well logs. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 39]])
- Variogram analysis of petrophysical properties in other carbonate reservoirs showed clear features related to structurally controlled anisotropies and high nugget variances in complex lithologies. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 50]])
- A total of 254 samples were collected from the outcrop for petrographic and petrophysical analyses. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 44]])
- Porosity was measured using gas expansion (helium) and permeability was captured in both vertical and horizontal directions. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 45]])
- The TKA-209 Gas Permeameter system is used to calculate permeability from pressure and flow data. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 49]])
- Klinkenberg correction was applied to determine liquid permeability (Kl) by plotting gas permeability (Kg) against the reciprocal of mean pressure (1/Pm). ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016]])

- Skeletal oolitic grainstone (D10-5) exhibited porosity up to 40%, primarily of intergranular type, with some intragranular, moldic ooid and moldic skeletal porosity. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 79]])
- The sandy fossiliferous packstone (D10-12) was identified as the first lithofacies selected for later porosity and permeability modeling due to its high fossil content and thickness (up to 280 cm). ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 87]])

## Limitations

Limited number of samples per section (due to thin lithofacies and accessibility issues) resulted in poor variogram shapes in the vertical direction; however, 3D models still provided acceptable representations of facies properties.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Apply similar research procedures with higher-resolution sampling on other outcrops in Eastern Saudi Arabia.
- Conduct detailed regional lithofacies investigations to correlate these deposits with subsurface Miocene reservoirs in the Eastern Arabian Peninsula and Iran.
- Use LiDAR for geometry modeling in future studies to save time/effort.
- Perform more detailed petrophysical analyses to build stronger correlations between carbonate textures and reservoir properties.
