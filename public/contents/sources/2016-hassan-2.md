# Porosity and Permeability Characterization within the Lithofacies Framework of A Miocene Dam Formation Outcrop, Eastern Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the porosity and permeability of the Miocene Dam Formation in Eastern Saudi Arabia by integrating lithofacies analysis with 3D geostatistical modeling. Using data from an outcrop study, it characterizes four specific carbonate-siliciclastic lithofacies and evaluates their petrophysical properties through univariate statistics and sequential Gaussian simulation.

---

## Research Problem

The high heterogeneity of carbonate-siliciclastic reservoirs in the Dam Formation (Middle Miocene) makes predicting reservoir properties difficult. This study addresses the need to characterize these properties within a specific lithofacies framework using outcrop data to overcome limitations of subsurface data.

## Objectives

- Characterize the sedimentary facies and depositional setting of the Dam Formation outcrop to provide a framework for understanding reservoir heterogeneity.
- Characterize the petrophysical properties (porosity and permeability) of selected lithofacies in the Dam Formation.
- Establish the variability patterns of facies for the outcrop body, and for the porosity and permeability for the selected unit(s).
- Produce several realizations including facies, porosity, and permeability models for better understanding of these parameters.
- Perform sedimentology and petrophysical analyses on an outcrop of the Dam Formation.
- Characterize lithofacies within a composite section of the Dam Formation.

## Methods

The study utilized a combination of field observation, petrography, SEM imaging, Micro-CT scanning, and geostatistical modeling. Porosity was measured using a TPI-219 Helium porosimeter, and permeability was measured using a TKA-209 Gas Permeameter on core plugs (mostly 1.5" diameter). Univariate statistics (mean, median, SD, CV, skewness, kurtosis) were used to analyze data distribution. Geostatistical modeling involved constructing a geometry model in Petrel based on 15 measured sections, followed by indicator simulation for lithofacies and sequential Gaussian simulation (SGS) for porosity and permeability.

## Data and Materials

The study utilized 254 porosity and permeability measurements from core plugs. Data were collected from four specific lithofacies: skeletal oolitic grainstone (51 plugs), sandy fossiliferous packstone (112 plugs), burrowed wackestone (50 plugs), and stromatolite grainstone (41 plugs). 15 measured sections were used for the 3D geometry and facies modeling.

## Key Findings

- The Dam Formation is composed of mixed carbonate-siliciclastic deposits in a tidal flat environment with a dominance of carbonate deposits. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 13]])
- Selected lithofacies for petrophysical investigation include skeletal oolitic grainstone, sandy fossiliferous packstone, burrowed wackestone, and stromatolite grainstone. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 13]])
- The porosity-permeability relationship exhibits poor correlation for these lithofacies. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 13]])
- Geostatistical models showed small scale heterogeneity in porosity and permeability even within the same lithofacies, attributed to depositional and diagenetic controls. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 14]])
- The study confirmed that such models can be used as a guide for better understanding of the depositional setting, stacking pattern, and property characterization. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 14]])
- The Dam Formation is a Middle Miocene formation composed of mixed carbonate-siliciclastic sediments deposited in a tidally-influenced shallow marine environment. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 158]])
- Lithofacies D10-5 (Skeletal oolitic grainstone) exhibited porosity up to 40% and was characterized by intergranular, moldic, and intragranular types. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 80]])
- Lithofacies D10-9 (Estuarine sandstone) showed a permeability of 10 cm. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 103]])
- Lithofacies D10-12 (Sandy fossiliferous packstone) had a mean permeability of 100.673 mD and porosity of 30-40%. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 104]])
- Lithofacies D10-14 (Grainstone) showed a mean permeability of 213.079 mD. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 106]])
- XRD analysis confirmed that the selected lithofacies are dominated by dolomite minerals, suggesting fabric-preserving dolomite. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 98]])
- Desiccation cracks in the mudstone units were often filled with evaporite threads (anhydrite), indicating a tidal flat environment. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 100]])
- Stromatolite grainstone (D10-19) exhibited fenestral porosity, which is an indicator of supratidal deposition. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 96]])
- Porosity measurements for the four lithofacies showed homogeneous relationships between mean and median, but only skeletal oolitic grainstone exhibited a nearly symmetrical distribution (skewness near zero). ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 114]])
- Permeability data were highly heterogeneous; however, log-transformation of permeability values improved the normality of distribution and reduced skewness. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 116]])

## Limitations

The study was limited by the small number of samples in the vertical direction for geostatistical modeling due to thin bed thickness and accessibility issues, leading to poor-shaped semivariograms in the vertical dimension.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Apply similar research procedures with higher-resolution sampling on other outcrops in Eastern Saudi Arabia.
- Conduct detailed regional lithofacies investigation to correlate these deposits with subsurface Miocene reservoirs.
- Use LiDAR for geometry modeling in future studies to save time and effort.
- Perform further petrophysical analysis to build a solid correlation between porosity and permeability for prediction purposes.
- Investigate the geochemistry and provenance of sandstone influx to understand its effect on reservoir quality.
