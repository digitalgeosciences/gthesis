# The Use of Geophysical Methods in Geothermal Exploration of the Wadi Al Lith Field in Western Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the geothermal potential of the Wadi Al Lith field in Western Saudi Arabia by integrating land gravity, magnetic, and electromagnetic data with airborne magnetic datasets. It develops a workflow to characterize subsurface structures like heat sources, reservoirs, and tectonic features (faults and dikes) that facilitate thermal fluid circulation.

---

## Research Problem

There is a lack of detailed characterization regarding the geometry of the geothermal reservoir and the tectonic structures governing thermal water flow in the Wadi Al Lith area. Existing information is primarily based on small-scale surveys near specific hot springs, leaving the broader field's potential and structural controls poorly understood.

## Methods

The study employs an integrated geophysical approach. Land gravity data were collected using CG-5 AUTOGRAV sensors and processed via Oasis montaj to produce Bouguer anomaly maps and derivative filters (HGM, TDR). Airborne magnetic data from the Saudi Geological Survey (SGS) were used for regional context. Land magnetic data were acquired with a Geometrics G-858 MagMapper gradiometer and analyzed using Werner deconvolution and analytic signal methods. Electromagnetic data included MT (processed via KMSProMT) and TEM (processed via AGS Workbench). Additional constraints from a water well and a 750m seismic profile were integrated into the modeling.

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes: (1) Land gravity data from multiple stations in the Wadi Al Lith area; (2) Airborne magnetic data covering a wide region; (3) Magnetotelluric (MT) soundings (e.g., MT11, MT13); (4) Transient Electromagnetic (TEM) soundings; (5) Geological maps and field photographs of the Ain Al Harrah hot spring.

## Key Findings

- The Curie isotherm was estimated at an average depth of 14.8 km based on airborne magnetic data analysis. ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 104]])
- A geotherm was constructed with a Moho discontinuity at 27 km and the LAB at 49.7 km to predict surface heat flow. ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 104]])
- Subsurface lineament density maps identified tectonic structures, including faults (based on geological maps) and mafic/felsic dikes (identified via magnetic analysis). ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 100]])
- The integration of potential field and electromagnetic data successfully modeled the heat source and reservoir features. ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 18]])
- Deep electromagnetic soundings (MT) confirmed the calculated geotherm by showing a significant reduction in resistivity at the Curie isotherm depth. ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 114]])
- The Wadi Al Lith area is characterized by high geothermal gradient values exceeding 19 °C/km and heat flow values between 47.7 and 87.8 mW/m². ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 36]])
- The depth of the Curie isotherm in the Wadi Al Lith area is shallower than 29.6 km. ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 36]])
- Magnetotelluric data indicates that the heat source for the prospective geothermal field is located at an approximate depth of 5-6 km. ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 37]])
- The average reservoir temperature of the Ain Al Harrah hot spring is 136 °C with a heat flow of approximately 183 mW/m² (based on silica and Na-K-Ca geothermometers). ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 35]])

- Gravity methods provide preliminary insights into large-scale tectonic elements but cannot identify high temperature or heat flow anomalies directly. ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 41]])
- Magnetic methods are effective for determining depth to basement and identifying mineralogical variations (e.g., magnetite, pyrrhotite). ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 42]])
- The Curie isotherm can be used to estimate the geotherm of an area; a shift towards shallower depths indicates higher temperatures. ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 50]])
- MT is identified as the primary EM method for geothermal exploration due to its ability to image both shallow and deep-seated targets across various frequencies. ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 43]])
- The integration of seismic attributes (instantaneous phase, RMS amplitude, and variance) helps identify lithology and sedimentary processes. ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 55]])

## Limitations

The study notes that the Werner method is considered oversimplified and may be less suitable for very deep sources compared to spectral methods. Additionally, the lack of specific local density data required using regional literature values (e.g., Langheim et al.).

## Recommendations and Future Work

Future work should include: 1) High-resolution seismic (3D) and non-seismic surveys (surface CSEM, micro-gravity). 2) Detailed geological and updated geochemical studies of surface/groundwater. 3) Drilling of exploratory boreholes to ground-truth the estimated surface heat flow.
