# Analysis of Syn- and Post-Depositional Controls on Facies Distribution, Depositional Architecture and Reservoir Quality Modifications of Early Syn-rift Continental Deposits (Early Oligocene – Early Miocene), NW Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the depositional architecture and reservoir quality of mixed siliciclastic-carbonate deposits at the transition between the Al Wajh Formation and Musayr Formation in the Midyan Basin, NW Saudi Arabia. Using sedimentological, petrographical, and outcrop permeability measurements, it identifies three distinct facies associations (siliciclastic-dominated, mixed, and carbonate-rich) and evaluates how depositional factors and diagenesis control reservoir properties.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of understanding regarding how syn- and post-depositional controls (depositionally driven texture and diagenetically induced porosity loss/gain) influence the reservoir quality of early syn-rift continental deposits in the Red Sea region, specifically the Al Wajh Formation.

## Objectives

- Construction of depositional framework of Al Wajh Formation in the Midyan Basin to understand the spatial distribution of lithofacies and facies associations.
- Construction of depositional framework and reservoir quality assessment of non-marine deposits of Al Wajh Formation, mixed siliciclastic deposits of transitional interval and marine carbonate deposits of Musayr Formation to differentiate between reservoir and non-reservoir elements.
- investigate the type of facies and quantify facies distribution in early rift fluvial systems
- determine stratal geometries and factors controlling facies distribution and stratal geometries
- propose new conceptual depositional model for the early rift fluvial deposits
- discuss implications of this study for the exploration and development of the analogous early rift fluvial successions in the subsurface

## Methods

The study utilized detailed sedimentological logs of six stratigraphic sections (T1-T6). A 3D outcrop model was generated using a Nikon D850 camera, GPS, and RealityCapture software. Paleocurrents were measured via sedimentary structures. Petrographic analysis was performed on thin sections (n=35) to determine mineralogy and texture. QEMSCAN was used for automated mineralogical analysis of thin sections to quantify minerals and clay content. SEM-EDS was used to analyze the morphology, chemistry, and pore-scale distribution of minerals. Permeability was measured directly on outcrops using a TinyPerm© portable handheld air permeameter (n=73). Porosity was estimated from thin section images using the Zeiss Axioscan digital petrography system with the Intellesis machine learning platform.

## Data and Materials

Data includes sedimentary logs from 10 sections across four subbasins (Sukk, Ifal, Maqna-1, Maqna-2), high-resolution 3D outcrop models of several sections, and a suite of mineralogical/petrophysical analyses on samples from the Al Wajh and Musayr Formations.

## Key Findings

- Identified 11 lithofacies (F1-F11) and four facies associations (FA1: alluvial fan, FA2: braided fluvial fan, FA3: floodplain, FA4: shoreface/delta front). ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 30]])
- Braided fluvial fan sandstones (FA2) represent excellent reservoirs with average porosity of 23.2% and permeability of 1884 mD. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 29]])
- Clay matrix content ranging from 10 to 20% has a deleterious impact on reservoir quality, lowering porosity and permeability to less than 10% and 10 mD, respectively. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 134]])
- Secondary porosity, formed mainly due to feldspar dissolution, can be as high as 13.5%. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 134]])
- Kaolinite is the primary authigenic clay in continental sandstones, while grain-replacive smectite is the main authigenic clay in nearshore deposits. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 30]])
- Mixed siliciclastic-carbonate deposits have average porosity of 7% and permeability of 109 mD, while pure carbonate deposits have average porosity of 15% and permeability of 46 mD. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 38]])
- Siliciclastic-rich facies (average permeability 1884 mD) are significantly more productive than mixed siliciclastic-carbonate or carbonate beds due to higher porosity and better sorting. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 38]])
- The transition from Al Wajh to Musayr formations shows a basinward shift from continental alluvial fans/braided streams to an open marine carbonate ramp. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 29]])
- Eleven lithofacies (F1-F11) and four facies associations (FA1, FA2, FA3, FA4) were identified in the Al Wajh Formation. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 64]])
- The study identifies a large alluvial-braided fluvial fan environment where northern subbasins (Sukk and Ifal) represent proximal settings and southern subbasins (Maqna-1, Maqna-2) represent medial and distal settings. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 64]])
- FA1 (alluvial fan) is present in all subbasins except Maqna-2; it shows a high relative proportion (36-100%) in northern subbasins. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 64]])
- FA4 (shoreface/delta front) has a lower relative proportion (9-13%) and is primarily distributed in the northern subbas1s. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 64]])
- Proportion of FA2 (braided fluvial fan) and FA3 (floodplain) increases toward southern subbasins (50-82% for FA2, 18% for FA3). ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 64]])
- Conglomeratic channel deposits in proximal settings have high thickness but may have marginal reservoir quality due to poor sorting and high matrix content. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 65]])
- Sandstone beds in medial settings (m scale) represent excellent reservoirs with significant thickness, better sorting, and higher spatial connectivity (10s of m). ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 65]])

## Limitations

The study notes that the high degree of faulting in the area makes bed-by-bed correlation over long distances challenging. Additionally, only a subset of samples (n=35) was used for thin sections due to the friable nature of the sandstones.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The results of this study can be used for modeling reservoir quality in the subsurface equivalent of the formation in the Red Sea or elsewhere, thereby improving reservoir quality prediction.
