# Analysis of Syn- and Post-Depositional Controls on Facies Distribution, Depositional Architecture and Reservoir Quality Modifications of Early Syn-rift Continental Deposits (Early Oligocene — Early Miocene), NW Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the depositional architecture and reservoir quality of a mixed siliciclastic-carbonate system at the transition between the Al Wajh Formation and Musayr Formation in the Midyan Basin, Red Sea region. It identifies three distinct facies associations (siliciclastic-rich, mixed, and carbonate-rich) and concludes that siliciclastic-rich units provide significantly higher reservoir quality compared to mixed or pure carbonate units.

---

## Research Problem

The understanding of facies distribution, stratal architecture, and reservoir quality in early syn-rift continental deposits (specifically the transition from non-marine siliciclastics to marine carbonates) is poorly understood due to their rarity. The study specifically addresses these issues in the Al Wajh Formation and Musayr Formation contact in the Midyan Basin.

## Methods

Petrographic analysis was performed on 88 thin sections using an Olympus BX53F microscope with Petrog software for point-counting (300 counts per section) and textural analysis. XRD analysis was conducted using a Malvern PANalytical Empyrean Cu LFF HR diffractometer to complement petrography and SEM-EDS data, utilizing the Rietveld method for mineral quantification. QEMSCAN was used to determine mineralogical composition and pore space morphology of Al Wajh sandstones. In-situ outcrop permeability measurements (n=71) were conducted using a TinyPerm handheld permeameter. Machine learning (Intellesis package) was applied to segment grain and pore phases in thin sections for porosity estimation.

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes data from the Midyan Basin in northwestern Saudi Arabia, specifically focusing on the Al Wajh Formation. It includes analysis of ten outcrops across four fault-controlled subbasins (Sukk, Ifal, Maqna-1, and Maqna-2) and identifies eleven lithofacies grouped into four facies associations.

## Key Findings

- The Al Wajh Formation contains four primary facies associations: FA1 (alluvial fan), FA2 (braided fluvial fan), FA3 (floodplain), and FA4 (shoreface/delta front). ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 74]])
- Lithofacies-based predicted mean permeability curves show that sandstone bodies have medium to high permeabilities, while conglomerate deposits have low to medium range permeabilities. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 79]])
- The study identified several lithofacies (F1-F11) including clast-supported conglomerates, graded sandstones, and mudstones. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 49]])
- Sedimentary logs show that the proportion of sandstone and mudstone increases southward while conglomerate size decreases in the Al Wajh Formation. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 74]])
- Diagenetic processes including compaction, dissolution, and authigenic mineral growth (e.g., smectite) were identified as key factors affecting reservoir quality. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 138]])
- Identified 11 lithofacies including clast-supported conglomerates (F1), matrix-supported conglomerates (F2), and various sandstone types (F6-F9). ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 28]])
- Identified four facies associations: alluvial fan (FA1), braided fluvial fan (FA2), floodplain (FA3), and shoreface/delta front (FA4). ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 29]])
- Braided fluvial fan environment sandstones (m scale) showed average porosity of 23.2% and permeability of 1884 mD. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 29]])
- Clay matrix content (10 to 20%) significantly reduced initial porosity and permeability to less than 10% and 10 mD, respectively. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 29]])
- Secondary porosity of up to 13.5% was formed primarily due to feldspar dissolution. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 30]])
- Kaolinite was the primary authigenic clay in continental sandstones, while smectite was the main authigenic clay in shallow marine sandstones. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 30]])
- Mixed siliciclastic-carbonate deposits showed average porosity of 7% and permeability of 109 mD; carbonate deposits showed average porosity of 15% and permeability of 46 mD. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 32]])
- Detrital smectite as grain-coating clay has the potential of preventing quartz cementation during deeper burial. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 30]])
- The Al Wajh Formation consists of basal conglomerates, predominantly well to moderately sorted sandstone in the middle, and mudstone in the upper part of the sequence. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 35]])
- Four distinct facies associations were identified: alluvial fan (FA1), braided fluvial fan (FA2), floodplain (FA3), and shoreface/delta front (FA4). ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 36]])

## Limitations

The text notes that it can be difficult to distinguish between siliciclastics, carbonates, and mixed strata seismically or acoustically unless significant post-depositional enhancement occurs, highlighting the need for field-based sedimentological analysis.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The study suggests that well spacing of 100 to 150 meters is likely sufficient to encounter the same reservoir flow unit in medial settings, while larger distances (e.g., 1 km) may hit different sand bodies.
