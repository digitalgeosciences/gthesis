# Utilizing Field Data to Understand the Effect of Drilling Parameters and Mud Rheology on Rate of Penetration in Carbonate Formations

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the relationship between drilling parameters (WOB, RPM, Torque, SPP) and mud rheology properties to predict and optimize the Rate of Penetration (ROP). By analyzing field data from three offshore wells, the study establishes a correlation model to quantify how these factors influence drilling efficiency.

---

## Research Problem

Predicting ROP is challenging due to the complexity of drilling processes; specifically, there is a lack of models that integrate both mechanical drilling parameters and mud rheology using large sets of real-world field data in carbonate formations.

## Objectives

- Understand the effect of drilling and mud parameters on rate of penetration from real data point of view
- Include mud properties effects which are rarely present in the literature
- Quantify the magnitude of the relationship between rate of penetration and drilling parameters, mud properties
- Develop a rate of penetration model that incorporates both drilling parameters and mud properties to predict future ROP values.
- Collect and screen field data for drilling parameters and mud properties from three offshore wells.
- Examine the correlation between individual drilling parameters and ROP using coefficient of determination (R2).

## Methods

The study utilizes field data from three offshore wells in an intermediate 16-inch section. Data is filtered to remove non-drilling activities (tripping, casing). Statistical analysis using the Least Square Method and Non-Linear Regression (via XLSTAT) is applied to determine correlation coefficients (R) and coefficients of determination (R2). The study evaluates parameters including WOB, RPM, Torque, SPP, GPM, Mud Density, Viscosity, Yield Point, and Solid Content.

## Data and Materials

Data from 3 offshore wells in a carbonate-rich section (5,000 to 9,000 ft depth). Total data points: ~7,800; total records exceeding 40,000. Mud properties were collected twice daily for the duration of the drilling operations.

## Key Findings

- The model developed in this research captures the effect of both drilling parameters and mud properties, showing a strong R2 value averaging around 0.93 for the three wells. ([[2017-al-abduljabbar|Al-AbdulJabbar, 2017, p. 12]])
- RPM shows a linear relationship with ROP in several models (Maurer, Bingham, Teale, Pessier & Fear) and non-linear/exponential relationships in others (Galle & Woods, Warren). ([[2017-al-abduljabbar|Al-AbdulJabbar, 2017, p. 20]])
- WOB is reported to have a quadratic relationship with ROP in some models (Maurer, Warren) and a power-law relationship in others (Bingham, Bourgoyne & Young). ([[2017-al-abduljabbar|Al-AbdulJabbar, 2017, p. 20]])
- Eckel's research showed that increasing viscosity tends to decrease the ROP until a certain point (40 cp), where no further effect is observed. ([[2017-al-abduljabbar|Al-AbdulJabbar, 2017, p. 25]])
- Beck confirmed an increase in ROP with increasing Reynolds number values, while a drop in ROP was seen with an increase in plastic viscosity and Methylene Blue Test (MBT) values. ([[2017-al-abduljabbar|Al-AbdulJabbar, 2017, p. 26]])
- Paiaman's study showed that ROP decreases with increased mud weight, plastic viscosity, and solid content, following a linear relationship. ([[2017-al-abduljabbar|Al-AbdulJabbar, 2017, p. 28]])
- Alum's model indicated that while plastic viscosity and gel strength show a decrease in ROP, their net effect is not as significant as previously thought; only annular pressure loss showed a strong relationship with ROP. ([[2017-al-abduljabbar|Al-AbdulJabbar, 2017, p. 29]])
- A regression analysis for mud weight showed an R2 of 0.7315 with the equation y = -14.784x + 158.96, indicating a strong correlation between mud weight and ROP. ([[2017-al-abduljabbar|Al-AbdulJabbar, 2017, p. 28]])
- A regression analysis for plastic viscosity showed an R2 of 0.6087 with the equation y = -4.3912x + 36.562, showing a negative correlation between viscosity and ROP. ([[2017-al-abduljabbar|Al-AbdulJabbar, 2017, p. 28]])
- A regression analysis for solid content showed an R2 of 0.6181 with the equation y = -2.0454x + 33.767. ([[2017-al-abduljabbar|Al-AbdulJabbar, 2017, p. 29]])
- Weight on Bit (WOB) showed a strong linear relationship with ROP across three wells, with an average correlation coefficient (R) of 85% and an average R2 of 72%. ([[2017-al-abduljabbar|Al-AbdulJabbar, 2017, p. 53]])
- A threshold for WOB was identified in Well-A at 13 Klbf; ROP did not respond significantly until this value was exceeded. ([[2017-al-abduljabbar|Al-AbdulJabbar, 2017, p. 53]])
- String Rotation Speed (RPM) showed a strong linear relationship with ROP, with an average correlation coefficient (R) of 89% and an average R2 of 80%. ([[2017-al-abduljabbar|Al-AbdulJabbar, 2017, p. 55]])
- Torque (T) demonstrated a strong relationship with ROP (average R = 85%, R2 = 72%), indicating its role in gauging mud property efficiency. ([[2017-al-abduljabbar|Al-AbdulJabbar, 2017, p. 58]])
- Well-C exhibited higher SPP values even at lower ROPs, suggesting specific hydraulic optimizations were made to achieve high penetration rates. ([[2017-al-abduljabbar|Al-AbdulJabbar, 2017, p. 60]])

## Limitations

Mud properties are not measured in real-time but are reported on daily logs; the study notes that a lack of instantaneous data limits the ability to observe immediate impacts of mud property changes on ROP.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The developed model can be used in the future to predict rate of penetration for different wells to enable optimization of drilling parameters.
