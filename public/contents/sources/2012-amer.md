# Effect Of Nano Silica on Portland Saudi Cement Type 'G' In High pressure High Temperature Applications

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the impact of varying concentrations of Nano silica (0%, 1.0%, and 2.0%) on the physical and mechanical properties of Portland Saudi cement type 'G' under high pressure and high temperature conditions. The study evaluates parameters such as thickening time, fluid loss, free water content, rheology, and compressive strength to determine the optimal slurry design for deep-well cementing operations.

---

## Research Problem

The research addresses the challenge of maintaining cement integrity and pumpability in deep well environments (high pressure/temperature) while incorporating nanotechnology to improve performance. Specifically, it investigates how different concentrations of Nano silica affect the rheological properties and compressive strength of a specific local cement type ('G').

## Objectives

- Determine the impact of varying concentrations (0%, 1.0%, 3.0%, and 5.0%) of nano-silica on the performance of Portland Saudi Cement Type 'G' in high pressure/high temperature conditions.
- Identify the applicable percentage range of Nano silica for cement mixture design.
- Evaluate the effect of Nano silica on cement slurry properties including thickening time, fluid loss, free water separation, rheological properties, compressive strength, density, and particles settling.
- Determine the applicability of mixing Nano silica with other products like silica flour and silica sand.
- Evaluate the impact of Nano silica on the behavior of other cement additives such as retarders and dispersants.
- Compare the results of nano-silica modified cement systems against a base case (standard Class G cement with silica flour).

## Methods

The study utilized three cement slurry designs with Nano silica concentrations of 0%, 1.0%, and 2.0%. Slurries were prepared by mixing Portland Saudi cement type 'G' with varying amounts of silica flour, silica sand, and Nano silica. Physical properties were tested including: (1) Thickening time using a specialized monitoring system to track consistency (Bc) over time; (2) Fluid loss tests conducted at 1000 psi and 194 °F for 30 minutes; (3) Free water tests after 2 hours of aging; (4) Rheology measurements to determine plastic viscosity (PV) and yield point (YP); and (5) Compressive strength testing via conventional crushing (6 samples per system) and ultrasonic cement analysis (UCA) at 290 °F and 4666 psi for 24 hours.

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes Portland Saudi Cement Type 'G', silica flour, expanding agents, dispersants, fluid loss control agents, retarders, and defoamers. The experimental design includes four concentrations of nano-silica (0%, 1.0%, 3.0%, and 5.0%) by cement weight. Testing conditions include a bottom hole circulating temperature (BHCT) of 228 °F, a bottom hole static temperature (BHST) of 290 °F, and pressures up to 4666 psi.

## Key Findings

- The addition of Nano silica was found to affect most of the addressed cement slurry properties, including thickening time, fluid loss, and rheology. ([[2012-amer|Amer, 2012, p. 14]])
- Research showed that additions of only 1.0% and 2.0% Nano silica were feasible for use in the cement mixture. ([[2012-amer|Amer, 2012, p. 14]])
- Nano silica is more effective in enhancing cement compressive strength than ordinary silica fume. ([[2012-amer|Amer, 2012, p. 36]])
- Adding Nano silica to a slurry with 2.5% by weight resulted in a yield stress increase of 66.5% and plastic viscosity increase of 3.6%. ([[2012-amer|Amer, 2012, p. 41]])
- Addition of Nano silica led to a reduction in thickness time from approximately 8 hours (at 0% concentration) to roughly 0.7 hours at high concentrations. ([[2012-amer|Amer, 2012, p. 43]])
- Adding Nano silica resulted in a decrease in porosity by 33.3% and permeability by 99% compared to standard formulations. ([[2012-amer|Amer, 2012, p. 44]])
- The addition of nano-silica was shown to reduce fluid loss in the cement slurry compared to the base case. ([[2012-amer|Amer, 2012, p. 45]])
- Nano-silica improved rheological properties, including an increase in compressive strength and a decrease in setting time. ([[2012-amer|Amer, 2012, p. 45]])
- The study evaluated four specific concentrations of nano-silice: 0%, 1.0%, 3.0%, and 5.0% by cement weight. ([[2012-amer|Amer, 2012, p. 49]])
- A base case slurry was formulated with Class G cement, 35% silica flour, 1.0% expanding agent, 0.4% dispersant, 0.2% fluid loss control agent 1, 0.5% fluid loss control agent 2, 1.0% retarder, and 0.25g defoamer. ([[2012-amer|Amer, 2012, p. 48]])
- The slurry was conditioned for 20 minutes at 194 °F before testing for fluid loss. ([[2012-amer|Amer, 2012, p. 58]])
- Compressive strength was measured using both a direct 'crushing' method and an indirect ultrasonic wave method (UCA). ([[2012-amer|Amer, 2012, p. 67]])
- The addition of Nano silica increased the thixotropy of the slurry; higher concentrations led to faster development of high gel states during static periods. ([[2012-amer|Amer, 2012, p. 90]])
- Cement slurry with 1.0% Nano silica was successfully tested, while the 3.0% concentration resulted in a slurry that was too thick (un-pumpable) even after adjusting dispersant and adding silica sand. ([[2012-amer|Amer, 2012, p. 91]])
- The 2.0% Nano silica system showed a significant reduction in thickening time, reaching 100 Bc in approximately 4 hours, compared to over 6 hours for the 0% and 1.0% systems. ([[2012-amer|Amer, 2012, p. 102]])

## Limitations

The results are limited to specific well requirements, cement types (Portland Saudi 'G'), and the presence of other materials like silica flour/sand which may mask the effects of Nano silica. Additionally, higher concentrations (>2%) were not tested due to mixing difficulties.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Test Nano silica alone without other silica products (flour/sand) to better isolate its benefits.
- Investigate applications in shallower environments with lower pressure and temperature.
- Use SEM to analyze particle distribution and bonding before and after adding Nano silica.
- Utilize XRF and XRD to determine the mineralogy and composition of the cement systems.
