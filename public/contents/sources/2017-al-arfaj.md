# EXPERIMENTAL AND THEORETICAL INVESTIGATION OF SHALE-FLUID INTERACTIONS FOR DRILLING APPLICATIONS

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the interaction between shale formations and drilling fluids, specifically focusing on the Qusaiba formation in Saudi Arabia. It employs a combination of experimental techniques (XRD, XRF, QEMSCAN, NMR) and molecular dynamics simulations to characterize clay mineralogy and evaluate the effectiveness of various inhibitors against swelling and dispersion.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of understanding regarding the interaction between specific shale minerals (like smectites and illite-smectites) and drilling fluids leads to challenges in maintaining wellbore stability. The study addresses this by characterizing Qalibah formation samples and modeling clay mineral behavior at the molecular level.

## Objectives

- Characterization of four shale samples in terms mineralogy, petrography and reactivity
- Experimental Investigation of shale-fluid interactions: investigation of shale inhibition additives to inhibit swelling and dispersion for each type of shales
- Studying the fluid imbibition into shale pore system using nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR)
- Understanding shale-fluid interactions using molecular simulation techniques
- Characterize shale samples from the Qalibah formation using micro-CT, SEM, XRD, XRF, and QEMSCAN.
- Evaluate the performance of different drilling fluids (water-based and oil-based) on shale stability through swelling, dispersion, and durability tests.

## Methods

The study follows a three-phase approach: 1. Characterization of four Qalibah formation samples (A, B, C from downhole; D from outcrop) using micro-CT for structural imaging, SEM for morphology, XRD/XRF/QEMSCAN for mineralogy and chemistry, and TGA for organic content. 2. Physical testing including swelling tests (linear swell meter), dispersion tests (hot-roll cell), and durability tests to assess drilling fluid performance. 3. Molecular dynamics simulations using the LAMMPS software and ClayFF_dioctahedral forcefield to model montmorillonite and rectorite structures, analyzing hydration energy, interlayer spacing, and temperature sensitivity.

## Data and Materials

Four shale samples from the Qalibah formation (A: silica-rich; B: clay-rich; C: mixed; D: outcrop). Three pure clay minerals (sodium montmorillonite, illite, illite-smectite) and six saturated salt solutions for adsorption isotherms.

## Key Findings

- The main clay types identified in downhole samples are illite and illite-smectite mixed layers. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 14]])
- The study confirmed that illite does not have a strong tendency to swell, while illite-smectite showed high levels of swelling when tested experimentally. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 170]])
- Molecular dynamics simulation results for total energy showed agreement with first principle calculations. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 14]])
- The study identified that the Qusaiba formation contains five cartographic units, with the first four being characterized by cycles of transgression and regression. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 20]])
- Wellbore instability problems (swelling, sloughing) are estimated to cause a loss of 10% to 20% of total well drilling costs. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 17]])
- The study confirmed that polyamine is a better shale stabilizer than amine due to its ability to inhibit both internal and external surfaces of reactive shales. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 48]])
- Sample A is characterized as a silica-rich sample with low organic matter content and a matrix of grains with some infilled minerals. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 60]])
- Sample B shows lamination with heavy mineral patches, high clay content, and small amounts of organic matter. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 60]])
- The study identifies that kaolinite is the most dispersive clay followed by illite, while smectite showed low tendency to disperse. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 53]])
- Sodium montmorillonite shows higher swelling and larger interlayer spacing compared to potassium montmorillonite due to its open structure. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 61]])
- Rectorite (illite-smectite) exhibits unique behavior as an intermediate between illite and smectite, showing significant swelling upon hydration. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 66]])
- The inclusion of polyamines in drilling fluids was shown to outperform pure or modified KCl systems in both dispersion and bulk hardness tests. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 53]])
- Sample B was identified as a silica-rich argillaceous mudstone with 65.6 wt% total clay content and high chlorite (31.2%) and illite (17.2%) concentrations. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 108]])
- Sample D, a clay-rich mudstone, showed the highest organic matter content with a TGA weight loss of 18% across three stages. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 124]])
- QEMSCAN analysis revealed that sample A and C had higher porosity (up to 63 µm) due to high quartz content, while B and D had lower porosity due to clay dominance. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 117]])

## Limitations

The study notes that the standard inhibition durability test (Amanullah, 2015) does not perfectly simulate downhole conditions because it involves a drying step; an 'enhanced' method was proposed to remove this step.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Conduct simulations of clays with specific inhibitors like KCl, polyamines, silicates, and glycol.
- Perform long-term linear swell meter tests to establish a water-to-clay ratio plot.
- Use humidity-regulated cells for XRD to better understand swelling.
- Investigate mechanical factors using both experimental and molecular dynamics methods.
- Utilize Micro-CT for quantifying fluid uptake in the presence/absence of inhibitors.
