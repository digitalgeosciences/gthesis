# REDUCING BARITE SAGGING BY USING COPOLYMER IN HIGH PRESSURE HIGH TEMPERATURE WELLS

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the use of a new copolymer in oil-based drilling fluids (OBM) to mitigate barite sagging in high-pressure, high-temperature (HPHT) wells. Through rheological analysis and viscoelastic measurements, it concludes that adding 1 lbm/bbl of the copolymer effectively maintains mud stability and reduces sag factors at temperatures up to 350°F.

---

## Research Problem

Barite sagging in oil-based drilling fluids (OBM) poses significant risks in high-pressure, high-temperature (HPHT) wells, where high temperatures reduce fluid viscosity and lead to the settling of weighting materials, potentially causing well control issues or stuck pipe.

## Objectives

- Study the effect of temperature on barite settling using a static aging cell.
- Perform vertical and deviated barite sag tests.
- Study the effect of adding copolymer on rheology properties of OBM under HPHT conditions.
- Design a new formulation of drilling fluids that could minimize the barite sag.

## Methods

The study utilized an invert emulsion oil-based mud (OBM) with a density of 14.7 ppg and an 80:20 oil-water ratio. Sag was evaluated using static aging cells at temperatures up to 350°F, viscometer sag shoe tests (VSST) at 100 rpm for 30 minutes, and flow loop simulations. Rheological properties (plastic viscosity, yield point, gel strength) were measured across a temperature range of 85°F to 300°F. Viscoelasticity was characterized using an Anton Paar 302 Rheometer via amplitude sweep and frequency sweep tests to determine storage modulus (G') and loss modulus (G''). Filtration performance was evaluated using an HPHT filter press at 250°F and 300 psi.

## Data and Materials

The study used a 14.7 ppg invert emulsion oil-based mud containing diesel, INVERMUL, Lime, Duratone, CaCl2, Geltone II, EZ-Mul, CaCO3, and barite. A new copolymer was added at a concentration of 1 lbm/bbl. Testing included samples aged for 24 hours in pressurized cells and filtration tests using 50-micron porous ceramic discs.

## Key Findings

- Adding 1 lbm/bbl of the new copolymer had no effect on drilling fluid density (14.7 ppg). ([[2018-basfar|BASFAR, 2018, p. 14]])
- The new copolymer increased gel strength by 20% compared to the OBM-base at 300°F. ([[2018-basfar|BASFAR, 2018, p. 54]])
- Adding 1 lbm/bbl of the copolymer prevented barite sagging at 350°F, with a sag factor of 0.504 in vertical cases and 0.51 in deviated cases. ([[2018-basfar|BASFAR, 2018, p. 50]])
- The storage modulus (G') increased by 40% after adding 1 lbm/bbl of the new copolymer, confirming improved sag resistance. ([[2018-basfar|BASFAR, 2018, p. 66]])
- The new copolymer enhanced electrical stability from 1470 volts to 1482 volts. ([[2018-basfar|BASFAR, 2018, p. 14]])
- Adding the copolymer reduced filtration volume by 20% (from 2.6 cm3/30min to 2.1 cm3/30min). ([[2018-basfar|BASFAR, 2018, p. 64]])
- The new formulation showed a wider transition zone between elastic and viscous behavior in amplitude sweep tests. ([[2018-basfar|BASFAR, 2018, p. 59]])
- Tan(delta) for the new formulation was significantly lower than the OBM-base, indicating higher elasticity. ([[2018-basfar|BASFAR, 2018, p. 62]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Study the effect of the copolymer on dynamic sag at high temperature by flow loop method.
- Study the effect of the copolymer on other viscoelastic properties such as dynamic time sweep and dynamic temperature sweep.
- Study the effect of adding ilmenite to barite as a weighting material in the OBM on enhancing fluid stability.
- Study the effect of copolymer on removal efficiency.
