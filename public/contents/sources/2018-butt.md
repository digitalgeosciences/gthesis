# SEDIMENTARY FACIES ANALYSIS, PETROGRAPHY AND DIAGENESIS OF THE EARLY MIOCENE NUTAYSH MEMBER OF THE BURQAN FORMATION IN MIDYAN BASIN, NW SAUDI ARABIA.

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the sedimentary facies, petrography, and diagenesis of the Early Miocene Nutaysh Member within the Burqan Formation in the Midyan Basin. Through detailed lithofacies analysis of five outcrop sections and modal analysis of 150 thin sections, it characterizes the depositional environment as a submarine fan system and evaluates the impact of diagenetic processes on reservoir quality.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the characterization of the Nutaysh member (Burqan Formation) in the Midyan Basin to understand how depositional environment and diagenetic processes (like carbonate cementation and smectite/chlorite coatings) influence porosity and permeability in a siliciclastic system.

## Objectives

- Identification and description of different lithofacies and related petrographic and diagenetic alternations of Burqan Formation in Midyan region from different parts of the basin.
- Interpretation of the diagenetic and depositional processes of the various lithofacies.
- Reconstruct the diagenetic history of the sediments of the Burqan Formation, to develop the paragenetic sequence showing the timing of various phases of diagenesis.
- Perform detailed lithofacies analysis on five well-exposed outcrops of the Nutaysh Member.
- Identify sedimentary facies associations and sub-lithofacies using the Pickeri scheme.
- Conduct petrographic and modal analysis to determine mineralogical composition, texture, and diagenetic features.

## Methods

The study utilized field investigation across five outcrop sections in the Midyan Basin. Lithofacies were identified using a descriptive scheme to categorize sedimentary environments. Petrographic analysis was conducted on 150 thin sections prepared from 200 rock samples (impregnated with blue epoxy resin). Modal analysis followed the Gazzi-Dickenson point counting method (300 points per section) to quantify mineralogy, cement types, and porosity. XRD analysis was used to identify authigenic minerals.

## Data and Materials

Analysis of five well-exposed outcrops in the northern, central, and southwestern margins of the Midyan basin; XRD analysis of clay separated from sandstone samples; SEM photomicrographs for grain coating identification; petrographic thin sections for mineralogy and cement quantification.

## Key Findings

- The Nutaysh member is characterized by a submarine fan system with variable paleocurrent directions, identified through four facies associations and thirteen sub-lithofacies. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 24]])
- Majority of the studied samples from the Nutaysh member fall into categories of subarkose, arkose, and lithic arkose; some are sublitharenite and feldspathic litharenite. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 24]])
- Petrographic characterization indicates a diverse provenance including igneous basement, metamorphic, and sedimentary rocks. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 24]])
- Pre-compaction calcite, Fe-dolomite, and dolomite cements constitute 5-20 percent of the whole rock volume in cemented samples. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 24]])
- Average visual porosity ranges from 8-22 percent. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 24]])
- Diagenetic clays are less than 1 percent by volume. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 24]])
- Section-3 contains a 125m thick sequence including coarse-grained siliciclastic facies and upper mixed siliciclastic/carbonate to carbonate facies. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 48]])
- The first 115 m of section-3 consists of conglomerate, sandstone, and two interbedded shale intervals arranged in two fining upward sequences. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 48]])
- Section-4 covers 650m of the Nutaysh Member, showing a prograding submarine fan system with three distinct coarsening and thickening upward sequences. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 62]])
- The second coarsening upward sequence in section-4 (180 m thick) contains 150m of classical turbidites in the upper part. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 63]])
- The Nutaysh Member is characterized by four facies associations and thirteen sub-lithofacies, including channelized sandstone units (C2.1) and graded stratified gravels (A2.4). ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 68]])
- Palaeocurrent measurements in the Nutaysh Member indicated sediment transport directions of N30E, N35E, and N34E toward a SE direction. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 65]])
- The average mineralogical composition of major framework grains in the Burqan Formation is Q71F21L8 (71% Quartz, 21% Feldspar, 8% Lithics). ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 149]])

## Limitations

The study notes that due to the structural complexity of the Midyan basin, it is nearly impossible to trace all architectural elements of individual submarine fan deposits.
