# SEDIMENTARY FACIES ANALYSIS, PETROGRAPHY AND DIAGENESIS OF THE EARLY MIOCENE NUTAYSH MEMBER OF THE BURQAN FORMATION IN MIDYAN BASIN, NW SAUDI ARABIA.

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the Nutaysh member of the Burqan Formation in the Midyan Basin, focusing on its sedimentary facies, petrographic characteristics, and diagenetic history. Through lithofacies analysis and mineralogical studies (XRD, SEM, EMP), it identifies various facies associations within a submarine fan system and characterizes the impact of early and late diagenesis on reservoir quality.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the need for a detailed characterization of the sedimentary facies, petrography, and diagenesis of the Nutaysh Member within the Burqan Formation in the Midyan Basin (Early Miocene) to understand its depositional environment and reservoir potential.

## Methods

The study involved reconnaissance fieldwork followed by detailed field mapping and description of five stratigraphic sections. Over 200 rock samples were collected for laboratory analysis. Petrographic studies included modal analysis (counting 300 points per thin section) to determine mineralogical content, texture, and porosity. Mineralogy of framework components, altered grains, and cements was analyzed using XRD, SEM, and electron microprobe (EMP). Lithofacies identification followed the Pickering et al. (1986) scheme.

## Data and Materials

The study analyzed five well-exposed outcrops of the Nutaysh member in the Midyan Basin (northern, central, and southwestern margins). Data included lithofacies classifications (13 sub-lithofacies), petrographic descriptions of grain types and cements, and XRD/SEM data for mineralogical characterization.

## Key Findings

- The Nutaysh member is characterized by a variety of lithofacies including sand-dominated lower units and shale-dominated upper units (Subayti member). ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 13]])
- Identified four facies associations and thirteen sub-lithofacies using the Pickeri identification scheme. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 2]])
- Sandstone samples are classified as compositionally arkosic, sub-arkosic, and lithic arkosic based on QFL plots. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 114]])
- Primary intergranular porosity is high in some friable sandstone units due to the absence of cement (only small patches of pyrite). ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 128]])
- Calcite cement is identified as a major cement occluding primary pore spaces in certain sections. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 130]])
- Secondary porosity was observed due to the partial dissolution of framework grains and cements. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 127]])
- The Nutaysh member is characterized as a submarine fan system with variable paleocurrent directions based on facies associations and architectural framework. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 24]])
- Most samples from the Nutaysh member are classified as subarkose, arkose, and lithic arkose; fewer are sublitharenite and just feldspathic litharenite. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 24]])
- Pre-compaction calcite, Fe-dolomite, and dolomite cements constitute 5-20 percent of the whole rock volume in cemented samples. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 24]])
- The average visual porosity ranges from 8-22 percent. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 24]])
- Diagenetic clays are less than 1 percent by volume. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 24]])
- Petrography showed that primary porosity was lost due to syndepositional euhedral to subhedral dolomite cement in some samples. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 139]])
- Secondary porosity was created by the partial dissolution of quartz grains and calcite cement. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 18]])
- Some samples showed high primary porosity with little bending in detrital biotite, indicating low to moderate compaction. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 136]])
- A variety of minerals including quartz (Qm), sericitized plagioclase feldspar (Pg), and chert fragments (Ch) were identified as framework components. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 138]])

## Limitations

The study notes that due to the structural complexity of the Midyan basin, it is nearly impossible to trace all architectural elements of individual submarine fan systems.
