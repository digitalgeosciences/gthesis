# CONTROLS ON THE SPATIAL VARIABILITY OF PORE SYSTEMS IN SHALLOW MARINE STRATA: THE CASE OF THE UPPER JUBAILA FORMATION OF THE ARAB-D RESERVOIR ANALOGUE, SAUDI ARABIA.

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the controls on pore system variability and reservoir quality in the Upper Jubaila Formation, an analogue for the Arab-D reservoir. By integrating petrographic analysis, geochemical data (XRF, XRD), and core analysis from two outcrops (Wadi Malham and Wadi Nisah), it identifies how dissolution and dolomitization primarily control high-quality pore systems compared to compaction-dominated zones.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the challenge of reservoir heterogeneity in carbonate rocks where diagenetic processes often overprint original depositional textures, making it difficult to predict reservoir quality based on lithology alone. Specifically, it examines how different diagenetic pathways (dissolution/dolomitization vs. compaction) impact pore systems and petrophysical properties in the Jubaila Formation.

## Objectives

- To characterize pore systems, rock quality variations, and geochemical signature in the upper Jubaila Formation based on the outcrop data in the Wadi Malham and Wadi Nisah
- To investigate dominant factors that control pore system variability (depositional or diagenesis or both) and its impact on reservoir quality
- Determine the primary diagenetic features (micritization, dissolution, dolomitization, compaction, fracturing, dedolomitization) affecting pore systems.
- Quantify porosity and permeability using routine core analysis (RCA).
- Evaluate the impact of mineralogy (calcite vs. dolomite) on reservoir quality through XRD and XRF analysis.
- Assess the influence of meteoric water and tectonic history on the isotopic signatures of the carbonate rocks.

## Methods

The study utilized thin-section petrography to identify three lithofacies (Stromatoporoid packstone, Dolomitic wackestone, and Dolomitic mudstone) and five pore types. Routine core analysis (RCA) was performed on 50 samples from both outcrops to measure porosity and permeability. X-ray Fluorescence (XRF) was used for elemental composition analysis of 58 samples, while X-ray Diffraction (XRD) determined mineral percentages. Stable isotope analysis ($\delta^{13}C$ and $\delta^{18}O$) was conducted on carbonate samples to identify diagenetic signatures. Factor analysis and Principal Component Analysis (PCA), followed by Hierarchical Cluster Analysis (HCPC), were used to correlate petrophysical properties with mineralogical and chemical data.

## Data and Materials

The study utilized 80 thin section samples (32 from Wadi Malham vertical, 18 from Wadi Nisah vertical, 30 from Wadi Malham lateral), 80 rock plugs for RCA (32 from Wadi Malham vertical, 18 from Wadi Nisah vertical, 30 from Wadi Malham lateral), and 58 samples each for XRF, XRD, and stable isotope analysis.

## Key Findings

- Three lithofacies were identified: stromatoporoid packstone (SP), dolomitic wackestone (DW), and dolomitic mudstone (DM). ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 18]])
- Seven diagenesis features were identified: dissolution, micritization, early dolomitization, late dolomitization, dedolomitization, burial/compaction, and fracturing. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 18]])
- Five pore types were identified: Intercrystal non-fabric preserving (BC-nfp), Intercrystal fabric preserving (BC-fp), Intraparticle (WP), Fracture (FR), and Microporosity. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 74]])
- RRT 3 (dominated by microporosity & fracture in all lithofacies at Wadi Nisah) showed low porosity (0.9–7.2%) and permeability (0.01–0.1 mD). ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 19]])
- Wadi Malham was dominated by dissolution and dolomitization, resulting in BC-nfp, BC-fp, and WP pore systems. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 18]])
- Wadi Nisah was more dominated by burial/compaction and fracturing, resulting in microporosity and fracture pore systems. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 18]])
- Stable isotope analysis showed Wadi Malham samples with δ13C from -0.44 to 2.416 ‰ VPDB and δ18O from -7.075 to -1.559 ‰ VPDB, while Wadi Nisah had δ13C from -0.83 to -1.72 ‰ VPDB and δ18O from -0.91 to -2.93 ‰ VPDB. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 19]])
- Wadi Malham showed a thickness of 12.5 meters, while Wadi Nisah reached 6.6 meters. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 54]])
- The Wadi Malham section showed a wide distribution of porosity (average 11.68%) and permeability (average 84.02 mD), while the Wadi Nisah section was consistently low (porosity average 3.11%, permeability 0.04 mD). ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 77]])
- RRT-1 (Intercrystal non-fabric preserving) is characterized by high porosity (4-26%) and permeability (0.1-1222 mD). ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 96]])
- RRT-2 (Intercrystal fabric preserving & Intraparticle) has moderate values of porosity (7.4–16%) and permeability (0.1–1.3 mD). ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 96]])
- RRT-3 (Microporosity dominated & Fracture) has low values of porosity (0.9–7.2%) and permeability (0.01–0.1 mD). ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 96]])
- The average mineral composition for non-fabric preserving (NFP) dolomite was 61.33% dolomite and 30.86% calcite. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 85]])
- PCA analysis showed that the first two dimensions accounted for 59.84% of the total variance, with cluster 2 (NFP) showing higher permeability (122.5 mD) compared to cluster 1 (FP). ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 89]])
- Wadi Malham samples showed a more comparable isotopic signature to Khurais and Ghawar fields, while Wadi Nisah was distinct due to high meteoric calcite-filled pore space. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 104]])
