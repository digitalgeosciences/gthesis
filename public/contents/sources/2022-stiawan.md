# CONTROLS ON THE SPATIAL VARIABILITY OF PORE SYSTEMS IN SHALLOW MARINE STRATA: THE CASE OF THE UPPER JUBAILA FORMATION OF THE ARAB-D RESERVOIR ANALOGUE, SAUDI ARABIA.

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the controls on pore system variability in the Upper Jubaila Formation (Arab-D reservoir analogue) by analyzing lithofacies, diagenetic features, and petrophysical properties. Using a combination of field observations at Wadi Malham and Wadi Nisah and laboratory analyses (XRD, XRF, stable isotopes), it identifies three distinct Reservoir Rock Types (RRTs) based on pore system distribution.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the challenge of understanding how diagenetic processes (specifically fabric-preserving vs. non-fabric-preserving dolomitization) and sedimentary textures influence the heterogeneity of pore systems in shallow marine carbonate strata, specifically within the upper Jubaila formation as an analogue for the Arab-D reservoir.

## Objectives

- Identify lithofacies and stratigraphic stacking patterns in the upper Jubaila formation.
- Characterize carbonate textures, diagenetic features, and pore types through petrographic analysis.
- Quantify mineral composition using X-ray Fluorescence (XRF) and X-ray Diffraction (XRD).
- Analyze stable isotopes ($Ϝ13C$ and $Ϝ18O$) to determine diagenetic environments.
- Evaluate the relationship between lithofacies, pore systems, and petrophysical properties (porosity/permeability).
- To characterize pore systems, rock quality variations, and geochemical signature in the upper Jubaila Formation based on outcrop data in Wadi Malham and Wadi Nisah.
- To investigate dominant factors that control pore system variability (depositional or diagenesis or both) and its impact on reservoir quality.
- Identify and characterize lithofacies within the Jubaila formation at Wadi Malham and Wadi Nisah.
- Determine the impact of diagenesis on pore systems and reservoir quality.
- Evaluate the relationship between different carbonate textures (packstone, wackestone, mudstone) and their respective petrophysical properties.
- Identify and characterize major diagenetic features (micritization, dissolution, dolomitization, burial/compaction, fracturing, dedolomitization) in the upper Jub1aila member.
- Quantify the impact of these diagenetic processes on pore types and petrophysical properties (porosity and permeability).
- Evaluate the influence of mineralogy and elemental composition on reservoir quality using XRD and XRF analysis.
- Determine the relationship between different dolomite types (fabric-preserving vs. non-fabric-preserving) and their respective petrophysical characteristics.
- Identify and characterize different pore systems within the Upper Jubaila Formation.
- Evaluate the impact of diagenesis on porosity and permeability values.
- Establish Reservoir Rock Types (RRT) based on integrated lithofacies, diagenesis, and petrophysical data.
- Compare the influence of tectonic and meteoric processes between Wadi Malham and Wadi Nisah outcrops.
- Identify and characterize the carbonate pore systems within the Upper Jubaila Formation.
- Evaluate the impact of diagenetic processes on the primary porosity and permeability.
- Assess the role of depositional textures in influencing reservoir quality.

## Methods

The study utilized thin-section petrography to identify pore types and diagenetic features across two sections (Wadi Malham and Wadi Nisah). Routine Core Analysis (RCA) was performed on 50 core plugs to measure porosity and permeability. X-ray Fluorescence (XRF) was used to determine the elemental composition of 58 samples, while X-ray Diffraction (XRD) provided mineralogical data for both fabric-preserving (FP) and non-fabric-preserving (NFP) dolomites. Stable carbon and oxygen isotope analysis were conducted on carbonate samples. Finally, Principal Component Analysis (PCA) was used to integrate XRD, XRF, stable isotopes, and petrophysical data to identify the primary drivers of reservoir quality.

## Data and Materials

Samples were collected from two outcrop locations: Wadi Malham (total thickness 12.5m) and Wadi Nisah (total thickness 6.6m). The study utilized 32 vertical hand samples from Wadi Malham, 18 from Wadi Nisah, and 30 lateral samples from a specific interval in Wadi Malham. A total of 50 thin sections were analyzed.

## Key Findings

- Three lithofacies were identified: (1) Stromatoporoid packstone (SP), (2) Dolomitic wackestone (DW), and (3) Dolomitic mudstone (DM). ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 33]])
- Dolomite crystal sizes in Wadi Malham ranged from 86 to 328 $\mu$m for NFP, while Wadi Nisah crystals were 53 to 222 $\mu$m. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 47]])
- Dedolomitization was identified as a significant diagenetic feature where dolomite is fully calcitized. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 50]])
- Three Reservoir Rock Types (RRT) were defined: RRT 1 (high quality, NFP-dominated), RRT 2 (moderate, FP and intraparticle dominated), and RRT 3 (poor, microporosity and fracture dominated). ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 77]])
- Fabric preserving dolomite (FP) was characterized by high Ca intensity in XRF and a higher percentage of calcite mineral. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 63]])
- Non-fabric preserving dolomite (NFP) showed fluctuating Ca intensity and a higher percentage of dolomite minerals. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 63]])
- The study identified three lithofacies: stromatoporoid packstone (SP), dolomitic wackestone (DW), and dolomitic mudstone (DM). ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 18]])
- Seven diagenesis features were identified: dissolution, micritization, early dolomitization, late dolomitization, dedolomitization, burial/compaction, and fracturing. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 18]])
- Five pore types were identified: intercrystal non-fabric preserved (BC-nfp), intercrystal fabric preserved (BC-fp), intraparticle (WP), fracture, and microporosity. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 18]])
- RRT 1 (intercrystal non-fabric preserved) is associated with dolomitic mudstone facies, showing high porosity (4-26%) and high permeability (0.1-1222 mD). ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 18]])
- RRT 2 (intercrystal fabric preserved and intraparticle) is associated with stromatoporoid packstone and dolomitic wackestone facies, showing moderate porosity (7.4-16%) and permeability (0.1-1.3 mD). ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 19]])
- RRT 3 (microporosity & fracture) is dominated in all lithofacies in the Wadi Nisah locality, with low porosity (0.9-7.2%) and permeability (0.01-0.1 mD). ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 19]])
- Stable isotope analysis showed distinct clusters: Wadi Malham had δ13C from -0.44 to 2.416 ‰ and δ18O from -7.075 to -1.559 ‰; Wadi Nisah had δ13C from -0.83 to -1.72 ‰ and δ18O from -0.91 to -2.93 ‰. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 19]])
- Wadi Malham is dominated by dissolution and dolomitization, resulting in BC-nfp, BC-fp, and WP pore systems. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 18]])
- Wadi Nisah is dominated by burial/compaction and fracturing, resulting in microporosity and fracture pore systems. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 18]])
