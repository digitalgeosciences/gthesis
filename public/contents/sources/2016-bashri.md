# High Resolution Facies Analysis and Sequence Stratigraphy of Mixed Clastic-Carbonate Deposits of Miocene Dam Formation Outcrops, Al Lidam Area, Eastern Province of Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the Miocene Dam Formation in the Al Lidam area of Saudi Arabia, focusing on its mixed siliciclastic-carbonate deposits. Through high-resolution facies analysis and sequence stratigraphy, it identifies 15 lithofacies and interprets a complex carbonate platform environment transitioning from deep to shallow marine settings.

---

## Research Problem

The complex tectonics during the Miocene caused significant heterogeneity in the stratigraphy of the Dam Formation; therefore, detailed facies analysis and sequence stratigraphy are needed to improve reservoir property prediction for these mixed clastic-carbonate deposits.

## Objectives

- To identify the different lithofacies of unstudied outcrops of the Dam Formation in its type locality, and investigate their vertical and lateral variations.
- To interpret the paleodepositional environments of the lithofacies in studied sections; including the main dominant processes and current strengths.
- Establish a sequence stratigraphic model of the Dam Formation in Al-Lidam area to understand the big picture of facies distribution in the study area for future higher-resolution interwell and field scale studies.
- Develop strategic plan for outcrops sampling in the study area based on the sequence stratigraphic framework.
- Identify and describe lithofacies based on lithology, sedimentary structures, fossil content, and paleocurrent patterns.
- Interpret depositional environments for each identified lithofacies.

## Methods

The study utilized field mapping of outcrops 1, 2, 8, 16, 19, and 23. Analytical techniques included thin-section petrography to identify mineralogy and grain types, SEM imaging for micro-scale features (e.g., globigerina and miliolids), XRD analysis to determine the mineralogical composition of mud layers (specifically identifying dolomite in evaporite-associated zones), and paleocurrent analysis using current lineations and cross-bedding orientations.

## Data and Materials

Data includes 6 outcrops in the Al-Lidam area; 4 were used for correlation and 2 for facies analysis. Samples were collected based on bed thickness (thin beds <40cm, thick beds >70cm). XRD was performed on mudstone samples to determine mineralogy.

## Key Findings

- Fifteen lithofacies were identified across the studied traverse, including various carbonate and siliciclastic types such as 'Channelized Medium Sandstone' and 'Interbedded Mudstone and Evaporites'. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 14]])
- The sedimentary facies are stacked in shallowing upward parasequences forming three 4th order sequences. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 15]])
- Two sequence boundaries characterized by subaerial exposure were identified within the formation. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 15]])
- The 'Interbedded Mudstone and Evaporites' facies (ME) showed a dolomite composition of approximately 97.8% based on XRD analysis. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 48]])
- The 'Interbedded Cross-bedded Sandstone and Mudstone' facies (SM) was interpreted as a shallow intertidal setting with evidence of both tidal and subaerial exposure. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 54]])
- The 'Channelized Medium Sandstone' facies (Sc) showed an average Ichnofabric Index of 4-5, suggesting brackish water conditions. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 64]])
- The 'Trough Cross-Bedded Sandstone' facies (St) was interpreted as upper shoreface deposits due to high energy and limited fossil preservation. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 65]])
- The 'Intra-formational Limestone Conglomerate' (Gmm) showed varying interpretations from supratidal/intertidal to distal tempestites depending on the specific outcrop location. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 73]])
- Fifteen lithofacies were identified, including stromatolites (BS), massive quartz skeletal peloidal wacke-packstone (PWm), and various cross-bedded grainstones. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 75]])
- The Dam Formation is characterized by a carbonate dominance (>80%) over siliciclastics, which are primarily restricted to intertidal/supratidal zones in the western part of the study area. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 120]])
- XRD analysis of mud layers interbedded with evaporites showed a dominance of dolomite (with minor quartz and calcite), indicating high water evaporation and Mg-rich fluids. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 123]])
- Paleocurrent analysis revealed a general eastward trend with a major SE direction, consistent with the E-W basin orientation. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 121]])
- Stromatolites were identified as significant environmental indicators, appearing in both upper shoreface and supratidal environments. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 117]])
- The study identified three 4th order sequences within the Dam Formation, with a clear transition from LST to TST and HST based on accommodation space changes. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 132]])
- The massive quartz skeletal peloidal wacke-packstone (PWm) is the thickest and most frequent facies, interpreted as a lower shoreface environment. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 119]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Expand the study area to cover a longer E-W traverse of the Dam Formation.
- Control the age of sediments using biostratigraphy or other chronostratigraphic techniques.
- Apply benthic foraminifera and bivalves biostratigraphy to create distribution models.
- Utilize elemental and isotope chemostratigraphy to delineate sequences due to high geochemical variation.
