# High Resolution Facies Analysis and Sequence Stratigraphy of Mixed Clastic-Carbonate Deposits of Miocene Dam Formation Outcrops, Al Lidam Area, Eastern Province of Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the high-resolution facies analysis and sequence stratigraphy of mixed clastic-carbonate deposits in the Miocene Dam Formation at Al Lidam, Eastern Province, Saudi Arabia. It identifies various carbonate lithofacies (e.g., oolitic grainstones, skeletal packstones) to reconstruct a complex ancient shoreline environment influenced by tidal currents and waves.

---

## Research Problem

The complex nature of mixed siliciclastic-carbonate deposits in the Dam Formation leads to significant lateral and vertical variability, making lithology alone an insufficient tool for correlation between different areas. A detailed depositional model is needed to improve prediction of reservoir properties and facilitate better sampling strategies for future studies.

## Objectives

- Investigate the Dam Formation stratigraphy through a detailed traverse of six outcrops
- Describe and interpret lithofacies based on lithology, sedimentary structures, fossils, and paleocurrents
- Identify and characterize sequence stratigraphic units within the formation
- Determine depositional environments for the identified lithofacies
- To identify the different lithofacies of unstudied outcrops of the Dam Formation in its type locality, and investigate their vertical and lateral variations.
- To interpret the paleodepositional environments of the lithofacies in studied sections; including the main dominant processes and current strengths.
- Establish a sequence stratigraphic model of the Dam Formation in Al-Lidam area to understand the big picture of facies distribution in the study area for future higher-resolution interwell and field scale studies.
- Develop strategic plan for outcrops sampling in the study area based on the sequence stratigraphic framework.
- Perform high-resolution facies analysis of the Dam Formation outcrops in the Al Lidam area.
- Identify and describe distinct carbonate and siliciclastic lithofacies.
- Interpret depositional environments for each identified lithofacies.
- Analyze the sequence stratigraphy of the mixed clastic-carbonate deposits.
- Perform high-resolution facies analysis of the Dam Formation outcrops.
- Identify and characterize distinct carbonate lithofacies based on sedimentary structures and petrography.
- Analyze paleocurrents to determine the paleo-hydrodynamic regime.
- Conduct sequence stratigraphic interpretation of the sedimentary successions.
- Identify and characterize lithofacies based on sedimentology, fossils, and structures.
- Determine depositional environments for the identified lithofacies.
- Establish a sequence stratigraphic framework for the mixed siliciclastic-carbonate deposits.
- Evaluate lithologic heterogeneity in the Al-Lidam area.

## Methods

The methodology involves detailed field sedimentology, where six outcrops were logged (four for correlation, two for description). Sedimentological logs include composition, textures, sedimentary structures, bed thicknesses, paleocurrent measurements, and fossil content. A photomosaic technique was used to create high-resolution images of the outcrops for mapping features. Laboratory work included slabbing samples, thin-section petrography using a polarizing microscope (including Alizarin Red staining), XRD, and SEM analysis to characterize mineralogy and textures.

## Data and Materials

Field data from multiple outcrops in the Al Lidam area; XRD analysis of mudstone, sandstone, and wackestone-packstone samples; thin sections of trough cross-bedded sandstones and massive quartz skeletal peloidal wacke-packstones; SEM images of planktonic and benthic foraminifera.

## Key Findings

- Fifteen distinct lithofacies were identified, including Channelized Medium Sandstone (Sc), Interbedded Mudstone and Evaporites (ME), and various carbonate facies such as Stromatolites (BS) and Skeletal Oolitic Grainstones. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 14]])
- The lithofacies were interpreted as a range of environments including subtidal shoreface, intertidal, foreshore, supratidal, and estuarine deposits. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 15]])
- Facies are stacked in shallowing upward parasequences forming three 4th order sequences. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 15]])
- Two sequence boundaries characterized by subaerial exposure were identified within the formation. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 15]])
- The Lowstand Systems Tract (LST) is represented by sabkha and intertidal siliciclastics. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 15]])
- The Transgressive Systems Tract (TST) includes estuarine sandstone, tidal carbonate channels, and quartz skeletal peloidal wackestone. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 15]])
- Highstand Systems Tract (HST) is represented by shallowing upward wackestone to grainstone cycles. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 15]])
- XRD analysis confirmed dolomite as the main mineral content in mudstones when interbedded with evaporites. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 12]])
- The Dam Formation is characterized by a high degree of variability and substitution between coarse and fine sediments of both carbonate and siliciclastic origins. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 21]])
- Al-Lidam area contains over 40 outcrops of the Dam Formation exposed as isolated mesas and benches, extending approximately 20 km. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 19]])
- The formation is correlatable to Miocene reservoirs in both Iran (Lower Fars/Fatha) and Iraq. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 33]])
- Stromatolites were found at the top of three deepening upward cycles, with a thickness of 0.5 m to 0.8 m. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 19]])
- The Al-Kharrara Member is composed of limestone, marl and claystone; the Al-Nakhash Member is composed of limestones associated with evaporites and microbial heads (stromatolites). ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 20]])
- Dill et al. (2005) identified seven members in the Dam Formation, including Salwa, Al Nakhsh, and Abu Samrah. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 22]])
- The study identified 15 carbonate and siliciclastic lithofacies within the Dam Formation. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 47]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Expand the study area to cover a longer E-W traverse of the Dam Formation.
- Control the age of sediments using biostratigraphy or other chronostratigraphic techniques.
- Apply benthic foraminifera and bivalves biostratigraphy to create Burdigalian species distribution models.
- Apply elemental and isotope chemostratigraphy to delineate sequences due to high geochemical variation between facies.
- Correlate findings of facies and stratal stacking patterns with subsurface data.
