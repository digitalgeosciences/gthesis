# Acoustic and resistivity studies of the Khobar Member, the Dammam Formation, Eastern Province, Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the lithology and petrophysical properties of the Khobar aquifer in the Dammam area, Eastern Province, Saudi Arabia. It utilizes a combination of laboratory measurements (XRD, thin sections, ultrasonic velocity) and field geophysical methods (seismic refraction, vertical electrical sounding) to establish relationships between acoustic/resistivity parameters and porosity.

---

## Research Problem

The Khobar aquifer is an important but complex reservoir in the D1ammam Formation; understanding its petrophysical properties (porosity, permeability) and their relationship with physical properties like acoustic velocity and electrical resistivity is necessary for characterizing its potential as a water source.

## Objectives

- To study the acoustic and electric parameters of the Khobar aquifer
- To interrelate these parameters with porosity
- Determine the lithology of the Khobar aquifer using XRD and thin section analysis.
- Measure porosity and permeability of core samples from the Khobar member.
- Establish relationships between porosity, permeability, and acoustic velocity for different saturation types (fresh, brackish, saline).
- Evaluate the impact of groundwater salinity on resistivity and its correlation with porosity.

## Methods

The research involved laboratory analysis of core samples from 12 outcrops. Rock identification was performed via thin-section petrography and X-ray diffractometry (XRD). Acoustic properties were measured using an ultrasonic tester (Model C368) on cores saturated with water of varying Total Dissolved Solids (TDS). Electrical resistivity was determined in the lab using a specialized test unit to calculate formation factor and porosity. Field studies included seismic refraction surveys using the ES-1225 seismograph and Vertical Electrical Sounding (VES) using the Schlumberger array and Syscal-R2 meter. Data interpretation for VES used the Resix-IP software.

## Data and Materials

Core samples from the Khobar Member; 8 distinct core locations/types analyzed via XRD and thin sections; 5 field resistivity sites (VES-1 to VES-5); 6 seismic refraction profiles (SP1000, SP2000, SP3000, SP4000, SP5000, SP6000).

## Key Findings

- The Khobar aquifer is characterized by a wide variation in transmissivity, with values ranging from 7.06 x 10^2 to 2.9 x 10^3 m/s. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 30]])
- The Khobar aquifer has a porosity of more than 3% and a transmissivity range between 10^7 and 3 x 10^8 m/sec. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 30]])
- Fossil water in the Khobar aquifer is more than 30,000 years old. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 30]])
- The Khobar Member consists of two units: an upper unit of limestone and a lower unit of dolomitic marl with limestone intercalations. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 24]])
- Core samples saturated with saline water showed velocities ranging from approximately 1,400 to 3,900 m/s (Table 2.3). ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 35]])
- Core samples saturated with brackish water yielded a variety of velocities, including values such as 3,101.8 m/s and 2,373.23 m/s (Table 2.4). ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 36]])
- Dry core samples exhibited a range of ultrasonic velocities between 1,800 and 2,750 m/s. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 38]])
- The Khobar aquifer's total dissolved solid (TDS) content increases along the regional groundwater flow toward the northeast. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 30]])
- XRD and thin section analysis confirmed the Khobar Aquifer consists primarily of dolomite and limestone with minor clay content. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 88]])
- Porosity values for dolomite samples were generally higher than those for limestone samples (e.g., Dolomite at site 5 showed 20-25% porosity). ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 63]])
- A power-law relationship was established between permeability and porosity: K = 10^{0.98 * π} (where π is porosity in percent). ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 64]])
- A strong linear correlation (R^2 = 0.94) was found between measured and calculated porosity from resistivity. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 66]])
- Acoustic velocity for the Khobar aquifer in the field ranges from 1800 m/s to 3970 m/s. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 72]])
- Laboratory studies showed acoustic velocity varies from 2000 m/s (dry dolomite) to 6000 m/s (saline-saturated limestone). ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 88]])
- Saturated samples with fresh water exhibited lower acoustic velocities compared to those saturated with saline or brackish water. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 88]])
