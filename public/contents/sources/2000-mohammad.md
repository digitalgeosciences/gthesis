# Acoustic and resistivity studies of the Khobar Member, the Dammam Formation, Eastern Province, Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the lithology and petrophysical properties of the Khobar Member within the Dammam Formation in the Eastern Province of Saudi Arabia. It utilizes laboratory measurements of acoustic and electrical properties, XRD, and thin-section analysis on core samples, combined with field seismic refraction and vertical electrical sounding (VES) to establish relationships between wave velocity, resistivity, lithology, and porosity.

---

## Research Problem

The Khobar aquifer is a critical but complex reservoir in the Eastern Province; understanding its petrophysical properties (porosity, permeability) and their relationship with geophysical parameters (acoustic velocity, electrical resistivity) across varying salinity levels is necessary for better characterization of the formation's lithology and hydrogeological behavior.

## Objectives

- To study the acoustic and electric parameters of the Khobar aquifer
- To interrelate these parameters with porosity
- Study the lithology of the Khobar Member using XRD and thin section analysis.
- Determine the electrical properties, acoustic properties, porosity, and permeability of the Khobar aquifer rocks through laboratory programs.
- Conduct seismic refraction surveys to determine the thickness and velocities of subsurface layers in the Dammam area.
- Evaluate the relationship between lithology and petrophysical properties.
- Study the lithology of the Khobar Aquifer using XRD and thin section analysis.
- Evaluate the distribution of groundwater salinity and its impact on resistivity values across different sites.
- Study the lithology of the Khobar aquifer using X-Ray Diffraction and thin section studies.
- Determine the electrical properties of the Al-Khobar aquifer rocks.
- Determine the acoustic properties, porosity, and permeability of the Al-Khobar aquifer rocks.
- Perform seismic refraction to study the subsurface structure.

## Methods

Lithology was determined using X-Ray Diffraction (XRD) and thin section petrography. Laboratory measurements for porosity were conducted via the gravimetrical saturation method on core samples. Permeability was measured using a liquid permeameter on consolidated cores. Electrical resistivity of core samples was measured in various fluids (saline, brackish, fresh water) to calculate formation factor and porosity. Field data collection included seismic refraction surveys using an ES-1225 seismograph and interpretation via the SIP software suite (SIPIK, SIPIN, SIPEDT, SIPT2). Electrical resistivity field work was conducted using Schlumberger Vertical Electrical Sounding (VES) with a Syscal-R2 meter, interpreted using Resix-IP software.

## Data and Materials

11 thin sections; 9 XRD samples from the Khobar Member; core samples for porosity and permeability measurements; seismic refraction data from 6 locations in the Dammam area (profiles SP1000 to SP6000); electrical resistivity data from 5 sites (VES-1 to VES-5).

## Key Findings

- The Khobar Member is characterized by a wide variation in transmissivity (7.06 x 10^-2 to 2.9 x 10^{-3} m/s) and low storativity (0.00013 x 10^{-3} to 7.8 x 10^{-3}), reflecting its confined nature. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 30]])
- The Khobar aquifer generally has a porosity of more than 3% and a transmissivity range between 10^7 and 3 x 10^8 m/s (Note: text likely implies 10^-2 to 10^{-3) based on context. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 30]])
- The Khobar Member consists of two units: an upper unit of limestone and a lower unit of dolomitic marl with limestone intercalations. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 24]])
- Lithology was identified as predominantly limestone (upper) and dolomitic marl (lower) through thin section and XRD analysis. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 17]])
- The study established general relationships between ultrasonic wave velocity, resistivity, lithology, and porosity across different TDS levels. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 14]])
- Dolomite samples exhibited higher porosity than limestone samples. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 64]])
- Permeability is related to porosity according to the power-law: K = 10^(0.98 + 0.5*log(phi)). ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 64]])
- A correlation was found between porosity derived from resistivity and measured porosity (phi_m = 0.96 * phi_r + 0.5). ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 64]])
- As porosity increases, the acoustic velocity of the samples decreases. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 64]])
- Note: The study found that dry samples have lower velocity values compared to saturated ones, and velocity increases with increasing TDS values. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000]])
- page_number_placeholder_for_above_finding_note_is_not_provided_in_text_but_it_is_on_page_64 ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000]])
- The Khobar aquifer's acoustic velocity ranges from 1800 m/sec (Profile SP 3000) to 3970 m/sec (Profile SP 4000). ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 72]])
- Specific seismic refraction profiles (SP 1000, 2000, 5000, 6000) yielded Khobar aquifer velocities of 2500 m/sec, 2336 m/sec, 2547 m/sec, and 2551 m/sec respectively. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 78]])
- Dolomite samples exhibited lower resistivities than limestone samples. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 73]])
- XRD and thin section analyses confirmed the Khobar Aquifer consists mainly of dolomite and limestone with a minor content of clay. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 88]])
