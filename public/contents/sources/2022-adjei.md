# DEVELOPMENT OF HIGH-PERFORMANCE LIGHTWEIGHT OIL-WELL CEMENT SYSTEMS

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the development of high-performance lightweight oil-well cement systems using various pozzolanic materials, including sodium bentonite, calcined calcium bentonite (CCB), and metakaolin derived from Qusaiba kaolinitic shale. The study evaluates these materials' impact on slurry stability, rheology, and mechanical properties under low-temperature and pressure conditions.

---

## Research Problem

Standard Portland cement (OPC) based systems face limitations in high-temperature, acidic, or saline environments and may not provide sufficient elasticity or strength when used with high volumes of fly ash. There is a need for more durable, lightweight alternatives that can withstand these conditions while providing effective zonal isolation.

## Objectives

- Investigate common issues with bentonite lightweight cements, such as hydration periods.
- Develop a new lightweight slurry based on a bentonite system.
- Develop lightweight cement systems using industrial waste materials (fly ash, GGBFS, silica fume), metakaolin, and expanded perlite.
- Develop a lightweight cement formulation with intrinsic thixotropic behavior and high early-age mechanical properties using industrial waste (fly ash, GGBFS, silica fume).
- Evaluate the effect of bentonite pre-hydration time on the stability and rheology of bentonite suspensions.
- Determine the impact of calcium chloride on the stability of bentonite in cement systems.

## Methods

The study utilized XRD (X-ray diffraction) to determine mineral composition and calcination effects, FTIR (Fourier-transform infrared spectroscopy) to analyze chemical structure changes during dehydroxylation, and TGA (thermogravimetry analysis) to quantify weight loss from water and hydroxyl groups. Particle size distribution (PSD) was measured using the Mastersizer 2000MU laser particle size analyzer. Mechanical properties including compressive strength were tested using a hydraulic press, while scratch strength and elastic constants were determined via scratch test equipment. Microstructural analysis was performed using SEM to observe hydration products like portlandite and C-S-H gel.

## Data and Materials

The study utilized Class G cement, raw and dehydroxylated sodium bentonite, fly ash, silica fume, and Qusaiba kaolinitic shale (QKS). Samples included various formulations of dehydroxylated bentonite (DB_10 to DB_50) and calcined calcium bentonite (CCB_10 to CCB_30). Comparative samples included fly ash-based systems (FA_20) and silica fume-enhanced systems.

## Key Findings

- A new 13.5 ppg fly ash system using a synergy of fly ash, silica fume, and GGBFS exhibited a 24h sonic strength of 3116 psi at 150°F/1500 psi. ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 20]])
- Dehydroxylated sodium bentonite (heated at 1526°F for 3 hours) provided a 24-hour compressive strength of 800 psi and 787 psi at 40% and 50% replacement, respectively. ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 21]])
- Calcined calcium bentonite (heated at 1526°F for 3 hours) showed optimal performance when used as a 20% replacement in 12.5 ppg systems. ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 21]])
- Kaolinitic shale from the Qassim outcrop, calcined at 1562°F, produced a highly amorphous material that outperformed commercial metakaolin in mechanical properties (compressive/tensile strength, Young's modulus). ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 21]])
- A cement-GWP (Granite Waste Powder) and perlite system with 30% GWP and 10% perlite showed favorable rheological properties and high mechanical strength. ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 22]])
- Silica fume added to fly ash systems increased early-age strength development and reduced fluid loss, while also providing resistance to gas migration. ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 47]])
- GGBFS-based slurries showed high strength at longer curing times and were effective in inhibiting gas migration in fields like Tunu in Indonesia. ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 49]])
- Metakaolin's pozzolanic reactivity at an early age is higher than that of silica fume, making it suitable for high-concentration applications (up to 25% BWOC). ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 53]])
- The developed lightweight slurry (LWS) achieved a density of 13.5 ppg with a 24-hour compressive strength of 3116 psi. ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 83]])
- Fly ash substitution reduced slurry density by 7.8% and 11.52% for 25% and 50% replacement, respectively. ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 78]])
- The LWS system exhibited intrinsic thixotropic behavior (hysteresis loop) even without the addition of thixotropic agents. ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 80]])
- Thixotropic properties, including gel strength, were observed to increase with increasing temperature in the LWS formulation. ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 80]])
- The LWS system showed a 3-day compressive strength of 100% for 0% fly ash, 97.69% for 25% fly ash, and 64.70% for 50% fly ash compared to the base. ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 78]])
- The LWS system had a 10-minute gel strength of 26.73 lb/100 ft2 at 150°F and a plastic viscosity of 20 cP. ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 79]])
- The LWS system had a thickening time between 3 hours 22 minutes (50 Bc) and 3 hours 55 minutes (70 Bc). ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 82]])

## Limitations

Geopolymers are more susceptible to water-based drilling fluids, exhibit low strength below 86 °F due to slow geopolymerization rates, and can show high brittleness. Additionally, the resistance of geopolymer to sulfate attack has not been extensively studied.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The study suggests that Qusaiba kaolinitic shale (QKS) is a viable source for metakaolin to develop high-performance lightweight cement systems. It also highlights the potential of various industrial and agricultural wastes (fly ash, GGBFS, silica fume, rice husk ash, etc.) as sustainable alternatives to traditional additives in oil-well cementing.
