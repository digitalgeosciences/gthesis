# Interaction between modern dune and interdune deposits in North of Al Jafurah Sand Sea and their significance to Reservoir Heterogeneity and Architecture, Eastern Saudi Arabia.

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the geomorphology and sedimentology of dune and interdune systems in the Jafurah Sand Sea, focusing on how wind and water interact to shape these features. It utilizes GPR and satellite imagery to characterize internal structures and determine migration patterns, concluding that these processes significantly impact reservoir heterogeneity.

---

## Research Problem

Aeolian systems are complex subsurface systems that act as important hydrocarbon reservoirs; however, their stratigraphical complexity—specifically the interaction between dune and interdune facies—is not fully understood, leading to challenges in identifying high-quality targets.

## Objectives

- Identify and document the spatial extent of modern interdune and dune bedforms.
- Study the aeolian dune and interdune deposits.
- Construct a facies depositional model of the aeolian system for the study area.
- Investigate the aeolian bedform internal structure using GPR.
- Study the relationship between the aeolian sub-environments (dune, interdune, and sand sheet) from central to marginal areas.
- Study the relationship between the aeolian system and adjoining systems (e.g., fluvial and marine systems).
- Study the effect of fluctuated water table on the development of the aeolian system.
- Investigate the ae-bedform internal structure using GPR.
- Study the relationship between aeolian sub-environments (dune, interdune, and sand sheet) from central to marginal areas.
- Study the relationship between aeolian system and adjoining systems (e.g., fluvial and marine systems).
- Study the effect of fluctuated water table on the development of aeolian system.
- Investigate the geomorphology of dune and interdune settings in the Jafurah Sand Sea.
- Analyze factors controlling the deposition of aeolian settings, specifically dune and interstellar facies interaction, extension, and distribution.
- Determine the impact of wind and water on the preservation and migration of dunes.
- Map internal structures of dune bedforms using Ground Penetrating Radar (GPR).
- Analyze and explain the factors controlling the deposition of aeolian setting, specifically dune and interdune facies interaction, extension, and distribution.

## Methods

The methodology is divided into three parts: 1) Remote sensing analysis using high-resolution satellite imagery (Google Earth Pro, 10-50m resolution) and higher resolution data (2m resolution) to map geomorphology and track changes since 1984. 2) Field work involving the excavation of trenches (by digging or bulldozer) and the collection of shallow cores from both dune and interdune deposits in wet and dry settings. 3) Laboratory work including grain texture analysis via sieve-analysis on collected samples to determine sedimentological properties.

## Data and Materials

Analysis of satellite imagery for the Jafurah Sand Sea; GPR data from 8 profiles (In-lines A-A', B-B', C-C', D-D' and cross-lines E-E', F-F', G-G', H-H') over a 100x200m area; physical measurements of grain size, sorting, and bedding from trench samples.

## Key Findings

- The study area contains a variety of dune types, primarily Barchanoid (Barchans and Barchanoid ridges) indicating unimodal wind regimes, and parabolic dunes stabilized by vegetation. ([[2022-al-alawi|Al-Alawi, 2022, p. 16]])
- Wind is the primary driver for the accumulation and distribution of aeolian features, with a prevailing direction from north to south. ([[2022-al-alawi|Al-Alawi, 2022, p. 16]])
- The Jafurah desert receives annual rainfall of 54-80 mm within a high humidity belt, leading to interactions between aeolian and fluvial depositional systems. ([[2022-al-alawi|Al-Alawi, 2022, p. 16]])
- Fluctuating water tables influenced by Arabian Gulf tides play a critical role in the development and stability of the sedimentary system. ([[2022-al-alawi|Al-Alawi, 2022, p. 16]])
- GPR survey successfully identified internal structures of dune bedforms, including wind ripples, grainflow, and avalanche cross-strata on the lee side. ([[2022-al-alawi|Al-Alawi, 2022, p. 92]])
- Interdune features include sand sheets (deflation surfaces), sabkhas (mud flats), and trenches showing salt layers or wind ripple deposits. ([[2022-al-alawi|Al-Alawi, 2022, p. 70]])
- Dunes in the Jafurah area are primarily of Barchanoid type (Barchans, Barchanoid ridges, and Transverse ridges), indicating unimodal wind regimes. ([[2022-al-alawi|Al-Alawi, 2022, p. 37]])
- Parabolic dunes are common in the region and are controlled by vegetation presence; they form through differential deflation of sandsheet areas. ([[2022-al-alawi|Al-Alawi, 2022, p. 55]])
- Interdune deposits vary based on moisture levels: dry, damp, wet, or evaporitic (sabkhas). ([[2022-al-alawi|Al-Alawi, 2022, p. 38]])
- Sand sheets in Jafurah are typically elongated parallel to the wind direction and can reach several meters in thickness. ([[2022-al-alawi|Al-Alawi, 2022, p. 38]])

## Limitations

The study notes that while 2D GPR lines were effective for characterizing internal structures and migration direction, a 3D data cube would be highly recommended to fully map aeolian bounding surfaces.

## Recommendations and Future Work

A 3D data cube is recommended for future studies to better map the complex aeolian bounding surfaces of the dune system.
