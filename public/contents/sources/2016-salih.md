# DEPOSITIONAL AND DIAGENETIC CONTROLS ON POROSITY EVOLUTION OF THE MIOCENE DAM FORMATION CARBONATES; AL-LIDAM AREA, EASTERN SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the depositional and diagenetic controls on porosity evolution in the Miocene Dam Formation carbonates within the Al-Lidam area of eastern Saudi Arabia. It utilizes a combination of field mapping, thin-section petrography, SEM, XRD, and XRF to characterize lithofacies and quantify the impact of various diagenetic processes (micritization, cementation, dolomitization) on reservoir quality.

---

## Research Problem

The primary challenge is understanding how complex interplay between depositional settings (e.g., intertidal vs. subtidal) and post-depositional diagenesis (e.g., meteoric leaching, dolomite formation) controls the distribution of porosity and permeability in carbonate reservoirs like the Dam Formation.

## Methods

The methodology included extensive field work to describe six outcrop sections and collect 108 samples. Laboratory analysis involved core plug testing for porosity and permeability using a Core Test TPI-219 Helium Porosimeter and Hassler Core holder assembly. Thin-section petrography was performed on all samples (using Alizarin Red S staining). XRD was conducted on 25 samples to identify mineralogy, XRF was performed on 78 samples for chemical composition, SEM was used on 18 samples to observe micro-porosity and diagenetic features, and cathodoluminescence microscopy was used on 8 samples to distinguish mineral species and paragenetic sequences.

## Data and Materials

Data includes 23 distinct outcrops in the Al-Lidam area; various lithofacies (f1 through f15) identified within the Dam Formation; mineralogical data from XRD/XRF; and petrographic observations of carbonate textures like ooids, peloids, and skeletal grains.

## Key Findings

- The study identifies 15 distinct lithofacies (f1-f15) within the Dam Formation, ranging from siliciclastic-carbonate sequences to pure carbonates. ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 30]])
- Micritization by endolithic micro-organisms was identified as a significant process affecting grain margins in several facies (e.g., f8, f12, f15). ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 67]])
- High-magnesium calcite cementation was observed on skeletal fragments of intertidal grainstone facies (f9). ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 69]])
- page_number_reference_note_for_finding_3_is_not_explicitly_numbered_but_in_section_5.2.2_on_page_70 ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016]])
- Fifteen lithofacies were identified, deposited in a carbonate ramp under supratidal, beach, intertidal, and shallow subtidal conditions. ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 16]])
- Intertidal grainstones and packstones are influenced by extensive meteoric diagenesis leading to moldic and vuggy porosity. ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 17]])
- marine diagenesis was observed but restricted to beach grainstone and subtidal quartz wacke-packstone lithofacies in the form of aragonite and high magnesium calcite cement. ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016]])
- page_number_placeholder_for_manual_check_needed_but_text_is_on_page_17 ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016]])
- The Dam Formation is characterized by a variety of lithofacies, including channelized medium sandstone (f1), interbedded mudstone and evaporites (f2), stromatolites (f3), and various grainstones with different cross-bedding styles. ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 49]])
- The presence of 'chicken wire' anhydrite in facies f2 indicates deposition from hypersaline water under arid conditions, which led to the dolomitization of underlying beds. ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 54]])
- Stromatolites (f3) were found at various stratigraphic levels with thicknesses ranging from 5 cm to 50 cm, indicating deposition in shallow intertidal to supredal environments. ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 53]])
- Trough cross-bedded sandstone (f7) shows evidence of high energy conditions and alternating oxidation/reduction states, interpreted as an upper shore face environment. ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 61]])
- Herringbone cross-bedding in facies f8 is a diagnostic feature for tidal settings, specifically the intertidal zone. ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 62]])
- Keystone vugs were identified in several facies (f8, f9), indicating periodic subaerial exposure and drying out of sediments. ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 64]])
- XRD analysis confirmed that the grainstone facies underlying the mudstone-evaporite facies (f2) were pervasively dolomitized. ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 52]])
