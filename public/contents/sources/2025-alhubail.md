# CO2-Carbonate Mudrock Interactions: Investigating The Influence of Organic Richness and Maturity Variations Through Stable Isotopic, Geochemical and Petrophysical Analysis

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the effects of CO2 treatment on carbonate mudrock source rock samples, specifically focusing on how organic richness and maturity variations influence stable isotopic responses. It also evaluates the geomechanical, geochemical, and petrophysical properties of these rocks after exposure to CO2.

---

## Research Problem

Understanding the impact of CO2 interaction with carbonate mudrocks (source rocks) is critical for carbon sequestration studies; specifically, how internal factors like organic richness and maturity affect the rock's response to CO2 treatment.

## Objectives

- To investigate the influence of organic richness and maturity variations on the stable isotopic responses of carbonate mudrock samples treated with CO2.
- To investigate the geochemical, geomechanical, and petrophysical properties of carbonate mudrock after treatment with CO2.

## Methods

The study utilizes a combination of stable isotope analysis to track chemical changes, core analysis for physical property measurement, and petrophysical evaluation. The methodology involves treating carbonate mudrock samples (varying in organic richness and maturity) with CO2 and then performing multi-parameter testing including geomechanical tests and geochemical profiling.

## Data and Materials

The study uses carbonate mudrock samples that are geographically different; one sample was collected from the Upper Cretaceous period. The specific number of samples is not detailed in the provided text, but they are categorized by organic richness and maturity levels.

## Key Findings

- Carbonate mudrocks exhibit varying responses to CO2 treatment based on their initial organic content and thermal maturity. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 10]])
- Stable isotope analysis is used to differentiate between carbonate minerals and organic matter components during the carbonation process. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 35]])
- Petrophysical properties (porosity, permeability) are altered by CO2-induced mineral changes in mudrock samples. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 10]])
- The study identifies that both organic richness and maturity are key variables in determining the geochemical response of carbonate mudrocks to CO2. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 40]])
- Petrophysical properties (porosity, permeability) were analyzed post-CO2 treatment to determine impact on reservoir quality. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 50]])
- Stable isotope analysis was used to track the chemical evolution of the carbonate mudrock during CO2 interaction. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 71]])
