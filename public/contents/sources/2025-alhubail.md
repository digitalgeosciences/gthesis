# CO2-Carbonate Mudrock Interactions: Investigating The Influence of Organic Richness and Maturity Variations Through Stable Isotopic, Geochemical and Petrophysical Analysis

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the effects of CO2 treatment on carbonate mudrock source rock samples, specifically focusing on how organic richness and maturity variations influence stable isotopic responses. It also evaluates the geochemical, geomechanical, and petrophysical properties of these rocks after exposure to CO2.

---

## Research Problem

Understanding how varying levels of organic richness and thermal maturity in carbonate mudrocks affect their response to CO2 injection, which is critical for evaluating potential carbon sequestration sites or understanding hydrocarbon interactions with CO2.

## Objectives

- Investigate the influence of organic richness and maturity variations on the stable isotopic responses of carbonate mudrock samples.
- Investigate geochemical, geomechanical, and petrophysical properties of carbonate mudrock after treatment with CO2.
- Investigate the influence of organic richness and maturity variations on stable isotopic responses in carbonate mudrock samples after CO2 treatment.
- Invest_investigate geochemical, geomechanical, and petrophysical properties of carbonate mudrock after treatment with CO2.
- To investigate the influence of organic richness and maturity variations on the stable isotopic responses of carbonate mudrock samples treated with CO2.
- To evaluate the geochemical, geomechanical, and petrophysical properties of carbonate mudrock after treatment with CO2.

## Methods

The study utilizes a combination of laboratory analyses including Rock-Eval Pyrolysis to determine organic richness (TOC) and maturity (Ro), XRF for elemental analysis, SEM-EDS for mineralogical characterization, XRD for mineral identification, and stable isotope analysis to track chemical changes. Petrophysical properties were evaluated through core analysis and thin-section petrography.

## Data and Materials

The study uses carbonate mudrock samples from different geographic locations (specifically mentioned as including an Upper Cretaceous sample). The data includes mineralogical compositions, TOC values, Rock-Eval parameters (S1, S2, HI, OI), and petrophysical measurements post-CO2 treatment.

## Key Findings

- Carbonate mudrocks with higher organic richness and maturity show distinct stable isotopic responses when treated with CO2. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 5]])
- Petrophysical properties of carbonate mudrock are significantly influenced by the presence of organic matter during CO2 interaction. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 10]])
- Carbonate mudrocks show distinct responses to CO2 treatment based on their initial organic content and maturity levels. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 17]])
- Stable isotope analysis revealed significant shifts in carbon isotopes following CO2 interaction, influenced by the presence of organic matter. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 18]])
- Geochemical analysis (XRF) identified key mineral phases and elements that interact with CO2 during the treatment process. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 20]])
- Petrophysical properties, including porosity and permeability, were altered by CO2-induced mineralization or dissolution in carbonate mudrocks. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 31]])
- The study identifies that organic richness and maturity significantly influence the stable isotopic response of carbonate mudrock when exposed to CO2. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 37]])
- Petrophysical properties (porosity, permeability) are affected by the interaction between CO2 and the carbonate mudrock matrix. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 40]])
- Geochemical analysis reveals specific changes in mineral chemistry following CO2 treatment. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 50]])
- The study highlights that different maturity levels lead to distinct geochemical signatures in the presence of CO2. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 43]])
- Carbonate mudrocks with varying organic richness and maturity show distinct stable isotopic responses when treated with CO2. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 71]])
- The study identifies specific changes in petrophysical properties (porosity, permeability) following CO2 treatment in carbonate mudrock samples. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 73]])
- Geochemical analysis reveals the impact of CO2 on the mineralogical composition and organic content of the source rock. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 75]])
- The interaction between CO2 and carbonate mudrock is influenced by the initial maturity of the organic matter. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 76]])
