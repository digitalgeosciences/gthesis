# EFFECT OF NANO CLAY ON OIL WELL CEMENT PROPERTIES FOR HIGH PRESSURE AND TEMPERAT.

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the impact of varying concentrations of Nano clay (0-4% BWOC) on the physical and mechanical properties of Class G oil well cement under high pressure and temperature (HPHT) conditions. The study concludes that Nano clay effectively acts as a retarder, improves rheological properties, and enhances early compressive strength compared to standard base mixes.

---

## Research Problem

Standard Class G cement undergoes significant degradation in compressive strength and increased permeability when exposed to high temperatures exceeding 230°F (strength retrogression). The study addresses how the addition of Nano clay can mitigate these effects while improving other critical properties like thickening time and free water separation.

## Objectives

- Evaluate the physical properties of Saudi cement class G with Nano clay addition
- Assess the impact of various additives (fluid loss agent, retarder, expanding agent, and friction reducing agent) in combination with Nano clay
- Determine the effect of different percentages of Nano clay on slurry properties under high temperature and pressure conditions
- Identify the best slurry design based on experimental results for use in Saudi Arabian oil/gas wells
- Determine the impact of Nano clay on thickening time under simulated well conditions
- Assess the compressive strength of cement containing different percentages of Nano clay (1%, 2%, 3%, and 4%)

## Methods

The study utilized a series of laboratory tests to evaluate cement slurry performance. Rheological properties were measured using a Fann Viscometer. Thickening time was determined using an HPHT consistometer according to API specifications. Free water separation was assessed by aging slurries for 2 hours at room temperature. Density was measured via pressurized mud balance. Porosity and permeability were tested using an Automated Porosimeter/Permeameter (AP-608) with a pulse decay technique. Microstructural analysis included SEM to observe morphology and XRD to identify mineral phases like C3S, C2S, ettringite, and portlandite.

## Data and Materials

The study uses Saudi cement class G, various chemical additives (fluid loss agents, retarders, expanding agents, friction reducing agents), and nano clay added at different percentages. Experimental tests are conducted under high-temperature and high-pressure conditions to simulate deep well environments.

## Key Findings

- Addition of Nano clay improves the rheological and mechanical properties of the cement slurry. ([[2013-murtaza|Murtaza, 2013, p. 20]])
- Nano clay reduces the free water content in the cement mixture. ([[2013-murtaza|Murtaza, 2013, p. 20]])
- retards the thickening time of the slurry. ([[2013-murtaza|Murtaza, 2013]])
- page_number_not_found_in_source_but_implied_by_context_on_page_20 ([[2013-murtaza|Murtaza, 2013]])
- Nano particles increase the viscosity and yield point of slurry, which results in improved performance of the slurry (Santra et al., 2012). ([[2013-murtaza|Murtaza, 2013, p. 52]])
- The addition of a lignosulfonate dispersant was found to decrease the viscosity of a Class G cement slurry. ([[2013-murtaza|Murtaza, 2013, p. 51]])
- Nano clay is identified as a layered material with a thickness in the order of 10 A° and a width up to 1000 nm, which can increase stiffness, strength, and heat resistance while decreasing permeability. ([[2013-murtaza|Murtaza, 2013, p. 46]])
- The slurry consistency at which the thickening-time test was terminated should be documented (e.g., 70 Bc or 100 Bc). ([[2013-murtaza|Murtaza, 2013, p. 53]])
- A well design for a Saudi Arabian well included a depth of 14,000 ft and a bottom hole static temperature (BHST) of 290°F. ([[2013-murtaza|Murtaza, 2013, p. 62]])
- The addition of Nano clay acts as a retarder, increasing thickening time from 3 hours (base mix) to 6 hours (1% NC), 8 hours (2% NC), and over 9 hours (3% NC). ([[2013-murtaza|Murtaza, 2013, p. 93]])
- Nano clay successfully eliminated free water separation in all tested systems (0, 1, 2, 3% BWOC) compared to the base mix which showed 2.1 ml/250ml of free water. ([[2013-murtaza|Murtaza, 2013, p. 97]])
- The addition of Nano clay increased plastic viscosity from 9.65 lbf/100ft2 (base) to 244.64 cP for the 3% NC system, though it had a minimal effect on yield point until 3% concentration. ([[2013-murtaza|Murtaza, 2013, p. 100]])
- The 3% NC system showed a significant increase in 10-minute gel strength to 35 lbf/100ft2 compared to the base mix of 29 lbf/100ft2. ([[2013-murtaza|Murtaza, 2013, p. 102]])
- The addition of Nano clay did not significantly impact density, with only a 1.25% reduction observed between the 0% and 3% NC systems. ([[2013-murtaza|Murtaza, 2013, p. 104]])
- The 1% NC system provided high compressive strength (7190 psi) after 48 hours, while the 3% NC system reached 6918 psi. ([[2013-murtaza|Murtaza, 2013, p. 114]])

## Limitations

The 4% Nano clay cement system was not included in final testing because it was too difficult to mix at standard water-cement ratios due to high surface area and absorption of water by the particles.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Investigate the effect of Nano clay without other silica products.
- Study Nano clay effects in shallow applications with low pressure/temperature and different water-to-cement ratios.
- Explore combinations of Nano clay and Nano silica separately for high temperature applications.
- Investigate other nanomaterials like Nano alumina, Carbon Nanotubes, and Nano Fe2O3.
