# Geostatistical estimation of reserves in the Abu-Tartur phosphate deposits western desert, Egypt.

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the geostatistical estimation of phosphate reserves in the Abu Tartur deposit, located in Egypt's Western Desert. It utilizes variogram analysis and kriging techniques to determine global and local estimates for tonnage, grade, and content based on data from 347 drill-holes.

---

## Research Problem

The need for a more precise and statistically robust method for estimating the quantity (tonnage) and quality (grade) of phosphate reserves in the Abu Tartur deposit, as classical estimation methods often lack confidence intervals and are based on arbitrary geometric assumptions.

## Objectives

- Perform initial statistical treatment of the data to determine sampling distributions.
- Compute horizontal and vertical variograms for phosphate grade, thickness, and accumulation.
- Fit theoretical models to the computed variograms.
- Determine global estimates for total tonnage, total phosphate content, and mean grade using geostatistical methods.
- Perform local estimation (kriging) to provide block-by-block estimates of the deposit.
- Perform a critical review of drilling data from the Abu Tartur phosphate deposit.

## Methods

The study employs several geostatistical techniques. Variograms were constructed for P2O5, thickness, and accumulation using the VARIO3 program to determine continuity, anisotropy, and range. Global estimation was performed using formulas based on variogram parameters (spherical and compound spherical models) to calculate tonnage, content, and grade with associated variances. Local estimation utilized block kriging via the KRIGE3 program for 1m thick blocks in a 400m x 400m grid. The results were processed through ISOPOLY and BLKPLT for mapping. A grade-tonnage relationship was derived using the REPORT program based on lognormal distribution assumptions.

## Data and Materials

Data from 347 drill-holes located in the Abu Tartur deposit, specifically targeting the lower phosphate horizon within the Sibaiya Formation. Data included P2O5 content (%), thickness (m), and accumulation (%m) for both 1-m composites and average drill-hole values.

## Key Findings

- The upper phosphate horizon has an average grade of 17% P2O50. and a thickness ranging between 0.4 m and 7.3 m. ([[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987, p. 35]])
- Statistical analysis showed that the distribution for P2O5% is slightly negatively skewed, while thickness and accumulation are slightly positively skewed. ([[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987, p. 58]])
- Spherical models were successfully fitted to all experimental variograms (horizontal and vertical) for phosphate grade, thickness, and accumulation. ([[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987, p. 18]])
- Global estimation results: tonnage = 308.36 ± 13.56 Mt; total phosphate content = 80.05 ± 0.15 Mt; mean grade = 26.00 ± 0.19%. ([[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987, p. 18]])
- Kriging standard deviations for the 3752 blocks were found to be relatively small, indicating high reliability of the kriged estimates. ([[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987, p. 18]])
- The phosphate deposit is located in the Sibaiya Formation (Late Campanian) and contains two main horizons separated by shaly and marly intercalations. ([[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987, p. 23]])
- The average value for P2O5 grade was estimated to be 26.58% with a range of 18% to 28%. ([[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987, p. 120]])
- Average thickness of the phosphate horizon was determined to be 2.95 m, ranging from approximately 1.0m to 5.0m. ([[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987, p. 120]])
- The average accumulation value for the deposit was estimated at 76.58 %m. ([[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987, p. 120]])
- Variogram analysis revealed that all variables exhibited spherical behavior, with P2O5 showing a compound spherical model fit (C1=0.0, C2=2.4, C3=2.1; a1=80m, a2=700m). ([[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987, p. 119]])
- The global estimate for total tonnage was calculated as 308.36 Mt with a 95% confidence level of ±13.56 Mt. ([[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987, p. 96]])
- The total phosphate content (P2O5) was estimated at 80.05 Mt with a 95% confidence level of ±0.15 Mt. ([[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987, p. 97]])
- The mean grade for the deposit was determined to be 26.4% with a 95% confidence level of ±0.19%. ([[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987, p. 98]])
- Kriging standard deviations for most blocks were less than 0.09, indicating high reliability in the local estimation. ([[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987, p. 105]])
- The grade-tonnage relationship showed an inverse relationship between phosphate grade and tonnage; e.g., a grade of 26.4% corresponds to 1410.75 Mt of reserves. ([[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987, p. 113]])

## Limitations

Classical methods were noted to have limitations including high costs of construction, arbitrary zones of influence for samples, lack of a theoretical basis for assigning values to blocks, and the inability to provide confidence intervals.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The author suggests that as more data becomes available, the kriged estimates and the grade-tonnage relationship should be regularly updated to improve accuracy for planning.
