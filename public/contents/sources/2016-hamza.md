# SANDSTONE ACIDIZING USING CHELATING AGENTS

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the use of chelating agents (DTPA, EDTA, and HEDTA) combined with potassium carbonate as a standalone stimulation fluid for Berea and Bandera sandstone formations. It evaluates the impact of these fluids on permeability, rock integrity, and chemical stability under various temperatures, injection rates, and dilution methods (deionized water vs. seawater).

---

## Research Problem

The challenge of finding effective acidizing fluids for sandstone formations that can avoid the precipitation of calcium sulfate scales (common when using seawater) while enhancing permeability through the dissolution of carbonate and dolomite minerals and the removal of iron from clay minerals.

## Objectives

- Introduce new formulation using DTPA chelating agent and potassium carbonate catalyst for sandstone acidizing
- Identify the optimum conditions of the formulated fluid in terms of DTPA concentration and catalyst/DTPA ratio using seawater and deionized water at high pH
- Determine the effectiveness of the catalytic-DTPA acid as a stand-alone stimulation fluid for both Berea and Bandera sandstone and compares this to EDTA and HEDTA chelating agents
- Examine the rock integrity after stimulating Berea and Bandera cores with EDTA and DTPA at high pH by acoustic measurements
- Determine the optimum concentration of chelating agents (EDTA, HEDTA, DTPA) for maximum rock solubility.
- Identify the optimal amount of potassium carbonate catalyst to enhance chelation of cations like Ca2+, Mg2+, and Fe3+.

## Methods

Core flooding tests were conducted on Berea and Bandera cores (2-6 inches long) to evaluate permeability enhancement. Samples were saturated with 3 wt. % KCl brine. Fluids included DTPA and EDTA at 20 wt. % concentration at pH 11, with 3 wt. % potassium carbonate as a catalyst. ICP analysis was used to measure the amount of dissolved Ca, Mg, Fe, and Al ions in effluents. CT scanning was performed to check for precipitates and assess rock integrity. Elastic moduli (Young's modulus, Shear modulus, Bulk modulus) were calculated from p-wave and s-wave velocities obtained via acoustic measurements under varying overburden pressures.

## Data and Materials

The study utilized crushed powder samples of Berea and Bandera sandstones, and core samples of the same formations (1.5 in. diameter, 2-6 in. length). Three types of chelating agents (EDTA, HEDTA, DTPA) were tested at concentrations from 5 to 25 wt.%. Potassium carbonate was added as a catalyst in amounts of 1, 3, 6, and 9 wt.%.

## Key Findings

- The optimum concentration of chelating agents (EDTA, HEDTA, and DTPA) in the new formulated fluids is 20 wt. % with a potassium carbonate catalyst ratio of 3 wt. % at pH of 11. ([[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016, p. 19]])
- The highest solubility was recorded for Bandera samples treated using DTPA at 150°F, reaching approximately 23%. ([[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016, p. 19]])
- Permeability ratio improved by 39% for Berea cores after adding 3 wt. % catalyst to 20 wt. % DTPA diluted with deionized water at pH 11, 5 cm3/min injection rate, and 250°F. ([[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016, p. 19]])
- For Bandera cores, diluting the acids with seawater showed a 40% enhancement in permeability ratio. ([[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016, p. 19]])
- Decreasing the injection rate increased the permeability ratio to more than double for Berea and 70% for Bandera using DTPA seawater base with 3 wt. % catalyst. ([[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016, p. 19]])
- The new formulated fluid performed better at 250°F; reducing the temperature to 150°F reduced its efficiency. ([[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016, p. 19]])
- Using long cores (6 inches) showed the performance of the new formulation better than 2-inch cores. ([[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016, p. 19]])
- Bandera cores acidized with 20 wt. % DTPA and 3 wt.% potassium carbonate diluted using seawater showed the largest decrease in Young's modulus and highest permeability enhancement after injecting 10 pore volumes. ([[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016, p. 19]])
- Berea samples showed insignificant change in elastic moduli values during stimulation. ([[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016, p. 19]])
- The optimum concentration for all three chelating agents (EDTA, HEDTA, DTPA) was found to be 20 wt. % at room temperature. ([[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016, p. 59]])
- For Bandera samples treated with DTPA, the solubility ratio was 6.2% using deionized water and 5.3% using seawater at room temperature. ([[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016, p. 60]])
- The addition of potassium carbonate up to 3 wt. % enhanced the solubility ratio of both Berea and Bandera sandstone; higher amounts did not provide further increase. ([[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016, p. 62]])
- the inclusion of potassium carbonate as a catalyst in sandstone acidizing is a first-time implementation using DTPA. ([[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016]])
