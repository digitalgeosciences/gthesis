# Improving Performance of Oil-Based Drilling Fluids with Organoclay

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the efficacy of various organoclays (OCs), including montmorillonite, sepiolite, and palygorskite, as rheology modifiers for oil-based drilling fluids (OBDFs) under high-pressure and high-temperature (HPHT) conditions. The study concludes that OCs significantly enhance emulsion stability, mitigate sagging issues, and improve rheological properties like plastic viscosity and yield point in challenging environments.

---

## Research Problem

The degradation of drilling fluid properties under HPHT conditions leads to wellbore instability, poor suspension of solids (sagging), and excessive fluid loss into the formation. The study addresses whether Claytone-EM can provide superior rheological and filtration performance compared to existing organoclays like MC-TONE in these challenging environments.

## Objectives

- Investigate different OCs alone and in combinations for their efficacy as a rheology modifier for OBDFs at HPHT conditions.
- Evaluate six OCs for rheology modification of OBDFs.
- Characterize the mineral and chemical compositions of Claytone-IMG 400 and Claytone-EM using XRD, XRF, and SEM.
- Evaluate the impact of Claytone-IMG 400 on drilling fluid density, electrical stability, sagging tendency, rheology, and filtration properties under HPHT conditions.
- Compare the performance of Claytone-IMG 400 with a pre-existing organoclay (MC-TONE).
- Evaluate the impact of Claytone-EM on the performance of OBDFs under HPHT conditions compared to MC-TONE.

## Methods

The study involved preparing two invert emulsion samples using a practical field formulation with various additives (primary/secondary emulsifiers, lime, CaCl2 brine, organoclay, etc.). Characterization of the organoclays was performed using XRD, XRF, SEM, and PSD analysis. Performance testing included: 1) Mud density and electrical stability measurements; 2) Static sag tests at 275°F and 500 psi; 3) Dynamic sag tests (viscometer sag shoe test); 4) Oscillatory amplitude, frequency, and time sweep tests using an Anton-Paar rheometer to determine G' and G''; 5) Rheology measurements (PV, YP, AV) using a Grace viscometer; 6) Filtration tests using an Ofite HPHT fluid loss tester.

## Data and Materials

Two types of organoclays: Claytone-IMG 400 (specific gravity 1.6 g/cm3, bulk density 30 lb/ft3) and Claytone-EM (specific gravity 1.6 g/cm3, bulk density 23 lb/ft3). Comparison material: MC-TONE organoclay. Testing conditions: 275°F temperature and 500 psi differential pressure.

## Key Findings

- Organoclays (OCs) significantly enhanced emulsion stability and mitigated sagging issues under both static and dynamic conditions. ([[2024-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2024, p. 16]])
- The addition of OCs led to improvements in plastic viscosity (PV), yield point (YP), apparent viscosity (AV), and the YP/PV ratio. ([[2024-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2024, p. 16]])
- OCs resulted in heightened gel strength (GS) and improved filtration properties of the drilling fluid. ([[2024-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2024, p. 16]])
- OMMT is the most popular commercial rheological additive used in OBDF, providing stability through dispersion, swelling, and exfoliation. ([[2024-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2024, p. 30]])
- CN-OMMT showed higher thermal stability and surface lipophilicity compared to CO-OMMT and NO-OMMT. ([[2024-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2024, p. 32]])
- OPAL exhibits excellent thermal stability due to its haystack structure in OBDFs, making it a promising rheological additive for high-temperature applications. ([[2024-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2024, p. 41]])
- The combination of OMMT and OSEP (or OPAL) provides stable, non-progressive gels and improved rheological properties compared to using them separately. ([[2024-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2024, p. 47]])
- At high OC concentrations, the yield stress varies according to a power law model, while at low concentrations, it varies linearly. ([[2024-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2024, p. 30]])
- Claytone-IMG 400 enhanced electrical stability by 9% (to 942 Volt) compared to MC-TONE (863 Volt). ([[2024-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2024, p. 70]])
- Claytone-IMG 400 improved plastic viscosity (PV) by 30%, yield point (YP) by 38%, and apparent viscosity (AV) by 33% compared to MC-TONE. ([[2024-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2024, p. 78]])
- The YP/PV ratio increased by 6% from 1.12 to 1.19 when using Claytone-IMG 400. ([[2024-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2024, p. 79]])
- Claytone-IMG 400 reduced filtration volume by 10% (from 5.0 cm3 to 4.5 cm3) and filter cake thickness by 37.5% (from 2.60 mm to 1.89 mm). ([[2024-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2024, p. 82]])
- Claytone-EM improved PV, YP, AV, and the YP/PV ratio by 11%, 85%, 28%, and 66% respectively compared to MC-TONE. ([[2024-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2024, p. 86]])
- Claytone-IMG 400 average particle size (D50) was 14.74 µm, while MC-TONE was 40.01 µm. ([[2024-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2024, p. 68]])
- Claytone-IMG 400 is composed of montmorillonite (71.1%) and clinochlore (28.7%). ([[2024-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2024, p. 65]])

## Limitations

The study's temperature range was limited; it is noted that exploration at extreme temperatures could be beneficial. Additionally, the absence of flow experiments limits a comprehensive understanding of sag effects.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Use surfactants with longer alkyl chains to prepare OMMT for greater viscosity, thixotropy, and thermal stability.
- Conduct more research on the application of OPAL and OSEP in OBDFs.
