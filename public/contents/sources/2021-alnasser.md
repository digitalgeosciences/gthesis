# CARBON NANOSTRUCTURE-BASED MATERIALS FOR REMOVAL OF ORGANIC CONTAMINANTS FROM WASTEWATER

**Summary**: The thesis investigated the synthesis and characterization of functionalized carbon nanostructure-based materials, specifically polyethylenimine modified graphene (PG), for removing organic contaminants like methylene blue from wastewater. The study concluded that PG exhibited high adsorption capacity (161.29 mg/g) and structural stability across various temperatures and pH levels.

---

## Research Problem

The removal of organic chemical contaminants, such as dyes and heavy metals, from industrial wastewater is a global environmental challenge due to their toxicity and persistence in the environment.

## Objectives

- To evaluate different prepared graphene-based materials as adsorbents for the removal of dye pollutants.
- To optimize the adsorption parameters for the best adsorbent.
- To investigate the adsorption data using different related models.
- To study the efficiency of the adsorbent in a dynamic mode.
- Design and synthesize functionalized carbon nanostructure-based materials including graphene and polymer/graphene.
- Characterize the developed materials to evaluate their effectiveness in removing organic contaminants.

## Methods

The research involved synthesizing five types of modified graphene (OAG, PG, EG, DG, and HG) via Hummer's method. Characterization was performed using SEM to observe morphology, TGA for thermal stability, FTIR to identify functional groups, and XRD to determine structural integrity. Batch adsorption experiments were conducted with methylene blue as a model pollutant under varying initial concentrations (50-200 ppm), contact times (up to 60 min), temperatures (20-60°C), and pH levels (2-10). Dynamic mode testing was performed using a fixed column system with a peristaltic pump. Concentration analysis was conducted via UV-VIS spectroscopy at 665 nm.

## Data and Materials

The study compared five different adsorbents for methylene blue removal. Experimental data included measurements of methylene blue (at 75, 100, and 200 ppm), heavy metals (Ni, Cr, and Pb at 25 and 50 ppm), and temperature variations from 293 K to 333 K.

## Key Findings

- Polyethylenimine modified graphene (PG) was identified as the most efficient adsorbent among five tested materials (OAG, PG, EG, DG, HG). ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 19]])
- The PG material exhibited a maximum adsorption capacity (q_m) of 161.29 mg/g for methylene blue. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 19]])
- PG demonstrated high structural stability even after use in the adsorption process, as confirmed by XRD and FTIR analysis. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 52]])
- TGA results showed that PG has excellent thermal stability up to more than 200°C. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 49]])
- The adsorption process for methylene blue on PG follows a two-step mechanism: fast adsorption (first 20 min) and slow adsorption (after 20 min). ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 53]])
- Polyethylenimine modified graphene (PG) showed the highest adsorption capacity for methylene blue among five tested adsorbents, reaching a maximum of 161.29 mg/g. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 65]])
- In a dynamic mode, the removal of all metals (Cr, Pb, Hg, As, Cu) reached more than 90% when the adsorbent dosage was increased to 0.05 g and methylene blue concentration was high. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 60]])
- The adsorption process on PG is primarily physical adsorption, as indicated by a Dubinin-Radushkevich (D-R) model calculation yielding an energy value of 7.071 kJ/mol. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 64]])
- Langmuir isotherm modeling for PG yielded a correlation coefficient of R^2 = 0.9365, indicating monolayer adsorption. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 61]])
- The pseudo-second-order kinetic model provided the best fit for methylene blue adsorption on PG (R² = 0.998 to 0.999) compared to pseudo-first-order (R² = 0.971 to 0.976) and Elovich models. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 72]])
- The adsorption of methylene blue on PG was found to be endothermic, with $\Delta H^\circ$ values between 29.159 kJ/mol and higher (positive values), indicating a physicochemical adsorption process. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 77]])
- The adsorption of methylene blue on PG was spontaneous, as indicated by negative $\Delta G^\circ$ values ranging from -5.548 to -10.206 kJ/mol. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 77]])
- A positive $\Delta S^\circ$ (up to 120.212 J/(mol·K)) indicated that the bonding of methylene blue to the surface of PG is not ionic and can be easily removed for reuse. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 77]])
- The adsorption rate-limiting step was influenced by both intraparticle diffusion and other adsorption processes, as evidenced by Weber's intraparticle diffusion model lines not passing through the origin. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 72]])
- Maximum uptake of methylene blue and metals was achieved at the lowest flow rate of 0.089 mL/s in dynamic adsorption tests. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 60]])
