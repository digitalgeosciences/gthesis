# CARBON NANOSTRUCTURE-BASED MATERIALS FOR REMOVAL OF ORGANIC CONTAMINANTS FROM WASTEWATER

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the synthesis and characterization of functionalized carbon nanostructure-based materials, specifically polyethylenimine modified graphene (PG), for removing organic contaminants like methylene blue from wastewater. The study evaluates the performance of these materials under varying concentrations, temperatures, pH levels, and in dynamic column conditions.

---

## Research Problem

Industrial wastewater contains high levels of toxic organic chemical contaminants (such as dyes) and heavy metals, posing a significant environmental challenge that requires efficient, low-cost removal methods.

## Objectives

- To evaluate different prepared graphene-based materials as adsorbents for the removal of dye pollutants.
- To optimize the adsorption parameters for the best adsorbent.
- To investigate the adsorption data using different related models.
- To study the efficiency of the adsorbent in a dynamic mode.
- Design and synthesize functionalized carbon nanostructure-based materials including graphene and polymer/graphene.
- Characterize the developed materials using state-of-the-art instrumentation (TGA, FTIR, XRD, SEM).
- Evaluate the adsorption capacity of modified graphene for methylene blue as a model pollutant.
- Investigate the impact of concentration, temperature, pH, and metal ions on the adsorption process.
- Characterize the developed materials using state-of-the-art instrumentation.
- Evaluate the adsorption performance of PG for methylene blue and heavy metals under various conditions (concentration, pH, temperature).
- Study the kinetics, thermodynamics, and dynamic behavior of the adsorption process.
- Design and synthesize functionalized carbon nanostructure-based materials including graphene and polymer/graphene
- Characterize the developed materials using state-of-the-art instrumentation
- Evaluate the removal of methylene blue in both batch and dynamic modes
- Characterize the developed materials using state-of-the-art instrumentation to evaluate their properties for contaminant removal

## Methods

The research involved synthesizing functionalized carbon nanostructures (graphene and polymer/graphene). Characterization was performed using SEM, TEM, FTIR, TGA, and XRD. Batch adsorption experiments were conducted to evaluate removal of methylene blue and heavy metals under varying conditions: initial concentration, contact time, temperature, pH, and adsorbent dosage. Equilibrium isotherms (Langmuir, Freundlich, Temkin, Scatchard, Dubinin-Radushkevich) and kinetic models (pseudo-first-order, pseudo-second-order, intraparticle diffusion, Elovich, and Yoon-Nelson) were applied to analyze the data. Dynamic adsorption was tested using a fixed column system with real wastewater mixed with contaminants.

## Data and Materials

Samples included 5 types of modified graphene; methylene blue was used as the model pollutant at concentrations of 50, 75, 100, and 200 ppm. Batch experiments were conducted for metals (Cr, Pb, Hg, As) at 1 ppm concentration. A fixed column system with dimensions 14x2x1.2 cm was used for dynamic studies.

## Key Findings

- Polyethylenimine modified graphene (PG) was found to have the highest efficiency in adsorbing methylene blue among the tested materials. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 18]])
- The PG material exhibited a maximum adsorption capacity (qm) of 161.29 mg/g for methylene blue. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 19]])
- PG can remove methylene blue and metals simultaneously and perform well across a wide range of temperatures and pH values. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 19]])
- The PG material demonstrated excellent structural stability even after use in the adsorption process. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 19]])
- PG performed well in dynamic mode, which is suitable for treating larger volumes of wastewater. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 19]])
- Polyethylenimine modified graphene (PG) exhibited a flake structure morphology as confirmed by SEM imaging. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 48]])
- The prepared PG material demonstrated excellent thermal stability up to more than 200 °C, exceeding the required 100 °C for industrial applications. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 49]])
- Methylene blue was identified as having a maximum absorption at a wavelength of 665 nm. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 46]])
- FTIR analysis identified key functional groups on the PG surface, including O-H (3400 cm-1), C-H (2950, 2920, 2860 cm-1), epoxide (1070 cm-1), and carbonyl (1720 cm-1). ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 50]])
- XRD patterns confirmed the structural stability of PG before and after use, with peaks at 2θ of approximately 11.2°, 25.6°, and 54.3° corresponding to (001), (002), and (004) planes. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 51]])
- The adsorption process for methylene blue followed a two-step mechanism: fast adsorption in the first 20 minutes and slow adsorption thereafter due to steric hindrance. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 53]])
- Adsorption capacity (qe) increased with initial concentration, reaching values of 147.32 mg/g for 75 ppm and 153.78 mg/g for 200 ppm within 60 minutes. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 53]])
- The highest adsorption amount for methylene blue was achieved at pH 10. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 57]])
- A competition between methylene blue and metals for active sites was observed; when methylene blue concentration was low, metals occupied more sites, reducing their removal percentage. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 58]])
- The Langmuir model provided the best fit for adsorption isotherms (R2 = 0.9365), indicating monolayer adsorption with a capacity of 161.29 mg/g. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 61]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

The author recommends larger-scale tests using pilot-scale combined adsorption columns and testing the materials under real industrial wastewater conditions. Long-term experiments are also recommended to ensure durability under continuous pressure and high temperatures.
