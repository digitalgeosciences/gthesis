# STRUCTURAL, STRATIGRAPHIC EVOLUTION AND RESERVOIR CHARACTERISTICS OF ABU-SUFYAN SUB-BASIN, MUGLAD RIFT BASIN, SUDAN

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the structural, stratigraphic evolution, and reservoir characteristics of the Sufyan Sub-basin within the Muglad Rift Basin in Sudan. It utilizes 2D/3D seismic data, well logs, and core analysis to characterize the Abu Gabra Formation as a primary source rock and its associated reservoirs, while modeling the basin's development as a transtensional pull-apart system influenced by the Central African Shear Zone.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the complex structural evolution and reservoir potential of the Sufyan Sub-basin (a part of the Muglad Rift Basin) to better understand its role as a hydrocarbon system, specifically focusing on the Abu Gabra Formation's role as a source rock and associated sedimentary units as reservoirs.

## Methods

The study employs a multi-disciplinary approach: (1) Structural analysis using 2D/3D seismic data, gravity surveys, and structural restoration in Move software to quantify extension and identify fault mechanisms. (2) Sedimentology and reservoir characterization via core analysis, wireline logs (GR, sonic, density, neutron), and thin-section petrography, SEM, and XRD to determine mineralogy and diagenesis. (3) Sequence stratigraphy using seismic facies analysis and synthetic seismograms to identify mfs and sequence boundaries. (4) 3D geostatistical modeling in Petrel using Sequential Indicator Simulation (SIS) for lithofacies and Sequential Gaussian Simulation (SGS) for porosity and shale volume.

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes 3D seismic data for structural mapping, wireline logs from at least 11 wells in the area, core samples from the Abu Gabra Formation, and mud log data. Specific focus is placed on the sedimentary record of the syn-rift and rift climax stages.

## Key Findings

- The Abu Gabra Formation contains lacustrine shales as a potential source rock and fluvio-deltaic sandstones as primary reservoir rocks. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 1]])
- The Sufyan Sub-basin is an East-West trending sub-basin in the northwestern part of the Muglad Basin, located in the eastern extension of the West and Central Africa Rift System (WCARS). ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 1]])
- The study identified four main depositional systems: fan-delta, braided delta, lacustrine system, and sublacustrine fan. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 8]])
- Petrographic analysis of sandstones revealed detrital components (WS, MS, PS) and authigenic minerals/cements. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 12]])
- Core porosity ranges were documented for the sandstones in the Abu Gabra Formation. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 12]])
- The Sufyan Sub-basin is a pull-apart basin influenced by both the Central African Fault Zone (CASZ) and the Muglad Basin extension. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 38]])
- The Abu Gabra Formation contains fluvial-deltaic sandstones as primary reservoir rocks, with distinct lithofacies including braided delta and fan-delta systems. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 100]])
- Porosity is significantly impacted by clay minerals; chlorite and kaolinite were identified as major pore-filling minerals in the Abu Gabra sandstones. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 161]])

- The sedimentary sequence of the Abu Gabra Formation is divided into three main stages: early syn-rift (alluvial fan, fluvial), rift climax (lacustrine/sublacustrine), and late syn-rift (braided delta, fan delta). ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 100]])
- The study identified four distinct zones in the 3D model; Zone 1 is dominated by channel fills (66.12%) and floodplain shale (25.46%). ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 204]])
- Zone 2 is characterized by a dominance of distributary channels (75.36%) and floodplain shales (18.48%). ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 204]])
- The sedimentary thickness of key facies like channel fills, crevasses splay, and floodplain shale in Zones 1 and 2 are predominantly less than 10m. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 206]])
- The Sufyan Sub-basin has a unique East-West orientation, differing from the general NW-SE trend of the Muglad Basin. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 23]])
- Source rock for hydrocarbons is believed to be the lacustrine shale of the Abu Gabra Formation. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 33]])

## Limitations

The geographic representation of sequence SQ-A remains uncertain as it was only penetrated at the basin margins; however, it is considered equivalent to SQ-A in the Fula Sub-basin based on seismic data.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Apply 3D structural restoration in the study area.
- Acquire conventional core in other reservoir sections such as sublacustrine fan deposits.
- Acquire high-resolution 3D seismic.
- Reprocess available 3D seismic data (pre-stack time/depth migration).
- Use seismic inversion (acoustic impedance) as a guide for porosity distribution.
