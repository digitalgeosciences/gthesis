# STRUCTURAL, STRATIGRAPHIC EVOLUTION AND RESERVOIR CHARACTERISTICS OF ABU-SUFYAN SUB-BASIN, MUGLAD RIFT BASIN, SUDAN

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the structural, stratigraphic evolution, and reservoir characteristics of the Sufyan Sub-basin within the Muglad Rift Basin in Sudan. It utilizes 2D/3D seismic data, well logs, and core analysis to model the basin's development through three distinct rift cycles and evaluate its potential as a hydrocarbon source and reservoir.

---

## Research Problem

The complex structural setting and depositional systems in the Sufyan Sub-basin are poorly understood, requiring detailed study of how tectonic evolution (specifically strike-slip and pull-apart mechanisms) influenced the distribution of sedimentary facies and reservoir quality within the Abu Gabra Formation.

## Objectives

- To characterize the structural evolution of the Sufyan Sub-basin using 2D/3D seismic data and gravity surveys.
- Identify the possible causes of the unique trend of Sufyan Sub-basin, and the possible relationship with the Central African Shear Zone (CASZ).
- Identify the forming mechanism model for Sufyan Sub-basin.
- Characterize the sedimentary facies associations, depositional environment and establish sequence stratigraphic framework.
- Construct models link between the structure and their stratigraphic units under the regional tectonic framework.
- Generate 3D Geostatistical model including the structural, faices, and properties models.

## Methods

The study employed a multi-disciplinary approach: 1) Structural analysis using 2D/3D seismic data, gravity surveys (Bouguer anomaly), and well logs to map faults and horizons. 2) Structural restoration using the 'Move' software with the inclined shear algorithm to determine extension percentages and stretch factors. 3) Sedimentology and reservoir characterization via core analysis, wireline logs (GR, RHOB, NPHI), and petrography (thin sections, SEM-EDS, XRD). 4) Sequence stratigraphy based on seismic facies analysis and well log correlation to identify MFS and sequence boundaries. 5) 3D geostatistical modeling in Petrel using Sequential Indicator Simulation (SIS) for lithofacies and Sequential Gaussian Simulation (SGS) for porosity and shale volume.

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes core samples from the Abu Gabra Formation, well logs (Gamma Ray) from multiple wells in the Sufyan Sub-basin, and 2D seismic data for structural mapping and stratigraphic correlation. The analysis covers three rift cycles: Early Cretaceous, Late Cretaceous, and Paleogene.

## Key Findings

- The Sufyan Sub-basin is identified as a dextral pull-apart basin influenced by both the Central African Shear Zone (CASZ) and Muglad Basin extension. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 90]])
- Source rock for hydrocarbons in the area is believed to be the lacustrine shale of the Abu Gabra Formation. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 33]])
- The primary reservoir rocks are fluvio-deltaic sandstones within the Abu Gabra Formation. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 33]])
- Four types of depositional systems were identified: braided delta, fan delta, sublacustrine fan, and lacustrine systems. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 24]])
- The Abu Gabra Formation is categorized as a 2nd order sequence and divided into five 3rd order sequences. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 24]])
- Hydrocarbon reservoirs are concentrated in underwater distributary channels, distal bar, and sheet sands dominated in the delta front on structural highs produced by strike-slip movements of NW-SE-striking faults. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 23]])
- The study identified a distinct E-W trend for Sufyan Sub-basin, differing from the general NW-SE trend of the Muglad Basin. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 23]])
- Porosity values in the Abu Gabra Formation reached up to 0.32 in certain zones. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 231]])
- The study identified a high probability of channel fills sand (66.12%) and floodplain shale (25.46%) in zone-1. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 204]])
- The Abu Gabra Formation contains lacustrine shales with TOC ranging from 1.45 to 5.15 wt% (average 2.84 wt%), qualifying it as a good to excellent source rock. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 64]])
- The first rift cycle produced the Abu Gabra Formation, which contains both the primary source rock and the main reservoir in the north-western Muglad Basin. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 45]])
- The Bentiu Formation is characterized by high sedimentation rates resulting in a thick vertical section of stacked channel and bar deposits (braided/meandering). ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 48]])
- The Darfur Group includes the Aradeiba, Zarqa, and Ghazal formations; the Aradeiba Formation acts as a major hydrocarbon seal. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 48]])
- Structural restoration of regional line A-A' yielded an extension of approximately 38.3% at the top of the basement with a stretch factor of 1.38. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 72]])
- The middle part of the basin shows higher expansion, with extension percentages ranging from 41.39% to 48.39% and stretch factors between 1.41 and 1.49. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 72]])

## Limitations

The calculation of extensional strains for some sections did not account for minor compressional strain during later basin inversion.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Apply 3D structural restoration in the study area.
- Acquire conventional core in other reservoir sections such as in sublacustrine fan deposits.
- Acquire high-resolution 3D seismic and reprocess existing data (pre-stack time/depth migration).
- Use seismic inversion (acoustic impedance) as a guide for porosity distribution.
