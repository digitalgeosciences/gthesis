# PREDICTION OF WATER SATURATION FROM WELL LOGS USING ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TECHNIQUES

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the use of machine learning, specifically Artificial Neural Networks (ANN), to predict water saturation from well logs in the Prinos Basin, Greece. It compares ANN performance against traditional petrophysical models like Archie's equation and finds that ANN provides high accuracy with low error rates.

---

## Research Problem

Traditional petrophysical models for estimating water saturation (e.g., Archie, Simandoux) often rely on parameters that are not readily available or require costly experimental analysis, leading to potential errors in reservoir evaluation.

## Objectives

- Evaluate the capability of machine learning techniques (ANN, ANFIS, FN, SVM, and RF) to predict water saturation from well logs.
- Develop an optimized ANN model for predicting water saturation using parameters like Vshale, porosity, permeability, and resistivity.
- Extract a predictive empirical equation from the trained ANN model.

## Methods

The study utilized 364 data points (Vshale, porosity, permeability, and deep resistivity) to train an ANN. The methodology included data preprocessing (removing non-real values and outliers), followed by optimizing hyperparameters such as training functions (Bayesian regularization backpropagation), activation functions (Logarithmic sigmoidal), and network architecture (single hidden layer with 5 neurons). A validation process was conducted using a separate dataset from Well no. 2 to test the model's robustness.

## Data and Materials

364 data points from Well-1 in the Prinos Basin, Greece; additional validation data from Well-2; parameters included Vshale (0.012–0.852), Porosity (0.005–0.173), Permeability (0.0003–13.86 mD), and Resistivity (2.537–19.63 Ohm.m).

## Key Findings

- The ANN model achieved a correlation coefficient (R) of 0.999 for both training and testing data. ([[2023-gad|Gad, 2023, p. 40]])
- The Average Absolute Percent Error (AAPE) was 0.90% for training and 1.08% for testing data. ([[2023-gad|Gad, 2023, p. 40]])
- The optimized ANN model used a single hidden layer with five neurons, utilizing the Bayesian regularization backpropagation function and Logarithmic sigmoidal activation. ([[2023-gad|Gad, 2023, p. 35]])
- An empirical correlation derived from the ANN model yielded an R value of 0.91 and an AAPE of 6% when validated with unseen data. ([[2023-gad|Gad, 2023, p. 42]])
- The study confirmed that machine learning can predict water saturation from conventional logs (NPHI, Resistivity, GR) with high accuracy. ([[2023-gad|Gad, 2023, p. 42]])

## Limitations

Traditional models are limited by the lack of available parameters like Rw or specific constants (a, m, n), and the requirement for costly core analysis.
