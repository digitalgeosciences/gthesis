# An Integrated Study to Characterize and Model Strata with Biogenically Influenced Flow Media in the Aruma Formation, Central Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the Aruma Formation in central Saudi Arabia, specifically focusing on how bioturbated strata with large Thalassinoides burrows influence reservoir and aquifer properties. Using a combination of field observations, petrography, XRD/XRF analysis, and 3D imaging (GPR and CT), the study identifies three distinct biogenically influenced fluid flow media (BIFM) based on burrow morphology and filling.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the difficulty of characterizing large, three-dimensional burrows (cm-scale) in carbonate reservoirs using standard sampling methods, which often fail to capture full burrow connectivity and volume. Specifically, it explores how these biogenic features influence fracture initiation, propagation, and overall reservoir quality in the Aruma Formation.

## Methods

The study employed a multi-method approach: 1) Field observations and photography of the Aruma Formation outcrops, followed by image processing using ImageJ and JMicrovision to quantify burrow attributes. 2) Laboratory analysis of rock samples using a medical CT scanner (Toshiba Alexion TSX-032A) to provide high-resolution 3D views of burrow connectivity. 3) Ground Penetrating Radar (GPR) surveys using a 2.7 GHz antenna over a 1m x 1m area, with data processed via ReflexW (including dewow, bandpass Butterworth, running average, and background removal filters). 4) Data integration into 3D models using MATLAB for resampling and normalization, followed by visualization in Voxler and Petrel.

## Data and Materials

The study utilized data from the Khanasir Member of the Aruma Formation, including: 28 cores for porosity and permeability measurements; multiple core samples for CT scanning; various outcrop sections for GPR surveys; and a suite of mineralogical analyses (XRD/XRF) on both host rock matrix and burrow fillings.

## Key Findings

- Burrows in the Khanasir Member can reach up to 8 cm in diameter, providing significant volume for potential flow paths. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 12]])
- The host rock matrix of unit 1 and unit 2 shows distinct porosity types: channel, vug, moldic, shelter, and intercrystal. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 44]])
- Burrow filling in unit 2A is characterized as micritic (nodular limestone) while the matrix is dolomite. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 34]])
- GPR was used to evaluate burrow percentage across X, Y, and Z axes, showing significant differences in distribution. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 11]])
- Fracture density is higher in areas where elongated, irregularly shaped burrows are affected by dissolution. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 60]])
- Burrows in the Aruma Formation were identified as three types: open, compacted and open, or filled with carbonate sediments, each representing unique fluid flow media. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 20]])
- GPR data provided information on burrow attributes (morphology, size, and percentage) that are difficult to obtain from CT scans or outcrop photographs due to the limitations of 2D views or small sample volumes. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 20]])
- Fracture characteristics were influenced by factors including burrow fillings, matrix mineralogy, and burrow percentage, confirming the impact of bioturbation on fracture networks. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 20]])
- Burrow diameters in the field reached up to 8 cm, which can create super-permeability zones in carbonate reservoirs. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 19]])
- A higher percentage of burrows tends to correlate with increased fracture densities where the burrows show greater brittleness. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 69]])
- GPR survey data for burrow percentage showed a mean value of approximately 18% (standard deviation ~6.0%) in certain sections. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 113]])
- CT scan analysis of hand samples showed a mean burrow percentage of approximately 13% (standard deviation ~5.0%). ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 113]])
- Unit 1 (approx. 18m thick) contains burrows with a burrow percentage (BP) of 10% to 41%, often filled with terra rossa soil (51.8% calcite, 33.4% quartz, and 14.7% palygorskite). ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 42]])
- The host rock matrix of unit 1 is composed of 100% calcite. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 42]])
- Unit 2 (approx. 17m thick) contains Thalassinoides burrows with a burrow percentage of 25-45%. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 45]])

## Limitations

The study identifies a limitation in the knowledge of intra-burrow porosity extent beyond exposed surfaces, suggesting that further research via wide diameter coreholes is needed to understand connectivity and bulk permeability.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Future work should include: 1) investigating why burrows with open shafts remain open, 2) determining the origin of dolomite in the host rock matrix of unit 2, 3) conducting high-resolution ground-penetrating radar (GPR) surveys on sub-unit tops, and 4) characterizing acoustic properties of bioturbated strata using various representative elementary volumes.
