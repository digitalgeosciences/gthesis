# THE PETROPHYSICAL IMPLICATIONS OF STROMATOLITES STRUCTURES ON RESERVOIR QUALITY OF THE DAM FORMATION, SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the relationship between stromatolite morphology, stratigraphic context, and petrophysical properties in the Miocene-aged Dam Formation of Saudi Arabia. By comparing samples above and below a sequence boundary (SB), it concludes that different depositional environments and diagenetic processes lead to distinct pore systems (vuggy vs. interparticle/moldic/microporosity) which significantly impact reservoir quality.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of thorough investigation into how different stromatolite morphologies (e.g., columnar vs. laterally linked) specifically impact petrophysical properties like porosity, permeability, and pore system architecture in carbonate reservoirs, specifically within the Dam Formation of Saudi Arabia.

## Objectives

- Evaluate the relationship between stromatolite morphology and petrophysical properties.
- Analyze the influence of stratigraphic context (above/below a sequence boundary) on stromatolite characteristics.
- Characterize the pore system architecture and laminae in both columnar (CF) and laterally linked (LL) stromatolites.
- Evaluate whether stromatolites affect petrophysical properties differently based on their forms (columnar vs. laterally linked).
- Determine if petrophysical properties of the same stromatolite morphology vary relative to their stratigraphic position across a major sequence boundary.
- Investigate the relationship between stromatolite morphology, stratigraphic context, and petrophysical properties in carbonate strata of the Dam Formation.
- Characterize the pore systems and mineralogy of columnar (CF) and laterally linked (LL) stromatolites.
- Evaluate the impact of pore size distribution on permeability variability using NMR data.
- Investigate the relationship between stromatolite morphology, stratigraphic context, and petrophysical properties in carbonate strata.
- Analyze the differences in porosity, permeability, and pore system architecture of stromatolites above and below a sequence boundary (SB).
- Evaluate how different stromatolite forms (columnar, laterally linked, transported) impact reservoir quality.
- Compare the pore systems of columnar stromatolites above and below a sequence boundary (SB).
- Evaluate the impact of mineralogy on petrophysical variations.
- Determine how different depositional environments influence reservoir quality.
- Investigate the relationship between stromatolite morphology and petrophysical properties.
- Evaluate the impact of stratigraphic context on stromatolite-related reservoir quality.
- Compare petrophysical properties of different stromatolite forms (columnar vs. horizontally-linked).
- Assess how diagenetic processes influence the pore architecture of stromatolites.
- Analyze core plug samples from different stromatolite types (columnar vs. laterally linked) to evaluate their impact on reservoir quality.

## Methods

The study involved field observations to identify and describe stromatolite forms (Columnar, Laterally Linked, Transported) in the Lidam area. 120 core plugs were sampled: 40 from columnar stromatolites (CF) above/below SB, 20 from laterally linked (LL), and 60 others including transported forms. Laboratory analysis included thin-section petrography using AxioScan system and ZEISS ZEN 3.8 software for pore system characterization. Petrophysical measurements were conducted on core plugs using a CMS-300TM system for porosity and permeability, and a Proceq Pundit PL-200 for ultrasonic velocity (P-wave) measurement.

## Data and Materials

120 total samples analyzed: 40 from columnar stromatolites (CF), 20 from laterally linked (LL) stromatolites, and additional samples from transported forms. Samples were categorized by stratigraphic position relative to the sequence boundary (SB) and orientation of core plug cuts (horizontal vs. vertical).

## Key Findings

- Porosity for CF forms ranged from 33.93% to 48.04%, while LL forms ranged from 32.28% to 54.90%. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 17]])
- Permeability for CF stromatolites varied from 1.38 mD to 1900.50 mD, while LL stromatolites ranged from 13.31 mD to 2017.78 mD. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 17]])
- CF stromatolite form shows a much stronger power-law correlation with porosity multiplied by T2 LM (R^2 = 0.86) compared to LL forms (R^2 = 0.03). ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 40]])
- Above the SB, stromatolites in transgressive system tracts show higher permeability due to grain-dominated sediments deposited during high-energy events. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 17]])
- CF stromatolite form shows a bimodal distribution of T2 (mesopores and macropores), while LL forms exhibit a mix of bimodal and unimodal distributions. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 31]])
- Petrophysical properties like permeability show high variability in CF forms, with values differing by two orders of magnitude for samples with similar porosity. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 41]])
- Both CF and LL stromatolite forms are classified as coarse-grained stromatolites with alternating dense, micrite-rich laminae and highly porous, grain-rich laminae (ooids, peloids, skeletal grains, and quartz). ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 30]])
- Porosity ranges from 33.93% to 48.04% for CF stromatolites and 32.28% to 54.90% for LL stromatolites. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 19]])
- Permeability in CF stromatolites ranges from 1.38 mD to 1900.50 mD, while LL stromatolites range from 13.31 mD to 2017.78 mD. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 19]])
- LL stromatolites exhibit less variable permeability compared to the highly variable CF forms despite similar porosity ranges. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 30]])
- Petrographic and NMR analyses alone could not fully explain the observed permeability variability, suggesting that pore connectivity and laminae orientation are critical factors. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 19]])
- CF stromatolites were found to be associated with high-energy water facies like laminated and cross-bedded oolitic grainstones. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 38]])
- Both CF and LL stromatolite forms are dominated by coarse-grained carbonate, consisting of grain-rich and micrite-rich laminae. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 41]])
- Micrite-rich laminae in both forms are typically 1 to 2 mm thick and contain sinuous, continuous biofilms at the top of each lamina. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 45]])
- The CF form is dominated by interparticle, intraparticle, and moldic porosity, while the LL form shows a dominance of vugs and fenestral porosity. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 48]])

## Limitations

The study is limited geographically and temporally to the Miocene Dam Formation in eastern Saudi Arabia. Additionally, it is difficult to distinguish between primary depositional features and secondary diagenetic alterations (e.g., vuggy porosity) without further geochemical analysis.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Future research should adopt an integrated approach using advanced imaging techniques like high-resolution CT scanning combined with petrophysical measurements (NMR and petrography) to better capture the spatial configuration of laminae and pore systems.
