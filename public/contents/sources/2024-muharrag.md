# THE PETROPHYSICAL IMPLICATIONS OF STROMATOLITES STRUCTURES ON RESERVOIR QUALITY OF THE DAM FORMATION, SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the relationship between stromatolite morphology (columnar and laterally linked), stratigraphic context, and petrophysical properties in the Miocene-aged Dam Formation of Saudi Arabia. Using a combination of field observations, thin-section petrography, and Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR) on 60 core samples, it concludes that while both stromatolite forms exhibit high porosity but wide permeability ranges, their specific morphologies and stratigraphic positions significantly influence pore system architecture and fluid flow behavior.

---

## Research Problem

The impact of stromatolite morphology and its associated sedimentary structures on petrophysical properties (porosity, permeability, and pore systems) in carbonate reservoirs is poorly understood, particularly regarding how these features vary across different morphological forms (e.g., columnar vs. laterally linked) and stratigraphic positions.

## Objectives

- Evaluate whether stromatolites affect petrophysical properties differently based on their forms (columnar vs. laterally linked).
- Determine if the petrophysical properties of the same stromatolite morphology vary relative to their stratigraphic position across a major sequence boundary.
- Investigate the relationship between stromatolite morphology, stratigraphic context, and petrophysical properties in carbonate strata.
- Analyze the impact of different stromatolite forms (CF and LL) on porosity and permeability distribution.
- Evaluate the role of pore size distribution (via NMR) and rock texture in explaining permeability variations.
- Determine the influence of the spatial arrangement of stromatolite laminae on fluid flow dynamics.

## Methods

The study involved field investigation at the Al-Lidam outcrop site to identify and sample stromatolite morphologies (CF and LL) based on size, orientation, and surrounding matrix. Laboratory analysis included core drilling of 1-inch diameter plugs, non-destructive CT scanning for internal structure evaluation, NMR testing using an Oxford GeoSpec+ system to analyze pore systems, and permeability/porosity measurements via a CMS-300 pressure decay system. Petrographic analysis was performed on thin sections prepared with blue-dyed epoxy to identify mineralogy (via XRD) and microfacies.

## Data and Materials

60 core plug samples in total: 40 from stromatolite forms (20 CF, 20 LL) collected above and below a sequence boundary, and 20 additional samples for specific comparative analysis. Samples were analyzed using NMR, petrography, XRD, and physical property measurements.

## Key Findings

- Porosity values for CF stromatolites ranged from 33.93% to 48.04%, while LL stromatolites showed a range of 32.28% to 54.90%. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 17]])
- Permeability for CF stromatolites ranged from 1.38 mD to 1900.50 mD, while LL stromatolites showed a range of 13.31 mD to 2017.78 mD. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 17]])
- LL stromatolite forms displayed less variability in permeability compared to the highly variable CF forms despite similar porosity ranges. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 17]])
- Petrography revealed that both CF and LL forms are dominated by coarse-grained carbonate with alternating grain-rich (ooids, peloids, quartz) and micrite-rich laminae. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 29]])
- Fenestral and vuggy porosity were identified in both micrite-rich and grain-rich laminae across both stromatolite forms. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 48]])
- NMR data for CF stromatolites showed a bimodal distribution (peaks at 0-100 ms and 100-1000+ ms), while LL forms showed a mix of bimodal and wide unimodal distributions. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 50]])
- Group 1 (CF) was dominated by micropores and mesopores, while Group 2 (CF) had higher relative abundance of macropores. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 53]])
- Petrography showed that micrite-rich laminae in CF forms are often tightly cemented, resulting in reduced pore spaces compared to LL forms. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 48]])
- The LL stromatolite form exhibits significantly lower incremental porosity for micropores and mesopores compared to the CF form, with a bimodal distribution in CF indicating distinct pore systems. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 54]])
- In the CF form, mean porosity is 40.86% (SD 3.74%), while the LL form has a higher mean porosity of 44.84% (SD 5.90%). ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 55]])
- The CF form shows a much wider permeability range (1.38 mD to 1900.50 mD) compared to the LL form, which is more homogeneous but lower on average. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 55]])
- No correlation was found between porosity and permeability for either stromatolite form. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 55]])
- NMR data showed a strong power-law correlation with permeability (R2 = 0.86) for the CF form, but no significant correlation (R2 = 0.03) for the LL form. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 59]])
- Samples B2 and B17 in the CF form had nearly identical porosity and NMR characteristics but differed by two orders of magnitude in permeability (3.1 mD vs 117.0 mD). ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 59]])
- Sample D11 and D3 in the LL form showed similar NMR values but different permeability (132 mD vs 2017 mD), with the higher porosity sample having lower permeability. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 62]])

## Limitations

The study is limited geographically and temporally to the Miocene Dam Formation in eastern Saudi Arabia; results may not generalize to other geological settings (e.g., Precambrian or modern stromatolites). Additionally, some petrophysical complexities might be obscured by diagenetic overprinting.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Future research should investigate specific factors controlling stromatolite development in the Dam Formation more deeply and consider the roles of pore connectivity and laminae orientation in determining permeability.
