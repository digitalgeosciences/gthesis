# A paleomagnetic study of central Harrat Rahat, Western Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis presents a paleomagnetic study of basaltic flows in the central Harrat Rahat area of western Saudi Arabia. It utilizes thermal and alternating-field demagnetization to isolate characteristic remanent magnetization (ChRM) from 23 sites across several lithostratigraphic units to determine mean pole positions.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of adequate paleomagnetic data in Saudi Arabia, specifically regarding the tectonic history of the Red Sea and Arabian Shield regions, necessitates a study of basaltic flows in the central Harrat Rahat area to better understand volcanic activities and regional geology.

## Methods

Paleomagnetic analysis was conducted on 23 sites across several lithostratigraphic units. Magnetic cleaning was performed to isolate ChRM, followed by Fisherian averaging of site mean directions. The modified Thellier-Thellier method (1959) was used for paleo-intensity measurements, involving heating samples in a series of temperature steps from 30°C to 600°C in both zero and applied fields (0.5 Oe). Microscopic study of polished sections was performed using a Carl Zeiss D-7082 Oberkochen microscope to identify titanomagnetite and ilmenite oxidation stages.

## Data and Materials

The study utilized 23 site mean directions from 15 reversed and 8 normal polarity sites. A total of 92 NRM-TRM curves were analyzed from samples collected from several locations (Madinah, Hammah, Shawahit) within the central Harrat Rahat area.

## Limitations

The majority of NRM-TRM curves deviated from a straight line so much that even a rough estimate of paleo-intensity could not be made for most samples. It was noted that non-ideal behavior in the Thellier method may be due to chemical or mineral alteration, requiring further investigation into specific sources of such behavior.

## Recommendations and Future Work

More paleomagnetic studies from carefully dated rocks from Cenozoic volcanic flows are needed to establish a reliable Cenozoic pole for Arabia. It is suggested to develop petrological, mineralogical, and magnetic criteria to select specimens with minimum secondary remanence and higher oxidation levels (titanomagnetite) for paleo-intensity experiments.
