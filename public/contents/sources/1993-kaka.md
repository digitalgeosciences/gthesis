# A paleomagnetic study of central Harrat Rahat, Western Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the paleomagnetic properties of Cenozoic basaltic flows in the central Harrat Rahat region of western Saudi Arabia. It utilizes thermal and alternating-field demagnetization to determine magnetic directions and intensities, ultimately providing data on the Arabian plate's movement and its relationship with the Red Sea rift.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the lack of reliable paleomagnetic data for the Arabian plate during the Neogene and Quaternary, specifically aiming to establish a stable pole position to understand the rotation history of Arabia relative to Africa and the timing of Red Sea rifting.

## Objectives

- To determine paleomagnetic directions from suitable samples of these basaltic flows, using standard paleomagnetic sampling techniques.
- To investigate paleo-intensities of the geomagnetic field by the Thellier's method from suitable samples.
- To study the opaque mineralogy of magnetic carriers of the rocks by reflected light microscope.
- To investigate the relationship between ideal behavior in the Thellier's experiments and the magnetic mineralogy of the samples.
- Determine the characteristic remanent magnetization (ChRM) from various basaltic samples in central Harrat Rahat.
- Identify and remove secondary magnetic components through thermal and alternating field demagnetization.

## Methods

The study employed thermal demagnetization (up to 600°C) and alternating field (AF) demagnetization to isolate ChRM. Principal Component Analysis (PCA), implemented via the BASIC program, was used to determine the best-fit line for magnetization vectors. Fisher statistics were applied to calculate mean pole positions from site-mean directions. Petrographic analysis using a Carl Zeiss D-7082 microscope was conducted to evaluate the mineralogy of titanomagnetite and ilmenite.

## Data and Materials

The study analyzed 23 sites across three lithostratigraphic units: Madinah Basalt (13 sites), Hammah Basalt, and Shawahit Basalt. A total of 92 NRM-TRM curves (Arai diagrams) and corresponding vector diagrams were plotted for the samples.

## Key Findings

- Mean direction of magnetization has an inclination of 38.5 and declination of 353.2 with a 95% confidence level (95%) of 5.4. ([[1993-kaka|Kaka, 1993, p. 67]])
- The Late Pliocene-Pleistocene pole position is at Lat. 86.74°N, long. 345.49°E. ([[1993-kaka|Kaka, 1993, p. 18]])
- Apparent polar wander path for Arabia shows divergence for Late Oligocene to Pleistocene time that is in good agreement with the opening of the Red Sea. ([[1993-kaka|Kaka, 1993, p. 18]])
- A total of 92 NRM-TRM curves were determined by Thellier's method from all 23 sites; most deviated significantly from a straight line, making reliable paleo-intensity determination difficult for many. ([[1993-kaka|Kaka, 1993, p. 18]])
- Samples with more oxidized titanomagnetite minerals are less susceptible to further alteration during heating. ([[1993-kaka|Kaka, 1993, p. 18]])
- The Shawahit Group comprises about 70 percent of the total volume of Harrat Rahat and is composed of OTB and AOB. ([[1993-kaka|Kaka, 1993, p. 30]])
- The Madina Basalt is divided into seven subunits (Qm1 to Qm7) based on increasing degrees of erosion. ([[1993-kaka|Kaka, 1993, p. 32]])
- The average thickness of the Harrat Rahat lava field was determined geophysically to be approximately 100 meters for the southern portion. ([[1993-kaka|Kaka, 1993, p. 28]])
- The mean direction of magnetization for the Pliocene-Pleistocene period was determined to have an inclination of 38.5° and a declination of 353.2° with a 95% confidence interval of 5.4°. ([[1993-kaka|Kaka, 1993, p. 64]])
- The calculated paleomagnetic pole for central Harrat Rahat is located at latitude 86.74° N and longitude 345.49° E. ([[1993-kaka|Kaka, 1993, p. 64]])
- Comparison of ChRM directions between Madinah and Hammah & Shawahit groups showed no significant difference at the 95% confidence level, indicating they are from a similar age (less than 3 Ma). ([[1993-kaka|Kaka, 1993, p. 63]])
- The study identified three types of remanence: primary TRM, TRM overprinted by weak/moderate IRM, and TRM overprinted by strong secondary remanence. ([[1993-kaka|Kaka, 1993, p. 55]])
- Titanomagnetite grains in Madinah Basalts were found to be at the class 2 stage of oxidation (limited ilmenite exsolution). ([[1993-kaka|Kaka, 1993, p. 90]])
- Hammah Basalt samples showed a trellis texture of ilmenite, indicating a class 3 stage of oxidation. ([[1993-kaka|Kaka, 1993, p. 91]])
- Shawahit Basalt samples exhibited 'meta-ilmenite' (mottled/lighter color), corresponding to the class 4 stage of oxidation. ([[1993-kaka|Kaka, 1993, p. 92]])

## Limitations

The study notes that many NRM-TRM curves deviated from a straight line, making it impossible to provide accurate paleo-intensity estimates for most samples due to secondary magnetization or non-linear TRM acquisition.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The author suggests more paleomagnetic studies on dated rocks from Cenozoic volcanic flows are needed to better determine the timing and extent of Red Sea rifting. Additionally, petrological and mineralogical criteria should be developed to select samples specifically suitable for paleo-intensity experiments.
