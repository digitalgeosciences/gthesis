# DEVELOPMENT AND TESTING OF AN ARTIFICIAL NEURAL NETWORK MODEL FOR PREDICTING BOTTOMHOLE PRESSURE IN VERTICAL MULTIPHASE FLOW

**Summary**: The thesis develops and tests an artificial neural network (ANN) model using the back-propagation algorithm to predict flowing bottomhole pressure in vertical multiphase wells. The study compares the ANN's performance against several established empirical correlations and mechanistic models, concluding that the ANN provides superior accuracy and consistency.

---

## Research Problem

The accurate prediction of flowing bottomhole pressure in vertical multiphase wells is challenging for existing empirical correlations and mechanistic models due to complex fluid behaviors. This research addresses the need for a more robust predictive tool using machine learning techniques.

## Objectives

- To construct an ANN model for predicting pressure drop in vertical multiphase flow.
- To test the constructed model against actual field data.
- To compare the developed model against the best available empirical correlations and mechanistic models.
- Develop an artificial neural network (ANN) model to predict flowing bottomhole pressure in vertical wells.
- Evaluate the performance of the ANN model against existing empirical correlations and mechanistic models.
- Perform trend analysis to ensure the model captures physical behavior.

## Methods

The study utilized a dataset of 386 samples from Middle East fields, which were filtered for quality using established models. Data pre-processing included normalization (mean/standard deviation) and post-processing involved denormalization. A multi-layer feed-forward neural network was developed with one input layer (9 parameters), three hidden layers (6, 3, and 3 neurons respectively), and one output layer. The back-propagation algorithm was used for training, with the number of iterations adjusted to avoid local minima. Performance was evaluated using Average Absolute Percent Error (AAPE), Correlation Coefficient (R), Root Mean Square Error (RMSE), and Standard Deviation (STD). Trend analysis was performed by varying single input parameters while keeping others constant.

## Data and Materials

386 initial data sets from Middle East fields; 206 final data sets after filtering for accuracy. The model used 9 input variables: oil rate, water rate, gas rate, pipe diameter, pipe length, wellhead pressure, oil density (API), surface temperature, and bottomhole temperature.

## Key Findings

- The ANN model outperformed all tested empirical correlations (e.g., Hagedorn and Brown, Beggs and Brill) and mechanistic models in terms of highest correlation coefficient and lowest average absolute percent error. ([[2004-ayoub|AYOUB, 2004, p. 3]])
- The data used for the model included oil rates from 280 to 19618 BPD, water cut up to 44.8%, and gas-oil ratios up to 675.5 SCF/STB. ([[2004-ayoub|AYOUB, 2004, p. 3]])
- A ratio of 3:1:1 between training, validation, and testing sets yielded the best performance for the ANN model. ([[2004-ayoub|AYOUB, 2004, p. 3]])
- The Hagedorn and Brown correlation was found to be better for water cuts greater than 80%. ([[2004-ayoub|AYOUB, 2004, p. 19]])
- The Orkiszewski correlation was found more accurate than other correlations in determining pressure drop especially when three-phase flow is introduced. ([[2004-ayoub|AYOUB, 2004, p. 18]])
- The Duns and Ros method was the least accurate one among tested correlations. ([[2004-ayoub|AYOUB, 2004, p. 18]])
- The ANN model achieved a correlation coefficient of 0.9735, the highest among all tested models. ([[2004-ayoub|AYOUB, 2004, p. 110]])
- The ANN model produced the lowest average absolute percent error (AAPE) of 2.1654%. ([[2004-ayoub|AYOUB, 2004, p. 110]])
- The ANN model achieved the lowest root mean square error (RMSE) of 2.8013. ([[2004-ayoub|AYOUB, 2004, p. 110]])
- The ANN model showed a lower standard deviation of 66.2448 compared to other models like Mukherjee and Brill (147.572). ([[2004-ayoub|AYOUB, 2004, p. 110]])
- Gas specific gravity was found to be an insignificant parameter for predicting bottomhole pressure. ([[2004-ayoub|AYOUB, 2004, p. 58]])
- The ANN model outperformed the Hagedorn and Brown correlation, which had a high standard deviation of 177.840. ([[2004-ayoub|AYOUB, 2004, p. 110]])
- The ANN model's residual range was -109 to 159, significantly lower than the Hansen and Kabir model (-302.46 to 669.76). ([[2004-ayoub|AYOUB, 2004, p. 103]])
- A trend analysis confirmed that the ANN model correctly predicts physical behavior, such as decreasing bottomhole pressure with increasing gas rate. ([[2004-ayoub|AYOUB, 2004, p. 68]])

## Limitations

The results of the new developed model can only be used within the range of the data used for training; caution should be taken beyond the range of used input variables. The model's performance may improve if more diverse data (horizontal, vertical, and inclined) are included.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Improve model performance by using a larger dataset with a wider range of variables.
- Incorporate horizontal, vertical, and inclined multiphase flow data to develop a universal model.
- Investigate the importance of all input variables further to potentially reduce the number of required inputs.
