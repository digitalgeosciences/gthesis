# Rock Physics Based Seismic Characterization of Saudi Clastic Reservoirs: Rock Physics Diagnostics of Wireline Data

**Summary**: The thesis establishes a rock physics model (RPM) for a Saudi clastic reservoir to interpret seismic data for volumetric properties like porosity and mineralogy. By applying rock physics diagnostics (RPD) on wireline data from 8 wells, the author determined that the stiff-sand model best fits the data and remains valid after Backus upscaling to the seismic scale.

---

## Research Problem

The challenge of converting seismically derived elastic properties (P-wave impedance and density) into volumetric rock properties (porosity, mineralogy, and hydrocarbon saturation) in a Saudi clastic reservoir with low-to-medium porosity sandstone.

## Objectives

- Establish a theoretical rock physics model (RPM) that honors and explains the wireline data under examination.
- Investigate whether and how these models hold at the seismic scale through upscaling.
- Assess the accuracy of applying these models at the seismic scale to determine feasibility for future interpretation.

## Methods

The study utilized Rock Physics Diagnostics (RPD) by cross-plotting elastic variables (e.g., Vp) against volumetric variables (porosity) and color-coding by a third variable (clay content). Gassmann's fluid substitution was used to bring data to a common fluid denominator (100% brine). The stiff-sand model was selected as the primary RPM due to its suitability for fast, stiff clastic rocks. Backus upscaling was applied to wireline logs to evaluate the models at seismic resolution. Finally, a mesh of P-wave impedance vs. Poisson's ratio was generated to provide a tool for predicting porosity and clay content from seismic volumes.

## Data and Materials

Wireline data (sonic, dipole, density) and mineralogical interpretations from 8 wells in a Saudi clastic field; historical laboratory data from Han (1986); and seismically derived impedance volumes.

## Key Findings

- The stiff-sand model was found to be the most accurate for modeling the P-wave and S-wave data, providing a good transition for clay content as porosity decreases. ([[2020-almumtin|Almumtin, 2020, p. 48]])
- The study confirmed that the selected RPM is valid at the seismic scale, with Backus-upscaled curves showing good agreement with original log curves except where high-frequency changes exceed seismic resolution. ([[2020-almumtin|Almumtin, 2020, p. 53]])
- A Poisson's ratio cut-off of 0.18 can effectively separate clean sand from silty sand in both well log and seismic resolutions. ([[2020-almumtin|Almumtin, 2020, p. 60]])
- P-wave impedance can be used to identify porous vs. tight sands in the seismic resolution using a cut-off of 13 km/s g/cc. ([[2020-almumtin|Almumtin, 2020, p. 60]])
- The study confirmed that the rock is very stiff, leading to a weak dependency of elastic properties on pore fluid types (gas vs. oil). ([[2020-almumtin|Almumtin, 2020, p. 43]])

## Limitations

Well logs are one-dimensional while seismic data provides volume; however, the study addresses this by using Backus upscaling and ensuring model validity at the seismic scale.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The established rock physics model and resulting mesh can be used to predict porosity and mineralogy in future seismic interpretation projects for similar clastic reservoirs.
