# BENTHIC FORAMINIFERAL TAXONOMY, DISTRIBUTION AND ECOLOGY IN THE ARABIAN GULF

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the taxonomy, distribution, and ecology of benthic foraminifera in various environments within the Arabian Gulf. It includes specific studies on high-salinity areas like Salwa Bay and coastal zones in Bahrain to understand how environmental factors like temperature and salinity influence foraminiferal diversity.

---

## Research Problem

Historical records on foraminiferal diversity in the Arabian Gulf often underestimate the actual benthic foraminiferal composition due to limited scope, lack of a taxonomic guide, and varying sampling strategies. The study aims to provide a comprehensive inventory and ecological analysis of these organisms which are significant carbonate producers and indicators of paleoenvironmental conditions.

## Methods

The study employed a dual approach: (1) Field Sampling: 32 samples were collected offshore in the northern Arabian Gulf using a Van Veen grab (0.1 m2 area). The top 2 cm was subsampled, preserved with 70% ethanol and Rose-Bengal stain, then wet-sieved through a 63 µm mesh. (2) Laboratory Analysis: Samples were split into aliquots of approximately 300 tests for quantitative picking. (3) Statistical Analysis: PCA (Principal Component Analysis) and Q-mode Cluster Analysis were used to identify distinct assemblages and environmental gradients. (4) Literature Review: A comprehensive review of literature dating back to the 1960s was conducted to compile a total biodiversity count.

## Key Findings

- Two new species were identified: Pseudonubeculina arabica N. gen. N. sp. (Holocene) and Pseudotriloculina hottingeri N. sp. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 36]])
- SEM-EDS analysis was performed on the internal lining of paratype #6/6c.6 to determine chemical composition. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 48]])
- Grain-size distribution and environmental factors were analyzed in Salwa Bay to assess foraminiferal diversity. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 72]])
- A total of 103 plates (implied by the range) or a large number of taxa were identified across various environments in the Gulf. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 152]])
- The new species Pseudotriloculina hottingeri n. sp. was identified with a depth range of 0.02–17 m, tolerating salinity of 36–52 ppt and temperatures of 27–30°C. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 20]])
- In Salwa Bay, benthic foraminiferal species were dominated by porcelaneous taxa (85.1%), followed by hyaline (14.9%). ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 20]])
- The ten most abundant species in Salwa Bay are: Peneroplis pertusus (24.0%), P. planatus (15.4%), Coscinospira hemprichii (9.1%), Ammonia convexa (6.6%), Quinqueloculina sp. 1 (3.9%), Elphidium advenum (3.9%), E. craticulatum (3.5%), E. gerthi (3.0%), A. tepida (3.0%), and Elphidium sp. 1 (3.0%). ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 20]])
- Peneroplis (58.4%) and Coscinospira (17.1%) account for 75.5% of all the observed test deformities in Salwa Bay. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 20]])
- The new genus Pseudonubeculina is characterized by a uniserial chamber arrangement and coarsely agglutinated bilamellar test wall with white high-Mg calcite cement. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 20]])
- Approximately 43% of the most common taxa in Salwa Bay exhibit mild to severe deformities such as fusion of two adults or double tests. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 20]])
- Analysis of 32 samples from the Iranian coast revealed 224 benthic foraminiferal species/subspecies belonging to 63 genera, 34 families, 22 superfamilies, and 6 orders. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 24]])
- Hyaline taxa accounted for 45.3% of the assemblage, while porcelaneous foraminiferal groups comprised 35.3%, and agglutinated groups were 19.4%. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 24]])
- The most abundant genera in the Iranian coast samples included Asterorotalia (13.3%), Quinqueloculina (12.4%), Bolivina (9.8%), and Nonion (8.6%). ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 24]])
- In Salwa Bay, porcelaneous taxa dominated the assemblage (85.1%), followed by hyaline taxa (14.9%). ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 23]])
- The species Pseudotriloculina hottingeri n. sp. was identified in 20 out of 78 samples, showing a preference for specific salinity (36-52‰) and temperature ranges. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 23]])

## Limitations

Limitations include the time-averaged nature of samples, use of grab sampling equipment, lack of documented sedimentary and geochemical data for all sites, and the absence of a comprehensive taxonomic guide specifically for the Arabian Gulf.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Future studies are needed to distinguish between test abnormalities caused by epibions and those attributable to different types of pollution. A systematic study of the area based on additional samples is required to better constrain the relationship between Cymbaloporetta sp. and its hosts.
