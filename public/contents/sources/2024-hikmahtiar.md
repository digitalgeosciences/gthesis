# Turnover in Agglutinated Foraminifera Across The Cretaceous/Paleogene Boundary in The Western Tethys

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the turnover and extinction rates of deep-water agglutinated benthic foraminifera (DWAF) across the Cretaceous/Paleogene boundary in the Scaglia Rossa Formation, Umbria-Marche Basin. By analyzing high-resolution samples from the Contessa Highway Section, it identifies new species and quantifies extinction rates while accounting for the Signor-Lipps effect.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the difficulty of establishing a definitive temporal connection between environmental crises in sedimentary records and volcanic events like the Deccan Trap eruptions, specifically focusing on how these factors influenced benthic foraminiferal diversity at the K/Pg boundary.

## Objectives

- Taxonomically document and identify agglutinated foraminifera across the Cretaceous/Paleogene boundary.
- Analyze the composition of DWAF assemblages to identify the nature of faunal turnover (extinction, Lazarus, and newly-appearing taxa).
- Quantify accumulation rates and morphogroups of benthic foraminifera before and after the boundary to investigate changes in food supply.
- Integrate benthic foraminiferal data with sedimentology and chemostratigraphy to interpret environmental changes related to Deccan Trap volcanism.
- Quantify the diversity and abundance of DWAF in the lower Paleocene of the Contessa Highway Section.
- Identify and describe new species of agglutinated foraminifera (Ammopemphix contessaensis, Nodulina kuhnti, and Nodulina eugubina).

## Methods

The study utilized high-resolution sampling (bed-by-bed, 10cm or 20cm intervals) of the lower Paleocene (Danian) in the Scaglia Rossa Formation. Samples were treated with hydrochloric acid (HCl) to dissolve carbonate and extract agglutinated foraminifera using a 63 µm sieve. Specimens larger than 125 µm were selected and mounted on microslides. Analysis was performed using a Neoscope JCM-7000 scanning electron microscope (SEM). Extinction rates were calculated by applying the Signor-Lipps effect to a series of increasing sample sizes (multiples of 5 up to 45 samples) to determine the minimum required sample size for accurate quantification.

## Data and Materials

44 samples from the lower Paleocene of the Contessa Highway section; 37 specimens of Ammopemphix contessaensis; 518 specimens of Nodulina kuhnti; 196 specimens of Nodulina eugubina; a total of 97 species identified across the K/Pg boundary interval.

## Key Findings

- The study identified three groups of DWAF: survivor species (including Lazarus taxa), extinction species, and incoming species. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 16]])
- A significant decrease in diversity was observed across the K/Pg boundary, with a rapid decline in specific morphogroups. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 26]])
- The abundance of tubular agglutinated foraminifera (M1) showed an 80% reduction starting from 5 cm below the boundary. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 26]])
- Infaunal forms such as Reophax sp. and Spiroplectinella israelskyi exhibited opportunistic behavior, blooming in the Early Paleocene after being rare in the Maastrichtian. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 16]])
- The species Arenoturrispirillina waskowskae is a new species identified from the Danian of Contessa, characterized by a diameter length of 200-450 µm and a conical shape. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 51]])
- Mercury (Hg) enrichment in the Bottaccione section is linked to Deccan Trap volcanism as a factor influencing K/Pg mass extinction. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 17]])
- The Scaglia Rossa Formation contains diverse DWAF assemblages, but their record across the boundary was previously only studied at low resolution. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 26]])
- A new species, Ammopemphix contessaensis, was identified in the lower Paleocene (Danian) at a stratigraphic height of 2.4 m above the K/Pg boundary. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 57]])
- Two new species, Nodulina kuhnti and Nodulina eugubina, were described from the Danian; N. kuhnti was found in nearly every lower Paleocene sample (518 specimens), while N. eugubina showed a significant drop in abundance after 0.6 m above the boundary. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 70]])
- The study identified 97 species across the K/Pg boundary, including 49 extinction taxa, 10 survivor taxa, 19 Lazarus taxa, and 19 first-occurrence taxa in the Paleocene. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 89]])
- Morphogroup analysis showed a shift from an epifaunal suspension-feeder dominated community (M1) in the Maastrichtian to one with higher proportions of detritivores and bacterial feeders (M2, M3) in the lower Paleocene. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 98]])
- The calculation of extinction rates using the Signor-Lipps effect showed that the apparent extinction rate dropped from 84% to 53% as the sample size increased, with a stable result achieved at approximately 30 samples. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 117]])
- The study suggests that oxygen levels were declining for approximately 100,000 years before the K/Pg boundary, evidenced by the shift toward more tolerant infaunal morphotypes (M2 and M3). ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 85]])
- A total of 48 species of deep-water agglutinated foraminifera were observed in the lowermost five meters of the Paleocene at Contessa Highway. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 167]])
- Approximately 53% of DWAF taxa disappeared at the K/Pg boundary, while 10% survived and 18% were identified as Lazarus taxa. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 167]])

## Limitations

A limitation from the Bottaccione section is the omission of two meters at the uppermost Cretaceous, meaning calculated Hg enrichment cannot accurately represent anomalies exactly at the K/Pg boundary. Additionally, some results are not correlated with other measurements due to a lack of significant results linked to organic matter.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Collect additional samples at the boundary to complete the record of mercury and compare it to diversity and abundance of agglutinated foraminifera.
- Integrate new data on calcareous nannoplankton to obtain a more detailed biostratigraphic record.
- Perform measurements of stable isotopes and other geochemical proxies to understand changes in paleoproductivity.
