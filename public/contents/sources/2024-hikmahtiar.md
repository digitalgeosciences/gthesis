# Turnover in Agglutinated Foraminifera Across The Cretaceous/Paleogene Boundary in The Western Tethys

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the turnover and recovery of deep-water agglutinated benthic foraminifera (DWAF) across the K/Pg boundary in the Scaglia Rossa Formation. By analyzing morphogroups and diversity indices, it concludes that while there was a significant shift in community structure toward more opportunistic types, the total organic carbon flux did not collapse at the boundary.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the lack of detailed faunal turnover analysis for deep-water agglutinated benthic foraminifera (DWAF) in the Western Tethys across the K/Pg boundary, specifically focusing on how these organisms responded to environmental changes caused by the Chicxulub impact and Deccan Traps volcanism.

## Methods

Samples were collected from the Contessa Highway (50 samples) and Bottaccione sections (21 samples). Samples were processed using a dilute hydrochloric acid (HCl) method to extract foraminifera from limestone. Microfossils were examined under a NIKON SMZ18 microscope and imaged using a NeoScope JCM-7000 SEM. Data analysis included diversity indices (Shannon & Weaver, Fisher's alpha), morphogroup classification based on feeding strategies, and statistical analysis of assemblages using the PAST software.

## Data and Materials

Samples were collected from two specific locations: the Contessa Highway Section and Bottaccione Section within the Scaglia Rossa Formation. The study includes detailed counts of various morphotypes (e.g., Bathysiphon, Kalamopsis, Nothia) across multiple samples (e.g., CON4.8 to CON255).

## Key Findings

- Identified three new species of agglutinated foraminifera: Arenoturrispirillina waskowskae, Ammopemphix contessaensis, and Nodulina kuhnti/Nodulina eugubina. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 12]])
- Quantified diversity indices (Alpha, Fisher index, Shannon index) for DWAF in both the Bottaccione and Contessa Highway sections. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 63]])
- Mercury (Hg) concentrations were measured in the lowermost Paleocene (Danian) and uppermost Cretaceous to assess volcanic influence. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 102]])
- Determined extinction rates of DWAF across the K/Pg boundary as a function of sample size. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 98]])
- The record shows an abrupt decrease in species across the K/Pg boundary. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 16]])
- Common occurrence of infaunal forms such as Reophax sp. and Spiroplectinella israelskyi are regarded as opportunistic behavior, as they were rare in the Maastrichtian but bloomed in the Early Paleocene. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 16]])
- Arenoturrispirillina waskowskae, Ammopemphix contessaensis, Nodulina kuhnti, and Nodulina eugubina are concentrated in lower Paleocene. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 17]])
- The DWAF morphogroups show an 80% reduction of tubular agglutinated foraminifera species starting from 5 cm below the boundary, followed by domination of epifaunal suspension and detritus feeders. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 26]])
- Evidence in Bottaccione shows that Phase-2 of Deccan volcanic eruption started having Hg/TOC spikes. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 24]])
- The study identified a new species, Arenoturrispirillina waskowskae, in the Danian section 1 to 5 meters above the K/Pg boundary. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 52]])
- A significant decrease in abundance and diversity of DWAF occurred at the clay layer of the K/Pg boundary, indicating a catastrophic event. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 35]])
- The study identified several morphogroups (M1 to M4a) that provide information on bathymetry and organic carbon flux. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024]])

- The Bottaccione section shows a transition from pinkish/reddish limestone to whiter rock in the top 50 cm, indicating reduced oxygen levels at the end of the Cretaceous. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 39]])
- Arenoturrispirillina waskowskae is characterized by a finely agglutinated test with a diameter of 200-450 µm and thickness of 150-250 µm. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 52]])

## Limitations

Limited data on the K/Pg boundary makes it difficult to confirm some information regarding Hg concentration spikes; also, a two-meter omission at the uppermost Cretaceous in the Bottacceon section limits accurate representation of anomalies at the K/Pg boundary.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Collect additional samples at the boundary to complete the mercury record and correlate it with foraminiferal diversity/abundance.
- Integrate new data on calcareous nannoplankton for a more detailed biostratigraphic record.
- Perform stable isotope measurements and other geochemical proxies to understand changes in paleoproductivity.
