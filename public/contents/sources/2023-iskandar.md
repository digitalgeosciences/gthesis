# Mantle Xenoliths of Harrat Uwayrid

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the mineral chemistry, petrogenesis, and geothermobarometric conditions of mantle peridotite xenoliths (lherzolites, harzburgites, and wehrlite) from the Harrat Uwayrid volcanic field. Analysis reveals that these xenoliths underwent varying degrees of partial melting (1-17%) and were equilibrated in a reduced environment at depths of 50-105 km.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the need for detailed petrogenetic data (melting degrees, oxygen fugacity, and thermobarometry) for mantle xenoliths in the Harrat Uwayrid field to understand the lithospheric mantle evolution of the Arabian plate.

## Objectives

- Perform bulk rock chemistry by ICP-OES and ICP-MS analysis to determine major and trace element concentrations.
- Document mineral chemistry using EPMA analysis to determine major element compositions of minerals in the xenoliths.
- Determine mineralogy and rock classification of the xenoliths.
- Identify petrogenetic processes of the xenoliths.
- Compare results with other Arabian xenolith studies.
- Provide constraints on the nature of the mantle below Harrat Uwayrid and the Arabian plate.

## Methods

The study involved field collection of 36 ultramafic xenolith samples from various locations in the Harrat Uwayrid field. Samples were prepared as polished thin sections (18 pieces) for petrographic microscopy and as small cubes (11 pieces) for bulk rock chemistry analysis via ICP-OES and ICP-MS at Bureau Veritas, Vancouver. Mineral chemistry was determined using EPMA at Korea University. Data interpretation included CIPW normative calculations, thermobarometry based on olivine-clinopyroxene and orthopyroxene-clinopyroxene pairs, and calculation of melting degrees (F) and oxygen fugacity (fO2).

## Data and Materials

36 ultramafic peridotite xenolith samples collected from 15 locations within the Harrat Uwayrid field; 18 thin sections for petrography/EPMA; 11 bulk rock samples for ICP-MS/ICP-OES.

## Key Findings

- Harrat Uwayrid peridotite xenoliths are classified as spinel lherzolites, spinel harzburgites (with rare wehrlites), and show no significant difference between older and younger host rocks. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 21]])
- Olivine Fo# values range from 83.46 to 93.05; clinopyroxene Mg# ranges from 83.33 to 93.84 (average 91.86); and Cr# values range from 2.60 to 16.90 (average 11.63). ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 21]])
- Orthopyroxene Mg# ranges from 89.40 to 93.68 (average 91.55), while orthopyroxene Cr# ranges from 3.41 to 16.34 (average 6.86). ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 22]])
- Calculated degrees of partial melting range from 1% to 17%. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 22]])
- Oxygen fugacity values are -1.89 to -0.22 (relative to FMQ), indicating equilibration in reduced conditions. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 22]])
- Thermobarometry indicates peridotite xenoliths equilibrated at ~1.72–3.76 GPa and temperatures of ~1090–1135°C, corresponding to depths of ~50–105 km. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 22]])
- Loss on ignition (LOI) values for all peridotite xenoliths were below 1%, indicating high sample freshness. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 66]])
- Approximately 70% of the samples are classified as lherzolites, while 4 are harzburgites and 2 are wehrlites. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 63]])
- Mantle peridotite xenoliths were identified as spinel lherzolite, spinel harzburgite, and wehrlite. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 102]])
- The degree of partial melting for the Harrat Uwayrid peridotite xenoliths was determined to be between 1% and 17%. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 87]])
- Oxygen fugacity (fO2) values ranged from -1.89 to -0.22, indicating equilibration in a reduced environment. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 90]])
- Geothermobarometry results showed temperatures between 1019°C and 1135°C and pressures of 1.72 to 3.76 GPa (depths of ~50 km to 105 km). ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 93]])
- Olivine Fo# values for the majority of samples ranged from 83.5 to 93.0, with most falling in the peridotitic range (88-94). ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 75]])
- Clinopyroxene Mg# values averaged 91.86 and ranged from 83.33 to 93.84, while Cr# values ranged from 2.60 to 16.90. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 75]])
- Spinel Mg# values were homogeneous between 75.80 and 81.40, with Cr# values between 8.90 and 30.70. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 82]])

## Limitations

The study notes that the pressure calculation for the spinel phase (PKB) has a high temperature sensitivity, leading to significant differences if even slight variations in temperature exist.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Perform point counting as a method for mode classification.
- Provide XRD and QEMSCAN results for classification constraints.
- Conduct isotope analysis for age determination and better petrogenesis analysis.
- Correlate the determined petrogenesis to major tectonic events.
