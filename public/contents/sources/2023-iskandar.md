# Mantle Xenoliths of Harrat Uwayrid

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the petrogenesis and geochemical characteristics of mantle xenoliths (spinel lherzolite, spinel harzburgite, and wehrlite) from the Harrat Uwayrid volcanic field. It concludes that these peridotites underwent multi-stage evolution involving melting, enrichment, and metasomatism related to the Red Sea rifting.

---

## Research Problem

Characterizing the geological evolution of the lithospheric mantle beneath the Harrat Uwayrid volcanic field (Upper Miocene to Quaternary) in western Saudi Arabia, specifically regarding its response to large-scale tectonic events on the Arabian plate.

## Objectives

- Provide new whole-rock and mineral chemistry data for Uwayrid mantle xenoliths.
- Determine degrees of partial melting and melt extraction from the source.
- Calculate oxygen fugacity (oxidation state) of the mantle source.
- Perform thermobarometry to determine depth and temperature of the peridotite xenoliths.
- Bulk rock chemistry by ICP-OES and ICP-MS analysis to determine major and trace element concentrations, respectively, of the xenoliths.
- Mineral chemistry documentation by EPMA analysis to determine major element compositions of the minerals in the xenoliths.
- Determine mineralogy and rock classification of the xenoliths.
- Identify petrogenetic process of the xenoliths.
- Comparisons of the study’s results to other Arabian xenolith studies.
- Provide constraints on the interpretations of the nature of the mantle below Harrat Uwayrid and the Arabian plate.
- Determine the modal mineralogy and petrological classification of peridotite xenoliths from Harrat Uwayrid.
- Analyze the bulk rock chemistry (major and trace elements) of the xenolith samples.
- Perform mineral-scale chemical analysis on olivine, clinopyroxene, and orthopyroxene.
- Calculate melting degrees, oxidation states, and thermobarometric conditions for the peridotite xenoliths.
- Determine the mineral chemistry of clinopyroxene and spinel in Harrat Uwayrid peridotite xenoliths.
- Quantify the degrees of partial melting and melt extraction for lherzolites and harzburgites.
- Calculate oxygen fugacity (fO2) to determine the oxidation state of the mantle source.
- Estimate the temperature and pressure conditions of formation using thermobarometry.
- Determine the mineralogical and chemical composition of peridotite xenoliths from Harrat Uwayrid.
- Assess the degree of partial melting and melt extraction from these mantle samples.
- Investigate the role of metasomatism and fluid infiltration in LREE enrichment.
- Establish geothermobarometric parameters for the latest equilibration of the peridotites.

## Methods

The methodology involved field collection of host lavas, cinders, and entrained xenoliths from multiple cinder cones in the Al-Jauww area. Samples were documented via photography and field descriptions (color, texture, grain size, fabric, mineralogy). Preparation included cutting rocks into 18 thin sections for petrographic microscopy using a Zeiss microscope and 11 samples as cubes for bulk rock chemistry analysis. Bulk chemistry was determined using ICP-OES and ICP-MS at Bureau Veritas via the Lithogeochemical Whole Rock Fusion (BVM Code LV 200) protocol. Mineral chemistry of the 18 thin sections was analyzed using a JEOL JXA–8600 SX EPMA with energy dispersive spectroscopy (INCA–6025).

## Data and Materials

The study analyzed mantle peridotite xenoliths ranging from cm-scale to fist-size. Data included whole-rock major/trace elements and mineral chemical data (olivine, clinopyroxene, orthopyroxene, spinel) for several samples in the Harrat Uwayrid field.

## Key Findings

- Harrat Uwayrid peridotite xenoliths are classified as spinel lherzolite, spinel harzburgite, and rare wehrlites. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 21]])
- Fo# of olivines from most of the Harrat Uwayrid peridotite xenoliths ranges from 83.46 – 93.05. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 21]])
- Mg# of clinopyroxene range from 83.33 – 93.84 with an average of 91.86; Cr# values range from 2.60 – 16.90 (average 11.63). ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 21]])
- Mg# of orthopyroxenes ranges from 89.40 – 93.68 (average 91.55), while orthopyroxene Cr# ranges from 3.41 – 16.34 (average 6.86). ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 22]])
- Calculated degrees of partial melting are low to moderate, ranging from 1 – 17% based on Cr# values. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 22]])
- Oxygen fugacity values are -1.89 to -0.22 (relative to FMQ), indicating equilibration in reduced conditions. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 22]])
- Thermobarometry suggests peridotite xenoliths equilibrated at ~1.72 – 3.76 GPa and temperatures of ~1090°C – 1135°C (or 1100-1220°C in Arabic summary). ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 22]])
- The calculated depth for these xenoliths is approximately 50 – 105 km. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 22]])
- Harrat Uwayrid host rocks are dated between 9 Ma and Present, with specific subdivisions of Upper Miocene (8.22 to 6.75 Ma) and Pliocene-Quaternary (5.20 to 0.34 Ma). ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 29]])
- Previous studies on Harrat Uwayrid xenoliths identified two main groups, IA and IB; Group IA are fertile lherzolites to residual harzburgite (Al/Si < Mg/Si), while Group IB range from lherzolite to wehrlite. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 34]])
- Geothermometric calculations for Group IA and IB xenoliths estimated T conditions of 850-1110°C and 950-1100°C, respectively. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 35]])
- Isotopic analysis of Group IA showed positive εNd and low 87Sr/86Sr, resembling modern mid-ocean ridge basalt (MORB). ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 35]])
- The study by Kaliwoda et al. (2008) reported Mg# of olivine (0.87–0.91), clinopyroxene (0.86–0.93), orthopyroxene (0.87–0.91), and spinel (0.61–0.83). ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 41]])
- Cr# values for clinopyroxene were 0.05–0.34, orthopyroxene 0.04–0.19, and spinel 0.07–0.64. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 41]])
- Geothermobarometric calculations for these samples estimated T ranging from ~800-1140°C and P of 1.5 to 3.3 GPa (depths of ~30–65 km). ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 41]])

## Limitations

The pressure calculation method (PKB) has a high temperature sensitivity; even slight differences in temperature can lead to significantly different results.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Perform point counting for mode classification.
- Provide XRD and QEMSCAN results for classification constraints.
- Conduct isotope analysis for age determination and better petrogenesis analysis.
- Correlate determined petrogenesis to major tectonic events.
