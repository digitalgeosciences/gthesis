# IMPROVING DRILLING PERFORMANCE IN ABRASIVE SANDSTONE FORMATION BY OPTIMIZING DRILL STRING AND BHA DESIGN

**Summary**: The thesis investigates improving drilling performance in abrasive sandstone formations by optimizing Bottom Hole Assembly (BHA) design and real-time parameters. Using transient and frequency simulations, the study identifies optimal RPM and WOB ranges to reduce lateral vibrations and stick-slip, ultimately increasing average Rate of Penetration (ROP) and footage per run.

---

## Research Problem

Drilling through abrasive sandstone formations often leads to high vibration levels (axial, torsional, and lateral) and bit wear, resulting in nonproductive time (NPT), tool failure, and reduced average footage per run. The study addresses the lack of integrated analysis between Mechanical Specific Energy (MSE), formation geomechanics, BHA design, and drilling parameters.

## Objectives

- Evaluate historical data to establish benchmarks for ROP and footage per run.
- Capture and analyze downhole dynamic data from RSS and LWD tools to identify vibration sources.
- Select optimum drill string and BHA designs using frequency and transient models.
- Determine optimal real-time drilling parameters to improve efficiency in abrasive sandstone.
- Validate the effectiveness of proposed BHA and parameter sets on new wells.
- Analyze historical data to identify performance controllers in abrasive sandstone.

## Methods

The research followed a five-phase methodology: 1) Evaluation of historical data from 12 wells to establish baseline ROP (5.84 ft/hr) and average footage per run (391 ft). 2) Analysis of downhole dynamic data (stick slip, shock peaks) from RSS and LWD tools across various well profiles. 3) Modeling using transient simulation (finite element analysis) and frequency-domain models to evaluate BHA designs under different parameters. 4) Optimization of drilling parameters based on MSE and geomechanical considerations. 5) Validation of the proposed solutions in new wells.

## Data and Materials

Data from 12 drilled wells were analyzed, including 51 bit runs. Parameters included ROP, footage per run, bit dull grading, stabilizer wear, and downhole dynamics (stick slip, shock peaks). Simulation parameters included a 2000 ft lateral length, 87-90 degree inclination, and OBM with 95-100 PCF.

## Key Findings

- The average footage per run for the baseline wells was 391 ft (SD: 67) and the mean ROP was 5.84 ft/hr (SD: 1.51). ([[2022-al-dahmoush|Al-Dahmoush, 2022, p. 38]])
- Outer cutters showed a higher level of damage than inner cutters, with wear being primarily due to bit rotation in abrasive formations. ([[2022-al-dahmoush|Al-Dahmoush, 2022, p. 42]])
- 90% of the bits were worn out (WT) as the primary dulling characteristic. ([[2022-al-dahmoush|Al-Dahmoush, 2022, p. 42]])
- Penetration rate (PR) was the most common reason for pulling out of the hole, accounting for 53% of cases. ([[2022-al-dahmoush|Al-Dahmoush, 2022, p. 42]])
- Stabilizer wear is directly linked to footage drilled; 93% of runs over 350 ft resulted in 1/8 under gauge stabilizers. ([[2022-al-dahmoush|Al-Dahmoush, 2022, p. 43]])

- The proposed BHA and parameters improved average footage per run by 133-178% while maintaining ROP, with potential to improve ROP by 20%. ([[2022-al-dahmoush|Al-Dahmoush, 2022, p. 15]])
- Transient simulation accurately predicted drilling motion, allowing for the identification of critical RPMs and optimal BHA configurations. ([[2022-al-dahmoush|Al-Dahmoush, 2022, p. 71]])
- The proposed BHA configuration with 80 rpm and 18 klbs WOB reduced lateral vibration to 8-16 G's and stick-slip to 18-19% (compared to 36% and 37% in standard configurations). ([[2022-al-dahmoush|Al-Dahmoush, 2022, p. 78]])
- Frequency analysis identified specific RPM values to avoid: 53, 88, 124, 139, and 146 rpm (inducing lateral vibrations at 5.27Hz, 8.84Hz, 12.44Hz, 13.88Hz, and 14.59Hz respectively). ([[2022-al-dahmoush|Al-Dahmoush, 2022, p. 80]])
- The proposed BHA and parameters improved average footage per run by 180% (from 391 ft to 1095 ft) in Well#14. ([[2022-al-dahmoush|Al-Dahmoush, 2022, p. 85]])
- The implementation of the optimized roadmap resulted in an ROP increase of approximately 21% compared to historical data. ([[2022-al-dahmoush|Al-Dahmoush, 2022, p. 85]])
- The study estimated a potential saving of at least 3 bit trips (over 120 hours) and 47 hours of bottom time by achieving the 20% ROP improvement. ([[2022-al-dahmoush|Al-Dahmoush, 2022, p. 86]])
- Well#13 showed an extension of footage per run by 780 ft on interbedded formations, nearly doubling bit life and saving two bits. ([[2022-al-dahmoush|Al-Dahmoush, 2022, p. 83]])

## Limitations

The study notes that in some instances where vibration was not released after reducing WOB to the safe zone, it was necessary to pick up off bottom to release energy; also, ROP was slightly below average in specific sections due to the need for frequent WOB adjustments on interbedded layers.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Future work should include: 1. Bit Design Modification (evaluating impact of number of plates, cutter orientation, and size). 2. Longer Lateral Length analysis (investigating vibration levels when lateral length exceeds 2000 ft).
