# Isolation and characterization of microalgae for biofuel production

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the isolation and characterization of three indigenous microalgae strains (Chlorella kessleri, Chlorella vulgaris, and Nanochloropsis oculata) from the Arabian Gulf for biofuel production. The study evaluates their growth kinetics, nutrient removal capabilities (nitrate and phosphate), and lipid content under varying temperatures and nutrient concentrations.

---

## Research Problem

The rapid depletion of fossil fuels and the need for sustainable, carbon-neutral alternatives like biofuels derived from microalgae, specifically focusing on high-yielding oleic strains in Saudi Arabia.

## Objectives

- Isolation of indigenous microalgae strains from the Arabian Gulf.
- Identify isolated strains based on morphological characteristics and 18S rRNA analysis.
- Check the growth performance of the isolates by varying nutrient (NO3 and PO4) and temperature (30°C and 35°C).
- Biofuel analysis of the strains by gas chromatography.
- Isolate microalgae strains from coastal waters of Jubail and Al-Khobar.
- Evaluate growth parameters (Optical Density and Dry Weight) under varying nutrient levels and temperatures.

## Methods

Microalgae were sampled from the coast of Al-Khobar and Jub1ail. Isolation was performed using streak plates, serial dilution, and micromanipulation to obtain axenic cultures. Growth kinetics were assessed via optical density (OD) measured with a spectrophotometer and dry weight measurement. Nutrient removal (nitrate and phosphate) was quantified using Hach kits (DR 6000 spectrophotometer and DRB200 reactor). Lipid content was extracted via direct esterification and quantified using GC-FID.

## Data and Materials

Samples were collected from two sites: Jubail Industrial City Coast (14 samples) and Al-Khobar coast (7 samples). Three specific strains (S1, S2, S3) were isolated and tested across 6 treatment combinations of temperature (30°C, 35°C) and three nutrient levels (N1=61.5 mg/L NO3; N2=123 mg/L NO3; N3=246 mg/L NO3).

## Key Findings

- Three distinct strains were successfully isolated: S1 (Chlorella kessleri), S2 (Chlorella vulgaris), and S3 (Nanochloropsis oculata). ([[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016, p. 51]])
- S2 showed the highest optical density at both 30°C and 35°C, reaching values of 1.81 and 1.45 respectively. ([[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016, p. 53]])
- The highest dry weight of 1.71 g/L was observed for strain S2 at nutrient level N3 at 30°C. ([[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016, p. 63]])
- All three strains (S1, S2, and S3) showed over 90% removal of phosphate at both 30°C and 35°C across all nutrient levels. ([[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016, p. 61]])
- Nitrate removal exceeded 99% for all strains at the end of the experiment at both temperatures. ([[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016, p. 63]])
- Lipid content was found to be 19%, 27%, and 14% in S1, S2, and S3 respectively. ([[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016, p. 12]])
- GC-FID analysis showed palmitic acid concentrations of 18%, 22%, and 16% for S1, S2, and S3 respectively. ([[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016, p. 12]])
- S3 showed the lowest growth performance in terms of optical density at both temperatures compared to S1 and S2. ([[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016, p. 53]])
- Strain S2 showed the highest lipid content (27%) and highest lipid productivity (1.13 g/l-day-1) compared to S1 (19%) and S3 (14%). ([[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016, p. 64]])
- S2 strain was identified as the most promising for biofuel production due to its high biomass productivity and higher content of palmitic and stearic acids. ([[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016, p. 68]])
- Fatty acid profiles showed S1 with 30.7% saturated, S2 with 34.4%, and S3 with 28% saturated fatty acids by dry weight. ([[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016, p. 64]])
- Nitrate and phosphate were almost completely removed (over 90%) within a 20-day incubation period for all strains at both temperatures. ([[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016, p. 60]])
- At 35°C, S2 showed 95-98% nitrate removal and 92-95% phosphate removal. ([[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016, p. 63]])
- S1 produced 0.82 g/L/day lipid productivity; S2 produced 1.13 g/L/day; S3 produced 0.58 g/L/day. ([[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016, p. 64]])
