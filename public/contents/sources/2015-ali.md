# High-resolution stratigraphic architecture and sedimentological heterogeneity within the Miocene Dam Formation, Eastern Province, Eastern Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the stratigraphic architecture and sedimentological heterogeneity of the Miocene Dam Formation in the Al-Lidam Area, Eastern Province, Saudi Arabia. Using high-resolution facies analysis and geochemical characterization (XRD, SEM, EDS), it identifies distinct carbonate-siliciclastic cycles and determines that the formation was deposited on a mixed carbonate-siliciclastic homoclinal ramp.

---

## Research Problem

The Dam Formation in the Al-Lidam Area represents a complex mixed carbonate-siliciclastic system where standard subsurface data is often limited. There is a need to understand its high-resolution stratigraphic architecture and facies heterogeneity to better characterize the reservoir potential of this Miocene formation.

## Methods

The study utilized high-resolution field mapping to identify 17 distinct lithofacies. Thin-section petrography was used to characterize mineralogy, grain size, and sedimentary structures. Sequence stratigraphy principles were applied to define composite sequences (CS) based on stacking patterns, cycle counts, and sequence boundaries (SB). Facies maps were constructed using Walther's law to interpret the spatial distribution of facies and their relationship to basin development.

## Data and Materials

The study analyzed outcrops in the Al-Lidam Area, specifically focusing on five main outcrop locations. It identified 17 distinct lithofacies (e.g., Csg, Cbg, Pbw, Bpp, Og, Mpg, Spg, Dsw, Fpg, Spgp, Pp, Spep, Qfs, Cfs, Msm, Ps). Data included petrographic descriptions of grain sizes, sedimentary structures, and mineral content (e.g., ooids, stromatolites, clastic detritus).

## Key Findings

- The Dam Formation contains a mix of carbonate and siliciclastic facies, including oolitic grainstone, stromatolites, and mud-cracked shales. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 14]])
- Four main sequences (CS1, CS2, CS3, and CS4) were defined based on cyclicity and stacking patterns; CS2 was further subdivided into two High Frequency Sequences (HFS1 and HFS2). ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 14]])
- The lithofacies include: 1) estuarine quartzose channelized calcareous sandstone, 2) skeletal grainstone sheets/mounds, 3) red mudstone beds (paleosols), and 4) oolitic grainstone with microbial laminites. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 14]])
- An increase in the percentage of siliciclastic sediments is observed in updip sections (northeast), while carbonates dominate in downdip sections (southwest). ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 15]])
- page_number_not_specified ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015]])
- The Dam Formation is a mixed carbonate-siliciclastic unit of Middle Miocene age, characterized by limestone, sandstone, and mudstone. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 17]])
- The lower contact of the Dam Formation is marked by the appearance of fossiliferous marl containing echinoids species of Echinocyamus over sandstone of the Hadrukh Formation. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 19]])
- The Dam Formation was deposited in a closed embayment and contains various lithologies including limestone, sandstone, and mudstone. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 19]])
- Al-Khaldi (2009) identified three composite sequences (CS1, CS2, CS3) where CS1 consists of four meter scale cycles, while CS2 and CS3 are composed of two high frequency cycles each. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 25]])
- The presence of eolianites facies on Lidam Escarpment and scattered gypsum crystals in Red mudstone beds indicates an arid climate. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 25]])
- The Dam Formation is characterized by three composite sequences (CS1, CS2, CS3) where TST flooding on Type-1 SB formed HFS2 and HFS3, and HFS2 and HFS4 were results of TST flooding on Type-2 SB. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 25]])
- The Dam Formation is a mixed carbonate-siliciclastic unit with varying porosity and permeability patterns influenced by both depositional and diagenetic controls. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 25]])
- Coated skeletal grainstone (Csg) shows porosity ranging from 25% to 30%, with XRD analysis indicating ~95% carbonate and 5% quartz. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 48]])
- Coated bioclastic grainstone (Cbg) has a porosity of 25-30% and is composed of 100% carbonate minerals. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 50]])
- Peloidal burrowed wackestone (Pbw) shows porosity between 5-7%, with XRD analysis showing >86% carbonate and 14% quartz. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 53]])

## Limitations

The study notes that the industry often relies on limited and large-spaced subsurface datasets, which can limit the understanding of reservoir heterogeneity.

## Recommendations and Future Work

More detailed measurements of outcropping paleocurrent indicators over a broader part of the Dam Formation would help in paleoshoreline reconstruction and improve understanding of shoreline heterogeneity.
