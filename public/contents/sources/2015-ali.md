# High-resolution stratigraphic architecture and sedimentological heterogeneity within the Miocene Dam Formation, Eastern Province, Eastern Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the stratigraphic architecture and sedimentological heterogeneity of the Miocene Dam Formation in the Al-Lidam Area, Eastern Province, Saudi Arabia. Using a combination of field mapping, thin-section petrography, XRD, and SEM-EDS analysis, the study identifies 17 lithofacies and defines four major composite sequences to characterize the carbonate-siliciclastic system.

---

## Research Problem

The Dam Formation contains complex mixed carbonate-siliciclastic successions where standard subsurface data is often limited. There is a need to understand its high-resolution stratigraphic architecture, facies heterogeneity, and the influence of sea-level changes on depositional environments in the Al-Lidam Area.

## Objectives

- To determine the facies architecture and heterogeneity (microscale, mesoscale, and macro-scale) and attempt correlation of sequences in the Dam Formation outcrops in Al-Lidam along a NS-transect.
- To characterize Miocene Dam Formation in terms of sedimentology and perform 2D-correlation.
- To identify carbonate geobodies and architecture for understanding depositional environment.
- Identify and characterize lithofacies within the Dam Formation at the Al-Lidam area.
- Determine the sedimentary structures and mineralogical composition of various carbonate and siliciclastic units.
- Establish a high-resolution stratigraphic model by identifying sequence boundaries, system tracts, and cycles.

## Methods

The study integrated field mapping (including high-resolution photography, lithostratigraphic logging, and sedimentary structure analysis) with laboratory analyses. Field methods included identifying lithofacies based on grain types using the Dunham classification, determining cyclicity/stratigraphy, and measuring paleocurrent directions. Laboratory techniques included thin-section petrography to identify mineralogy and texture, SEM-EDS for mineralogical determination and identification of dissolution features, and XRD to determine carbonate vs. siliciclastic content.

## Data and Materials

Data includes 17 distinct lithofacies across 5 main outcrop locations in the Al-Lidam Area. Geobody dimensions were recorded for sheet, lens, and channel geometries. Petrographic observations were made on oolitic grainstones and other carbonate facies. Geochemical data (XRD, SEM, EDS) were collected to categorize facies into four groups based on mineral content.

## Key Findings

- The Dam Formation is characterized as a mixed carbonate-siliciclastic unit with distinct facies including estuarine quartzose channelized calcareous sandstone, skeletal grainstone sheets/mounds, red mudstone beds (paleosols), and oolitic grainstones. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 14]])
- The lithofacies Coated Skeletal Grainstone (Csg) shows a porosity range of 25% to 30% and is composed of over 95% carbonate with only ~5% quartz. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 48]])
- The Peloidal Burrowed Wackestone (Pbw) facies has a lower porosity range of less than 5-7% and contains approximately 14% quartz. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 53]])
- Ooid grainstone (Og) shows a porosity ranging from 15 to 20% and is composed of more than 95% carbonate. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 58]])
- The study identified four main sequences (CS1, CS2, CS3, and CS4) based on cyclicity and stacking patterns, with CS2 further subdivided into HFS-1 and HFS-2. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 14]])
- A significant increase in the percentage of siliciclastic sediments is observed in the updip (northeast) sections, while carbonates dominate in downdip (southwest) sections. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 15]])
- The presence of oolitic grainstone and stromatolites indicates a shallow subtidal to intertidal environment under arid/hypersaline conditions. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 25]])
- The study identified 17 distinct lithofacies within the Dam Formation, including ooid grainstone, skeletal wackestone, and quartz sandstone. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 110]])
- Ooid grainstone facies (Spg) shows average porosity of 15-20% in some sections with large grains and micritised envelopes. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 59]])
- Micritised oolitic peloidal grainstone (Mpg) is the most frequent facies, showing carbonate dominance in XRD and Ca, Mg, Si abundance in EDS. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 61]])
- Sandy peloidal skeletal grainstone (Spg) contains 70% carbonate and 30% quartz with porosity ranging from 10-15%. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 64]])
- Dolomitic Skeletal Wackestone (Dsw) is composed of >95% carbonate and <5% quartz, showing evidence of dolomitization. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 66]])
- Foraminiferal peloidal grainstone (Fpg) shows carbonate content >86% and contains minerals like dolomite as indicated by EDS. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 68]])
- Sandy peloidal grainstone-packstone (Spgp) is composed of >60% carbonate and 40% quartz with porosity <5-10%. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 71]])
- Peloidal packstone (Pp) shows carbonate content >86% and 14% quartz, with some portions showing dolomitization. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 74]])
