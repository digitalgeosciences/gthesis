# HIGH RESOLUTION SEDIMENTOLOGOY AND RESERVOIR PROPERTIES OF ULAYYAH RESERVOIR EQUIVALENT, UPPER JURASSIC HANIFA FORMATION, OUTCROP APPROACH, CENTRAL SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the sedimentology and reservoir properties of the Ulayyah reservoir equivalent within the Hanifa formation in Central Saudi Arabia using an outcrop approach. It highlights how sedimentary architecture, specifically at different scales (large, medium, and small), impacts porosity and permeability, and explores the integration of digital tools like LiDAR and photogrammetry into geostatistical modeling.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of detailed understanding of intra-reservoir stratigraphy and heterogeneity within the Ulayyah Member due to limited subsurface data. The study addresses this by using an outcrop analog in central Saudi Arabia to provide a high-resolution model of lithofacies, petrophysical properties (porosity/permeability), and depositional environments.

## Objectives

- Propose the paleo-depositional environment based on lithofacies zones description and their biofacies assemblages of the outcrop succession
- Develop high resolution 3-D sedimentological and stratigraphical models.
- Characterize the outcrop using Spectral Gamma Ray (SGR) logs and geochemical signature
- Distribute outcrop porosity and permeability data into the high resolution 3-D facies model.
- Describe sedimentary lithofacies and determine their depositional environments for the Ullayah Reservoir equivalent.
- Establish a high-resolution sequence stratigraphic framework using field observations and chemostratigraphy.

## Methods

The study employed a multidisciplinary approach: (1) Field investigation including detailed sedimentological mapping, lithofacies identification, and sampling of every bed; (2) Laboratory work involving thin-section petrography with alizarin red staining to identify carbonate minerals; (3) Spectral Gamma Ray (SGR) logging using a 1024-channel spectrometer to measure U, Th, and K levels for lithofacies correlation; (4) Geochemical analysis including XRF on 80 samples, SEM-EDS, and XRD on 10 selected samples; (5) Petrophysical testing of 75 core plugs using a saturation apparatus and TKA-209 gas permeameter to measure porosity and permeability; (6) Geostatistical modeling in Petrel software using Sequential Indicator Simulation (SIS) for facies and Sequential Gaussian Simulation (SGS) for property distribution.

## Data and Materials

The study utilized 150 total samples from the outcrop area, of which 80 were selected for geochemical analysis, 80 for petrography, and 75 core plugs were used for porosity and permeability measurements. SGR data was collected at 20 cm intervals across several stratigraphic sections.

## Key Findings

- The Ulayyah Member is composed of medium- to thick-bedded, mostly grainy limestones with various skeletal (brachiopods, bivalves, foraminifera, Cladocoropsis, encrusting stromatoporoid, corals and echinoderms) and non--skeletal (peloids, ooids, intraclasts, and oncoids) components. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 12]])
- Field analysis revealed six high frequency sequences of fourth-order depositional sequences in each of the four stratigraphic measured sections. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 13]])
- High stand systems tracts (HST) show shallowing-upward trends in which deep-water facies are overlain by shallow-water facies. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 13]])
- The best reservoir potential is attributed to the cross-stratified well sorted and laminated sandy grainstone of sand shoal with a maximum porosity value of 32-25%. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 13]])
- SGR and chemical analysis signatures show moderate correlation with lithofacies for each high frequencies sequence, their sequence hierarchy and stacking pattern. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 13]])
- The outcrop analog 3D model revealed heterogeneity of the reservoir lithofacies at a higher resolution than that in the subsurface model. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 14]])
- SGR log provides significant rock component information (Th, K, U) used for lithofacies correlation and sequence stratigraphy. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 43]])
- The study identified that the best reservoir potential is located in the platform margin. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 13]])
- The Ullayah Reservoir is characterized by a variety of lithofacies including lime mudstone, spiculitic wackestone, and several types of grainstones (e.g., oolitic, skeletal, and intraclast-rich). ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 70]])
- Spiculitic wackestones show moldic porosity ranging from 2-8% due to the dissolution of silica. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 71]])
- The presence of Cladocoropsis and dasyclad indicates a variety of lagoonal environments, with some areas being restricted or high-energy. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 96]])
- Chemostratigraphy revealed two distinct zones: the lower part shows higher clay content (Al2O3, SiO2) and higher U/Th concentrations in SGR logs. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 101]])
- SGR analysis showed that Potassium (K) and Thorium (Th) are indicators of aluminosilicate content, while Uranium (U) is associated with specific carbonate-dominated intervals. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 104]])
- The study identified three main zones for modeling: Deep Lagoon, Shallow Lagoon, and Shoal Lime Sand. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 113]])
- Porosity values were found to be higher in the reservoir-quality lithofacies (cross-bedded skeletal grainstone and laminated sandy grainstone). ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 133]])

## Limitations

The variogram calculation module in PETREL does not provide individual data points, which limits the ability to perform complex multi-variate analysis (e.g., nested functions) within that specific software.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Use LiDAR techniques for the Ulayyah reservoir equivalent of the Hanifa formation; conduct comprehensive diagenesis analysis to identify numerous reservoir units within the Ulayyah member.
