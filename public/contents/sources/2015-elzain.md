# HIGH RESOLUTION SEDIMENTOLOGOY AND RESERVOIR PROPERTIES OF ULAYYAH RESERVOIR EQUIV_ALENT, UPPER JURASSIC HANIFA FORMATION, OUTCROP APPROACH CENTRAL SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the Upper Jurassic Hanifa Formation in central Saudi Arabia, specifically focusing on a stratigraphically equivalent unit to the Middle Jurassic Upper Ulayyah reservoir. By integrating sedimentological, chemostratigraphical, biostratigraphical, and spectral gamma-ray data with 3D modeling, it characterizes lithofacies heterogeneity and predicts reservoir properties at sub-seismic scales.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the lack of detailed understanding regarding intra-reservoir stratigraphy, sedimentology, and heterogeneity within Jurassic carbonate reservoirs (specifically the Ulayyah reservoir equivalent in the Hanifa formation), which are critical for accurately modeling fluid flow paths and maximizing recovery rates.

## Objectives

- Characterize a strata equivalent to the Middle Jurassic Upper Ulayyah reservoir units using high-resolution outcrop analogs.
- Integrate multi-disciplinary data (sedimentological, chemostratigraphical, biostratigraphical, and gamma ray) to understand lithofacies and depositional environments.
- Develop 3D models of facies, porosity, and permeability based on outcrop data to provide predictive information at sub-seismic scales.
- Propose the paleo-depositional environment based on lithofacies zones description and their biofacies assemblages of the outcrop succession
- Develop high resolution 3-D sedimentological and stratigraphical models.
- Characterize the outcrop using Spectral Gamma Ray (SGR) logs and geochemical signature
- Distribute outcrop porosity and permeability data into the high resolution 3-D facies model.
- Conduct high-resolution sedimentological and stratigraphic investigations at the Ulayyah Member outcrop analog.
- Characterize lithofacies and determine their corresponding depositional environments.
- Quantify petrophysical properties (porosity and permeability) of the reservoir rocks.
- Develop a geostatistical model to represent the 3D distribution of facies and property values.
- Perform high-resolution sedimentological analysis of the Ulayyah Reservoir Equivalent.
- Characterize lithofacies and their associated reservoir properties (porosity, permeability).
- Utilize chemostratigraphy to identify geochemically distinct units and track mineralogical changes.
- Apply Spectral Gamma Ray (SGR) logging to differentiate between siliciclastic-rich and carbonate-dominated intervals.
- Develop a 3D outcrop model of facies, porosity, and permeability to bridge the gap between seismic and well data.
- Characterize high-resolution sedimentary architecture of the Ulayyah reservoir.
- Quantify spatial variability of lithofacies and petrophysical properties (porosity, permeability) using semivariogram analysis.
- Develop 3D geocellular models to represent the distribution of lithofacies and reservoir properties.
- Evaluate the impact of sedimentary architecture on reservoir quality indices.
- Perform high-resolution sedimentological characterization of the Ulayyah reservoir equivalent.
- Evaluate reservoir properties within the Hanifa Formation.
- Utilize geostatistical modeling (variograms) to model lithofacies distribution.
- Assess the utility of digital outcrop models (DOM) and LiDAR data in improving reservoir model accuracy.
- Characterize the reservoir properties and facies architecture of the Hanifa Formation.
- Evaluate the impact of intra-reservoir heterogeneity on reservoir quality.

## Methods

The methodology involves field investigation including detailed sedimentological mapping, bed-by-bed sampling, and high-resolution stratigraphic logging. Laboratory work includes thin-section petrography (with alizarin red staining), XRF analysis for geochemistry, and SEM-EDS/XRD for mineralogy. Petrophysical properties were measured using a saturation apparatus and TKA-209 gas permeameter systems on core plugs. Geostatistical modeling was performed in Petrel software using Sequential Indicator Simulation (SIS) for facies modeling and Sequential Gaussian Simulation (SGS) for property modeling, incorporating 20 realizations of porosity and permeability.

## Data and Materials

The study utilized samples from the Ulayyah Member outcrop analog at Jabal Abakkayn. Data included: 80 representative samples for geochemistry/petrography; 150 total samples collected during field work; 37-40 measurements per section for SGR logging (using a Gamma Surveyor); and core plugs for petrophysical analysis.

## Key Findings

- The Upper Jurassic (Oxfordian-Kimmeridgian) Upper Ulayyah Reservoir strata are composed of medium- to thick-bedded, mostly grainy limestones with various skeletal and non-skeletal components. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 12]])
- Field analysis revealed six high-frequency sequences of fourth-order depositional sequences in each of the four stratigraphic measured sections. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 13]])
- High stand systems tracts (HST) show shallowing-upward trends where deep-water facies are overlain by shallow-water facies. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 13]])
- The best reservoir potential is attributed to the cross-stratified well sorted and laminated sandy grainstone of sand shoal with a maximum porosity value of 32-25%. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 13]])
- SGR and chemical analysis signatures show moderate correlation with lithofacies for each high frequency sequence, their sequence hierarchy, and stacking pattern. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 13]])
- The geochemical data shows marked differences in character and distribution of different lithofacies, which might help in reservoir layering and zonation. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 13]])
- The 3D model revealed heterogeneity of the reservoir lithofacies at a higher resolution than that in the subsurface model. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 14]])
- Lithofacies include lime mudstone, burrowed wackestone, burrowed packstone, oncloid fossiliferous intraclast grainstone, oolitic skeletal dominated grainstone, massive skeletal intraclast grainstone, stromatoporoid-cladocropsis wackestone/packstone, foraminiferal skeletal grainstone, x-bedded skeletal sandy grainstone, and laminated skeletal sandy grainstone. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 7]])
- The Ulayyah Member is late Oxfordian in its basal part (Alveosepta jaccardi) and potentially early Kimmeridgian in its upper part (echinid faunas). ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 42]])
- The Hanifa Formation is characterized by aggrading and prograding grainstones in the Ulayyah member. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 31]])
- The Hanifa Formation represents a major source-rock bed for most of the Jurassic reservoirs in Saudi Arabia, with average TOC values of about 3 wt.% (up to 13 wt.%). ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 36]])
- The Ulayyah Member is situated between the Hawtah Member and the Jubaila Formation. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 41]])
- Spiculitic wackestones show moldic porosity of 2-8% resulting from the dissolution of Demospongia spicules. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 72]])
- Skeletal and oolitic carbonate grainstones (from literature) showed a maximum porosity of 23% and permeability up to 700 mD. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 45]])
- The SGR log provides significant rock component information, used for lithofacies correlation and sequence stratigraphy. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 43]])

## Limitations

The variogram calculation module in Petrel does not provide individual data points, which limits the ability to perform more refined calculations (e.g., using nested functions). This suggests the use of alternative software like GSLIB or SGeMS for advanced variogram analysis.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The study provides an outcrop analog that is predictive and provides quantitative information at the sub-seismic scale regarding the distribution, size, and heterogeneities of the reservoir rock lithofacies.
