# SOURCE-ROCK CHARACTERIZATION OF LATE JURASSIC (EARLY KIMMERIDGIAN) CARBONATE MUDROCKS OF THE JAFURAH SUB-BASIN, EASTERN SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the petroleum generation potential, kerogen type, and thermal maturity of Late Jurassic (Early Kimmeridgian) carbonate mudrocks in the Jafurah sub-basin. Using RockEval pyrolysis on over 150 samples and subsequent GC-MS analysis on bitumen extracts, the study identifies several lithofacies with significant organic richness and high hydrocarbon potential.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the lack of data regarding the petroleum generation potential and geological characteristics of a relatively unknown and minimally studied late Jurassic (early Kimmeridgian) source-rock interval in the Jafurah sub-basin.

## Objectives

- Quantitative characterization and typing of the studied source formation by determining TOC and geochemical attributes.
- Identification of hydrocarbon components and biomarkers to determine the origin and environment of deposition.
- Evaluation of the thermal maturity of the source formation through pyrolysis and biomarker correlation.
- Integration of these geochemical results with other components of the overall project.
- Investigate organic richness and generation potential using RockEval pyrolysis
- Determine kerogen type and origin of the organic matter

## Methods

The study utilized RockEval 6 pyrolysis on 150 samples to determine TOC, S1, S2, S3, Tmax, HI, OI, and PI. A subset of 15 samples underwent bitumen extraction using a Dionex Accelerated Solvent Extractor (ASE) 200 with dichloromethane as the solvent. These extracts were further fractionated into aliphatic and aromatic components via Solid Phase Extraction (SPE). Finally, Gas Chromatography (GC) and Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS) were used to identify biomarkers and determine maturity indices like CPI, TMNR, MPI-1, and MPR.

## Data and Materials

Data from 5 wells (J-1 to J-5) in the Jafurah sub-basin; over 150 RockEval pyrolysis samples; 15 bitumen extraction and GC-MS samples; analysis of 12 distinct lithofacies.

## Key Findings

- Lithofacies 2, 6, 8, 10, and 11 were characterized as poor to fair source-rock lithofacies with TOC values from 0.5 to 2.0% and average HI of 130 to 140 mgHC/gTOC. ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 77]])
- Lithofacies 1 and 12 were identified as non-source rock lithofacies due to low TOC values (less than 1%). ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 77]])
- Tmax results for samples with TOC > 2% indicated a mature oil to condensate-wet gas window, with estimated Vitrinite reflectance (VRo) between 0.9 and 1.2%. ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 54]])
- Carbon Preference Index (CPI) values for the extracted bitumen samples ranged from 0.9 to 1.4, indicating mature organic matter input. ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 58]])
- The presence of high-abundance methylated biomarkers in the aromatic fraction suggested a significant algal contribution to the source rock. ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 72]])
- Retene was found in minor amounts, indicating lower terrestrial input and higher marine organic matter content. ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 71]])
- Lithofacies 4 and 7 are characterized as good to excellent source-rock lithofacies with TOC values ranging from 3.0 to 5.5% and average HI of 190 to 225 mgHC/gTOC. ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 78]])
- CPI values ranged from 0.9 to 1.0, indicating a mature marine source for the organic matter. ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 78]])
- Sterane C27 concentration indicates a marine source of origin, while the overall distribution in ternary diagrams shows an open marine and mixed planktonic-bacterial zone. ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 80]])
- Cross-plots of sterane C27/(C27+C29) versus Pr/Ph indicate pelagic anoxic conditions in an open-marine environment. ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 80]])
- Low DBT/P and Pr/Ph ratios (falling in Zones 1B and 2) confirm deposition under reducing conditions with varying availability of reactive sulfur and iron. ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 83]])
- Kerogen type was identified as mixed Type II/III and Type III, but further analysis using Pr/Ph and n-C17/n-C18 ratios suggests a primary Type II kerogen source that is mature. ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 85]])
- Tmax values from pyrolysis (after extraction) ranged from 451 to 468°C, corresponding to a vitrinite reflectance (VRo) of 0.9 to 1.2% (normal oil to condensate wet gas window). ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 88]])
- Biomarker-derived maturity estimates ranged from 0.7 to 1.0% VRo, which are lower than pyrolysis values due to the lack of clay minerals in carbonate-rich rocks. ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 91]])

## Limitations

The study notes that the absence of clays in carbonate-rich source rocks reduces the catalytic activity required for biomarker transformations, potentially leading to lower thermal maturity estimations from biomarkers compared to pyrolysis.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Perform organic petrography to directly determine vitrinite reflectance values.
- Conduct organic stable isotope analyses to refine origin and depositional environment.
- Perform SARA analysis to quantify asphaltenes and resins.
- Perform oil-to-source correlation if oil samples are available.
- Use two-phase fluid inclusions in minerals like dolomite to estimate reservoir conditions.
