# Lower Cretaceous stratigraphy and depositional history of the Rub' Al Khali basin

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the Lower Cretaceous Thamama Group in the Rub' Al Khali basin, focusing on the stratigraphic sequence and depositional history of the Sulaiy, Yamama, Buwaib/Biyadh (undiff.), and Shu'aiba Formations. It identifies two major shallowing-upward carbonate cycles and characterizes the transition from deep-water mudstones to shallow-marine carbonates.

---

## Research Problem

The complexity of defining and correlating the Lower Cretaceous Thamama Group units in the Rub' Al Khali basin due to lateral facies changes (e.g., the transition from Biyadh sandstone to limestone) and varying regional interpretations of stratigraphic boundaries.

## Objectives

- To correlate stratigraphic units and propose a practical stratigraphic nomenclature applicable within the whole basin.
- To define the general facies distribution and sedimentary environments of the Lower Cretaceous rocks in the Rub' Al Khali basin.
- Define the stratigraphy of the Tham1ama Group in the Rub' Al Khali basin.
- Establish a consistent correlation between outcrop data and subsurface wireline logs.
- Determine the depositional history and cycles of the Lower Cretaceous sequence.
- Determine the stratigraphic sequence and thickness variation of the Sulaiy Formation.
- Characterize the lithology and depositional environment of the Yamama Formation.
- Define the boundaries and internal members of the Buwaib/Biyadh (undiff.) formation.
- Evaluate the extent and characteristics of the Shu'aiba Formation.
- Establish the depositional history and cycle analysis of the Thamama Group.
- Determine the stratigraphy of the Lower Cretaceous Thamama Group in the Rub' Al Khali basin.
- Establish the depositional history and environmental changes during the Lower Cretaceous.
- Correlate lithological units across the Rub' Al Khali basin.

## Methods

The study utilized data from 23 exploratory wells and 7 stratigraphic test wells. Analysis included well-log interpretation (gamma ray, sonic, formation-density, and neutron logs) to establish stratigraphic divisions. Additionally, 28 cores totaling over 400 feet were analyzed for lithofacies identification. Over 30 samples were prepared as thin sections for petrographic description using the Dunham (1962) carbonate classification.

## Data and Materials

Data includes well logs from multiple wells (e.g., W20, W13, W26, W8), core samples from various intervals, Saudi Aramco lithologic logs, and isopach maps of the Rub' Al Khali basin.

## Key Findings

- The Thamama Group is over 2900 feet (884 m) thick and consists of the Sulaiy, Yamama, Buwaib/Biyadh (undiff.), and Shu'aiba Formations. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 15]])
- Lower Cretaceous sediments were deposited during two major shallowing-upward cycles: the first starting with lime mudstone of the Sulaiy and ending with wackestone/packstone of the Yamama; the second beginning with argillaceous lime mudstone of the basal Buwaib/Biyadh and ending with thick skeletal packstone/grainstone of the Shu'aiba. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 15]])
- Thick wedges of siliciclastics, the Biyadh Sandstone, prograded into the basin from the west. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 15]])
- The Thamama Group is over 2900 feet (884m) thick in the Rub' Al Khali basin. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 45]])
- The Sulaiy Formation is characterized by fine-grained lime mudstone and wackestone, representing a deeper water phase compared to the Hith Anhydrite. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 49]])
- The Yamama Formation consists of calcarenite and calcarenitic limestone, transitioning from the Sulaiy's lime mudstone. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 38]])
- The Buwaib Formation is interpreted as a basinal marine sediment (mudstones/limestones) deposited after a period of erosion following Yamama deposition. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 49]])
- The Biyadh Sandstone represents a clastic wedge in the west, transitioning to limestone facies toward the east. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 64]])
- The Shu'aiba Formation is an Aptian-aged carbonate horizon that forms the uppermost unit of the Thamama Group. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 32]])
- Three distinct depositional cycles are identified: (1) Sulaiy and Yamama, (2) Buwaib and Biyadh, and (3) Shu'aiba. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 50]])
- The Sulaiy Formation reaches a maximum thickness of nearly 670 ft (204 meters) at Well W20 in the northeastern Rub' Al Khali basin, and thins to approximately 530-557 ft in the western areas. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 67]])
- The Sulaiy Formation is a widespread carbonate unit consisting mainly of lime mudstone to fine pelletoidal wackestone in northern, central and western Rub' Al Khali basin. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 68]])
- The Yamama Formation attains its maximum thickness of nearly 500 feet (152 m) in the northeastern corner of the study area. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 74]])
- The Yamama Formation is characterized by interbedded porous pelletoidal lime packstone to lime wackestone and argillaceous lime mudstone in the northern and northwestern parts, while it becomes more dolomitic and anhydritic to the northeast. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 74]])
- The Buwaib/Biyadh (undiff.) formation is divided into three informal members: a lower member (argillaceous lime mudstone to basinal shale), a middle member (cyclic shallow shelf carbonates), and an upper member (porous, pelletal and skeletal wackestone to packstone). ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 85]])

## Limitations

The distinction between the Buwaib and Biyadh formations is difficult to maintain in certain areas due to lateral facies changes from sandstone to limestone.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Further detailed study is required to formally extend the terminology in the whole basin.
