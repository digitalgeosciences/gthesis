# Lower Cretaceous stratigraphy and depositional history of the Rub' Al Khali basin

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the Lower Cretaceous Thamama Group in the Rub' Al Khali basin, focusing on its stratigraphic sequence and depositional history. It identifies three major carbonate cycles (Sulaiy, Yamama, and Buwaib/Biyadh) and concludes that these units represent a series of shallowing-upward sequences reflecting significant sea-level changes.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the complex stratigraphy and depositional history of the Lower Cretaceous Thamama Group in the Rub' Al Khali basin, specifically characterizing the transition from deep to shallow marine environments during the Early Cretaceous.

## Objectives

- To correlate stratigraphic units and propose a practical stratigraphic nomenclature applicable within the whole basin.
- To define the general facies distribution and sedimentary environments of the Lower Cretaceous rocks in the Rub' Al Khali basin.
- Define the stratigraphic boundaries and lithology of the Sulaiy, Yamama, Buwaib/Suiya (undiff.), and Shu'aiba Formations.
- Analyze the depositional history and environmental changes within the Thamama Group.
- Evaluate the potential for hydrocarbon accumulation in these units based on porosity and thickness.
- Determine the stratigraphy and depositional history of the Lower Cretaceous Thamama Group.

## Methods

The study utilized 23 exploratory wells and 7 stratigraphic test wells. Analysis included well-log data (gamma ray, sonic, formation-density, and neutron logs) to establish and correlate major stratigraphic divisions. Over 400 feet of core from 11 wells were analyzed for lithofacies identification. Petrographic analysis was performed on over 30 samples prepared as thin sections.

## Data and Materials

Data includes well logs (wireline and lithologic) from the Rub' Al Khali basin, isopach maps for Sulaiy and Yamama Formations, and stratigraphic sections across the study area.

## Key Findings

- The Thamama Group is over 2900 feet (884m) thick in the Rub' Al Khali basin. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 15]])
- The first depositional cycle began with lime mudstone of the Sulaiy and concluded with wackestone and packstone interbeds of the Yamama. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 15]])
- The second cycle began with argillaceous lime mudstone of the basal Buwaib/Biyadh (undiff.) and concluded with thick skeletal packstone and grainstone of the Shu'aiba. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 15]])
- The Biyadh Sandstone is interpreted to be continental sands, prograding into the basin from the west. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 15]])
- A widespread unconformity (post-Thamama) concludes the Lower Cretaceous and separates it from the Wasia Group. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 15]])
- The Sulaiy Formation is considered to be of possible Late Jurassic to Early Cretaceous (Tithonian - Valanginian) age based on Pseudocyclammina sulaiyana. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 37]])
- The Yamama Formation is considered to be Valanginian in age based on Everticyclammina eccentrica, Pseudocyclammina cylindrica, and Pygurus rostratus. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 38]])
- The Buwaib Formation is interpreted to be Early Cretaceous (Hauterivian) based on Everticyclammina greigi and E. hensoni. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 39]])
- The Shu'aiba Formation is of Aptian age and represents the uppermost unit of the Thamama Group. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 48]])
- The first cycle (Sulaiy-Yamama) was deposited during an initial Cretaceous flooding of the stable cratonal platform. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 50]])
- The Sulaiy Formation is a widespread carbonate unit consisting mainly of lime mudstone to fine pelletoidal wackestone, reaching a maximum thickness of nearly 670 ft (204 meters) at Well W20. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 67]])
- The Yamama Formation is characterized by interbedded porous pelletoidal lime packstone to lime wackestone and argillaceous lime mudstone in the northern/northwestern parts, while it becomes more dolomitic and anhydritic toward the northeast. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 74]])
- The Buwaib/Biyadh (undiff.) formation is divided into three informal members: a lower member (argillaceous lime mudstone to grainstone), a middle member (marine limestone with many shallowing-upward subcycles), and an upper member (porous, pelletal, and skeletal wackestone to packstone). ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 85]])
- The Shu'aiba Formation is a thick, porous carbonate unit representing the last and thickest shallowing-upward cycle of the Lower Cretaceous. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 100]])
- A significant pre-Wasia regional unconformity marks a major drop in sea level and a 1.5 million-year hiatus at the top of the Shu'aiba Formation. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 93]])

## Limitations

Difficulty in separating Buwaib Formation from both underlying Yamama and overlying Biyadh in many wells due to lack of distinct markers.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Further detailed study is required in order to formally extend the terminology in the whole basin.
