# Effect of a Hydraulic Fracture on the Performance of a Layered Gas Condensate Reservoir

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the safety practices and requirements for residential buildings in Saudi Arabia, specifically focusing on fire and smoke control measures. It evaluates the effectiveness of current design practices by surveying design offices and residents across several cities including Dammam, Khobar, Mullah, Jeddah, and Taif.

---

## Research Problem

There is a lack of awareness and consistent implementation of safety measures (particularly fire and smoke control) in the design and use of residential buildings in Saudi Arabia, with significant differences in safety focus between low-rise (villas/LRAB) and high-rise apartment buildings (HRAB).

## Objectives

- To study the effect of a hydraulic fracture on the performance of a layered gas condensate reservoir using a compositional simulator.
- To compare the condensate saturation distribution before and after the fracturing around the wellbore.
- To study the effect of fracture length and fracture conductivity on the long term productivity.
- To determine the optimum fracture parameters for the system considered.
- To study the effect of flow rate on the performance of fractured gas condensate reservoirs.
- To study the development in the layered system with and without cross-flow between layers.

## Methods

The research was conducted in six phases: 1) Literature Review (gathering info on building safety codes/practices); 2) Questionnaire Design (two types: one for designers, one for owners/tenards); 3) Administration of Questionnaires (distributed to design offices and residents in Dammam, Khobar, Makkah, Jeddah, and Taif); 4) Data Analysis (using Statistical Analysis System (SAS) to calculate Effectiveness Index (EI)); 5) Development of Residential Safety Compliance checklist; 6) Formulation of Safety Audit Checklist.

## Data and Materials

The study uses actual reservoir fluid data from a Saudi Arabian gas condensate reservoir (Table 4.1). Simulation models included a single-layer system for validation against Gringarten et al. [32] and Cinco Lee et. al [33], and a multi-layered system to model the complex flow behavior of the field.

## Key Findings

- Hydraulic fracturing was effective in reducing the effect of condensate blockage on productivity index (PI), increasing it up to 3.6 times below the dew point pressure after 10 years compared to a non-fractured well. ([[2000-hashmi|Hashmi, 2000, p. 22]])
- Hydraulic fracturing resulted in an increase of 60% cumulative gas production and 43% cumulative condensate production. ([[2000-hashmi|Hashmi, 2000, p. 22]])
- The study found that the effect of increased fracture conductivity was more pronounced for a longer fracture. ([[2000-hashmi|Hashmi, 2000, p. 22]])

- The increase in fracture length improved long-term performance, but the effect was much more pronounced for a highly conductive fracture (Fcp > 5). ([[2000-hashmi|Hashmi, 2000, p. 22]])
- Formation cross-flow improved the production performance of a gas condensate reservoir. ([[2000-hashmi|Hashmi, 2000, p. 22]])
- The average permeability of the five layers ranged from 0.0815 md to 115.736 md, with an average of 7.4024 md. ([[2000-hashmi|Hashmi, 2000, p. 40]])
- Porosity varied between 3.19% and 21.3%, with an average of 10.13%. ([[2000-hashmi|Hashmi, 2000, p. 40]])
- The study determined a pore size distribution index (n) of 0.73 based on Leverett J-function analysis. ([[2000-hashmi|Hashmi, 2000, p. 49]])
- Hydraulic fracturing increases the time at which the dew point pressure is reached during depletion due to a larger inflow area. ([[2000-hashmi|Hashmi, 2000, p. 99]])
- The production rate of gas in the fractured case was more than 3 times higher than the non-fractured case after 10 years. ([[2000-hashmi|Hashmi, 2000, p. 101]])
- Condensate production in the fractured case was approximately twice as high as the non-fractured case after 10 years. ([[2000-hashmi|Hashmi, 2000, p. 101]])
- The productivity index (PI) of the fractured well was 3.6 times larger than the non-fractured case after 10 years. ([[2000-hashmi|Hashmi, 2000, p. 106]])
- cumulative gas production in the fractured case is more than 60% higher than the non-fractured case over a 10-year period. ([[2000-hashmi|Hashmi, 2000]])

## Limitations

The study notes that the lack of clear rules or enforcement by authorities for low-rise buildings leads to inconsistent safety practices. Additionally, it highlights that while designers claim electrical safety is well-considered, civil defense reports indicate electrical shocks are a primary cause of fire accidents.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Designers should give more attention to discussing safety issues with clients to increase awareness and willingness to invest in safety measures. Authorities should ensure consistent enforcement of safety standards across all building types (villas, LRAB, and HRAB).
