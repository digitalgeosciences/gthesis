# Digital outcrop properties and facies modelling of the Lower Silurian Qusaiba Member and Sharawra Member, Qalibah Formation

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the Qalibah Formation (Qusaiba and Sharawra Members) in the Qassim Region using a combination of digital outcrop modeling, petrophysical analysis, and geochemistry. It concludes that integrating high-resolution 3D digital outcrop models with traditional sedimentology and petrophysics provides a more accurate representation of reservoir heterogeneity compared to conventional methods.

---

## Research Problem

The Qalibah Formation exhibits significant vertical and horizontal heterogeneity in the Qusiba and Sharawra Members, which is difficult to characterize using standard methods due to complex facies distributions and steep outcrops. There is a lack of integrated 3D digital outcrop modeling and detailed petrophysical analysis for this formation in the Qassim Region.

## Objectives

- Construction of facies analysis to better understand lithological variations in space and time.
- Obtaining petrophysical properties (porosity, permeability, geochemical composition, and spectral gamma ray) to provide information on reservoir characteristics.
- Constructing 3D static models utilizing outcrop facies and petrophysical properties integrated with photogrammetric data from drone imaging.
- Identify and analyze distinct lithofacies within the Qalibah Formation.
- Evaluate the petrophysical properties (porosity and permeability) of the Qusaiba and Sharawra Members.
- Construct 3D static models using Digital Outcrop Modeling to visualize lithofacies and property distribution.

## Methods

The study utilized four outcrops near Qusaiba Village. Methodology included: 1) Sedimentological/stratigraphic investigation (lithology, grain size, structures, bed geometry). 2) Systematic sample collection (42 samples per outcrop). 3) Photogrammetry using ground-based and drone imagery (~40,000 photos). 4) Survey-grade GPS (Trimble R10) for georeferencing. 5) Core plug preparation (1.5 inch diameter) from hand samples. 6) Petrophysical analysis: Helium porosity, Nitrogen permeability, NMR, and Spot permeability. 7) Geochemical analysis: Powdered XRD (PANalytical Empyrean) and XRF. 8) Digital Outcrop Modeling: Point cloud generation, TIN mesh creation, and integration into ArcGIS for surface identification. 9) Petrel modeling: Construction of 1m x 1m cell size grids with up to 400 layers across three zones to model facies, porosity, and permeability.

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes data from the Qalibah Formation, specifically focusing on the Qusaiba Member and Sharawra Member. Data includes core samples, outcrop measurements (four specific outcrops: OC1, OC3, OC4), and geochemical profiles to characterize lithofacies and petrophysical properties.

## Key Findings

- Overall porosity values in the Qalibah Formation range from 0.5% to 34%, while permeability ranges from 0.01 mD to 550 mD. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 42]])
- Lithofacies 1 (LF1) in the Qusaiba Member exhibits the lowest porosity (1-5%) and permeability (0.01-0.1 mD). ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 42]])
- Lithofacies 3 (LF3) in the Sharawra Member shows the highest values, with porosity from 17% to 35% and permeability from 70 mD to 550 mD. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 42]])
- XRF analysis of the Qusaiba Member revealed high concentrations of sulfur (S) and iron (Fe), while XRD showed low Si content and high illite/kaolinite trends. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 54]])
- The Sharawra Member shows a rapid decrease in sulfur, with increasing potassium (K) and decreasing iron (Fe) and zinc (Zn) in XRF results. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 54]])
- Tawil Formation is characterized by a distinct geochemical signature of high calcium (Ca) in XRF and predominantly calcite in XRD. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 54]])
- The Qusaiba Member is primarily composed of laminated shale and mudstone, while the Sharawra Member consists mainly of sandstone with interbedded siltstones/mudstones. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 25]])
- A significant sedimentary hiatus in the uppermost sections of the sequence is attributed to uplift from the Caledonian Orogeny. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 26]])
- The Qalibah Formation contains four distinct lithofacies: LF1 (fissile shale with sandstone lenses), LF2 (heterolithic sandstones/mudstones), LF3 (hummocky cross-stratified sandstones), and the Tawil Formation's sandstone/carbonate alternations. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 80]])
- The Qusaiba Member is characterized by the lowest porosity and permeability values due to its compact and fine-grained nature, though it exhibits local fluctuations from sand/siltstone lenses. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 81]])
- The Sharawra Member shows higher porosity and permeability values compared to the Qusaiba Member, with a distinct transition between LF2 and LF3 corresponding to changes in hydrodynamic conditions. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 81]])
- Lithofacies 3 (LF3) of the Sharawra Member is interpreted as storm events deposited above the storm weather wave base based on well-preserved hummocky cross-stratification. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 80]])
- The Tawil Formation shows significant horizontal variations between outcrops, likely due to the random collection of carbonate and sandstone samples. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 81]])
- Lithofacies 4 (LF4) of the Tawil Formation exhibits the highest porosity and permeability, indicating a high-energy depositional environment with coarser, less packed sediments. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 81]])
- 3D static models constructed using Petrel and Digital Outcrop Modeling provided high-resolution visualization of lithofacies distribution and petrophysical properties. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 84]])
