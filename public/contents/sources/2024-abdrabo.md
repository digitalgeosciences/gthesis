# Digital outcrop properties and facies modelling of the Lower Silurian Qusaiba Member and Sharawra Member, Qalibah Formation

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the heterogeneity of the Lower Silurian Qalibah Formation (Qusaiba and Sharawra Members) in the Qassim Region using a combination of sedimentological analysis, petrophysical measurements, and 3D digital outcrop modeling. The study concludes that integrating these multidisciplinary approaches provides higher resolution models for characterizing complex lithologies compared to conventional methods.

---

## Research Problem

The study addresses the challenge of characterizing complex, heterogeneous unconventional reservoirs (Qusaiba Member and Sharawra Member) within the Qalibah Formation using integrated multi-disciplinary approaches including sedimentology, petrophysics, and 3D digital outcrop modeling.

## Objectives

- To integrate sedimentology, petrophysics, and remote sensing (digital outcrop modeling) to construct high-resolution reservoir models.
- To evaluate the impact of 3D digital outcrop models on characterizing lithofacies and property distribution.
- To bridge the gap between surface (outcrop) and subsurface correlation using detailed petrophysical analysis.
- Construction of facies analysis to understand lithological variations in space and time.
- Obtaining petrophysical properties including porosity, permeability, geochemical composition, and spectral gamma ray.
- Constructing a 3D static model utilizing outcrop facies and petrophysical properties integrated with photogrammetric data.
- Develop a comprehensive 3D static model to elucidate lithofacies distribution and petrophysical properties in the Qalibah Formation.
- Integrate data from detailed digital outcrop modeling (DOM) to delineate critical surfaces and bed geometries.
- Evaluate the impact of lithofacies on porosity and permeability across different outcrops.
- Utilize geochemical analysis to define member boundaries and facilitate lithofacies correlation.
- Construct high-resolution 3D digital outcrop models for the Qusaiba and Sharawra Members.
- Integrate sedimentology and petrophysics with remote sensing data to improve reservoir modeling.
- Characterize the facies and properties of the Lower Silurian Qalibah Formation.

## Methods

The study utilized four distinct outcrops (OC1, OC2, OC3, OC4) and a shallow core sample. Methodology included: 1) Field work involving stratigraphic logging of outcrops; 2) Sample preparation for laboratory analysis; 3) Laboratory work including petrophysical measurements (helium porosity, nitrogen/spot permeability), geochemical analysis via XRF to determine elemental concentrations, and XRD for mineralogy; 4) Digital outcrop modeling (DOM) using photogrammetry/point clouds to create a Triangulated Irregular Network (TIN) mesh; 5) Petrel software was used to construct 3D static models for lithofacies, porosity, and permeability.

## Data and Materials

The study analyzed three specific outcrops (OC1, OC3, and OC4) within the Qalibah Formation. Elemental analysis was performed on samples from the Tawil Formation, Sharawra Member, and Qusaiba Member. Petrophysical data for porosity and permeability were collected across four lithofacies (LF1-LF4).

## Key Findings

- Porosity values for the studied units ranged from 2.37% to 37.58%. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 15]])
- Permeability values ranged from 0.002 mD to 83.69 mD. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 15]])
- The Qusaiba Member is characterized as a shale-dominant section (lithofacies LF1), while the Sharawra Member is sand-rich and shows a coarsening upward trend from silty to sandy lithologies. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 16]])
- The Qusaiba Member uncomfortably overlies the Sarah Formation, while the Sharawra Member conformally overlies the Qusaiba Member. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 16]])
- XRD analysis identified specific mineral percentages for each lithofacies across the Tawil, Sharawra, and Qusaiba units. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 47]])
- XRF analysis revealed variations in elements such as S, Mg, K, Ca, Fe, Ni, and Zn across the three geological units. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 48]])
- The Qalibah Formation is characterized by four distinct lithofacies: LF1 (fissile shale), LF2 (interbedded sandstone/mudstone), LF3 (hummocky cross-stratified sandstone), and LF4 (massive sandstone/carbonate). ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 50]])
- Overall porosity values in the Qalibah Formation range from 0.5% to 34%, while permeability ranges from 0.01 mD to 550 mD. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 52]])
- LF1 (Qusaiba Member) exhibits the lowest porosity (1-5%) and permeability (0.01-0.1 mD), with occasional spikes up to 20% in sand lenses. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 53]])
- LF3 (Sharawra Member) shows the highest petrophysical values, with porosity ranging from 17% to 35% and permeability from 70 mD to 550 mD. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 53]])
- XRF analysis of the Qusaiba Member revealed high concentrations of sulfur (S) and iron (Fe), while XRD showed low Si content and high illite/kaolinite trends. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 54]])
- The Sharawra Member showed a rapid decrease in sulfur, an increase in potassium (K), and a decrease in iron (Fe) and zinc (Z) in XRF results. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 54]])
- Tawil Formation was uniquely identified by a positive spike in calcium (Ca) in XRF and a predominance of calcite in XRD. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 55]])
- The Qusaiba Member is characterized by fissile shale lithofacies (LF1) with varying colors and occasional sandstone lenses, indicating low-energy depositional conditions. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 79]])
- The Sharawra Member contains two main lithofacies: LF2 (heterolithic sandstone/mudstone interpreted as turbidity current deposits) and LF3 (hummocky cross-stratified sandstones interpreted as storm events). ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 79]])
