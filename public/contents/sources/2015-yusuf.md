# INFLUENCE OF TREATED SEWAGE EFFLUENTS ON SAUDI COAST OF THE ARABIAN GULF FROM WATER QUALITY AND MICROBIAL ASPECTS

**Summary**: The thesis investigated the impact of treated sewage effluents on water quality and microbial aspects in three outfall locations (Al-Khobar, Dammam, and Rahima) along the Saudi Arabian coast of the Arabian Gulf. The study found that all three outfalls served as significant sources of inorganic nutrients, with particularly high phosphate levels across all sites and significantly higher nitrate/nitrite in Dammam and Rahima.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of published reports on the assessment of the influence of treated sewage effluents on microbiological and water quality characteristics in coastal Saudi waters of the Arabian Gulf, specifically regarding nutrient loading and pathogenic risks.

## Objectives

- assess the water quality (nutrients and chlorophyll a) of the sewage outfall locations
- study the microbial diversity in relation to the effect of treated sewage effluents discharge in the coastal waters
- evaluate the risk of pathogenic bacterial contamination in the coastal waters using indicator bacteria

## Methods

Water samples were collected from three outfall locations (Al-Khobar, Dammam, Rahima) and a control site (Half Moon Bay). In-situ parameters (temperature, pH, DO, salinity) were measured using Eutech CyberScan and ATAGO refractometers. BOD was determined via the 5-Day BOD test; COD was determined by titrimetric standard method. Nutrients (nitrate, nitrite, phosphate) and silicate were analyzed using a Skalar San++ auto-analyzer. Chlorophyll a was measured using a Turner Designs Trilogy fluorometer after extraction with acetone. Microbial analysis included heterotrophic plate counts on nutrient agar and total Coliform enumeration on Levine EMB agar using membrane filter techniques. Bacterial identification of 50 isolates was performed using API 20 E and API 20 NE strips.

## Data and Materials

Water samples from 9 sampling stations across 3 transects for each of the 4 locations (3 outfalls + 1 control); 3 replicate effluent samples from discharge points; 50 isolated bacterial colonies for identification.

## Key Findings

- All three outfall locations were identified as significant sources of inorganic nutrients, with phosphate concentrations exceeding PME limits in all cases (ranging from 0.62 to 64.76 µg/L). ([[2015-yusuf|Yusuf, 2015, p. 14]])
- Nitrate and nitrite concentrations were significantly higher in Dammam and Rahima compared to the control site. ([[2015-yusuf|Yusuf, 2015, p. 14]])
- Ammonia levels were notably higher in Al-Khobar and Dammam compared to other sites. ([[2015-yusuf|Yusuf, 2015, p. 14]])
- Chlorophyll a concentrations were significantly high only in Dammam, indicating poor water quality. ([[2015-yusuf|Yusuf, 2015, p. 14]])
- Total Coliform counts in sampling stations ranged from 4000 to 310000 CFU/100ml; heterotrophic plate counts ranged from 70 to 210000 CFU/ml. ([[2015-yusuf|Yusuf, 2015, p. 14]])
- The lowest bacterial counts were recorded in Rahima, where tertiary treatment was applied. ([[2015-yusuf|Yusuf, 2015, p. 14]])
- BOD values at Dammam (27.55 mg/L) and Al-Khobar (6.5 mg/L) were higher than the control site (7.2 mg/L). ([[2015-yusuf|Yusuf, 2015, p. 35]])
- Nitrate concentrations in Dammam reached up to 17.89 µmol/L and Rahima reached 14.30 µmol/L. ([[2015-yusuf|Yusuf, 2015, p. 36]])
- Chlorophyll a mean concentration at the Dammam outfall was 35.04 µg/L, significantly higher than other sites. ([[2015-yusuf|Yusuf, 2015, p. 39]])
- Eight types of pathogenic bacteria were identified: E. coli, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, Vibrio cholera, Aeromonas hydrophila, Salmonella typhi, Shigella spp., Campylobacter jejuni, and Yensinia enterocolitica. ([[2015-yusuf|Yusuf, 2015, p. 45]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

Adopt tertiary treatment similar to Rahima in all STPs; perform regular maintenance of existing plants; implement technologies to reduce inorganic nutrient release; conduct regular monitoring of trophic state and pathogenic contamination; control shellfish harvesting and recreational activities near outfalls.
