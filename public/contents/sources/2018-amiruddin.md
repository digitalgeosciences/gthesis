# ECOTOXICITY OF ARABIAN LIGHT CRUDE OIL AND OIL BASED DRILLING MUD ON THE LOCAL ARABIAN KILLIFISH (Aphanius dispar) AND COMMERCIAL BRINE SHRIMP (Artemia sp.)

**Summary**: The thesis investigated the ecotoxicity of Arabian Light Crude Oil (ALCO), its water-soluble fraction (WSF), and oil-based drilling mud (OBM) components on local fish (Aphanius dispar) and commercial brine shrimp (Artemia sp.). The study utilized LC50 and EC50 tests to determine mortality and hatching inhibition, concluding that while direct exposure to crude oil had limited impact due to low PAH solubility in seawater, the addition of dispersants significantly increased toxicity.

---

## Research Problem

Oil spills and drilling activities in the Arabian Gulf pose significant risks to marine life. The study addresses the specific toxicity levels of local species (Aphanius dispar) and commercial indicators (Artemia sp.) exposed to various fractions of Arabian Light Crude Oil and oil-based mud, including the effects of chemical dispersants.

## Objectives

- Evaluate the feasibility of using the local Arabian Killifish (Aphanius dispar) in acute toxicity (LC50) studies using Arabian light crude oil and oil-based drilling mud.
- Evaluate the feasibility of using the commercial Brine Shrimp (Artemia sp.) in acute toxicity (LC50) studies using Arabian light crude oil and oil-based drilling mud.
- Evaluate the feasibility of using the commercial Brine Shrimp (Artemia sp.) in sublethal toxicity (EC50) studies using Arabian light crude oil and oil-based drilling mud.
- Determine the LC50 of Arabian Killifish exposed to Arabian Light crude oil, its water-soluble fraction (WSF), and oil-based mud (OBM) components.
- Determine the LC50 and EC50 of Brine Shrimp exposed to Arabian Light crude oil, WSF, and OBM components.
- Quantify the concentration of Polycyclic Aromatic Hydrocarbons (PAHs) in Arabian Light crude oil, OBM, and dispersants using GC-MS.
- Determine the LC50 of Arabian Killifish (Aphanius dispar) exposed to Arabian Light crude oil, its water-soluble fraction (WSF), and oil-based drilling mud (OBM).
- Evaluate the impact of a chemical dispersant (Surfatron) on the toxicity of Arabian Light crude oil.
- Determine the LC50 of commercial Brine Shrimp (Artemia sp.) exposed to WSF of Arabian Light crude oil and SPP of oil-based mud.
- Assess the hatching efficiency (EC50) of Brine Shrimp cysts exposed to these toxicants.
- Evaluate the ecotoxicity of Arabian Light crude oil on local fish (Aphanius dispar) and commercial brine shrimp (Artemia sp.).
- Assess the toxicity of oil-based drilling mud (OBM) on both species.
- Determine the effectiveness of using local species as indicators for short-term toxicity tests.
- Determine the LC50 of Arabian Light crude oil on Aphanius dispar
- Determine the LC50 of oil-based drilling mud (solid phase) on Artemia sp.
- Determine the LC50 of suspended particulate phase (SPP) in oil-based drilling mud on Artemia sp.
- Determine the LC50 of water soluble fraction (WSF) in oil-based drilling mud on Artemia sp.

## Methods

The study involved preparing various test solutions including Arabian Light Crude Oil, its Water Soluble Fraction (WSF), and Oil-Based Mud components (Suspended Particulate Phase - SPP, and Solid Phase - SP). Toxicity tests were conducted on Arabian Killifish for 96 hours to determine LC50 values. For Brine Shrimp, toxicity tests were conducted for 48 hours to determine both LC50 and EC50 (hatching efficiency) values. Chemical analysis of the samples was performed using Gas Chromatography (GC) and High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC). The study also included a positive control test using CuSO4.

## Data and Materials

The study utilized Arabian Light Crude Oil, oil-based mud, and an oil dispersant (Surfatron). Samples of the local species Arabian Killifish were collected from Tarut Bay. Commercial Brine Shrimp cysts were sourced from Ocean Nutrition, USA. Chemical analysis was performed on various fractions: WSF of ALCO, SPP of OBM, and SP of OBM.

## Key Findings

- The LC50 of Arabian killifish (Aphanius dispar) exposed to Arabian Light crude oil plus oil dispersant for 96h is 164.19 mgL-1. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 16]])
- The LC50 of Arabian killifish (Aphanius dispar) exposed to the Solid Phase (SP) of Oil-Based Mud for 96h is 137,565.36 mgL-1. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 16]])
- The LC50 of Artemia sp. exposed to the SPP of oil-based mud for 48h is 38.82%. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 16]])
- The EC50 of the cysts of the Brine Shrimp (Artemia sp.) exposed to the SPP of Oil-Based Mud is 5.01%. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 16]])
- The EC50 of the cysts of the Brine Shrimp (Artemia sp.) exposed to WSF of Arabian Light Crude Oil is 5.95%. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 16]])
- The concentration of total PAHs in WSF of Arabian light crude oil was 39.55 ng/ml. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 17]])
- The concentration of total PAHs in SPP of oil-based mud was 345.58 ng/ml. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 17]])
- Arabian Killifish (Aphanius dispar) can be used for short-term (96 h) toxicity tests using the Arabian Light Crude Oil plus the dispersant and the SP of oil-based mud. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 17]])
- Brine Shrimp (Artemia sp.) is more feasible for short-term toxicity tests on Arabian Light crude oil and oil-based mud (OBM). ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 17]])
- The LC50 of Arabian Killifish exposed to CuSO4 as a positive control was 29.65 mgL-1 (95% Lower Conf. Limit: 1.067; 95% Upper Conf. Limit: 3.703). ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 50]])
- All Arabian Killifish exposed to Arabian Light crude oil remained alive after 96 hours at concentrations up to 450 mgL-1. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 52]])
- The study identified specific concentration ranges for testing: WSF and SPP (1.5% to 100%), and Solid Phase (10,000 mg to 750,000 mg). ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 42]])
- GC-MS analysis was used to determine the concentration of PAHs in Arabian Light crude oil, OBM, and dispersants. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 48]])
- The LC50 of Arabian Killifish exposed to oil dispersant (Surfatron) for 96 hours was 262.9 mgL-1. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 54]])
- The LC50 of Arabian Killifish exposed to Arabian Light crude oil plus dispersant for 96 hours was 164.19 mgL-1. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 70]])

## Limitations

The study noted that LC50 for Brine Shrimp exposed to WSF could not be calculated because mortality exceeded 100% at the highest concentrations, and it also noted that low PAH solubility in seawater limited the toxicity of raw crude oil compared to dispersed oil.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The author recommends evaluating the embryos of the local species Arabian killifish (Aphanius dispar) to assess more sensitive developmental and pathological effects of oil exposure.
