# Biostratigraphy and Benthic Foraminiferal Morphogroups of the Miocene Mixed Carbonate and Siliciclastic Dam Formation in the Al-Lidam area, Eastern Province of Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the biostratigraphy and benthic foraminiferal morphogroups of the Miocene Dam Formation in the Al-Lidam area, Eastern Province, Saudi Arabia. It utilizes acetic acid processing and thin-section petrography to identify microfossils and characterize lithofacies, concluding that the assemblage is dominated by epifaunal taxa indicative of a shallow marine environment.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of detailed micropaleontological studies on foraminifera in the Dam Formation, specifically regarding their distribution in the Al-Lidam area, limits the understanding of depositional history and paleoenvironmental changes during the Miocene.

## Objectives

- Investigate four outcrops along the west-to-east direction from the Al-Lidam escarpment
- Perform paleoenvironmental reconstruction based on foraminiferal assemblages
- Determine the vertical and lateral distribution of foraminiferal assemblages
- Optimize the acetic acid method for extracting microfossils from carbonate rock
- Identification and documentation of the foraminiferal species and foraminiferal biofacies of the Dam Formation at its type locality.
- Implementation and optimization of the Acetic Acid method for extracting microfossils from indurated Miocene carbonates.
- Analyses of foraminiferal assemblages using morphogroups with the objective of interpreting paleobathymetric and paleoenvironmental changes that prevailed at the time of deposition.
- To understand the vertical and lateral distribution of the foraminiferal species and foraminiferal morphogroups in response to lithofacies and paleoenvironment, which is associated with sea-level changes (transgressive – regressive episodes), and depositional cycles.
- Integration of foraminiferal biofacies data with the established sedimentology, and the stratigraphic study by Bashri (2015), and Ali (2016) to enhance and better constrain interpretations and understanding of the Dam Formation.
- Determine morphotypes and morphogroups of benthic foraminiferal assemblages to reveal paleoenvironmental changes.
- Evaluate different acetic acid concentrations for optimal microfossil recovery from carbonate and siliciclastic rocks.
- Identify lithofacies within the Dam Formation in the Al-Lidam area.
- Extract foraminifera from lithified carbonate rocks using acetic acid treatment.
- Identify benthic foraminiferal morphotypes and species within the Dam Formation.
- Determine the age of the Dam Formation based on biostratigraphic indicators.
- Interpret the depositional environment and paleoenvironmental conditions of the Dam Formation.
- Determine the biostratigraphy and benthic foraminiferal morphogroups of the Miocene Dam Formation.
- Identify the paleoenvironmental conditions during the deposition of the Dam Formation.
- Determine the biostratigraphy of the Miocene Dam Formation
- Identify and characterize benthic foraminiferal morphogroups within the Dam Formation
- Analyze the depositional environment of the mixed carbonate and siliciclastic units

## Methods

The methodology involves a combination of field investigation and laboratory analysis. Samples were collected from four outcrops in the Al-Lidam area. Petrographic thin sections were used to determine lithofacies and biofacies. To overcome the limitations of 1D views in thin sections, an acetic acid treatment (80% acetic acid, 20% distilled water) was used to extract microfossils from lithified carbonate rocks. The process involved breaking samples into 5mm fragments, soaking for 10 hours, washing through standard sieves (500, 250, 125, and 63 μm), drying at low temperature (40-50°C), and picking under a binocular stereo-microscope.

## Data and Materials

Analysis of samples from the Dam Formation in the Al-Lidam area; identification of various foraminiferal genera including Amphistegina, Ammonia, Borelis melo, Cibicides, Cibicidoides, Coscinospira, Cornuspira, Discorbinella, Discorbis, Elphidium, Operculina, Peneroplis, Planorbulina, Pullenia, Pseudotriloculina, Pyrgo, Rotalia, Stilostomella, Sigmoilinita, Spirolina, Triloculina, and Quinqueloculina.

## Key Findings

- The foraminiferal assemblage from the Dam Formation is dominated by calcareous porcellaneous Miliolina genera (Quinqueloculina, Peneroplis, Triloculina, Cornuspira, Sigmoilinita, Coscinospira, Spirolina, Pyrgo, Borelis). ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 15]])
- The assemblage includes hyaline forms (Elphidium, Ammonia, Cibicides, Discorbinella) and a minor percentage of agglutinated forms like Textularina. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 16]])
- High percentages of calcareous porcellaneous taxa and the absence of planktonic foraminifera indicate a restricted carbonate platform environment or very shallow hypersaline lagoon. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 16]])
- The paleoenvironment is interpreted as a gently sloping ramp (inner ramp) ranging from supratidal to subtidal with local patch reefs, in an arid subtropical environment with temperatures between 20 and 35°C. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 16]])
- The present-day Arabian Gulf is considered a valid modern analogue for the Miocene Dam Formation as environmental conditions have not changed drastically since the Miocene. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 16]])
- Acetic acid disaggregation showed promising results, successfully extracting microfossils from carbonate rock without destroying fossil content. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 15]])
- The Dam Formation is characterized by sedimentary rocks including sandstone, marl, sandy limestone, clay, conglomerate, and thin gypsum layers, indicating continental to shallow marine environments. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 25]])
- The Dam Formation was deposited in a restricted carbonate platform environment during the collision between the Arabian and Eurasian plates. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 26]])
- The Dam Formation is regionally equivalent to the Fatha Formation (formerly Lower Fars) in SE Iraq and Kuwait, Jebel Cap in Bahrain, and the Dam Formation in Qatar and Western UAE. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 26]])
- Foraminiferal assemblages in the Dam Formation at the Dammam Dome are similar to modern foraminifera from the Arabian Gulf. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 34]])
- The study of foraminifera in the Dam Formation has identified several milioline genera, including Agglutinella, Archaias, Dendritina, Peneroplis, Pygro, Sigmoilina, Spirolina, Triloculina, and Quinqueluculina. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 33]])
- The Dam Formation was deposited under warm climatic (25° to 30°C) conditions in a very shallow tidal flat setting, with salinity ranging between 35 and 50 ppt. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 33]])
- The acetic acid method reduced the weight of obtained residue by approximately 18-20g for 50-80% concentrations and 13-14g for 90% and 100% concentrations from an initial 100g sample. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 52]])
- The Dam Formation is characterized by seven lithofacies, with carbonate lithofacies accounting for approximately 85% of the formation. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 58]])
- The total assemblage is dominated by epifaunal taxa (e.g., Morphogroup A), which is typical of a shallow marine environment. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 67]])

## Limitations

Due to the preservation status, most microfossil specimens occur as molds with their outer walls dissolved during diagenesis, making it difficult to identify some genera to the exact species level; identification is mostly limited to the generic level.

## Recommendations and Future Work

For standard recovery without time constraints, lower acid concentrations (e.g., 60-80%) are recommended for better preservation. For industrial settings requiring quick results (e.g., drilling monitoring), higher concentrations with shorter reaction times are recommended as they still provide moderate microfossil recovery.
