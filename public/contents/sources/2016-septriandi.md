# Biostratigraphy and Benthic Foraminiferal Morphogroups of the Miocene Mixed Carbonate and Siliciclastic Dam Formation in the Al-Lidam area, Eastern Province of Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the biostratigraphy and benthic foraminiferal morphogroups of the Miocene Dam Formation in the Al-Lidam area, Eastern Province, Saudi Arabia. Using acetic acid extraction and morphogroup analysis, it characterizes lithofacies and reconstructs paleoenvironmental conditions, concluding that the formation was deposited in a restricted carbonate platform with high salinity.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of detailed micropaleontological studies on foraminiferal distribution within the Dam Formation in the Al-Lidam area, despite its importance in the Arabian petroleum system and its distinct mixed carbonate and siliciclastic character.

## Objectives

- Identification and documentation of the foraminiferal species and foraminiferal biofacies of the Dam Formation at its type locality.
- Implementation and optimization of the Acetic Acid method for extracting microfossils from indurated Miocene carbonates.
- Analyses of foraminiferal assemblages using morphogroups with the objective of interpreting paleobathymetric and paleoenvironmental changes that prevailed at the time of deposition.
- To understand the vertical and lateral distribution of the foraminiferal species and foraminiferal morphogroups in response to lithofacies and paleoenvironment, which is associated with sea-level changes (transgressive – regressive episodes), and depositional cycles.
- Integration of foraminiferal biofacies data with the established sedimentology, and the stratigraphic study by Bashri (2015), and Ali (2016) to enhance and better constrain interpretations and understanding of the Dam Formation.
- Identify benthic foraminiferal species and morphogroups within the Dam Formation.

## Methods

The study utilized a combination of field investigation and laboratory analysis. 80 samples were collected from four outcrops in the Al-Lidam area. Petrographic thin sections were used for lithofacies and biofacies identification. The acetic acid method was employed to extract microfossils from carbonate rocks, involving crushing into 5mm fragments, soaking in 80% acetic acid/20% water for 10 hours, washing through standard sieves (500, 250, 125, and 63 μm), and drying at 40-50°C. Optimization of the method involved testing various concentrations (50%, 60%, 70%, 80%, 90%, 100%) and reaction times. Foraminifera were identified using a binocular stereoscopic microscope and documented via digital photography.

## Data and Materials

Analysis of samples from the Dam Formation in the Al-Lidam area, including 46 identified benthic foraminiferal species across 24 genera and 16 families. Data included counts of various morphotypes (e.g., C1: 40%, A2: 26%, A1: 19%) and identification of 11 distinct foraminiferal assemblages.

## Key Findings

- The Dam Formation is dominated by calcareous porcellaneous Miliolina genera (Quinqueloculina, Peneroplis, Triloculina, Cornuspira, Sigmoilinita, Coscinospira, Spirolina, Pyrgo, Borelis), followed by hyaline forms (Elphidium, Ammonia, Cibicides, Discorbinella). ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 16]])
- The high percentage of calcareous porcellaneous taxa and the absence of planktonic foraminifera indicate that the Dam Formation was deposited in a restricted carbonate platform environment, very shallow hypersaline lagoon, gently sloping ramp (inner ramp) which ranges from supratidal to subtidal with local patch reefs towards the basin. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 16]])
- The study determined that the Dam Formation was deposited in an arid subtropical environment with water temperature ranging between 20 and 35°C. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 16]])
- Seven lithofacies were identified: Mudstone and Evaporites (ME), Sandstone and Mudstone (SM), Stromatolitic Limestone (SL), Skeletal Oolitic Grainstone (SOG), Skeletal Peloidal-Oolitic Grainstone (SPOG), Skeletal Packstone-Grainstone (SPG), and Quartz Skeletal Wackstone-Passstone (QSWP). ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 58]])
- Carbonate lithofacies account for approximately 85% of the formation, while siliciclastic lithofacies comprise 15%, mostly in the western part. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 58]])
- The acetic acid method at 60% to 80% concentration with a 10-hour processing time yielded 'good' recovery of foraminifera, while 90% and 100% concentrations were classified as fair to moderate. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 53]])
- The presence of reworked larger foraminifera in the Skeletal Packstone-Grainstone (SPG) lithofacies indicates the likely presence of a patch reef adjacent to the study area. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 60]])
- The sedimentary environment was characterized by high salinity, with some studies suggesting ranges between 35 and 50 ppt for similar formations in the region. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 33]])
- The study identified 46 species of benthic foraminifera belonging to 24 genera, 16 families, and three suborders. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 70]])
- Morphogroup C1 (Miliolid-Quinqueloculina) was the most dominant morphotype at 40%, followed by A2 (26%) and A1 (19%). ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 67]])
- The foraminiferal assemblage is composed of three main suborders: Miliolina (75%), Rotaliina (24%), and Texturalina (1%). ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 71]])
- Acetic acid treatment was successful in extracting foraminifera from lithified carbonate rocks without destroying the fossil content. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 90]])
- The presence of Borelis melo melo provided evidence for a Burdigalian (late early Miocene) age for the Dam Formation. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 92]])
- Specific lithofacies like SPG (Skeletal Peloidal packstone-grainstone) showed higher percentages of miliolids, indicating a more marine environment (middle ramp). ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 95]])
- The study identified 11 distinct foraminiferal assemblages that show vertical and lateral variations in the Dam Formation. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 73]])

## Limitations

Petrographic thin sections only allow a one-dimensional view of specimens, making precise taxonomical identification difficult; however, they are useful for lithofacies determination.

## Recommendations and Future Work

For standard recovery without time constraints, lower acid concentrations (e.g., 60-80%) are recommended as more environmentally friendly. For industrial settings requiring quick results to check fossil content or quantify biogenic proportions, higher concentrations with shorter reaction times are recommended.
