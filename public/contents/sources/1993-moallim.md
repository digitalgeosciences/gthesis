# Management of groundwater resources in Somalia

**Summary**: The thesis evaluates the groundwater resources in Somalia, focusing on several major aquifers including Baidoa, Jesomma, Aurado, Taleh, and Merca. It assesses hydraulic properties through pumping tests and hydrochemical analysis to determine if available water can meet domestic, livestock, industrial, and agricultural demands over a 20-year period.

---

## Research Problem

Somalia is an arid country with limited surface water; the study addresses the need for a systematic assessment of groundwater storage capacities, hydraulic properties (transmissivity), and chemical quality across major aquifers to inform sustainable water management.

## Methods

The study involved the compilation of scattered hydrogeologic data to determine hydraulic properties (transmissivity, storage coefficient) and hydrologic parameters. Pumping tests were conducted on several major aquifers (Baidoa, Jesomma, Aurado, Taleh, Murca). Hydrochemical analysis was used to assess water quality (TDS, specific conductivity). A 20-year projection (1990-2010) was modeled to compare available resources against projected growth in domestic, livestock, industrial, and agricultural sectors.

## Data and Materials

Data includes lithological logs from various wells, pumping test results, hydrogeochemical data, and historical records of rainfall, temperature, and evaporation across Somalia. Specific formations studied include the Adigrat, Mansa-Guda, Hamantey, Sawer, and Baidoa (Uaney, Baidoa, and Goloda members).

## Key Findings

- Somalia receives an average annual rainfall of approximately 300 mm, making groundwater a critical resource. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 22]])
- The Baidoa aquifer's hydraulic properties and behavior were characterized through pumping tests and water level hydrographs. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 65]])
- The Jesomma aquifer was evaluated for hydraulic properties, storage, and chemical quality (TDS and specific conductivity). ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 83]])
- The Aurado aquifer's hydraulic properties were determined to assess its potential for water supply. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 101]])
- The Taleh aquifer was analyzed for recharge, production, and hydraulic properties. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 113]])
- The Merca aquifer's relationship with the Shabelle River and its chemical quality (TDS) were assessed. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 126]])
- Water resources are currently sufficient to meet demand, but long-term forecasting shows that groundwater alone will not be sufficient for growing demands without conjunctive use of surface water. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 22]])
- The average annual precipitation in Somalia is approximately 300mm, while the potential evaporation rate exceeds it by more than six times (approx. 2300mm/year), resulting in a significant water deficit. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 34]])
- The Baidoa Member of the Baidoa Formation is deeply weathered and karstified, with some parts forming several meters of caliche (hard soils). ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 25]])
- The Goloda Member of the Baidoa Formation reaches a thickness of 650 meters in the Lugh-Mandcra basin and is approximately 350 meters thick at the Baidoa town type section. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 25]])
- The Hamantey Formation, which includes oolitic limestone and various facies (neritic to lagoonal), reaches a thickness of about 2100 meters in the middle of the Somali Embayment. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 24]])
- The Sawer Formation is characterized by bedded, coarse-grained limestone and sand marls followed by micritic light and gray limestone, with a thickness ranging from 140 to 210 meters. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 24]])
- The Adigrat Formation consists of varicolored quartzitic, micaccous, crossbedded sandstones and is approximately 200 meters thick at its type section near Berbera. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 25]])
- Water quality in the Central region was found to be generally poor (TDS = 2000 to 4000 mg/l) in many areas, particularly in shallow aquifers. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 31]])
- The Aurado and Talch aquifers were identified as having promising potential for water development in Northern Somalia. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 31]])

## Limitations

Data for private sector wells in the southwestern part of the Merca aquifer is not available. The study notes that current water shortages are largely due to a lack of development and proper distribution plans rather than absolute scarcity.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Conduct more exploration to delineate aquifer boundaries; drill more production wells in the Aurado, Jesomma, Merca, and Baidoa aquifers; develop surface reservoirs to collect rainwater; implement a national water resources development master plan; and initiate health programs for water-related diseases.
