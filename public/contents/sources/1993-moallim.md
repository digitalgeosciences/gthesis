# Management of groundwater resources in Somalia

**Summary**: The thesis evaluates the availability, quality, and management of groundwater resources in Somalia by analyzing several major aquifers (Taleh, Merca, Baidoa, Aurado, and Jesomma). It utilizes pumping tests to determine hydraulic properties and hydrochemical analysis to assess water suitability for domestic, agricultural, and industrial use.

---

## Research Problem

Somalia faces significant challenges in managing its groundwater resources due to arid conditions, limited infrastructure, and the need to balance growing demand from domestic, livestock, and agricultural sectors against available reserves.

## Objectives

- Synthesize and interpret hydrogeologic data to determine hydraulic properties of major aquifers.
- Assess the availability of surface and groundwater resources for conjunctive use.
- Evaluate the quality of groundwater in different aquifers to determine suitability for various uses.
- Develop a comprehensive plan for the future development and distribution of water resources in Somalia.
- Describe, assess, and quantify groundwater potential of major accessible aquifers in Somalia.
- Determine physical characteristics of aquifers such as transmissivity and storage coefficient using pumping test data.

## Methods

The study utilized pumping tests to determine transmissivity and storage coefficients (using Jacob's method) across various locations. Water quality was assessed using chemical analysis, specifically measuring Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) and Electrical Conductivity (EC), to categorize water types (e.g., NaCl, CaSO4, NaSO4). Hydrogeological modeling of aquifer volumes was performed under both confined and unconfined conditions.

## Data and Materials

Data includes pumping test results from multiple wells in the Baidoa, Jesomma, and Aurado aquifers; hydrochemical analysis of water samples; long-term (1965-1987) water level records for several observation wells; and geological maps/lithological descriptions.

## Key Findings

- The average potential evaporation rate in Somalia is approximately 2300mm per year, exceeding the mean annual precipitation of 300mm by more than six times. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 34]])
- The Baidoa aquifer consists of three members: Uaney Member (green sandstone/marls), Baidoa Member, and Goloda Member. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 25]])
- Groundwater in the Central region is often poor quality with TDS values ranging from 2000 to 4000 mg/l. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 31]])
- The Aurado and Taleh aquifers were identified as having promising potential for agricultural use in the Aragavo area. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 31]])
- The study concludes that while current resources meet demand, long-term projections show groundwater is limited and insufficient if used alone to satisfy growing demands. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 22]])
- The Baidoa aquifer is considered sustainable with an estimated annual recharge of 115.5 x 10^6 m3, while the current annual withdrawal is only about 1.3 x 10^6 m3 (approx. 1.13% of recharge). ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 63]])
- The average thickness of the Merca formation aquifer is approximately 600 meters, and it provides a potential source for high-quality water in southern Somalia. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 66]])
- Unconsolidated aquifers are limited in area (3-5 km wide) but have an estimated annual recharge of 288 x 10^6 m3 based on a rainfall of 300 mm and a 5% infiltration rate. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 77]])
- The Taleh formation is a potential aquifer but has lower water quality in the southern part due to its anhydritic lithology. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 62]])
- The Aurado formation provides high-quality water and is considered one of the primary aquifers in the country. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 63]])
- The Jesomma formation is identified as a potential aquifer with good water quality, having a thickness ranging from 250 to 400 meters. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 60]])
- Surface water in the Jubba and Shabelle rivers provides a total volume of 4347.5 x 10^6 m3 annually, capable of irrigating over 80,000 hectares. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 74]])
- Infiltration rates in the Baidoa town area were measured at 18.2 cm/hr (sandy clay loam) and 7.5 cm/hr (light clay system). ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 60]])
- The Baidoa aquifer is a transmissive aquifer with an average transmissivity of 2.71 x 10^4 m^2/sec, significantly enhanced by secondary permeability from karstification and fractures. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 95]])
- The Jesomma aquifer is highly transmissive (average 3.11 x 10^7 m^2/sec) and shows a trend of increasing chloride and sulfate ions toward the southeast, indicating groundwater movement. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 114]])

## Limitations

Data for the eastern part of the Aurado aquifer is limited because the depth exceeds 600 meters and no wells are currently tapping that area. Information on private sector well production in the Merca aquifer since 1983 is not available.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The study recommends implementing a system of small dams and embankments to store rainwater, developing new groundwater resources in deeper basins, and establishing better distribution systems (pipelines or diversion) for surface water from the southern rivers.
