# A Study on the Effect of THMC Conditions on Scratch Test Measurements

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the impact of THMC (Thermal, Hydrochemical, Mechanical) conditions—specifically brine saturation, oil saturation, and aging at high temperatures—on rock strength measurements obtained via scratch tests. It evaluates four rock types (Indiana Limestone, Desert Pink Limestone, Berea Buff Sandstone, and Berea Grey Sandstone) to determine the critical depth of cut and quantify how different fluids and temperatures affect the Unconfined Compressive Strength (UCS).

---

## Research Problem

The determination of accurate rock strength (UCS) in varying reservoir conditions is complicated by the transition between ductile and brittle failure modes during scratch testing. There is a need to understand how environmental factors like high-temperature aging, brine salinity, and crude oil presence influence these measurements and the identification of the critical depth of cut.

## Objectives

- Investigate the effect of saturation and temperature conditions on unconfined compressive strength derived from scratch test for various carbonate and sandstone samples.
- Morphological analysis of scratch generated powder.
- Developing best practices to carry-out scratch tests as no standard exists.
- Evaluate the effect of different saturating fluids (brine, distilled water, crude oil) on the UCS of limestone and sandstone.
- Assess the effect of high-temperature aging (150°C) on rock strength.
- Determine the critical depth of cut (dc) using multiple methods: Force Signal Analysis, Force Inflection Point Analysis, Size Effect Law (SEL), and Digital Imaging Analysis.

## Methods

The study utilized a scratch machine to perform tests on core samples at various depths of cut (0.05 mm to 0.4 mm). Samples were saturated in brine (20% NaCl), distilled water, and crude oil. Aging was performed at 150°C for one week. Data analysis included Kernel Density Estimation (KDE) to handle non-normal distributions of UCS values. Critical depth of cut was determined using four methods: Force Signal Analysis (identifying the transition from white noise to saw-tooth patterns), Force Inflection Point Analysis (plotting force vs. depth of cut), Size Effect Law (fitting a log-log plot of nominal stress vs. depth of cut), and Digital Imaging Analysis (using micro-CT to analyze grain size and shape). Ultrasonic analysis was used to measure P-wave and S-wave velocities to calculate dynamic Poisson's ratio.

## Data and Materials

4 rock types: Indiana Limestone, Desert Pink Limestone, Berea Buff Sandstone, and Berea Grey Sandstone. Samples were sourced from Kocurek, USA. Experimental parameters included 12 different depth of cut (DOC) increments (0.05 mm to 0.4 mm), three scratches per location for statistical significance, and aging at 150°C.

## Key Findings

- Saturation in brine caused a weakening trend in limestone samples (Indiana and Desert Pink) while showing strengthening behavior in sandstone samples (Berea Buff and Berea Grey). ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019, p. 61]])
- Distilled water saturation resulted in a consistent decrease in strength for all rock types, with a larger shift observed in the higher porosity Desert Pink limestone. ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019, p. 63]])
- Oil saturation caused distinct strengthening in Desert Pink limestone but an overall weakening effect in both Berea Buff and Berea Grey sandstones. ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019, p. 83]])
- Aging in brine showed no significant change for Indiana and Desert Pink limestones, but a flatter curve (indicating different behavior) was reported for Berea Buff sandstone. ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019, p. 72]])
- Aging in distilled water resulted in a consistent decrease of approximately 11% in the strength of sandstone samples. ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019, p. 79]])
- Aging in crude oil showed a strengthening effect on Desert Pink Limestone (approx. 17%) and a weakening effect on Berea Grey sandstone (approx. 14%). ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019, p. 120]])
- The inclusion of an ultrasonic device add-on allowed for the measurement of dynamic Poisson's ratio, which increased more significantly in water than in brine for sandstone samples. ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019, p. 85]])
- The critical depth of cut (dc) was determined as 0.29 mm using the Size Effect Law (SEL) method. ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019, p. 45]])
- Brine saturation caused a strength increase of approximately 13% in Berea Buff sandstone and 3.6% in Beree Grey sandstone, attributed to salt crystallization and clay flocculation. ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019, p. 82]])
- Distilled water saturation resulted in significant weakening for both sandstone types (up to 30% reduction) and limestone samples. ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019, p. 89]])
- Crude oil aging led to a 20.3% decrease in UCS for Berea Buff Sandstone, while Desert Pink Limestone showed an increase of 16.3%. ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019, p. 107]])
- High-temperature aging (150°C) without fluid resulted in a strength reduction of approximately 10% for both sandstone types. ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019, p. 102]])
- Micro-CT analysis showed that grain size increased significantly at a depth of cut of 0.2 mm, indicating the transition to brittle failure. ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019, p. 48]])
- Dynamic Poisson's ratio increased for sandstone samples after saturation in water and brine due to increased bulk density from pore fluid inclusion. ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019, p. 85]])
- Distilled water caused a reduction in UCS for all rock types, ranging from 19-25% for sandstones and up to 24% for Desert Pink Limestone. ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019, p. 120]])

## Limitations

The study notes that the high degree of variability in limestone samples makes it difficult to draw definitive conclusions regarding the specific effects of fluids on those rock types. Additionally, some results were considered inconclusive due to the non-smooth distribution of data from scratch tests.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Use QEMSCAN for high-resolution chemical analysis and mineral mapping to understand fluid-mineral interactions.
- Implement micro-CT analysis at elevated temperatures to study pore-scale mechanisms.
- Develop scratch testing platforms specifically designed for high-temperature environments.
- Incorporate signal processing of force signals to identify the onset of brittle failure modes.
