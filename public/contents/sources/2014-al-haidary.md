# WELLBORE STABILITY ASSESSMENT IN A SHALE FORMATION

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the mechanical instability of wellbores in shale formations within 'Field X' by integrating rock mechanical properties and in-situ stress data into a 1D Mechanical Earth Model (MEM). It concludes that the Mogi-Coulomb failure criterion is most effective for predicting drilling hazards like stuck pipe and lost circulation, allowing for the calculation of safe mud weight windows.

---

## Research Problem

The determination of an accurate wellbore stability model and safe mud weight window in a shale formation (specifically within 'Field X') where drilling challenges such as mud losses, stuck pipes, and tight holes occur due to complex stress regimes and rock properties.

## Objectives

- To measure the rock mechanical properties governing shale strength using preserved core samples.
- To calibrate and construct the profile of the rock properties.
- To estimate the in-situ stresses, stresses around the wellbore and pore pressure.
- To integrate the rock properties profile into a wellbore instability model along with in-situ stresses profile.
- To predict of the critical mud weight window with respect to depth, azimuth and inclination.
- To validate the model by field failure cases (stuck pipe, tight hole, over pull and lost circulation).

## Methods

The study utilizes a 1D-MEM approach. Core samples from WELL L were subjected to triaxial tests to determine static rock mechanical properties (Young's Modulus, Poisson's ratio, UCS, friction angle). These results were calibrated against dynamic properties derived from well logs (GR, density, sonic). In-situ stresses were estimated using the integration of density and depth profiles for vertical stress, micro-frac tests for minimum horizontal stress (closure pressure), and a set of equations to derive maximum horizontal stress. The Mogi-Coulomb failure criterion was selected as the primary model for predicting wellbore stability.

## Data and Materials

Data includes 27 wells from Field X (offshore and onshore), including 16 vertical and lateral wells for history matching. Specific logs include image logs, caliper logs, Gamma Ray (GR), Resistivity (RF), Density (RHOB), and Neutron (NPHI). Formation-specific data are analyzed for the Mauddud, Shuaiba, Biyadh, Aruma, Safaniya, and Khufj formations.

## Key Findings

- The Mogi-Coulomb failure criterion was found to be the most appropriate for matching predicted failures with drilling history. ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 16]])
- Measured formation pressure from RDT tests in WELL L ranged from 68 to 70 pcf (0.47 - 0.48 psi/ft). ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 26]])
- Estimated closure pressures from micro-frac tests were in the range of 106 to 120 pcf (0.73 - 0.83 psi/ft). ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 26]])
- Core-to-log depth shifts for Rumaila, Wara, Biyadh, and Buwaib were approximately 1.42, 1.28, 1.91, and 1.0 feet respectively. ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 23]])
- Wellbore stability issues in the study area were primarily linked to mechanical factors rather than chemical reactions of the shale. ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 19]])
- Only 10% of wells drilled within the safe mud weight window faced problems, compared to 40% for those outside the window. ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 36]])
- Average overburden stress gradient was approximately 1.0 psi/ft across the studied wells. ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 63]])
- Estimated pore pressure gradient averaged 0.48 psi/ft for the selected wells in Field X. ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 66]])
- The orientation of minimum horizontal stress (Sh) was determined to be approximately 110° NE based on image interpretation of Well L. ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 67]])
- Static Young's Modulus (E_STA) for shale showed high correlation with dynamic values (R² = 0.9444) using the formula: E_STA = 1.038 * exp(0.1196 * E_DYN). ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 52]])
- Static Young's Modulus (E_STA) for sand showed a correlation of R² = 0.646 using the formula: E_STA = 0.1196 * exp(0.7752 * E_DYN). ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 52]])
- Unconfined Compressive Strength (UCS) for shale was correlated to dynamic shear modulus (G_DYN) with R² = 0.722. ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 54]])
- The friction angle (FANG) for shale, sand, and carbonate were determined as approximately 25°, 35°, and 42° respectively. ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 50]])
- Tensile strength was estimated as 1/10 of the UCS value. ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 50]])
- The Mogi-Coulomb failure criterion provided a significantly better match to actual caliper-based failure readings compared to the Mohr-Coulomb criterion for predicting shear failure. ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 65]])

## Limitations

Limited lab test data for carbonate facies required the use of published correlations (Chang et al., 2006) rather than site-specific ones. Data gaps in density logs were filled using power law curves.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Casing should be set in Wara formations to isolate problematic shale; monitor flow rates to avoid surge/swab; optimize hole cleaning via RPM and mud rheology; acquire more logs (sonic, density, caliper, resistivity) for better model calibration; implement Real-Time Geomechanics monitoring.
