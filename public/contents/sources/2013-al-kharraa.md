# ASSESSMENT OF UNCERTAINTY IN POROSITY MEASUREMENTS USING NMR AND CONVENTIONAL LOGS

**Summary**: The thesis evaluates the accuracy and uncertainty of porosity measurements in carbonate reservoirs using NMR logging compared to conventional tools (Neutron-Density and Sonic) and core laboratory analysis. It concludes that while NMR is highly accurate for clean limestone, its reliability decreases in dolomitic formations due to rapid T2 decay in smaller pores.

---

## Research Problem

Carbonate reservoirs present complex petrophysical challenges due to heterogeneity and triple porosity systems (pores, vugs, and fractures). Conventional logging tools like neutron-density and sonic are highly dependent on lithology, leading to significant uncertainty in porosity estimation, especially in dolomitic formations where mineralogy varies significantly.

## Objectives

- Compare available porosity measurement methods (conventional logging tools and NMR logging tool) in two carbonate fields to assess uncertainty.
- Validate results using core laboratory analysis.
- Assess the accuracy of porosity measurements specifically using NMR logging.
- Develop an empirical correlation to correct NMR porosity measurements in dolomitic sections using JMP statistical software.
- Evaluate the accuracy of NMR logging for porosity determination in clean limestone and dolomitic carbonate formations.
- Compare NMR logging results with core analysis, CT scans, and conventional logs (Neutron-Density, Sonic).

## Methods

The study utilized 335 samples from two carbonate fields in Saudi Arabia. Porosity was measured via stress core laboratory analysis, Neutron-Density (N-D) logging, Sonic logging, and NMR logging. Data were validated by matching depths and using cross-plots for conventional logs. Statistical parameters including Average Absolute Percent Relative Error (AARE), Maximum Absolute Percent Relative Error (EMAX), Relative Error Standard Deviation (ESt.D), and Correlation Coefficient (R) were used to evaluate accuracy. A classification system was established to separate 'clean limestone' from 'dolomitic formations' based on dolomite content to analyze the impact of pore geometry on NMR signal detection.

## Data and Materials

Core samples from Arab-D reservoirs; data from two different NMR tools (Tool-A: pad tool, Tool-B: centered tool); Neutron-Density logs; Sonic logs; CT scan images of core samples; 76 core samples used for validating the dolomite correction correlation.

## Key Findings

- NMR logging showed a mean porosity of 0.1262 with an average absolute error (AARE) of 35.9% across the total dataset. ([[2013-al-kharraa|Al-Kharraa, 2013, p. 36]])
- In clean limestone formations, NMR was the most accurate tool with an AARE of 9.74%, a correlation coefficient of 0.966, and an ESt.D of 7.26%. ([[2013-al-kharraa|Al-Kharraa, 2013, p. 47]])
- In dolomitic formations, NMR accuracy significantly degraded with an AARE of 84.4%, a correlation coefficient of 0.791, and an ESt.D of 117. ([[2013-al-kharraa|Al-Kharraa, 2013, p. 48]])
- Sonic logging was the least accurate method overall, showing an AARE of 88.7% and a low correlation coefficient (R) of 0.6636. ([[2013-al-kharraa|Al-Kharraa, 2013, p. 39]])
- Neutron-Density (N-D) logging showed better accuracy than NMR in dolomitic formations, with an AARE of 40.2% compared to 84.4%. ([[2013-al-kharraa|Al-Kharraa, 2013, p. 45]])
- The study identified that the rapid T2 decay in smaller pores (common in compacted dolomite) causes NMR tools to under-estimate porosity in those specific lithologies. ([[2013-al-kharraa|Al-Kharraa, 2013, p. 45]])
- In clean limestone formations, NMR logging was the most accurate tool with an AARE of 10% compared to 14.3% for N-D and 42.3% for sonic tools. ([[2013-al-kharraa|Al-Kharraa, 2013, p. 80]])
- In dolomitic sections, Neutron-Density showed the lowest AARE (40.2%) compared to 84% for NMR and 174% for sonic tools. ([[2013-al-kharraa|Al-Kharraa, 2013, p. 80]])
- Tool-B (centered) performed better than Tool-A (pad tool) in carbonate wells with washouts, showing lower AARE (38%) vs. 50% and less scattered data (60% ESt.D vs. 104% ESt.D). ([[2013-al-kharraa|Al-Kharraa, 2013, p. 59]])
- The proposed NMR correlation for dolomitic sections reduced the AARE from 84% to approximately 27% and improved the correlation coefficient (R) from 0.79 to 0.93. ([[2013-al-kharraa|Al-Kharraa, 2013, p. 75]])
- NMR tool accuracy is significantly impacted by pore size distribution, specifically in dolomitic sections where small pore throats cause inaccurate measurements compared to core porosity. ([[2013-al-kharraa|Al-Kharraa, 2013, p. 71]])

## Limitations

Conventional logging tools are heavily influenced by lithology and mineralogy, leading to significant uncertainty in complex carbonate systems. Specifically, the sonic tool assumes a pure limestone matrix, which fails when dolomite or other minerals are present.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The study suggests that while NMR is an excellent tool for clean limestone, it must be used with caution or integrated with other tools in dolomitic formations where pore geometry leads to rapid T2 decay.
