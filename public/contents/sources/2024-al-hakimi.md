# PETROGENESIS OF NORTHERN MIDYAN TERRANE GRANITOIDS, ARABIAN SHIELD: CONSTRAINTS FROM GEOCHEMISTRY, S ‑N ISOTOPES, AND U-P ZIRCON DATING

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the petrogenesis of A-type and I-type granitoid complexes in the northwestern Midyan terrane of the Arabian Shield. Using a combination of whole-rock geochemistry, Sr-Nd isotopes, and U-Pb zircon dating (including SHRIMP), the study identifies two distinct magmatic pulses and provides evidence for slab failure as a primary mechanism for post-collisional magmatism.

---

## Research Problem

There is a lack of detailed investigation into the petrogenesis, mineral paragenesis, and potential ore-forming processes of Ediacaran granitoid types in the northwestern Midyan Terrane. Specifically, the role of slab failure as a mechanism for post-collisional magmatism in this region remains largely unexplored.

## Objectives

- To investigate the petrographic, chemical, and isotopic nature of granitoids that occur in the most northeastern Midyan Terrane.
- To define the ages of granite emplacement, and evaluate the magmatic sources.
- To comprehend the geological and tectonic setting of the Midyan Region complexes.
- Determine the mineralogical composition and textures of the NW Midyan granitoid rocks using XRD and QEMSCAN.
- Characterize the whole-rock geochemistry (major and trace elements) to differentiate between A-type and I-type granitoids.
- Perform U-Pb SHRIMP dating on zircons to establish a precise geochronology of the magmatic pulses.

## Methods

Petrographic analysis was conducted using thin sections prepared by VanPetro; mineralogy was identified via SEM, XRD, and QEMSCAN. Whole rock geochemistry included major elements determined by ICP-AES and trace elements by ICP-MS at BVM Laboratories. Sr and Nd isotopes were analyzed using thermal ionization mass spectrometry. U-Pb dating of zircons (63-245 μm) was performed using SHRIMP-IIe/MC at KBSI, with cathodoluminescence (CL) imaging used to characterize internal textures. Data interpretation involved various discrimination diagrams (e.g., TAS, MALI, and multi-element plots like Rb vs. (Y+Nb)) and temperature estimation via zircon saturation thermometry.

## Data and Materials

37 samples were selected for whole rock geochemistry; 26 samples were analyzed by QEMSCAN and 14 by SEM; 9 samples were chosen for U-Pb SHRIMP zircon dating; 10 samples were used for Sr and Nd isotope analysis. Zircon grains ranged from 63 to 245 μm.

## Key Findings

- Whole rock data reveals clear distinctions between A-type (metaluminous, enriched in LREE) and I-type (metaluminous-peraluminous) granitoids. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 17]])
- U-Pb zircon dating indicates two distinct pulses of granitoid magmatism: ~640-624 Ma for A-types and ~618-598 Ma for I-types. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 17]])
- Isotopic compositions suggest a juvenile crustal source for the ANS, with some I-types potentially derived from partial melting of pre-existing crust. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 17]])
- Enriched Sr-Nd isotope compositions suggest derivation from slab failure (specifically during Ediacaran). ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 17]])
- high Sm/Yb, Nb/Y, La/Yb, and Gd/Yb ratios were identified as signatures of slab failure magmatism. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024]])

- Granitoid samples primarily consist of quartz (15–42%), K-Feldspar (22-58%), plagioclase (39–63%), amphibole (6-28%), biotite (3–14%), muscovite (0-4%) and pyroxene (0.1 – 2%). ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 50]])
- The study identified two distinct magmatic pulses: a ~600-645 Ma group classified as A-type granite, and a ~597 to 620 Ma group classified as I-type and A-type granitoid. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 85]])
- A-type granites in the NW Midyan terrane have 10,000Ga/Al ratios >2.5, while I-type granites are <2.5. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 63]])
- I-type granitoids show a range of SiO2 (54.08-77.48 wt.%), and high total alkalis (Na2O + K2O) ranging from 2.40 to 11.2 wt.%, and low contents of MgO (0.10–5.46 wt.%). ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 63]])
- The Eu/Eu* values for the whole rock range from 0.22 to 3.44, with A-type samples showing 0.22-0.96 and I-type samples showing 0.24-3.24. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 72]])
- The Sr initial 87Sr/86Sr ratios are generally low (0.705 to 0.718), indicating a source in the partial melting of mafic material in an island-arc setting with minimal crustal contribution. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 93]])
- The εNd(t) values for A-type granites are positive, ranging from +5.8 to +4.65 (TDM ages 849-947 Ma), and I-type show +6.05 to +3.80 (TDM age 818-984 Ma). ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 75]])
- Zircon grains exhibit high Th/U ratios ranging from 0.1 to 1.7%, confirming their magmatic origin. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 79]])
- A-type granitoids yielded crystallization temperatures of 750-900 °C, while I-type yielded 650-800 °C. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 83]])

## Limitations

The text mentions that many complexes have poorly constrained ages from previous studies using conventional methods (Rb-Sr and U-Pb in zircon), which this study aims to address with SHRIMP.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Determine why slab failure occurred specifically in the NW Midyan terrane compared to other complexes in the ANS.
- Conduct individual and comprehensive studies for each suite and complex.
- Utilize geophysical methods for regional and local comparisons to support the slab failure model.
