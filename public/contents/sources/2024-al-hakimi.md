# PETROGENESIS OF NORTHERN MIDYAN TERRANE GRANITOIDS, ARABIAN SHIELD: CONSTRAINTS FROM GEOCHEMISTRY, S ‑N ISOTOPES, AND U-P ZIRCON DATING

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the petrogenesis and tectonic history of granitic rocks in the NW Midyan terrane of the Arabian Shield using U-Pb zircon dating, geochemistry, and S-N isotopes. It identifies two distinct magmatic pulses (A-type and I-type) and proposes a slab failure model to explain the transition from late-orogenic to post-orogenic magmatism between 645 and 598 Ma.

---

## Research Problem

There is a lack of detailed investigation into the petrogenesis, mineral paragenesis, and potential ore-forming processes of Late Cryogenian-Ediacaran granitoids in the northern Midyan Terrane, despite their economic importance as hosts for critical metals like REE, U, and W.

## Objectives

- Identify and characterize different granitoid types (A-type and I-type) in the NW Midyan region.
- Determine the age of these granitoids using U-Pb zircon dating.
- Constrain the magmatic sources and tectonic settings using Sr-Nd isotopes and whole-rock geochemistry.
- To investigate the petrographic, chemical, and isotopic nature of granitoids that occur in the most northeastern Midyan Terrane.
- To define the ages of granite emplacement, and evaluate the magmatic sources.
- To comprehend the geological and tectonic setting of the Midyan Region complexes.
- Characterize the mineralogy and texture of the granitoid rocks using XRD, QEMSCAN, and petrography.
- Determine the whole-rock geochemistry (major and trace elements) to understand the differentiation of the suites.
- Perform Sr-Nd isotope analysis to determine the source and evolution of the magmas.
- Conduct U-Pb zircon dating using SHRIMP to constrain the timing of emplacement for each suite.
- Classify granitoid samples into A-type and I-type groups based on mineral chemistry and whole-rock geochemistry.
- Characterize the mineralogy and textures of the different granitoid groups (A-type, I-type granodiorite, I-type diorite, and gabbro).
- Determine the age and magmatic origin of zircons using U-Pb SHRIMP dating and cathodoluminescence imaging.
- Evaluate the geochemical signatures and isotopic compositions to constrain the petrogenesis of the Northern Midyan terrane.
- Determine the geochronology of the NW Midyan terrane granitoids using U-Pb zircon dating.
- Characterize the geochemistry of A-type and I-type granitic rocks to identify their tectonic settings.
- Evaluate the role of slab failure as a mechanism for magmatism in the Arabian Shield.
- Determine the geochronological age of granites using Zircon U-Pb SHRIMP dating.
- Analyze the geochemical and Sr-Nd isotopic compositions to constrain petrogenesis.
- Identify economic mineral potential (REE, Uranium) within the granitoids.
- Evaluate the role of slab failure in the formation of these plutonic rocks.
- Characterize the mineral chemistry and major/trace element geochemistry of granitic rocks in the Northern Midyan region.
- Determine the absolute ages of various plutonic units using U-Pb zircon dating.
- Analyze S-N isotopic data to constrain the petrogenesis and source characteristics of the granitoids.
- Differentiate between I-type, A-type, and other granitic suites based on geochemical and mineralogical data.

## Methods

The study utilized field mapping and sample collection (49 samples) followed by laboratory preparation. Petrographic analysis was supported by XRD and QEMSCAN for mineral identification. Whole-rock geochemistry included major element analysis via ICP-AES and trace/REE analysis via ICP-MS at Bureau Veritas Minerals. Sr-Nd isotopes were measured using thermal ionization mass spectrometry. U-Pb dating of zircons (63-245 μm) was performed using SHRIMP-IIe/MC, with cathodoluminescence (CL) and backscattered electron (BSE) imaging for mineral identification.

## Data and Materials

The study analyzed multiple samples of granitic rocks (e.g., TB-55b, TB-51a, TB-29, TB-39b, TB-4b, TB-5b) from the NW Midyan region. Data included 20-31 spot analyses per sample for U-Pb dating and various geochemical measurements (Sr, Nd, Pb isotopes; trace elements like Ba, Sr, La, Th, etc.).

## Key Findings

- A-type granitoids are metaluminous and enriched in Light rare earth elements (LREE). ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 17]])
- I-type granitoids are characterized as metaluminous-peraluminous. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 17]])
- U-Pb zircon dating indicates two distinct pulses of magmatism: ~640-624 Ma for A-types and ~618-598 Ma for I-types. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 17]])
- Sr-Nd isotope compositions suggest a juvenile crustal source for the Arabian-Nubian Shield (ANS). ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 17]])
- Enriched Sr-Nd isotope compositions in some I-types indicate derivation from slab failure. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 17]])
- High Sm/Yb, Nb/Y, La/Yb, and Gd/Yb ratios provide a signature of slab failure magmatism. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 17]])
- The study identifies a new magmatic event in the Arabian Shield (AS) besides A-type and I-type arc magmas. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 17]])
- The Arabian Shield contains one of the largest peralkaline granite regions in the world, with at least 49 separate alkaline to peralkaline granite intrusions known. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 32]])
- Granitoid rocks in the Midyan terrane comprise approximately 60% of the Precambrian outcrop and are significant sources for polymetallic mineralization (Mo, Nb, W, Zr, REE, Sn, Ta, and U). ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 34]])
- Note: The study identifies two main groups in the Midyan terrane: Al Bad supersuite (575–570 Ma) and Muwaylih suite (710-725 Ma). ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024]])
- The study identified five major complexes: Muwaylih (710-725 Ma), Ifal (600-625 Ma), Atiya, Haql, and Midyan suites. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 37]])
- The Sawda Complex is a post-tectonic nepheline syenite suite with U-Pb dating of approximately 553 ± 4 Ma. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 39]])
- Granitoid samples primarily consist of quartz (15–42%), K-Feldspar (22-58%), plagioclase (39–63%), amphibole (6-28%), biotite (3–14%), muscovite (0-4%) and pyroxene (0.1–2%). ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 50]])
- The Midyan terrane records a magmatic transition from syntectonic I-type low-K calc-alkaline plutons before 650 Ma to A-type granites after 575 Ma. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 40]])
- The study area is situated in the Tabuk Province, specifically the Midyan Peninsula, bounded by the Hisma plateau and Red Sea. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 37]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Further investigations are required to determine why slab failure occurred specifically in the NW Midyan terrane compared to other complexes.
- Each suite and complex requires an individual, comprehensive study.
- Regional and local comparisons should utilize geophysical methods to support the slab failure model.
