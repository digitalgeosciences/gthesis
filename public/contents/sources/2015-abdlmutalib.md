# The relationship between litho-stratigraphy and geomechanical properties of Arab-D reservoir outcrop analogue-Central Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the relationship between litho-stratigraphy and geomechanical properties in the Arab-D member and underlying Upper Jubaila Formation using a surface outcrop analogue. It combines field fracture analysis (orientation, spacing, intensity) with laboratory measurements of geomechanical properties (Schmidt Hammer, Point Load, ultrasonic velocity) to evaluate how lithofacies influence reservoir behavior.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of high-resolution geological and geomechanical data in the subsurface for tight carbonate reservoirs like Arab-D makes it difficult to predict fracture behavior and geomechanical properties. This study uses a surface outcrop analogue in Wadi Nisah to provide high-resolution data (meter to centimeter scale) to bridge this gap.

## Objectives

- To characterize the geomechanical properties of the Arab-D and Jubaila Formation at outcrop scale.
- To identify and characterize the fractures types and their origin.
- To describe and characterize the lithofacies and stratigraphy.
- To investigate the relationship between sedimentology and geomechanical properties of Arab-D and Upper Jubaila Formation.
- Conduct field investigations to identify and characterize fracture sets in the Arab-D member and Upper Jubaila Formation.
- Measure geomechanical properties (e.g., Schmidt hammer hardness, porosity, permeability) of rock samples.
- Analyze the relationship between lithofacies, bed thickness, and fracture parameters (intensity, spacing, aperture).
- Determine cross-cutting relationships to establish relative ages of different fracture sets.
- Characterize fractures of the late Jurassic Arab-D member and underlying Upper Jubaila Formation
- Measure geomechanical properties (strength, hardness, elastic moduli) of rock samples
- Determine the relationship between lithofacies and geomechanical properties
- Correlate geomechanical properties with porosity and permeability
- Measure geomechanical properties of these formations
- Perform sedimentological and fracture analysis (orientation, length, spacing, aperture, and displacement)
- Conduct petrographic analysis to determine porosity and permeability

## Methods

The study employed an integrated field and laboratory approach. Field work included identifying lithofacies associations, analyzing fracture patterns (strike, dip, scale, spacing, intensity, aperture), and using LiDAR for data acquisition/processing. Laboratory analysis involved thin-section petrography to define lithofacies under the Dunham classification, and geomechanical testing including Schmidt Hammer Rebound tests, Point Load Index tests, and ultrasonic pulse velocity tests (using Pundit equipment). Data processing included stereonet analysis in RockWare and regression modeling in Excel to correlate geomechanical properties with porosity, permeability, and lithofacies.

## Data and Materials

60 samples collected from the outcrop area. Data includes fracture measurements from field observations, petrographic descriptions of lithofacies, and laboratory data for porosity, permeability, Schmidt Hammer Rebound Number (RN), Point Load Index (IS(50)), P-wave/S-wave velocities, and dynamic elastic moduli.

## Key Findings

- The study identified three distinct geomechanical units: Unit-1 shows high variability in strength index, porosity, and wave velocity due to strong stratigraphic cyclicity; Unit-2 is characterized by lower strength indices but higher porosity and consistency (equivalent to upper Upper Jubaila); Unit-3 has higher strength indices and lower porosity. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 14]])
- Fracture analysis revealed three sets of fractures intersecting the Upper Jubaila Formation and five sets affecting the Arab-D member. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 14]])
- Note: The study found that geomechanical properties can be predicted using core-based porosity and permeability values effectively, suggesting these parameters are reliable indicators for reservoir behavior. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015]])
- page_number_for_above_finding_note_is_not_explicitly_stated_but_appears_in_abstract_summary_on_pages_14-15 ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015]])
- Lithofacies were identified as ranging from lower to upper slope and ramp crest (Upper Jubaila) and deep to shallow lagoonal settings (Arab-D). ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 14]])
- Fracture intensity and other properties are controlled by factors including lithofacies changes, bed thicknesses, and burial depth. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 14]])
- Fracture intensity was found to be inversely correlated with bed thickness and directly related to fracture spacing in both the Arab-D Member and Upper Jubaila Formation. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 23]])
- The upper unit of the Upper Jubaila Formation is characterized by higher fracture intensity and smaller spacing, while the lower part has lower intensity and larger spacing. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 24]])
- Stromatoporoid packstones lithofacies are more fractured than dolomitic mudstone lithofacies, indicating a significant textural influence on fracture patterns. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 25]])
- The N40E fracture set in the Upper Jubaila Formation is more intensive and more closely spaced than the N20E set. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 26]])
- NW-oriented fractures in the Upper Jubaila are large scale, cut through several beds, and show some extensional vertical displacement. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 31]])
- The Arab-D member is divided into two units: a top unit (tidal flat lithofacies) with small intensity and a lower unit (skeletal bank association) with higher intensity and smaller spacing. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 38]])
- N-S oriented fractures in the Arab-D member are small scale, cut through thin beds, and have high intensity and small spacing. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 57]])
- E-W oriented fractures in the Arab-D member are the youngest set, followed by N-S, with N20E being the oldest. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 49]])
- The NW-oriented fracture set in the Upper Jubaila is cross-cut by NE sets, indicating the NE set is younger. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 49]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Expand study to include all Jurassic rock units to understand the effects of Central Arabian Graben System on all formations.
- Integrge this data with subsurface data for better understanding of fracture development models in Arab-D Reservoir.
- Carry out more tests and focus on small scale targets such as lithofacies and lithofacies associations.
- More elaboration of elemental and mineralogical effect on geomechanical properties through XRD and XRF.
