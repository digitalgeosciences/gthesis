# The relationship between litho-stratigraphy and geomechanical properties of Arab-D reservoir outcrop analogue-Central Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the relationship between lithostratigraphy and geomechanical properties in the Arab-D member and underlying Upper Jubaila Formation in Central Saudi Arabia. It utilizes a combination of field observations, petrographic analysis, and laboratory testing (Schmidt Hammer, Point Load Index, ultrasonic wave velocity) to characterize fracture behavior and rock strength.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of high-resolution geomechanical data for tight carbonate reservoirs (Arab-D) in the subsurface is addressed by using a stratigraphically equivalent outcrop analogue to understand how lithofacies and stratigraphic position influence fracture development and mechanical properties.

## Objectives

- To characterize the geomechanical properties of the Arab-D and Jubaila Formation at outcrop scale.
- To identify and characterize the fractures types and their origin.
- To describe and characterize the lithofacies and stratigraphy.
- To investigate the relationship between sedimentology and geomechanical properties of Arab-D and Upper Jubaila Formation.
- Characterize fractures of the late Jurassic Arab-D member and underlying Upper Jubaila Formation
- Measure geomechanical properties (Schmidt Hammer, Point Load Index) and petrophysical properties (porosity, permeability)

## Methods

The study utilized integrated field and laboratory investigations. Field work included identifying lithofacies, analyzing fracture patterns (orientation, scale, spacing, intensity, aperture), and collecting 60 samples for analysis. Laboratory work involved thin section petrography using the Dunham classification, porosity/permeability measurements, and geomechanical testing including Schmidt Hammer Rebound tests, Point Load Index tests, and Ultrasonic Pulse Velocity tests. Fracture data were analyzed using RockWare software to generate rose diagrams and stereoplots. Statistical correlations (linear, polynomial, exponential, or power) were performed in Excel to relate mechanical properties to porosity and permeability.

## Data and Materials

60 samples collected from the Wadi Nisah outcrop; 120 fractures analyzed along two high-resolution scan-lines; data on fracture orientation, size, spacing, aperture, and displacement; petrographic analysis of thin sections; measurements of Schmidt Hammer Rebound Number (RN), Point Load Index (IS(50)), P-wave and S-wave velocities, porosity, and permeability.

## Key Findings

- The Upper Jubaila Formation is characterized by stromatoporoid wackstone/packstone and dolomitic mudstone/wackstone lithofacies in a lower to upper slope and ramp crest setting. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 77]])
- Arab-D member features skeletal bank (peloidal fossiliferous grainstone, borrowed wackstone) and tidal flat (laminated mudstone, wavy rippled sandy grainstone) lithofacies in deep to shallow lagoonal settings. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 77]])
- Three fracture sets were identified in the Upper Jubaila Formation: N40E, N20E, and NW; the NW set is the most common and shows high intensity with small spacing. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 39]])
- Five fracture sets affect the Arab-D member (NW, N-S, N20E, E-W, and localized), with the NW set showing vertical displacement in some cases. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 57]])
- Fracture intensity was found to be inversely correlated with bed thickness and directly related to fracture spacing. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 39]])
- The maximum Schmidt Hammer Rebound Number (RN) of 55 was recorded for peloidal fossiliferous grainstone and wavy rippled sandy grainstone, while the minimum value of 15 was found in heavily fractured laminated mudstone. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 88]])
- The maximum Point Load Index (IS(50)) reached 14 MPa for wavy rippled sandy grainstone, and the minimum was 1.5 MPa for laminated mudstone. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 88]])
- A moderate correlation (R² = 0.6) was established between Schmidt Hammer Rebound Number and Point Load index. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 88]])
- Three geomechanical units were defined: Unit-1 (tidal flat, high variability), Unit-2 (lower Arab-D/upper Jubaila, low RN, high porosity), and Unit-3 (lower Upper Jubaila, high RN, low porosity). ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 81]])
- Geomechanical properties can be predicted using core-based porosity and permeability values effectively. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 15]])
- Porosity values ranged from 0.82% to 5.4% (mean 2.1%) and permeability ranged from 0.01 md to 1.26 md (average 0.2 md), indicating low values due to intensive diagenesis. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 90]])
- Schmidt Hammer Rebound Number (RN) showed a moderate correlation with porosity and a weak-to-moderate correlation with permeability. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 90]])
- Point Load Index (IL) exhibited a clear power-function like correlation with permeability in the Arab-D member. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 90]])
- Upper Jubaila samples were restricted to a narrow zone of low permeability and high Schmidt Hammer/Point Load indices due to minimal fracture impact. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 90]])
- Ultrasonic P-wave velocities ranged from 4,000 m/s to 10,000 m/s; higher values were attributed to heavy diagenetic compaction and the presence of dolomite and quartz. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 95]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Expand study to include all Jurassic rock units to understand the impact of the Central Arabian Graben System.
- Integrate outcrop data with subsurface data for better understanding of fracture development models.
- Conduct more tests on small-scale targets like lithofacies and lithofacies associations.
- Perform further elemental and mineralogical analysis using XRD and XRF to determine their effects on geomechanical properties.
