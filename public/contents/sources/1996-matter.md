# Geology of the early paleogene phosphorite deposits of Northwestern Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the mineralogical, geochemical, and petrographic characteristics of Early Paleogene phosphorite deposits in the Hazm Al-Jalamid area of northwestern Saudi Arabia. It utilizes chemical analysis (XRF, PIXE) and thin-section petrography to characterize different zones (Zone 1 and Zone 2) and establish correlations between phosphorus content and various trace elements.

---

## Research Problem

Lack of detailed study on the mineralogical composition, trace element content, and geochemical characteristics of known Early Paleogene phosphorite deposits in northwestern Saudi Arabia, which complicates the classification of ore grades and understanding of their origin.

## Objectives

- Petrographical and mineralogical study of the phosphorite ore composition.
- Analyze the trace elements and geochemical characteristics of the phosphorite deposits.
- To discuss the origin of the deposits (biogenic, biochemogenic, chemogenic, diagenetic, or volcanogenic).
- Provide a practical means for classifying various grades of the phosphorite
- Assessment of the economic importance of the phosphorite deposits of northwestern Saudi Arabia in terms of both major and trace elements.
- Geochemical analysis of the studied phosphorite deposits based on data obtained from PIXE, natural gamma-ray spectroscopy, SEM, and field data.

## Methods

The study involved field sampling from surface outcrops and subsurface sections (core samples and well cuttings) in the Turayf and Thaniyat Turayf areas. Analytical techniques included thin-section petrography using a binocular microscope, SEM-EDS for microchemical analysis and X-ray mapping/line scans to determine trace element distribution, Natural Gamma-ray Spectroscopy (NGS) to measure U, Th, and K concentrations, and Proton Induced X-ray Emission (PIXE) for high-sensitivity trace element determination. Data from these techniques were integrated with lithostratigraphic and biostratigraphic data to interpret paleoenvironmental conditions.

## Data and Materials

Samples from the Early Paleogene phosphorite-bearing succession in northwestern Saudi Arabia, specifically focusing on the Hazm al-Jalamid and Thaniyat Turayf areas. Data includes P2O5 content (15-39%), trace element concentrations (ppm), and mineralogical counts for apatite, dolomite, and calcite.

## Key Findings

- The ore is mainly composed of microcrystalline apatite (average 46.35%), dolomite (average 36.69%), and calcite (averaging 13.14%). ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 26]])
- Trace elements include uranium (up to 300 ppm) and vanadium (up to 3000 ppm). ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 26]])
- A practical classification was established using three cut points at 18%, 8%, and 1% P2O5, separating phosphorites from phosphoritic and slightly phosphoritic rocks. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 26]])
- The study identified twelve microfacies types: calcareous phosphorite, siliceous phosphorite, shale, sandstone, chert, dolomite, silicified limestone, coquina, sparite, microsparite, biomicrosparite, and micaceous sparite. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 46]])
- The Sirhan-Turayf region represents the eastern limit of an extensive Late Cretaceous-Early Paleogene phosphorite belt extending from Morocco to Iraq along the southern edge of the Tethys Sea. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 46]])
- Zone 1 Phosphorite is associated with limestone and chert units (Middle Eocene), while Zone 3 is associated with dolomites, marls, and cherts (Late Paleocene to Early Eocene). ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 47]])
- The Thaniyat Turayf area contains two distinct deposits: a Western Deposit (123 million tons at 20.6% P2O5) and an Eastern deposit (34 million tons at 24% P2O5). ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 42]])
- The Arqah Phosphorite Member contains beds with P2O5 values up to 33.3% at depths of 110 cm to 200 cm. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 44]])
- Phosphorite deposits in the region vary in phosphate content from 8% to 33%. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 58]])
- The primary mineral is carbonate-apatite (francolite or dahlite) with gangue minerals consisting of calcite, dolomite, quartz, gypsum, and bassanite. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 58]])
- A secondary mineral identified is metayumunite [Ca(UO2)(VO4)2·3H2O]. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 58]])
- The Thaniyat Phosphorite Member was determined to be of Danian (Early Paleocene) age based on dinoflagellates. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 54]])
- The Ghinah Phosphorite Member is considered younger than Cretaceous and older than Lutetian. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 56]])
- Apatite occurs as rounded pellets which are generally well liberated in material finer than 0.5 mm. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 61]])
- Carbonates are the dominant gangue minerals in the phosphorite samples. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 61]])
