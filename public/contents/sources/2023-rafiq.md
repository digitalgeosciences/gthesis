# Structural characterization of a Geothermal Field: Al-Lith, western Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the Al-Lith geothermal field in western Saudi Arabia by integrating remote sensing, rock/fracture geochemistry, and hydrochemistry to characterize its structural and thermal properties. The study concludes that the area possesses a medium-to-high enthalpy system with a reservoir temperature of 150-205°C, supported by a well-connected network of faults and fractures.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of a comprehensive understanding of the surface and subsurface structural features, as well as the geochemical and hydrogeological properties, of the Al-Lith geothermal field hinders its potential for exploration and development.

## Objectives

- Perform a structural and geological mapping of the Al-Lith area to extract main structural features and lineaments both manually and digitally.
- Identify areas with high fracture densities using the fault fracture density (FFD) method and relate this to geothermal potential.
- Characterize the geochemical and hydrogeological properties of the rocks and fluids in the Al-Lith geothermal field.
- Characterize surface and subsurface geological structures using remote sensing and lineament analysis.
- Perform mineralogical and petrographic analysis on rock samples to determine alteration levels.
- Conduct hydrochemical and stable isotope analyses of thermal water to determine source, age, and circulation paths.

## Methods

The study utilizes an integrated methodology: 1. Surface lineament extraction using Landsat-8 imagery and DEM data via PCI Geomatics software (including edge detection, thresholding, and curve extraction). 2. Subsurface lineament analysis of aeromagnetic data processed by the Saudi Geological Survey (SGS) using the CET Grid Analysis module in Seequent Oasis montaj. 3. Fracture characterization through Fault Fracture Density (FFD) calculations. 4. Rock and fracture geochemistry to determine mineralogy and chemical composition of rock/fracture cement. 5. Hydrogeochemistry analysis of fluids from Ain Al Harrah hot spring to assess temperature, fluid properties, and hydrochemical processes.

## Data and Materials

Data includes: Landsat-8 imagery (USGS GloVis), DEM data, aeromagnetic data (SGS processed), geological maps/toposheets (SGS). Field samples include rock samples from multiple outcrops for geochemistry/petrography and water samples from the Ain Al Harrah hot spring.

## Key Findings

- The Al-Lith geothermal field is influenced by a complex network of faults and fractures that control the flow of water and heat. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 15]])
- Surface lineament density analysis identified ten zones (A-J), with the highest density in the northern part (Zone A). ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 16]])
- Subsurface lineament analysis showed five high-density zones, three of which correlate well with surface features. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 16]])
- Hydrogeochemistry results suggested that thermal water is affected by water-rock interactions, mixing, and dilution. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 16]])
- Rock and fracture geochemistry indicated the area consists primarily of granitic/felsic igneous and low-grade metamorphic rocks. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 16]])
- The Ain Al Harrah hot spring has a reservoir temperature of 136°C, discharge enthalpy of 219 kJ/kg, and heat flow of 183 mW/m2. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 28]])
- Fracture sizes in some areas reached up to 1 cm (e.g., silica-filled fractures). ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 48]])
- The study area is dominated by Late Proterozoic plutonic, metasedimentary, and metavolcanic rocks to Quaternary alluvium. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 54]])
- Lineament density was significantly higher in areas with high topography (northern part), with 10 high-density zones identified (Zone A to J). ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 60]])
- Average aperture size of open fractures is 2.88 cm, while it is 1.30 cm for veins; most veins are silica-filled. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 64]])
- page_number_reference_note_for_finding_at_64_is_actually_on_page_64_of_the_source_text_provided_as_50_in_original_pdf_count_but_using_provided_markers ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023]])
- Petrographic analysis revealed rocks are predominantly volcaniclastic, granitoid, and low-grade metamorphic with significant hydrothermal alteration. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 69]])
- Mineralogical analysis showed primary minerals (K-feldspar, Quartz, Muscovite) and secondary minerals (chlorite, epidote, albite), indicating medium to high temperature hydrothermal alteration. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 77]])
- Geochemical results showed rocks/fractures are enriched in Si, Mg, Al, and Fe, with significant variations linked to alteration intensity. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 81]])
- Thermal waters of Ain Al-Harrah were nearly 60°C with a high salinity (EC values between 2800–3161 μS cm⁻¹). ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 84]])
