# Structural characterization of a Geothermal Field: Al-Lith, western Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the structural and hydrochemical characteristics of the Al-Lith geothermal field in western Saudi Arabia to evaluate its potential for sustainable energy. It employs an integrated approach including remote sensing, surface/subsurface mapping, rock geochemistry, and hydrochemistry to characterize the fracture network and fluid chemistry.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of a detailed understanding of the structural architecture, fluid circulation through fractured basement rocks, and the specific geochemical/hydrogeological characteristics of the Al-Lith geothermal field hinders its potential for exploration and development.

## Objectives

- Characterize surface and subsurface geological and structural features of the Al-Lith geothermal field.
- Analyze lineament densities and delineate high permeability zones.
- Characterize the geochemical and hydrogeological properties of rocks and fluids in the geothermal field.
- Perform a structural and geological mapping of the Al-Lith area to extract main structural features and lineaments manually and digitally.
- Identify areas with high fracture densities using the Fault Fracture Density (FFD) method and relate these to geothermal potential.
- Characterize the geochemical and hydrogeological properties of the rocks and fluids in the Al-Lith geothermal field.
- Characterize the surface and subsurface structural features of the Al-Lith geothermal field.
- Map the fracture network of the geothermal reservoir.
- Analyze the hydrochemical data to understand fluid chemistry and potential recharge sources.
- Integrate remote sensing, mapping, geochemistry, and hydrochemistry to evaluate the geothermal potential.
- Characterize surface and subsurface geological features using DEM data and satellite imagery
- Analyze lineament density and orientation to identify potential fluid pathways
- Perform petrographic and mineralogical analysis to determine the degree of hydrothermal alteration
- Evaluate chemical composition of rocks and fracture fill materials via XRF and XRD
- Characterize the surface and subsurface geological features of the Al-Lith geothermal field.
- Analyze the hydrogeochemistry of thermal spring waters to determine chemical composition and origin.
- Estimate reservoir temperatures using various geothermometers (cation-based and silica-based).
- Evaluate the connectivity between surface and subsurface fracture networks.
- Characterize surface and subsurface geological structures using remote sensing and lineament analysis.
- Evaluate rock and fracture geochemistry to identify mineralogical changes and hydrothermal activity.
- Analyze hydrochemistry of thermal fluids to determine origin, temperature, and chemical composition.
- Identify high-permeability zones for potential geothermal exploration.

## Methods

The study used 12m and 30m resolution Digital Elevation Models (DEM) for surface topography mapping. Lineaments were extracted using PCI Geomatics software from DEM and Landsat-8 imagery, followed by density analysis (Fault Fracture Density - FFD) and orientation analysis via rose diagrams in ArcGIS. Land Surface Temperature (LST) was mapped using TIRS bands of Landsat-8. Field observations included fracture measurements (aperture, orientation). Petrographic analysis was performed on 21 samples to identify minerals like quartz, orthoclase, and chlorite. Mineralogical composition was quantified via XRD and QEMSCAN, while chemical compositions were determined through XRF.

## Data and Materials

Data includes: 12m and 30m DEMs; Landsat-8 imagery; aeromagnetic data; field measurements from five locations (AL-01 to AL-05); rock samples for XRD, QEMSCAN, and XRF; and thermal spring water samples from the Ain Al-Harrah hot spring.

## Key Findings

- Surface lineament density analysis subdivided the study area into ten zones (A-J), with the highest density in Zone A (northern part). ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 16]])
- Subsurface lineament analysis identified five high-density zones, three of which showed a very good correlation with surface features. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 16]])
- Rocks in the study area are primarily composed of granitic and felsic igneous rocks and low-grade metamorphic rocks. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 16]])
- Hydrogeochemistry results indicated that thermal water is affected by a combination of water-rock interactions, mixing, and dilution. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 16]])
- The study identified high-permeability zones and associated high-potential geothermal areas in the Al-Lith field. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 16]])
- The Al-Lith area is characterized as a medium-enthalpy geothermal resource with high surface temperatures of up to 95°C at the Ain Al Harrah spring. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 28]])
- Ain Al Harrah hot spring exhibits reservoir temperature of 136°C, discharge enthalpy of 219 kJ/kg, and heat flow of 183 mW/m². ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 28]])
- The study identified ten zones (J-A) based on surface fracture density; the highest density was found in the northern part (Zone A). ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 18]])
- Subsurface structural analysis identified five high-density areas, three of which showed strong correlation with surface features. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 18]])
- The bedrock in the study area consists primarily of granitic, volcanic, and low-grade metamorphic rocks. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 18]])
- The study area is dominated by late Proterozoic plutonic, metasedimentary, and metavolcanic rocks to Quaternary alluvium deposits. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 34]])
- Pre-Cambrian layered rocks in the quadrangle were intruded by Pre-Phanerozoic plutonic rocks three times, resulting in complex structural features like vertical isoclinal folding and steeply dipping faults. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 34]])
- The Bathan Formation (Pliocene) consists of terrigenous clastic rocks including boulder/pebble conglomerates and coarse-grained sandstones, linked to Red Sea basin evolution. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 44]])
- Miocene gabbro dikes in the area are approximately 22 million years old (19-27 Ma) and range in width from 75 to 500 m. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 44]])
- The quartz diorite complex was radiometrically dated with a whole-rock Rb-Sr age of 895±173 m.y. and an initial Sr 87/Sr 86 ratio of .7025±.0003. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 40]])

## Limitations

The accuracy of temperature estimates based on cation geothermometry is questioned because the samples have not yet reached full equilibrium with their host rocks (immature water).

## Recommendations and Future Work

Further exploration and research of the Al-Lith resource are warranted to unlock its potential as a sustainable energy source; results can serve as a framework for other geothermal fields.
