# DETECTION OF SHALLOW SUBSURFACE CAVITIES USING 2D &amp; 3D ELECTRICAL RESISTIVITY TOMOGRAPHY ALONG THE RED SEA COAST, DUBA REGION, TABUK-SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the detection of shallow subsurface cavities and weathered zones in coralline limestone along the Red Sea coast using 2D and 3D electrical resistivity tomography (ERT). The study concludes that ERT is a precise and efficient method for delineating soil heterogeneity and identifying potential hazards like sediment-filled voids.

---

## Research Problem

The presence of karst features, such as cavities and weathered zones in Quaternary coralline limestone, poses significant risks to civil engineering projects due to the potential for ground subsidence. Traditional drilling methods are costly and time-consuming for extensive mapping of these features.

## Objectives

- detect and map shallow subsurface cavities and weathered zones
- evaluate their depth (roof and floor) and the lateral dimensions
- detect different geologic layers and the water table
- correlate ERT results with available geotechnical data and topographic features using aerial images
- Detect shallow subsurface cavities using 2D and 3D electrical resistivity tomography (ERT).
- Correlate ERT results with borehole logs (RQD values) and topographic features.

## Methods

The study utilized a Syscal Pro earth resistivity meter to collect data using 1022 electrodes. Two survey designs were employed: 2D pole-dipole and 2D Wenner-Schlumberger arrays for 2D profiles, and a 3D pole-dipole array for 3D volumes. Data was processed using ERTLab software, involving statistical analysis to remove 'bad data points' (outliers) before performing iterative inversions based on the Finite Element Method. Results were visualized as XY depth-slices, XZ cross-sections, and 3D iso-resistivity volumes.

## Data and Materials

Data from a survey area approximately 300m from the Red Sea shoreline; 1022 electrodes used for data collection; 4 boreholes (BH-14, BH-15, BH-16, BH-17) providing RQD and stratigraphy data; 3D resistivity models covering depths up to 20m.

## Key Findings

- The ERT survey successfully identified several low resistivity areas, which were subsequently confirmed as weak zones or sediment-filled underground cavities through drilling. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 16]])
- The inverted resistivity data provided a clear image of weathered soils, the distribution of weak zones, and the water table. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 16]])
- The survey area was characterized by coralline limestone of Quaternary age, which is soft, porous, and nonhomogeneous. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 19]])
- Ground penetrating radar (GPR) was found unsuitable for the site due to high ground salinity causing signal attenuation. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 25]])
- The 2D transects were spaced at 3.36 m with an electrode spacing of 1.6 m, while 3D profiles had an electrode spacing of 2.5 m. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 37]])
- The survey identified a well-defined water table at approximately 10.0 m depth, characterized by low resistivity values (< 3 Ohm.m). ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 73]])
- High resistivity anomalies (up to 200 Ohm.m) between 0 and 5 meters depth were identified as being spatially correlated with dry wadi beds. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 75]])
- No evidence of large cavities or voids was found, as these would typically manifest as very high resistivity values (several thousands of Ohm.m), whereas the maximum recorded value was 200 Ohm.m. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 81]])
- RQD values for coralline limestone were poor to very poor, ranging from 0% to 31%, with a mean of 5% in most boreholes except BH-16 (mean 22%). ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 70]])
- A specific high resistivity zone (80 to 120 Ohm.m) between 1.5 m and 4.5 m depth in the vicinity of BH-16 was attributed to more compact volumes of limestone. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 71]])

## Limitations

The interpretation of ERT data is inherently ambiguous due to factors like temperature, porosity, conductivity, salinity, and clay content; however, this was mitigated by correlating results with borehole logs and aerial photography.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Future research should use combinations of resistivity and shear-wave tomography (e.g., the ratio of resistivity to Vs) to reduce ambiguity in identifying voids and distinguish between alluvium/rock contacts.
