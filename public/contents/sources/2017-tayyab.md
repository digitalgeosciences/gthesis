# DETECTION OF SHALLOW SUBSURFACE CAVITIES USING 2D &amp; 3D ELECTRICAL RESISTIVITY TOMOGRAPHY ALONG THE RED SEA COAST, DUBA REGION, TABUK-SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the detection of shallow subsurface cavities and geological features in a coralline limestone formation along the Red Sea coast using 2D and 3D electrical resistivity tomography (ERT). The study concludes that while no large voids were found, ERT successfully mapped the groundwater table, lithological variations, and paleo-wadi beds.

---

## Research Problem

Poor rock quality and subsurface voids were encountered during a geotechnical investigation for building construction along the Red Sea coast in Duba Region, Tabuk, Saudi Arabia, creating concerns about the structural integrity of the foundation support in complex Quaternary coralline limestone beds.

## Objectives

- Determine the extent of shallow voids through geophysical investigation.
- Identify areas prone to ground subsidence due to the collapse of cavities.
- Map soil heterogeneity and identify weathered zones in the top 15-20 m.
- Demonstrate the applicability of ERT for investigating complex geological sites.
- detect and map shallow subsurface cavities and weathered zones
- evaluate their depth (roof and floor) and the lateral dimensions
- detect different geologic layers and the water table
- correlate ERT results with available geotechnical data and topographic features using aerial images
- Investigate different stratigraphic layers using 2D ERT data.
- Acquire 3D ERT data and merge it with 2D datasets to create a single global 3D inversion for visualization.
- Correlate ERT results with borehole logs and topographic features from aerial photographs to mitigate the non-uniqueness of the inverse problem.
- Perform 2D and 3D electrical resistivity tomography (ERT) to map subsurface features.
- Correlate ERT results with geotechnical data (RQD values) from boreholes.
- Identify potential cavities or voids in the limestone formation.
- Map the groundwater table and its influence on rock quality.
- Identify and map subsurface cavities using 2D and 3D electrical resistivity tomography (ERT).
- Evaluate the suitability of the rock formation for building construction in the Duba Region.
- Provide a detailed characterization of the subsoil conditions including depth to bedrock and presence of high-resistivity anomalies.
- To identify and map subsurface cavities and voids in the coastal rock formations.
- To evaluate the suitability of the ground for construction using 2D and 3D ERT techniques.
- To characterize the electrical resistivity of the Quaternary coralline limestone beds.
- Identify subsurface cavities and poor rock quality using 2D and 3D ERT.
- Assess the suitability of the subsoil for construction purposes.

## Methods

The study utilizes Electrical Resistivity Tomography (ERT) in both 2D and 3D configurations to image the subsurface. The methodology involves selecting appropriate electrode arrays (such as Wenner, Schlumberger, dipole-dipole, pole-dipole, or pole-pole) to measure apparent resistivity. These measurements are then used to create a spatial distribution of intrinsic resistivity to identify anomalies like air-filled cavities (high resistivity) or water-filled voids (low resistivity). The results are cross-referenced with borehole logs and aerial imagery for validation.

## Data and Materials

Data acquired from 12 parallel 2D profiles over an area of ~4,500 m² and two C-shaped 3D loops. The system utilized 72 electrodes with a sampling rate of 6,000-7,000 measurements per hour. A total of approximately 64,000 measurements were collected from a grid of 1022 electrode combinations.

## Key Findings

- The ERT survey successfully identified several low resistivity areas (< 3.0 Ohm.m) which were later confirmed by drilling as sediment-filled underground cavities or weak zones. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 16]])
- High resistivity volumes (> 70 Ohm.m) were identified as potential shallow resistive anomalies (e.g., dry river beds/wadi beds). ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 12]])
- The 3D tomogram showed a lift in low resistivity volume on the north-western side, attributed to high water permeation or high degree of fracturing. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 12]])
- Correlation between ERT results and RQD values confirmed that areas with lower resistivity corresponded to zones with poor rock quality (low RQD). ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 18]])
- The survey successfully mapped the water table, showing a distinct boundary at low resistivity levels. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 12]])
- ERT was selected as the primary tool because it offers rapid, less labor-intensive, and cost-effective imaging of shallow subsurface with adequate resolution compared to other methods in high-salinity environments. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 25]])
- Ground penetrating radar (GPR) was ruled out for the site due to high ground salinity which significantly attenuated the signal. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 25]])
- Empty (air-filled) cavities are expected to have higher resistivity than undisturbed ground, while flooded (water-filled) cavities result in lower values. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 33]])
- The study area is characterized by coralline limestone of Quaternary age along the Red Sea coast. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 22]])
- The survey area was characterized by smoothly varying topography and required investigation down to 20 m depth. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 54]])
- Data filtering during pre-processing successfully removed 7.8% of erroneous data points (bad data) before inversion. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 60]])
- The Pole-Dipole array provided good signal-to-noise characteristics and intermediate depth penetration, while the Wenner-Schlumberger array served as a compromise between resolution and depth. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 58]])
- Contact resistance (CRS) values were successfully maintained below 2,000 ohms for the majority of measurements. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 56]])
- The groundwater table was identified as a low resistivity layer (< 3 Ohm.m) starting at approximately 10 m depth. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 73]])
- RQD values for the coralline limestone were generally poor to very poor, ranging from 0% to 12% (mean 5%) in BH-14, BH-15, and BH-17. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 70]])

## Limitations

The interpretation of ERT data is inherently ambiguous due to factors like temperature, porosity, and salinity; however, the use of borehole logs as constraints helped reduce this uncertainty. The study noted that while no voids were found in the current survey, a larger area or different methods might be needed for full characterization.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Future studies should combine ERT with shear-wave tomography to improve resolution and distinguish between features like alluvium/rock contacts. Using the ratio of resistivity to shear-wave velocity (Vs) is suggested to specifically highlight voids while suppressing other signals.
