# High-Resolution Sequence Stratigraphy of the Middle Jurasic Lower Fadhili Reserv.

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the high-resolution sequence stratigraphy of the Middle Jurassic Lower Fadhili carbonate reservoir within the Dhruma Formation in the Khurais Field. Using detailed sedimentological descriptions and core analysis from nine wells, it identifies two main composite sequences (LFC1 and LFC2) and characterizes five distinct facies to establish a stratigraphic framework for reservoir characterization.

---

## Research Problem

The need for a high-resolution sequence stratigraphic framework to identify fluid flow units and good reservoir facies within the Lower Fadhili carbonate reservoir, which currently has poor to moderate quality and is not yet produced.

## Objectives

- Build high-resolution sequence stratigraphy of the Lower Fadhili reservoir in the Khurais complex.
- Identify and describe facies and create depositional model of the Lower Fadhili reservoir in the Khurais complex.
- Correlate reservoir and seal facies within the defined high-resolution sequence stratigraphic framework.
- Describe cores with good recovery and thin sections of the reservoir.
- Define significant stratigraphic surfaces and rock contacts.
- Understand the main control on the depositional cycles.
- Set criteria for picking the depositional cycles.
- Define a high-frequency sequence stratigraphic framework for the Lower Fadhili reservoir in Khurais complex
- Identify lithofacies based on core studies to understand sedimentology
- Utilize borehole gamma-ray logs to correlate parasequences and identify systems tracts
- Identify good reservoir facies within the sequence stratigraphic framework
- Define the high-resolution sequence stratigraphy of the Lower Fadhili reservoir.
- Identify and characterize the two main composite sequences (LFC1 and LFC2).
- Determine the depositional environments for various facies including stromatoporoid, Thaumatoporella, and Pfenderina trochoidea.
- Identify sequence boundaries such as karst surfaces and unconformities.
- Provide a sedimentological description of the Lower Fadhili reservoir.
- Establish a high-resolution sequence stratigraphy for the reservoir.
- Develop a depositional model for the Lower Fadhili reservoir.
- Perform core description and facies interpretation of the Lower Fadhili Reservoir.
- Integrate borehole gamma ray logs with core data for high-resolution sequence stratigraphy.
- Identify and characterize high-frequency sequences (HFS) within the Lower Fadhili member.

## Methods

The study utilized two main categories: 1. Geological Core Description: performed at Saudi Aramco facilities using hand lenses, binocular microscopes, and specific core description sheets (scale 1:120) to record features like stylolites, porosity, mineralogy, sedimentary structures, carbonate texture (modified Dunham), grain type, bed boundaries, grain size, fossil content, and cyclicity. Core-to-log calibration was performed by matching core descriptions with wireline gamma-ray logs. Lithofacies were identified based on sedimentological characteristics. 2. Sequence Stratigraphic Interpretation: included building a conceptual depositional model (2D), identifying parasequences using sedimentary structures and vertical facies changes, correlating parasequences across wells using gamma-ray character and core data, and identifying systems tracts and Maximum Flooding Surfaces (MFS) based on stacking patterns and accommodation/sedimentation rates.

## Data and Materials

Core data from seven cored wells (Well-A through Well-G) in the Khurais complex; Gamma-ray logs (API 0-50) for all studied wells; Core photographs and thin section images; Palynology study data (Saudi Aramco internal report No. 134).

## Key Findings

- The Lower Fadhili reservoir is composed of two composite sequences: Lower Fadhili composite sequence 1 (LFC1) and Lower Fadhili composite sequence 2 (LFC2). ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 13]])
- Each composite sequence is composed of four high-frequency sequences (HFS). ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 13]])
- The reservoir is characterized by five main facies: skeletal coated-grain grainstone (upper shoreface), peloid coated-grain grainstone and packstone (lower shoreface), Thaumatoporella wackestone (inner lagoon), stromatoporoid wackestone/packstone (outer lagoon), and Pfenderina trochoidea wackestone/mudstone (transgressive lagoon). ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 13]])
- The base of LFC1 overlies a subaerial exposure (karst) surface on top of the ooid coated-grain intraclast grainstone beach facies, which contains meteoric calcite cement. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 14]])

- The maximum flooding surface (MFS) can be traced across the fields and coincides with a thin layer of Pfenderina trochoidea wackestone facies. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 14]])
- In the highstand systems tract (HST) of LFC1, the abundance of Pfenderina trochoidea decreases upwards while the abundance of sponge spicules and Thaumatoporella increases upwards. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 14]])
- The second composite sequence (LFC2) highstand was dominated by fining-upward cycles of stromatoporoid packstone/wackestone facies, shallow marine lagoonal wackestone and shoreface coated-grain grainstone. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 14]])
- The peloid coated-grain grainstone and packstone facies has the best reservoir quality because the interparticle porosity is well connected. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 15]])
- The Lower Fadhili was deposited in a shallow marine, tropical intra-shelf basin. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 14]])
- The Lower Fadhili Reservoir is equivalent to the D6 unit. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 30]])
- The subsidence rate for the Lower and Middle Dhruma Formation in Saudi Arabia was 48 m/Ma. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 33]])
- The sedimentary environment of the Dhruma Formation is interpreted as a moderately shallow lagoonal setting with local stromatoporoid buildups. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 39]])
- Argillaceous mudstone and calcareous shale facies are typically 0.5 to 1.0 meters thick (mudstone) or 0.3 to 2.7 meters thick (shale). ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 49]])
- Cemented ooid coated-grain intraclast grainstone beds range from a few centimeters to 3.3 meters thick. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 50]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Establish additional regional sequence stratigraphic correlation between the Khurais area and nearby fields (e.g., Rimthan, Qatar Arch).
- Integrate multi-disciplinary data (production, pressure, petrophysical) for reservoir characterization.
- Investigate the source of early ferroan calcite cement in the beach facies.
- Perform biostratigraphic analysis on thin sections to better understand depositional settings.
- Conduct isotope studies to improve understanding of diagenesis, climate, and stratigraphy.
- Define the relationship between stromatoporoid morphologies and their depositional environments.
