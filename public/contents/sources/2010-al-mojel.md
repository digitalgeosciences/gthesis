# High-Resolution Sequence Stratigraphy of the Middle Jurasic Lower Fadhili Reserv.

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the high-resolution sequence stratigraphy and depositional model of the Middle Jurassic Lower Fadhili carbonate reservoir in the Khurais complex. It utilizes core descriptions, gamma-ray logs, and petrography to define two main composite sequences (LFC1 and LFC2) and identify key stratigraphic boundaries such as karst surfaces and hardgrounds.

---

## Research Problem

The Lower Fadhili reservoir has not been produced and requires a detailed stratigraphic framework and facies analysis to facilitate future development studies and identify high-quality reservoir zones within the complex carbonate system of the Khurais field.

## Objectives

- Build high-resolution sequence stratigraphy of the Lower Fadhili reservoir in the Khurais complex.
- Identify and describe facies and create depositional model of the Lower Fadhili reservoir in the Khurais complex.
- Correlate reservoir and seal facies within the defined high-resolution sequence stratigraphic framework.
- Describe cores with good recovery and thin sections of the reservoir.
- Define significant stratigraphic surfaces and rock contacts.
- Understand the main control on the depositional cycles.

## Methods

The study utilized a two-part methodology: 1. Geological Core Description: Detailed bed-by-bed sedimentological descriptions were performed using hand lenses and binocular microscopes, focusing on stylolites, porosity types, mineralogy, sedimentary structures (burrows, hardgrounds), carbonate texture (modified Dunham), grain type, and fossil content. 2. Sequence Stratigraphic Interpretation: A conceptual depositional model was built first; then parasequences were identified based on sedimentological characteristics and vertical facies changes. These were correlated across nine wells using gamma-ray logs and core descriptions to identify systems tracts, maximum flooding surfaces (MFS), and sequence boundaries.

## Data and Materials

The study utilized core samples from multiple wells in the Khurais complex (e.g., Wells A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H, I) and corresponding gamma-ray logs for the Lower Fadhili reservoir, which has a total thickness of up to 48 meters.

## Key Findings

- The Lower Fadhili reservoir is composed of two composite sequences: LFC1 and LFC2, each containing four high-frequency sequences (HFS). ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 13]])
- Five primary facies were identified in the reservoir: skeletal coated-grain grainstone (upper shoreface), peloid coated-grain grainstone/packstone (lower shoreface), Thaumatoporella wackestone (inner lagoon), stromatoporoid wackestone/packstone (outer lagoon), and Pfenderina trochoidea wackestone/mudstone (transgressive lagoon). ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 13]])
- The peloid coated-grain grainstone and packstone facies contains the best reservoir quality due to well-connected interparticle porosity. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 15]])
- The base of LFC1 overlies a subaerial exposure (karst) surface on top of the ooid coated-grain intraclast grainstone beach facies, which contains meteoric calcite cement. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 14]])
- The top of LFC2 is marked by a major subaerial exposure surface (karst), filled from above by green calcareous shale (reservoir seal). ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 14]])
- Maximum flooding surfaces (MFS) can be traced across the fields and coincide with a thin layer of Pfenderina trochoidea wackestone. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 14]])
- The average subsidence rate for the Dhruma Formation in the outcrop is 48 m/Ma. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 33]])
- The Lower Fadhili reservoir was deposited in a shallow marine, tropical intra-shelf basin on a carbonate ramp. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 14]])
- The Lower Fadhili reservoir is divided into two main composite sequences: LFC1 (base marked by an unconformity with karst features) and LFC2 (top marked by a major karst surface). ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 93]])
- LFC1 contains four high-frequency sequences (HFS1-HFS4), while LFC2 contains four high-frequency sequences (HFS5-HFS8). ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 73]])
- The transgressive system tract (TST) of LFC1 is characterized by a retrograding pattern and backstepping of the Pfenderina trochoidea wackestone facies. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 90]])
- The maximum flooding surface (MFS) of LFC2 corresponds to a thick stromatoporoid facies, indicating an open and well-circulated marine environment. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 91]])
- Type 1 sequence boundaries (unconformities) are identified at the base of LFC1 and top of LFC2, characterized by karst features and meteoric cementation. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 85]])
- Type 2 sequence boundaries (conformable) are identified as hardgrounds in the high-stand systems tract (HST), such as at the top of LFC1. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 86]])
- Sponge-spicule, Thaumatoporella wackestone, Stromatoporoid wackestone/packstone, and Pfenderina trochoidea wackestone facies have poor reservoir quality due to microporosity. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 94]])

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Establish additional regional sequence stratigraphic correlation between the Khurais area and nearby fields (e.g., Rimthan, Qatar Arch).
- Integrate multi-disciplinary data sets (production, pressure, petrophysical) for reservoir characterization.
- Perform thin section studies on ooid coated-grain intraclast grainstone to understand early ferroan calcite cementation.
- Conduct micropaleontological studies of biocomponents in Khurais complex to refine the depositional model.
- Perform isotope studies to better understand diagenesis, climate, and stratigraphy.
