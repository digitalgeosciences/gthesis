# QUANTIFYING THE DYNAMICS OF MIXED SILICICLASTIC AND CARBONATE SYSTEM OF THE TOARCIAN MARRAT FORMATION, SAUDI ARABIA: INSIGHTS FROM 3D STRATIGRAPHIC FORWARD MODELING

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the interplay between clastic influx and carbonate factories in the Toarcian Marrat Formation of Saudi Arabia using 3D stratigraphic forward modeling. It aims to quantify how these competing systems influenced the development of sequence architecture and geometry, ultimately providing a better understanding of spatial heterogeneity and potential reservoir identification.

---

## Research Problem

Understanding and quantifying the controlling processes behind the development of the mixed carbonate-clastic system in the Marrat Formation is challenging due to high heterogeneity and limited data for predicting lateral and vertical variations of lithology and facies.

## Objectives

- Quantify the dynamics of the mixed siliciclastic and carbonate system of the Toarcian Marrat Formation.
- Unravel the interplay between different allogenic and autogenic factors contributing to the formation of various sequence architectures.
- Develop a more realistic reference model by identifying the best-fit scenario for the Marrat Formation.
- Assess the impact of sediment supply, carbonate production, and sea level on sedimentary architecture.
- To understand the interplay between siliciclastic and carbonate sedimentation in Marrat Formation.
- To test multiple scenarios that best-fit with the deposition in Marrat Formation with stratigraphic forward modelling.
- To understand the impact of different input parameters governing the sedimentation using sensitivity analysis with stratigraphic forward modelling.
- To build sequence architecture (lateral and vertical lithology/facies variations) that captured the distribution of reservoir/source rock potential with stratigraphic forward modelling.
- Predict sequence architecture at the third stratigraphic order with limited datasets.
- Determine the outcomes of testing various geological assumptions or scenarios applied to the sedimentation processes of the Marrat Formation.
- Provide insight into a variability of regional-scale geometries as lateral and vertical texture/facies heterogeneity.
- Evaluate the influence of sediment supply, water discharge, and carbonate production on basin filling.
- Model the evolution of sedimentary units (Lower, Middle, and Upper Marrat) under varying sea-level conditions.
- Validate the stratigraphic forward model against actual outcrop and well data.
- Quantify the controlling parameters' response to the sequence architecture and geometry based on sensitivity analysis.
- Develop a more realistic reference model for the Marrat Formation using stratigraphic forward modeling.
- Evaluate the impact of sediment supply, carbonate production, sea level changes, transport coefficients, and wave energy on basin filling.
- Quantify the controlling processes behind the development of the Marrat Formation.
- Evaluate the impact of various parameters (eustasy, subsidence, sediment supply, carbonate production, and wave energy) on sedimentary architecture.
- Develop a robust reference model for the Marrat Formation to aid in identifying potential petroleum reservoirs and stratigraphic traps.
- Investigate the interaction between clastic influx and carbonate factories in a transgressive-regressive sequence.
- Use 3D stratigraphic forward modeling to provide insights into energy prospectivity.

## Methods

The study utilizes stratigraphic forward modeling (SFM) using Dionisos software as the primary platform, with Petrel used for data preparation and 3D basemaps. The methodology involves four stages: data preparation, analysis of input parameters, stratigraphic forward modeling, and model calibration. Input parameters include basin conditions (600 km x 500 km area, 100 kyr time steps), sea level curves based on Arabian Plate records, wave energy (30-110 kW/m2), sediment characteristics (five classes: medium carbonate, mud carbonate, sand, silt, and shale), and carbonate production rates. Sediment transport is modeled using empirical equations for gravity-driven and water-driven diffusion.

## Data and Materials

The study utilizes geological data from the Marrat Formation in the central Arabian platform, including outcrop data for calibration of the forward model. The research incorporates multi-parameter inputs: sediment supply (clastic and carbonate), sea level changes (eustasy), subsidence, wave energy, and sediment transport coefficients.

## Key Findings

- Sensitivity analysis indicates that sediment supply and carbonate production exert greater control compared to sea level changes on dictating the sedimentary architecture and dynamics. ([[2024-fahmi|Fahmi, 2024, p. 17]])
- The study revealed distinct aggradation-progradation geometries within the Marrat Formation that show good agreement with outcrop and subsurface observations. ([[2024-fahmi|Fahmi, 2024, p. 16]])
- Accurate quantification of sediment supply enables a more precise spatial delineation of siliciclastic and carbonate distributions across the basin, particularly to capture along-strike variability. ([[2024-fahmi|Fahmi, 2024, p. 16]])
- The Marrat Formation represents an ideal mixed carbonate-siliciclastic system developed under transgressive–regressive sequence. ([[2024-fahmi|Fahmi, 2024, p. 16]])
- The Marrat Formation is characterized by a complex lithological composition of interbedded sandstones, mudstones (claystone), with limestone and anhydrites in both lower and upper parts. ([[2024-fahmi|Fahmi, 2024, p. 28]])
- The Lower Marrat member is distinguished by an iron-stained surface overlaying the Late Triassic Minjur Sandstone, potentially reaching a thickness of up to 47 meters. ([[2024-fahmi|Fahmi, 2024, p. 29]])
- The Middle Marrat member features a significant reduction in thickness toward southern areas, where reddish mudstones are replaced by sandstone-rich deposits. ([[2024-fahmi|Fahmi, 2024, p. 30]])

- The best-fit reference model was identified with a double sediment supply source scenario filling the basin. ([[2024-fahmi|Fahmi, 2024, p. 40]])

## Limitations

The study faces uncertainties due to a lack of tectonic subsidence studies, uncertainty in global/regional eustasy, variable ecological constraints on carbonate production, and the absence of distributed well data and seismic data.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Future improvements should include higher resolution models (4th to 5th sequence order), high-resolution sequence stratigraphy data, biostratigraphy for paleobathymetry, cyclostratigraphy for sea-level proxies, and integration with seismic data.
