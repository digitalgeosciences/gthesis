# BASELINE MONITORING OF SELECTED ORGANOCHLORINE PESTICIDES (OCPS) AND ORGANOPHOSPHORUS PESTICIDES (OPPS) IN THE ARABIAN GULF

**Summary**: The thesis establishes a baseline for Organochlorine Pesticides (OCPs) and Organophosphorus Pesticides (OPPs) in the Arabian Gulf by analyzing sea water, marine sediment, and biota from Tarut Bay and Jubail Coastal Area. It utilizes GC-MS to quantify these pesticides, concluding that while OCPs are prevalent across all matrices, OPPs are primarily found in sediments due to their tendency to adsorb and persist.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of regular environmental monitoring programs for Persistent Organic Pollutants (POPs) in the Arabian Gulf despite its sensitive ecosystem and the need to assess the impact of OCPs and OPPs on local marine life and water quality.

## Objectives

- To determine the concentrations of selected OCPs and OPPs in aqueous, sediment and biota samples.
- To compare the contamination levels with the reported concentration values in the literature and WHO acceptable limits.
- Determine the baseline levels of OCPs and OPPs in sea water, sediment, and biota.
- Compare pesticide concentrations between Tarut Bay and Jubail Coastal Area.
- Assess the bioaccumulation of these pesticides in various fish species.

## Methods

The study utilized two primary extraction methods: Solid-phase Extraction (SPE) for liquid samples (sea water) and Ultrasound-assisted Extraction (UAE) for solid samples (marine sediment). Sea water was processed using C18 SPE discs with cyclohexane and acetone as eluents. Sediment samples were extracted using a mixture of cyclohexane and acetone under sonication. Both types of extracts were pre-concentrated to 1 mL before analysis via Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS).

## Data and Materials

Analysis of 6 OCPs (alpha-BHC, Heptachlor epoxide, Endosulfan, Dieldrin, o,p'-DDD, p,p'-DDT) and 5 OPPs (O,O,O-Triethyl Thiophosphate, Disulfoton, Methyl Parathion, Parathion, Famphur). Samples included sea water from Tarut Bay and Jubail, marine sediments from both areas, and biota samples including Oyster, Shrimp, Squid, Biyadh, Qeed, Emperor, Crab, Solea, Ibrahim, and Indian Mackerel.

## Key Findings

- DDD, a metabolite of DDT, was prevalent in all samples with an average concentration of 0.118 mg/L and a maximum of 0.300 mg/L in water samples. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 14]])
- The total level of OCPs in Tarut Bay was calculated at 1.628 mg/L with an average of 0.407 mg/L. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 14]])
- OCPs were not detected in Fresh Water samples at both Tarut Bay and Al-Oyoon. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 14]])
- OPPs were not detected in sea water samples but were found in significant amounts in marine sediments (average 20.6 mg/kg for Tarut Bay and 15.4 mg/kg for Jubail). ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 102]])
- In Biota, the level of OCPs was found to be orders of magnitude higher than OPPs. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 14]])
- OCP levels in Biotas were within acceptable limits according to WHO provisions. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 14]])
- The highest concentration of OPPs in Biota was found in Indian Mackerel and Crab, while the lowest value was in Shrimp. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 14]])
- Concentrations of OCPs in Tarut Bay significantly exceeded those in the Jubail coastal area due to agricultural runoff from Tarut Island. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 14]])
- The highest concentration of OCPs in sea water was found at TSW3 (0.982 mg/l), with o,p'-DDD being the most prevalent pesticide across all samples. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 88]])
- In marine sediments, Tarut Bay showed higher average OCP concentrations (0.369 mg/kg) compared to Jubail coastal area (0.290 mg/kg). ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 92]])
- Dieldrin was the highest OCP observed in Biota, with a maximum concentration of 0.133 mg/kg in SOLEA. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 96]])
- Parathion was the most abundant OPP detected in fresh water samples, reaching up to 0.386 mg/L at site OGW5. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 97]])
- The ratio of DDD/DDT indicates that no new DDT is being introduced into the environment, as o,p'-DDD exists primarily as a metabolite. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 93]])

## Limitations

Some levels of OPPs and OCPs in certain samples could not be established due to detection limits or sample constraints; specifically, some fresh water and sea water samples showed non-detectable (ND) results for specific pesticides.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Establish a structured program to monitor organic pollutants to assess risks to human health and the environment.
- Develop new methods for detecting trace organic pollutants to ensure reliable determination at different times.
- Increase public awareness regarding the dangers of pesticide use.
