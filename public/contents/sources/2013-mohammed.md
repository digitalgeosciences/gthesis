# BASELINE MONITORING OF SELECTED ORGANOCHLORINE PESTICIDES (OCPS) AND ORGANOPHOSPHORUS PESTICIDES (OPPS) IN THE ARABIAN GULF

**Summary**: The thesis establishes a baseline monitoring of Organochlorine Pesticides (OCPs) and Organophosphorus Pesticides (OPPs) in the Arabian Gulf. It analyzes these contaminants across four locations including Tarut Bay, Jubail Coastal Area, Tarut Island, and Al-Oyoon agricultural area using various extraction methods for sea water, groundwater, sediment, and biota.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of regular environmental monitoring programs for persistent organic pollutants (POPs) in the Arabian Gulf since 2005 despite its sensitive ecosystem and the transition from OCPs to OPPs which still pose significant health risks.

## Objectives

- Determine baseline levels of selected OCPs and OPPs in sea water, groundwater, marine sediment, and biota.
- Identify the prevalence of specific pesticides like DDD (a metabolite of DDT) across different sample types.
- Evaluate the concentration of these pesticides against safety limits provided by organizations like the WHO.
- To determine the concentrations of selected OCPs and OPPs in aqueous, sediment and biota samples.
- To compare the contamination levels with the reported concentration values in the literature and WHO acceptable limits.
- Determine baseline levels of OCPs and OPPs in sea water, sediment, and biota.
- Assess differences in pollutant concentrations across different sampling locations (coastal vs. agricultural).
- Quantify specific pesticides including alpha-BHC, Heptachlor epoxide, Endosulfan, Dieldrin, o,p'-DDD, p,p'-DDT, Triethyl Thiophosphate, Disulfoton, Methyl Parathion, and Parathion.
- Determine the levels of Organochlorine Pesticides (OCPs) in marine sediment, sea water, and biota.
- Determine the levels of Organophosphorus Pesticides (OPPs) in sea water and fresh water samples.
- Assess the impact of agricultural activities on pesticide concentration in Tarut Bay.

## Methods

The study utilized two primary extraction methods: Ultrasonication Assisted Extraction (UAE) for solid samples (sediment and biota) and Solid-Phase Extraction (SPE) for liquid samples (sea water and groundwater). Following extraction, the concentration of target analytes was determined using Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS). Calibration curves were established for specific pesticides including Heptachlor, Dieldrin, Endosulfan, alpha BHC, o,p'-DDT, p',p-DDD, and various organophosphorus compounds like Parathion and Disulfoton.

## Data and Materials

Samples of sea water, groundwater, sediment, and biota (fish species) were collected from four locations: Tarut Bay, Jubail Coastal Area, Tarut Island, and Al-Oyoon agricultural area. The study also references data from 17 sediment samples from the Gulf of Aden, 36 samples of water/sediment/fish in other regions, and whale tissue analysis.

## Key Findings

- DDD, a metabolite of DDT, was prevalent in all samples and exhibited the highest concentrations among other pesticides. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 14]])
- The average concentration of DDD in all water samples was 0.118 mg/L, with a peak concentration of 0.300 mg/L. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 14]])
- The total level of OCPs in Tarut Bay was calculated at 1.628 mg/L, with an average of 0.407 mg/L. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 14]])
- OCPs were not detected in fresh water samples at both Tarut Bay and Al-Oyoon. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 14]])
- OPPs were not detected in sea water samples. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 14]])
- In biota, the level of OCPs was found to be orders of magnitude higher than OPPs. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 14]])
- The levels of OCPs detected in biotas were within acceptable limits according to WHO provisions. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 14]])
- The highest concentration of OPPs in biota was found in Indian Mackerel and Crab, while the lowest value was observed in Shrimp. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 14]])
- Concentrations of OCPs in Tarut Bay significantly exceeded those in the Jubail coastal area due to agricultural runoff from Tarut Island. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 15]])
- The concentration of DDTs in various countries' sediments ranged from 0.004 to 0.312 ng/g (specifically 0.004-0.139 ng/g for Saudi Arabia). ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 36]])
- Polychlorinated biphenyls (PCBs) were found in the range of 0.40 to 4.97 ng/g dry sample weight in Gulf of Aden samples. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 37]])
- The mean concentration of OCPs in sediment samples was found in a range of 0.014 to 2.571 ug/g. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 39]])
- Total polychlorinated biphenyls (PCBs) were found from 62 to 601 ng/g and total Organochlorine pesticides (OCPs) from 44.5 to 145 ng/g in sediment samples. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 39]])
- Chlorpyrifos levels were found at 139, 135.5, 24.5, 303.8 and 89.6 ppb in water samples across different seasons. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 41]])
- Malathion results of spring 1999 and winter 2001 were 446.0 and 71.9 ppb, respectively. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013]])

## Limitations

Some levels of OPPs and OCPs in certain samples could not be established due to investigation limitations. Specific challenges were noted in tracking concentrations in fresh water in Tarut Bay and Al-Oyoon, as well as understanding OPPs in sea water.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Establish a structured program to monitor organic pollutants for human health and environmental risk assessment.
- Implement new methods for detecting trace organic pollutants to ensure reliable determination over time.
- Increase public awareness regarding the dangers of pesticides and their impact on the environment.
