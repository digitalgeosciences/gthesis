# High-Resolution Stratigraphy, Biostratigraphy, and Diagenesis of Upper Khartam Member, Khuff Formation: Implication on Reservoir Quality and Architecture. Outcrop Analog from Central Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the high-resolution stratigraphic framework and diagenetic impacts on reservoir quality in the Upper Khartam Member of the Khuff Formation using outcrop analogs from Central Saudi Arabia. It utilizes microfacies analysis, petrography, and geostatistical modeling to evaluate how architectural elements and diagenesis control porosity, permeability, and lateral connectivity.

---

## Research Problem

The Khuff reservoirs are characterized by complex heterogeneities at sub-seismic scales that are difficult to resolve with standard seismic data. There is a need to understand the interplay between primary sedimentary architecture (lithofacies) and subsequent diagenetic overprinting on porosity and permeability in the Upper Khartam Member.

## Objectives

- To investigate the detailed sedimentological and diagenetic controls on reservoir quality and heterogeneity at scale ranging from pore-geometry to inter-well spacing
- To determine the lithofacies and lithofacies associations
- To construct a high-resolution stratigraphic scheme using sequence stratigraphic concept
- To analyze the diagenetic evolution of the reservoir and to construct qualitative diagenetic models.
- To investigate the impact of the high-resolution stratigraphy and diagenesis on reservoir quality and heterogeneity
- To develop an integrated qualitative-quantitative model that will lead to a better description of reservoir characterization of upper Khartam reservoir analog

## Methods

The study employs an integrated approach combining field and laboratory work. Techniques include microfacies analysis, biostratigraphy (to refine chronostratigraphy), and sequence stratigraphy to define beds, bedsets, and high-frequency sequences. Petrophysical measurements (porosity and permeability) were conducted on approximately 100 samples. Geostatistical tools, including semivariogram analysis and geocellular modeling (using the Assigning Value method), were used to upscale data from outcrop scales to reservoir scales. Analytical techniques included XRD for mineralogy, SEM-EDS for pore-lining minerals, and LIDAR/image analysis for large-scale mapping.

## Data and Materials

Data includes 4 outcrop localities in central Saudi Arabia (Buraydah and Al Faydah quadrangles), approximately 100 representative samples for microfacies and petrophysical analysis, and high-resolution photo-mosaics covering an extent of about 7 km.

## Key Findings

- Seventeen lithofacies were defined, including coarse-grained oolitic grainstone (LFT 1), interlaminated quartz-bearing recrystallized limestone (LFT 2), and marlstone (LFT 7). ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 30]])
- The study identified seven depositional settings: intertidal-subtidal flats, supratidal settings, intertidal channels and creeks, shoal ridges, reef complex, and outer ramp settings. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 30]])
- The study identified approximately 20,000 beds tracked laterally, ranging in thickness from 5 to 40 cm and extending between 5 and 300 m. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 82]])
- Six major diagenetic processes were identified: dissolution, pore lining and pore blocking calcite cementation, dolomitization, fracturing, and stylolitization. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 31]])
- Ten porosity types were differentiated: intergranular, intragranular, shelter, dissolution-enlarged, moldic, vuggy, micro-porosity, and porosity in relation to dolomite–dedolomite, dolomite-leaching, and fracture development. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 31]])
- Intertidal sheets (LFT 1) are dominated by dissolution, moldic, and vuggy porosity, while recrystallized limestone features intercrystalline and fracture porosity. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 31]])
- XRD data showed a control of mineralogical associations (such as SiO2 and SrSO4) on the lateral intermittence of the intra-reservoir body. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 31]])
- SEM observations revealed that clay minerals played a central role in lining pore space, significantly impacting reservoir quality. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 32]])
- Stylolites and fractures acted as primary controls on the lateral segmentation of single bodies into different hydraulic units by acting as conduits to or barriers for vertical fluid flow. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 32]])
- Porosity of intertidal sheets has a large horizontal range (approx. 300 m) compared to intertidal channels and creeks (approx. 150 m). ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 264]])
- The Upper Khartam Member is characterized by oolitic to peloidal to bioclastic lithofacies, with some parts dominated by detrital to lacustrine claystone and dolomitic sandstone. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 64]])
- Seventeen lithofacies were defined; LFT 1 (coarse-grained oolitic grainstone) is the most dominant in terms of facies abundance (25.8%) and quantity (39.5%). ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 88]])
- Lithofacies LFT 7 (Marlstone) has a high facies abundance of 20.4% and is critical for sequence stratigraphic interpretations. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 93]])
- High-frequency sequences (likely fifth-order) are predominantly regressive and are linked to shifts in shoreline position, potentially related to Milankovitch cycles. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 82]])
- The Khartam member is approximately 37m thick in the type section but varies significantly (up to 60m) in other locations like Darma quadrangle. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 67]])

## Limitations

Data was drastically distorted at the high-frequency sequences, though bedset levels preserved some original data to a certain extent.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The findings provide a unique input for enhanced geological modeling and fluid flow simulation to better assess Khuff reservoirs in Eastern Arabian Fields.
