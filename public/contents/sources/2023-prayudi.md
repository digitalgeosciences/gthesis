# Effects of Physio-chemical Variations on Modern Intertidal and Lagoonal Benthic Communities in the Hypersaline Environment of the western Arabian Gulf

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the resilience of intertidal biocalcifying organisms and benthic foraminiferal communities in the hypersaline, high-temperature environment of the western Arabian Gulf. It concludes that these organisms exhibit high thermal tolerance and that morphological deformities in foraminifera are likely driven by natural salinity fluctuations rather than anthropogenic pollutants.

---

## Research Problem

The impact of extreme physical and chemical stressors (high temperature, salinity, and potential pollution) on intertidal benthic communities in the Western Arabian Gulf is poorly understood, particularly regarding the specific thermal tolerance thresholds of local gastropod and ostracod species.

## Objectives

- Identify the upper thermal limits of selected intertidal communities, such as mollusks (gastropods), crustaceans (ostracods), and foraminifera under controlled conditions.
- Quantify the level of selected physio-chemical parameters such as temperature, salinity, trace metals (Mn, Cr, Cu, Ni, As, Pb, Cd, Zn, and Hg), organic constituents (total organic carbon and total carbon) and the total assemblages of foraminifera with normal and abnormal/deformed morphologies.
- Implement appropriate statistical analyses for quantifying the assemblages of foraminifera (diversity indices) and correlating between the morphological deformities and the most influential physio-chemical parameters.
- Investigate the resilience of intertidal biocalcifying organisms to high temperatures.
- Assess the impact of hypersaline conditions on living benthic foraminiferal communities.
- Determine the cause of morphological deformities in foraminifera (anthropogenic vs. natural).

## Methods

The study utilized field sampling from two locations: a lagoon in Bahrain (Murray's Pool) and a seasonal pond in Saudi Arabia (Half Moon Bay). Laboratory experiments were conducted to determine thermal limits for gastropods (Pirenella conica and Planaxis sulcatus) using three scenarios (full exposure, partial exposure, and full coverage) in petri dishes. Ostracods (Cyprideis sp.) were subjected to a 10-minute interval heating protocol with 5°C increments. Data analysis was performed using Python libraries (pandas, numpy, matplotlib, seaborn) via the Spyder IDE.

## Data and Materials

Samples from 6 locations in the western Arabian Gulf (Saudi Arabia and Bahrain). Data includes counts of over 109 foraminifera specimens across various species, including LBF and SBF. Field data included temperature, salinity, and pH measurements; laboratory data included trace elements, TOC, and grain size distribution.

## Key Findings

- Gastropod species Pirenella conica showed an HCT of 31.6°C in the first scenario, with half entering a comatose state at 37.5°C and all becoming immobile at 39°C. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 46]])
- In the second scenario (partial coverage), Pirenella conica showed HCT of 39.1°C, CT50 of 44.8°C, and CT100 of 48°C. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 46]])
- The tropical periwinkle (Planaxis sulcatus) showed HCT at 37.6-38.2°C in scenarios 2 and 3, with CT100 reaching 43.2°C to 44.1°C. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 48]])
- Ostracods (Cyprideis sp.) showed an HCT of 39.3°C, a CT50 of 46-47°C, and a CT100 of 47.9°C. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 48]])
- The upper lethal limit (ULT) for ostracods was observed at approximately 53.5°C. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 48]])
- A high proportion (25%) of living foraminifera displayed various deformities despite minimal pollution levels, likely due to salinity fluctuations and hypersaline constraints. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 17]])
- Both gastropod and ostracod species exhibited high thermal tolerance, with many remaining mobile or alive at temperatures exceeding 40°C (some up to 48°C). ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 53]])
- Approximately one-quarter of foraminifera specimens were living at the time of sampling across all sites. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 56]])
- Morphological deformities in foraminiferal tests increased significantly (averaging over 40%) in areas with hypersalinity above 40 PSU. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 57]])
- Trace element and organic content analysis showed no significant anthropogenic pollution, with values well below international safety limits. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 65]])
- Grain size distribution was consistent across sites, with 95% to 99% of the material being sand-sized (3 Φ to 1 Φ). ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 65]])
- LBF exhibited a higher occurrence of morphological deformities (>40%) compared to SBF across all salinity gradients. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 69]])
- Over 50% of deformed specimens were identified in adult forms, particularly in LBF (e.g., flaring in Peneroplis). ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 70]])
- EBH-FS locality exhibited the highest species diversity (74 species) and highest number of specimens per gram. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 66]])

## Limitations

Direct observation during thermal exposure was not feasible due to equipment limitations; therefore, regular specimen checks were performed at specified intervals. The study notes that some results are based on average values from main and control experiments.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Conduct more thermal experimental approaches for various intertidal communities in the Gulf.
- Use more controlled instrumentation (e.g., thermal baths) for precise temperature control in future studies.
- Prolong exposure times with varied timing and temperatures to observe behavior changes clearly.
- Increase sampling across the Western Arabian Gulf to provide a more representative dataset.
