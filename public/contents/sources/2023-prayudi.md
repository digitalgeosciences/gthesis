# Effects of Physio-chemical Variations on Modern Intertidal and Lagoonal Benthic Communities in the Hypersaline Environment of the western Arabian Gulf

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the diversity and morphological deformities of benthic foraminiferal communities in the hypersaline, naturally stressed environments of the western Arabian Gulf. By analyzing physical/chemical parameters (salinity, temperature, trace elements) and foraminiferal assemblages across six sites, it concludes that morphologic abnormalities are likely driven by extreme salinity rather than anthropogenic pollution.

---

## Research Problem

The impact of rising temperatures and hypersaline conditions on marine life in the Arabian Gulf is significant, yet there is a lack of experimental data regarding the specific upper thermal thresholds of local biocalcifying organisms (gastropods and ostracods) in these high-stress environments.

## Objectives

- Assess the upper thermal limits and salinity tolerance of representative species including gastropods (Pirenella conica, Planaxis sulcatus) and ostracods (Cyprideis sp.).
- Evaluate the occurrence of morphological deformities in benthic foraminiferal communities as indicators of environmental stress.
- Provide a natural baseline for biocalcifying organisms in relatively pristine zones of the western Gulf.
- Identify the upper thermal limits of selected intertidal communities, such as mollusks (gastropods), crustaceans (ostracods), and foraminifera under controlled conditions.
- Quantify the level of selected physio-chemical parameters such as temperature, salinity, trace metals (Mn, Cr, Cu, Ni, As, Pb, Cd, Zn, and Hg), organic constituents (total organic carbon and total carbon) and the total assemblages of foraminifera with normal and abnormal/deformed morphologies.
- Implement appropriate statistical analyses for quantifying the assemblages of foraminifera (diversity indices) and correlating between the morphological deformities and the most influential physio-chemical parameters.
- Evaluate the resilience of intertidal biocalcifying organisms (gastropods and ostracods) to thermal stress in the western Arabian Gulf.
- Determine specific thermal thresholds (HCT, CT50, CT100, LT50) for local gastropod species (Pirenella conica and Planaxis sulcatus).
- Assess the impact of hypersalinity on benthic foraminiferal communities in the western Arabian Gulf.
- Quantify morphological deformities in foraminifera as a response to salinity-induced constraints.
- Investigate the diversity of foraminifera in intertidal and lagoonal areas of the western Arabian Gulf.
- Assess the impact of varying salinity levels on the morphology of benthic foraminiferal communities.
- Determine if anthropogenic factors (trace elements, hydrocarbons) or natural stressors are the primary drivers of morphological deformities.
- Evaluate the resilience of intertidal biocalcifiers in the western Arabian Gulf.
- Quantify the occurrence of morphological deformities among benthic foraminiferal assemblages.
- Assess the impact of anthropogenic pollutants versus natural stressors (salinity, temperature) on foraminiferal morphology.
- Examine the resilience of intertidal biocalcifying organisms in the naturally stressed environment of the western Arabian Gulf.
- Assess the diversity and composition of benthic foraminiferal communities in hypersaline conditions.
- Evaluate the impact of physico-chemical variations on these marine communities.
- Examine the resilience of intertidal biocalcifying organisms in the western Arabian Gulf's naturally stressed environment.
- Assess the impact of physico-chemical variations on benthic foraminiferal assemblages.
- Identify and characterize morphological deformities in modern foraminifera as indicators of environmental stress.

## Methods

Field sampling was conducted at six sites in Saudi Arabia and Bahrain. Physical parameters (pH, salinity, temperature) were measured using a multiparameter probe (Hanna HI98194). Sediment samples were analyzed for trace elements (Mn, Cr, Cu, Ni, As, Pb, Cd, Zn, Hg) using an iCAP RQ ICP-MS after nitric acid digestion. Organic content (TOC and TC) was quantified using an Elementar Soli TOC Cube analyzer. Grain size distribution was determined via HELOS Particle Size Analysis. Foraminifera were stained with Rose Bengal for identification of living/dead populations; 300 specimens per sample were selected for analysis. Morphological deformities were categorized as mild or severe. Statistical analyses (diversity indices: richness, dominance, Shannon-Wiener, evenness, Fisher's alpha) were performed using PAST v4.03 software.

## Data and Materials

Two gastropod species (Pirenella conica, Planaxis sulcatus) and one ostracod genus (Cyprideis sp.). Samples were collected from two sites: Murray's Pool (Bahrain) and Half Moon Bay (Saudi Arabia). A total of 18 snail specimens per trial across three scenarios was used for the gastropod study. Sediment samples were also collected to replicate local conditions.

## Key Findings

- Gastropods and ostracods exhibited elevated thermal tolerance limits when immersed compared to standard expectations, demonstrating resilience to current extremes. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 17]])
- A high proportion (25%) of living foraminifera displayed various deformities despite minimal pollution levels in the sampled areas. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 17]])
- Note: The specific percentage of deformity is linked to salinity fluctuations and hypersaline constraints. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023]])
- page_num_ref_note_only_not_in_json_field_but_included_in_logic_for_finding_quality_check_during_extraction_process_if_needed_by_user_query_instruction_actually_it_is_just_a_finding_item ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023]])
- Both gastropods and ostracods exhibited elevated upper thermal tolerance, particularly when immersed in water within the experimental setup. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 31]])
- Some specimens exhibited distinct behaviors, such as varying recovery times after the experiment and partial immobility during the trial, though none displayed complete immobility. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 31]])
- In Murray's Pool, living meiofaunal organisms were exclusively sighted in the tidal channels and permanent pools where salinity ranged from 43 to 47 PSU. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 33]])
- Meio- and macrofaunal assemblages in the seasonal pools of the Saudi Arabian locality thrive in salinities exceeding 60 PSU. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 33]])
- Surface substrate temperatures in Murray's Pool exceeded 52°C in summer, while Saudi Arabian sites reached over 55°C. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 33]])
- The mud creeper (Pirenella conica) showed a heat coma temperature (HCT) of 31.6°C in open air, while full water immersion increased the threshold for half of the population to reach 47.6°C. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 46]])
- The tropical periwinkle (Planaxis sulcatus) showed a gradual decline in activity with an HCT at 30.7°C and a CT50 of approximately 40.1-40.9°C. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 48]])
- Ostracods (Cyprideis sp.) exhibited high resilience with an HCT of 39.3°C, a CT50 of 46-47°C, and an upper lethal temperature (LT100) of 53.5°C. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 49]])
- Approximately one-quarter of foraminifera specimens were living at the time of sampling in the western Arabian Gulf. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 56]])
- Morphological deformities in foraminifera increased significantly (averaging above 40%) across a salinity gradient above 40 PSU. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 57]])
- Environmental analysis of marine sediment showed no significant anthropogenic stressors, suggesting deformities are likely due to salinity-induced constraints. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 57]])

## Limitations

The study notes that while current data suggests salinity is the primary driver of deformities, further validation through experimental work with cultured specimens in controlled laboratory settings is required to confirm these findings and eliminate other variables.

## Recommendations and Future Work

- Conduct more thermal experimental approaches for various intertidal and shallow water communities in the Arabian Gulf.
- Use controlled instrumentation like a thermal bath instead of an incubator for more precise results.
- Prolong exposure times with varied timing and temperature to identify specific behaviors (comatose/immobilized).
- Increase sampling across the Western Arabian Gulf with sufficient spacing to provide more representative data for statistical analysis.
