# High-Resolution Stratigraphy and Diagenesis of Upper Khartam Member, Khuff Formation: Implication on Reservoir Quality and Architecture. Outcrop Analog from Central Saudi Arabia

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the high-resolution stratigraphy and diagenetic impacts on reservoir quality in the Upper Khartam Member of the Khuff Formation. By analyzing four outcrop locations in Central Saudi Arabia using microfacies analysis, XRD, and SEM-EDS, it concludes that while original depositional textures had high potential, diagenetic processes like cementation, dissolution, and pore-lining clay minerals significantly degraded reservoir quality.

---

## Research Problem

Subsurface data for the Khuff reservoirs are often insufficient to reveal high-resolution architectural and reservoir quality elements due to large inter-well spacing and low vertical resolution of seismic data. The Upper Khartam Member, specifically, requires detailed stratigraphic and diagenetic characterization to understand its heterogeneity.

## Methods

The study utilized four outcrop localities in central Saudi Arabia. Approximately 100 samples were collected for microfacies, micropaleontology, and porosity/permeability analysis. High-resolution photo-mosaics were created to map the area. Thin-section petrography was used to identify lithofacies (17 types) and diagenetic features. XRD and SEM-EDS were employed to analyze mineralogical associations and pore-lining minerals. Geocellular modeling (using a variety of methods including 'Assigning Value') was performed at three levels: high-frequency sequence (HFS), bedset (BS), and bed level, to model lithofacies and petrophysical properties.

## Data and Materials

The study involves four outcrop locations in central Saudi Arabia (Ad Dawadimi quadrangle area). Approximately 1000 samples were collected for microfacies, micropaleontology, and porosity/permeability analysis. Data includes LIDAR scans, Gamma Ray logs on outcrops, and core plug measurements.

## Key Findings

- The Upper Khartam Member is dominated by prograding intertidal-subtidal flats with significant occurrences of intertidal channels and shoal ridges. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 15]])
- Lithofacies were identified as including oolitic grainstone, recrystallized limestone, and marlstone (e.g., LFT 1, LFT 3, LFT 7). ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 14]])
- XRF analysis of the marlstone lithofacies (LFT 7) provided elemental composition data to characterize mineralogy. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 10]])
- High-frequency sequences (HFS) and fourth-order sequences (FSQ) were identified as key units for stratigraphic analysis. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 8]])
- Reservoir quality is influenced by both primary depositional architecture and secondary diagenetic processes. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 13]])
- The Upper Khartam Member is characterized by regressive cycles dominated by regressive deposits followed by pulses of transgressions, with a boundary to the Lower Khartam marked by thin (20 cm) skeletal accumulation. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 18]])
- Seventeen lithofacies were defined, including coarse-grained oolitic grainstone, interlaminated quartz-bearing recrystallized limestone, and marlstone. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 30]])
- Seven depositional settings were identified: intertidal-subtidal flats, supratidal settings, intertidal channels and creeks, shoal ridges, reef complex, and outer ramp settings. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 31]])
- Approximately 20,000 beds were defined and traced laterally, ranging in thickness from 5 to 40 cm and extending between 5 and 300 m. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 31]])
- Six major diagenetic processes were identified: dissolution, pore lining and pore blocking calcite cementation, dolomitization, fracturing, and stylolitization. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 31]])
- Ten porosity types were differentiated, including intergranular, intragranular, shelter, dissolution-enlarged, moldic, vuggy, micro-porosity, and porosity related to dolomite-dedolomite, dolomite-leaching, and fracture development. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 31]])
- Clay minerals played a central role in lining the pore space and affecting reservoir quality; their presence caused a reduction in permeability values. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 170]])
- XRD data showed that mineralogical associations like SiO2 and SrSO4 have a proportional relationship with FZI, while Si, Sr, Fe, and SO show inverse relationships. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 189]])
- Intertidal sheets (e.g., B12B) possess relatively large horizontal ranges for porosity (~300 m) compared to intertidal channels and creeks (~150 m). ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 220]])
- Stylolites act as barriers for vertical fluid flow, while fractures act as conduits, both influencing the differential cementation and dissolution processes. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 32]])

## Limitations

Subsurface data alone cannot capture the complex heterogeneities at sub-seismic scales; therefore, outcrop analogues were required to bridge the gap between core/well logs and seismic data.

## Recommendations and Future Work

The results provide unique input for enhanced geological modeling and fluid flow simulation, specifically for the assessment of Khuff reservoirs in Eastern Arabian gas fields.
