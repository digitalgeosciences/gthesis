# TAXONOMY, BIOSTRATIGRAPHY AND MORPHOGROUPS OF BENTHIC FORAMINIFERA IN THE D1–D2 UNITS OF THE DHRUMA FORMATION, CENTRAL REGION OF SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the taxonomy, biostratigraphy, and morphogroups of benthic foraminifera in the D1 and D2 units of the Dhruma Formation in central Saudi Arabia. Using both thin-section analysis and acetic acid leaching (disaggregated samples), the study identifies a high diversity of foraminiferal taxa, suggesting a stable marine environment during the Bajocian.

---

## Research Problem

Existing knowledge of foraminifera in the Dhruma Formation is primarily based on thin-section analysis of carbonate beds, leaving the shale beds (D1 and D2 units) poorly understood taxonomically and environmentally.

## Objectives

- Taxonomic classification and documentation of the foraminiferal species from the lower Dhruma units utilizing disaggregation techniques.
- Review and modify the stratigraphic range for the identified species.
- Re-illustrate, describe and photograph the physically extracted key species from the lower Dhruma Formation.
- Interpret the paleoenvironmental conditions using morphogroup analysis.
- Perform a detailed taxonomic study of benthic foraminifera in the D1 and D2 units of the Dhruma Formation.
- Identify morphogroups and assess the diversity of microfauna.

## Methods

The study involved collecting 50 samples from three outcrops (D1A, D1B, D2W) near Khashm ad Dhibi. Disaggregation was performed via boiling with detergent for shale/muddy samples and acetic acid leaching (80% acetic acid, 20% water) for indurated carbonate samples. Samples were sieved through 63, 125, and 250 μm meshes. Microfossils were picked under a binocular stereomicroscope. Identification was based on the works of Loeblich & Tappan (1987) and Kaminski (2014). Paleoenvironmental interpretation was conducted via morphogroup analysis using the modified Reolid (2008) scheme.

## Data and Materials

Samples from three Bajocian outcrops representing D1 and D2 units; 32 genera and 63 species of foraminifera identified; various biocomponents including ostracods, holothurian sclerites, echinoid spines, and sponge spicules.

## Key Findings

- A total of 63 foraminiferal species were retrieved from the samples, including ten species reported for the first time (e.g., Haplophragmoides sp. 1, Gaudryinopsis sp. 1-5). ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 10]])
- The lower Dhruma has a high diversity of smaller benthic foraminifera, with several endemic species. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 10]])
- Identification to the species level was possible for 89.93% (7,383) of the 8,209 specimens collected. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 38]])
- The suborder Lagenina is the most abundant (39.33%), followed by Verneuilinina (10.38%) and Lituolina (9.18%). ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 38]])
- The family Polymorphinidae is the most abundant (33.95%). ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 39]])
- The four most abundant species are cf. Reophax dentaliniformis Brady, Nautiloculina oolithica Mohler, Haplophragmoides bartensteini Kalantari, and Verneuilinoides conoidalis Azbel. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 186]])
- Sample D1A shows a high diversity of morphogroups (8 types) with an almost equal ratio between calcareous (50.45%) and agglutinated (49.55%) taxa, suggesting well-oxygenated conditions. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 62]])
- Section D1B shows a higher proportion of agglutinated forms (57.06%) compared to D1A, with morphotypes D and C3 being dominant. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 62]])
- Section D2W is dominated by calcareous morphotype G (36.55%) and agglutinated morphotype C3 (24.14%), suggesting a shallower environment with higher oxygen levels. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 62]])
- The average diversity of the samples, as measured by Fisher's D, ranges from 0.342 to 6.025. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 48]])
- A total of 32 genera and 63 species of foraminiferal taxa were identified from the D1 and D2 units. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 185]])
- Ten new species to science were identified: Haplophragmoides sp. 1, Pseudobolivina? sp. 1, Trochammina sp. 1, Trochammina sp. 2, Gaudryinopsis sp. 1, Gaudryinopsis sp. 2, Gaudryinopsis sp. 3, Gaudryinopsis sp. 4, Gaudryinopsis sp. 5, and Lenticulina sp. 1. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 187]])
- 67.5% of species in the studied area are identified with 95% statistical confidence based on the rarefaction curve. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 188]])
- The assemblages are dominated by the family Polymorphinidae, Haplophragmoididae, and Verneuilinidae. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 190]])
- Presence of sponge spicules (monaxon and triaxon), echinoid spines, ostracods, holothurian sclerites, juvenile gastropods, and calcareous nannofossils suggests a normal marine environment. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 190]])

## Limitations

The study notes that results from disaggregated samples might be incomplete due to the high induration of some limestone rocks (D1B and D2W), which can hinder the liberation of microfossils during acid leaching.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Further work is needed to investigate the lateral and vertical extension of the reported taxa on the Dhruma Formation outcrops and in age-equivalent subsurface samples.
