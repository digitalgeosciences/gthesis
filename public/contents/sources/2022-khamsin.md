# TAXONOMY, BIOSTRATIGRAPHY AND MORPHOGROUPS OF BENTHIC FORAMINIFERA IN THE D1–D2 UNITS OF THE DHRUMA FORMATION, CENTRAL REGION OF SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the foraminiferal content of the lower Dhruma Formation (D1 and D2 units) in central Saudi Arabia using disaggregation techniques. It focuses on taxonomy, distribution, morphogroups, and paleoenvironmental reconstruction to address limited knowledge regarding shale-hosted benthic foraminifera in this region.

---

## Research Problem

Existing studies of the Dhruma Formation primarily focus on carbonate beds using thin-section analysis, leaving the foraminiferal assemblages in the lower Dhruma (D1 and D2 units) poorly understood. Specifically, there is a lack of detailed taxonomy and morphogroup data for these shale-rich sections.

## Objectives

- Taxonomic classification and documentation of the foraminiferal species from the lower Dhruma units utilizing disaggregation techniques.
- Review and modify the stratigraphic range for the identified species.
- Re-illustrate, describe and photograph the physically extracted key species from the lower Dhruma Formation.
- Interpret the paleoenvironmental conditions using morphogroup analysis.
- Identify and describe benthic foraminiferal species within the D1-D2 units.
- Perform morphogroup analysis to interpret paleoenvironmental parameters such as oxygen levels and nutrient availability.
- Confirm the age of sections using calcareous nannofossils.
- Identify and describe benthic foraminiferal taxa in the D1 and D2 units of the Dhruma Formation.
- Establish morphogroups for agglutinated and calcareous foraminifera to understand morphological diversity.
- Provide biostratigraphic data based on foraminiferal assemblages in the study area.
- Identify and describe benthic foraminifera species within the D1-D2 units of the Dhruma Formation.
- Establish biostratigraphic zones based on foraminiferal assemblages.
- Analyze morphogroups to understand evolutionary trends or environmental influences.
- Perform a detailed taxonomic study of benthic foraminifera from the D1 and D2 units of the Dhruma Formation.
- Identify morphogroups to assess paleoenvironmental conditions.
- Establish biostratigraphic markers for the Bajocian age in the Dhruma Formation.
- To provide a comprehensive taxonomy of benthic foraminifera in the D1-D2 units.
- To establish biostratigraphic zones based on benthic foraminiferal assemblages.
- To identify and characterize morphogroups of benthic foraminifera to interpret paleoenvironmental conditions.

## Methods

The study utilized disaggregation techniques to extract microfossils. For shale or mud-dominated samples, a method involving soaking in water, boiling with industrial soap as a surfactant, and sieving through 63-micron mesh was used. For indurated carbonate samples, an acid leaching process using 80% acetic acid (CH3COOH) for 10-15 hours followed by washing through 250, 125, and 63 µm sieves was employed. Samples were dried at 40-50°C. Microfossils were picked under a binocular stereomicroscope for semi-quantitative analysis. Well-preserved specimens were imaged using a JEOL scanning electron microscope.

## Data and Materials

A collection of 390 specimens of various foraminiferal species were analyzed from marly beds within the D1 unit of the Dhruma Formation. Specific counts are provided for several taxa, such as 390 for Verneuilinoides aff. minutus (implied by context), 19 for Verneuilinoides aff. minutus, 39 for Verneuilinoides sp. 2, 103 for Siphovalvulina colomi, and 157 for Redmondoides medius.

## Key Findings

- A total of 63 foraminiferal species were retrieved from the samples, including ten species reported for the first time. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 10]])
- The lower Dhruma has a high diversity of smaller benthic foraminifera, with several endemic species. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 10]])
- The paleoenvironment of the lower Dhruma is interpreted as marine with normal environmental conditions. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 10]])
- The D2 unit (Dhibi limestone) is approximately 6 m thick and consists of massive creamy limestone. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 23]])
- The D1 units consist of roughly 6.4 m of green and purple shale interbedded with carbonate beds. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 23]])
- Identification of species was possible for 7383 specimens (89.93%), indicating good preservation. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 38]])
- The most abundant suborder is Lagenina (39.33%), followed by Verneuilinina (10.38%). ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 38]])
- Ten new species were described for the first time: Haplophragmoides sp. 1, Pseudobolivina? sp. 1, Trochammina sp. 1, Trochammina sp. 2, Gaudryinopsis sp. 1, Gaudryinopsis sp. 2, Gaudryinopsis sp. 3, Gaudryinopsis sp. 4, Gaudryinopsis sp. 5, and Lenticulina sp. 1. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 39]])
- Section D1A is dominated by calcareous morphotype J3 (36.57%) and agglutinated morphotype D (20.82%), suggesting a well-oxygenated environment with high organic matter flux. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 62]])
- Section D1B shows an increase in agglutinated forms (57.06%) compared to D1A, with morphotypes D (35.68%) and C3 (21.33%) being dominant. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 62]])
- Section D2W is dominated by calcareous morphotype G (36.55%), which was absent in D1A and only 2.52% in D1B, suggesting a shallow environment with high oxygen levels. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 62]])
- The average diversity of the samples is high, as indicated by Shannon-Wiener index values (H') ranging from 0.54 to 0.94, suggesting no stressful environmental conditions. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 49]])
- Calcareous nannofossils in D1A indicate an early Bajocian age (NJ9 to NJ10a biozones). ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 40]])
- A total of 63 benthic foraminiferal taxa were identified in the study. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 68]])
- 57 of the identified taxa were successfully described to the species level. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 68]])

## Limitations

The study is solely based on disaggregated samples; results might be incomplete or not fully representative of all taxa due to the high induration of some limestone rocks (e.g., D1B and D2W sections).

## Recommendations and Future Work

Further work is needed to investigate the lateral and vertical extension of the reported taxa on the Dhruma Formation outcrops and in age-equivalent subsurface samples.
