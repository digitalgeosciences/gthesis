# INTEGRATED RESERVOIR CHARACTERIZATION OF THE VALANGINIAN CARBONATES IN CENTRAL SAUDI ARABIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the petrophysical properties of Valanginian carbonates in central Saudi Arabia by integrating sedimentological, geochemical (stable isotopes), and petrophysical analyses. It identifies five microfacies across a carbonate ramp setting and concludes that sedimentary environment and diagenetic processes like dissolution and fracturing significantly impact reservoir quality.

---

## Research Problem

There is limited knowledge on how the interplay between depositional and diagenetic processes impacts the petrophysical properties of Valanginian carbonates in Saudi Arabia, despite their global importance as prolific reservoirs.

## Objectives

- Understanding the spatial-temporal distribution of lithofacies and depositional environment in the early Cretaceous carbonates.
- To unravel the role of depositional and diagenetic processes in the reservoir quality.
- To perform 2D-3D reservoir modeling for the Early Cretaceous carbonates.
- Investigate the excellent exposures of the Valanginian carbonates in central Saudi Arabia
- Determine the impact of depositional environment on reservoir quality
- Evaluate the effect of diagenetic processes on petrophysical properties

## Methods

The study utilized field observations and petrographic thin sections to identify five microfacies. Laboratory analysis included: (1) Petrographic thin section analysis using a Zeiss Axioscan Z1 system; (2) SEM imaging with a JEOL JCM-7000 for mineral crystallization; (3) XRD (PanAnalytical) and XRF (Bruker M4 TORNADO) for mineralogy and elemental composition; (4) Stable carbon and oxygen isotope analysis using the Kiel IV carbonate device and Thermo Finnigan MAT-253 plus mass spectrometer; (5) Petrophysical measurements of porosity (Core Test System INC AP-608) and permeability (NER Autoscan helium permeameter); (6) 3D reservoir modeling using Petrel software to integrate petrophysical data across the study area.

## Data and Materials

139 total samples collected from seven sedimentary sections in the Jinadriyah area. Specifically: 26 thin sections for microfacies/diagenesis; 103 samples for XRF and XRD; 25 samples for stable isotope analysis; 2 samples for XRF mapping; 5 samples for SEM; and 36 core plugs for petrophysical measurements.

## Key Findings

- Five microfacies were identified, representing a carbonate ramp depositional setting from the inner ramp to outer ramp. ([[2024-allam|Allam, 2024, p. 11]])
- The δ18O and δ13C isotopic compositions showed differences of 2.94 and 0.64‰, with mean values of -3.58‰ and 0.45‰ respectively. ([[2024-allam|Allam, 2024, p. 11]])
- The comparison of the isotopic values to early Cretaceous rocks indicates that δ18O shifted towards more negative values, reflecting early-stage burial diagenesis. ([[2024-allam|Allam, 2024]])

- Valanginian carbonates were deposited in shallow marine carbonate ramps, mostly in inner ramp settings. ([[2024-allam|Allam, 2024, p. 72]])
- The carbonates are mainly formed of calcite minerals, with dolomitization playing a minor role. ([[2024-allam|Allam, 2024, p. 72]])
- Quartz presence indicates siliciclastic input and high-energy events; abrupt changes in quartz signify distinct energetic events. ([[2024-allam|Allam, 2024, p. 72]])
- Low Fe and Mn concentrations suggest the outcrop was not subjected to meteoric diagenesis for a long time before burial. ([[2024-allam|Allam, 2024, p. 72]])
- Stable carbon and oxygen analyses indicate that samples underwent early-stage burial diagenesis. ([[2024-allam|Allam, 2024, p. 70]])
- All whole rock samples exhibited Z values greater than 120, indicating a marine depositional environment. ([[2024-allam|Allam, 2024, p. 70]])
- The average porosity was determined to be 7.91%. ([[2024-allam|Allam, 2024, p. 72]])
- Permeability was measured at 0.296 mD. ([[2024-allam|Allam, 2024, p. 72]])
- The study identified a range of diagenetic transformations: micritization, cementation, compaction, dissolution, dolomitization, and fracturing. ([[2024-allam|Allam, 2024, p. 72]])
