# SEQUENCE STRATIGRAPHIC ANALYSIS AND POROSITY EVALUATION OF A FIELD USING 3D SEISMIC AND WELL LOGS: AN EXAMPLE FROM THE NIGER DELTA, NIGERIA

**Summary**: The thesis investigates the sequence stratigraphic evolution and reservoir potential of the Otu field in the Niger Delta using 3D seismic data and wireline logs from 13 wells. It identifies three main sequences bounded by four erosional unconformities (SB1-SB4) and three maximum flooding surfaces, concluding that these features provide critical information for identifying stratigraphic traps.

---

## Research Problem

The lack of established relationships between stratigraphy and structures in the Otu field makes evaluation difficult; specifically, there is a need for a detailed understanding of the tectono-stratigraphic evolution and reservoir characterization using integrated 3D seismic and well log data.

## Methods

The methodology involves integrating 3D seismic data with wireline logs (gamma ray, resistivity, density, neutron) from 13 wells. Lithostratigraphy is identified using GR log cut-offs (e.g., <35 API for sand). Facies and depositional environments are determined based on GR log patterns (blocky, coarsening upward, serrated, bell-shaped). Sequence stratigraphy involves identifying MFSs and SBs to define systems tracts and depositional sequences. Seismic interpretation includes structural mapping of faults and horizons. Seismic geomorphology is applied to the Agbada formation using attributes like spectral decomposition and coherency. Porosity is evaluated by cross-plotting density and neutron logs using Techlog software.

## Data and Materials

3D seismic amplitude data covering 421 sq. km with a bin spacing of 25x25m; 13 wireline logs (including GR, LLD, RHOCN, DT); checkshot and deviation survey data; well locations for Otu 4, 5, 6, 8, 11, 19, 21, 29, 32, 36, 38, 46, and 56.

## Key Findings

- The subsurface facies reveal three sequences bounded by four sequence boundaries (SB1 to SB4) interpreted as erosional unconformities. ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 17]])
- Three maximum flooding surfaces (MFS1, MFS2, and MFS3) were identified within the stratigraphic framework. ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 17]])
- LSTs in Otu field are fluvial channel sands formed at low sea level with high sediment supply relative to accommodation space. ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 18]])
- FSSTs consist of prograding deltaic wedge sediments formed during forced regression. ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 18]])
- TSTs are primarily marine shales formed during high sea levels with low sediment supply. ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 18]])
- HSTs consist of coarsening and shallowing upward intervals with deltaic fluvial sands near the top. ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 18]])
- Channel belts are oriented perpendicular to faults, while individual channels (incised valleys) are parallel to and less affected by faults. ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 18]])
- Chronostratigraphic correlation revealed that stratigraphic surfaces are not laterally continuous due to syn-depositional faulting. ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 18]])
- A meandering channel belt with point bar features was identified in the NE-SW direction on SB-1 using spectral decomposition at 10Hz and 40Hz. ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 89]])
- A subtle, sinuous channel oriented along the hanging wall of a fault was identified on SB-3. ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 87]])
- The study identified 14 reservoir sands in Otu field ranging from Sand A (shallowest) to Sand N (deepest). ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 46]])
- The Agbada formation is a paralic sequence of interbedded sands and shales, approximately 3,700 m thick, ranging from Eocene to Pleistocene in age. ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 26]])
- The study identified three sequences bounded by four sequence boundaries (SB1 to SB4) interpreted as erosional unconformities with three maximum flooding surfaces (MFS1, MFS2, and MFS3). ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 2]])
- The Akata formation is a marine-origin lithostratigraphic unit consisting of thick shale sequences approximately 7,000 m in thickness. ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 26]])
- The Benin formation consists of continental sandstones deposited in a delta plain as point bars or channel fills, with thicknesses ranging from 300 to 3,000 m. ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 26]])

## Limitations

The study notes that determining the depositional environments of sediments filling sequence surface topography is more difficult to constrain without core samples.

## Recommendations and Future Work

Additional biostratigraphic, core, and permeability data are needed to integrate results for a more reliable interpretation in the field.
