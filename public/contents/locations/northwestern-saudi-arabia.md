## Key Findings From This Location

- Several major phosphorite deposits were identified in this region since 1965. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 27]])

- Well locations are at the NE flank of the NW-SE oriented Al Jauf graben. ([[2012-ghazwani|Ghazwani, 2012, p. 20]])

- Not stated in source ([[2013-mark|Mark, 2013]])

- Identified distinct sedimentary characteristics between Al Ula and Tabuk areas, including different facies associations. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 232]])

- The Midyan Peninsula area shows complex syn-rift sequences including siliciclastics, carbonates, and evaporites. ([[2015-koeshidayatullah|Koeshidayatullah, 2015, p. 40]])

- The study area includes several well-exposed outcrops of the Nutaysh member. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 6]])

- The study area includes three specific sections (Section-1, 2, and 3) located at approximately 27.4°N, 38.0°E. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 4]])

- Outcrops are located approximately 90 km northwest of Tayma town. ([[2022-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2022, p. 73]])

- The study area is in an arid environment with seasonal raining periods from November to May. ([[2022-al-sadah|Al-Sadah, 2022, p. 77]])

- The study area includes several sub-basins (Sukk, Ifal, Maqna-1, Maqna-2). ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 39]])

- Harrat Uwayrid is situated between Tabuk to the north and Al Ula to the south. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 27]])

- Study conducted on outcrops in this region to characterize the Qalibah Formation. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 63]])

- The basin is a potentially fault-controlled basin at the Najd Fault system. ([[2024-malik|Malik, 2024, p. 14]])

- Located at 27°54'56''N, 35°47'47''E. ([[2024-sulistyohariyanto|Sulistyohariyanto, 2024, p. 22]])
