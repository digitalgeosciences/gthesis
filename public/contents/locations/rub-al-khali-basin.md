## Key Findings From This Location

- The Rub' Al Khali basin is an elongated basin that plunges gently northeast, with a length of 1300 km and width of 300 km. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 17]])

- The Qusaiba Shale is located within this basin and serves as a case study for unconventional resources. ([[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015, p. 20]])

- The Qusaiba Shale within this basin was analyzed for mineralogical and petrophysical properties. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 46]])

- The basin contains high temperature and high pressure (HTHP) zones with micro-darcy permeability. ([[2016-ejaz|Ejaz, 2016, p. 18]])

- The Sarah Formation is a key target for tight gas exploration in this basin. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 17]])

- The Wajid Group serves as a potential hydrocarbon reservoir within this basin. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 17]])

- The Rub Al-Khali is characterized by sand dunes separated by salt flats (Sabkhas) with elevations between 90m and 250m. ([[2017-almarzooq|Almarzooq, 2017, p. 14]])
