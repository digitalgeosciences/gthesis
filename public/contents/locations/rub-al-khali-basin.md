## Key Findings From This Location

- The study area is located in the northern part of the Rub’ Al-Khali Basin, southwest of the Ghawar field. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 26]])

- The basin contains a thick sequence (over 2900 ft) of the Thamama Group. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 15]])

- The Qusaiba Shale is a major target for unconventional resources in this basin. ([[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015, p. 32]])

- The Qusaiba Shale is a significant target for unconventional exploration in this region. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 46]])

- The area contains significant tight gas resources in Late Ordovician Sarah and Middle Ordovician Qasim formations. ([[2016-ejaz|Ejaz, 2016, p. 20]])

- The Wajid Group is a potential hydrocarbon reservoir within this basin. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 128]])

- The area is characterized by sand dunes separated by salt flats (Sabkhas) with elevations between 90m and 250m. ([[2017-almarzooq|Almarzooq, 2017, p. 14]])

- The area contains a complex system of ancient valley sediment deposits and significant reservoir potential. ([[2021-alqarni|Alqarni, 2021, p. 33]])

- The Qusaiba shale within this basin is a primary source rock for the Ghawar field. ([[2021-lawal|LAWAL, 2021, p. 116]])

- Rub'AlKhali desert sands show a very narrow QCI range (8.8 to 9.4), indicating intense homogenization. ([[2022-radwan|Radwan, 2022, p. 44]])

- The area exhibits high contrast between unconsolidated sands and compacted salt flats, creating significant static issues. ([[2022-aljishi|AlJishi, 2022, p. 19]])

- Referenced in this thesis. ([[2024-jahlan|Jahlan, 2024]])

- The basin is characterized by a complex history of tectonic and sedimentary evolution since the Precambrian. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 31]])
