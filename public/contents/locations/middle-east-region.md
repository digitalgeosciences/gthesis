## Key Findings From This Location

- Data included 145 sets of gas analysis and 2635 points from 372 tests in the Middle East. ([[1990-al-jarri|Al-Jarri, 1990, p. 43]])

- The study used 3,554 production test data points from ten fields in the Middle East. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 60]])

- Data from 206 points in this region covered a wide range of oil rates, water cuts, and gas-oil ratios. ([[2004-ayoub|AYOUB, 2004, p. 3]])

- Data from this region provided a robust dataset of 4031 points for model training. ([[2009-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2009, p. 30]])

- Data used for development covered a range of conditions including temperatures up to 309 °F and GORs up to 3,321 SCF/STB. ([[2010-al-dhamen|Al-Dhamen, 2010, p. 10]])

- Real models included a field with 130 wells (M3) and another with 97 wells (M4). ([[2010-mohammed|Mohammed, 2010, p. 28]])

- Data from two different fields in this region were used to ensure model diversity. ([[2011-al-dhufairi|AL-DHUFAIRI, 2011, p. 42]])

- Data from 1207 wells in the Middle East were used for initial analysis. ([[2011-al-shammari|Al-Shammari, 2011, p. 32]])

- Not stated in source ([[2013-elsayed|Elsayed, 2013]])

- Data from three vertical wells in this region were used to develop and validate ROP models. ([[2013-khamis|Khamis, 2013, p. 59]])

- Not stated in source ([[2013-al-mutairi|AL-MUTAIRI, 2013]])

- The study focused on a carbonate reservoir located in the Middle East. ([[2014-al-julaih|Al-Julaih, 2014, p. 21]])

- MEC samples showed distinct surface characteristics compared to pure calcite and dolomite. ([[2014-kasha|Kasha, 2014, p. 76]])

- Most of the Middle East carbonate reservoirs with high salinity and temperature are not the best candidate for conventional chemical EOR. ([[2014-azad|Azad, 2014, p. 42]])

- Data were collected from several wells in a large carbonate field in this region. ([[2015-alsahaf|Alsahaf, 2015, p. 13]])

- Samples were collected from a drilling rig during high pressure and high temperature operations. ([[2016-geri|GERI, 2016, p. 110]])

- The study used an actual drilling fluid formula currently used in one of the tight gas reservoirs in this region. ([[2017-jafarov|Jafarov, 2017, p. 62]])

- Six carbonate samples from Middle East reservoir were selected. ([[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019, p. 18]])

- Not stated in source ([[2020-mohammed|Mohammed, 2020]])

- Referenced in this thesis. ([[2022-algharbi|ALGHARBI, 2022]])

- Case studies included an offshore carbonate field and an organic-rich mudrock formation in the Middle East. ([[2024-alabbad|Alabbad, 2024, p. 102]])

- The Middle East has significant carbonate deposits, accounting for 70% and 90% of oil and gas reserves respectively. ([[2024-yusmananto|Yusmananto, 2024, p. 27]])

- Specifically targets Arabian carbonates with high anhydrite content. ([[2024-isah|Isah, 2024, p. 73]])

- Data points were extracted from several wells located in a Middle East oil field. ([[2024-alhamood|ALHAMOOD, 2024, p. 18]])

- Data were collected from various fields with a wide diversity of PVT properties. ([[1992-al-towailib-2|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 60]])

- Data included oil rates up to 19,618 BPD and gas-oil ratios up to 675.5 SCF/STB. ([[2004-ayoub-2|AYOUB, 2004, p. 3]])

- Data from this region were used to validate the impact of skin and friction across various reservoir dimensions. ([[1999-hasan|Hasan, 1999, p. 108]])

- 74 data sets from this region were used to develop and validate the new correlation. ([[2001-humoud|Humoud, 2001, p. 56]])
