## Key Findings From This Location

- Refraction profiles were made at six locations within the Dammam area to determine subsurface thickness and velocities. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 41]])

- The Dam Formation is located at the Dammam Dome and was deposited during a major Neogene transgression. ([[2006-al-enezi|Al-Enezi, 2006, p. 30]])

- Identified specific hot spots where metals like Cd, Cr, Cu, and Zn exceeded safety limits. ([[2014-adedapo|Adedapo, 2014, p. 12]])

- The average concentration of PM10 in Dammam was 126.00 µg/m3. ([[2016-lawal-2|Lawal, 2016, p. 14]])

- The study identified 28 sensitive receptors within this area for monitoring. ([[2022-mustapha|Mustapha, 2022, p. 14]])

- The Dammam aquifer is one of the essential aquifers in the eastern provinces, with high transmissivity (1070-2497 m2/day). ([[2022-pambudi|Pambudi, 2022, p. 58]])

- The Ithra field data showed severe noise levels in many shots, requiring advanced processing techniques like SVI. ([[2022-awais|Awais, 2022, p. 59]])

- The study area contains a partially saturated zone up to 7.6 m deep within the dune. ([[2023-alsahwan|AlSahwan, 2023, p. 38]])

- Data from this location were found unsuitable for study due to lack of reflection events. ([[2024-jahlan|Jahlan, 2024, p. 30]])

- The study site was approximately 30 km east of Dhahran city. ([[2024-tarchi|Tarchi, 2024, p. 40]])

- The study focuses on a specific dune near Dammam Airport. ([[2025-oyekan|Oyekan, 2025, p. 30]])
