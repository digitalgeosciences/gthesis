## Key Findings From This Location

- The Gulf is characterized by a high degree of variability in sedimentary environments due to limited oceanic influence and extreme climate. ([[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988, p. 25]])

- Foraminiferal diversity in the Gulf tends to increase with depth but decrease with salinity. ([[2006-al-enezi|Al-Enezi, 2006, p. 13]])

- The gulf is rich with good fishing grounds and extensive coral reefs but faces pressure from industrialization. ([[2012-al-rowaished|Al-Rowaished, 2012, p. 27]])

- Samples were collected from Tarut, Mussalamia, and Abu Ali. ([[2012-amao|Amao, 2012, p. 22]])

- OPPs were not detected in sea water samples across the study area. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 14]])

- The coastal area contains a diverse and rich community of oil-degrading strains. ([[2015-oyehan|Oyehan, 2015, p. 18]])

- Significant variation in organotin levels was observed across different locations along the coast. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 11]])

- Significant nutrient enrichment was observed at all three outfall locations compared to the control site. ([[2015-yusuf|Yusuf, 2015, p. 14]])

- Not stated in source ([[2015-malik|Malik, 2015]])

- The study area included Eastern Bahrain (Murray's Pool) and the Saudi coastline. ([[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015, p. 80]])

- Identified critical ecosystems like coral reefs, seagrass beds, salt marshes, and mangroves in the study area. ([[2016-sudibyo|Sudibyo, 2016, p. 13]])

- Identified a wide range of benthic foraminifera from intertidal to deep offshore environments. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 143]])

- The Arabian Gulf is a primary source of hypersaline water containing organic pollutants like pyrene. ([[2017-budiyanto|Budiyanto, 2017, p. 6]])

- The study focused on the effects of Arabian Light Crude Oil on organisms within the Arabian Gulf ecosystem. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 67]])

- The study area includes several sites like Tarut Bay and Abu Ali Island. ([[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019, p. 20]])
