## Key Findings From This Location

- conducted research at Arabian Gulf ([[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988]])

- Foraminiferal diversity in the Gulf increases with depth and decreases with salinity. ([[2006-al-enezi|Al-Enezi, 2006, p. 14]])

- Ten (10) species of endolithic cyanobacteria were identified in the Arabian Gulf. ([[2012-amao|Amao, 2012, p. 31]])

- Seawater from this region contains specific ions (Ca2+, Mg2+, SO4 2-) that can act as wettibility modifiers. ([[2012-kassim|KASSIM, 2012, p. 18]])

- The study established baseline data for OCPs and OPPs across multiple sites in the gulf. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 14]])

- The study area included Jubail, Qatif, Khobar and Half Moon Bay. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 23]])

- The Gulf is a poorly flushed system with an estimated water residence time of over 3 years along the Arabian coast. ([[2015-yusuf|Yusuf, 2015, p. 26]])

- The region is characterized by high salinity (up to 60-70% in some areas) and unique marine habitats. ([[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015, p. 32]])

- The study area covers a coastline of approximately 452.209 km from Khafji to Dammam. ([[2016-sudibyo|Sudibyo, 2016, p. 38]])

- The Gulf is a naturally stressed environment due to extremes of salinity and temperature. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 26]])

- Oil spills are common occurrences in the Arabian Gulf due to industrial activities. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 49]])

- The area is one of the highest anthropogenically impacted zones in the world. ([[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019, p. 20]])

- The Arabian Gulf is a naturally stressed marine environment with high salinity and temperature. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 17]])

- The Gulf is a mixed system of carbonate and siliciclastic realms. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 23]])

- The Arabian Gulf presents unique challenges due to its high evaporation rates and extreme salinity levels. ([[2024-alsadah|Alsadah, 2024, p. 17]])
