## Key Findings Across Sources

- studied the Wajid Sandstone ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014]])

- The formation is divided into five members: Dibsiyah, Sanamah, Qalibah, Khusayyayn, and Juwayl. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 48]])

- The narrow range of QCI in Rub'AlKhali suggests it is mainly sourced from the Wajid Sandstone to the west. ([[2022-radwan|Radwan, 2022, p. 44]])

- Braided fluvial fan sandstones (FA2) showed average porosity of 23.2% and permeability of 1884 mD. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 29]])

- Zone C is located within several units including the wajid-sandstone, showing high fault and fracture counts. ([[2023-arrofi|Arrofi, 2023, p. 83]])

- The Wajid Sandstone is represented by an approximately 300m thick sequence of medium to coarse grained, poorly to well-sorted, mineralogically mature, pebbly sandstone and conglomerate. ([[1999-babalola|Babalola, 1999, p. 18]])
