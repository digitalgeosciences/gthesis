## Key Findings Across Sources

- studied the Khobar Aquifer ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000]])

- Potential contamination of the Khobar aquifer is possible due to poor well casing and fissures in the upper confining layer. ([[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011, p. 150]])

- The Khobar aquifer has a thickness of 80m and a transmissivity ranging from 0.9e-2 to 0.29 m2/s. ([[2024-ahmad|Ahmad, 2024, p. 20]])

- The thickness varies from 35 to 125 meters, with permeability influenced by fracturing and karstification. ([[1995-hasan|Hasan, 1995, p. 55]])

- The Khobar aquifer has an average transmissivity of 2.9 x 10^3 m^2/s and a storativity of 2.1 x 10^-2. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 43]])
