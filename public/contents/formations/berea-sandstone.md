## Key Findings Across Sources

- EDTA at pH 12.2 showed the best results on Berea sandstone cores. ([[2013-elsayed|Elsayed, 2013, p. 14]])

- Chelating agent (DTPA-K5) increased oil recovery for Berea sandstone rocks after being flooded with seawater. ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 103]])

- The core exhibited a porosity of 19.7% and permeability of approximately 18-19 mD. ([[2016-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2016, p. 64]])

- The Berea sample showed an average permeability of 151.2 mD and a regained permeability of 85.2%. ([[2017-mohammed-3|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 126]])

- The developed simulators matched the recorded experimental estimates from Arihara (198). ([[2016-david|DAVID, 2016, p. 111]])

- Permeability of Berea was between 150-250 mD. ([[2024-suleiman-2|Suleiman, 2024, p. 47]])

- Injection curves were independent of sample size for Berea samples. ([[1991-ali|Ali, 1991, p. 77]])
