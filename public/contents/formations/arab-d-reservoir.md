## Key Findings Across Sources

- The reservoir is approximately 300 feet thick and shows a significant increase in dolomite content toward the base. ([[2002-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2002, p. 27]])

- The core samples were used to study the effect of injected water salinity on oil recovery in the Arab-D context. ([[1990-al-mumen|Al-Mumen, 1990, p. 25]])

- The gel was effective at plugging pores even when using high-salinity Arab-D brine. ([[1997-al-nashmi|Al-Nashmi, 1997, p. 129]])

- The Arab-D is a highly heterogeneous reservoir with an average thickness of 40m (136 ft). ([[1996-al-salem|Al-Salem, 1996, p. 38]])

- The Arab-D reservoir is composed of limestone, dolomite and anhydrite. ([[2003-al-shridi|Al-Shridi, 2003, p. 18]])

- The crude from the Arab-D field was used for testing at three pressure levels: 500, 250, and 50 psi. ([[1997-al-taweel|Al-Taweel, 1997, p. 45]])

- The Arab-D formation is a depleted zone that experienced water influx from the overpressured Arab-C. ([[2011-al-dossary|Al-Dossary, 2011, p. 16]])

- Well Y-2 in this formation showed a significant increase in productivity index after acid treatment. ([[2011-alburaikan|AlBuraikan, 2011, p. 76]])

- The rock is primarily composed of dolomitized limestone and shows complex interactions with Arabian Gulf seawater. ([[2012-kassim|KASSIM, 2012, p. 18]])

- The study focused on both clean limestone and dolomitic sections within the Arab-D formation. ([[2013-al-kharraa|Al-Kharraa, 2013, p. 68]])

- The reservoir contains 44 cycles with thicknesses ranging from 15.2 cm to 255 cm. ([[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013, p. 18]])

- The Arab-D member is divided into two units based on lithofacies: upper unit (tidal flat) with small intensity, lower unit (skeletal bank/tidal flat) with higher intensity. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 53]])

- Dead crude oil (API = 30.77) from Uthmaniyah Arab-D crude oil was used in this study. ([[2015-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2015, p. 15]])

- Formation brine was characterized with an approximate TDS of 206,911 ppm. ([[2016-adam|ADAM, 2016, p. 50]])

- The outcrop sample contained approximately 95% calcite and 4% quartz. ([[2017-sadeed|SADEED, 2017, p. 56]])

- The study analyzed the impact of well arrangement on pore pressure build-up in the Arab-D reservoir. ([[2017-khan|Khan, 2017, p. 39]])

- The correlation was tested on Arab-D data and showed reasonable accuracy. ([[2017-hasan|Hasan, 2017, p. 149]])

- The upper Jubaila member (J2) is part of the Arab-D reservoir and is one of the most important reservoirs in the Middle East. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 18]])

- The Arab-D samples were a composite of dolomite and limestone with varying percentages. ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023, p. 31]])

- The advisory system facilitated the fastest drilling operation in the Arab Reservoir of the MRJN Field (77.79 fph). ([[2023-murif|Murif, 2023, p. 218]])

- Contains up to 24% anhydrite, highlighting significant mineralogical heterogeneity. ([[2024-isah|Isah, 2024, p. 71]])

- The Arab-D Limestone Member is the main reservoir in the Kingdom and contains productive oil reserves in nine widely separated smaller fields. ([[1992-abdulghani|Abdulghani, 1992, p. 21]])

- The study focused on a limestone reservoir with high temperature (90°C) and high salinity (57,000 ppm). ([[1994-eme|Eme, 1994, p. 24]])
