## Key Findings Across Sources

- Not stated in source ([[1992-el-sahn|El-Sahn, 1992]])

- The UER aquifer experienced a 480% increase in extraction between 1967 and 2010, leading to an average head drop of 8.5m. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 15]])

- The UER formation consists of fossiliferous fine-grained recrystallized limestones with intercalated calcarenites and dolomites. ([[2018-mohamed|Mohamed, 2018, p. 37]])

- The UER formation can reach a subsurface thickness of up to 500 m. ([[2018-hanif|Hanif, 2018, p. 26]])

- The Umm Er Radhuma Formation is approximately 243m thick and contains a very fine to medium-grained yellowish gray dolomite. ([[2024-ahmed|Ahmed, 2024, p. 33]])

- The isochlore map shows very high concentrations in excess of four times the concentration of seawater towards the coast. ([[1995-hasan|Hasan, 1995, p. 103]])
