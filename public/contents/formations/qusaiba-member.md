## Key Findings Across Sources

- The Hanadir member is a potential source rock with average thickness of 300 ft. ([[2012-ghazwani|Ghazwani, 2012, p. 14]])

- The Qusaiba Shale was categorized into three lithofacies based on mineralogy and texture. ([[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015, p. 21]])

- The Qusaiba hot-shale is up to 75m thick with a TOC of up to 14%. ([[2015-own|Own, 2015, p. 32]])

- The Qusaiba member contains five distinct lithofacies ranging from fissile shale to various types of sandstone. ([[2017-mahgoub|Mahgoub, 2017, p. 19]])

- The shale rocks showed high affinity toward CO2, with uptake increasing as CO2 fraction increased. ([[2016-elnaeem|Elnaeem, 2016, p. 75]])

- The formation contains five cartographic units with varying lithologies of clayey siltstone and sandstone. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 20]])

- The basal part is a world-class source rock (Qusaiba Hot Shale). ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 17]])

- The Qusaiba is characterized by high gamma-ray signatures and serves as a major stratigraphic marker. ([[2020-gbiyanju|Gbiyanju, 2020, p. 43]])

- The Qusaiba shale is considered a 'hot shale' and a major producer for Middle East reservoirs. ([[2021-lawal|LAWAL, 2021, p. 19]])

- The QSA member was found to contain 77% kaolinite and 22.6% quartz, making it suitable for producing high-quality metakaolin. ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 143]])

- Mineralogy includes 39% Kaolinite, 14% Illite, and 7% Chlorite. ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 52]])

- Lithofacies were categorized into proximal, medial, and distal shelf deposits; interparticle pores were found in all lithofacies. ([[2022-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2022, p. 37]])

- The UER aquifer thickness varies from <40m to 500m across the study area. ([[2022-pambudi|Pambudi, 2022, p. 54]])

- The Qusaiba Member is primarily composed of laminated shale and mudstone with high gamma ray values in 'hot' zones. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 25]])
