## Key Findings Across Sources

- The study focused on the interval from Base Khuff to Pre Unayzah Unconformity. ([[1997-al-marhoun|Al-Marhoun, 1997, p. 10]])

- The Upper Khartam Member of the Khuff Formation contains significant moldic, vuggy, and intercrystalline porosity. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 71]])

- The Upper Khartam Member of the Khuff Formation is a key target for gas production, showing significant vertical and lateral heterogeneities. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 18]])

- Porosity ranges from 0.6% to 29.1%. ([[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016, p. 19]])

- The formation contains multiple members with varying lithofacies and diagenetic impacts on porosity/permeability. ([[2016-makkawi|Makkawi, 2016, p. 8]])

- The formation is divided into several members including Huqayl, Midhnab, and Khartam. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 12]])

- Included in the core study of unconventional rocks in KSA. ([[2020-gbiyanju|Gbiyanju, 2020, p. 26]])

- Samples 1118-15 and 1118-17 showed QCI values of 9.53 and 9.46 respectively. ([[2022-radwan|Radwan, 2022, p. 42]])

- Cementation of oomolds by calcite significantly reduced pore aspect ratio, impacting velocity results. ([[2022-salih|Salih, 2022, p. 60]])

- The Upper Khartam Member hosts very large gas reserves across the Arabian Gulf and Iran. ([[2022-babker|Babker, 2022, p. 14]])

- The formation contains intercrystalline microporosity (3-18%) and is a tight, unconventional reservoir. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 14]])

- The UKM contains four distinct facies associations (FA1-FA4) with varying porosity and permeability. ([[2024-yusmananto|Yusmananto, 2024, p. 16]])

- The Gudair Formation is characterized by its detrital, locally glauconitic nature. ([[1989-abdou|Abdou, 1989, p. 38]])
