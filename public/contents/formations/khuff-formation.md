## Key Findings Across Sources

- The Base Khuff served as the lower boundary of the target reservoir zone. ([[1997-al-marhoun|Al-Marhoun, 1997, p. 37]])

- The Khartam Member is the uppermost member of the Khuff Formation and contains well-sorted oolitic grainstone, cross-bedded oolitic skeletal grainstone, and graded mudstone to packstone facies. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 23]])

- The Khuff reservoirs exhibit vertical and lateral heterogeneities which cannot be captured fully within the interwell spacing from subsurface data. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 17]])

- Significant impact of CO2 storage was noted on Khuff limestone. ([[2014-al-ameri|AL-AMERI, 2014, p. 25]])

- The formation is divided into three units (A, B, and C) with distinct mechanical behaviors; Khuff C shows higher average Young's modulus than Khuff B. ([[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016, p. 77]])

- The Upper Khartam Member is equivalent to Khuff-A in the subsurface of eastern Saudi Arabia. ([[2016-makkawi|Makkawi, 2016, p. 25]])

- The Khuff formation is hosting important carbonate reservoirs in the Eastern Saudi Arabian fields; these are estimated to contain about 15 to 20% of the world's gas reserve. ([[2017-adam-2|Adam, 2017, p. 36]])

- Referenced in this thesis. ([[2017-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2017]])

- The Khuff formation is characterized by high heterogeneity and significant dolomite content (average 46.7%). ([[2017-hasan|Hasan, 2017, p. 29]])

- The formation is characterized as a tight gas sandstone with low permeability and high rock strength. ([[2018-aldossary|AlDossary, 2018, p. 79]])

- The simulation was run for five years on a 1km x 1km area of the Khuff reservoir. ([[2020-mohammed|Mohammed, 2020, p. 77]])

- The Khuff contains tight carbonates, shale, and anhydrite, serving as a cap rock for the pre-Khuff system. ([[2020-gbiyanju|Gbiyanju, 2020, p. 46]])

- Showed that dolomitization and cementation are key drivers of variance in the Khuff's velocity-porosity relationship. ([[2022-salih|Salih, 2022, p. 43]])

- The Khuff Formation contains up to 30% of the world's gas reserves. ([[2022-babker|Babker, 2022, p. 32]])

- High H2S content in the Khuff formation leads to significant iron sulfide scale issues. ([[2023-badhafere|Badhafere, 2023, p. 52]])

- The formation contains 5-10% evaporites, with individual bed thickness up to several meters. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 32]])
