## Key Findings Across Sources

- The Dhruma Formation is divided into three units: lower Dhruma, middle Dhruma, and upper Dhruma. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 27]])

- The Dhruma Formation contains 10 lithofacies ranging from deep water muds to shallow water oolitic grainstones. ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 53]])

- The Dhruma Formation contains three reservoirs: Faridah, Sharar, and Lower Fadhili. ([[2017-yousif|Yousif, 2017, p. 45]])

- The formation is primarily composed of a thick carbonate sequence with a thin bedded clastic sequence at the base. ([[2018-ismanto|Ismanto, 2018, p. 16]])

- The study identified 15 distinct lithofacies within the D5 and D6 units of the Dhruma Formation. ([[2020-al-zoukani|Al-Zoukani, 2020, p. 18]])

- XRD, XRF, and thin section results confirmed that the blocks are from one of the five Jurassic formations (Dhruma, Tuwaiq Mountain, Hanifa, Jubaila, or Arab). ([[2020-aly|Aly, 2020, p. 153]])

- The Dhurma Formation had a high Lorenz coefficient of 0.628. ([[2021-al-nuwais|Al-Nuwais, 2021, p. 124]])

- The D1 unit is of early Bajocian age, while the D2 unit is assigned an early late Bajocian age. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 19]])

- The Minjur & Dhruma aquifer is mainly made up of Upper Jurassic-age rock of limestone and sandstone. ([[2022-pambudi|Pambudi, 2022, p. 45]])

- The Dhruma Formation is predominantly carbonate in the subsurface, but its lithology varies from carbonate to claystone in exposed areas. ([[2024-jalloh|Jalloh, 2024, p. 37]])
