## Key Findings Across Sources

- Characterized by low porosity and permeability, with specific lithofacies like subarkoze sandstone showing higher quality due to fractures and feldspar dissolution. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 20]])

- The formation contains quartz arenites with high porosity and permeability ranging from 5 to 53 mD. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 23]])

- The Sarah Formation is considered as an important reservoir target, especially as tight gas reservoir. ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 15]])

- The formation contains five distinct lithofacies with varying petrophysical and mechanical characteristics. ([[2016-babker|Babker, 2016, p. 13]])

- The formation shows high stiffness and brittle nature with average Young's modulus values indicating suitability for hydraulic fracturing. ([[2016-ejaz|Ejaz, 2016, p. 14]])

- The formation is characterized by high heterogeneity, with a Dykstra-Parsons coefficient between 0.227 and 0.293. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 19]])

- The formation contains a variety of facies including massive, laminated, and deformed sandstones. ([[2021-alqarni|Alqarni, 2021, p. 31]])
