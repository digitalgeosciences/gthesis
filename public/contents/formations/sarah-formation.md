## Key Findings Across Sources

- The Sarah formation shows lower pressure close to hydrostatic levels compared to the Qalibah. ([[2013-mark|Mark, 2013, p. 96]])

- The formation contains various facies including trough cross-bedded sandstone, horizontally stratified sandstone, and ferruginous sandstone. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 12]])

- The formation contains quartz arenites with clay content < 5% and authigenic kaolinite coatings that preserved primary porosity. ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 16]])

- The average porosity for the sandstone facies is 15 percent, while the siltstone ranges around 7 percent. ([[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016, p. 19]])

- The formation contains five distinct lithofacies and three types of fracture modes (opening, sliding, and shear). ([[2016-babker|Babker, 2016, p. 13]])

- The formation shows high stiffness and brittle nature with a maximum stress gradient of 1.18 psi/ft. ([[2016-ejaz|Ejaz, 2016, p. 14]])

- The formation contains diverse lithofacies and is characterized by glaciogenic features. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 17]])

- Not stated in source ([[2019-al-dawood|Al-Dawood, 2019]])

- The formation contains quartz-arenite to subarkose type sandstones with significant mineralogical and diagenetic controls on quality. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 51]])

- The formation contains diverse lithofacies such as MS (33.33%) and LA (36.66%) with significant thickness variations due to paleotopography. ([[2021-alqarni|Alqarni, 2021, p. 45]])

- Sample 1118-04 and 1118-05 showed QCI values of 9.03 and 9.48 respectively. ([[2022-radwan|Radwan, 2022, p. 43]])

- The Qusaiba Member uncomfortably overlies the Sarah Formation. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 16]])
