# Digital Outcrop Modeling

**Summary**: A method to create a 3D digital representation of an outcrop to analyze geological features and quantify dimensions.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2014-sami|Sami, 2014]] used digital-outcrop-modeling to characterize the Upper Khartam Member's geometry and bed thickness.

- [[2023-butt|Butt, 2023]] used digital_outcrop_modeling to create virtual 3D models from 11,500 photographs to evaluate lithofacies distribution.

- [[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024]] used digital-outcrop-modeling to create 3D models of the Qalibah Formation's lithofacies and petrophysical properties.

- [[2024-ahmed|Ahmed, 2024]] used digital outcrop modeling to characterize fracture dimensions and distribution in the Hofuf Formation.

## Key Results and Findings

- The DOM allowed for the calculation of average bed thicknesses, which were compared between field measurements and ArcMap data. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 131]])

- The model allowed for the identification of eleven lithofacies (F1-F11) and four facies associations across four subbasins. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 64]])

- The integration of DOM with sedimentology and petrophysics provided higher resolution models for characterizing complex geometries compared to conventional methods. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 14]])

- Identified a distinction between fissures (aperture > 1 cm) and joints (aperture < 1 cm) through DOM analysis. ([[2024-ahmed|Ahmed, 2024, p. 102]])
