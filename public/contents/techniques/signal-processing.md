## Key Results and Findings

- Least-squares fitting was used to estimate the diffracted signal s(t) from the raw data x(t). ([[2009-al-lehyani|Al-Lehyani, 2009, p. 24]])

- Auto pickers with different time windows (150ms vs 200ms) created distinct artifacts in the velocity volume. ([[2010-al-moqbel|Al-Moqbel, 2010, p. 53]])

- Post-stack conditioning ensured the external pilot was free from artificial breaks before final residual statics calculation. ([[2011-toma|Toma, 2011, p. 54]])

- Signal processing is critical for overcoming challenges like noise and limited resolution in raw data. ([[2015-mohammed|Mohammed, 2015, p. 2]])

- Signal enhancement was necessary to distinguish weak microseismic signals from background noise in complex environments. ([[2015-own|Own, 2015, p. 68]])

- The results of the magic cube operator with the larger spatial window produced better definition of the channel system than the Sobel filter. ([[2015-adetokunbo|Adetokunbo, 2015, p. 58]])

- Summation of aligned correlograms reduced noise to an average of 0.05. ([[2016-edigbue|Edigbue, 2016, p. 60]])

- Trace energy was used as a metric to quantify degradation, where real data showed only 1.29% degradation compared to higher predicted values. ([[2018-putra|Putra, 2018, p. 70]])

- Band-pass filtering is an essential method applied to remove coherent noise by avoiding any noise stretching after the squaring technique. ([[2020-alzahrani|Alzahrani, 2020, p. 33]])

- The Hilbert transform allows the calculation of instantaneous amplitude (envelope) from a real signal. ([[2021-almashhor|AlMashhor, 2021, p. 38]])

- Band-pass filtering between 5-25 Hz was found most suitable in removing considerable ambient noise without much effect on the signal of interest. ([[2021-shaheen|Shaheen, 2021, p. 33]])

- Exponential Moving Average (EMA) with a damping factor of 0.9 was used to reveal underlying trends in raw sensor data. ([[2021-alsaihati|Alsaihati, 2021, p. 56]])

- Different filtering methods were evaluated for their ability to remove noise from sensor streams. ([[2022-algharbi|ALGHARBI, 2022, p. 70]])

- Spiking deconvolution was used to produce a better signature of the event for easier tracking across different elevations. ([[2022-aljishi|AlJishi, 2022, p. 88]])

- The study also found that the use of stochastic methods to model DCR and SR data can provide a useful tool for modeling the uncertainties in the data. ([[2023-edigbue|Edigbue, 2023, p. 17]])

- Signal processing steps were integrated into a four-step automated workflow (HEEH) to eliminate human intervention. ([[2023-bargees|Bargees, 2023, p. 31]])

- SVI successfully enhanced the SNR, allowing for clearer first arrival picking. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 41]])

- Not stated in source. ([[2023-muhammad|Muhammad, 2023]])

- Denoising significantly improved results compared to models that attempted to handle noise directly. ([[2023-almarzooq|Almarzooq, 2023, p. 120]])

- First arrival picks were used to calculate P-wave propagation velocities for each geophone. ([[2024-makki|Makki, 2024, p. 61]])

- F-K filtering removed linear noise below 1500 m/s while preserving primary reflections (Page 57). ([[2024-jahlan|Jahlan, 2024, p. 57]])

- Not stated in source ([[2023-saadeldin|SAADELDIN, 2023]])

- Residual times were limited to +/- 2 s to avoid cycle skipping during the stacking process. ([[2025-kesumastuti|Kesumastuti, 2025, p. 52]])

- Kalman filters were used in the control system to estimate state variables like stiffness and viscosity. ([[2002-al-ali|Al-Ali, 2002, p. 32]])
