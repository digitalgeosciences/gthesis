# Ift Measurement

**Summary**: Measurement of the force existing between two immiscible liquids.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2014-azad|Azad, 2014]] used ift-measurement to determine the reduction in interfacial tension by various surfactants.

- [[2015-he|He, 2015]] used IFT measurements to evaluate the effectiveness of HEDTA as a demulsifier in breaking water-in-oil emulsions.

- [[2016-alhamad|AlHamad, 2016]] performed IFT measurements to quantify the reduction in surface tension by PVP and nanoparticles.

- [[2014-alam|ALAM, 2014]] performed IFT measurements using the IFT700 to evaluate the effect of citric acid on oil-water interaction.

## Key Results and Findings

- The IFT tensiometer was used to characterize the term in the denominator of the capillary number equation. ([[2014-azad|Azad, 2014, p. 90]])

- HEDTA showed significant reduction in IFT when concentration increased from 46.67 g/L to 90.87 g/L due to carboxyl group concentration. ([[2015-he|He, 2015, p. 115]])

- The combination of 1 wt% PVP and 0.05 wt% nanoparticles reduced IFT by 19% compared to pure seawater. ([[2016-alhamad|AlHamad, 2016, p. 74]])

- IFT values for 10% spent citric acid were as low as 4.75 mN/m at 100°C and atmospheric pressure. ([[2014-alam|ALAM, 2014, p. 118]])
