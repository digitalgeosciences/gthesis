# Gamma-Ray Spectrometry

**Summary**: Measurement of natural radioactivity to determine concentrations of U, Th, K, and REE.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[1996-matter|Matter, 1996]] employed natural gamma-ray spectroscopy to quantify Uranium, Vanadium, Thorium, Potassium, and Rare Earth Elements.

- [[2014-sami|Sami, 2014]] used gamma-ray-spectrometry via the Gamma Surveyor II tool to measure K, U, and Th concentrations.

- [[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015]] used Spectral Gamma Ray (SGR) logs to characterize the outcrop's geochemistry.

- [[2016-babker|Babker, 2016]] used gamma-ray spectrometry to characterize lithofacies across four outcrop sections.

- [[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017]] applied gamma-ray spectrometry to quantify elemental content for lithofacies characterization.

## Key Results and Findings

- Used for the geochemical characterization of the studied phosphorite deposits. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 64]])

- The system provides counts per second for uranium, thorium, and potassium to calculate API values. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 61]])

- Used to characterize the outcrop using SGR logs and geochemical signature. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 20]])

- Spectral gamma ray readings were integrated with Schmidt hammer measurements to define the response of lithologic units to geomechanical tests. ([[2016-babker|Babker, 2016, p. 101]])

- Geochemical analysis revealed a strong association between the elemental content (U, K, and Th) and the lithofacies distribution. ([[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017, p. 121]])
