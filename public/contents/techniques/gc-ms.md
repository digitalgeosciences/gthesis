# Gas Chromatography–Mass Spectrometry

**Summary**: A technique used to separate and identify compounds in a mixture for quantification of organic pollutants.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011]] used GC/MS to quantify trihalomethanes (THMs) in blended water samples.

- [[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011]] used GC-MS to analyze aromatic hydrocarbons in surface water samples.

- [[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013]] used gc-ms to analyze MTBE and its by-products (acetone, TBA, TBF).

- [[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013]] used GC-MS to determine the concentration of OCPs and OPPs in water, sediment, and biota samples.

- [[2014-abdallah|Abdallah, 2014]] used GC/MS to determine the peak areas of EDC and hydrocarbons in 82 samples.

- [[2014-adebayo|Adebayo, 2014]] used GC-MS to analyze the concentration of MTBE in aqueous solutions.

- [[2015-oyehan|Oyehan, 2015]] used GC-MS to analyze the degradation of phenanthrene, pyrene, and diesel components.

- [[2015-danmaliki|Danmaliki, 2015]] used GC-SCD to monitor the concentration of sulfur compounds during adsorption experiments.

- [[2015-hassan|Hassan, 2015]] used GC-MS for monitoring reaction products.

- [[2015-bello|Bello, 2015]] used GC/MS for the identification and quantification of naphthalene in plant materials.

- [[2015-akere|Akere, 2015]] used GC-MS to determine the concentration of six organotin species in sediment and biota samples.

- [[2015-malik|Malik, 2015]] used GC-MS for detection and analysis of residual PAHs.

- [[2016-sameh|Sameh, 2016]] used GC-MS to quantify toluene and its by-products in deionized water and groundwater samples.

- [[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016]] used GC-FID to determine the fatty acid profiles of isolated microalgae strains.

- [[2017-budiyanto|Budiyanto, 2017]] used GC-MS to quantify pyrene degradation during incubation periods.

- [[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018]] used GC-MS to determine the concentration of PAHs in Arabian Light crude oil, OBM, and dispersants.

- [[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018]] used GC-MS to determine the concentration of MTBE in aqueous solutions.

- [[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019]] used GC-MS to analyze aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) in sediment and plant tissues.

- [[2019-alhaiqi|Alhaiqi, 2019]] used GC/MS to quantify the concentration of Phenanthrene, Fluoranthene, and Pyrene in soil and plant tissues.

- [[2020-usman|Usman, 2020]] applied GC-MS analysis on a subset of bitumen extracts to determine kerogen type and origin.

- [[2024-almutawa|AlMutawa, 2024]] used GC-FID to measure soil TPHs and analyze the chemical composition of hydrocarbon samples.

- [[2025-almousa|Almousa, 2025]] used GC-MS with a headspace analyzer to measure MTBE and BTEX concentrations following EPA Method 8260.

## Key Results and Findings

- Quantified TTHM levels ranging from 0 to 247 ppb under varying chlorine dosages. ([[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011, p. 9]])

- GC-MS was used to detect hydrocarbon content including phenol, toluene, and benzene. ([[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011, p. 50]])

- Used to monitor the concentration of acetone, TBA, and TBF during treatment. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 10]])

- The analysis revealed that DDD was the most prevalent pesticide across all sample types. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 14]])

- The average reduction rate of EDC in closed test tubes was 7% using GC/MS analysis. ([[2014-abdallah|Abdallah, 2014, p. 93]])

- Used for the instrumental analysis of MTBE concentrations after batch treatment experiments. ([[2014-adebayo|Adebayo, 2014, p. 36]])

- Isolates BC5 and LB reduced 100 ppm of phenanthrene by approximately 95% within 15 days as measured by GC-MS. ([[2015-oyehan|Oyehan, 2015, p. 18]])

- The GC-SCD was used to measure thiophene, benzothiophene (BT), and dibenzothiophene (DBT). ([[2015-danmaliki|Danmaliki, 2015, p. 107]])

- Used for quantitation of reaction products and phenol degradation efficiency. ([[2015-hassan|Hassan, 2015, p. 28]])

- Used for PAH analysis. ([[2015-bello|Bello, 2015, p. 12]])

- Detected MBT, MPhT, DBT, DPhT, TBT, and TPhT in various concentrations across sampling sites. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 11]])

- Used to determine the concentration of naphthalene, phenanthrene, and pyrene after 18 days of incubation. ([[2015-malik|Malik, 2015, p. 15]])

- GC-MS was used to confirm the removal of Toluene and detect by-products like benzaldehyde. ([[2016-sameh|Sameh, 2016, p. 45]])

- GC-FID analysis revealed palmitic acid concentrations of 18%, 22%, and 16% for S1, S2, and S3 respectively. ([[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016, p. 12]])

- GC-MS was used to determine the concentration of pyrene in samples to calculate degradation rates. ([[2017-budiyanto|Budiyanto, 2017, p. 14]])

- GC-MS was used to analyze samples for specific PAH masses including 128, 142, 152, 154, 166, 178, 202, 228, 252, 276, and 278. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 48]])

- Used to monitor MTBE concentrations during batch adsorption experiments. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 48]])

- Identified specific polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) in the study area. ([[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019, p. 15]])

- The method allowed for the detection of specific PAH concentrations across different time intervals (30, 60, 90 days). ([[2019-alhaiqi|Alhaiqi, 2019, p. 14]])

- Analysis of n-alkane isoprenoids and molecular biomarkers indicated a Type II kerogen with little to no sulfur. ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 17]])

- The GC-FID was utilized to confirm that aromatic, aliphatic, and cyclic aliphatic compounds were effectively degraded in some conditions. ([[2024-almutawa|AlMutawa, 2024, p. 21]])

- Used for measuring concentration of MTBE (1000 mg/L) and BTEX components in the fixed-bed column samples. ([[2025-almousa|Almousa, 2025, p. 30]])
