# Sequential Gaussian Simulation

**Summary**: A stochastic simulation method that generates multiple realizations of a random variable to quantify uncertainty.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2002-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2002]] used sequential Gaussian Simulation (SGS) to model porosity and assess the probability distribution of values.

- [[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001]] applied sequential-gaussian-simulation to model porosity across the Hanifa Reservoir.

- [[1997-al-qassab|Al-Qassab, 1997]] applied sequential Gaussian Simulation to overcome the smoothing effects of kriged porosity results.

- [[1996-al-salem|Al-Salem, 1996]] used Gaussian sequential simulation to construct 3D models and capture heterogeneity.

- [[2013-atig|atig, 2013]] used sequential indicator simulation to capture the heterogeneity of the reservoir and provide multiple realizations.

## Key Results and Findings

- The width of the distribution in SGS results represents the uncertainty associated with the property at each point. ([[2002-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2002, p. 76]])

- SGS was used in all four modeling scenarios to create 3-D realizations of porosity. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 23]])

- The smoothed results of kriged porosity was overcomed by using Sequential Gaussian Simulation. ([[1997-al-qassab|Al-Qassab, 1997, p. 16]])

- Simulation appeared to have reproduced actual heterogeneity in the distribution of porosity within the reservoir. ([[1996-al-salem|Al-Salem, 1996, p. 19]])

- The results have high variability but accurate anisotropy and semi-variogram measures of spatial correlation. ([[2013-atig|atig, 2013, p. 31]])

- SGS was used for porosity, permeability, and water saturation to capture the uncertainty in property distribution. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 26]])

- SGS was used to create three realizations of the lithofacies model based on the variogram models. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 128]])

- The technique involves transforming data to a normal distribution before simulation. ([[2016-hassan-2|Hassan, 2016, p. 70]])

- Twelve simulations were generated using the SGS back-transformed algorithm. ([[2022-karami|Karami, 2022, p. 50]])
