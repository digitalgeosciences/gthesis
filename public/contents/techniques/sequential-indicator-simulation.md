# Sequential Indicator Simulation

**Summary**: A stochastic simulation method for categorical variables like facies.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001]] used sequential-indicator-simulation to build a 3D facies model as a guide for porosity modeling.

- [[1997-al-qassab|Al-Qassab, 1997]] used Sequential Indicator Simulation to build the geological architecture of lithofacies.

- [[2013-atig|atig, 2013]] applied sequential indicator simulation to model the distribution of sand bodies in the Safaniya and Khafji members.

- [[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015]] applied sequential indicator simulation (SIS) to model the lithofacies defined during analysis.

- [[2016-hassan-2|Hassan, 2016]] used sequential-indicator-simulation to model discrete categories such as facies membership.

## Key Results and Findings

- The SIS approach successfully modeled the coarsening upward sequence of the Hanifa Reservoir. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 65]])

- The non parametric Sequential Indicator Simulation was then used to build geological architecture of lithofacies using the results obtained from indicator kriging of lithofacies. ([[1997-al-qassab|Al-Qassab, 1997, p. 16]])

- SIS models showed slightly lower prediction percentages than indicator kriging models. ([[2013-atig|atig, 2013, p. 8]])

- SIS was used to generate 3D models of the distinct lithological units within the reservoir. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 26]])

- SIS is similar to SGS but uses indicator kriging for discrete cumulative density functions. ([[2016-hassan-2|Hassan, 2016, p. 71]])

- SIS was selected for its ability to capture small geological variability and assign unique semivariograms for each lithofacies. ([[2016-makkawi|Makkawi, 2016, p. 32]])
