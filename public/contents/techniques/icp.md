# Inductively Coupled Plasma

**Summary**: An analytical technique used to determine the concentration of metal ions and non-metals in liquid samples.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[1990-al-mumen|Al-Mumen, 1990]] used ICP to analyze the chemical composition of produced waters from various brine runs.

- [[2012-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2012]] used ICP to analyze aluminum and silicon content in liquids during solubility tests.

- [[2015-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2015]] used ICP to analyze the chemical composition of produced fluids from core flood experiments.

- [[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016]] used ICP to analyze effluents and determine the concentration of chelated cations.

- [[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023]] used ICP analysis to quantify calcium and magnesium ions to confirm the dissolution of carbonate minerals.

- [[2023-othman|Othman, 2023]] used ICP to measure the chelation efficiency of different agents on calcium and magnesium.

## Key Results and Findings

- Analysis of wasia, sea, and Arab-D waters were done by inductively coupled plasma (ICP) method. ([[1990-al-mumen|Al-Mumen, 1990, p. 50]])

- Used to determine if an acid was capable of leaching more aluminum and silicon (more reactive). ([[2012-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2012, p. 24]])

- ICP analysis showed that GLDA was effective at dissolving Ca and Fe without significantly impacting Si or Al (silicate minerals). ([[2015-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2015, p. 57]])

- Used to identify the types and concentrations of chelated cations (Ca, Mg, Fe) to understand the catalyst's mechanism. ([[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016, p. 52]])

- ICP analysis showed evidence of the feasibility of chelating agents to dissolve the carbonate samples by quantifying calcium and magnesium ions. ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023, p. 61]])

- DTPA outperformed other chelating agents, showing lower concentrations of Ca and Mg even at lower dosages. ([[2023-othman|Othman, 2023, p. 172]])
