# Environmental Risk Assessment

**Summary**: A systematic process to evaluate the potential risks posed by environmental contaminants.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- The calculation of the IER for several parameters like BOD and sulfate showed high risk in specific zones. ([[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011, p. 137]])

- In the EIR method, all fluids are evaluated for their chemical components... including toxicity, biodegradation potential, Persistence, Bioaccumulation, and Heavy metal concentrations. ([[2012-attyah|Attyah, 2012, p. 43]])

- Identified high-priority areas including Safaniyah, Khursaniyah-Jubail, and Tarut Bay. ([[2016-sudibyo|Sudibyo, 2016, p. 14]])

- Buffer zones were used in GIS to map risk areas from identified larval breeding sites. ([[2016-yasin|Yasin, 2016, p. 52]])

- A 50% average metal loss threshold was established as a high-risk indicator for casing failure. ([[2017-al-ajmi|Al-Ajmi, 2017, p. 34]])

- Both Arabian Killifish and Brine Shrimp were found suitable for different aspects of toxicity testing. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 17]])

- Soil samples from both sites showed heavy metal concentrations that required monitoring to determine the extent of contamination. ([[2022-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2022, p. 51]])

- Spent caustic is hazardous due to toxic, odorous, and corrosive components. ([[2023-al-aqeel|Al-Aqeel, 2023, p. 18]])

- Referenced in this thesis. ([[2023-khalid|Khalid, 2023]])

- Risk assessments included calculation of Average Daily Dose (ADD), Hazard Quotient (HQ), and Cancer Risk (CR). ([[2024-alenazi|Alenazi, 2024, p. 82]])

- Risk was quantified using Hazard Quotient (HQ) and Hazard Index (HI). ([[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025, p. 39]])
