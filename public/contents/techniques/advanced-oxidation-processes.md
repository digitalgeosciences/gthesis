# Advanced Oxidation Processes

**Summary**: Chemical treatment using highly reactive oxidants like hydroxyl radicals to degrade organic contaminants.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2009-zubair|Zubair, 2009]] used advanced-oxidation-processes to treat phenol and evaluate bromate formation.

- [[2016-sameh|Sameh, 2016]] utilized AOPs, specifically UV-H2O2, for the degradation of toluene.

- [[2020-ghazwani|Ghazwani, 2020]] applied advanced-oxidation-processes (specifically Fenton-based) to mineralize ammonia.

## Key Results and Findings

- Continuous Ozonation (1 liter per minute) was the most effective AOP technique for complete degradation of phenol within five minutes. ([[2009-zubair|Zubair, 2009, p. 13]])

- UV-H2O2 is an AOP that generates hydroxyl radicals (OH•) with a high oxidation potential of 2.80 V. ([[2016-sameh|Sameh, 2016, p. 24]])

- The hydroxyl radical is a powerful oxidant that mineralizes efficiently pollutants contained in polluted water. ([[2020-ghazwani|Ghazwani, 2020, p. 25]])
