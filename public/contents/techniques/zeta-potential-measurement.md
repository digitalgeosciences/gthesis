# Zeta Potential Measurement

**Summary**: A method to measure the electrical potential at the surface of a particle in a liquid medium.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[1992-rodriguez|Rodriguez, 1992]] used Zeta-meter 3.0 and microscopy to determine zeta potential as a function of pH and surfactant concentration.

- [[2012-kassim|KASSIM, 2012]] measured zeta potential of limestone particles in various brine compositions to identify potential determining ions.

- [[2013-elsayed|Elsayed, 2013]] performed zeta potential measurements to analyze the impact of chelating agents on rock surface charge.

- [[2014-kasha|Kasha, 2014]] used zeta potential measurements to evaluate the impact of various ions on carbonate surfaces.

- [[2015-musa|Musa, 2015]] used zeta potential measurements to determine the surface charge of the polymers.

- [[2015-he|He, 2015]] used zeta potential measurements to characterize the stability and surface charge of oil droplets in produced water.

- [[2015-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2015]] performed zeta-potential measurements on crushed rock samples to evaluate the impact of EDTA and pH on water film stability.

- [[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015]] used zeta potential measurements to characterize the change in rock surface charge and mineralogy after treatment with chelating agents.

- [[2016-alhamad|AlHamad, 2016]] used zeta potential measurements to evaluate the dispersion stability of nanoparticles in seawater with varying PVP concentrations.

- [[2017-mohamed|MOHAMED, 2017]] used zeta potential measurements to evaluate fluid stability across a range of pH values.

- [[2017-alakbari|Alakbari, 2017]] used zeta potential measurements to evaluate the stability of barite, calcium carbonate, and nanoclay across a range of pH values.

- [[2017-sadeed|SADEED, 2017]] used zeta potential measurements to determine changes in rock surface charges and identify the role of monovalent ions.

- [[2017-janjua|Janjua, 2017]] used zeta-potential-measurement to evaluate the impact of chemical concentration on rock surface interaction.

- [[2021-gasimli|Gasimli, 2021]] used zeta-potential measurements to investigate iron precipitation and solid/solid interaction with carbonate rocks across pH 1-13.

- [[2023-suleiman|Suleiman, 2023]] used zeta potential measurements to assess the surface charge of various minerals and their interaction with asphaltenes.

## Key Results and Findings

- The isoelectric point was found to be 4.2 in deionized water. ([[1992-rodriguez|Rodriguez, 1992, p. 50]])

- The zeta potential of limestone particles in modified seawater brines ranged between 0 and -12 mV. ([[2012-kassim|KASSIM, 2012, p. 170]])

- Chelating agents changed the rock charge to a higher negative value than deionized water. ([[2013-elsayed|Elsayed, 2013, p. 15]])

- Zeta potential values for modified calcite were higher than those in NaCl brine due to the suppression of electric double layer by high salt concentration. ([[2014-kasha|Kasha, 2014, p. 82]])

- CPZDA, HCDAP, and HCPZA all exhibited negative zeta potentials in basic solutions. ([[2015-musa|Musa, 2015, p. 61]])

- Zeta potential values decreased as PAM concentration increased, but did not vary significantly with different molecular weights. ([[2015-he|He, 2015, p. 28]])

- Zeta potentials for 5wt% Na4EDTA at pH 12.00 were -21.07 mV (Gray Berea) and -28.02 mV (Gray Bandera). ([[2015-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2015, p. 23]])

- Zeta potential values became more negative for Berea, Bandera, and Scioto rocks after DTPA-K5 treatment, indicating a shift toward water-wet surfaces. ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 73]])

- PVP at 1 wt% provided the highest zeta potential (-16.82 mV), indicating superior dispersion for 0.05 wt% nanoparticles. ([[2016-alhamad|AlHamad, 2016, p. 65]])

- Showed improved stability for micronized barite at pH > 8, while other sizes were unstable (-30 to +30 mV). ([[2017-mohamed|MOHAMED, 2017, p. 15]])

- Nanosilica was found stable at high pH (>10), while other solids were not. ([[2017-alakbari|Alakbari, 2017, p. 90]])

- Zeta potential of calcite and carbonate outcrop is negative in DI water due to preferential leaching of Ca2+ ions from the calcium carbonate lattice. ([[2017-sadeed|SADEED, 2017, p. 18]])

- Negative values were obtained for both VES and VES-CA systems, with values decreasing until 0.3% or 0.5% depending on the system. ([[2017-janjua|Janjua, 2017, p. 167]])

- Zeta-potential was used to understand the behavior of scale crystals on the rock surface in different pH environments. ([[2021-gasimli|Gasimli, 2021, p. 22]])

- Zeta potential was used to evaluate the impact of mineral surface chemistry on asphaltene adsorption. ([[2023-suleiman|Suleiman, 2023, p. 39]])

- Showed near-zero and low values (less than ±10) for anhydrite-carbonate systems, indicating an unstable colloidal system. ([[2024-isah|Isah, 2024, p. 70]])

- The rock was found to have an isoelectric point of 4.2 in deionized water, but remained negatively charged at high brine salinities. ([[1992-rodriguez-2|Rodriguez, 1992, p. 90]])

- Measurements were made on particles over 1um to maintain a high ratio of particle radius to double layer thickness. ([[1987-ahmed|Ahmed, 1987, p. 52]])
