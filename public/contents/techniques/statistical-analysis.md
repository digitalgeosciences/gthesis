# Statistical Analysis

**Summary**: The application of statistical methods to evaluate the accuracy and reliability of different models.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[1990-al-jarri|Al-Jarri, 1990]] applied statistical analysis including average absolute percent relative error and standard1.00, 1.25, 1.50, etc., for gas relative density.

- [[1995-al-khamis|Al-Khamis, 1995]] applied statistical analysis to compare the error rates of various Z-factor correlations.

- [[1996-mehmood|Mehmood, 1996]] applied statistical-analysis to compare new and existing correlations using metrics like standard deviation and coefficient of determination.

- [[1994-muhammad|Muhammad, 1994]] used statistical-analysis to determine the significance of redox concentration, polymer type, and concentration on gelation time.

- [[1989-al-muraikhi|Al-Muraikhi, 1989]] applied statistical analysis to compare the performance of various multiphase flow models.

- [[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992]] applied statistical analysis to validate the new correlation against existing ones.

- [[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015]] applied statistical-analysis using PRIMER, SPSS, and Statistica to evaluate the impact of pollutants on foraminiferal assemblages.

- [[2016-yasin|Yasin, 2016]] applied statistical analysis via IBM SPSS to correlate mosquito abundance with temperature, humidity, and rainfall.

- [[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018]] used SPSS to estimate LC50 and EC50 values by fitting log-logistic functions.

- [[2019-elzenary|Elzenary, 2019]] applied statistical analysis to filter out outliers from the dataset before training AI models.

- [[2021-al-nuwais|Al-Nuwais, 2021]] applied statistical-analysis to determine the degree of reservoir heterogeneity.

- [[2022-algharbi|ALGHARBI, 2022]] applied various statistical filters (Kalman, Moving Average, etc.) to evaluate their effectiveness in cleaning dynamic drilling data.

- [[2024-alenazi|Alenazi, 2024]] applied statistical-analysis including EF, Igeo, and PLI to characterize contamination levels.

- [[2023-saadeldin|SAADELDIN, 2023]] used statistical analysis (mean, median, standard deviation, kurtosis, skewness) to characterize the dataset.

## Key Results and Findings

- The study's correlation showed an average absolute percent relative error of 3.49% for oil compressibility. ([[1990-al-jarri|Al-Jarri, 1990, p. 91]])

- Average absolute relative errors were calculated for both original and smoothed data sets. ([[1995-al-khamis|Al-Khamis, 1995, p. 59]])

- Statistical comparison showed that newly developed correlations outperformed all existing ones in the study's scope. ([[1996-mehmood|Mehmood, 1996, p. 53]])

- Statistical analysis results indicated that redox concentration, initial polymer concentration, and polymer type have significant effects on the gelation time. ([[1994-muhammad|Muhammad, 1994, p. 126]])

- Comparison of average absolute percent error was used to rank the correlation performances. ([[1989-al-muraikhi|Al-Muraikhi, 1989, p. 16]])

- Statistical error analysis showed that for the data used in this study, the new correlation outperforms all the published correlations in literature. ([[1992-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 20]])

- Parameters like Ea, EMax, CC, RMSE, and Standard Deviation were used to quantify model performance. ([[2011-al-shammari|Al-Shammari, 2011, p. 46]])

- nMDS showed a significant separation (p < 0.001) between polluted and unpolluted transects. ([[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015, p. 138]])

- Regression analysis showed that climatic factors accounted for 64.3% of larval variance and 84.5% of adult variance. ([[2016-yasin|Yasin, 2016, p. 16]])

- LC50 for Arabian Killifish exposed to oil plus dispersant was calculated as 164.2 mgL-1. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 53]])

- Data showed good quality for prediction after filtering ROP values over 30 m/hr. ([[2019-elzenary|Elzenary, 2019, p. 64]])

- The coefficient of variation (CV) for permeability was 1.202–2.271, indicating high heterogeneity. ([[2021-al-nuwais|Al-Nuwais, 2021, p. 13]])

- Exponential smoothing was identified as the best statistical method for maintaining full data range while removing significant noise. ([[2022-algharbi|ALGHARBI, 2022, p. 87]])

- The Pollution Load Index (PLI) and Geo-accumulation Index (Igeo) were used to determine the extent of heavy metal pollution across different cities. ([[2024-alenazi|Alenazi, 2024, p. 44]])

- Data showed a wide range for each parameter, which is better as it covers a wider range for the model trained dataset. ([[2023-saadeldin|SAADELDIN, 2023, p. 31]])

- The CatBoost model consistently had the highest R and R2 values across all three tectonic types. ([[2025-rachmadan|Rachmadan, 2025, p. 70]])

- T-statistics for all coefficients were significantly different from zero at the 99 percent confidence level. ([[1992-al-towailib-2|Al-Towailib, 1992, p. 82]])

- Effectiveness Index (EI) values were used to categorize the quality of design practices into categories like 'extremely effective' or 'ineffective'. ([[2000-hashmi|Hashmi, 2000, p. 307]])
