## Key Results and Findings

- pore size distribution and connectivity are main controls affecting porosity and permeability. ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 164]])

- MICP measurements showed that all core samples are of bimodal pore type and are very homogeneous. ([[2015-adebayo|Adebayo, 2015, p. 16]])

- Over 36 core plugs were analyzed using MICP to characterize the reservoir's heterogeneous environment. ([[2015-al-amri|Al-Amri, 2015, p. 109]])

- MICP results revealed that permeability is very low in the muddy texture samples, with pore-throat sizes mostly below 5 microns. ([[2020-al-zoukani|Al-Zoukani, 2020, p. 20]])

- The prediction model for pore throat size achieved 0.985 CC, 0.97 R2, and 0.016 RMSE. ([[2022-suleiman|Suleiman, 2022, p. 17]])

- MICP analysis revealed porosity bimodality in Sample 10 that was not captured by standard T2 measurements. ([[2023-al-boghail|AL-BOGHAIL, 2023, p. 84]])
