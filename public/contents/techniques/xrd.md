# X-Ray Diffraction

**Summary**: A method to determine the elemental composition of materials based on their interaction with gamma rays.

---

- [[1996-matter|Matter, 1996]] used Natural Gamma-ray Spectroscopy (NGS) to quantify uranium, thorium, and potassium in phosphorite samples.

## Key Results and Findings

- Identified uranium content up to 300 ppm in the studied deposits. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 26]])

- Lithology was identified through XRD and thin section studies as limestone and dolomitic marl. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 17]])

- Used to identify clay minerals from peaks shown in diffractograms for both air-dry and glycolated samples. ([[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001, p. 30]])

- Identified specific minerals like illite and calcite within the cementation phases. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 21]])

- XRD data provided evidence for the two distinct mineralogical units (dolomite and non-dolomite) in the core samples. ([[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988, p. 10]])

- Not stated in source ([[2006-al-enezi|Al-Enezi, 2006]])

- The XRD patterns for anatase TiO2 powder showed sharp peaks at 25.46, 37.94, 48.18, 55.207, and 70.44 degrees. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 35]])

- Identified key minerals including Quartz (Q), Kaolinite (K), Iron oxide (Fe), and Chlorite (C) across various samples. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 14]])

- Quantified minerals including quartz, feldspar, and clay minerals like kaolinite. ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 16]])

- XRD was performed on representative samples to identify mineral composition. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 58]])

- XRD analysis was performed on selected samples to determine mineralogical composition. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 16]])

- XRD analysis showed Lithofacies-I is composed of quartz, muscovite, kaolinite, and albite. ([[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015, p. 40]])

- XRD patterns were analyzed for AC samples treated with NaOH and HNO3 at 30, 60, and 90°C to identify phase changes. ([[2015-danmaliki|Danmaliki, 2015, p. 88]])

- XRD spectrum of the synthesized graphene oxide was obtained (Figure 4.23). ([[2015-hassan|Hassan, 2015, p. 106]])

- Used to determine the mineralogical composition of both Musayr Formation and Wadi Waqb Member samples. ([[2015-koeshidayatullah|Koeshidayatullah, 2015, p. 11]])

- Used to analyze and confirm the mineralogical components of grainstone lithofacies. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 65]])

- The Csg facies was found to be 95% carbonate with 5% quartz, while Pbw showed 86% carbonate and 14% quartz. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 53]])

- Identified minerals including Kaolinite, Muscovite, Palygorskite, and Orthoclase across multiple samples (e.g., Q3-14-57). ([[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016, p. 10]])

- Not stated in source ([[2016-hassan-2|Hassan, 2016]])

- Used in laboratory analysis to investigate lithological heterogeneity from macro to micro scale. ([[2016-babker|Babker, 2016, p. 12]])

- XRD analysis showed dolomite as the main mineral content in the mudstone when interbedded with evaporites. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 12]])

- Used to quantify mineral content like clay and carbonates. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 36]])

- XRD results showed calcite as the major element with no traces of dolomite across multiple lithofacies (e.g., Cgdp, Esp, Og). ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 10]])

- The mineralogical analysis obtained from XRD showed that calcite and montmorillonite clay minerals are the main constituents of the PM. ([[2016-lawal-2|Lawal, 2016, p. 15]])

- XRD patterns confirmed the formation of specific crystalline phases for each nanoparticle type. ([[2016-salawu|Salawu, 2016, p. 77]])

- Identified major mineralogical constituents as calcite, dolomite, and anhydrite. ([[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016, p. 19]])

- XRD analysis confirmed that grainstone facies underlying the mudstone-evaporite facies (f2) were pervasively dolomitized. ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 52]])

- XRD analysis showed that quartz is the only silica phase present in all chert samples (100% quartz). ([[2016-radwan|Radwan, 2016, p. 55]])

- Used to determine mineral content in various lithofacies. ([[2016-makkawi|Makkawi, 2016, p. 24]])

- Identified kaolinite, quartz, muscovite, halite, and dickite in the fissile shale lithofacies. ([[2017-mahgoub|Mahgoub, 2017, p. 40]])

- XRD data was summarized for bulk and clay mineralogy, identifying key components like calcite and clays. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 161]])

- Used for mineralogical investigations of the 17 identified lithofacies. ([[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017, p. 15]])

- Quantified mineralogy for six well intervals (Well-A through Well-F). ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 14]])

- XRD was used to confirm the phase composition of RFA, AFA, and PEFA. ([[2017-olabemiwo|Olabemiwo, 2017, p. 53]])

- Used to identify minerals such as calcite, dolomite, and quartz in the carbonate system. ([[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017, p. 16]])

- XRD data showed that mineralogical associations like SiO2 and SrSO4 have a proportional relationship with FZI. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 189]])

- XRD data showed the dominance of calcite with minor quartz presence. ([[2017-yousif|Yousif, 2017, p. 110]])

- XRD results were obtained for representative samples from Wajid Group sandstones (Table 6.3). ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 10]])

- XRD identified chlorite and kaolinite as primary diagenetic phases affecting porosity. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 161]])

- Used to determine the mineralogy of framework components, altered grains, and various diagenetic cements. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 144]])

- Quantified mineral percentages and clay contents in shale samples across different lithofacies. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 71]])

- Calcite was identified as the dominant mineral (over 90%), followed by dolomite (less than 10%). ([[2018-ismanto|Ismanto, 2018, p. 12]])

- Identified three distinct clusters based on mineral composition for the sampled particulate matter. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 135]])
