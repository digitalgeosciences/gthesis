## Key Results and Findings

- Used to determine mineralogical variations in glaciolacustrine and subglacial environments. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 20]])

- Used to characterize solid components that contribute to emulsion stability. ([[1995-al-jaziri|Al-Jaziri, 1995, p. 46]])

- Identified the lithology of the aquifer through analysis of 9 field samples in dry powder form. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 54]])

- Identified clay minerals from peaks in diffractograms for both air-dry and glycolated samples. ([[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001, p. 30]])

- Identified illite, chlorite, and smectite as major components of the clay cement. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 52]])

- Identified aragonite (60%), calcite (20%), and quartz (20%) in oolitic sands; 75% quartz and 15% feldspar in quartz sand. ([[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988, p. 48]])

- XRD was used to analyze the minerals present in the sample, including clay fraction analysis for kaolinite/chlorite identification. ([[2011-al-nasser|Al-Nasser, 2011, p. 26]])

- Used to analyze solid samples after solubility tests to identify precipitates and confirm mineral content. ([[2012-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2012, p. 24]])

- XRD provides the phase identification of filter cake fabric and determines modal amounts of minerals (quantitative analysis). ([[2012-geri|Geri, 2012, p. 49]])

- Both calcite crystals and outcrop carbonate samples were confirmed as primarily composed of calcite (CaCO3). ([[2012-jabbar|JABBAR, 2012, p. 35]])

- Used to differentiate between carbonate mineral types in the Upper Jubaila and Arab-D members. ([[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013, p. 47]])

- XRD was used to determine that the form and structure of hydration products are a function of temperature and chemical composition. ([[2013-murtaza|Murtaza, 2013, p. 57]])

- Identified quartz, kaolinite, iron oxide, and chlorite as primary minerals in the formation. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 83]])

- XRD was used to identify the mineralogical composition of the rock samples of Sarah Formation, specifically identifying clay minerals. ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 32]])

- Identified 69% Calcite and 31% Dolomite in samples treated with Chelating Agent I. ([[2014-al-duailej|Al-Duailej, 2014, p. 65]])

- XRD analysis was performed on core plugs from WELL L, although results were averaged over the entire core sample rather than per individual depth. ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 41]])

- Identified various minerals and cement types within the carbonate system. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 58]])

- Not stated in source ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014]])

- Identified minerals such as quartz (SiO2) and plagioclase (An). ([[2014-elhag|ELHAG, 2014]])

- Identified mineral phases for Pink Desert and Khuff limestone samples post-sequestration. ([[2014-al-ameri|AL-AMERI, 2014, p. 59]])
