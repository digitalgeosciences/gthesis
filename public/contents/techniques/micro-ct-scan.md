# Micro-CT Scan

**Summary**: A non-destructive imaging technique to visualize internal features like pores and mineral distribution.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014]] used CT-scan to quantify open pores and identify iron oxide as high-density inclusions.

- [[2014-al-duailej|Al-Duailej, 2014]] applied micro-CT scans to visualize the 3D morphology of wormholes created by different acid systems.

- [[2014-khalid|Khalid, 2014]] used Micro XCT to characterize the 3D image of the disks and observe surface reaction areas.

- [[2015-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2015]] used micro-ct-scan to evaluate the impact of chelating agents on rock integrity and identify wormholes.

- [[2016-hassan-2|Hassan, 2016]] used Micro CT-Scan on selected lithofacies to characterize petrophysical properties.

- [[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016]] used micro-CT imaging to visualize the interior structure of eight subsurface shale plugs.

- [[2015-fogang|FOGANG, 2015]] applied cryo-TEM to observe the morphology of cylindrical micelles at 30°C and 60°C.

- [[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016]] proposed micro-CT scans for future study of microfractures and stylolites.

- [[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017]] used Micro Ct-Scan to visualize the 3D pore network and identify diagenetic/depositional controls on petrophysical properties.

- [[2019-khan|Khan, 2019]] applied micro-ct-scan to characterize the size and shape of particles in scratch residue at different depths of cut.

- [[2019-elsayed|Elsayed, 2019]] used micro-ct-scan to build 3D pore network models for visualizing pore size distribution.

- [[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019]] used micro-ct-scan to capture high-resolution 3D images of carbonate and sandstone samples.

- [[2020-mansouri|Mansouri, 2020]] used micro-ct-scan to analyze the volume and connectivity of basalt vesicles.

- [[2020-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2020]] applied micro-ct-scan to visualize the distribution of carbonated regions in both longitudinal and cross-sectional views.

- [[2021-khan|KHAN, 2021]] used medical CT scans to observe the failure patterns in rock samples during geomechanical testing.

- [[2022-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2022]] used µCT scanning for three-dimensional visualization of the pore systems in both Qusaiba and Jurassic mudrocks.

- [[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023]] applied micro-ct-scan to observe wormhole generation and changes in pore structure.

- [[2023-pratama|PRATAMA, 2023]] used micro-CT scans to observe the evolution of pore space and mineral dissolution.

- [[2023-abdelaal|Abdelaal, 2023]] applied CT-scan to visualize the sedimentation patterns of particles within the geopolymer samples.

- [[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024]] applied micro-ct-scan to quantify wormhole volume and visualize structure in limestone samples.

- [[2024-almukainah|Almukainah, 2024]] applied micro-ct-scan to visualize the internal structure of the cores and track wormhole changes.

- [[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024]] used micro-ct-scan to observe the development of microfractures and improvements in porosity after TCF treatment.

- [[2024-akomolafe|Akomolafe, 2024]] used micro-CT scan to analyze the extent of hematite invasion after filtration experiments.

- [[2025-refaat|Refaat, 2025]] used micro-ct-scan to characterize the internal geometry and volume of pores within burrow fillings.

- [[2024-suleiman-6|Suleiman, 2024]] used micro-CT scan to visualize the effect of thermal shock on the internal structure and identify microfractures.

## Key Results and Findings

- The CT-scan images revealed that the sample is predominantly composed of quartz with bright spots representing iron oxide. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 124]])

- Confirmed the formation of broad wormholes for Chelating Agent I at 2.0 cm3/min and narrower ones at 3.0 cm3/min. ([[2014-al-duailej|Al-Duailej, 2014, p. 71]])

- Micro XCT images showed that higher porosity led to increased dissolution rates due to larger surface area. ([[2014-khalid|Khalid, 2014, p. 15]])

- The CT scan results show that, channels/wormholes have been created inside the core samples which may lead to bypass more oil. ([[2015-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2015, p. 112]])

- Micro CT-Scan was applied on four selected lithofacies to get more detailed sedimentological and petrophysical properties. ([[2016-hassan-2|Hassan, 2016, p. 45]])

- Used to visualize internal structures, though software limitations hindered quantification of specific components. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 47]])

- Cryo-TEM confirmed the presence of cylindrical micelles even after dilution in ethyl acetate. ([[2015-fogang|FOGANG, 2015, p. 45]])

- Not stated in source ([[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016]])

- Identified the distribution of vuggy, moldic, and intercrystalline microporosity across different facies. ([[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017, p. 140]])

- Micro-CT revealed a significant increase in grain size at 0.2 mm depth, indicating a transition from ductile to brittle failure. ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019, p. 48]])

- Micro CT results for pore size distribution were found to be comparable to the T2 relaxation distribution. ([[2019-elsayed|Elsayed, 2019, p. 16]])

- Micro-CT at 4μm resolution revealed pore connectivity from top to bottom in sub-plugs. ([[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019, p. 90]])

- Confirmed that the aquifer has two distinct zones based on vesicle connectivity and fracture aperture. ([[2020-mansouri|Mansouri, 2020, p. 13]])

- Micro-CT scans confirmed that 0.3% BWOC of laponite reduced the carbonation depth compared to the base sample. ([[2020-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2020, p. 83]])

- Medical CT scanning was used to describe the weakening mechanism of rocks, where failure starts as micro-sized discontinuities followed by clay swelling. ([[2021-khan|KHAN, 2021, p. 47]])

- Provided 3D volumes for pore system and mineral phases based on grayscale color density. ([[2022-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2022, p. 91]])

- Used to identify the evolution of pore structure after live brine injection. ([[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023, p. 15]])

- Micro-CT images were used to differentiate between pre-reaction and post-reaction conditions in 10mm and 20mm slices. ([[2023-pratama|PRATAMA, 2023, p. 57]])

- CT-scan imaging confirmed that 3% BWOB perlite resulted in a nearly uniform color (minimal density difference) across all slices. ([[2023-abdelaal|Abdelaal, 2023, p. 134]])

- The micro-ct scan had a resolution of 49 μm, allowing for the visualization of fine details in wormholes. ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 47]])

- Micro-CT provided high-resolution images that helped assess alterations in wormhole structure and visually illustrate mineral matrix changes. ([[2024-almukainah|Almukainah, 2024, p. 50]])

- Micro-CT scans were used to show the development of microfractures and improvements in porosity and permeability. ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024, p. 39]])

- Used for non-destructive imaging of internal structures, specifically identifying the distribution of minerals within the core. ([[2024-akomolafe|Akomolafe, 2024, p. 43]])

- Micro-CT scans at 9 μm resolution allowed for the identification of distinct pore volumes and the quantification of isolated vs. connected pores. ([[2025-refaat|Refaat, 2025, p. 45]])

- Used to analyze fracture propagation and orientation after breakdown experiments. ([[2024-suleiman-6|Suleiman, 2024, p. 48]])
