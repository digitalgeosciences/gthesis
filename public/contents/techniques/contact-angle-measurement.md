# Contact Angle Measurement

**Summary**: A method to determine the wettability of a rock surface by measuring the angle at which a liquid droplet meets the solid surface.

---

- [[2012-jabbar|JABBAR, 2012]] used contact angle measurement to evaluate the impact of brine chemistry on carbonate surfaces.

- [[2013-al-mutairi|AL-MUTAIRI, 2013]] used contact-angle-measurement to establish an empirical relationship between contact angle and exposure time.

- [[2014-shakeel|SHAKEEL, 2014]] used contact-eangle-measurement to determine the initial and final states of rock wettibility.

## Key Results and Findings

- Contact angles for MO(SA)/Carbonate systems showed good repeatability, while results for calcite were less consistent due to surface heterogeneity. ([[2012-jabbar|JABBAR, 2012, p. 89]])

- Contact angle was found to decrease exponentially with the time of exposure to brine until reaching a stable value. ([[2013-al-mutairi|AL-MUTAIRI, 2013, p. 19]])

- Wettability was determined by contact angle measurements on a droplet of crude oil placed on the rock surface in the presence of brine. ([[2014-shakeel|SHAKEEL, 2014, p. 43]])

- 0.05 wt% nanoparticles reduced the contact angle to 36.5° at ambient conditions, indicating a stronger water-wet state. ([[2016-alhamad|AlHamad, 2016, p. 76]])

- The contact angle experiments suggested two weeks thermal aging is enough to restore the original wettibility for calcite rock discs. ([[2017-ahmed-3|Ahmed, 2017, p. 18]])

- Salicylic acid increased the contact angle from 63.64° to 111.18°, while NaI brine reduced it back to 61.21°. ([[2020-opoku|Opoku, 2020, p. 121]])

- Large contact angles indicate a hydrophobic surface and are an indicator to inhibit shale swelling. ([[2021-khan|KHAN, 2021, p. 58]])

- Surfactants with one phenyl group in the spacer performed best, changing oil-wet rock to water-wet conditions. ([[2022-xiao|Xiao, 2022, p. 21]])

- Contact angle measurement was used to determine if sacrificial fluids could provide long-term protection against wettibility changes. ([[2023-suleiman|Suleiman, 2023, p. 43]])

- ZIS treatment resulted in a contact angle of approximately 75.7°, shifting the rock surface to intermediate-wet. ([[2024-al-azani|Al-Azani, 2024, p. 95]])

- Determined that anhydrite presence tends to shift the system toward a more water-wet state. ([[2024-isah|Isah, 2024, p. 70]])
