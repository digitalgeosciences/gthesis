# Rock-Eval Pyrolysis

**Summary**: A method used to evaluate the geochemical potential of organic matter in sedimentary rocks.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2012-ghazwani|Ghazwani, 2012]] used Rock-Eval pyrolysis to determine TOC, HI, and Tmax for Hanadir samples from three wells.

- [[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017]] applied Rock-Eval pyrolysis to determine TOC, S1, S2, and Tmax for source rock characterization.

- [[2020-usman|Usman, 2020]] used RockEval pyrolysis on over 150 samples to estimate organic richness (TOC) and generation potential.

- [[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022]] used Rock-Eval pyrolysis to determine TOC and thermal maturity in the Jubaila Formation.

- [[2023-al-eid|Al-Eid, 2023]] used rock-eval-pyrolysis to measure S1, S2, and TOC across various time cycles for three core samples.

- [[2024-malik|Malik, 2024]] applied Rock-Eval Pyrolysis to assess organic content in the Dhaiqa Basin.

- [[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025]] applied Rock-Eval pyrolysis to determine TOC and maturity levels in carbonate mudrocks.

## Key Results and Findings

- Well A and B results indicated source rock is in gas generation window (low HI, high Tmax). ([[2012-ghazwani|Ghazwani, 2012, p. 15]])

- TOC and pyrolysis data were used to categorize the formation into three tiers based on richness and maturity. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 158]])

- Pyrolysis Tmax and HI values were used to assess maturity and kerogen type, identifying a mixed Type II/III or Type II character. ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 17]])

- Used for characterizing 185 samples from the Jubaila Formation to understand quality (generating potential), kerogen type, and thermal maturity. ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 51]])

- Un-preserved samples showed significant drops in S1, S2, and TOC compared to preserved samples from the same interval. ([[2023-al-eid|Al-Eid, 2023, p. 17]])

- Used to provide detailed geochemical studies for understanding the sedimentology. ([[2024-malik|Malik, 2024, p. 14]])

- TOC values were used to categorize samples by organic richness. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 10]])
