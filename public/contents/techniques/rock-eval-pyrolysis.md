# Rock-Eval Pyrolysis

**Summary**: A method to evaluate the quantity, quality, and maturity of organic matter in source rocks.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2012-ghazwani|Ghazwani, 2012]] used Rock-Eval pyrolysis to assess Hanadir source rock potential.

- [[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017]] used rock-eval-pyrolysis to determine TOC and pyrolytic yield for the Tuwaiq Mountain Formation.

- [[2020-usman|Usman, 2020]] used RockEval pyrolysis to characterize the organic content and maturity of carbonate mudrocks in the Jafurah sub-basin.

- [[2021-lawal|LAWAL, 2021]] used Rock-Eval pyrolysis to determine the total organic carbon (TOC) of the source rock.

- [[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022]] applied pyrolysis to determine TOC content across different wells.

- [[2023-suleiman|Suleiman, 2023]] used Rock-Eval 7 to calculate total organic carbon (TOC) and total sulfur content in asphaltene samples.

- [[2023-al-eid|Al-Eid, 2023]] used rock-eval-pyrolysis to determine S1, S2, TOC, and TMAX across various time cycles.

- [[2024-malik|Malik, 2024]] used Rock Eval Pyrolysis to determine S1, S2, T-max, HI, and OI for source rock potential.

- [[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024]] used Rock-Eval pyrolysis to characterize kerogen maturity and hydrocarbon potential (S1, S2) in shale samples.

- [[2024-suleiman-6|Suleiman, 2024]] used Rock-Eval analysis to determine TOC, S1, and S2 peaks in shale samples.

## Key Results and Findings

- Results for wells A and B indicated the source rock is in the gas generation window with high Tmax and low HI. ([[2012-ghazwani|Ghazwani, 2012, p. 15]])

- Total pyrolytic yield (SI + S2) reached as high as 30.84 mg HC/g rock, indicating excellent source potential. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 58]])

- 150 samples were analyzed, identifying several lithofacies with high TOC (up to 3.0%) and HI values up to 200 mgHC/gTOC. ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 53]])

- The TOC content was determined to be high, at approximately 12 wt.%. ([[2021-lawal|LAWAL, 2021, p. 78]])

- Well J1 was identified as the most organic-rich interval, while well J2 was the least. ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 139]])

- Rock-Eval was used to quantify the hydrogen and oxygen indices and other parameters for asphaltene characterization. ([[2023-suleiman|Suleiman, 2023, p. 41]])

- S1 and S2 values showed significant drops in un-preserved samples compared to preserved ones from the same interval. ([[2023-al-eid|Al-Eid, 2023, p. 17]])

- TOC measurements were performed using a Rock Eval 7 Source Rock Analyzer to constrain kerogen type and source rock potential. ([[2024-malik|Malik, 2024, p. 35]])

- The study used Tmax, HI, OI, and PI indices derived from Rock-Eval to assess the impact of thermochemical treatment on organic matter. ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024, p. 35]])

- TOC was calculated by summing carbon from both pyrolysis and oxidation phases. ([[2024-suleiman-6|Suleiman, 2024, p. 59]])
