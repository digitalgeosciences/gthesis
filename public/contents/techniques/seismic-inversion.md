# Seismic Inversion

**Summary**: A geophysics technique to convert seismic data into rock properties like acoustic impedance.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2002-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2002]] used seismic inversion to transform seismic traces into acoustic impedances for petrophysical modeling.

- [[2014-kola|Kola, 2014]] applied AVO analysis to determine the utility of simplified TPP and TPS equations.

- [[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015]] applied post-stack seismic inversion to derive acoustic impedance from 3D seismic data.

- [[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016]] applied seismic-inversion to determine organic richness and fracture gradients.

- [[2016-ali|Ali, 2016]] used joint inversion of acoustic velocity and impedance to identify pore fluids from surface seismic data.

- [[2016-houssain|Houssain, 2016]] used seismic-inversion to relate the derived acoustic impedance to porosity for quantitative interpretation.

- [[2018-mohamed|Mohamed, 2018]] evaluated acoustic FWI on both acoustic and viscoelastic synthetic data to determine its effectiveness in complex near-surface environments.

## Key Results and Findings

- Acou150-200m range of seismic data was used to derive acoustic impedance, which correlates with porosity. ([[2002-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2002, p. 21]])

- Simplified equations were found to be highly accurate for TPP (under 0.001% error) and acceptable for TPS at common exploration angles. ([[2014-kola|Kola, 2014, p. 40]])

- Inversion results were compared against log-derived values to validate the transformation. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 48]])

- Seismic inversion successfully pinpointed sweet spots in the Barnett Shale by identifying high frackability zones. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 112]])

- Inversion of AI, P-wave velocity, density, and compressional modulus resulted in a percent error of almost 0% for gas, live oil, and brine in cases 1 and 2. ([[2016-ali|Ali, 2016, p. 14]])

- A second-order polynomial fit (R^2 = 0.9) was established between porosity and acoustic impedance. ([[2016-houssain|Houssain, 2016, p. 89]])

- Acoustic FWI successfully reconstructed velocity images with small local anomalies and strong velocity contrast. ([[2018-mohamed|Mohamed, 2018, p. 15]])
