# Finite Element Modeling

**Summary**: A numerical technique for solving complex engineering and physics problems by dividing a large system into smaller, simpler parts.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2017-khan|Khan, 2017]] used finite-element-modeling via COMSOL to couple CO2 flow and geomechanical behavior in carbonate reservoirs.

- [[2018-aldossary|AlDossary, 2018]] used finite element modeling to simulate stress and strain changes in a 3D geomechanical model.

- [[2023-al-garni|Al-Garni, 2023]] used finite element modeling in COMSOL Multiphysics to predict potential instability in wormholes.

## Key Results and Findings

- The model successfully predicted pore pressure distribution and stress fields in both single-porosity and multi-layered systems. ([[2017-khan|Khan, 2017, p. 39]])

- The FEM approach allowed for the calculation of local stress concentrations and rotations resulting from abrupt changes in elastic properties at stratigraphic boundaries. ([[2018-aldossary|AlDossary, 2018, p. 60]])

- Analysis showed that branched and horizontal/tilted wormholes were more vulnerable to strain than larger, vertical ones. ([[2023-al-garni|Al-Garni, 2023, p. 47]])
