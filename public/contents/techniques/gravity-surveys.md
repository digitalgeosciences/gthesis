# Gravity Surveys

**Summary**: A geophysical method measuring variations in Earth's gravity to map density differences.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2012-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2012]] used gravity data to interpret regional faults and the general orientation of lineaments.

- [[2014-saeed|Saeed, 2014]] used gravity survey to measure density contrasts of cavity filling materials.

- [[2018-hanif|Hanif, 2018]] used gravity-survey to identify fault structures and cavity potential in the Dammam Dome.

## Key Results and Findings

- Gravity data helped identify large-scale features like deep basement structures and shallow features such as dykes and sills. ([[2012-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2012, p. 20]])

- The maximum gravity anomaly (gmax) was used as a primary input for the joint inversion calculation. ([[2014-saeed|Saeed, 2014, p. 39]])

- The survey identified 67 faults with primary orientations of N 39° W and N 48° E. ([[2018-hanif|Hanif, 2018, p. 15]])

- Positive gravity anomalies were associated with higher TDS values, while negative anomalies indicated lower TDS and better water quality. ([[2024-ahmed|Ahmed, 2024, p. 20]])

- Used to identify structural features like faults and dikes that control geothermal fluid circulation. ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 40]])
