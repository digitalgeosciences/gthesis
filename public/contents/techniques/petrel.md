# Petrel

**Summary**: A commercial software suite for reservoir modeling and engineering.

## Key Results and Findings

- Petrel was used to integrate multi-source data into a coherent 3D model of the fractured system. ([[2012-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2012, p. 23]])

- Petrel was used to construct a model with over 23 million cells in the northern area of interest. ([[2013-atig|atig, 2013, p. 53]])

- Used for SIS and SGS algorithms to distribute lithofacies and petrophysical properties in a 3D grid. ([[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013, p. 60]])

- Not stated in source ([[2013-khamis|Khamis, 2013]])

- Not stated in source ([[2015-adeniji|Adeniji, 2015]])

- The variogram calculation module in PETREL did not provide individual data points, limiting the ability to model using various nested functions. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 147]])

- Petrel was used to correlate 14 vertical logs across three depositional zones. ([[2017-mahgoub|Mahgoub, 2017, p. 46]])

- The 2D model successfully predicted gas potential variations across the J140-150 range based on heat flow inputs. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 140]])

- The 3D model successfully integrated structural variation, lithofacies heterogeneity, and porosity distribution. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 210]])

- Not stated in source (mentioned in techniques list). ([[2022-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2022]])

- The SIS algorithm successfully produced a 3D model that distinguished the two interpreted fourth-order sequences. ([[2022-bashri|Bashri, 2022, p. 124]])

- The model successfully differentiated between grain-dominated facies (north) and mud-dominated facies (south). ([[2022-babker|Babker, 2022, p. 96]])

- The 3D models of the reservoir showed that higher porosities and lower water saturations coincided with channel intervals, confirming suitability for storage. ([[2024-maigana|Maigana, 2024, p. 70]])
