# Seismic Attribute Analysis

**Summary**: The calculation of various physical properties from seismic traces to enhance interpretation.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- Nine different attributes were used to establish a multi-variant regression for property estimation. ([[1997-al-marhoun|Al-Marhoun, 1997, p. 16]])

- Seismic attributes including instantaneous frequency and RMS amplitude were used as inputs for the GRNN models. ([[2009-sitouah|Sitouah, 2009, p. 13]])

- Seven specific attributes (Instantaneous Frequency, t*Attenuation, Cosine of Phase, Vp/Vs, P-Impedance, Reflection Acoustic Impedance, and Dominant frequency) were used as the primary training inputs. ([[2013-mark|Mark, 2013, p. 11]])

- Selected attributes were used to model porosity, permeability, and water saturation in the Aradeiba-D member. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 76]])

- The method provides better edge detection for steeply dipping events compared to the Sobel operator. ([[2015-adetokunbo|Adetokunbo, 2015, p. 13]])

- RMS attribute was used to distinguish between sandstone and claystone facies based on amplitude and continuity. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 218]])

- Instantaneous phase is independent of amplitude and influenced by the propagation phase of the wave-front. ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 55]])

- RMS amplitude maps showed a distribution of sandy intervals, while Chaos maps identified sedimentary textures around channel boundaries. ([[2024-maigana|Maigana, 2024, p. 8]])
