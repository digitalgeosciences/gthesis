# Ray Tracing

**Summary**: A method to calculate the paths of seismic waves through a medium with varying velocity properties.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-own|Own, 2015]] applied ray_tracing to model signal propagation from perforation shots to receivers.

- [[2018-mohamed|Mohamed, 2018]] used ray tracing to evaluate the impact of Karst and high velocity contrast on illumination.

- [[2024-felemban|Felemban, 2024]] used back-propagation ray-tracing to determine the first angle of incidence in the primary formations.

- [[2025-oyekan|Oyekan, 2025]] applied ray-tracing to calculate offsets for the diving direct P-wave (DDW).

## Key Results and Findings

- Ray tracing was used to evaluate different monitoring configurations and their respective location errors. ([[2015-own|Own, 2015, p. 35]])

- Ray tracing showed that Karst bodies can dramatically change the behavior of the wave path. ([[2018-mohamed|Mohamed, 2018, p. 47]])

- Used to identify critical angles where phase changes occur, impacting semblance energy. ([[2024-felemban|Felemban, 2024, p. 63]])

- The objective function aimed at minimizing the error between observed offset and the offset calculated using ray tracing. ([[2025-oyekan|Oyekan, 2025, p. 12]])
