# Well Log Analysis

**Summary**: The measurement of physical properties of rocks and fluids in a borehole using electrical or nuclear tools.

---

- [[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001]] used well-log-analysis to provide the primary data for porosity modeling as it is more widely available than core samples.

## Key Results and Findings

- Well logs are used as the base for porosity modeling because they are available at most wells and offer a larger volume of investigation compared to core plugs. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 31]])

- Integration of well information with seismic data is essential due to the differing spatial and vertical resolutions of each data type. ([[1997-al-marhoun|Al-Marhoun, 1997, p. 92]])

- Lithological logs were used to identify the thickness and facies of formations such as the Baidoa, Hamantey, and Sawer formations. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 24]])

- Lithofacies were determined using a suite of logs including Density, Neutron, and Gamma ray with specific cutoffs. ([[1997-al-qassab|Al-Qassab, 1997, p. 50]])

- Well-log derived porosity was used as the primary input for both kriging and simulation models. ([[1996-al-salem|Al-Salem, 1996, p. 19]])

- Gamma ray (GR), Sonic (DT), and Bulk Density (RB) logs were used to distinguish between sandstone and shale/mudstone units and identify depositional motifs. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 110]])

- All wells have porosity logs derived from sonic, neutron, and density logs. ([[2003-al-shridi|Al-Shridi, 2003, p. 18]])

- Well-log data (gamma ray, sonic, formation-density, and neutron) were used to define the boundaries of the Sulaiy, Yamama, Buwaib/Biyadh, and Shu'aiba formations. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 24]])

- Well log data combined with seismic attributes were used to train and test both MLP and GRNN models. ([[2009-sitouah|Sitouah, 2009, p. 15]])

- Well logs were used to identify petrophysical rocks in uncored wells by correlating them to gamma ray, density, neutron, and sonic logs. ([[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010, p. 70]])

- Gamma-ray logs were used to distinguish between shale minerals (high gamma) and limestone minerals (low gamma). ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 43]])

- Used to define the structural boundaries and thickness of the reservoir units. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 31]])

- Well log data from KFUPM were used to determine that the UER aquifer has a mean thickness of 400 m regionally but approximately 85 m in the local study area. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 54]])

- Fractures observed from the FMI-log of five wells showed similar trends to faults detected from seismic data. ([[2012-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2012, p. 11]])

- The delta Log R method successfully identified the Hanadir source rock zone across three wells (A, B, and C). ([[2012-ghazwani|Ghazwani, 2012, p. 56]])

- Not specifically quantified in this thesis as it is used for general context. ([[2012-attyah|Attyah, 2012]])

- Mud-weight data proved to be indicative of the pore pressure conditions of the target formations. ([[2013-mark|Mark, 2013, p. 104]])

- Standard well-log-analysis underestimates hydrocarbon saturation in thin-bedded sands due to the averaging effect of low-resolution tools. ([[2013-abdullah|Abdullah, 2013, p. 16]])

- Petrophysical parameters were calculated using a combination of sonic, density, and neutron logs. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 39]])

- Well log analysis allowed for the identification of three main facies: fluvial channel, bay, and marine. ([[2013-atig|atig, 2013, p. 44]])

- SGR log motifs were successfully used to identify reservoir zonation and lithofacies associations. ([[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013, p. 171]])

- Utilized VSP geometry with receivers below the target horizon for TAVO analysis. ([[2014-kola|Kola, 2014, p. 15]])

- Core porosity in the oolitic grainstone ranged from 25% to 35%. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 95]])

- SGR logs for U, Th, and K were used to differentiate between various carbonate facies. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 57]])

- S-wave travel time increased significantly due to the presence of fractures. ([[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015, p. 45]])

- Well logs (density and neutron) were used as the primary ground truth for calibrating seismic attributes. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 19]])

- A cutoff value of 150 API gamma-ray units distinguishes potential source shale from non-source shale. ([[2015-own|Own, 2015, p. 34]])

- Well logging curves were used to classify three distinct lithofacies (sand, shaly-sand, shale) and evaluate petrophysical properties. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 13]])

- Average porosity was 2.1% with a maximum of 5.4%, while average permeability was 0.2 md. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 90]])

- SGR signatures showed moderate correlation with lithofacies for each high-frequency sequence. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015]])

- Identified four distinct log patterns: blocky (sandstone), coarsening upward (shaly sandstone), serrated (mudrock), and bell-shaped (heterolith). ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 60]])

- Unayzah-C appears as chaotic and irregular with no or very weak bedding on borehole image logs. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 10]])

- Identified (E/k) as the most robust brittle indicator among several log-derived parameters. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 58]])

- GR log motifs were used to define nine distinct lithofacies associations across three formations. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 17]])

- Well log data were used in multivariate statistical methods (K-means, PCA) to identify distinct lithofacies and boundaries. ([[2016-ejaz|Ejaz, 2016, p. 13]])

- Derived four dynamic elastic moduli (G, K, E, v) for the entire Khuff Formation. ([[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016, p. 30]])

- Not stated in source ([[2016-ali|Ali, 2016]])

- GWLA was used to determine a rock physics model by improving raw data from gamma ray, density, resistivity, caliper, and sonic logs. ([[2016-houssain|Houssain, 2016, p. 17]])

- High gamma-ray responses indicated high organic content in the fissile shale. ([[2017-mahgoub|Mahgoub, 2017, p. 64]])

- Used to identify reservoir and non-reservoir facies based on porosity, permeability, and hydrocarbon saturation. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 30]])

- Used as a tool for identifying lithofacies and providing a regional correlation guide. ([[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017, p. 41]])

- Nine hydraulic flow units were established with FZI values ranging from 0.0938 to 1.5. ([[2017-yousif|Yousif, 2017, p. 20]])

- Gamma ray logs were used to distinguish between sandstone-dominated (yellow) and mudstone-dominated (green) units in braided delta systems. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 142]])
