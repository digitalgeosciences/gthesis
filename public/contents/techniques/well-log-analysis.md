# Well Log Analysis

**Summary**: The analysis of geophysical logs to determine lithology and petrophysical properties.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- Th, K, and U concentrations were used to delineate clay types and identify mineralogical variations across six wells. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 130]])

- Resistivity values for the Khobar Aquifer range from 10 ϕ₀-m to 500 ϕ₀-m. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 89]])

- Well-log porosity was used as the primary input for both kriging and simulation models. ([[1996-al-salem|Al-Salem, 1996, p. 19]])

- Used GR, RB, and DT logs to interpret the depositional environment of the Unayzah Formation. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 41]])

- All wells had porosity logs derived from sonic, neutron, and density logs. ([[2003-al-shridi|Al-Shridi, 2003, p. 18]])

- Used to identify that Well-E was a non-representative sample due to geological discontinuities. ([[2001-al-subai|Al-Subai, 2001, p. 45]])

- Well-log data (gamma ray, sonic, formation-density, and neutron) were used to define the boundaries of the Sulaiy, Yamama, Buwaib, and Shu'aiba formations. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 24]])

- Wash-out impacts density readings significantly due to its shallow depth of investigation (0.5 ft). ([[2009-al-ali|Al-Ali, 2009, p. 3]])

- Well log data were used in combination with seismic attributes to improve prediction accuracy. ([[2009-sitouah|Sitouah, 2009, p. 14]])

- Log data were used to predict hydraulic flow units in non-cored wells using multi-linear equations. ([[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010, p. 162]])

- Gamma-ray logs were used to distinguish between shale minerals (high values) and limestone minerals (low values). ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 43]])

- The analysis package was capable of modeling multilayer or multilaterals with each layer having its own fluid, completion, and inflow model. ([[2011-almubarak|ALMUBARAK, 2011, p. 21]])

- Data from 7 wells were analyzed to compare actual vs. predicted values. ([[2011-al-dhufairi|AL-DHUFAIRI, 2011, p. 143]])

- Confirmed cross flow between Arab-C and D zones in Well-B. ([[2011-al-dossary|Al-Dossary, 2011, p. 70]])

- Used to confirm full isolation of problematic zones (e.g., Formulation-5) by identifying distinct 'E' shape signatures. ([[2011-al-amri|Al-Amri, 2011, p. 18]])

- Gamma-ray, caliper, density, neutron, and sonic logs were used for log-to-core calibration. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 43]])

- Water saturation calculations showed a 5.3% difference in Reservoir D when comparing hydraulic units vs. average well methods. ([[2011-al-nasser|Al-Nasser, 2011, p. 67]])

- Formation analysis logs were used to identify the productive sandstone reservoirs (Unayzah-A and Unayzah-B). ([[2011-alburaikan|AlBuraikan, 2011, p. 54]])

- FMI tool provided high-resolution micro-resistivity images allowing for the identification of conductive fractures. ([[2012-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2012, p. 77]])

- Well log data (gamma ray, neutron, density) were combined with dynamic pressure data as primary inputs for the AI models. ([[2012-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2012, p. 16]])

- Sonic logging showed the highest AARE (88.7%) due to its reliance on assumed matrix values for pure limestone. ([[2013-al-kharraa|Al-Kharraa, 2013, p. 39]])

- Porosity was determined from sonic (DT), neutron (NPHI), density (RHOZ) logs and the density-neutron combination. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 53]])

- Identified a sharp break in Th, K, and TC counts at the boundary between Upper Jubaila and Arab-D members. ([[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013, p. 176]])

- Pressure derivative plots were used to identify distinct flow regimes, such as transition periods between matrix and fracture contributions. ([[2012-al-abdulmohsin|Al-Abdulmohsin, 2012, p. 43]])

- Not stated in source ([[2013-khan-2|Khan, 2013]])

- Logs showed that Zone-1 is un-swept, while lower zones were swept with a small remaining oil column of 5-20 ft. ([[2014-al-julaih|Al-Julaih, 2014, p. 41]])

- Log-based rock mechanical properties were calibrated using laboratory values before being used in geomechanical simulations. ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 41]])

- Integrated log analysis was more effective for locating small leaks than individual tools. ([[2014-al-hussain|Al-Hussain, 2014, p. 30]])

- MSFL and density logs were used to identify open fractures in the Qusaiba Shale. ([[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015, p. 68]])

- Corrected neutron porosity values were successfully derived for sandstone, limestone, and dolomite using specific correction formulas. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 36]])
