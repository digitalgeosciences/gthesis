# Reservoir Simulation

**Summary**: The use of mathematical models to simulate fluid flow and pressure distribution in a reservoir.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- Simulations showed that horizontal wells with larger contact areas result in lower pressure drops and delayed breakthrough. ([[1993-kandil|Kandil, 1993, p. 10]])

- The numerical model showed excellent agreement with semianalytical solutions for both infinite and just finite conductivity cases. ([[1990-mimoune|Mimoune, 1990, p. 47]])

- Pseudo relative permeability curves generated from the cross-sectional models were used to determine the water saturation front location. ([[1992-al-otaibi|Al-Otaibi, 1992, p. 100]])

- Not stated in source ([[1993-al-towailib|Al-Towailib, 1993]])

- Models were used to evaluate different scenarios and make predictions for multi-lateral wells. ([[2011-almubarak|ALMUBARAK, 2011, p. 38]])

- FrontSim was used to model four different scenarios ranging from a simple homogeneous layer to complex real-world reservoirs. ([[2010-mohammed|Mohammed, 2010, p. 24]])

- Simulation results showed that horizontal wells produce reserves significantly faster than vertical wells in low-permeability (1 mD) reservoirs. ([[2012-baarimah|BAARIMAH, 2012, p. 70]])

- The simulator was used to determine abandonment pressure, ultimate recovery factor, and time to reach abandonment condition for various well types. ([[2012-kamal|Kamal, 2012, p. 47]])

- Simulated models successfully replicated the expected transition periods for both single and dual fractured systems. ([[2012-al-abdulmohsin|Al-Abdulmohsin, 2012, p. 68]])

- The use of two different permeability functions successfully modeled the sudden rise in permeability at breakthrough. ([[2013-kareem|Kareem, 2013, p. 104]])

- ECLIPSE 100 was utilized to model foam as a tracer, accounting for adsorption onto rock surfaces and decay over time. ([[2013-sibaweihi|Sibaweihi, 2013, p. 34]])

- The model predicted an ultimate oil recovery of 45% when both solubility and wettibility alteration were included. ([[2013-al-mutairi|AL-MUTAIRI, 2013, p. 125]])

- The study compared LUS and MOL algorithms in simulating well tests for both single and dual porosity reservoirs. ([[2013-al-nemer|Al-Nemer, 2013, p. 79]])

- Simulation was used to evaluate NPV and URF as objective functions for optimized variables like concentration and well location. ([[2013-ahmed|AHMED, 2013, p. 20]])

- Forward model upscaling is integrated with history matching to reduce total calculation time. ([[2013-mehmood|Mehmood, 2013, p. 28]])

- The full-field simulation model showed that placing horizontal wells in the top layer results in significant added recovery. ([[2014-al-julaih|Al-Julaih, 2014, p. 14]])

- Integrated with MATLAB to provide the necessary data for evaluating the objective functions (NPV and VRR). ([[2014-ahmed-2|Ahmed, 2014, p. 16]])

- ECLIPSE 100 was used as the evaluation tool for every proposed configuration to predict production behavior. ([[2014-ali|Ali, 2014, p. 53]])

- Simulated results showed that high PI wells are restricted by wellbore hydraulics rather than inflow performance. ([[2014-al-gahtani|Al-Gahtani, 2014, p. 130]])

- Simulation results were used to compare the performance of standard patterns against optimized well locations. ([[2014-al-ismael|AL-ISMAEL, 2014, p. 35]])

- Simulations were conducted on a 30x30x3 grid to evaluate various EOR scenarios over a 20-year period. ([[2014-azad|Azad, 2014, p. 135]])

- A 3D synthetic reservoir comprising sixty-thousand grid cells was used in this study. ([[2015-adeniji|Adeniji, 2015, p. 22]])

- The integration of optimization algorithms with CMG STARS allows for the handling of more design elements in ES-SAGD. ([[2015-moussa|Moussa, 2015, p. 12]])

- Simulation results were used to compare SAGD and VAPEX performances across three optimization cases. ([[2015-khan|KHAN, 2015, p. 15]])

- Coupling the simulation with Differential Evolution successfully maximized NPV in three different cases. ([[2015-rammay|RAMMAY, 2015, p. 16]])

- Coupling the transport flow with a geochemical model allows for representation of mineral-aqueous equilibrium and surface adsorption. ([[2016-adam|ADAM, 2016, p. 20]])

- Simulation results were used as the basis for training the artificial neural networks to develop correlation equations. ([[2016-mahboob|MAHBOOB, 2016, p. 20]])

- Simulation results provided the basis for training the neural network across a wide range of mobility ratios and heterogeneity values. ([[2016-kalam|Kalam, 2016, p. 45]])

- Simulations concluded that injecting CO2 at residual oil saturation followed by water flooding yielded better results. ([[2014-alam|ALAM, 2014, p. 18]])

- Simulations showed that the inclusion of a memory term significantly affects wellbore pressure calculations in certain scenarios. ([[2016-david|DAVID, 2016, p. 85]])

- The simulation showed that pore pressure increases more significantly when caprock is intact compared to fractured. ([[2017-khan|Khan, 2017, p. 65]])

- The use of an implicit scheme with flow rates as primary variables leads to faster convergence than using velocity. ([[2018-elsaieed|ElSaieed, 2018, p. 33]])

- The trilinear model successfully matched the production history for Well A. ([[2018-aleid|Aleid, 2018, p. 63]])

- Used as a basis for the correlation and tuning mechanism. ([[2018-homoud|Homoud, 2018, p. 78]])

- Simulation results (26%, 12%, and 24% tertiary; 63%, 55%, and 67% total) matched the experimental values well. ([[2019-alghamdi|AlGhamdi, 2019, p. 18]])

- Coupled a 1-D wellbore model with a 3-D reservoir model using Peaceman's formulation for exchange terms. ([[2019-suleiman|Suleiman, 2019, p. 42]])

- Standard numerical simulations are accurate but slow, requiring significant computational resources for complex systems. ([[2018-salih|Salih, 2018, p. 13]])

- Each time step in the RL methods involved one simulation run to update the Q-table or state values. ([[2019-alsamadony|Alsamadony, 2019, p. 57]])

- Simulation results showed that thermochemical treatment is better than conventional gas injection, with 89.1% recovery vs 25.35%. ([[2020-mohammed|Mohammed, 2020, p. 22]])

- Multi-mesh methods like DMM, EDMM, TMM, and ETMM were shown to provide a balance between computational cost and simulation accuracy compared to full fine-scale simulations. ([[2022-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2022, p. 12]])

- The TRF model successfully captured the transition between different dissolution patterns (face, conical, and wormhole). ([[2022-aboluhom|Aboluhom, 2022, p. 58]])

- The integrated model successfully calculated the impact of 3,000 bbl vs 1,500 bbl treatments on production across 60 scenarios. ([[2022-alsulaiman|AlSulaiman, 2022, p. 62]])

- Simulations showed that optimized horizontal wells yielded higher oil recovery than the thinning base case for high thickening index polymers. ([[2023-alameer|Alameer, 2023, p. 71]])

- The simulation was used to calculate NPV and Recovery Factor (RF) for both GP and SLP paths. ([[2023-alharbi|Alharbi, 2023, p. 60]])

- The simulation showed that VES flooding reduced the water cut by 47.8% compared to traditional waterflooding. ([[2023-ahmed|Ahmed, 2023, p. 215]])
