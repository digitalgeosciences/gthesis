# Biostratigraphy

**Summary**: The branch of stratigraphy that uses fossils to identify and date geological strata.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[1992-el-sahn|El-Sahn, 1992]] applied biostratigraphy using foraminifera to date the succession.

- [[2006-al-enezi|Al-Enezi, 2006]] used micropaleontological techniques to identify foraminiferal species in both recent and Miocene samples.

- [[2012-ghazwani|Ghazwani, 2012]] used micropaleontology to understand depositional environment and anoxia.

- [[2016-malik|Malik, 2016]] used micropaleontology to identify foraminifera and establish biozones.

- [[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016]] applied biostratigraphy to determine the age of the Dam Formation.

- [[2017-amao|Amao, 2017]] used foraminiferal assemblages as indicators of environmental conditions.

- [[2017-adam-2|Adam, 2017]] applied micropaleontology to identify Earlandia foraminifera as a biostratigraphic marker.

- [[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018]] used biostratigraphy based on graptolites, chitinozoans, and foraminifera to determine the age of the Qusaiba Formation.

- [[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022]] used foraminiferal content to establish the biostratigraphy of the Dhruma Formation.

- [[2022-khan|Khan, 2022]] used micropaleontology to identify benthic foraminiferal assemblages as indicators of sea level changes.

- [[2022-al-sadah|Al-Sadah, 2022]] used micropaleontology to identify Borelis melo as a key indicator for Middle Miocene ages.

- [[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024]] performed biostratigraphic analysis to determine the age range of new species.

- [[2024-fahmi|Fahmi, 2024]] used biostratigraphy to determine the age boundaries of the Marrat Formation.

- [[2025-ferreira|Ferreira, 2025]] used micropaleontology to analyze foraminifera in a dataset.

## Key Results and Findings

- A rich assemblage of Nummulitids was used to confirm a Late Eocene-Early Oligocene age. ([[1992-el-sahn|El-Sahn, 1992, p. 13]])

- Identified 47 species from the Gulf and 51 species from the Dam Formation using standard micropaleontological techniques. ([[2006-al-enezi|Al-Enezi, 2006, p. 13]])

- Identified the oldest foraminifera reported from Saudi Arabia in the Hanadir Member. ([[2012-ghazwani|Ghazwani, 2012, p. 37]])

- 35 species belonging to 19 different genera were identified, providing data for sequence stratigraphy. ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 17]])

- The presence of Borelis melo melo assigned the formation a Burdigalian (late early Miocene) age. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 92]])

- Foraminifera are used as proxies for monitoring contemporary changes. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 109]])

- Earlandia foraminifera were found in 6 samples, primarily in LFT 2 lithofacies. ([[2017-adam-2|Adam, 2017, p. 156]])

- Identified three graptolite biozones (convolutus, sedgwickii, and guerichi) and several chitinozoan zones. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 27]])

- The study confirmed an early Bajocian age for the D1 unit based on nannofossil assemblages (Carinolithus superbus, etc.). ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 40]])

- Benthic foraminifera were used to characterize the transition from shallow marine to restricted lagoonal environments. ([[2022-khan|Khan, 2022, p. 57]])

- Borelis melo was observed in both thin section analysis and hand specimen examination, marking a Middle Miocene age. ([[2022-al-sadah|Al-Sadah, 2022, p. 73]])

- Two Turonian biozones were established based on planktonic and agglutinated foraminifera. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 14]])

- Biostratigraphic data provided insights into depositional bathymetry during formation's deposition. ([[2024-fahmi|Fahmi, 2024, p. 53]])

- Few-shot learning with GANs provided enhanced segmentation capabilities for micropaleontological datasets. ([[2025-ferreira|Ferreira, 2025, p. 51]])

- Established a 4-zone biostratigraphic sequence for the Late Cretaceous section. ([[1989-abdou|Abdou, 1989, p. 12]])
