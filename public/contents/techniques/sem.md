# Scanning Electron Microscopy

**Summary**: A technique used to observe and analyze the morphology and composition of materials at the microscopic level.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[1996-jarad|Jarad, 1996]] used SEM observations to determine that phosphorite grains have different concentrations, suggesting a polygenetic origin or poly-diagenetic history.

- [[1996-matter|Matter, 1996]] used SEM with EDS to characterize mineralogy and elemental distribution in phosphorite samples.

- [[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001]] used SEM to observe the authigenic morphology of illite and chlorite cements.

- [[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002]] applied SEM to visualize authigenic cements and grain-sintering features.

## Key Results and Findings

- SEM observations indicated that the phosphorite grains break cleanly from the carbonate matrix, which is an advantage in ore enrichment. ([[1996-jarad|Jarad, 1996, p. 48]])

- The JSM-5800LV system allowed for observation in both low and high vacuum modes, enabling analysis of non-conductive minerals. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 70]])

- Confirmed the authigenic origin of illite as fibrous, lath-like structures and chlorite as delicate platelet crystals. ([[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001, p. 70]])

- SEM images confirmed quartz overgrowths and clay coatings on sand grains. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 17]])

- SEM was used to identify specific minerals like palygorskite in dolomitic green mudstone. ([[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988, p. 17]])

- Used to visualize the structure of MWCNTs as shown in Figure 5.1. ([[2009-al-khaldi|Al-Khaldi, 2009, p. 58]])

- SEM was used to observe resin casts in calcite to identify branching endolith species. ([[2012-amao|Amao, 2012, p. 62]])

- SEM images showed micro porosity evolved around the suture for attached forms like Placopsilina. ([[2012-ghazwani|Ghazwani, 2012, p. 122]])

- SEM images revealed distinct pore diameters in macro rhombic spar calcites and various micrite types. ([[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013, p. 135]])

- SEM analysis confirmed kaolinite as the dominant cementing material in the Sarah Formation. ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 163]])

- Used to visualize the surface of raw, acid-treated, and metal oxide impregnated fly ash. ([[2014-adebayo|Adebayo, 2014, p. 47]])

- SEM images showed variation in presence of microporosity in the studied samples. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 121]])

- SEM analysis provided high-resolution imagery of the sampled minerals and textures. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 16]])

- SEM was used to determine cell size and shape for various isolates including BC1, BC6, and others. ([[2015-oyehan|Oyehan, 2015, p. 49]])

- Used to link diagenetic events to changes in pore-water chemistry. ([[2015-koeshidayatullah|Koeshidayatullah, 2015, p. 27]])

- Used to visualize and characterize the physical structure of the 7 bacterial isolates. ([[2015-malik|Malik, 2015, p. 28]])

- SEM images of the Cbg facies showed dissolved bioclastic fragments. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 51]])

- SEM was used to visualize the dorsal/ventral views and test walls of species like Ammonia parkinsoniana and Murrayinella sp. ([[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015, p. 42]])

- Not stated in source ([[2016-hassan-2|Hassan, 2016]])

- Used as part of the laboratory analysis to investigate lithological heterogeneity. ([[2016-babker|Babker, 2016, p. 12]])

- SEM images confirmed the presence of Globigerina and Miliolids in the wacke-packstone. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 82]])

- SEM images showed the fine grained texture of facies such as Em-w and Fp-g. ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 12]])

- Identified various shapes (angular, irregular, crystalline) and sizes (up to 8 µm) across different locations. ([[2016-lawal-2|Lawal, 2016, p. 73]])

- SEM microphotographs showed high-magnesium calcite cementation on skeletal fragments (f9) and meniscus cement between ooids. ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 89]])

- Identified moldic (mo) and cemented pores, as well as calcite cement types. ([[2016-makkawi|Makkawi, 2016, p. 49]])

- Confirmed the presence of platy micas within the muscovite siltstone lithofacies. ([[2017-mahgoub|Mahgoub, 2017, p. 46]])

- Used in conjunction with optical microscopy to characterize the spatial distribution of Tuwaiq Mountain lithofacies. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 23]])

- Used alongside XRD for detailed mineralogical investigation. ([[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017, p. 15]])

- SEM images revealed test morphological abnormalities at spots where Cymbaloporetta sp. was exclusively present. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 30]])

- Morphological characterization was performed for three isolates (10PY1A, 10PY2B, and 20PY1A) using SEM. ([[2017-budiyanto|Budiyanto, 2017, p. 11]])

- FESEM was used to observe the changes in surface morphology after acid treatment and polyelectrolyte coating. ([[2017-olabemiwo|Olabemiwo, 2017, p. 41]])
