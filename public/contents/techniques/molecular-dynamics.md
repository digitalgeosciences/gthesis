# Molecular Dynamics

**Summary**: A computer simulation method for analyzing the physical movements of atoms and molecules.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017]] applied molecular-dynamics to study the behavior of montmorillonite and rectorite under different hydration levels.

- [[2021-aslanov|Aslanov, 2021]] used molecular dynamics to model the mechanical behavior of kerogen structures.

- [[2023-afagwu|AFAGWU, 2023]] used molecular dynamics to model methane diffusion within realistic kerogen structures under varying pressure and stress conditions.

## Key Results and Findings

- Determined that potassium ions lead to more stable structures with smaller interlayer spacing compared to sodium ions. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 61]])

- The simulation revealed that kerogen exhibits elastomer-like behavior regardless of maturity level. ([[2021-aslanov|Aslanov, 2021, p. 31]])

- The MD simulations allowed for the calculation of self-diffusion coefficients between 1.44 x 10^-9 and 2.90 x 10^-9 m^2/s. ([[2023-afagwu|AFAGWU, 2023, p. 144]])
