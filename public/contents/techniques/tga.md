# TGA

**Summary**: A technique where a sample's mass is measured as it is heated to determine thermal stability and composition.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[1999-iddris|Iddris, 1999]] used TGA to characterize the thermal stability and oxidation behavior of crude oil samples.

- [[2011-al-dossary|Al-Dossary, 2011]] used TGA to analyze the chemical composition and stability of cement samples after exposure to Arab-C water.

- [[2014-adebayo|Adebayo, 2014]] performed TGA to evaluate accuracy of modification process based on weight percentage determination.

- [[2014-alao|Alao, 2014]] used TGA to evaluate the thermal stability and decomposition products of modified VES samples.

- [[2014-azad|Azad, 2014]] used TGA to monitor the thermal stability and decomposition of the VES systems.

- [[2015-musa|Musa, 2015]] used TGA to evaluate the thermal stability of the resins between 20 and 800 °C.

- [[2015-danmaliki|Danmaliki, 2015]] used TGA to determine the thermal stability of the adsorbent.

- [[2016-salawu|Salawu, 2016]] used TGA to determine the weight loss profile of AgNP, Fe2O3, and nZVI as a function of temperature.

- [[2017-mohammed-3|MOHAMMED, 2017]] used TGA to determine the thermal stability and degradation points of various polymers (AMPS, HPAM, Xanthan, HPG, TVP) under high-temperature conditions.

- [[2017-olabemiwo|Olabemiwo, 2017]] used TGA to evaluate changes in sample weight due to temperature increases and determine organic content.

- [[2017-janjua|Janjua, 2017]] used TGA to evaluate the thermal degradation and stability of VES and VES/CA samples.

- [[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017]] used tga to determine the total organic carbon (TOC) content.

- [[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018]] used TGA to assess the thermal properties of the produced SDAC.

- [[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021]] used TGA to determine the thermal stability of the PG material.

- [[2021-lawal|LAWAL, 2021]] used TGA to quantify mass loss and gas evolution during non-isothermal heating.

- [[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022]] used TGA to assess the amount of portlandite consumed in CCB and fly ash systems.

- [[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022]] used TGA to determine the thermal stability and volatile fraction of the shale inhibitors.

- [[2022-xiao|Xiao, 2022]] used tga to distinguish between physical desorption and chemisorption of organic materials on rock surfaces.

- [[2023-baig|Baig, 2023]] used TGA to evaluate the thermal stability of bio-polymers and synthetic copolymers.

- [[2023-khalid|Khalid, 2023]] used TGA to evaluate the polymer thermal resistance property.

- [[2024-abdallah|Abdallah, 2024]] used TGA to assess thermal degradation behavior of HPG and CMHPG.

## Key Results and Findings

- Thermal analysis (TGA) revealed that LTO peaks at 270 °C while HTO peaks at 415 °C. ([[1999-iddris|Iddris, 1999, p. 15]])

- TGA results were used to compare the chemical composition of cement before and after exposure to harsh environments. ([[2011-al-dossary|Al-Dossary, 2011, p. 90]])

- Used to determine the weight percentage of adsorbent materials after various treatment steps. ([[2014-adebayo|Adebayo, 2014, p. 44]])

- CNT-included samples showed the highest thermal endurance with respect to solvent losses at 220°C for divalent ions. ([[2014-alao|Alao, 2014, p. 18]])

- TGA tracks three stages: desolvation, dehydration, and decomposition. ([[2014-azad|Azad, 2014, p. 95]])

- Thermo-gravimetric analysis (TGA) for the resin was employed using a thermal analyzer SDT-Q600, TA instruments, USA. ([[2015-musa|Musa, 2015, p. 43]])

- TGA was used to test the stability of sorbents up to 800°C. ([[2015-danmaliki|Danmaliki, 2015, p. 83]])

- All nanoparticles experienced rapid decrease in weight after 300°C due to the decomposition of organic capping agents. ([[2016-salawu|Salawu, 2016, p. 93]])

- Determined polymer stability in the 200°F to 300°F range typical for high-temperature reservoirs. ([[2017-mohammed-3|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 65]])

- Used to assess the stability and composition of the modified fly ash materials. ([[2017-olabemiwo|Olabemiwo, 2017, p. 47]])

- The temperature at which major change in weight takes place was identified as the upper limit for thermal stability. ([[2017-janjua|Janjua, 2017, p. 98]])

- Determined the thermal stability and organic matter content of the samples. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 76]])

- Adsorbent 2Z-700-90 showed a premium thermal ability of 50%. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 12]])

- The PG material exhibited excellent thermal stability up to more than 200°C. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 49]])

- TGA showed that the mass of the rock sample decreases with an increase in temperature, indicating maturation-driven phase changes. ([[2021-lawal|LAWAL, 2021, p. 87]])

- The weight-loss trend for CCB_20 and FA_20 was similar, indicating that both materials exhibit very similar pozzolanic reactivity. ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 131]])

- TGA revealed that all samples except Nano-Silica were primarily organic/water-based materials with over 95% total weight loss. ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 71]])

- mass loss at different temperatures is related to the mass of the adsorbed component, such as the decomposition of chemisorbed stearic acid. ([[2022-xiao|Xiao, 2022, p. 40]])

- Synthetic copolymer AN 125 showed superior thermal stability compared to Xanthan Gum, which began degrading at 250°C. ([[2023-baig|Baig, 2023, p. 34]])

- The char residue increased from 8.4 to 13.7 wt.% with gradual addition of MOF, indicating improvements in fire retardance. ([[2023-khalid|Khalid, 2023, p. 90]])

- Used to evaluate the thermal stability and weight loss zones of guar-based polymers. ([[2024-abdallah|Abdallah, 2024, p. 37]])
