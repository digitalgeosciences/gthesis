# Rock Physics

**Summary**: The study of the relationship between rock properties and elastic wave propagation.

## Key Results and Findings

- Gassmann's model was found to underestimate saturated velocities in low-porosity carbonate rocks. ([[2012-elhaj|Elhaj, 2012, p. 23]])

- TAVO provides a more robust way to estimate $\beta/\alpha$ and $\Delta\beta/\beta$ compared to standard reflection AVO in certain contexts. ([[2014-kola|Kola, 2014, p. 15]])

- The model accurately predicted the impact of gas, oil, and brine on acoustic impedance and velocity. ([[2016-ali|Ali, 2016, p. 39]])

- The stiff-sand model was identified as the most appropriate transform for the study's clastic reservoir. ([[2016-houssain|Houssain, 2016, p. 17]])

- ANN models provided the highest R2 values for both compressional and shear wave predictions compared to ANFIS and SVM. ([[2016-tariq|Tariq, 2016, p. 70]])

- Established specific correlations for $V_p$, $V_s$, and density to derive Young's modulus and Poisson's ratio. ([[2018-aldossary|AlDossary, 2018, p. 47]])

- Dynamic Poisson's ratio was measured using an ultrasonic add-on. ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019, p. 85]])

- Porosity derived from neural network guided inversion was more reliable than AI-derived porosity. ([[2019-al-dawood|Al-Dawood, 2019, p. 99]])

- The stiff-sand model was selected as it is more rigorous and allows for more parameter adjustment compared to Raymer's model. ([[2020-almumtin|Almumtin, 2020, p. 48]])

- The constant-cement model was identified as the most accurate RPM for both TGS and high-porosity sand datasets. ([[2021-suleymanov|Suleymanov, 2021, p. 55]])

- Established that high EPAR values (moldic/vuggy) correlate with higher velocities at given porosities. ([[2022-salih|Salih, 2022, p. 41]])

- The model successfully predicted a jump in VP at full saturation due to bulk modulus changes. ([[2023-alsahwan|AlSahwan, 2023, p. 29]])

- The stiff-rock model successfully matched both offshore chalk and onshore carbonate data across different geographic locations. ([[2024-alabbad|Alabbad, 2024, p. 53]])

- Rock-fluid interactions are complex and require a range of experimental techniques at multiple scales. ([[2024-isah|Isah, 2024, p. 21]])

- Rock physics models are used to interpret seismic data by relating elastic properties to lithology and pore type. ([[2024-allehyani|allehyani, 2024, p. 36]])

- The Hertz-Mindlin and Gassmann models were integrated to create a $V_p(z, S_w)$ function that accounts for mineralogy and fluid substitution. ([[2025-oyekan|Oyekan, 2025, p. 41]])
