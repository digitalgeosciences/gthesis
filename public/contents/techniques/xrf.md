# X-Ray Fluorescence

**Summary**: A technique used to determine the elemental composition of a sample.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017]] used XRF to characterize the geochemical properties of the Sarah Formation.

- [[1996-matter|Matter, 1996]] applied PIXE to determine the concentration of major and trace elements in rock samples.

## Key Results and Findings

- Used to identify chemical variations in different lithofacies. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 20]])

- Provided high sensitivity for trace elements with a clear distinction between minerals like apatite and gangue. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 90]])

- XRF analysis was performed on cement samples to identify chemical alterations resulting from environmental exposure. ([[2011-al-dossary|Al-Dossary, 2011, p. 90]])

- XRF analysis showed that the rock contained 41.7% Si, 2.02% Al, and 1.91% Fe. ([[2013-elsayed|Elsayed, 2013, p. 53]])

- Confirmed presence of Si, Al, Fe, Ca, K, Mg, and Na. ([[2014-elhag|ELHAG, 2014]])

- Date seeds were found to contain potassium (37.34%) and calcium (29.69%) as major contributors. ([[2014-wajheeuddin|Wajheeuddin, 2014, p. 53]])

- XRF spectroscopy was used to study rock precipitates. ([[2015-adebayo|Adebayo, 2015, p. 16]])

- XRF analysis showed that Nano clay is silicon-aluminum based as it contains higher percentages of silicon and aluminum. ([[2015-thalab|Thalab, 2015, p. 69]])

- XRF was used in related studies to identify metals like Ba, Co, Cr, Mo, Ni, Pb, Rb, Sr, V, Y, Zn, and Zr. ([[2015-jawad|Jawad, 2015, p. 48]])

- The elemental composition was determined for the core plugs using XRF analysis. ([[2015-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2015, p. 66]])

- 47 samples were analyzed under XRF to acquire detailed elemental composition of the Sharawra Formation. ([[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016, p. 52]])

- XRF data provided information on the distribution of elements across different lithofacies. ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 10]])

- Confirmed the presence of major elements (C, O, F, Na, Mg, Al, Si, K, Ca) across all sites. ([[2016-lawal-2|Lawal, 2016, p. 15]])

- Identified minor differences in chemical composition between Sample 1 and Sample 2, leading to a ~1 g/L difference in solubility. ([[2017-mohamed|MOHAMED, 2017, p. 74]])

- Used to evaluate chemostratigraphic responses in mixed systems. ([[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017, p. 41]])

- XRF confirmed that barite contains elements with high electronegativity (e.g., Sulfur at 2.5), affecting its stability. ([[2017-alakbari|Alakbari, 2017, p. 38]])

- RFA had high sulfur (51.44%) but no silicon, while AFA's sulfur increased to 86.25%. ([[2017-olabemiwo|Olabemiwo, 2017, p. 71]])

- XRF confirmed the presence of Ca, O, and C in the sample spectra. ([[2017-sadeed|SADEED, 2017, p. 110]])

- Confirmed 97.14% total concentration of elements in the sample. ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 73]])

- XRF analysis showed the concentration of sulfur to be relatively high (2100-293 ppm; mean 913 ppm) in TMF samples. ([[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017, p. 125]])

- Analysis showed high content of Si, Al, Fe, Ca, and Sr in marlstone samples. ([[2017-adam-2|Adam, 2017, p. 193]])

- The XRF analysis shows little Magnesium (Figure 5.11). ([[2017-yousif|Yousif, 2017, p. 135]])

- Used to provide data for calculating P/Ti and Ba/Al ratios to assess paleoproductivity. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 33]])

- Zinc and Barium were significantly higher in Jubail samples compared to Ras Tanura. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 157]])

- XRF was used to measure the norm. wt. % of elements in both soil types. ([[2019-alhaiqi|Alhaiqi, 2019, p. 43]])

- Used to prove if a treatment solution causes a certain mineral enrichment or impoverishment on the surface. ([[2019-suleiman-3|Suleiman, 2019, p. 48]])

- Determined that kaolinite has high silicon (32%) and aluminum content. ([[2019-gomaa|Gomaa, 2019, p. 104]])

- XRF analysis showed no or undetectable nitrogen content in the rocks. ([[2019-ahmed-2|Ahmed, 2019, p. 53]])

- XRF data provided high-resolution elemental concentration profiles that revealed subtle variations in lithofacies despite apparent homogeneity. ([[2020-gbiyanju|Gbiyanju, 2020, p. 30]])

- Hardness showed a strong positive relationship with calcium (Ca) and a negative relationship with Al, Fe, Si, and S. ([[2021-albensaad|Albensaad, 2021, p. 52]])

- XRF confirmed that laponite has significantly higher silica content than standard Class G cement. ([[2020-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2020, p. 76]])

- XRF data allowed for the conversion of elemental weights into specific carbonate and silicate minerals. ([[2022-khan|Khan, 2022, p. 96]])

- Showed strong correlation between TOC and redox-sensitive elements (Mo, Cu, Ni). ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 156]])

- Used to distinguish two groups of dolomites (fabric preserving and non-fabric preserving). ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 51]])

- The analysis identified distinct chemostratigraphic divisions (C1-C4) that correlated with lithostratigraphic boundaries like the Hawtah/Ulayyah transition. ([[2022-bashri|Bashri, 2022, p. 99]])

- Used to calculate Chemical Index of Alteration (CIA) and plot on A-CN-K ternary diagrams. ([[2022-argadestya|Argadestya, 2022, p. 49]])

- Determined that hematite consists mainly of iron (60.7 wt%). ([[2022-aljaberi|AlJaberi, 2022, p. 57]])

- Used to evaluate sample holder content as a percentage of key components. ([[2023-basfar|Basfar, 2023, p. 45]])

- XRF results showed high concentration of Calcium (Ca) with an average of 51% in Core 1 and 90% in Core 2. ([[2023-al-eid|Al-Eid, 2023, p. 27]])

- The sample showed more than 96% purity (S: 70.64%, Fe: 23.83%). ([[2023-badhafere|Badhafere, 2023, p. 54]])

- Found high concentrations of Si, Mg, Al, and Fe as indicators of hydrothermal alteration. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 81]])

- XRF was performed on different OC samples to measure the elemental composition and chemical characteristics. ([[2023-al-dogail|AL-DOGAIL, 2023, p. 105]])

- Determined chemical oxides for all collected materials including barite, hematite, ilmenite, Micromax, laponite, perlite, and vermiculite. ([[2023-ahmed-2|AHMED, 2023, p. 91]])

- XRF revealed high sulfur (S) and iron (Fe) concentrations in the Qusaiba Member. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 54]])

- XRF confirmed that hematite has a high iron amount (around 95%) and FFA contains significant amounts of SiO2 and Al2O3. ([[2023-abdelaal|Abdelaal, 2023, p. 53]])

- XRF analysis showed HAP concentration was highest at the surface, decreasing toward the interior beyond 5 mm. ([[2023-desouky|Desouky, 2023, p. 40]])

- Confirmed that the host rock matrix of unit 1 consisted of 100% calcite. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 42]])

- XRF results agreed with mineralogy, providing a good proxy for mineral composition. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 14]])

- XRF showed a reduction in multivalent ions Ca2+ and Al3+ after exposure to carbonated water. ([[2024-suleiman-3|Suleiman, 2024, p. 61]])

- XRF was included as a measurement to assess the impact on the samples post-treatment. ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024, p. 13]])

- Determined that magnesium-rich basalt provides higher carbonation capacity. ([[2025-alghawi|Alghawi, 2025, p. 32]])

- XRF confirmed that Material U had significantly higher calcium content than Material L, potentially explaining differences in hydraulic performance. ([[2026-fatima|Fatima, 2026, p. 109]])
