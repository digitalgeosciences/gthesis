# X-Ray Fluorescence

**Summary**: A technique for determining the elemental composition of materials by inducing X-ray emission.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[1996-matter|Matter, 1996]] utilized micro-PIXE to determine trace element concentrations in phosphorite samples.

- [[2012-ghazwani|Ghazwani, 2012]] used Fe-S-C analysis (based on XRF data) to determine redox conditions.

- [[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016]] used XRF on 47 samples to determine the chemical composition and lateral/vertical variations in elements across the formation.

- [[2016-malik|Malik, 2016]] applied XRF to analyze the distribution of elements across different lithofacies.

- [[2016-lawal-2|Lawal, 2016]] used XRF to confirm the elemental compositions of PM samples across different sites.

- [[2016-radwan|Radwan, 2016]] applied XRF to measure trace element concentrations in carbonate samples.

- [[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017]] used XRF to analyze major and minor elements in the Dam Formation.

- [[2017-amao|Amao, 2017]] applied EDX analysis to characterize the chemical composition of internal linings in foraminiferal tests.

- [[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017]] applied XRF to identify major and trace elements for mineralogical characterization.

- [[2017-olabemiwo|Olabemiwo, 2017]] used XRF to determine the elemental composition of RFA, AFA, and PE-FA.

- [[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017]] used XRF to identify siliciclastic input and chemical signatures in the TMF.

- [[2017-adam|Adam, 2017]] used XRF to characterize the mineralogy and chemical composition of marlstone lithofacies (LFT 7).

- [[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018]] applied XRF to analyze major and trace elements in the Qusaiba Shale.

- [[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019]] applied XRF to determine the elemental composition of PM10 samples.

- [[2019-alhaiqi|Alhaiqi, 2019]] used XRF analysis to assess the purity and quality of sandy and limy soils.

- [[2019-ahmed|Ahmed, 2019]] used XRF to determine elemental concentrations in a Wasia outcrop.

- [[2020-islam|Islam, 2020]] used XRF to analyze 31 elements and their oxides in three sections of the Sarah Formation.

- [[2020-gbiyanju|Gbiyanju, 2020]] used XRF to obtain chemical data for chemostratigraphy and rock typing.

- [[2021-ahmed|Ahmed, 2021]] applied XRF on powder samples and thin sections to map chemical elements like Ca, Mg, Fe, Si, Al, K, and S.

- [[2021-albensaad|Albensaad, 2021]] used XRF on 25 samples to determine chemical composition and correlate it with mechanical properties.

- [[2022-khan|Khan, 2022]] applied XRF analysis to obtain elemental weight percentages for core samples BR1, BR2, and BR3.

- [[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022]] applied XRF to analyze chemical elements and redox-sensitive metals in samples.

- [[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022]] applied XRF to analyze the chemical signatures of dolomite samples in the Wadi Malham section.

## Key Results and Findings

- Micro-PIXE identified elements such as V, Cr, Fe, Ni, Cu, Zn, Hg, As, U, Sr, and Y with high precision. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 64]])

- Analysis showed most samples were deposited under oxic conditions, with some Hanadir samples in anoxic-dysoxic ranges. ([[2012-ghazwani|Ghazwani, 2012, p. 120]])

- XRF provided a detailed elemental analysis of both shale and sandstone units to identify chemical trends. ([[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016, p. 52]])

- XRF data showed the distribution of elements for each identified lithofacies (e.g., Cgdp, Esp, Og). ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 10]])

- The elemental composition showed the presence of carbon (C), oxygen (O), fluorine (F), sodium (Na), magnesium (Mg), aluminum (Al), silicon (Si), potassium (K) and calcium (Ca). ([[2016-lawal-2|Lawal, 2016, p. 15]])

- Identified differences in Na and Fe concentrations between the middle (stagnant) and upper (circulating) zones. ([[2016-radwan|Radwan, 2016, p. 76]])

- Showed a fair to good correlation between SGR (K%) and XRF (K2O%). ([[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017, p. 11]])

- The inner lining showed significant amounts of Calcium (approx. 20% by weight). ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 72]])

- Identified Si, Al, Fe, K, S, Ca, Ti, Mn, P, Mg as major elements and Cr, Sr, V, Zn, Zr, Ni, Ba as trace elements. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 110]])

- Table 4.1 shows elements like Silicon (0.76% in AFA) and Sulphur (86.25% in AFA). ([[2017-olabemiwo|Olabemiwo, 2017, p. 71]])

- XRF results showed a signature of siliciclastic input, possibly from tidal channels or windblown dust. ([[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017, p. 20]])

- Elemental composition was determined for selected samples of the marlstone lithofacies type (LFT 7). ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 10]])

- Identified major element oxide distributions and trace element concentrations to derive environmental proxies. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 75]])

- Quantified chemical elements within the particulate matter collected from industrial and coastal sites. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 133]])

- Used to confirm soil characteristics before spiking with contaminants. ([[2019-alhaiqi|Alhaiqi, 2019, p. 44]])

- X-ray fluorescence of the elemental data concentrations [in %] from a Wasia outcrop. ([[2019-ahmed|Ahmed, 2019, p. 11]])

- Si and SiO2 were found as the highest components (89% and 80%) across all samples. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 84]])

- Successfully utilized XRF spectra raw data to estimate grain size and perform rock typing. ([[2020-gbiyanju|Gbiyanju, 2020, p. 210]])

- XRF showed high calcium abundance (up to 90.21%) and significant iron presence due to diagenetic effects. ([[2021-ahmed|Ahmed, 2021, p. 70]])

- Hardness showed a strong positive relationship with calcite from XRF, while showing negative relationships with Al, Fe, Si, and S. ([[2021-albensaad|Albensaad, 2021, p. 15]])

- Provided data on element weights which were then converted to mineral equivalents (MgCO3, CaCO3, SiO2). ([[2022-khan|Khan, 2022, p. 94]])

- Showed strong correlation between TOC and Mo, Cu, Ni across all wells. ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 19]])

- FP dolomites showed high Ca intensity (89.23 count/sec) and low Mg intensity (5.52 count/sec). ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 83]])

- Quantified elemental and oxide content to compare with Martian rover data. ([[2022-argadestya|Argadestya, 2022, p. 63]])

- Not stated in source ([[2023-al-eid|Al-Eid, 2023]])

- Identified major elements (Si, Mg, Al, Fe) and trace elements (Cl, K, Ca, Na). ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 81]])

- Used in conjunction with XRD for mineral and chemical analysis. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 35]])

- Low concentrations of iron and manganese indicated no exposure to meteoric diagenesis. ([[2024-allam|Allam, 2024, p. 11]])

- XRF data were presented as well-log-like displays to show geochemical variations across units. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 48]])

- Not stated in source ([[2024-alsadah|Alsadah, 2024]])

- Confirmed that unit 1's host rock matrix was 100% calcite while its burrow filling contained significant amounts of quartz and palygorskite. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 42]])

- Used to identify and measure specific heavy metals (HMs) within selected building environments. ([[2024-alenazi|Alenazi, 2024]])

- XRF results agreed with mineralogy, providing a good proxy for mineral composition. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 14]])

- Identified key elements involved in CO2 interaction. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 20]])

- XRF analysis was used to identify mineral content before and after reaction. ([[2025-alghawi|Alghawi, 2025, p. 47]])
