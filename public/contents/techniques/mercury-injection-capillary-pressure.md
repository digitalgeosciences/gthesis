# Mercury Injection Capillary Pressure

**Summary**: A laboratory method used to measure the pressure required to force mercury into a rock plug to determine pore throat sizes and capillary pressure.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010]] used mercury-_injection_ to characterize capillary pressure and pore geometry.

- [[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014]] used MICP to determine the impact of pore geometry on permeability.

- [[2020-al-zoukani|Al-Zoukani, 2020]] applied MICP to characterize pore throat size distribution (PTSD) and identify reservoir rock types.

- [[2024-yusmananto|Yusmananto, 2024]] used mercury-injection-capillary-pressure to characterize limestone textures in the UKM.

## Key Results and Findings

- Correcting MICP data for reservoir conditions (temperature, pressure, and clay-bound water) is necessary before using it in saturation-height models. ([[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010, p. 193]])

- Used to detect micro-heterogeneity within the five paleovalleys. ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 16]])

- MICP results revealed that permeability is very low in muddy texture samples, with pore-throat sizes mostly below 5 microns. ([[2020-al-zoukani|Al-Zoukani, 2020, p. 23]])

- MICP analysis was conducted on various limestone textures associated with the Facies Association scheme. ([[2024-yusmananto|Yusmananto, 2024, p. 98]])
