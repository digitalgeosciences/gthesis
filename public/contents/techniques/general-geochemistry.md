# General Geochemistry

**Summary**: The study of the chemical composition of rocks and minerals.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[1995-al-jaziri|Al-Jaziri, 1995]] Used to analyze the chemistry of the oil and water phases.

- [[1996-matter|Matter, 1996]] performed geochemical analyses to determine trace element concentrations.

- [[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016]] performed geochemical studies to determine source area and mineralogy.

- [[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016]] used ICP analysis to analyze effluents from core flooding experiments.

- [[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017]] applied geochemical analysis to identify lithofacies and reservoir zonation.

- [[2017-mahrous|Mahrous, 2017]] applied geochemistry to model the interaction between HCl acid, carbonate rock, and dissolved CO2.

- [[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019]] applied chemical-analysis to determine concentrations of TPH and PAHs in sediment and plant tissues.

- [[2021-altowilib|Altowilib, 2021]] performed geochemical analysis on water samples to determine concentration of anions, cations, TDS, and pH.

- [[2022-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2022]] performed chemical analysis on soil from two dumpsites to assess heavy metal content, TOC, pH, and EC.

- [[2023-al-eid|Al-Eid, 2023]] conducted geochemistry to evaluate hydrocarbon loss in core samples.

- [[2023-al-aqeel|Al-Aqeel, 2023]] applied chemical-analysis to monitor COD and TOC levels during treatment.

- [[2024-alenazi|Alenazi, 2024]] used chemical-analysis to quantify heavy metal concentrations in AC filters.

- [[2024-al-drwish|AL-DRWISH, 2024]] analyzed the impact of GLDA and CI on surfactant performance.

## Key Results and Findings

- Not stated in source ([[1995-al-jaziri|Al-Jaziri, 1995]])

- Uranium and Vanadium levels were found to be high enough to justify economic exploitation. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 26]])

- Major and trace elements geochemistry indicate a passive continental margin as the tectonic setting. ([[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016, p. 19]])

- Used to monitor the chemical composition of spent acids and identify potential precipitates. ([[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016, p. 58]])

- Geochemical analysis showed a 15% increase in U readings attributed to radioactive evaporites. ([[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017, p. 137]])

- Accounting for full chemical speciation (including H2CO3, HCO3-, and CO3-2) is necessary because CO2 solubility in acidic solutions is limited. ([[2017-mahrous|Mahrous, 2017, p. 20]])

- Two and three ring PAHs were abundant in plant samples while high molecular PAHs were considerably higher in sediment. ([[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019, p. 115]])

- Formation water had higher salinity, polarity, and water hardness than seawater; seawater had higher sulfate ions and pH. ([[2021-altowilib|Altowilib, 2021, p. 49]])

- Heavy metal concentration was within normal ranges compared to EPA standards, but electrical conductivity (EC) was significantly higher than normal levels (2000 S/cm vs 1000 S/cm). ([[2022-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2022, p. 56]])

- S1 values dropped by 56% to 87% in unpreserved high-maturity cores. ([[2023-al-eid|Al-Eid, 2023, p. 91]])

- Initial COD was 670 mg/L and TOC was 251 mg/L for the waste stream. ([[2023-al-aqeel|Al-Aqeel, 2023, p. 39]])

- Concentrations of HMs like Zn, Cu, and Pb were found to be significantly higher than crustal background values across all study sites. ([[2024-alenazi|Alenazi, 2024, p. 67]])

- Corrosion inhibitors (CI) had a major impact on foam viscosity, reducing it by 57% for Duomeen TTM. ([[2024-al-drwish|AL-DRWISH, 2024, p. 140]])

- The adsorption of surfactant in the presence of alkali was lower than that without alkali. ([[1994-eme|Eme, 1994, p. 24]])
