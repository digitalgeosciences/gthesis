# Sequence Stratigraphy

**Summary**: The study of rock units in terms of their position in time and space.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- The succession represents a shallowing upward, carbonate cycle. ([[1992-el-sahn|El-Sahn, 1992, p. 99]])

- Two major shallowing-upward cycles were identified: one from Sulaiy to Yamama, and another from Buwaib/Biyadh to Shu'aiba. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 15]])

- A sequence stratigraphic framework allows for the identification of reservoir-wide baffles and distinct fluid zones. ([[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010, p. 45]])

- Defined two composite sequences (LFC1 and LFC2) each containing four high-frequency sequences. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 13]])

- A high-resolution framework was built for Arab-C carbonates and associated sedimentary models were generated. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 12]])

- The nine zones represent different phases of delta development (e.g., highstand, falling stage, transgressive). ([[2013-atig|atig, 2013, p. 46]])

- Identified 4 types of HFSs in the outcrop. ([[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013, p. 79]])

- Three HFSs were identified, showing a long-term, shallowing-upward trend after initial transgression. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 103]])

- A significant lithological break between the Saq Formation and Cruziana Series indicates a change in facies or hiatus. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 59]])

- Parasequences (likely 5th order) were identified as the smallest depositional packages. ([[2015-koeshidayatullah|Koeshidayatullah, 2015, p. 19]])

- Identified high-frequency sequences and cycles within the Ullayah Reservoir. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 65]])

- Identified three sequences bounded by four erosional unconformities (SB1-SB4). ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 17]])

- Four sequences were defined based on cyclicity and stacking patterns, with some subdivided into High Frequency Sequences. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 14]])

- Two sequence boundaries characterized by subaerial exposure were delineated. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 15]])

- Identified three sequence orders: second order super sequence, second order, and third order. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 17]])

- Three composite sequences were determined along with 2 sequence boundaries. ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 17]])

- Three fourth-order, shallowing-upward sequences were identified. ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 17]])

- Five medium scales of depositional cycles were identified, each corresponding to a specific stratigraphic level. ([[2016-makkawi|Makkawi, 2016, p. 123]])

- Defined three to five composite sequences across different outcrops, identifying TST and HST components. ([[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017, p. 12]])

- The high-resolution stratigraphy is likely to be a good analog for the Khuff reservoirs in Eastern Arabia. ([[2017-adam-2|Adam, 2017, p. 32]])

- The Abu Gabra Formation is divided into five 3rd order sequences. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 24]])

- Organic-rich intervals are primarily associated with the Transgressive System Tract (TST). ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 25]])

- Fischer plot showed facies partitioning and a similar pattern to the global sea level curve. ([[2022-bashri|Bashri, 2022, p. 72]])

- The Dhaiqa Basin was divided into 194 smaller cycles covering a total depositional thickness of 403.5m. ([[2024-malik|Malik, 2024, p. 68]])

- The model successfully predicted sequence architecture at the third stratigraphic order. ([[2024-fahmi|Fahmi, 2024, p. 21]])

- Identified transgressive-regressive (T-R) cycles characterized by deepening and shallowing upward sequences. ([[2024-jalloh|Jalloh, 2024, p. 15]])

- Identified several stratigraphic phenomena including unconformities at the Wasi'a/Aruma contact. ([[1989-abdou|Abdou, 1989, p. 16]])

- LST units were found to have higher Young's modulus and Poisson's ratio compared to TST units. ([[2002-elhassan|ElHassan, 2002, p. 93]])
