# Total Organic Carbon Analysis

**Summary**: A method to measure the total amount of carbon in a sample as carbon dioxide.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011]] used TOC analysis to determine organic content in blended water samples.

- [[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016]] used TOC analysis to determine source rock potential of shale interbeds.

- [[2023-afagwu|AFAGWU, 2023]] performed TOC analysis to determine the extent of carbon compounds in clay samples.

- [[2024-almutawa|AlMutawa, 2024]] used TOC analysis to monitor the reduction of organic carbon over a four-week period under varying conditions.

## Key Results and Findings

- TOC levels were found to be very low, ranging from 0.056 to 0.098 ppm. ([[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011, p. 52]])

- 15 shale samples were analyzed to acquire the TOC results for the study's only organic geochemistry component. ([[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016, p. 55]])

- Determined that clay samples with high purity had significant mineral content but required assessment for micropore impact from organics. ([[2023-afagwu|AFAGWU, 2023, p. 64]])

- A 38% average reduction in TOC was achieved using indigenous bacterial strains. ([[2024-almutawa|AlMutawa, 2024, p. 12]])
