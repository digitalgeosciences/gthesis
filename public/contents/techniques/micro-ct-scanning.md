# Micro Ct Scanning

**Summary**: A non-destructive imaging technique to visualize internal pore structure and mineral distribution.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016]] used micro-ct-scanning to visualize the interior structure of four pairs of subsurface shale plugs.

- [[2020-mansouri|Mansouri, 2020]] used Micro-CT scans of basalt vesicles to analyze pore space connectivity and volume.

- [[2022-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2022]] used micro-CT scanning to visualize the pore systems in F5 and F6 facies.

- [[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023]] used micro-ct-scanning to understand the impact of creating wormholes on rock properties.

- [[2023-ahmed|Ahmed, 2023]] utilized micro-ct-scanning to observe multi-phase flow and pore connectivity.

- [[2025-refaat|Refaat, 2025]] used micro-ct-scanning to characterize the 3D pore architecture of burrow fillings.

## Key Results and Findings

- Limited by software availability for quantification of pores and minerals. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 47]])

- Confirmed that the aquifer has two nearly distinct zones based on vesicle connectivity and size. ([[2020-mansouri|Mansouri, 2020, p. 13]])

- Revealed that the pore connectivity in F6 was better than that of F5. ([[2022-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2022, p. 93]])

- Used to observe wormhole geometry and distribution in limestone samples. ([[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023, p. 36]])

- Micro-CT scanning revealed that higher degrees of oil-wetness provide greater additional recovery from gas injection due to the connectivity of the oil phase. ([[2023-ahmed|Ahmed, 2023, p. 46]])

- Micro-CT scans allowed for the differentiation of rock components based on density contrast, where carbonate allochems appeared reddish, mud matrix as whitish, and pores as blue. ([[2025-refaat|Refaat, 2025, p. 69]])
