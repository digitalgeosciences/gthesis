# Micro CT Scanning

**Summary**: A non-destructive imaging technique used to visualize pore-scale features and fluid distribution in rock samples.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2017-ahmed-3|Ahmed, 2017]] used micro-CT scan results to determine in-situ wettibility of the aged rocks.

- [[2022-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2022]] used micro-CT scanning to visualize the 3D pore system in fine-grained facies.

- [[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023]] used medical-CT-scanning to visualize the structure of the rock and quantify wormhole volume.

- [[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024]] used micro-ct-scanning to visualize the morphology and calculate the volume of wormholes in limestone samples.

- [[2024-isah|Isah, 2024]] applied micro-CT to obtain 3D pore-scale visualization of the pore network in tight rocks.

## Key Results and Findings

- Micro-CT scan results indicated a water-wet in-situ wettibility for the two weeks aged rock. ([[2017-ahmed-3|Ahmed, 2017, p. 18]])

- Revealed that F6 had a better pore system than F5, with a higher volume of pores (0.03 vs 0.02). ([[2022-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2022, p. 65]])

- Medical CT scans revealed that wormholes become more branched as temperature increases. ([[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023, p. 99]])

- Micro-CT scans with a resolution of 49 μm revealed that 2000 psi experiments produced more complex, larger wormhole structures compared to other pressures. ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 83]])

- Used for identifying dissolution and precipitation in the fluid-rock system after CO2 exposure. ([[2024-isah|Isah, 2024, p. 59]])
