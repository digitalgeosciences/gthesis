# Refraction Seismics

**Summary**: A geophysical method used to determine the depth and velocity of subsurface layers based on seismic wave travel times.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000]] applied seismic-refraction to 6 locations in the Dammam area to determine thickness and velocities of subsurface layers.

## Key Results and Findings

- Used to map the boundaries of an aquifer and identify lithological changes. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 39]])

- First arrival errors were less than 5% after applying the supervirtual method. ([[2016-edigbue|Edigbue, 2016, p. 51]])

- First arrival traveltime is essential for both reflection and refraction data processing. ([[2021-almashhor|AlMashhor, 2021, p. 20]])

- The SR method reached a maximum depth of 60m on Profile 1, identifying high-velocity bedrock zones. ([[2023-edigbue|Edigbue, 2023, p. 74]])

- Accurate first arrival picking is critical for determining the velocity and thickness of near-surface layers. ([[2023-bargees|Bargees, 2023, p. 17]])
