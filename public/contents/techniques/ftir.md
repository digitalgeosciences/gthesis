# Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy

**Summary**: A technique used to identify chemical bonds and functional groups in a sample.

## Key Results and Findings

- FTIR was used to confirm the successful synthesis of CPZDA, HCDAP, and HCPZA by identifying characteristic peaks. ([[2015-musa|Musa, 2015, p. 11]])

- FTIR spectra were compared across different treatment temperatures (30, 60, and 90°C). ([[2015-danmaliki|Danmaliki, 2015, p. 84]])

- FTIR spectrum of the synthesized graphene oxide was obtained (Figure 4.22). ([[2015-hassan|Hassan, 2015, p. 104]])

- FT-IR spectra confirmed the presence of specific functional groups on AgNP, Fe2O3, and nZVI. ([[2016-salawu|Salawu, 2016, p. 75]])

- FTIR was used to confirm the presence of polyelectrolytes on the surface of the fly ash. ([[2017-olabemiwo|Olabemiwo, 2017, p. 52]])

- FTIR identified hydroxy groups, carboxylates, and aliphatic -CH3 as the key functional groups for adsorption. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 12]])

- Characterization results showed that graphene was successfully functionalized for both adsorbents. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 15]])
