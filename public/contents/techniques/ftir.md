# Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy

**Summary**: A technique used to identify functional groups in a material based on their infrared absorption spectra.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-musa|Musa, 2015]] used FT-IR to identify the functional groups within the materials and confirm chemical structure.

- [[2015-he|He, 2015]] used FTIR to analyze the chemical composition of demulsifiers and their interaction with oil/water systems.

- [[2015-danmaliki|Danmaliki, 2015]] used FT-IR to determine the functional groups present in the synthesized materials.

- [[2015-hassan|Hassan, 2015]] used FTIR to determine the type of functional groups on graphene oxide.

- [[2016-salawu|Salawu, 2016]] used FT-IR to identify the chemical bonds and functional groups on the nanoparticle surfaces.

- [[2016-geri|GERI, 2016]] used FTIR to evaluate the surface functional groups of barite before and after dissolution.

- [[2017-mohammed-3|MOHAMMED, 2017]] used FTIR to investigate the thickening-breaking mechanism and the effect of pH/temperature on polymer functionality.

- [[2017-alakbari|Alakbari, 2017]] used FTIR to identify the functional groups of nanosilica and nanoclay.

- [[2017-olabemiwo|Olabemiwo, 2017]] used FTIR to ascertain the functional groups present on the fly ash surface.

- [[2017-janjua|Janjua, 2017]] used FTIR to monitor structural changes in the VES during long-term aging at high temperatures.

- [[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018]] used FTIR to identify the functional groups of the adsorbent.

- [[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019]] used FTIR to determine the presence of functional groups on GPDADMAC and GPHM.

- [[2019-mohammad|Mohammad, 2019]] used FT-IR to confirm the successful chemical modification of xanthan gum by identifying new peaks from acrylic acid.

- [[2020-gowida|Gowida, 2020]] applied FTIR analysis to investigate the chemical changes in toluene after exposure to different consolidated samples.

- [[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021]] used FTIR to identify the functional groups of PG before and after adsorption.

- [[2021-siddiq|Siddiq, 2021]] used FTIR to identify surface functional groups in raw and modified biochar.

- [[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022]] used FTIR to determine the optimal calcination temperature for kaolinitic shale.

- [[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022]] used FT-IR to determine the chemical composition of the inhibitors.

- [[2022-xiao|Xiao, 2022]] used ftir to identify the specific types of adsorption (e.g., carbonate-specific interactions) on rock surfaces.

- [[2023-suleiman|Suleiman, 2023]] used FTIR to examine the functional groups contained in asphaltene samples and treatment fluids.

## Key Results and Findings

- FT-IR spectroscopy was used as each material has a particular fingerprint based on the functional group within the material to identify it. ([[2015-musa|Musa, 2015, p. 45]])

- Analysis was performed using FTIR spectrum to evaluate various demulsifiers including alkylphenol-formaldehyde resins. ([[2015-he|He, 2015, p. 29]])

- FT-IR was used to confirm the presence of acidic and basic functional groups after acid/base treatment. ([[2015-danmaliki|Danmaliki, 2015, p. 77]])

- FTIR showed a broad absorption peak at 3444 cm-1 corresponding to -OH group. ([[2015-hassan|Hassan, 2015, p. 119]])

- Bands at 3400 cm-1 (O-H) and 1637 cm-1 (C=O) were identified as indicators of organic capping agents from plant extracts. ([[2016-salawu|Salawu, 2016, p. 95]])

- Identified changes in surface chemistry during the treatment process. ([[2016-geri|GERI, 2016, p. 57]])

- Monitored functional group changes in GLDA solutions at different pH levels (4 and 12). ([[2017-mohammed-3|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 65]])

- Nanosilica showed peaks at 518.8 (Si-O-Si), 1541 (c-H), 1716.6 (C=O), and 2360.8 (CO2). ([[2017-alakbari|Alakbari, 2017, p. 43]])

- Confirmed the presence of specific chemical groups after acid treatment and polyelectrolyte coating. ([[2017-olabemiwo|Olabemiwo, 2017, p. 48]])

- Structural changes were identified in both spectra, particularly around 3300 cm-1 (OH group) and 1050-1150 cm-1 (O-C bond). ([[2017-janjua|Janjua, 2017, p. 140]])

- FTIR suggested hydroxyl group, carboxylates and aliphatic -CH3 as the probable interplaying functional groups. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 12]])

- Characterization results showed that graphene was successfully functionalized for both adsorbents. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 15]])

- The modified xanthan gum showed a new peak at 1,645 cm⁻¹ (C=C) not present in standard xanthan. ([[2019-mohammad|Mohammad, 2019, p. 60]])

- FTIR confirmed that Sample 5 had insufficient asphaltene-to-coke conversion due to its shorter oxidation time. ([[2020-gowida|Gowida, 2020, p. 68]])

- The characteristic bands (e.g., 3400 cm-1 for O-H, 2950 cm-1 for C-H) remained consistent after use, confirming structural stability. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 50]])

- FTIR was used to determine the change in surface-functional groups after adsorption experiments. ([[2021-siddiq|Siddiq, 2021, p. 12]])

- The study showed that heating the powder at 1562°F yields a highly amorphous material as confirmed by XRD and FTIR. ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 21]])

- Used to identify interaction, local environment, and configuration of the shale inhibitors. ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 44]])

- ATR-FTIR confirmed that fibers were primarily composed of Polyethylene terephthalate (PET). ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 61]])

- The changes in peaks before and after EOR treatment indicate the mechanism and efficacy of wettibility alteration. ([[2022-xiao|Xiao, 2022, p. 39]])

- FTIR was utilized for material characterization of both the asphaltenes and the sacrificial fluids. ([[2023-suleiman|Suleiman, 2023, p. 42]])

- Confirmed the presence of urethane and urea groups with peaks at 2919, 1624, 1247, 1144, and 1001 cm-1. ([[2023-khalid|Khalid, 2023, p. 66]])

- Used to evaluate changes in polymer chemistry during thermochemical treatment. ([[2024-abdallah|Abdallah, 2024, p. 20]])
