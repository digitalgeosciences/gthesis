## Key Results and Findings

- All programs for normalization, transformation of input data, and training were implemented in MATLAB. ([[2001-sultan|SULTAN, 2001, p. 137]])

- The software allowed for the monitoring of training, validation, and testing sets simultaneously. ([[2004-ayoub|AYOUB, 2004, p. 66]])

- The Matlab-based programs successfully solved multi-variable equations to determine a, m, and n simultaneously. ([[2009-al-gathe|AL-GATHE, 2009, p. 92]])

- The MATLAB implementation allowed for the automated calculation of semblance maps across multiple velocity iterations. ([[2009-al-lehyani|Al-Lehyani, 2009, p. 20]])

- Used to implement the neural network architecture and perform statistical analysis on 4031 data points. ([[2009-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2009, p. 30]])

- The Huthali method uses a MATLAB-based SQP procedure to minimize arrival time residuals. ([[2010-mohammed|Mohammed, 2010, p. 67]])

- Matlab was used to implement various backpropagation functions and kernel selections for AI tools. ([[2011-al-dhufairi|AL-DHUFAIRI, 2011, p. 27]])

- The MATLAB implementation successfully integrated the flow chart logic including iterative updates for porosity and permeability based on mineral volume fraction. ([[2013-kareem|Kareem, 2013, p. 75]])

- CMA-ES and DE were implemented in MATLAB as objective function optimizers to determine optimal well locations and cycle ratios. ([[2013-sibaweihi|Sibaweihi, 2013, p. 14]])

- Three specific codes (main, PSO, and DSE) were developed in Matlab. ([[2013-khamis|Khamis, 2013, p. 105]])

- The numerical solution of the model was found to be stable and consistent with established models in cases without wettibility alteration. ([[2013-al-mutairi|AL-MUTAIRI, 2013, p. 20]])

- Used to perform the optimization of parameters including concentration and well placement. ([[2013-ahmed|AHMED, 2013, p. 20]])

- The DE algorithm was coded and coupled with the formulated problem in MATLAB. ([[2014-ahmed|Ahmed, 2014, p. 25]])

- The Matlab function was written to calculate the Bouguer gravity anomaly for the defined models. ([[2014-saeed|Saeed, 2014, p. 58]])

- MATLAB was used to calculate RMSE, MAE, and MAPE for both models. ([[2014-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2014, p. 137]])

- Not stated in source ([[2015-adeniji|Adeniji, 2015]])

- The software successfully modeled pressure distributions at various time steps (0.5, 20 days) and wellbore pressures. ([[2016-david|DAVID, 2016, p. 80]])

- Matlab was used to develop three specific codes: a main code, a PSO code, and a DSE function code including the Penalty approach. ([[2017-al-rubaii|AL-RUBAII, 2017, p. 156]])

- The model successfully simulated the diversion effect where the injection rate ratio stabilized at 3.5. ([[2019-alawi|ALAWI, 2019, p. 39]])

- All models were developed using MATLAB's specialized toolboxes for neural networks and fuzzy logic. ([[2021-aldhaif|AlDhaif, 2021, p. 45]])

- Not stated in source (mentioned in techniques list). ([[2022-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2022]])

- The TRF model is equipped with a friendly user interface in MATLAB, allowing for easy control and report exportation. ([[2022-aboluhom|Aboluhom, 2022, p. 54]])

- The 2D model was set in MATLAB with a 300x300 grid block size. ([[2022-alkathim|Alkathim, 2022, p. 59]])

- The energy balance was solved numerically in MATLAB to find temperature at multiple time steps. ([[2022-alnasser|Alnasser, 2022, p. 56]])
