## Key Results and Findings

- Determined that transverse fractures are less effective in high permeability formations. ([[1993-kandil|Kandil, 1993, p. 73]])

- Determined that the square-root method can be used for multi-fracture systems under specific conditions. ([[1990-mimoune|Mimoune, 1990, p. 15]])

- Fracture permeability is much higher than matrix permeability, which dictates flow behavior. ([[2009-bajaalah|BAJAALAH, 2009, p. 17]])

- Fracture characterization was performed across multiple scales using gravity, seismic, satellite, and well data. ([[2012-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2012, p. 56]])

- HFVW outperformed other wells in low permeability zones, likely due to enhanced connectivity from fracturing. ([[2012-kamal|Kamal, 2012, p. 95]])

- Intersecting natural fractures has a negative impact on well performance due to excessive water production. ([[2014-al-julaih|Al-Julaih, 2014, p. 80]])

- Fracture density was higher in Lithofacies-III compared to other lithofacies. ([[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015, p. 59]])

- Fracture intensity was found inversely correlated with bed thickness and directly related to fracture spacing. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 39]])

- Fracture conductivity above 2000 md-ft showed negligible gains in gas production but increased costs significantly. ([[2015-rammay|RAMMAY, 2015, p. 84]])

- Fractures were found to be mostly nonconductive (cemented or filled with sand/clay gouge) in the Unayzah-A reservoir. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 32]])

- Three types of fracture modes (opening, sliding, shear) were identified, with only Mode 1 providing improved reservoir quality. ([[2016-babker|Babker, 2016, p. 76]])

- Fracture density is higher in fissile shale and mudstone than in sandstone units. ([[2017-mahgoub|Mahgoub, 2017, p. 19]])

- Fractures are primarily vertical to sub-vertical, Mode 1 type (joints), often open or filled with calcite/iron oxide. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 18]])

- Crosslinked Gels produced fractures with large widths (up to 4 units) and long heights. ([[2017-muqtadir|Muqtadir, 2017, p. 175]])

- Fracture half-length and height have a 1:1 ratio for production increase in both Well A and Well C. ([[2018-aleid|Aleid, 2018, p. 118]])

- Conductivity is governed by rock hardness, etching pattern, and magnitude of closure stress. ([[2019-suleiman-3|Suleiman, 2019, p. 17]])

- Natural fracture spacing (10, 40, 60 ft) significantly impacted the hydraulic fracture length. ([[2022-alsulaiman|AlSulaiman, 2022, p. 51]])

- Analysis showed that GLDA provides a viable alternative for creating durable pathways in carbonate rocks. ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023, p. 43]])

- High-permeability zones are identified by the Fault Fracture Density (FFD) method. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 49]])

- Meluhu Formation exhibited the highest fracture density among studied units. ([[2023-arrofi|Arrofi, 2023, p. 37]])

- DAP treatment mitigated the deformability caused by acid etching, leading to increased fracture stiffness. ([[2023-desouky|Desouky, 2023, p. 115]])

- Fractures in unit 1 are primarily controlled by burrow features (percentage, morphology) and host rock cementation. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 109]])

- Lineaments identified via TDR were interpreted as potential fracture zones for fluid flow. ([[2024-ahmed|Ahmed, 2024, p. 71]])

- Fracture zones were identified as primary conduits for thermal fluids, with an E-W trend in the southern part. ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 136]])

- Not stated in source ([[2024-suleiman-5|Suleiman, 2024]])

- A fractured well shows a significantly higher productivity index (PI) and delayed dew point reach compared to non-fractured wells. ([[2000-hashmi|Hashmi, 2000, p. 106]])
