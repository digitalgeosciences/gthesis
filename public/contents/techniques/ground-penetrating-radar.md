# Ground Penetrating Radar

**Summary**: A geophysical method using electromagnetic waves to image subsurface structures and reflectors.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2013-yousif|Yousif, 2013]] used ground-penetrating-radar to detect reflectors in the Hofuf formation.

- [[2014-saeed|Saeed, 2014]] used ground penetrating radar (GPR) to determine the dielectric permittivity of cavity fillings.

- [[2022-al-alawi|Al-Alawi, 2022]] used GPR to map the internal structure of dune bedforms and identify bounding surfaces.

- [[2022-argadestya|Argadestya, 2022]] applied GPR to identify internal structures and buried foredunes in the Parangkusumo shoreface and dunes.

## Key Results and Findings

- The GPR successfully detected reflectors within a small thickness (13m) even when they were close to each other. ([[2013-yousif|Yousif, 2013, p. 66]])

- The travel time (Tc) from radargrams was used as a primary input for the joint inversion calculation. ([[2014-saeed|Saeed, 2014, p. 37]])

- GPR successfully mapped internal structures, identifying truncation, downlap, and bounding surfaces, confirming a unidirectional wind regime. ([[2022-al-alawi|Al-Alawi, 2022, p. 111]])

- Revealed an established foredune approximately 6m beneath the modern lee surface. ([[2022-argadestya|Argadestya, 2022, p. 70]])

- The GPR velocity was calculated as 0.1629 m/ns using a CMP gather and the back-shifting method. ([[2024-alkhunaizi|Alkhunaizi, 2024, p. 45]])

- GPR data provided information on burrow attributes (morphology, size, and percentage) that cannot be obtained from CT scans or outcrop photographs alone. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 19]])

- Successfully identified root concentrations, particularly in the topsoil. ([[2024-prasetyo|Prasetyo, 2024, p. 14]])
