# Ground Penetrating Radar

**Summary**: A geophysical method using electromagnetic waves to image subsurface structures and reflectors.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2013-yousif|Yousif, 2013]] used ground-penetrating-radar to detect subsurface reflectors in the Hofuf Formation.

- [[2014-saeed|Saeed, 2014]] used ground-penetrating-radar to determine the location and size of cavities and their filling materials.

## Key Results and Findings

- The 100 MHz antenna provided high resolution for identifying sedimentary layers, outperforming seismic methods in these specific conditions. ([[2013-yousif|Yousif, 2013, p. 35]])

- The method is effective for detecting cavities due to the contrast in dielectric permittivity between the cavity fill and surrounding rock. ([[2014-saeed|Saeed, 2014, p. 21]])
