# Ground Penetrating Radar

**Summary**: A geophysical method using electromagnetic waves to image subsurface structures and reflectors.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2013-yousif|Yousif, 2013]] used ground-penetrating-radar to detect subsurface reflectors in the Hofuf Formation.

- [[2014-saeed|Saeed, 2014]] used ground-penetrating-radar to determine the location and size of cavities and their filling materials.

- [[2022-al-alawi|Al-Alawi, 2022]] used GPR to investigate the internal structure of aeolian bedforms in the Jafurah Sand Sea.

- [[2022-argadestya|Argadestya, 2022]] used GPR to obtain facies for the Parangkusumo Shoreface and parabolic sand dune.

- [[2024-alkhunaizi|Alkhunaizi, 2024]] used GPR to determine velocity values and identify sand-sabkha contacts in a dune environment.

- [[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024]] used GPR to characterize burrow attributes like diameter and percentage in the field.

- [[2024-prasetyo|Prasetyo, 2024]] used GPR to identify root concentrations in the topsoil of a scaled experiment.

## Key Results and Findings

- The 100 MHz antenna provided high resolution for identifying sedimentary layers, outperforming seismic methods in these specific conditions. ([[2013-yousif|Yousif, 2013, p. 35]])

- The method is effective for detecting cavities due to the contrast in dielectric permittivity between the cavity fill and surrounding rock. ([[2014-saeed|Saeed, 2014, p. 21]])

- GPR successfully mapped dune internal structures including wind ripples, grainflow, and avalanche cross-strata. ([[2022-al-alawi|Al-Alawi, 2022, p. 92]])

- Provided a GPR facies for the shoreface and dunes, comparable to Mars' RIMFAX payload. ([[2022-argadestya|Argadestya, 2022, p. 23]])

- The GPR velocity was calculated as 0.1629 m/ns for the study area. ([[2024-alkhunaizi|Alkhunaizi, 2024, p. 8]])

- GPR data allowed for a comparative analysis of burrow attributes across different axes. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 109]])

- Successfully identified root concentrations, particularly in the topsoil. ([[2024-prasetyo|Prasetyo, 2024, p. 14]])
