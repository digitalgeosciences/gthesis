# Chemostratigraphy

**Summary**: The study of the variation in chemical composition of rocks to identify stratigraphic units.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017]] used chemostratigraphy to correlate lithofacies and SGR data.

- [[2020-gbiyanju|Gbiyanju, 2020]] employed chemostratigraphy to establish a high-resolution correlation structure where biostratigraphy was poor.

- [[2022-bashri|Bashri, 2022]] applied chemostratigraphy based on Si, Al, Ca, Mn, and Fe to correlate outcrops with subsurface data.

## Key Results and Findings

- Chemostratigraphic analysis identified 5-7 reservoir zones across different outcrops. ([[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017, p. 114]])

- Chemostratigraphy successfully identified subtle variations in lithofacies despite apparent homogeneity in reservoir lithology. ([[2020-gbiyanju|Gbiyanju, 2020, p. 22]])

- Mn log is lithology independent and highly correlatable between studied outcrops and subsurface data. ([[2022-bashri|Bashri, 2022, p. 20]])
