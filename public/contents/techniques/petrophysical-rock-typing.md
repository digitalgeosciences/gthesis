# Petrophysical Rock Typing

**Summary**: The classification of rocks into groups with similar petrophysical properties like porosity and permeability.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- Hydraulic Flow Units (HFUs) are used to categorize rock quality and predict permeability in uncored intervals. ([[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010, p. 139]])

- LSA allows for more accurate rock typing in thinly bedded sequences by resolving individual layers. ([[2013-abdullah|Abdullah, 2013, p. 64]])

- Ranked flow units based on porosity-permeability relationships and lithofacies characteristics. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 126]])

- Three electro-facies were established: sand, shaly_sand, and shale. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 83]])

- Distribute outcrop porosity and permeability data into the high resolution 3-D facies model. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 20]])

- Quantified the difference in elastic constants between shale and gas sand using a 1997 dataset. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 59]])

- Electrofacies are highly effective for identifying major units and boundaries in the Sarah Formation. ([[2016-ejaz|Ejaz, 2016, p. 14]])

- Petrophysical properties were calculated from conditioned logs to determine lithology, porosity, and fluid type. ([[2016-houssain|Houssain, 2016, p. 17]])

- Rock types were differentiated based on porosity, permeability, and TOC content. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 163]])

- Petrophysical parameters like porosity and permeability were measured for 63 samples to assess geomechanical behavior. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 28]])
