# Lineament Analysis

**Summary**: The identification and mapping of linear features on the Earth's surface to identify structural trends.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017]] used lineament-analysis from satellite imagery and DEM data to map regional fracture traces.

- [[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023]] used lineament analysis to identify structural controls for fluid flow in the geothermal system.

- [[2023-arrofi|Arrofi, 2023]] used lineament analysis to determine orientation, length distribution, and density in both study areas.

- [[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024]] performed lineament analysis to identify structural elements like faults and dikes.

## Key Results and Findings

- Lineaments were categorized into five sets: NW-SE (1450), NW-SE (1350), NNE-SSW (0150), NE-SW (0350), and ENE-WSW (0700). ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 69]])

- Identified 10 high-density zones (A-J) that correlate with potential permeability pathways. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 60]])

- Automatically extracted lineaments in Northern Konawe followed a power law distribution (R² = 0.9615). ([[2023-arrofi|Arrofi, 2023, p. 29]])

- Identified NE-SW trending structures as primary conduits for thermal fluids. ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 35]])
