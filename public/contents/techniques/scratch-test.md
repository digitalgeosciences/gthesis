# Scratch Test

**Summary**: A method to determine rock strength by measuring the energy required to cut a sample with a cutter.

---

- [[2019-khan|Khan, 2019]] used scratch tests to measure unconfined compressive strength (UCS) and intrinsic specific energy (ISE).

- [[2019-gomaa|Gomaa, 2019]] applied scratch tests to assess the effect of treatment fluids on rock integrity and mechanical properties.

- [[2020-gowida|Gowida, 2020]] used scratch tests to determine the compressive strength of the consolidated sand samples.

## Key Results and Findings

- The study confirmed that ISE is directly correlated to UCS, providing a non-destructive way to determine rock strength. ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019, p. 35]])

- The results showed that the mechanical response was highly affected by the original composition of the cores. ([[2019-gomaa|Gomaa, 2019, p. 20]])

- The scratch test provided values comparable to conventional uniaxial compressive strength (UCS) testers while allowing for repeated testing on the same sample. ([[2020-gowida|Gowida, 2020, p. 67]])

- Scratch test results showed good agreement with hardness-based UCS in several instances. ([[2021-albensaad|Albensaad, 2021, p. 14]])

- Scratch tests were used to assess the hardness of the same rock portion before and after treatment to avoid heterogeneity effects. ([[2022-samarkin|Samarkin, 2022, p. 45]])

- The UCS of Berea Buff samples decreased from 50.25 MPa to 40.51 MPa after 5 days of interaction. ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 57]])

- Scratch measurements were correlated with horizontal and vertical forces to estimate intrinsic energy. ([[2021-khan|KHAN, 2021, p. 65]])

- Determined that Xanthan gum addition significantly improved the consolidation strength of the sand. ([[2023-omarov|Omarov, 2023, p. 49]])

- Solution 10 (cellulose) achieved the highest strength of 1100 Psi at 70°C. ([[2023-baig|Baig, 2023, p. 54]])

- The scratch test provided a consistent measurement of UCS and allowed for the calculation of YM and PR. ([[2023-abdelaal|Abdelaal, 2023, p. 108]])

- Determined that 7 scratches were required for accurate ISE estimation, selecting points with highest linearity. ([[2024-suleiman-6|Suleiman, 2024, p. 57]])
