# Ct Scan

**Summary**: A non-destructive imaging technique used to visualize internal features and quantify porosity based on density contrast.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014]] used CT-scan to identify the distribution of iron oxide (high density) versus quartz and pores.

- [[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022]] used CT scanning to determine the burrow percentage (BP) and identify burrowing styles within the Hanifa Formation.

- [[2022-salih|Salih, 2022]] used CT-scan for bioturbation estimation in carbonate samples.

- [[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023]] applied ct-scanning to quantify wormhole volume and branching.

- [[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024]] applied CT scanning to two rock samples from subunit 1A to evaluate burrow connectivity and morphology.

- [[2024-akomolafe|Akomolafe, 2024]] applied CT scans to visualize the distribution and extent of hematite invasion within the sample.

- [[2024-allehyani|allehyani, 2024]] applied CT scan analysis to quantify bioturbation intensity and identify burrow styles.

## Key Results and Findings

- The CT-scan images revealed that the sample is composed of one material (quartz) with bright spots representing iron oxide. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 124]])

- CT scan data revealed a distinction between pipe framework styles (connected) and bio-retexturing styles (not connected). ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 45]])

- Used specifically to quantify the impact of biological activity on rock texture and porosity. ([[2022-salih|Salih, 2022, p. 41]])

- A linear relationship was found between the change in porosity and the wormhole volume determined via CT. ([[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023, p. 109]])

- CT scans provided high-resolution 2D slices and 3D views for measuring shaft diameter and length. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 119]])

- Images highlighted significant clogging and porosity reduction at the top where hematite was concentrated. ([[2024-akomolafe|Akomolafe, 2024, p. 79]])

- Quantified the Burrow Percentage (BP) along core segments, identifying a transition from BN to BR styles. ([[2024-allehyani|allehyani, 2024, p. 62]])
