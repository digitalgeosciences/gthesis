# Core Analysis

**Summary**: The physical and chemical evaluation of core samples to determine reservoir properties.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- Berea core samples were used to measure invasion depth and skin factor under various flooding conditions. ([[2001-mohammed|Mohammed, 2001, p. 107]])

- Permeability values were found to be higher in dolomite compared to limestone. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 59]])

- 40 Berea sandstone core plugs were used to measure permeability at various stages of damage and stimulation. ([[1999-al-muhareb|Al-Muhareb, 1999, p. 35]])

- Coreflooding showed that the apparent gelation time in porous media was significantly shorter than in beaker tests. ([[1997-al-nashmi|Al-Nashmi, 1997, p. 62]])

- Core samples were saturated with brine and desaturated using a porous plate cell to measure resistivity at various pressure steps. ([[2009-al-gathe|AL-GATHE, 2009, p. 43]])

- 44 plug samples were analyzed for porosity and permeability under both ambient (500 psi) and stressed (2500 psi) conditions. ([[2009-al-gathe-2|AL-GATHE, 2009, p. 32]])

- The study confirmed that the pulse decay technique can be used to measure permeability from cuttings even when they are not at full reservoir depth (overburden). ([[2009-mansur|Mansur, 2009, p. 18]])

- The PPD technique was successfully used to differentiate between matrix and fracture permeability components in low-permeability samples. ([[2009-bajaalah|BAJAALAH, 2009, p. 70]])

- Core data provided the primary basis for defining hydraulic flow units and sedimentary facies. ([[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010, p. 156]])

- Core analysis identified five distinct lithofacies based on sedimentary characteristics like texture, fossil content, and structure. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 43]])

- Permeability tests were conducted on various cement samples to determine their effectiveness in preventing fluid migration between zones. ([[2011-al-dossary|Al-Dossary, 2011, p. 90]])

- 1,160 feet of core were used to define five distinct litho-facies including dune, sand sheet, and interdune types. ([[2011-al-masrahy|AL-MASRAHY, 2011, p. 48]])

- 465 meters (1,440 feet) of core were analyzed across the Arab-C carbonate to identify facies and cycles. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 41]])

- 10% acetic/1.5% HF showed 0% solubility, while 6% HCl/1.5% HF dissolved 13% by weight. ([[2012-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2012, p. 34]])

- Determined that section 10 had a breakthrough pore volume of 0.3599. ([[2013-khan|Khan, 2013, p. 139]])

- Analysis of reservoir core properties before and after CO2 injection showed only a minor change in porosity in dolomite rocks. ([[2015-adebayo|Adebayo, 2015, p. 53]])

- Corrected core porosity and permeability were used as the primary training data for the prediction models. ([[2015-al-amri|Al-Amri, 2015, p. 38]])

- Poor petrophysical properties occurred mainly at the boundary between different sandstone bodies during iron transformation. ([[2016-babker|Babker, 2016, p. 68]])

- Core samples show a direct relation between P/S wave velocities and porosity/density. ([[2016-ejaz|Ejaz, 2016, p. 14]])

- Permeability ratio improved by 39% for Berea and 40% for Bandera when using specific chelating agent formulations. ([[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016, p. 19]])

- Permeability values range from 0.04-0.3 mD in middle/lower parts and 0.03-9 mD in the upper part. ([[2016-radwan|Radwan, 2016, p. 67]])

- Concentrations of 7.5 wt% and 10 wt% citric acid resulted in recovery of 74% and 84% of OOIP, respectively. ([[2014-alam|ALAM, 2014, p. 136]])

- Berea sample had 22% porosity and a measured permeability of 151.2 mD. ([[2017-mohammed-3|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 124]])

- Core samples were saturated with 3 wt% KCl brine to prevent the loosening of clay particles within the pores of the sandstone. ([[2017-jafarov|Jafarov, 2017, p. 46]])

- Addition of 1% Nano zeolite reduced permeability by approximately 98%. ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 108]])

- 202 core samples were used to provide the basis for training and testing the prediction algorithms. ([[2017-busaleh|BuSaleh, 2017, p. 72]])

- The highest porosity and permeability values were found in burrowed wackestone due to dedolomitization. ([[2018-ismanto|Ismanto, 2018, p. 17]])

- Retained permeability was calculated as high as 100% for the tested cores. ([[2018-alqahtani|AlQahtani, 2018, p. 72]])

- Permeability increased by 40% for Gray Berea and 37.5% for Scioto cores after three cycles. ([[2019-gomaa|Gomaa, 2019, p. 19]])

- Core samples were analyzed for porosity using the liquid saturation method. ([[2019-al-garadi|AL-GARADI, 2019, p. 36]])

- Core tests showed that nitrogen gas pre-injection reduced required acid pore volumes for breakthrough. ([[2019-alawi|ALAWI, 2019, p. 38]])

- Linear cores showed higher permeability (up to 28.6 mD) compared to the larger blocks. ([[2020-aly|Aly, 2020, p. 130]])

- Core data was used to benchmark 11 classes of lithologies and 5 categories of sedimentary textures in the study's reference material. ([[2019-alqunais|Alqunais, 2019, p. 67]])

- Permeability remained between 2.63 and 2.69 Darcies across all test conditions, showing minimal impact from the oxidation process. ([[2020-hiba|HIBA, 2020, p. 48]])

- Permeability reduction reached over 90% for several samples treated with DAP. ([[2022-samarkin|Samarkin, 2022, p. 78]])

- RRT 1 had high permeability (0.1-1222 mD), RRT 2 had moderate permeability (0.1–1.3 mD), and RRT 3 had low permeability (0.01–0.1 mD). ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 19]])

- The average core plugs porosity was 16.9% and the average absolute liquid corrected permeability was 0.72 mD. ([[2021-khan|KHAN, 2021, p. 133]])

- Permeability values ranged from 5 to 56,150 mD (average 2,479 mD). ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 165]])

- The Eco Park site showed a trend of improving conditions in the top samples of the core. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 54]])

- Solution 13 (AN 125) resulted in approximately a 30% reduction in permeability. ([[2023-baig|Baig, 2023, p. 14]])

- A delicate procedure was followed to alter the wettibility of the sister sample without compromising the pore structure. ([[2023-abdulwahab|Abdulwahab, 2023, p. 14]])

- The use of ionic liquids improved regained permeability by 25% compared to the base case. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 48]])

- Permeability increased from 3.80 mD to 5.59 mD due to the acid-based capillary rise experiments. ([[2023-pratama|PRATAMA, 2023, p. 123]])

- DAP-treated Indiana limestone showed 168 m.D conductivity at 3,200 psi compared to 97 m.D in untreated samples. ([[2023-desouky|Desouky, 2023, p. 103]])

- Foamed acid significantly improved fracture conductivity compared to conventional acid treatments. ([[2024-al-drwish|AL-DRWISH, 2024, p. 30]])

- Indiana limestone showed a permeability range of 90-110 mD, while Buda/Leuder samples were tight with 1-2 mD. ([[2024-akomolafe|Akomolafe, 2024, p. 57]])

- Core analysis revealed two distinct mineralogical units: a 40m dolomite interval and a 3-9m non-dolomitic interval. ([[1988-darwish-2|Darwish, 1988, p. 122]])

- Porosity was determined as a weighted average for each layer from core samples. ([[1999-fawumi|Fawumi, 1999, p. 53]])

- 827 experimental data points were obtained from these tests across various sandstone types. ([[1994-al-fattah|Al-Fattah, 1994, p. 79]])

- Whole core samples are more representative of the formation than standard core plugs. ([[2000-hamid|Hamid, 2000, p. 23]])

- Core analysis provided data for permeability ranging from 0.0815 md to 115.736 md. ([[2000-hashmi|Hashmi, 2000, p. 40]])

- Laboratory tests provide a means to simulate interactions between drilling mud and formation before field implementation. ([[1999-hasan|Hasan, 1999, p. 38]])
