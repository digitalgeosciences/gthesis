# Core Analysis

**Summary**: Laboratory measurements of physical properties like porosity and permeability on core samples.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000]] performed core-analysis to determine the porosity and permeability of Khobar aquifer rocks.

- [[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988]] used core analysis to characterize the 10 rock cores along the King Fahd causeway.

- [[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010]] performed core-analysis on 370.9 meters of core from nine wells.

- [[2011-al-masrahy|AL-MASRAHY, 2011]] used core analysis to identify depositional facies in the Unayzah-A member.

- [[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011]] used core-analysis to characterize the grainstone and mudstone facies within the Arab-C sequences.

- [[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015]] used core-analysis to validate log-derived properties and define lithofacies.

- [[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016]] performed core_analysis to determine the reservoir quality of various lithofacies.

- [[2016-babker|Babker, 2016]] performed core analysis to determine porosity and permeability for each lithofacies.

- [[2016-ejaz|Ejaz, 2016]] used core-analysis to calibrate geomechanical properties and validate log-derived models.

- [[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016]] used core-analysis to measure plug porosity and determine grain density across the Khuff units.

- [[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017]] performed uniaxial compression tests to determine UCS and Young's Modulus for 82 sandstone samples.

## Key Results and Findings

- Established a power-law relationship between permeability and porosity: K = 10^(0.98 + 0.5*log(phi)). ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 64]])

- Identified two distinct mineralogical units: a lower dolomite interval (40m thick) and an upper non-dolomite interval (3-9m thick). ([[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988, p. 183]])

- Identified five primary reservoir facies and two non-genetically related facies (marginal marine and beach). ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 13]])

- 1160 feet of core data were used to identify dunes, interdunes, sand sheets, and playa lakes. ([[2011-al-masrahy|AL-MASRAHY, 2011, p. 10]])

- Core analysis identified oolitic skeletal grainstones as the primary transgressive facies in several high-frequency sequences. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 79]])

- Four conventional cores were used for the determination of lithofacies. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 10]])

- 24 core plugs were analyzed, showing a wide range of liquid permeabilities from several mD to hundreds of mD. ([[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016, p. 158]])

- Porosity and permeability were measured using TPI-219 helium-porosimeter and TKA-209 gas-permeameter. ([[2016-babker|Babker, 2016, p. 34]])

- Core samples showed direct correlation between P/S wave velocities and porosity/density. ([[2016-ejaz|Ejaz, 2016, p. 14]])

- Core samples in Khuff B showed a range of porosity from 0.7% to 23.2%. ([[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016, p. 53]])

- The highest values of UCS were observed in sandstone samples from Juayl Formation with an average value equal to 7.97 MPa. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 99]])
