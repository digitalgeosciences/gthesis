# Ion Chromatography

**Summary**: An analytical technique used to separate and quantify ions in a liquid sample.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- Used for the quantification of bromate, which is a critical byproduct of ozonation. ([[2009-zubair|Zubair, 2009, p. 25]])

- Bromide concentrations were found in the range of 0.25 to 0.98 ppm in blended water samples. ([[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011, p. 38]])

- IC analysis confirmed that 5% EDTA led to a Ca2+ concentration in the effluent higher than the injected fluid, indicating rock dissolution. ([[2013-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2013, p. 73]])

- Sulfate and chloride ions showed more significant retention than magnesium and sodium ions during the flooding process. ([[2017-ahmed-3|Ahmed, 2017, p. 94]])

- Ion chromatography confirmed the production of salt (NaCl) during the thermochemical process. ([[2020-mohammed|Mohammed, 2020, p. 119]])

- Quantitation was accomplished by measuring the peak area compared to a calibration curve from known standards. ([[2020-ghazwani|Ghazwani, 2020, p. 22]])

- Determined that high Ca and Mg concentrations are essential for carbonate precipitation. ([[2025-alghawi|Alghawi, 2025, p. 48]])
