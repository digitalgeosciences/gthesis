# Thin-Section Petrography

**Summary**: The study of rock thin sections under a polarizing microscope to determine mineralogy, texture, and pore characteristics.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- Petrographic point-counting provided a viable shortcut for obtaining rapid estimates of hydrogeologic inputs like porosity and permeability. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 17]])

- Identified carbonate-apatite as the primary mineral with calcite and dolomite as dominant gangue minerals. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 58]])

- Thin sections were used to identify rock types and estimate porosities. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 17]])

- Used to identify porosity through 200-point counts and visual charts for percentage calculation. ([[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001, p. 29]])

- Identified five distinctive microfacies used to interpret age and paleoenvironmental conditions. ([[1992-el-sahn|El-Sahn, 1992, p. 13]])

- Identified calcite, clay, and quartz overgrowths as major authigenic cements. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 23]])

- Thin sections were used to identify lithofacies and provide detailed descriptions of the carbonate rocks in the Shu'aiba and Yamama formations. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 27]])

- Petrographic analysis identified five distinct non-dolomitic lithofacies including oolitic sand and skeletal grainstone. ([[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988, p. 12]])

- Identified 51 species of foraminifera within the Dam Formation carbonate rocks. ([[2006-al-enezi|Al-Enezi, 2006, p. 13]])

- Thin sections were used to define geological facies which are then linked to petrophysical rock property groups. ([[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010, p. 46]])

- Identified five distinct facies based on petrographic and sedimentological characteristics. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 13]])

- Identified several distinct carbonate types including mollusk-rich ooid grainstones and thrombolites. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 31]])

- Identified mineral assemblage as granodioritic gneiss consisting of quartz, hornblende, K-feldspar, plagioclase, and biotite. ([[2012-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2012, p. 36]])

- Identified several diagenetic stages including early dolomitization, dissolution, and cementation. ([[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013, p. 74]])

- Identified monocrystalline quartz as the dominant component in several samples, with varying degrees of iron cementation. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 13]])

- Showed good correlation between the textural variability and porosity and permeability measurements. ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 16]])

- Identified three distinct lithofacies: Well-Sorted Oolitic Grainstone, Cross-bedded Oolitic Skeletal Grainstone, and Graded Mudstone to Packstone. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 41]])

- Thin sections revealed that the dominant grain types were very fine to fine ooids, detrital peloids, and calcisiltites. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 93]])

- Identified various sedimentary structures and minerals in the clastic units through detailed microscopic analysis. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 168]])

- Identified three distinct mudstone facies based on petrographic characteristics. ([[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015, p. 19]])

- Identified oolitic grainstone with suture contacts and foraminiferal packstone characterized by miliolid forams. ([[2015-koeshidayatullah|Koeshidayatullah, 2015, p. 40]])

- Identified several lithofacies including dolomitic mudstone, stromatoporoid packstone, and various grainstones. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 10]])

- Used in conjunction with alizarin red staining to differentiate carbonate minerals. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 56]])

- Identified 17 distinct lithofacies including oolitic grainstone, stromatolites, and mud-cracked shales. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 14]])

- Identified 9 distinct lithofacies (LF1-LF9) based on grain size, sorting, and cementation. ([[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016, p. 13]])

- Not stated in source ([[2016-hassan-2|Hassan, 2016]])

- Used to investigate lithological heterogeneity from macro to micro scale. ([[2016-babker|Babker, 2016, p. 12]])

- Thin section analysis was used to distinguish between various carbonate and clastic facies. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 14]])

- Thin section analysis revealed specific textures such as micritization in Fp-g facies and oolitic structures in Bog facies. ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 12]])

- Identified bedding-parallel stylolites as common in limestone and dolomitic limestone lithologies. ([[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016, p. 19]])

- Thin-section petrography was used to differentiate calcite from dolomite using Alizarin Red S stain. ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 46]])

- Petrography identified 10 distinct microfabric types in the carbonate samples. ([[2016-radwan|Radwan, 2016, p. 36]])

- Alizarin Red staining was used in thin sections to distinguish between different carbonate minerals. ([[2016-makkawi|Makkawi, 2016, p. 39]])

- Identified large foraminiferal fragments (alveolinids, lepidocyclinids) in sample 10. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 65]])

- Used alongside SEM and XRD for petrographic characterization. ([[2017-mahgoub|Mahgoub, 2017, p. 19]])

- Used to characterize the spatial distribution of Tuwaiq Mountain lithofacies at multiple scales. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 23]])

- Used to characterize 17 distinct lithofacies. ([[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017, p. 15]])

- Identified four distinct lithofacies (FA1-FA4) based on textural and mineralogical characteristics. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 9]])

- Identified various carbonate textures including rudstone, wackestone, and grainstone. ([[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017, p. 67]])

- Lithofacies were identified as oolitic grainstone, recrystallized limestone, and marlstone through microscopic observation. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 15]])

- Identified 9 distinct lithofacies (LFT1-LFT9) based on petrographic analysis of the D5 and D6 members. ([[2017-yousif|Yousif, 2017, p. 86]])

- Identified various cement types including calcite, pyrite, and hematite, as well as secondary porosity from grain dissolution. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 127]])

- Identified several mudstone and siltstone facies (Mdg, Mlg, Mzr) based on mineralogy and texture. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 36]])

- Identified 8 distinct microfacies based on petrographic analysis. ([[2018-ismanto|Ismanto, 2018, p. 17]])
