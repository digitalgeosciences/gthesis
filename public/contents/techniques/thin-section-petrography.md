# Thin-Section Petrography

**Summary**: The study of thin sections of rock to determine mineralogy, texture, and sedimentary structures.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- Used to characterize the petrographical features of the Sarah Formation. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 20]])

- Thin-section analysis provides a viable alternative for analyzing petrophysical data in areas where conventional methods are not financially feasible. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 42]])

- Identified and classified rock types for 11 different samples. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 54]])

- Identified primary and secondary porosity through 200-point counts and visual comparison. ([[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001, p. 49]])

- Identified five distinct microfacies based on thin section analysis. ([[1992-el-sahn|El-Sahn, 1992, p. 13]])

- Identified three distinct groups/units based on grain size, sorting, and matrix content across Haradh, Tinat, and Waqr fields. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 43]])

- Identified distinct lithofacies such as green mudstone (microcrystalline dolomite with 4-15% clay) and pelletal grainstones. ([[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988, p. 68]])

- Identified 51 species from the Dam Formation using thin-section analysis. ([[2006-al-enezi|Al-Enezi, 2006, p. 13]])

- A total of 100 thin sections were used to provide detailed investigation of the grain and pore types. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 43]])

- Identified four facies types (mudstone, packstone, wackestone, and grainstone) in the Dam Formation. ([[2012-elhaj|Elhaj, 2012, p. 13]])

- Identified 7 lithofacies based on petrographic observations of minerals like calcite and dolomite. ([[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013, p. 64]])

- Thin sections were used in conjunction with SEM to provide a detailed view of the rock's internal architecture. ([[2013-khan|Khan, 2013, p. 48]])

- Identified quartz arenites with high purity (98%) and identified key clay minerals like kaolinite. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 71]])

- Thin section petrography showed good correlation between the textural variability and porosity and permeability measurements. ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 16]])

- Identified distinct lithofacies including well-sorted oolitic grainstone, cross-bedded oolitic skeletal grainstone, and graded mudstone to packstone. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 17]])

- Thin sections revealed transitions from oolitic grainstones to packstones across different units. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 178]])
