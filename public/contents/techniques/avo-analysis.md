# AVO Analysis

**Summary**: Analysis of how seismic wave amplitudes vary with the angle of incidence to characterize lithology and fluids.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2014-kola|Kola, 2014]] proposed a TAVO method using transmission rather than reflection amplitudes to avoid issues like attenuation and ray bending.

- [[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016]] applied AVO analysis to determine the relationship between reflection coefficients and offset.

- [[2017-almarzooq|Almarzooq, 2017]] aimed to produce data suitable for avo-analysis by removing sand dune artifacts.

- [[2019-al-dawood|Al-Dawood, 2019]] used avo-analysis to validate seismic reflection anomalies and classify gas sands into four classes.

- [[2021-suleymanov|Suleymanov, 2021]] applied avo-analysis to determine the character of AVO curves (e.g., Class I, III).

## Key Results and Findings

- TAVO successfully estimated $\beta/\alpha$ and $\Delta\beta/\beta$ for three different reservoir models with high accuracy at low-to-mid angles. ([[2014-kola|Kola, 2014, p. 52]])

- The AVO equation was used to define the average response for an isotropic medium before being modified into the AVOA equation to account for anisotropy. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 20]])

- Sand dune amplification compromises the suitability of processed data for AVO analysis. ([[2017-almarzooq|Almarzooq, 2017, p. 14]])

- The synthetic AVA curve for well C was classified as Class IV. ([[2019-al-dawood|Al-Dawood, 2019, p. 51]])

- Different elastic constants result in distinct AVO classes; for example, a specific configuration resulted in an AVO class III response. ([[2021-suleymanov|Suleymanov, 2021, p. 46]])
