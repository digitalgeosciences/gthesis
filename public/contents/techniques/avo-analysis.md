# AVO Analysis

**Summary**: A method used in geophysics to characterize reservoirs by analyzing how seismic wave amplitudes change as a function of incidence angle.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2014-kola|Kola, 2014]] used avo-analysis to evaluate the relationship between transmission coefficients and elastic properties.

- [[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016]] used AVO analysis to evaluate the impact of fractures on the AVO gradient.

- [[2016-ali|Ali, 2016]] used avo-analysis to delineate OWC in various fields including those in the eastern province.

## Key Results and Findings

- Inversion resulted in a fairly good estimation for average S-wave/P-wave velocity and S-wave velocity difference up to intermediate angles. ([[2014-kola|Kola, 2014, p. 15]])

- AVOA is more reliable than azimuthal variations in P-wave velocity for predicting anisotropy related to fracture presence. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 18]])

- AVO inversion for three lines on two structures gave positive AVO and was able to delineate the OWC. ([[2016-ali|Ali, 2016, p. 26]])

- The proposed method provides a dataset that may be suitable for further AVO analyses by reducing sand dune amplification effects. ([[2017-almarzooq|Almarzooq, 2017, p. 14]])
