# Machine Learning Geoscience

**Summary**: A branch of AI focused on building systems that learn from and make{} decisions based on data.

- [[2011-al-dhufairi|AL-DHUFAIRI, 2011]] used machine-learning to predict mobility profiles from well log data.

## Key Results and Findings

- Multiple cases (e.g., Well-6, Case -10) achieved R=1 in predicting mobility values. ([[2011-al-dhufairi|AL-DHUFAIRI, 2011, p. 154]])

- The ANFIS model achieved an Ea of 4.93% and a CC of 0.93, outperforming all empirical models. ([[2011-al-shammari|Al-Shammari, 2011, p. 46]])

- SVM and GRNN were identified as the most accurate models for predicting flow contribution from individual layers. ([[2012-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2012, p. 12]])

- SVM was able to accurately predict reservoir properties from computed seismic attributes, providing a robust way to isolate pressure-affected regions from lithology effects. ([[2013-mark|Mark, 2013, p. 12]])

- Neural Networks (NN) using a cascade-forward architecture achieved correlation coefficients of 0.895 and 0.892 for Wells 1 and 2 respectively. ([[2013-khamis|Khamis, 2013, p. 94]])

- The AI-based model achieved an overall accuracy of 93% in identifying leak zones. ([[2014-al-hussain|Al-Hussain, 2014, p. 97]])

- Four specific AI techniques (ANN, SVM, Functional Networks, and T2FLS) were evaluated for their ability to predict water saturation from well log and core data. ([[2014-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2014, p. 34]])

- RBFN showed improved correlation and lower error compared to multi-linear regression in predicting log properties. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 64]])

- Hybrid models combining feature selection with these algorithms outperformed non-hybrid models in predicting FZI. ([[2015-alsahaf|Alsahaf, 2015, p. 19]])

- The KNN=2 model provided the best correlation (R² = 0.91) for predicting permeability in carbonate reservoirs. ([[2015-al-amri|Al-Amri, 2015, p. 78]])

- SVM was selected as a potential extreme machine learning technique due to its ability to produce accurate predictions from small data sets, which is common in oil and gas fields. ([[2016-tariq|Tariq, 2016, p. 54]])

- The model showed good agreement with experimental data, yielding an average error of 8.4%. ([[2016-mahboob-2|Mahboob, 2016, p. 145]])

- ANN - trainlm showed excellent results in training and testing for all three data categories. ([[2017-busaleh|BuSaleh, 2017, p. 59]])

- The RF technique identified five key inputs: Qo, Pds, GOR, Pwh, and Pwf, which were sufficient for accurate modeling. ([[2018-al-kadem|Al-Kadem, 2018, p. 79]])

- Both ANN and ANFIS models showed high accuracy in predicting ECD values with correlation coefficients exceeding 0.99. ([[2019-elzenary|Elzenary, 2019, p. 67]])

- SVM and FN provided the highest accuracy (R=1.0, RMSE=0) during training phases. ([[2019-ahmed|Ahmed, 2019, p. 153]])

- The multiclass error-correcting output codes (ECOC) model using a decision tree learner predicted etching patterns with an accuracy of 95%. ([[2019-suleiman-3|Suleiman, 2019, p. 18]])

- All applied tabular temporal difference methods successfully solved the study's HM problem but were less efficient than EnKF. ([[2019-alsamadony|Alsamadony, 2019, p. 13]])

- ANN-SAE systems were shown to model heterogeneous channel reservoirs effectively. ([[2020-abdallah|Abdallah, 2020, p. 31]])

- SVM was found to be effective for predicting ROP based on MSE inputs with high accuracy. ([[2020-al-abduljabbar|Al-AbdulJabbar, 2020, p. 34]])

- Deep learning's ability to recognize complex structures in high-dimensional data gives it the edge over other techniques. ([[2021-shaheen|Shaheen, 2021, p. 23]])

- Machine learning is capable of handling non-linear systems without explicit relationship assumptions. ([[2021-alsaihati|Alsaihati, 2021, p. 38]])

- Machine learning models provided high accuracy for predicting four rheological properties with R > 0.90 and AAPE < 10%. ([[2021-alsabaa|Alsabaa, 2021, p. 124]])

- LSTM, a type of machine learning, was found to be the most accurate model for all three phases compared to ANN, SVM, and Decision Trees. ([[2022-jin|Jin, 2022, p. 52]])

- The ensemble model using a classification step followed by regression yielded the highest accuracy for permeability prediction (CC of 0.97). ([[2022-suleiman|Suleiman, 2022, p. 68]])

- The artificial neural network (ANN) model had a prediction error of 3.3% and provided a reliable tool for predicting productivity folds. ([[2022-alsulaiman|AlSulaiman, 2022, p. 13]])

- AI models are only as good as the input data they process. ([[2022-algharbi|ALGHARBI, 2022, p. 29]])

- Machine learning provides a faster and more affordable simulation technique that is user-friendly and does not mandate fast processors compared to traditional two-scale continuum models. ([[2022-alkathim|Alkathim, 2022, p. 14]])

- Unsupervised methods like DBSCAN are more flexible than supervised ones as they do not require labeled data. ([[2022-awais|Awais, 2022, p. 26]])

- ML models (ANN, RF) outperformed traditional methods for 1D property prediction. ([[2023-kusuma|Kusuma, 2023, p. 18]])

- The ANN model (a subset of ML) was the most accurate method for predicting pore pressure compared to RBF and GRN models. ([[2023-alzayer|Alzayer, 2023, p. 11]])

- Machine learning models showed higher accuracy compared to traditional petrophysical equations in several studies. ([[2023-gad|Gad, 2023, p. 18]])

- The XGBoost model was optimized using K-fold cross-validation and grid search to forecast adsorption values. ([[2022-kalam|Kalam, 2022, p. 110]])

- The FNO model showed robustness in handling noisy arrival times and missing picks, even with high-level noise. ([[2023-suleymanli|Suleymanli, 2023, p. 52]])

- Deep learning models can potentially learn and counteract complexities like non-linearity and parameter sensitivity. ([[2023-almarzooq|Almarzooq, 2023, p. 44]])

- Machine learning can be used to provide a hybrid prediction framework that reduces errors in estimating PV and YP of water-based drilling fluids. ([[2023-murif|Murif, 2023, p. 80]])

- The ANN model achieved a correlation coefficient of 0.96 and AAPE of ~5% for training/testing data. ([[2024-almomen|Almomen, 2024, p. 78]])

- The models showed a high correlation coefficient (higher than 0.9) and technically accepted average absolute percentage error (below 7.5%). ([[2023-saadeldin|SAADELDIN, 2023, p. 15]])

- Random Forest and Gradient Boosting models achieved up to 97% accuracy in predicting NMR porosity. ([[2024-alhamood|ALHAMOOD, 2024, p. 19]])

- The XGB model outperformed other ML methods with high R2 values (e.g., 0.941 for Kc) and low AAPE. ([[2024-al-drwish|AL-DRWISH, 2024, p. 218]])

- The CatBoost model outperformed the others across all three tectonic categories with the lowest Mean Squared Error values (0.72, 0.48, 0.69). ([[2025-rachmadan|Rachmadan, 2025, p. 14]])

- Machine learning is becoming a core tool for automating and enhancing geological feature detection. ([[2025-ferreira|Ferreira, 2025, p. 16]])

- Five different models were compared to evaluate the effectiveness of various features in rock typing. ([[2024-jibrin|Jibrin, 2024, p. 39]])

- The back-propagation algorithm was used as the most robust learning algorithm for developing the model. ([[2004-ayoub-2|AYOUB, 2004, p. 3]])
