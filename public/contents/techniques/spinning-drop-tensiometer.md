# Spinning Drop Tensiometer

**Summary**: An instrument used to measure the interfacial tension between two immiscible liquids.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2014-azad|Azad, 2014]] used spinning-drop-tensiometer to determine the IFT reduction of various VES formulations in high salinity.

- [[2017-janjua|Janjua, 2017]] used spinning-drop-tensiometer to determine the interfacial tension between Wafra Ratawi crude oil and various VES systems.

- [[2019-alghamdi|AlGhamdi, 2019]] used a spinning drop tensiometer to determine the IFT of the developed surfactant mixtures.

## Key Results and Findings

- VES-2 achieved an IFT of 0.096 mN/m at 57,000 ppm salinity and 50°C. ([[2014-azad|Azad, 2014, p. 141]])

- The instrument is able to measure ultra-low interfacial tension in the range of 10^-4 to 10 mN/m. ([[2017-janjua|Janjua, 2017, p. 74]])

- Rhamnolipid was shown to reduce surface tension from 72 mN/m to 29 mN/m or less in some studies. ([[2019-alghamdi|AlGhamdi, 2019, p. 30]])
