# Adsorption Experiments

**Summary**: Experimental methods to quantify how much surfactant is retained by the rock surface.

## Key Results and Findings

- Triton X-102 showed the lowest adsorption level at 107°C and 57,000 ppm salinity. ([[1992-rodriguez|Rodriguez, 1992, p. 21]])

- The maximum removal of As(III) using MWCNT-Fe was 77.45% at a dose of 10 mg for 1 mg/L concentration. ([[2009-al-khaldi|Al-Khaldi, 2009, p. 94]])

- Silver oxide impregnated fly ash (Ag2O-FA) was the only tested adsorbent that brought about notable removal of MTBE, reaching 24% at optimal conditions. ([[2014-adebayo|Adebayo, 2014, p. 73]])

- The amount of refractory sulfur compounds adsorbed followed the order DBT > BT > T. ([[2015-danmaliki|Danmaliki, 2015, p. 19]])

- Adsorption efficiency was found to be dependent on pH, contact time, and adsorbent dosage. ([[2016-salawu|Salawu, 2016, p. 21]])

- The Freundlich isotherm was found to be the best fit for describing the experimental data. ([[2016-elnaeem|Elnaeem, 2016, p. 67]])

- Optimized conditions (4 g/L dose, 15 min contact time) yielded a 98.6% removal rate for Cd(II). ([[2017-olabemiwo|Olabemiwo, 2017, p. 14]])

- GPDADMAC achieved 80% removal for phenol while GPHM removed over 60% of cadmium under optimized conditions. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 15]])

- Determined that montmorillonite has the highest affinity for methane among tested clays. ([[2023-afagwu|AFAGWU, 2023, p. 49]])

- Carbonate beds showed higher removal efficiency and capacity for MTBE compared to sandstone beds. ([[2025-almousa|Almousa, 2025, p. 57]])

- Triton X-102 showed the lowest adsorption level and highest desorption level under high temperature/salinity conditions. ([[1992-rodriguez-2|Rodriguez, 1992, p. 81]])

- Adsorption of sulfonates was found to be highly dependent on pH, temperature, and ethoxylation number. ([[1987-ahmed|Ahmed, 1987, p. 123]])
