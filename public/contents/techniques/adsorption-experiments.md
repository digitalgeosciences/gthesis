# Adsorption Experiments

**Summary**: A method to study the removal of solutes from a liquid phase by an adsorbent.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- Batch trials showed that increasing the adsorbent dose provided more binding sites for heavy metals. ([[2009-al-khaldi|Al-Khaldi, 2009, p. 65]])

- The study evaluates how parameters like pH, dosage, and contact time affect adsorption efficiency. ([[2014-adebayo|Adebayo, 2014, p. 18]])

- The experimental data fitted Langmuir, Freundlich, and Temkin isotherms for heavy metal adsorption. ([[2015-musa|Musa, 2015, p. 13]])

- Batch mode was used for initial assessment, while fixed-bed systems were used for breakthrough studies. ([[2015-danmaliki|Danmaliki, 2015, p. 64]])

- AgNP showed high efficiency in removing Hg(II) under various conditions including pH and dosage. ([[2016-salawu|Salawu, 2016, p. 90]])

- The study of the effect of solution conditions such as pH, contact time, dosage... on the adsorption efficiency. ([[2017-olabemiwo|Olabemiwo, 2017, p. 33]])

- GPDADMAC achieved 80% removal of 10 mg/l phenol, while GPHM removed over 60% of 0.5 ppm cadmium. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 15]])
