# UV Photocatalysis

**Summary**: A process using UV light and a semiconductor photocatalyst to degrade organic pollutants.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013]] used uv-photocatalysis to degrade MTBE in spiked water samples.

- [[2015-hassan|Hassan, 2015]] applied uv-photocatalysis to degrade phenol and synthesize organic compounds.

- [[2016-sameh|Sameh, 2016]] applied the UV-H2O2 process to remediate water contaminated with Toluene.

## Key Results and Findings

- MTBE removal was found to be mainly due to the photocatalytic effect of TiO2, with removal rates reaching about 95%. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 10]])

- Used organic dyes as photocatalysts for both synthesis and degradation processes. ([[2015-hassan|Hassan, 2015, p. 16]])

- The UV-H2O2 process achieved 97% removal of Toluene in 15 minutes in deionized water. ([[2016-sameh|Sameh, 2016, p. 15]])
