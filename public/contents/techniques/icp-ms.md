# ICP-MS

**Summary**: A technique used for determining the concentration of elements and isotopes in a sample.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2009-al-khaldi|Al-Khaldi, 2009]] used ICP-MS to determine concentrations of arsenic and mercury.

- [[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011]] used ICP-MS to measure heavy and trace metal concentrations in groundwater samples.

- [[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013]] applied ICP-MS to 200 samples to provide geochemical data for lithofacies correlation.

- [[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015]] used ICP-MS to determine the heavy metal content in sediment samples.

- [[2015-jawad|Jawad, 2015]] used ICP-MS (XSERIES 2) to quantify concentrations of heavy metals like Ba, Cr, and Pb in sediment samples.

- [[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019]] used ICP-MS to measure cadmium concentrations in water samples.

- [[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019]] used ICP-MS to analyze the concentration of heavy metals and trace elements.

## Key Results and Findings

- Not stated in source ([[2009-al-khaldi|Al-Khaldi, 2009]])

- Used to determine the concentration of heavy and trace metals in both shallow and deep aquifers. ([[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011, p. 47]])

- Used to determine elemental concentrations across a wide range of lithofacies in the study area. ([[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013, p. 47]])

- None of the 63 measured metals exceeded the ER-L (Effect Range-Low) standards. ([[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015, p. 105]])

- Successfully quantified a suite of trace metals including V, Cr, Co, Ni, Cu, Zn, Mo, Ba, and Pb in the study area. ([[2015-jawad|Jawad, 2015, p. 57]])

- GPHM removed more than 60% of 0.5 ppm cadmium within 10 min. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 15]])

- The analysis of trace metals using the ICP-MS showed that the levels of lithium, cobalt, vanadium, arsenic, silver, manganese, nickel molybdenum, copper, chromium, cadmium, and lead, are within the regulatory authority limits. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 22]])
