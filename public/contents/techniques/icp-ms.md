# ICP-MS

**Summary**: A technique for detecting and quantifying metals and non-metals in liquid samples.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011]] used ICP-MS to measure heavy and trace metals in surface, shallow, and deep groundwater samples.

- [[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013]] used ICP-MS to identify chemical groups associated with specific lithofacies.

- [[2015-musa|Musa, 2015]] used ICP-MS to determine metal concentrations in real wastewater samples.

- [[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015]] used ICP-MS to determine heavy metal content in sediments.

- [[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015]] used ICP-MS to track changes in mineralogy (Fe, Ca, Mg, Al) of sandstone rocks after conditioning with DTPA-K5.

- [[2015-jawad|Jawad, 2015]] used ICP-MS to determine the concentration of trace metals (V, Cr, Co, Ni, Cu, Zn, Mo, Ba, Pb) in sediment.

- [[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016]] used ICP-OES to determine minor trace elements at ppm levels.

- [[2017-ahmed-2|AHMED, 2017]] used ICP-OES to measure calcium concentration in the effluent from RDA experiments.

- [[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019]] used ICP-MS to determine cadmium concentrations before and after treatment.

- [[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019]] used ICP-MS to quantify trace metal concentrations in particulate matter from Jubail and Ras Tanura.

- [[2019-gomaa|Gomaa, 2019]] used ICP-OES to analyze elements in the effluent produced after treating Scioto cores with in-situ generated HF fluids.

- [[2021-gasimli|Gasimli, 2021]] used ICP-OES to monitor iron concentration during coreflooding experiments.

- [[2022-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2022]] used ICP-MS to determine the concentration of heavy metals in soil samples from dumpsites.

- [[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023]] used ICP-MS to determine trace element concentrations in peridotite samples.

- [[2023-omarov|Omarov, 2023]] used ICP-OES to monitor the concentration of Ca and Mg ions during the EICP process.

- [[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023]] used ICP-MS to determine concentrations of heavy metals like Pb, As, and Hg in sediment.

- [[2024-alsadah|Alsadah, 2024]] used ICP-MS to determine Mg, Ca, and Na concentrations in foraminifera.

## Key Results and Findings

- Detected high concentrations of Be (0.21 ppm), Al (up to 0.37 ppm), Fe (up to 1.2 ppm), Cr (0.49 ppm), Cu (1.49 ppm), Pb (0.09 ppm), and As (0.3 ppm) in surface water. ([[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011, p. 61]])

- Identified four distinct element groups, where Group-1 (SiO2, Al2O3) and Group-4 (U, K, Th) were high in reservoir facies. ([[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013, p. 189]])

- Inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometry (ICP-MS) ... was used for the determination the metals concentrations of some metal in real wastewater samples. ([[2015-musa|Musa, 2015, p. 48]])

- Analyzed 63 elements with detection limits as low as mg/kg-1 to µg/kg. ([[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015, p. 103]])

- Identified that Fe+3 was the most significantly absorbed ion from the rock surface during the conditioning process. ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 55]])

- Quantified concentrations such as 2.67 ppm for Barium and 0.20 ppm for Lead. ([[2015-jawad|Jawad, 2015, p. 57]])

- ICP-OES provided more precision than atomic absorption spectrophotometers, allowing detection of elements like Zr, Ta, and rare earths. ([[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016, p. 53]])

- Calcium concentrations were monitored to determine the dissolution rate of the carbonate rock. ([[2017-ahmed-2|AHMED, 2017, p. 43]])

- GPHM removed more than 60% of 0.5 ppm cadmium within 10 min. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 15]])

- Identified 12 types of trace metals with concentrations between 0.001 and 0.06 µg/m3, all within regulatory limits. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 168]])

- The analysis identified various minerals and their dissolution amounts during the treatment process. ([[2019-gomaa|Gomaa, 2019, p. 16]])

- The maximum Fe3+ concentration in effluent samples was determined as 450 ppm for 20 wt% EDTA at 77°F. ([[2021-gasimli|Gasimli, 2021, p. 113]])

- ICP-MS was used to measure heavy metal concentrations, identifying significant levels of Al, Cr, Ni, Co, Cu, As, Cd, Hg, and Pb in both study sites. ([[2022-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2022, p. 51]])

- Determined trace and rare earth elements with detection limits as low as 0.01 g/g. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 49]])

- Quantified ion consumption as a proxy for precipitation progress, showing that M2 solutions were most sensitive to temperature changes. ([[2023-omarov|Omarov, 2023, p. 54]])

- Confirmed that trace element levels were below international safety limits (e.g., Pb < 2 ppm). ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 65]])

- ICP-MS provided more sensitive results than SEM-EDX but showed no clear trend with temperature or salinity across most months. ([[2024-alsadah|Alsadah, 2024, p. 33]])

- Not stated in source ([[2024-alenazi|Alenazi, 2024]])

- Detection limits of most trace elements are <0.01 µg/g. ([[2024-sulistyohariyanto|Sulistyohariyanto, 2024, p. 47]])

- Quantified Fe levels which are critical for forming siderite during mineralization. ([[2025-alghawi|Alghawi, 2025, p. 48]])
