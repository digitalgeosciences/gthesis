# ICP-MS

**Summary**: A technique used for determining the concentration of elements and isotopes in a sample.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2009-al-khaldi|Al-Khaldi, 2009]] used ICP-MS to determine concentrations of arsenic and mercury.

- [[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011]] used ICP-MS to measure heavy and trace metal concentrations in groundwater samples.

- [[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013]] applied ICP-MS to 200 samples to provide geochemical data for lithofacies correlation.

- [[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015]] used ICP-MS to determine the heavy metal content in sediment samples.

- [[2015-jawad|Jawad, 2015]] used ICP-MS (XSERIES 2) to quantify concentrations of heavy metals like Ba, Cr, and Pb in sediment samples.

- [[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019]] used ICP-MS to measure cadmium concentrations in water samples.

- [[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019]] used ICP-MS to analyze the concentration of heavy metals and trace elements.

- [[2019-ahmed|Ahmed, 2019]] applied ICP-MS to measure trace metal concentrations in the groundwater.

- [[2022-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2022]] applied ICP-MS to quantify heavy metal concentrations (e.g., As, Cd, Pb) in soils from two dumpsites.

- [[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023]] applied ICP-MS to determine whole-rock major and trace element chemistry.

- [[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023]] used ICP-MS to quantify trace element parameters (Mn, Cr, Cu, Ni, As, Pb, Cd, Zn, Hg) in sediment samples.

- [[2024-alsadah|Alsadah, 2024]] used ICP-MS to determine the Mg/Ca ratio of foraminifera following 0.3N HNO3 digestion.

- [[2024-ahmed|Ahmed, 2024]] used ICP-MS to analyze minor and trace elements in groundwater samples.

- [[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024]] used ICP-MS to determine whole rock geochemistry and trace elements.

- [[2024-malik|Malik, 2024]] applied ICP-MS to measure stable oxygen (δ18O) and carbon (δ13C) isotopes in carbonates.

- [[2025-alghawi|Alghawi, 2025]] used ICP-MS to measure iron levels in the water samples.

## Key Results and Findings

- Not stated in source ([[2009-al-khaldi|Al-Khaldi, 2009]])

- Used to determine the concentration of heavy and trace metals in both shallow and deep aquifers. ([[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011, p. 47]])

- Used to determine elemental concentrations across a wide range of lithofacies in the study area. ([[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013, p. 47]])

- None of the 63 measured metals exceeded the ER-L (Effect Range-Low) standards. ([[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015, p. 105]])

- Successfully quantified a suite of trace metals including V, Cr, Co, Ni, Cu, Zn, Mo, Ba, and Pb in the study area. ([[2015-jawad|Jawad, 2015, p. 57]])

- GPHM removed more than 60% of 0.5 ppm cadmium within 10 min. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 15]])

- The analysis of trace metals using the ICP-MS showed that the levels of lithium, cobalt, vanadium, arsenic, silver, manganese, nickel molybdenum, copper, chromium, cadmium, and lead, are within the regulatory authority limits. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 22]])

- Boron, Manganese, and Iron were the only heavy metals exceeding drinking limits. ([[2019-ahmed|Ahmed, 2019, p. 26]])

- Heavy metal concentrations were found to be within normal ranges and below EPA standards. ([[2022-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2022, p. 54]])

- Revealed distinct Light REE (LREE) enrichment relative to chondritic values. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 22]])

- All analyzed trace elements were found at levels below international safety limits for marine sediments. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 48]])

- ICP-MS provided high-precision measurement of magnesium and calcium concentrations. ([[2024-alsadah|Alsadah, 2024, p. 13]])

- The following elements are expected: Al, As, B, Li, Ag, Cd, Fe, Cr, Co, Cu, Pb, Mn, Mo, Ni, V, and Zn. ([[2024-ahmed|Ahmed, 2024, p. 43]])

- Revealed distinct metaluminous signatures in both A-type and I-type granitoids. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 44]])

- Used to determine the origin of carbonate sediments by analyzing isotopic signatures. ([[2024-malik|Malik, 2024, p. 24]])

- Iron was measured using ICP-MS following a 0.25 dilution in 10 ml of HNO3. ([[2025-alghawi|Alghawi, 2025, p. 48]])
