# CT Scanning

**Summary**: A non-destructive imaging technique used to evaluate internal structures and volumes.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2012-geri|Geri, 2012]] used CT scan to assess the homogeneity of the filter cake and measure thickness and porosity.

- [[2013-al-kharraa|Al-Kharraa, 2013]] used CT scans to analyze high-density (dolomite) and low-density features in core samples.

- [[2013-khan|Khan, 2013]] used ct-scanning to observe wormhole morphology before and after acidizing.

- [[2013-kareem|Kareem, 2013]] used CT scan images as the primary input for determining initial porosity and permeability distributions.

- [[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014]] used CT-scan to visualize the distribution of iron oxide and characterize the 3D pore structure.

- [[2014-al-duailej|Al-Duailej, 2014]] used CT scan to provide a graphical representation of density distribution and identify wormhole paths.

- [[2014-jangda|Jangda, 2014]] used CT scans to verify core porosity measurements compared to weight methods.

- [[2015-barri|Barri, 2015]] used CT scan to assess the rock integrity after stimulation.

- [[2015-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2015]] used CT scan to characterize the pore geometry and identify wormholes after core flooding.

- [[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016]] used CT scans to evaluate the core samples before and after acidizing.

- [[2016-adam|ADAM, 2016]] used CT-scan to assess the homogeneity of core slices.

- [[2016-geri|GERI, 2016]] used CT scan to determine the thickness of the filter cake before and after treatment.

- [[2017-jafarov|Jafarov, 2017]] used CT scan to analyze the volume of fluid invasion into 0.25" and 2" core samples before and after filtration tests.

- [[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017]] used micro-ct to examine the structure heterogeneity, bedding, and fractures in Qalibah formation samples.

- [[2017-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2017]] used CT scans to characterize wormhole morphology and connectivity.

- [[2017-muqtadir|Muqtadir, 2017]] applied CT scan to identify significant features and ensure the integrity of the drilled holes.

- [[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019]] used micro-CT to scan sub-plugs at a resolution of 4 µm.

- [[2019-alawi|ALAWI, 2019]] used CT scan to observe wormhole generation and propagation during core flood experiments.

- [[2020-isah|Isah, 2020]] applied CT scanning to investigate structural effects on displacement characteristics in heterogeneous samples.

- [[2020-aly|Aly, 2020]] used ct-scanning to visualize the 3D structure of wormholes in both large carbonate blocks and smaller linear cores.

- [[2020-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2020]] applied micro-CT scanning to determine the carbonation depth and volume of carbonate formation in cement samples.

- [[2021-al-shuqayyiqi|Al-Shuqayyiqi, 2021]] used ct-scanning to determine the saturation profile in each part of the fracture system.

- [[2021-lawal|LAWAL, 2021]] used micro-CT to provide nondestructively visualization of the amount and distribution of the internal cracks of the shale samples.

- [[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022]] used CT scanning to quantify burrow percentage and connectivity in 9.4 cm core samples.

- [[2022-salih|Salih, 2022]] applied CT-scan to estimate the volume of bioturbation within carbonate samples.

- [[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023]] recommended CT imaging to quantify the dissolution volume generated by the fluid.

- [[2023-al-garni|Al-Garni, 2023]] used medical-type X-ray (CT) scanner to evaluate wormhole stability before and after hydrostatic tests.

- [[2023-ahmed-2|AHMED, 2023]] used ct-scanning to verify the uniformity of the cement with Micromax, laponite, perlite, and vermiculite.

- [[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024]] used CT scanning to show the morphology and connectivity of burrows in the Khanasir Member.

- [[2024-suleiman-4|Suleiman, 2024]] used CT scans for the treated samples to evaluate any degradation that occurred to the gel.

- [[2024-akomolafe|Akomolafe, 2024]] used CT scans to visualize the distribution and extent of hematite invasion within the sample.

## Key Results and Findings

- CT scan is a good tool for filter cake properties measurement, specifically identifying two different layers (external and internal). ([[2012-geri|Geri, 2012, p. 27]])

- CT scan images were used to characterize the distribution of minerals like dolomite within the sample. ([[2013-al-kharraa|Al-Kharraa, 2013, p. 52]])

- CT scans allowed for the quantification of dissolution volume by comparing pre- and post-acidization images. ([[2013-khan|Khan, 2013, p. 42]])

- The algorithm successfully converted RGB values from JPEG files into a calibrated scale of -2000 to 3000 'CT scan numbers'. ([[2013-kareem|Kareem, 2013, p. 78]])

- Showed clear internal features and helped in understanding the complex pore patterns. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 99]])

- The size and path of generated wormholes are very distinctive in the CT scan images and can be measured qualitatively. ([[2014-al-duailej|Al-Duailej, 2014, p. 23]])

- The scan for experiment 6 was performed with a newer machine, resulting in a much closer match of the porosity values. ([[2014-jangda|Jangda, 2014, p. 59]])

- Not stated in source ([[2015-barri|Barri, 2015]])

- CT scans confirmed that 10wt% concentrations led to significant rock dissolution and the creation of channels/wormholes. ([[2015-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2015, p. 93]])

- Used to detect alterations of properties of the rock that may happen inside the cores during treatment. ([[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016, p. 53]])

- Approximately 70 slices of images were obtained for each piece of core sample. ([[2016-adam|ADAM, 2016, p. 58]])

- The original filter cake thickness was 1.3 mm, reduced to 0.12 mm after removal. ([[2016-geri|GERI, 2016, p. 128]])

- CT scans provided a clear view of how fluids invaded the rock, allowing for slice-by-slice analysis using VoxelCalc software. ([[2017-jafarov|Jafarov, 2017, p. 61]])

- Identified heavy mineral grain patches and micro-fractures in sample A. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 60]])

- Identified that HCl in seawater produced less branched, potentially plugged wormholes compared to chelating agents. ([[2017-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2017, p. 73]])

- CT scans were performed after plugging, after drilling the central borehole, and after running the fracturing test. ([[2017-muqtadir|Muqtadir, 2017, p. 49]])

- At this scale, pore connectivity is revealed from top to bottom. ([[2019-g201703430kfupmedusa|g201703430@kfupm.edu.sa, 2019, p. 19]])

- Used to monitor the physical growth of wormholes in carbonate samples. ([[2019-alawi|ALAWI, 2019, p. 55]])

- CT images confirmed the presence of vugs and fractures causing non-uniform saturation profiles in sample D-17. ([[2020-isah|Isah, 2020, p. 72]])

- High-energy CT scanning provided an accuracy of up to 0.29mm for visualizing wormhole structures. ([[2020-aly|Aly, 2020, p. 56]])

- Micro-CT scan confirmed that 0.2% graphite reduced carbonation depth by 29.8% after 10 days. ([[2020-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2020, p. 20]])

- Average CT number reduced from 2241 to 2079 in Experiment 1, indicating a change in overall density. ([[2021-al-shuqayyiqi|Al-Shuqayyiqi, 2021, p. 30]])

- Micro-CT images showed that different orientations of the cracks strongly influenced the elastic and hydraulic properties of shale specimens. ([[2021-lawal|LAWAL, 2021, p. 120]])

- The average burrow percentage (BP) from 6 studied cores ranged from 1.45% to 19.06%. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 58]])

- Quantified the extent of burrow networks in the Hanifa Formation. ([[2022-salih|Salih, 2022, p. 76]])

- Not stated in source ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023]])

- The CT scans revealed that Austin chalk was significantly more affected by high stress than Indiana limestone. ([[2023-al-garni|Al-Garni, 2023, p. 48]])

- Micro-CT provides high resolution (1-30 µm) for identifying features like fractures and pore networks. ([[2023-al-boghail|AL-BOGHAIL, 2023, p. 32]])

- An increase in CT number from the base case indicated higher degrees of calcite and dolomite precipitation. ([[2023-baig|Baig, 2023, p. 66]])

- CT scan images confirmed the uniformity of the cement for all improved systems while showing critical segregation in barite, ilmenite, and hematite samples. ([[2023-ahmed-2|AHMED, 2023, p. 28]])

- Used to provide 3D visualization of burrow morphology, which was then integrated with GPR data for better modeling. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 33]])

- CT scan results indicated minimum degradation in both gels, with less significant impact on nano-silica gel. ([[2024-suleiman-4|Suleiman, 2024, p. 21]])

- Images highlighted significant clogging and porosity reduction at the top of the sample where hematite was concentrated. ([[2024-akomolafe|Akomolafe, 2024, p. 79]])

- CT scans confirmed the transition from BN style at the top of strata to BR style toward the bottom. ([[2024-allehyani|allehyani, 2024, p. 51]])

- Micro-CT scan data allowed for the segmentation of burrow fillings into three components: pores, matrix, and grains, revealing that pore systems were predominantly isolated. ([[2025-refaat|Refaat, 2025, p. 62]])
