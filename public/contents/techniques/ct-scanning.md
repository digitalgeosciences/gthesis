# CT Scanning

**Summary**: A non-destructive 3D imaging technique to visualize internal structures and pore networks.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014]] applied CT scanning to analyze the porosity and connectivity of the Sarah Formation samples.

- [[2016-hassan-2|Hassan, 2016]] used micro-scan to visualize the 3D pore network within the Dam Formation.

- [[2020-mansouri|Mansouri, 2020]] used Micro-CT scan to determine the connectivity of the basalt's voids and quantify porosity percentages.

- [[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022]] used CT scanning to determine the volume and percentage of burrow fills within the 9.4 cm cores.

- [[2022-salih|Salih, 2022]] used CT scanning to estimate the volume of bioturbation and map burrow networks in Hanifa Formation samples.

- [[2023-al-garni|Al-Garni, 2023]] used CT scan images to analyze the morphology of wormholes before and after high pressure tests.

- [[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023]] used ct-scanning to observe the wormhole generation and changes in pore structure.

- [[2024-alabbad|Alabbad, 2024]] used CT scanning for 3D volume reconstruction of mineral phases.

- [[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024]] used ct-scanning to estimate porosity from core plugs.

- [[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024]] applied CT scanning to characterize the 3D geometry and morphology of Thalassinoides burrows in cores.

- [[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024]] used ct-scanning to analyze the 3D pore connectivity and structure of stromatolites.

- [[2024-allehyani|allehyani, 2024]] used CT scans to image samples in three dimensions to accurately quantify the bioturbation style and intensity.

- [[2025-refaat|Refaat, 2025]] applied ct-scanning to map the morphology and burrowing style of the Thalassinoides networks.

## Key Results and Findings

- Visualized the distribution of pores and the impact of cementation on permeability. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 99]])

- Not stated in source ([[2016-hassan-2|Hassan, 2016]])

- Micro-CT scan results showed that the basalt voids or pore space connectivity in eastern side of the study area are totally unconnected unlike the west side. ([[2020-mansouri|Mansouri, 2020, p. 87]])

- Quantified burrow percentages (BP) ranging from 5% to 40% across three different outcrop locations. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 36]])

- Quantified the extent of burrow systems and their impact on reservoir properties. ([[2022-salih|Salih, 2022, p. 76]])

- CT scans revealed that wormhole branches were more susceptible to deformation and closure in chalk samples. ([[2023-al-garni|Al-Garni, 2023, p. 13]])

- Used to visualize wormholes at different salinities and temperatures (Figures 36, 50). ([[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023, p. 14]])

- Not stated in source ([[2024-alabbad|Alabbad, 2024]])

- Squelch et al. (2012) demonstrated the use of CT scans for estimating porosity in high permeability cores. ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 90]])

- CT scans revealed diagnostic criteria for Thalassinoides, including vertical shafts and horizontal tunnels. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 41]])

- The author served as a CT scan geologist for Saudi Aramco from 2017 to 2022. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 118]])

- only the slabs yielded high-quality CT images, as the medical x-ray CT scanner energy was not sufficient to penetrate the thicker and denser prisms. ([[2024-allehyani|allehyani, 2024, p. 20]])

- Revealed distinct density contrasts between burrow fillings and host rock matrix in pipe framework styles. ([[2025-refaat|Refaat, 2025, p. 50]])
