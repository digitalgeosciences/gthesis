# Hydrochemical Analysis

**Summary**: The chemical analysis of water to determine its quality and mineral content.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- Chemical analysis identified patterns in TDS and specific conductivity across different aquifer systems. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 150]])

- Analysis showed high concentrations of nitrate, nitrite, and aromatic hydrocarbons in shallow groundwater. ([[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011, p. 12]])

- Analysis revealed a sulphate-chloride type with Na+ and K+ as dominant cations, indicating high salinity. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 16]])

- Nitrate and nitrite were significantly higher in Dammam and Rahima; ammonia in Al-Khobar and Dammam; and phosphate in all three outfall locations compared to the control. ([[2015-yusuf|Yusuf, 2015, p. 14]])

- No heavy metals were found in excess of permitted standards, indicating a non-polluted site. ([[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015, p. 107]])

- Groundwater had a TDS of approximately 5000 ppm with high concentrations of chloride and sulfate. ([[2016-sameh|Sameh, 2016, p. 86]])

- Wasia groundwater quality generally ranges from poor to unsuitable for drinking according to WQI. ([[2019-ahmed|Ahmed, 2019, p. 13]])

- 67% of samples showed high nitrate levels, likely from fertilizers. ([[2020-mansouri|Mansouri, 2020, p. 13]])

- Used to determine the source, origin, and flow path of the thermal waters. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 52]])

- Analysis of Al Ardah hot springs provided data for characterizing the thermal water type. ([[2023-arrofi|Arrofi, 2023, p. 54]])

- Dendrogram clustering identified distinct groups based on chemical parameters. ([[2024-ahmed|Ahmed, 2024, p. 90]])

- Characterization of TWW and groundwater is essential to manage mixing ratios and assess long-term aquifer health. ([[2026-fatima|Fatima, 2026, p. 52]])

- Brine salinity increases with the rise of water level following rainfall and decreases during summer evaporation loss. ([[1994-al-guwaizani|Al-Guwaizani, 1994, p. 12]])

- Chloride is used as a conservative and highly mobile aqueous species, making it an ideal indicator of possible saltwater intrusion. ([[1995-hasan|Hasan, 1995, p. 113]])

- Analysis revealed that both aquifers are oversaturated with calcite and dolomite, while undersaturated with gypsum. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 19]])
