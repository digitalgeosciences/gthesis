# Hydrochemical Analysis

**Summary**: The chemical analysis of water to determine its quality and mineral content.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993]] applied hydrochemical analysis to evaluate the chemical quality (TDS, specific conductivity) of aquifers like Baidoa, Jesomma, Aurado, Taleh, and Merca.

- [[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011]] used hydrochemical-analysis to evaluate heavy metals, nitrates, nitrites, and hydrocarbons in groundwater.

- [[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012]] performed hydrochemical-analysis on eight groundwater samples to characterize the aquifer's chemical signature.

- [[2015-yusuf|Yusuf, 2015]] used hydrochemical-analysis to determine nutrient concentrations (nitrate, nitrite, ammonia, phosphate) at outfall sites.

- [[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015]] applied hydrochemical-analysis to assess heavy metals, hydrocarbons, and nutrients.

- [[2016-sameh|Sameh, 2016]] performed hydrochemical-analysis on groundwater samples to assess salinity effects.

## Key Results and Findings

- Chemical analysis identified patterns in TDS and specific conductivity across different aquifer systems. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 150]])

- Analysis showed high concentrations of nitrate, nitrite, and aromatic hydrocarbons in shallow groundwater. ([[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011, p. 12]])

- Analysis revealed a sulphate-chloride type with Na+ and K+ as dominant cations, indicating high salinity. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 16]])

- Nitrate and nitrite were significantly higher in Dammam and Rahima; ammonia in Al-Khobar and Dammam; and phosphate in all three outfall locations compared to the control. ([[2015-yusuf|Yusuf, 2015, p. 14]])

- No heavy metals were found in excess of permitted standards, indicating a non-polluted site. ([[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015, p. 107]])

- Groundwater had a TDS of approximately 5000 ppm with high concentrations of chloride and sulfate. ([[2016-sameh|Sameh, 2016, p. 86]])
