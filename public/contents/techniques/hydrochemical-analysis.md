# Hydrochemical Analysis

**Summary**: The chemical analysis of water to determine its mineral content and suitability for use.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- Analysis included TDS and specific conductivity measurements to characterize water types. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 150]])

- Analysis revealed high concentrations of nitrate (up to 5322 ppm) and phosphate (up to 6030 ppm) in shallow groundwater. ([[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011, p. 76]])

- Samples were classified as alkaline water with a sulfate-chloride signature, unsuitable for drinking or irrigation. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 16]])

- Nitrate and nitrite were significantly higher in Dammam and Rahima compared to the control site. ([[2015-yusuf|Yusuf, 2015, p. 14]])

- Nitrate concentrations reached up to 395.2 mg/l with an average of 68.2 mg/l. ([[2019-ahmed-2|Ahmed, 2019, p. 41]])

- 60% of the water samples had NO3- above permissible limits, posing a high risk for drinking. ([[2020-mansouri|Mansouri, 2020, p. 104]])

- Identified the water as a Na-HCO3 type with high chloride content, indicating significant rock-water interaction. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 85]])

- Hot spring waters in Jizan were found to be chloride water type, indicating a deep source. ([[2023-arrofi|Arrofi, 2023, p. 38]])

- Analysis showed a predominance of Na-Cl type water, indicating influence from evaporation and dissolution of evaporite rocks. ([[2024-ahmed|Ahmed, 2024, p. 111]])

- Na+ and Cl- show a 60% increase from summer to winter, linked to the dissolution of halite during high water levels. ([[1994-al-guwaizani|Al-Guwaizani, 1994, p. 75]])

- Analysis revealed that both aquifers are over-saturated with calcite and dolomite, and under-saturated with gypsum. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 19]])
