# Pendant Drop

**Summary**: A method to measure interfacial tension and contact angle of fluids on surfaces.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2021-alqam|ALQAM, 2021]] used pendant drop measurements to determine the impact of carbonated water on the contact angle and IFT.

- [[2023-al-hakami|Al-Hakami, 2023]] applied the pendant drop method via a Drop Shape Analyzer (DSA 100 HP) to determine IFT and subsequent MMP.

- [[2024-muhammed|Muhammed, 2024]] used pendant drop method to determine surface tension (ST) for various gas-mixture/brine systems.

## Key Results and Findings

- The pendant drop system measured contact angles for carbonate samples, showing a shift from oil-wet to water-wet with carbonated water. ([[2021-alqam|ALQAM, 2021, p. 120]])

- IFT values were measured at 50 and 90°C for various crude oils and chemical concentrations. ([[2023-al-hakami|Al-Hakami, 2023, p. 92]])

- Surface tension of the gas-mixture/brine systems declined with increasing pressure, temperature, and CO2 fraction. ([[2024-muhammed|Muhammed, 2024, p. 91]])
