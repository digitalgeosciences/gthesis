## Key Results and Findings

- Referenced in this thesis. ([[2010-al-moqbel|Al-Moqbel, 2010]])

- Model 1 (oil-sand) and Model 2 (gas-sand) showed distinct differences in TPP response, while Models 1 and 3 both exhibited gas-sand characteristics. ([[2014-kola|Kola, 2014, p. 14]])

- The stiff-sand model was the most appropriate for this reservoir's lithology. ([[2016-houssain|Houssain, 2016, p. 84]])

- The 2D viscoelastic model included Vp, Vs, Qp, Qs, and density values for various formations. ([[2018-abdulrahman|Abdulrahman, 2018, p. 25]])

- FWI successfully reconstructed velocity images with small local anomalies and strong contrast. ([[2018-mohamed|Mohamed, 2018, p. 15]])

- A robust velocity model was achieved by integrating seismic gather flatness with magnetic-derived depth constraints. ([[2020-abdallah-2|Abdallah, 2020, p. 84]])

- The stiff-sand model was selected as the primary RPM for its rigor and ability to handle parameter adjustments. ([[2020-almumtin|Almumtin, 2020, p. 48]])

- The constant-cement model was selected as the most robust RPM for both TGS and high-porosity sand units. ([[2021-suleymanov|Suleymanov, 2021, p. 55]])

- Corrected NMO velocities were successfully derived from imperfectly corrected data. ([[2023-muhammad|Muhammad, 2023, p. 16]])

- A range of 5,000 to 25,000 ft/s was identified as the standard physical range for subsurface velocities. ([[2023-almarzooq|Almarzooq, 2023, p. 35]])

- The stiff-rock model successfully captured the data for both carbonate and clastic systems. ([[2024-alabbad|Alabbad, 2024, p. 53]])

- Integrated models provided more accurate statics than models based solely on sparse upholes. ([[2002-al-ali|Al-Ali, 2002, p. 19]])
