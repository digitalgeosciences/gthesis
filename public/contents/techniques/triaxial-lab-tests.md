# Triaxial Lab Tests

**Summary**: A method to measure the mechanical properties of rock samples under triaxial stress conditions.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016]] used triaxial lab tests to determine tensile strength, friction angle, and cohesion in Khuff A, B, and C units.

- [[2023-al-garni|Al-Garni, 2023]] applied triaxial tests to determine Young's modulus and permeability under high confining pressures up to 88 MPa.

## Key Results and Findings

- Friction angles from triaxial tests ranged from 28.4° to 44.5° in Khuff A and 22.4° to 60.9° in Khuff B. ([[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016, p. 95]])

- The study reported the permeability of samples under increasing confining pressure using a triaxial/acoustic cell. ([[2023-al-garni|Al-Garni, 2023, p. 12]])
