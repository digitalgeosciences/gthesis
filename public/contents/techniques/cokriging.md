# Cokriging

**Summary**: A geostatistical method that uses secondary data (e.g., seismic) to improve the estimation of a primary variable (e.g., porosity).

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2002-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2002]] applied cokriging to integrate high-density seismic impedance with sparse well logs.

- [[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001]] utilized collocated-cokriging to integrate seismic impedance as soft data into the 3-D porosity model.

- [[2002-al-ali|Al-Ali, 2002]] applied cokriging to integrate dense vibrator data with sparse uphole data.

## Key Results and Findings

- Cokriging reduced variance and improved resolution compared to standard kriging, with errors between 0% and 5.5%. ([[2002-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2002, p. 129]])

- Collocated cokriging was identified as a powerful and fast method for integrating high-density seismic data with sparse well data. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 94]])

- Cokriging produced a more detailed and accurate velocity map than standard kriging by incorporating the denser vibrator dataset. ([[2002-al-ali|Al-Ali, 2002, p. 74]])
