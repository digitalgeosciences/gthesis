# Ultrasonic Velocity Measurement

**Summary**: A method to measure the speed of sound through rock samples, often used as a proxy for porosity and lithology.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000]] used ultrasonic-velocity-measurement on core samples saturated with different TDS levels to determine acoustic properties.

- [[2012-elhaj|Elhaj, 2012]] applied ultrasonic-velocity-measurement to determine Vp and Vs as functions of confining pressure and saturation.

- [[2014-al-ameri|AL-AMERI, 2014]] used ultrasonic velocity measurements to evaluate the impact of CO2 on rock stiffness and elasticity.

- [[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015]] performed ultrasonic velocity measurements on 19 shale samples using Autolab 500 equipment.

- [[2016-ejaz|Ejaz, 2016]] applied ultrasonic-velocity-measurement using Autolab 500 equipment to determine Young's modulus and Poisson's ratio.

- [[2019-al-garadi|AL-GARADI, 2019]] used ultrasonic velocity measurements in conjunction with NMR to monitor changes in core samples.

- [[2021-lawal|LAWAL, 2021]] applied ultrasonic velocity measurement at three orientations (0, 45, 90) under varying confining pressures to calculate the dynamic index of brittleness.

## Key Results and Findings

- Established relationships between ultrasonic wave velocity and porosity across various saturation states. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 32]])

- P-wave velocity increased while S-wave velocity decreased with confining pressure under saturated conditions. ([[2012-elhaj|Elhaj, 2012, p. 13]])

- Ultrasonic measurements were conducted at confining pressures from 5 MPa to 20 MPa for both virgin and CO2-saturated samples. ([[2014-al-ameri|AL-AMERI, 2014, p. 49]])

- Determined P-wave and S-wave velocities at various confining pressures (2000-6000 psi). ([[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015, p. 84]])

- Sample 8H showed a significant increase in compressional and shear velocity with an increase in confining pressure. ([[2016-ejaz|Ejaz, 2016, p. 55]])

- Ultrasonic measurements were conducted on both untreated and treated cores to observe physical property changes. ([[2019-al-garadi|AL-GARADI, 2019, p. 34]])

- The orientation of cracks influenced the acoustic velocities, which were used to derive the dynamic brittleness index. ([[2021-lawal|LAWAL, 2021, p. 123]])
