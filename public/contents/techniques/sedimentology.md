**Summary**: The study of the processes and/or causes of the formation of sedimentary rocks.

## Key Results and Findings

- Lithofacies were identified as nearshore, glaciofluvial, glaciolacustrine delta, and subglacial environments. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 137]])

- Not stated in source ([[1995-al-jaziri|Al-Jaziri, 1995]])

- Petrographic analysis allows for the determination of petrophysical properties from rock textures. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 41]])

- Identified 12 distinct microfacies types in the deposits. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 46]])

- Identified a transition from subtidal to restricted lagoonal environments in the dolomite interval. ([[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988, p. 19]])

- Sedimentary facies are used as an intermediate step to link geological observations (core/seismic) to petrophysical properties. ([[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010, p. 43]])

- Identified five distinct litho-facies based on sedimentary structure and texture. ([[2011-al-masrahy|AL-MASRAHY, 2011, p. 48]])

- Sedimentology revealed that mudstone is dominant in the area, leading to generally poor porosity. ([[2013-yousif|Yousif, 2013, p. 66]])

- Identified sedimentary structures like trough cross bedding and horizontal stratification as indicators of fluvial systems. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 72]])

- Sedimentological heterogeneity is a key factor in reservoir quality. ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 164]])

- The investigation revealed that the studied part of Upper Khartam comprising of six lithofacies association deposited in three depositional settings. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 17]])

- Al Ula is dominated by high-energy, braided-fluvial, in-channel deposition. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 25]])

- Sediment samples from Jubail, Khobar and Qatif are significantly different from the control location (p<0.05). ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 42]])

- Arab-D members were deposited in deep to shallow lagoonal settings. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 77]])

- The sedimentary structure and grain size of Foraminiferal peloidal grainstone (Fpg) facies indicates high energy, below the fair weather wave base. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 116]])

- Grain size analysis showed a gradual decrease of fine sand content seaward. ([[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015, p. 105]])

- Lithofacies analysis revealed a mixed siliciclastic-carbonate deposit with 21 different lithofacies identified. ([[2016-hassan-2|Hassan, 2016, p. 158]])

- Fifteen distinct lithofacies were identified based on lithology, structure, and fossils. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 46]])

- Identified distinct sedimentary environments including alluvial fan, fluvial, shoreface, and lacustrine. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 17]])

- Identified a variety of environments including shoals, lagoons, and tidal flats. ([[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017, p. 80]])

- Sediments are influenced by both biological communities and submarine topography. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 136]])

- Lithofacies were grouped into five major carbonate paleoenvironments ranging from distal-to-proximal carbonate ramp settings. ([[2017-yousif|Yousif, 2017, p. 173]])

- The Nutaysh member is characterized by a submarine fan system with varying paleocurrent directions. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 24]])

- Identified four distinct siliciclastic lithofacies representing a shoreface progradation. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 44]])

- Identified distinct sedimentary features such as grainflow, wind ripples, and mud-flat related deposits in the dunes. ([[2022-al-alawi|Al-Alawi, 2022, p. 80]])

- The sedimentary environment was interpreted as glacio-fluvial to glaciolacustrine. ([[2021-alqarni|Alqarni, 2021, p. 125]])

- Oolitic grainstones and packstones indicate high-energy shoal settings with varying degrees of sorting. ([[2021-alabyadh|Alabyadh, 2021, p. 100]])

- Host rock was deposited in a low energy - quit water environment below the wave base. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 81]])

- Sediment was sampled from both high tide and low tide zones using 0.5m x 0.5m quadrates. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 57]])

- QCI values of sandstones vary temporally and are associated with abundance of siliceous sponges and eolian environments. ([[2022-radwan|Radwan, 2022, p. 82]])

- Nine sedimentary lithofacies were interpreted, which are grouped into four facies associations. ([[2022-bashri|Bashri, 2022, p. 46]])

- Identified distinct sedimentary environments including channel-side bars, point bars, and mid-channel bars. ([[2022-argadestya|Argadestya, 2022, p. 61]])

- Ten lithofacies were identified based on petrographic characteristics and sedimentary structures. ([[2022-babker|Babker, 2022, p. 56]])

- Identified four distinct facies associations (FA1-FA4) representing an alluvial-braided fluvial fan system. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 64]])

- Sediment core 25 showed higher BFAR (111.4-608 n/cm2/yr) than Core 12 (35.8-516.5 n/cm2/yr). ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 53]])

- Sediments were predominantly sand-sized (95-99%) across all sites. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 65]])

- The study integrated sedimentology, seismic stratigraphy, and biostratigraphy. ([[2024-fahmi|Fahmi, 2024, p. 42]])

- Not stated in source ([[2024-suleiman-5|Suleiman, 2024]])

- Identified fluctuations between shallow platform and shelf environments. ([[1989-abdou|Abdou, 1989, p. 17]])

- The sedimentary structure (mainly large scale, low angle cross-bedding and cross-lamination) ... indicate a braided river depositional environment. ([[1999-babalola|Babalola, 1999, p. 18]])

- Dolomitic rocks represent upward-shallowing cycles from subtidal to restricted lagoonal environments. ([[1988-darwish-2|Darwish, 1988, p. 108]])
