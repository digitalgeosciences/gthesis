# Lithofacies Analysis

**Summary**: The classification of rocks based on their physical and chemical characteristics as a result of depositional processes.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011]] performed lithofacies-analysis to identify five distinct depositional environments in the Arab-C.

- [[2013-mark|Mark, 2013]] used lithofacies-analysis to differentiate pressure from other factors.

- [[2013-atig|atig, 2013]] performed lithofacies-analysis to categorize the core samples into six distinct types.

- [[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014]] used lithofacies analysis to characterize the sedimentary architecture of the Sarah Formation.

- [[2016-tariq|Tariq, 2016]] performed lithology identification using density-neutron porosity cross plots to characterize the reservoir.

- [[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022]] performed lithofacies-analysis to differentiate between host rock and burrow infills.

## Key Results and Findings

- Five facies associations were identified: Sabkha, Tidal flat (stromatolites), Lagoon, Shoal, and Foreshoal. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 56]])

- Traditional methods were often masked by rock properties such as formation lithology and hydrocarbon content. ([[2013-mark|Mark, 2013, p. 65]])

- Six primary facies were identified: channel, splay, mouth bar, bay, swamp, and marine. ([[2013-atig|atig, 2013, p. 40]])

- Not stated in source ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014]])

- Well No. 1 was identified as a limestone region with traces of sandy-lime formation. ([[2016-tariq|Tariq, 2016, p. 82]])

- Host rock was interpreted as a product of different water bathymetry (middle ramp). ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 73]])
