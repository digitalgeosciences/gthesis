# Stochastic Simulation

**Summary**: A stochastic simulation technique used to generate multiple equally probable realizations of a spatial variable.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- Conditional simulation was used to determine the fluctuation pattern of P20% within the deposit. ([[1996-jarad|Jarad, 1996, p. 20]])

- SIS was used to define the distribution of eight distinct facies within the SFNC sequence. ([[1997-al-qassab|Al-Qassab, 1997, p. 17]])

- Monte Carlo simulations produced good hydrocarbon volume estimates despite the uncertainty in basic parameters. ([[2003-al-shridi|Al-Shridi, 2003, p. 13]])

- SIS provides high variability but accurate anisotropy and semi-variogram measures of spatial correlation. ([[2013-atig|atig, 2013, p. 31]])

- The SIS models revealed a more realistic vertical succession of lithofacies compared to kriging results, showing sand as the dominant lithofacies. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 157]])

- The SIS model showed a layer cake pattern in low resolution, highlighting challenges with thin layers during upscaling. ([[2017-yousif|Yousif, 2017, p. 20]])

- The SIS algorithm successfully modeled the distribution of facies association in four distinct zones. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 209]])

- The simulation results stabilized once the number of realizations exceeded 17,000. ([[2024-astsauri|Astsauri, 2024, p. 44]])
