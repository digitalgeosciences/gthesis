# Neural Networks

**Summary**: A computational modeling technique used to find patterns in data and predict outcomes based on input variables.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2002-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2002]] utilized neural-networks for multi-attribute transforms to model porosity and permeability.

- [[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002]] used neural networks to classify lithofacies and predict porosity in the Unayzah Formation.

- [[2001-sultan|SULTAN, 2001]] used neural-networks to identify well-test interpretation models and flow regimes in horizontal wells.

- [[2004-ayoub|AYOUB, 2004]] used neural-networks to predict bottomhole pressure in vertical multiphase flow.

- [[2009-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2009]] developed Artificial Neural Network (ANN) models to estimate flow rate and predict choke size.

- [[2009-al-ali|Al-Ali, 2009]] used artificial neural networks to predict missing LWD density data and correct environmental artifacts.

- [[2009-sitouah|Sitouah, 2009]] used neural networks to compare MLP and GRNN performance in predicting porosity and permeability.

- [[2010-al-dhamen|Al-Dhamen, 2010]] constructed an artificial neural network (ANN) with two hidden layers to estimate dew-point pressure.

- [[2011-al-dhufairi|AL-DHUFAIRI, 2011]] used ANN to predict mobility profiles using various training functions like trainlm and traingdx.

- [[2012-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2012]] used GRNN to predict gas flow rates in multi-layered reservoirs.

- [[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013]] applied neural-networks for facies classification using GR, DT, NPHI, RHOZ, and RLA5 logs.

- [[2013-atig|atig, 2013]] applied neural-networks to predict facies in 188 non-cored wells based on RHOB, NPHI, VSH, and PHIE logs.

- [[2014-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2014]] used neural-networks to establish a relationship between well log data and water saturation.

- [[2015-alsahaf|Alsahaf, 2015]] applied neural networks (specifically FFNN, RBFNN, and GRNN) to predict FZI values from well log data.

- [[2016-mahboob|MAHBOOB, 2016]] used artificial neural networks to develop empirical correlations for movable oil recovery factor based on simulation results.

- [[2016-kalam|Kalam, 2016]] used artificial neural networks to develop a new empirical correlation for predicting movable oil recovery from simulation data.

- [[2016-tariq|Tariq, 2016]] implemented ANN to predict sonic logs and rock mechanical parameters from well log data.

- [[2017-hasan|Hasan, 2017]] applied neural-networks to model the heterogeneity of the carbonate system in the Khuff formation.

- [[2018-al-kadem|Al-Kadem, 2018]] developed an ANN model to capture the full range of GOR for water cut estimation.

- [[2018-al-mashhad|Al-Mashhad, 2018]] implemented ANN models to predict oil flow rates in H and ML wells using various training functions like trainlm and traingdx.

- [[2019-elzenary|Elzenary, 2019]] used ANN to predict Equivalent Circulating Density (ECD) from surface parameters like ROP and mud weight.

- [[2019-ahmed|Ahmed, 2019]] implemented ANN with a one-layer configuration and five neurons to predict lost circulation.

- [[2020-abdallah|Abdallah, 2020]] used ANN to predict porosity and permeability from well log data (GR, RHOB, NPHI) to characterize potential CO2 sequestration sites.

- [[2020-al-abduljabbar|Al-AbdulJabbar, 2020]] used ANN to predict ROP across various well profiles and identify key drilling parameters.

- [[2021-aldhaif|AlDhaif, 2021]] used artificial neural network (ANN) models to estimate oil flow rates in three different flow regimes.

- [[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022]] developed an ANN model to predict TOC based on well-log data and XRF elements.

- [[2021-alsabaa|Alsabaa, 2021]] applied ANN techniques to develop models for predicting mud rheology properties.

- [[2022-jin|Jin, 2022]] utilized neural-networks, specifically LSTM, to handle time-series production data.

- [[2022-suleiman|Suleiman, 2022]] applied various ANN architectures (FFNN, CFNN, ELMNN) to predict permeability and rock types from NMR data.

- [[2022-algharbi|ALGHARBI, 2022]] used ANN to evaluate the performance of different data quality improvement techniques on drilling datasets.

- [[2022-alkathim|Alkathim, 2022]] used ANN to predict PVBT and develop an empirical correlation based on the best performing model.

- [[2023-alzayer|Alzayer, 2023]] implemented an artificial neural network (ANN) with a single layer and 5 neurons using Bayesian regularization for prediction.

- [[2023-gad|Gad, 2023]] used ANN to predict water saturation from well logs in the Prinos Basin.

- [[2023-suleymanli|Suleymanli, 2023]] used a four-layer feed-forward neural network to interpolate and extrapolate missing travel time values for receivers removed during testing.

- [[2023-almarzooq|Almarzooq, 2023]] implemented neural networks to automate the inversion of VSP corridor stacks.

- [[2024-almomen|Almomen, 2024]] applied ANN with a single layer, 4 neurons, and 'newpr' function to predict ROP.

- [[2023-saadeldin|SAADELDIN, 2023]] used ANN to predict three types of downhole vibrations (axial, lateral, and torsional) using surface drilling parameters.

- [[2005-ademola|ADEMOLA, 2005]] developed neural network models for all five PVT parameters to compare their performance against empirical correlations.

- [[2004-ayoub-2|AYOUB, 2004]] used artificial neural networks (ANN) to predict bottomhole pressure in vertical multiphase flow.

## Key Results and Findings

- Neural Network technology was used to reach higher confidence by calibrating geostatistical models with multi-attribute regression results. ([[2002-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2002, p. 130]])

- Neural networks achieved 95% classification accuracy for lithofacies across all three study wells. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 134]])

- The modular approach with direct data utilization was found most suitable for field implementation. ([[2001-sultan|SULTAN, 2001, p. 29]])

- The ANN model outperformed all tested empirical correlations and mechanistic models across multiple statistical metrics (correlation, AAPE, RMSE). ([[2004-ayoub|AYOUB, 2004, p. 3]])

- The ANN models outperformed existing correlations with average absolute percent errors of 3.7% for choke size and 6.7% for flow rate. ([[2009-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2009, p. 6]])

- The ANN model successfully predicted target values using a multi-layer architecture with hidden layers of sigmoid neurons. ([[2009-al-ali|Al-Ali, 2009, p. 32]])

- GRNN outperformed MLP in accuracy and robustness. ([[2009-sitouah|Sitouah, 2009, p. 16]])

- The ANN model was the best among all models, showing an average absolute error of 6.5%. ([[2010-al-dhamen|Al-Dhamen, 2010, p. 68]])

- The 'trainlm' function with two layers and preprocessing (mapstd, processpca) provided the highest accuracy (R=1.0) across multiple wells. ([[2011-al-dhufairi|AL-DHUFAIRI, 2011, p. 63]])

- The normalized GRNN model showed a high correlation coefficient and low mean absolute error (114 MSCF). ([[2012-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2012, p. 50]])

- Neural networks provided a basis for facies classification which was then used to assign petrophysical properties. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 14]])

- The model successfully predicted bay and marine facies but required grouping of fluvial categories (channel, splay, mouthbar) into one category to improve accuracy. ([[2013-atig|atig, 2013, p. 43]])

- The ANN model was shown to have excellent results in predicting subsurface fluids (water, oil, and gas) when tested against actual data values. ([[2014-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2014, p. 33]])

- FFNN was identified as the best-performing neural network model for FZI prediction compared to RBFNN and GRNN. ([[2015-alsahaf|Alsahaf, 2015, p. 59]])

- Both correlation models developed using the artificial neural network technique matched all simulator results with high accuracy. ([[2016-mahboob|MAHBOOB, 2016, p. 21]])

- The ANN-based correlation matches simulator results with high accuracy and successfully predicts both unseen simulations and field project performance. ([[2016-kalam|Kalam, 2016, p. 25]])

- The study utilized ANN as one of three primary AI techniques to model the relationship between input variables (like Gamma Ray and Bulk Density) and target outputs like Young's Modulus. ([[2016-tariq|Tariq, 2016, p. 48]])

- Neural networks yielded R^2 values of 0.73 for training and 0.71 for testing. ([[2017-hasan|Hasan, 2017, p. 21]])

- The ANN model with five inputs achieved an average Ear% of 8.13% and Eaa% of 2.93% for all wells, including high GOR cases. ([[2018-al-kadem|Al-Kadem, 2018, p. 104]])

- ANN models offered the highest accuracy with an AAPE of 6.9% compared to 38% for existing correlations. ([[2018-al-mashhad|Al-Mashhad, 2018, p. 18]])

- The ANN model achieved a correlation coefficient of 0.9982 for testing data with an AAPE of 0.2237%. ([[2019-elzenary|Elzenary, 2019, p. 67]])

- The ANN model achieved a correlation coefficient of 0.994 and RMSE of 0.053 on the testing dataset. ([[2019-ahmed|Ahmed, 2019, p. 117]])

- An ANN with 18 neurons in the hidden layer and a log-sigmoidal transfer function was optimized for predicting permeability. ([[2020-abdallah|Abdallah, 2020, p. 15]])

- ANN models were shown to provide high accuracy for ROP prediction, with some studies reporting correlation coefficients as high as 0.99. ([[2020-al-abduljabbar|Al-AbdulJabbar, 2020, p. 33]])

- The ANN model was the recommended model for both Subcritical (WC=0) and Critical flow cases, with R2 values of 0.979 and 0.9801 respectively. ([[2021-aldhaif|AlDhaif, 2021, p. 49]])

- The model provides a continuous, high-resolution vertical and lateral distribution of TOC. ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 175]])

- The ANN models showed a correlation coefficient higher than 0.96 and AAPE less than 3.34% for the training set. ([[2021-alsabaa|Alsabaa, 2021, p. 59]])

- The LSTM architecture was found superior for capturing temporal dependencies in well production data. ([[2022-jin|Jin, 2022, p. 52]])

- The Elman network (ELMNN) with Bayesian regularization outperformed both FFNN and CFNN in predicting permeability. ([[2022-suleiman|Suleiman, 2022, p. 51]])

- An ANN architecture achieved a MAPE of 6.2% when predicting hook-load from raw data. ([[2022-algharbi|ALGHARBI, 2022, p. 48]])

- The ANN model with 2 hidden layers outperformed other models with an AAPE of 11.27% and a CC of 0.98. ([[2022-alkathim|Alkathim, 2022, p. 98]])

- The ANN model achieved high accuracy with an AAPE of 4.22% during training. ([[2023-alzayer|Alzayer, 2023, p. 27]])

- The ANN model achieved a correlation coefficient (R) of 0.999 and an AAPE of less than 2% for training and testing data. ([[2023-gad|Gad, 2023, p. 40]])

- The 4-layer, 60-node per layer architecture successfully predicted missing receiver values to allow FNO processing. ([[2023-suleymanli|Suleymanli, 2023, p. 31]])

- The consecutive neural network approach (denoising, feature extraction, and inversion) outperformed standalone models in speed and accuracy. ([[2023-almarzooq|Almarzooq, 2023, p. 16]])

- The ANN model achieved an R of 0.96 and AAPE of 4.68% on training data. ([[2024-almomen|Almomen, 2024, p. 78]])

- The ANN model achieved R values of 0.99 for axial and lateral vibrations and 0.96 for torsional vibrations with AAPE below 2.4%. ([[2023-saadeldin|SAADELDIN, 2023, p. 38]])

- Neural Network models' predictions were better for all PVT properties studied compared to standard correlations. ([[2005-ademola|ADEMOLA, 2005, p. 3]])

- The ANN model outperformed all tested empirical correlations and mechanistic models in terms of correlation coefficients and error metrics. ([[2004-ayoub-2|AYOUB, 2004, p. 3]])
