# Forward Modeling

**Summary**: The process of calculating predicted signals based on a known model to compare against observed data.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-own|Own, 2015]] used forward-modeling to evaluate the impact of different velocity models and geometries.

- [[2018-hanif|Hanif, 2018]] used forward-modeling to determine the density of karstified zones.

- [[2021-suleymanov|Suleymanov, 2021]] used forward-modeling to create 'what-if' scenarios for lithology, fluid, and porosity variations.

- [[2024-fahmi|Fahmi, 2024]] used stratigraphic forward modeling to quantify the controlling parameters in the Marrat Formation's sedimentation process.

## Key Results and Findings

- Fermat's ray tracing (Cat3D) was used to calculate travel times in 3D structural velocity models. ([[2015-own|Own, 2015, p. 56]])

- The total misfit for the forward modeling was 0.5 mGal. ([[2018-hanif|Hanif, 2018, p. 75]])

- Forward modeling generated a suite of AVO curves and synthetic seismograms that serve as field guides for interpretation. ([[2021-suleymanov|Suleymanov, 2021, p. 63]])

- The model successfully captured lateral and vertical texture/facies heterogeneity in regional-scale geometries under a third sequence stratigraphic framework. ([[2024-fahmi|Fahmi, 2024, p. 113]])
