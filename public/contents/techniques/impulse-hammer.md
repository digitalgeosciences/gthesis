# Impulse Hammer

**Summary**: A non-destructive method to estimate rock mechanical properties in the field or lab.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2020-gbiyanju|Gbiyanju, 2020]] used impulse hammer testing to provide a non-destructive alternative to triaxial tests.

- [[2021-albensaad|Albensaad, 2021]] applied impulse hammer testing using the New England Research Autoscan tool to determine reduced Young's modulus (E*).

- [[2022-samarkin|Samarkin, 2022]] used impulse hammering via AutoscanTM to determine the Young's modulus and hardness of samples.

## Key Results and Findings

- The highest response was found in Well 2 (78 GPa) and the lowest in Well 5 (11 GPa). ([[2021-albensaad|Albensaad, 2021, p. 57]])

- Hardness measurements using the impulse hammer showed significant increases in limestone and chalk after treatment. ([[2022-samarkin|Samarkin, 2022, p. 56]])
