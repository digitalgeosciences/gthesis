# Morphology Analysis

**Summary**: The study of the size, shape, and structure of organisms to identify species.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016]] performed morphological identification to characterize the isolated strains.

- [[2017-amao|Amao, 2017]] analyzed morphological abnormalities in foraminifera to distinguish between epibiont effects and pollution.

- [[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018]] used morphology-analysis to categorize foraminifera into groups A, B, and C.

- [[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023]] analyzed morphological deformities in foraminiferal tests to identify environmental stress indicators.

## Key Results and Findings

- Three strains were identified as Chlorella kessleri, Chlorella vulgaris, and Nanochloropsis oculata. ([[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016, p. 11]])

- Abnormalities occurred at a rate of 1.9%, exceeding the 1% threshold for natural populations. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 30]])

- Morphogroup A1 is interpreted as suspension feeders in a muddy part of lower shoreface environment. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 64]])

- Morphological deformities were frequently observed in LBF assemblages, reaching levels exceeding 40%. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 75]])
