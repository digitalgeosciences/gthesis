# Molecular Microbiology

**Summary**: The use of molecular techniques to identify and characterize microbial species based on genetic material.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2011-salamah|Salamah, 2011]] used 16S rRNA sequencing to characterize the isolated bacterial strains Pseudoxanthomonas sp. and Bacillus sp.

- [[2012-amao|Amao, 2012]] applied DNA isolation, PCR, and DGG to genetically identify isolated endolithic cyanobacteria.

- [[2015-oyehan|Oyehan, 2015]] used 16S rRNA sequencing and phylogenetic analysis to identify the 15 bacterial isolates.

- [[2015-malik|Malik, 2015]] used 16S RNA to identify bacterial strains from petroleum polluted waters in the Arabian Gulf.

- [[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015]] applied 18S rDNA sequencing and ML methods to characterize the molecular phylogeny of foraminifera.

- [[2017-budiyanto|Budiyanto, 2017]] used 16S RNA sequences to identify isolated halophile bacteria species and construct a phylogenetic tree.

## Key Results and Findings

- Two strains were successfully identified as Pseudoxanthomonas sp. and Bacillus sp. using 16S rRNA gene sequencing. ([[2011-salamah|Salamah, 2011, p. 11]])

- Molecular tools are required to separate species based on unique DNA makeup when morphometric criteria fail. ([[2012-amao|Amao, 2012, p. 20]])

- 87% of the isolates were similar to P. aeruginosa, while others were related to Cupriavidus taiwanensis, P. citronellolis, and Ochrobactrum intermedium. ([[2015-oyehan|Oyehan, 2015, p. 18]])

- Not stated in source ([[2015-malik|Malik, 2015]])

- All genotypic groups were monophyletic with bootstrap values of 97-100%. ([[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015, p. 63]])

- Used to identify specific strains from the Halobacteriaceae family. ([[2017-budiyanto|Budiyanto, 2017, p. 25]])
