## Key Results and Findings

- Geomechanics is the study of rock behavior, applicable to wellbore stability, sand production, and reservoir compaction. ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 19]])

- Geomechanical parameters like Young's modulus and Poisson's ratio were used to develop a 1-D MEM. ([[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015, p. 35]])

- Three geomechanical units were established based on the relationship between geomechanical properties and lithofacies characteristics. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 81]])

- Geomechanical parameters like point load, Schmidt hammer, and UCS were used to define five distinct geomechanical units. ([[2016-babker|Babker, 2016, p. 126]])

- Geomechanical properties like Young's modulus and Poisson's ratio are key indicators of rock brittleness. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 39]])

- High Young's modulus and low Poisson's ratio indicate a brittle nature favorable for hydraulic fracturing. ([[2016-ejaz|Ejaz, 2016, p. 48]])

- Identified a strike-slip stress regime where sigma_Hmax > sigma_v > sigma_hmin. ([[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016, p. 119]])

- ANFIS was identified as the most accurate method for predicting UCS in limestone. ([[2016-tariq|Tariq, 2016, p. 125]])

- Geomechanical parameters like Schmidt hammer and point load index were significantly higher in sandstone units than in shale units. ([[2017-mahgoub|Mahgoub, 2017, p. 19]])

- Coupled geomechanical and stability analyses were performed for both single-porosity and naturally fractured systems. ([[2017-khan|Khan, 2017, p. 39]])

- Juwayl Formation showed the highest average values for all three geomechanical parameters (RN: 31.40, UCS: 7.97 MPa, E: 48.28 GPa). ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 97]])

- Geomechanics plays a key role in determining fracture length, height, and width, as well as identifying potential well failures. ([[2017-muqtadir|Muqtadir, 2017, p. 33]])

- 3D geomechanical models were found more helpful than 1D models for designing hydraulic fractures in complex depleted tight gas reservoirs as they capture stress contrasts in adjacent layers. ([[2018-aldossary|AlDossary, 2018, p. 80]])

- The MEM was calibrated using core data to ensure it was robust enough to predict loss circulation events. ([[2019-al-nutaifi|Al-Nutaifi, 2019, p. 27]])

- Thermal aging at 150°C was used to simulate reservoir conditions for both sandstone and limestone. ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019, p. 73]])

- Young's moduli for kerogen were approximately three times higher than those of asphaltene, but asphaltene was more resistant to compression (higher Bulk modulus). ([[2021-aslanov|Aslanov, 2021, p. 56]])

- Young's modulus, Poisson's ratio, and UCS were identified as key parameters for evaluating rock geomechanical behavior. ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 27]])

- TML mudrocks showed less brittleness than Hanifa due to higher clay content. ([[2022-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2022, p. 20]])

- Young's modulus reduction was linked to the time required for wormhole generation. ([[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023, p. 103]])

- Determined that macroscale behavior is influenced by both soft and hard components in a proportion corresponding to their frequencies. ([[2024-alvayed|Alvayed, 2024, p. 31]])

- Exposure to CO2-saturated brine caused reductions of up to 30% in elastic moduli (Young's modulus and shear modulus). ([[2024-badhafere|BADHAFERE, 2024, p. 35]])

- The relationship between the magnitudes of the three principal stresses suggests a normal fault regime. ([[2002-elhassan|ElHassan, 2002, p. 19]])
