# Micro-XRF

**Summary**: A non-destructive technique to determine the elemental composition and mineralogy of rock samples.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2020-gbiyanju|Gbiyanju, 2020]] used MicroXRF to map elements and identify minerals in core plugs from the Khuff, Unayzah, and Qusaiba formations.

- [[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022]] used micro-XRF to determine the concentration of major and trace elements across the basin.

- [[2022-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2022]] applied micro-XRF to determine the composition of each lamina, burrow fill, and matrix.

## Key Results and Findings

- Identified Co, Sr, Ti, and Zr as proxy indicators for rock strength based on elemental distribution maps. ([[2020-gbiyanju|Gbiyanju, 2020, p. 90]])

- Elemental analysis from micro-XRF showed high Ca, moderate Si/Al/Ti/K/Fe, and fluctuations in Mo, Ni, Cu, V. ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 95]])

- Used to identify Chemo-Sedimentary Facies (CSFA) to evaluate sedimentary fabrics. ([[2022-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2022, p. 52]])
