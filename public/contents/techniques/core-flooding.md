# Core Flooding

**Summary**: A laboratory technique where fluids are pumped through a core sample to simulate reservoir displacement.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- Coreflooding experiments were performed at 90°C and 1500 psi using various brine concentrations and actual waters (Sea, Wasia). ([[1990-al-mumen|Al-Mumen, 1990, p. 37]])

- Permeability reduction ranged from 92% to 99.8% across various cores. ([[1997-al-nashmi|Al-Nashmi, 1997, p. 126]])

- The combination of solvent and hot water reduced injection pressure compared to cold or hot water alone. ([[1995-okasha|OKASHA, 1995, p. 10]])

- The study used core flooding to compare EDTA at different concentrations against conventional waterflooding. ([[2013-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2013, p. 22]])

- Core flooding experiments showed that chelating agents provided up to 30% additional oil recovery from OOIP. ([[2013-elsayed|Elsayed, 2013, p. 14]])

- The core flood showed an ultimate oil recovery of about 56% after approximately 5 pore volumes of CO2 were injected. ([[2013-al-mutairi|AL-MUTAIRI, 2013, p. 95]])

- The difference in pressure dropped immediately indicating the completion of a breakthrough and the connection between the two ends of the core plug samples. ([[2014-al-duailej|Al-Duailej, 2014, p. 47]])

- Core-flooding experiments provided the nearest depiction of an actual reservoir that can be achieved on the lab scale. ([[2014-jangda|Jangda, 2014, p. 57]])

- Core flooding showed that a 0.3% VES injection provided an incremental recovery of 12% over water flooding in carbonate samples. ([[2014-azad|Azad, 2014, p. 73]])

- Core-flooding experiments were conducted on Indiana limestone core plugs at reservoir conditions to observe wettibility changes. ([[2014-shakeel|SHAKEEL, 2014, p. 40]])

- Core flooding studies showed that the optimum injection rate for the chelating agents was in the range of 1.5 – 2.0 cc/m. ([[2015-barri|Barri, 2015, p. 22]])

- The use of GLDA at pH 4.3 resulted in an increase in the oil production rate by 60% without increasing water cut. ([[2015-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2015, p. 61]])

- Core flooding showed that 10 wt.% EDTA at pH 11 significantly improved permeability in Indiana limestone. ([[2015-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2015, p. 55]])

- The D20-C3-sea formulation yielded a permeability ratio of 1.40, indicating high effectiveness in stabilizing clays while enhancing flow. ([[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016, p. 69]])

- Steady-state foam flooding experiments were performed in Berea Grey Sandstone cores to study stability and texture. ([[2016-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2016, p. 41]])

- Used to measure stable pressure drop across different injection rates. ([[2016-adam|ADAM, 2016, p. 63]])

- Acid injection (15 wt. % HCl) caused a significant reduction in permeability from 4.1 mD to 0.85 mD in Bandera sandstone. ([[2016-jad|Jad, 2016, p. 44]])

- The 10wt% encapsulated citric acid experiment yielded the highest recovery factor of 84%. ([[2014-alam|ALAM, 2014, p. 18]])

- The Berea sample showed a regained permeability of 85.2% after being flooded with the high-viscosity fluid. ([[2017-mohammed-3|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 128]])

- Porosity decreased from 26% to 22% after one month of exposure in low-pressure systems. ([[2017-ahmed|Ahmed, 2017, p. 49]])

- Coreflooding experiments showed that foam significantly improved oil recovery compared to pure gas injection. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 14]])

- The novel waste oil acid achieved an optimum injection rate of 5 cc/min, resulting in lower PVBT compared to other fluids at higher rates. ([[2017-ahmed-2|AHMED, 2017, p. 80]])

- Identified 0.5 cm3/min as the optimal injection rate for GLDA/SW based on PDR analysis. ([[2017-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2017, p. 114]])

- The core-flooding experiments supported the importance of optimizing the design of CEOR processes considering chemical type, concentration, slug size, and sequence. ([[2017-ahmed-3|Ahmed, 2017, p. 19]])

- Core flooding experiments were conducted on Indiana limestone samples at 100°C and 3,500 psi back pressure. ([[2019-al-azani|AL-AZANI, 2019, p. 80]])

- The first formulation achieved 23% tertiary recovery and 62% OOIP; the third reached 24% tertiary and nearly 70% total recovery. ([[2019-alghamdi|AlGhamdi, 2019, p. 18]])

- Modified xanthan gum showed a 19% tertiary recovery compared to 14% for standard xanthan. ([[2019-mohammad|Mohammad, 2019, p. 14]])

- Gray Berea core permeability increased by 42% after three treatment cycles. ([[2019-gomaa|Gomaa, 2019, p. 113]])

- Core flooding was used to measure pressure drop, oil recovery factor, and breakthrough time across seven experiments. ([[2019-abdelaal|Abdelaal, 2019, p. 55]])

- Core flooding showed that alternating thermochemical and HCl fluids reduced the required acid volume for breakthrough. ([[2019-alawi|ALAWI, 2019, p. 76]])

- Coreflooding showed that thermochemical treatment removed 73% of the condensate liquid from sandstone samples. ([[2020-mohammed|Mohammed, 2020, p. 148]])

- Cyclic injection produced more oil than continuous gas injection in both carbonate and sandstone rocks. ([[2021-almarri|AlMarri, 2021, p. 16]])

- Bulk permeability values for the upper part of the stratum ranged from 0.676 to 3.823 mD. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 61]])

- Completion fluids prepared using ionic liquids resulted in complete stabilization of clays and nearly maintained the in-situ permeability of tight sandstone. ([[2021-khan|KHAN, 2021, p. 21]])

- Dynamic adsorption was reduced from 0.269 to 0.041 mg/g-rock by adding 1% formic acid. ([[2022-kalam|Kalam, 2022, p. 150]])

- Core flooding experiments showed that C7 asphaltenes are harder to remove via shear forces than C5 asphaltenes, leading to more significant pore plugging. ([[2023-suleiman|Suleiman, 2023, p. 96]])

- Coreflooding showed that GLDA provided a significant increase in conductivity compared to pre-stimulation values. ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023, p. 43]])

- The apparent viscosity of AN-125 SH transitioned to shear-thickening beyond a critical shear rate of 18.7 ft/day. ([[2023-dafaalla|Dafaalla, 2023, p. 101]])

- Sx1 showed a significant reduction in MMP and FCMP at 90°C for heavy crudes. ([[2023-al-hakami|Al-Hakami, 2023, p. 132]])

- The corefold results showed a regained permeability of 96% for CMHPG dual crosslinked (borate and zirconate). ([[2024-abdallah|Abdallah, 2024, p. 142]])

- Evaluated the impact of salinity on permeability and the effectiveness of chelating agents in preventing scale. ([[2023-othman|Othman, 2023, p. 35]])

- Determined that CO2 cushion gas provided the highest recovery at high injection rates (34.35%). ([[2024-muhammed|Muhammed, 2024, p. 265]])

- Nanosilice-based fluid showed 100% plugging efficiency with only a 10 psi increase for five pore volumes. ([[2024-alabdrabalnabi|Alabdrabalnabi, 2024, p. 53]])

- Core flooding confirmed that TCF treatment successfully generated heat and pressure for kerogen conversion. ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024, p. 53]])

- The study determined that viscoelastic polymer flooding resulted in earlier desaturation (at 2 ft/day) compared to surfactant-glycerol flooding (at 3 ft/day). ([[2024-jameel|Jameel, 2024, p. 17]])

- Core flooding was used to determine that higher flow rates led to increased precipitation rates. ([[2004-ahmed|Ahmed, 2004, p. 15]])

- The average water saturation was calculated from the produced volume of oil or brine at various centrifuge speeds. ([[1995-hussain|Hussain, 1995, p. 53]])
