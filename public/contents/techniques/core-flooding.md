# Core Flooding

**Summary**: A method to measure the permeability of a core sample by circulating fluid through it under controlled pressure.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022]] applied core flooding to 9.4 cm cores to determine bulk permeability of the burrowed strata.

- [[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023]] applied core flooding to simulate the injection of carbonic acid into limestone under varying salinity and temperature conditions.

- [[2024-abdallah|Abdallah, 2024]] performed coreflooding experiments to assess TCFs as cleanup agents for various fracturing fluid systems.

## Key Results and Findings

- Determined average bulk permeability for the burrowed mud-dominated strata across three sites. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 39]])

- The core flooding system allowed for the measurement of permeability before and after the experiment using a data logger and acquisition system. ([[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023, p. 71]])

- Coreflood experiments were conducted for different fracturing fluid systems including HPG, CMHPG, and slick water to evaluate TCF effectiveness. ([[2024-abdallah|Abdallah, 2024, p. 132]])
