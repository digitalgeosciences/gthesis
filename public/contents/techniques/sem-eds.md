# SEM with Energy Dispersive Spectroscopy

**Summary**: A technique used to examine surface morphology and elemental composition at the microscopic level.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017]] applied SEM/EDX to analyze mineralogy and texture of core samples.

## Key Results and Findings

- Used in conjunction with XRD to characterize lithofacies and petrographical features. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 20]])

- Used to observe cement morphology and cement-pore relationships in core samples. ([[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001, p. 29]])

- Identified quartz overgrowths, clay coatings, and poikilotopic calcite as primary cements. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 47]])

- SEM results showed that the chemical composition is not constant from toe to heel due to drilling solids. ([[2012-geri|Geri, 2012, p. 126]])

- Not stated in source ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013]])

- Used for characterizing the surface of carbonate rocks following brine treatments. ([[2012-jabbar|JABBAR, 2012, p. 20]])

- Used in conjunction with XRD to characterize the mineralogy of the outcrop. ([[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013, p. 47]])

- Chlorine was detected in both treated and untreated samples, indicating it could not be used as an indicator for acid migration. ([[2013-khan|Khan, 2013, p. 104]])

- Identified kaolinite and iron oxide filling pores as primary factors for reduced permeability. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 93]])

- Identified calcium-rich minerals in carbonate samples. ([[2014-kasha|Kasha, 2014, p. 45]])

- SEM images showed that bent-2 had a more uniform structure and clearer wavy layered patterns than bent-1. ([[2014-elhag|ELHAG, 2014, p. 93]])

- Enabled observation of features too small or rough for binocular microscopes. ([[2014-al-ameri|AL-AMERI, 2014, p. 62]])

- SEM-EDS identified major elements Al, Si, K, O, and Na in Lithofacies-I. ([[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015, p. 40]])

- SEM-EDX was employed to obtain the elemental spectrum and to get elemental analysis of the polymers as well as; Cadmium (Cd), Cobalt (Co), Chromium (Cr) and Nickel-loaded polymers. ([[2015-musa|Musa, 2015, p. 44]])

- SEM/EDX image of AC/CeO2/Fe after adsorption of refractory sulfur compound (Figure 4.47). ([[2015-danmaliki|Danmaliki, 2015, p. 16]])

- SEM analysis confirmed the presence of higher percentages of silicon in the tested samples. ([[2015-thalab|Thalab, 2015, p. 48]])

- SEM analysis showed that firing caused a reduction in space between clay particles by 10 to 20 vol % without grain shattering. ([[2015-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2015, p. 44]])

- Used on 10 representative samples to differentiate between carbonate minerals. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 56]])

- SEM images of the CBG facies showed dissolved bioclastic fragments. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 50]])

- SEM provided a much higher magnification than optical microscopy, allowing for the identification of grain size, shape, and contact. ([[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016, p. 44]])

- Identified distinct shapes (rod-like, platy, spherical) and quantified elements like C, O, F, Na, Mg, Al, Si, K, and Ca. ([[2016-lawal-2|Lawal, 2016, p. 15]])

- Identified microcrystalline quartz as the primary component of chert and detected evidence of evaporite relicts. ([[2016-radwan|Radwan, 2016, p. 64]])

- Identified pore types such as moldic, intraparticle, and intercrystalline pores. ([[2016-makkawi|Makkawi, 2016, p. 41]])

- Identified impurities (Si, Al, Ca) at 5-7 wt% in industrial barite samples. ([[2016-geri|GERI, 2016, p. 94]])

- SEM images showed the migration of clay minerals in sandstone rocks. ([[2016-jad|Jad, 2016, p. 16]])

- Identified the presence of specific elements on the surface of the modified fly ash. ([[2017-olabemiwo|Olabemiwo, 2017, p. 46]])

- Not stated in source ([[2017-sadeed|SADEED, 2017]])

- Confirmed the presence of CSH (C/S = 2) in the G mix via EDS spectrum. ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 110]])

- Revealed different crystal habits and the presence of intercrystalline microporosity within micrite. ([[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017, p. 107]])

- SEM observations reveal that clay minerals played a central role in lining the pore space and affecting reservoir quality. ([[2017-adam-2|Adam, 2017, p. 32]])

- Used to identify the cement and clay type of the studied sandstone. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 28]])

- Chlorite was recorded in all examined samples, with some showing high concentrations (up to 37.40%). ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 181]])

- SEM photographs show the presence of smectite as grain coating in sandstone samples, which could result in the preservation of reservoir quality by prohibiting quartz overgrowths. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 192]])

- SEM-EDX suggested that minerals (Fe, Al, Mg, Mn, Ca, Na) were removed by higher pyrolytic temperatures and acid washing. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 12]])

- Used to confirm mineralogy in lithofacies Mdg, Mlg, and Mzr. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 43]])

- SEM showed that average size of particles in Jubail (1.4 - 2.8 µm) is smaller than Ras Tanura (5 - 7 µm). ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 22]])

- SEM images showed that dolomite rhombs were present as fine crystals under 10 microns in LFT2. ([[2020-al-zoukani|Al-Zoukani, 2020, p. 113]])

- SEM identified kaolinite as the primary cement, appearing in several textural morphologies. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 79]])

- SEM-EDS confirmed that zinc was present in areas of mineral alteration (P1, P4) but absent in original rock grains (P2, P3). ([[2022-samarkin|Samarkin, 2022, p. 55]])

- Revealed a high amount of calcium and silicon in the hydrated product, indicating C-S-H and C-A-S-H gel formation. ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 85]])

- SEM-EDS confirmed that pure quartzose sands (≥99% quartz) have the best thermo-physical properties. ([[2022-radwan|Radwan, 2022, p. 52]])

- Identified interparticle pores (500 nm to 4 μm) and intraparticle pores within clay minerals. ([[2022-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2022, p. 39]])

- Barium sulphate showed the highest weight percentage (40.37%) in the barite sample. ([[2022-aljaberi|AlJaberi, 2022, p. 42]])

- Identified grain-replacive smectite in nearshore deposits. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 30]])

- Mapped the distribution of elements and confirmed the homogeneity of four phyllosilicate materials. ([[2023-afagwu|AFAGWU, 2023, p. 63]])

- SEM images showed HAP forming as dendrite shapes sprouting from host calcite grains, while EDS confirmed the presence of phosphorus at grain boundaries. ([[2023-desouky|Desouky, 2023, p. 78]])

- SEM-EDX results matched ICP-MS results only in certain months (January, February, and April). ([[2024-alsadah|Alsadah, 2024, p. 49]])

- The EDS emphasized quartz enrichment and the presence of kaolinite that appeared in XRD test. ([[2002-elhassan|ElHassan, 2002, p. 19]])

- SEM showed anhydrite as fine crystal laths (10-20 $\mu$m) in the brown mud layer. ([[1994-al-guwaizani|Al-Guwaizani, 1994, p. 60]])
