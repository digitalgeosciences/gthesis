# SEM with Energy Dispersive Spectroscopy

**Summary**: A technique used to determine the chemical composition of minerals and their distribution in a sample.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[1996-matter|Matter, 1996]] used electron microprobe analysis to characterize the mineralogy of apatite, dolomite, and calcite.

- [[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001]] applied SEM-EDX to study cement morphology and composition in Jauf sandstone samples.

- [[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002]] applied SEM-EDS to observe cement morphology and determine the composition of representative minerals in the Unayzah Formation.

- [[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988]] applied SEM-EDS to identify mineral elements and pyrite crystals in the dolomite groundmass.

- [[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013]] used SEM-EDS to examine the surface morphology and chemical composition of the nanomaterials.

- [[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014]] used SEM-EDS to examine the mineralogy and microstructure of Sarah Formation samples.

- [[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014]] applied SEM-EDS to 15 samples to identify clay types based on morphology and evaluate their effects on porosity.

- [[2014-adebayo|Adebayo, 2014]] used FESEM and EDX to determine the surface morphology, microstructure, and elemental composition of the adsorbent materials.

- [[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015]] applied SEM-EDS to determine the elemental composition of minerals in the Qusaiba Shale.

- [[2015-oyehan|Oyehan, 2015]] used SEM-EDS to characterize particulates in environmental samples.

## Key Results and Findings

- Determined average apatite content of 46.35% and dolomite of 36.69%. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 17]])

- Used to distinguish diagenetic from detrital features and determine the composition of representative authigenic minerals. ([[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001, p. 29]])

- Used to identify clay cements (illite, chlorite, smectite) that significantly reduce permeability. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 32]])

- SEM-EDS identified pyrite crystal in dolomite groundmass at borehole 3/32. ([[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988, p. 175]])

- The EDX spectrum of TiO2 powder showed titanium (Ti) at 46.29 wt % and oxygen (O) at 53.71 wt %. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 34]])

- Provided detailed views of grain surfaces and cementation types. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 80]])

- Used to get a clearer idea of grain boundaries, microstructures, and elemental analysis. ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 32]])

- EDX was used as a fingerprint for the elemental composition of the samples. ([[2014-adebayo|Adebayo, 2014, p. 47]])

- Identified organic matter contamination and specific mineral compositions in Lithofacies-I. ([[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015, p. 19]])

- Used to assess indoor particulates as part of previous research by the author. ([[2015-oyehan|Oyehan, 2015, p. 160]])

- SEM-EDS was used to provide images and EDX spectra for CPZDA, HCDAP, and HCPZA. ([[2015-musa|Musa, 2015, p. 11]])

- SEM/EDX images showed the distribution of metal nanoparticles on the activated carbon surface. ([[2015-danmaliki|Danmaliki, 2015, p. 87]])

- Identified chemical signatures in various sedimentary textures. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 63]])

- EDS analysis showed the absence of Si and hence the presence of Mg and Ca in the Fpg facies. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 68]])

- Identified clay minerals and cementation types in various sandstone facies. ([[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016, p. 14]])

- Morphological analysis showed that the shapes of PM samples collected from Dhahran were predominantly rod-like and platy with size between 2 – 6 µm. ([[2016-lawal-2|Lawal, 2016, p. 15]])

- SEM images confirmed the formation of nanoparticles and their distribution within the polymer membrane. ([[2016-salawu|Salawu, 2016, p. 67]])

- SEM was utilized on 18 samples to identify micro-porosity, grain shape, cement, and matrix. ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 46]])

- SEM-EDS was used to determine element distribution for silicon, calcium, and sulphur in evaporite relicts within cherts. ([[2016-radwan|Radwan, 2016, p. 49]])

- Analysis was performed on points within the internal lining of paratype #6/6c.6. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 48]])

- Used for observing mineral structures or biological samples in various environmental contexts. ([[2017-budiyanto|Budiyanto, 2017, p. 117]])

- Identified specific mineral types filling pores within the sandstone units. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 14]])

- Not stated in source ([[2017-olabemiwo|Olabemiwo, 2017]])

- Identified small scale diagenetic features and microporosity in the TMF. ([[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017, p. 17]])

- SEM observations revealed that clay minerals lining the pores significantly reduced permeability values. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 170]])

- SEM-EDS confirmed the presence of chlorite (Mg, Al, Fe)12 [(Si, Al)8 O20] (OH)16 as a pore-filling mineral. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 158]])

- SEM-EDS data supported the identification of calcite, dolomite, and Fe-dolomite as major carbonate cements. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 166]])

- SEM-EDX confirmed that mineral elements (Fe, Al, Mg, Mn, Ca, Na) were removed by high temperature pyrolysis and acid washing. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 12]])

- Identified clay minerals within different mudstone and siltstone facies. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 177]])

- Analysis confirmed that particle size and shape varied between Jubail, Khobar, and Dammam. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 45]])

- Identified authigenic kaolinite in various morphologies (book-stacked, hexagonal) as a primary pore-filler. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 79]])
