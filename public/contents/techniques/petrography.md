# Petrography

**Summary**: The study of rocks in thin sections under a polarizing microscope.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- Identified apatite as rounded pellets well liberated in material finer than 0.5 mm. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 61]])

- Thin sections were prepared from core samples to provide petrographic description and photography for lithofacies identification. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 27]])

- Petrography identified distinct facies like peloidal grainstone, which are often cemented. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 88]])

- Identified 8 distinct pore types (vuggy, moldic, etc.) linked to specific geometries like elongated or rounded shapes. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 123]])

- Petrographic analysis revealed a complete loss of feldspars above the base of the Upper Siq Sandstone. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 25]])

- Sediment type is controlled by biological communities and submarine topography. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 136]])

- Identified nine distinct lithofacies types (LFT1-LFT9) based on petrographic analysis. ([[2017-yousif|Yousif, 2017, p. 18]])

- Petrography identified six distinct pore types: microporosity (44%), fracture porosity (21%), moldic (9%), vuggy (9%), intraparticle (13%), and intergranular (4%). ([[2020-al-zoukani|Al-Zoukani, 2020, p. 137]])

- Petrography revealed that burrow infills consist of grain-dominated textures with corroded peloids and coated grains. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 51]])

- Identified moldic, vuggy, and intercrystalline pores across multiple carbonate formations. ([[2022-salih|Salih, 2022, p. 126]])

- Petrographic point-count data showed that monocrystalline quartz is the dominant detrital grain (average 29.0%). ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 156]])

- Identified quartz veins, orthoclase, and chlorite as key indicators of hydrothermal activity. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 72]])

- Petrography revealed that most dolomitization occurred in the host rock matrix of unit 2A, while burrow fillings remained largely undolomitized. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 56]])

- Petrography revealed distinct textures such as porphyritic (Fig. 10) and subophitic (Fig. 11). ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 13]])

- Petrographic results point to no to very low compaction of sediments under the microscope. ([[2024-malik|Malik, 2024, p. 101]])

- Al-Ghurayyah pluton is divided into non-amphibole bearing albite granite, amphibole-bearing porphyritic albite granite, and silicified porphyritic granite. ([[2024-sulistyohariyanto|Sulistyohariyanto, 2024, p. 35]])

- Petrography revealed that both CF and LL forms consist of grain-rich and micrite-rich laminae with distinct mineralogical components like ooids and clotted micrite. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 41]])

- Thin section analysis revealed distinct differences in the host rock matrix between the upper and lower parts of the vertical core. ([[2024-allehyani|allehyani, 2024, p. 83]])

- Petrographic observations confirmed that original textural features were well preserved in the dolomite, supporting an early diagenetic origin. ([[1988-darwish-2|Darwish, 1988, p. 87]])
