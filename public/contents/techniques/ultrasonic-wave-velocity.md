# Ultrasonic Wave Velocity

**Summary**: Measurement of the speed of ultrasonic waves through rock to determine elastic properties.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016]] used ultrasonic velocity measurements to characterize petrophysical properties and derive elastic constants.

- [[2021-albensaad|Albensaad, 2021]] measured ultrasonic wave velocities to assess mudrock heterogeneity.

- [[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021]] used ultrasonic measurements to determine Vp and Vs for calculating dynamic elastic moduli.

## Key Results and Findings

- Used as a primary method for determining dynamic elastic parameters like Young's modulus and Poisson's ratio. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 46]])

- Core-based acoustic properties tend to match log-based acoustic properties; discrepancies in shear wave values are likely due to sample anisotropy. ([[2021-albensaad|Albensaad, 2021, p. 98]])

- The shear wave velocity (Vs) showed a reduction of 6.6% for Micromax-WBM samples. ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 86]])
