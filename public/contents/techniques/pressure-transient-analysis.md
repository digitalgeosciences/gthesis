# Pressure Transient Analysis

**Summary**: The analysis of pressure changes over time or distance in a reservoir to characterize properties and geometry.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[1990-mimoune|Mimoune, 1990]] applied pressure-transient-analysis to identify flow regimes like bilinear and pseudoradial flow.

- [[2001-sultan|SULTAN, 2001]] applied pressure-transient-analysis to interpret horizontal well behavior using dimensionless parameters.

## Key Results and Findings

- Pressure-transient-analysis was used to determine that the standard 0.25 slope for bilinear flow is not observed in multi-fracture systems with finite conductivity. ([[1990-mimoune|Mimoune, 1990, p. 111]])

- The study used 11 models based on combinations of wellbore storage, skin, and reservoir types (homogeneous/dual porosity). ([[2001-sultan|SULTAN, 2001, p. 116]])

- BHP was predicted for various tubing sizes and oil rates. ([[2011-al-shammari|Al-Shammari, 2011, p. 41]])

- The study demonstrates that PTA can be applied to real-time data from PDHMS to provide insights into reservoir parameters like permeability and distance to boundaries. ([[2011-alburaikan|AlBuraikan, 2011, p. 21]])

- Referenced in this thesis. ([[2012-al-abdulmohsin|Al-Abdulmohsin, 2012]])

- Pressure derivative curves deviate significantly when alpha is increased, impacting graphical analysis of permeability. ([[2013-khan-2|Khan, 2013, p. 17]])

- The model allows for the calculation of flow and pressure profiles along the horizontal section under different constant rates and ratios from both ends. ([[2014-al-gahtani|Al-Gahtani, 2014, p. 13]])

- Standard PTA overestimates near-wellbore skin and underestimates skin if changing viscosity due to temperature effects is ignored. ([[2022-alnasser|Alnasser, 2022, p. 53]])
