# Electrical Resistivity Tomography

**Summary**: A geophysical method to measure the subsurface resistivity of rocks.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- Resistivity field data were interpreted to understand the distribution of the aquifer's properties. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 14]])

- The survey identified low resistivity areas (< 3.0 Ohm.m) corresponding to sediment-filled voids. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 16]])

- The joint inversion of DCR and SR data provides a more comprehensive understanding of the subsurface structure and its properties. ([[2023-edigbue|Edigbue, 2023, p. 19]])

- The ERT identified a high resistance anomaly of 76.5 ohms.m. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 11]])

- The study utilized conductivity (C) as a primary input for the CRIM model to determine Sw. ([[2024-tarchi|Tarchi, 2024, p. 14]])

- Captured root activity variations over seven months, highlighting potential for precision agriculture. ([[2024-prasetyo|Prasetyo, 2024, p. 14]])

- Used to track the evolution of electrical conductivity during the recharge process. ([[2026-fatima|Fatima, 2026, p. 142]])

- The correlation of the resistivity model with stratigraphic units revealed a probable boundary of a collapsing structure in the Rus formation. ([[1995-hasan|Hasan, 1995, p. 28]])
