# Variogram Analysis

**Summary**: A geostatistical tool used to model the spatial continuity of a property.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- Omnidirectional variograms for porosity showed ranges from 2000 to 3000, with higher values in upper zones indicating larger structures. ([[2002-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2002, p. 119]])

- Experimental semivariograms were fitted with spherical, exponential, Gaussian, linear, and De Wijsian models to ensure unique solutions in kriging systems. ([[1996-jarad|Jarad, 1996, p. 99]])

- The experimental semi-variogram must be fitted with an appropriate model semi-variogram (e.g., spherical, exponential, gaussian). ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 46]])

- Compound spherical models were found to fit the experimental semivariograms well. ([[1991-makkawi|Makkawi, 1991, p. 19]])

- Spatial continuity was then modeled using variograms which is the basic tool for identifying the patterns of spatial continuity of a variable. ([[1997-al-qassab|Al-Qassab, 1997, p. 16]])

- Three models (spherical, exponential, and Gaussian) were fitted to experimental semivariograms to define spatial parameters. ([[1996-al-salem|Al-Salem, 1996, p. 90]])

- Data were fitted to a 'Spherical model' because the data tended to follow that behavior. ([[2012-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2012, p. 122]])

- Three main transition models (spherical, Gaussian, and exponential) were evaluated based on semivariogram analysis. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 64]])

- The variation in permeability was referred to local random heterogeneity (nugget effect) which had been confirmed by conditional simulation. ([[2016-hassan-2|Hassan, 2016, p. 41]])

- The major direction of the variability was found to be toward the northeast (NE). ([[2016-makkawi|Makkawi, 2016, p. 122]])

- Porosity models of intertidal sheets had larger horizontal ranges (300m) than those of intertidal creeks or channels (150m). ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 220]])

- Used to determine the spatial continuity of lithofacies and porosity. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 219]])

- Variogram models were used to estimate parameters for both sampled and transformed data. ([[2022-karami|Karami, 2022, p. 48]])

- Variograms were used to model the spatial distribution of reservoir properties. ([[2022-babker|Babker, 2022]])

- Used to determine the degree of continuity or heterogeneity in the carbonate units. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 23]])

- Spherical models were successfully fitted to all experimental variograms. ([[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987, p. 18]])

- All semi-variograms revealed a northwesterly trending anisotropy at approximately 145 degrees from the north. ([[2002-al-ali|Al-Ali, 2002, p. 98]])
