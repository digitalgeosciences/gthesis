# Variogram Analysis

**Summary**: A mathematical function used to quantify the similarity or dissimilarity between sample values as a function of distance.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- The semivariograms for P2O5 were found to be approximately symmetrical, confirming normal distribution. ([[1996-jarad|Jarad, 1996, p. 96]])

- Compound spherical models were found to fit the experimental semivariograms of both CaO% and MgO% logarithmic values. ([[1991-makkawi|Makkawi, 1991, p. 19]])

- Variograms were used to determine the range, sill, and nugget effect for both isotropic and vertical directions. ([[1997-al-qassab|Al-Qassab, 1997, p. 16]])

- Spherical models were found to be the most suitable for fitting vertical semivariograms in several zones. ([[1996-al-salem|Al-Salem, 1996, p. 96]])

- The Gaussian model was selected as the best fit for both of the porosity and permeability semivariograms. ([[2016-hassan-2|Hassan, 2016, p. 146]])

- The major range for the lithofacies was found to be oriented approximately north–south, varying between 500 and 1000 m. ([[2016-makkawi|Makkawi, 2016, p. 85]])

- Spherical models were successfully fitted to all experimental variograms for the three key variables. ([[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987, p. 18]])
