# Triaxial Test

**Summary**: A mechanical test to determine the strength and elastic properties of rock or cement under triaxial stress.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2011-al-dossary|Al-Dossary, 2011]] performed triaxial tests to calculate Young's modulus and Poisson's ratio for cement samples.

- [[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014]] performed triaxial tests on core samples from WELL L to obtain static rock mechanics data.

- [[2014-al-ameri|AL-AMERI, 2014]] applied triaxial tests to evaluate Young's modulus, Poisson's ratio, and ultimate compressive strength.

- [[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016]] performed triaxial tests on 70 samples to determine Young's modulus, Poisson's ratio, UCS, and friction angle.

- [[2017-muqtadir|Muqtadir, 2017]] performed triaxial tests using an AutoLab 1500 system to evaluate core stability.

- [[2020-gbiyanju|Gbiyanju, 2020]] performed triaxial tests to obtain static and dynamic moduli (Es, Ed) for comparison with non--destructive methods.

- [[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021]] used UPV to obtain the dynamic geomechanical properties of core samples, specifically Young's modulus and Poisson's ratio.

- [[2023-al-garni|Al-Garni, 2023]] used triaxial testing in an AutoLab 1500 system to measure Young's modulus and permeability under high stress.

- [[2002-elhassan|ElHassan, 2002]] performed triaxial tests on core plugs at 2900 psi to obtain static Young's modulus and Poisson's ratio.

## Key Results and Findings

- Static E increased from 0.125 to 0.29 after exposure. ([[2011-al-dossary|Al-Dossary, 2011, p. 101]])

- Triaxial tests were used to derive values for Young's Modulus, Poisson's ratio, UCS, and friction angle. ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 40]])

- Identified a reduction in Young's modulus and tensile strength post-CO2 treatment. ([[2014-al-ameri|AL-AMERI, 2014, p. 53]])

- Determined average Young's modulus for Khuff B as 3,547 kpsi and Khuff C as 4,359 kpsi. ([[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016, p. 77]])

- The AutoLab 1500 was used for both triaxial and ultrasonic testing, providing confining and axial pressures up to 10,000 psi. ([[2017-muqtadir|Muqtadir, 2017, p. 36]])

- Triaxial measurements provided the baseline for validating the correlation between E* and standard rock elastic properties. ([[2020-gbiyanju|Gbiyanju, 2020, p. 104]])

- Vp increased from 2304 m/s (30 min) to 2425 m/s (5 days), while Vs increased from 1250 m/s to 1305 m/s over the same period. ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 54]])

- The results confirmed that Austin chalk was more susceptible to damage than Indiana limestone under high confining pressures. ([[2023-al-garni|Al-Garni, 2023, p. 54]])

- Triaxial measurements were made in a triaxial cell by recording axial and radial strain as the sample was loaded axially under constant confining stress. ([[2002-elhassan|ElHassan, 2002, p. 32]])
