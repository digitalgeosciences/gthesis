# Image Log Analysis

**Summary**: The use of electrical or acoustic tools to provide a 360-degree image of the borehole wall for structural and sedimentary analysis.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2011-al-masrahy|AL-MASRAHY, 2011]] applied image log analysis to determine dip directions in eolian dune facies.

- [[2013-abdullah|Abdullah, 2013]] used image-log-analysis to provide evidence of lamination and generate a high-resolution resistivity curve (SRES).

- [[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016]] applied image-log-analysis to identify fractures, stylolites, and validate stress models via breakout analysis.

## Key Results and Findings

- 4000 feet of image log data were analyzed across 21 wells to gather depositional dip-directional data. ([[2011-al-masrahy|AL-MASRAHY, 2011, p. 10]])

- High-resolution imagery provides clear evidence of laminations that are below the resolution of standard tools. ([[2013-abdullah|Abdullah, 2013, p. 21]])

- No faults or major natural open fractures were identified on borehole resistivity images. ([[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016, p. 123]])
