# Image Log Analysis

**Summary**: The interpretation of electrical borehole images to determine sedimentary structures and dip directions.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2011-al-masrahy|AL-MASRAHY, 2011]] applied image-log-analysis to 4,000 feet of data from 21 wells to gather depositional dip-directional data.

- [[2013-abdullah|Abdullah, 2013]] used borehole images to provide evidence of lamination and generate SRES curves.

- [[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014]] applied image-log-analysis to Well L to determine the orientation of minimum and maximum horizontal stresses.

- [[2019-al-nutaifi|Al-Nutaifi, 2019]] used image log analysis to characterize natural fractures and determine the full stress tensor.

- [[2019-alqunais|Alqunais, 2019]] applied image-log-analysis to identify thin beds and distinguish between shaly sands and laminated reservoirs.

## Key Results and Findings

- Image logs allowed the identification of eolian dune cross-bedded sandstones in uncored wells. ([[2011-al-masrahy|AL-MASRAHY, 2011, p. 49]])

- High-resolution images confirmed the presence of thin beds in the zone from 55 ft to 20 ft. ([[2013-abdullah|Abdullah, 2013, p. 78]])

- Image logs in Well L revealed natural fractures in the Mauddud formation that caused lost circulation. ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 73]])

- Image logs were used to distinguish between natural fractures (sinusoidal) and induced fractures. ([[2019-al-nutaifi|Al-Nutaifi, 2019, p. 46]])

- High-resolution imaging identified hydrocarbon-bearing sands as thin as 0.24 inches, which were missed by standard logs. ([[2019-alqunais|Alqunais, 2019, p. 15]])
