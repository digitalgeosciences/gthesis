# Coreflooding

**Summary**: A laboratory technique where fluids are pumped through a core sample to simulate reservoir flow and evaluate recovery.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[1990-al-mumen|Al-Mumen, 1990]] used coreflooding to evaluate the impact of varying water salinity on oil recovery in Berea sandstone samples.

- [[1997-al-nashmi|Al-Nashmi, 1997]] used coreflooding to evaluate gel effectiveness in plugging sandstone and limestone cores.

- [[1995-okasha|OKASHA, 1995]] used coreflooding to evaluate the displacement efficiency of hot water and solvent in a tar-containing reservoir model.

- [[2012-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2012]] performed coreflood experiments to evaluate organic-HF acids under simulated reservoir conditions.

- [[2013-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2013]] used coreflooding to evaluate the effectiveness of EDTA and HEDTA in carbonate cores.

- [[2013-geri|Geri, 2013]] performed coreflooding experiments on limestone rocks to determine the effectiveness of ASG process as potential EOR technique.

- [[2013-khan|Khan, 2013]] used coreflooding to study wormhole propagation in Indiana Limestone cores at pressures up to 5,000 psi.

- [[2013-elsayed|Elsayed, 2013]] used coreflooding to evaluate the effectiveness of different chelating agents on oil recovery in Berea sandstone.

- [[2013-al-mutairi|AL-MUTAIRI, 2013]] used coreflooding to evaluate the impact of wettability on oil recovery during CO2 injection.

- [[2014-al-duailej|Al-Duailej, 2014]] used coreflooding to monitor breakthrough and differential pressure during acid injection.

- [[2014-jangda|Jangda, 2014]] used coreflooding to evaluate the performance of a novel surfactant-CO2 system in limestone cores.

- [[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015]] conducted coreflooding experiments on Berea sandstone to test the effectiveness of DTPA-K5 in enhancing oil recovery.

## Key Results and Findings

- Coreflooding experiments showed that oil recovery increased with salinity up to 10% for NaCl brines. ([[1990-al-mumen|Al-Mumen, 1990, p. 152]])

- The permeability of the cores was reduced to less than 1.0% of its original value. ([[1997-al-nashmi|Al-Nashmi, 1997, p. 16]])

- The linear composite core arrangement allowed for the study of multi-fluid interactions under confining pressure. ([[1995-okasha|OKASHA, 1995, p. 47]])

- Used to compare permeability values before and after acid treatment to determine effectiveness. ([[2012-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2012, p. 25]])

- Core flooding showed that 5 wt% EDTA increased oil recovery to 72% compared to 58% for seawater. ([[2013-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2013, p. 74]])

- Maximum recovery of 47.7% of residual oil in place in low permeability was observed compared to 43% of residual oil in place on high permeability. ([[2013-geri|Geri, 2013, p. 17]])

- Coreflooding experiments showed that higher pressure (3,000-5,000 psi) led to faster tip progression due to CO2 remaining in the aqueous phase. ([[2013-khan|Khan, 2013, p. 54]])

- Coreflooding experiments showed that 5% Na4EDTA at pH 12.2 provided significantly higher oil recovery than standard sea water flooding. ([[2013-elsayed|Elsayed, 2013, p. 65]])

- The model's inclusion of continuous wettibility alteration was confirmed by an actual core flooding experiment, showing higher ultimate oil recovery. ([[2013-al-mutairi|AL-MUTAIRI, 2013, p. 106]])

- Coreflooding data provided verification of breakthrough occurrence. ([[2014-al-duailej|Al-Duailej, 2014, p. 109]])

- The study utilized an engineered coreflooding rig to test three different injection strategies for sc-CO2 and surfactants. ([[2014-jangda|Jangda, 2014, p. 36]])

- Silurian dolomite cores with 21% porosity and 100-170mD permeability were used for testing. ([[2014-alao|Alao, 2014, p. 53]])

- Core flooding with 1.5 PV of the best VES system reduced the remaining oil saturation to 0.23. ([[2014-azad|Azad, 2014, p. 24]])

- 5wt% EDTA in seawater was identified as the optimum concentration for maximizing recovery without compromising rock integrity. ([[2015-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2015, p. 23]])

- The addition of DTPA-K5 as a secondary flood increased oil recovery by 19.2% and improved permeability by 10%. ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 48]])

- The best sequential scenario for enhancing the oil recovery can be achieved by flooding with 3wt. % followed by 5wt% for both chelants GLDA and EDTA. ([[2015-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2015, p. 16]])

- Unsteady state coreflooding was used to capture the transition in rock properties during smart water injection. ([[2016-adam|ADAM, 2016, p. 19]])

- Coreflooding results showed a 84% permeability reduction in sandstone after DIW flooding. ([[2016-jad|Jad, 2016, p. 15]])

- The mixture of Al2O3 (0.05 wt%) and SiO2 (0.01 wt%) showed an incremental oil recovery factor of 23.724% under reservoir conditions. ([[2016-alhamad|AlHamad, 2016, p. 24]])

- The flooding experiments were in reasonable conformance with the rheological properties regarding yielding high return permeability. ([[2017-mohammed-3|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 17]])

- Coreflooding experiments showed that foaming successfully lowered the mobility of injected gases, leading to higher oil recovery compared to pure gas flooding. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 75]])

- The waste oil system achieved the lowest pore volume of acid to break through (PVBT) compared to diesel and plain HCl. ([[2017-ahmed-2|AHMED, 2017, p. 14]])

- Core flooding was performed for two scenarios: (i) sea water and VES, and (ii) sea water and VES/CA. ([[2017-janjua|Janjua, 2017, p. 18]])

- The S1P1 combination yielded a 31.29% recovery of ROS, while other combinations like S2P2 only achieved 9.07%. ([[2017-ahmed-3|Ahmed, 2017, p. 83]])

- The CO2-TEC blend (85 mol% CO2) increased final oil recovery from 60.14% to 72.67% in dead crude and from 49.13% to 67.89% in live crude. ([[2019-al-azani|AL-AZANI, 2019, p. 18]])

- The modified xanthan (NP) yielded a 5.28% higher recovery of IOIP compared to standard xanthan. ([[2019-mohammad|Mohammad, 2019, p. 87]])

- Coreflooding confirmed that thermochemical treatment increased absolute permeability by more than 200 times in some samples. ([[2020-mohammed|Mohammed, 2020, p. 22]])

- Ultimately recovered 86% in Experiment 1 with 70% foam quality. ([[2021-al-shuqayyiqi|Al-Shuqayyiqi, 2021, p. 30]])

- Coreflooding was used to determine the impact of EDTA and DTPA on rock permeability during iron sulfide formation. ([[2021-gasimli|Gasimli, 2021, p. 16]])

- The study used a backpressure of 1000 psi and a confining pressure of 1500 psi during coreflooding. ([[2021-khan|KHAN, 2021, p. 63]])

- Core flooding experiments were used to determine how IFT reduction and WA contribute to the displacement of various residual oil categories. ([[2022-xiao|Xiao, 2022, p. 130]])

- Wormhole breakthrough was identified by measuring the pressure drop across the core during acid injection. ([[2023-al-garni|Al-Garni, 2023, p. 27]])

- Live brine injection generated wormholes in all rock samples, increasing porosity and permeability. ([[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023, p. 15]])

- Coreflooding with 1.25% VES achieved an oil recovery of 50%. ([[2023-ahmed|Ahmed, 2023, p. 174]])

- Showed that higher rates (20, 32.5 ft/day) led to enhanced recovery in heterogeneous formations where shear thickening was evident. ([[2023-dafaalla|Dafaalla, 2023, p. 18]])

- Coreflood results showed 87% permeability regain for HPG, 96% for CMHPG, and 90% for slickwater. ([[2024-abdallah|Abdallah, 2024, p. 20]])

- Coreflooding on an outcrop plug with GS in seawater resulted in 23% OOIP additional recovery. ([[2024-al-azani|Al-Azani, 2024, p. 24]])

- An injection rate of 1 cm3/min resulted in the lowest PVBT (18 pore volumes). ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 51]])

- The system was used to quantify the amount of initial and residual saturations of fluids in pore space after each cyclic CO2/water injection. ([[2024-suleiman-3|Suleiman, 2024, p. 35]])

- Coreflooding experiments were conducted over 6 months to assess durability under high pressure (up to 1000 psi). ([[2024-suleiman-4|Suleiman, 2024, p. 20]])

- Core flooding allows for the evaluation of fluid-rock interaction under realistic reservoir conditions of pressure and temperature. ([[2024-isah|Isah, 2024, p. 35]])
