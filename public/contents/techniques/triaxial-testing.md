# Triaxial Testing

**Summary**: A method used to measure the elastic properties and velocities of rock samples under varying confining pressures.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016]] used triaxial testing to determine P-wave and S-wave velocities for shale plugs at various confining pressures.

- [[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016]] applied triaxial testing to determine Young's modulus, UCS, cohesion, and friction angle.

- [[2019-al-nutaifi|Al-Nutaifi, 2019]] used multi-stage triaxial testing on core plugs from Well-A and Well-F to determine static and dynamic rock mechanical properties.

## Key Results and Findings

- Measured P-wave and S-wave velocities for vertical (2V) and horizontal (2H, 3H, 4H) plugs across a range of 5 to 35 MPa. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 51]])

- Young’s modulus, unconfined compressive strength, cohesion, and friction angle are primarily controlled by porosity. ([[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016, p. 124]])

- Triaxial tests provided data for 10+ samples with varying Young's moduli (e.g., Sample 1: 17,254 psi; Sample 10: 21,454 psi). ([[2019-al-nutaifi|Al-Nutaifi, 2019, p. 58]])
