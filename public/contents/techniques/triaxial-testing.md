# Triaxial Testing

**Summary**: A laboratory test to determine the strength and elastic properties of rock samples under triaxial stress.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016]] performed triaxial testing on 69 core plugs to calibrate dynamic logs to static values.

- [[2023-al-garni|Al-Garni, 2023]] applied triaxial testing in an AutoLab 1500 system to derive Young's modulus and Poisson's ratio.

- [[2002-elhassan|ElHassan, 2002]] performed triaxial testing on core plugs to determine Young's modulus and Poisson's ratio.

## Key Results and Findings

- Used for calibrating Young's modulus and Poisson's ratio from dynamic to static values. ([[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016, p. 31]])

- Austin chalk showed a lower Young's modulus (approx. 22-28 MPa) compared to Indiana limestone (34-35 MPa). ([[2023-al-garni|Al-Garni, 2023, p. 47]])

- A confining stress of 2900 psi was used for all samples to simulate approximate reservoir stress condition. ([[2002-elhassan|ElHassan, 2002, p. 32]])
