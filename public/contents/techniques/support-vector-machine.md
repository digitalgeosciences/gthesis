# Support Vector Machine

**Summary**: A supervised learning method for classification and regression that maps input vectors into a high-dimensional space.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2011-al-dhufairi|AL-DHUFAIRI, 2011]] used SVM to predict mobility profiles using various kernel functions.

- [[2014-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2014]] used support-vector-machine to predict water saturation due to its stability and ability to handle non-linear problems.

- [[2015-alsahaf|Alsahaf, 2015]] utilized support-vector-machine (SVM) as a core prediction engine in several hybrid models.

- [[2018-al-mashhad|Al-Mashhad, 2018]] utilized SVM with a Gaussian Kernel function to predict oil flow rates.

- [[2019-ahmed|Ahmed, 2019]] utilized support vector machine (SVM) as one of five AI techniques for predicting loss zones.

- [[2021-aldhaif|AlDhaif, 2021]] applied support vector machine (SVM) to evaluate its ability to predict oil flow rates across different cases.

- [[2021-alsaihati|Alsaihati, 2021]] used support vector machines to evaluate lost circulation detection.

- [[2021-alsabaa|Alsabaa, 2021]] evaluated SVM as a tool to solve complex non-linear problems in mud rheology prediction.

- [[2022-alsulaiman|AlSulaiman, 2022]] used support-vector-machine to evaluate its ability to predict productivity folds in carbonate reservoirs.

- [[2022-alkathim|Alkathim, 2022]] implemented SVM as a third AI technique to evaluate prediction performance against ANN and FL.

## Key Results and Findings

- The SVM showed excellent performance but was outperformed by the ANN model. ([[2011-al-dhufairi|AL-DHUFAIRI, 2011, p. 11]])

- SVM was shown to outperform common published empirical correlations in predicting properties like bubble point pressure and volume factor. ([[2014-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2014, p. 33]])

- Hybridization of SVM with feature selection algorithms like Decision Trees and Fuzzy Information Entropy significantly improved its performance. ([[2015-alsahaf|Alsahaf, 2015, p. 78]])

- Not stated in source. ([[2018-al-mashhad|Al-Mashhad, 2018]])

- SVM was identified as one of the best tools with R = 0.997 and RMSE = 0.0376. ([[2019-ahmed|Ahmed, 2019, p. 20]])

- The SVM model yielded an R2 of 0.89304 and AAPE of 1.40466% for the Subcritical Flow (WC ≠ 0) case. ([[2021-aldhaif|AlDhaif, 2021, p. 59]])

- Capable of handling non-linearly separable datasets using kernel functions. ([[2021-alsaihati|Alsaihati, 2021, p. 44]])
