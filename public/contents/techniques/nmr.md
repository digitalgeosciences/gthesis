# Nuclear Magnetic Resonance

**Summary**: A logging technique that measures the distribution of relaxation times of protons to determine porosity and fluid types.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010]] applied nmr to distinguish between free fluid and bound water based on T-2 cutoff values.

- [[2013-abdullah|Abdullah, 2013]] used NMR to characterize T2 distributions and calculate clay-bound water in thin beds.

- [[2015-musa|Musa, 2015]] used H-1 NMR to confirm the purity of monomer 2 before polymerization.

- [[2015-hassan|Hassan, 2015]] used NMR to confirm the chemical structures of synthesized products.

## Key Results and Findings

- NMR porosity is independent of lithology, but its T-2 distribution shape depends on the fluids contained in the pore space. ([[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010, p. 101]])

- NMR provides a more accurate representation of total porosity and fluid distribution compared to standard logs in complex lithologies. ([[2013-abdullah|Abdullah, 2013, p. 17]])

- Signals of CH2P- of 2 at δ 3.11 versus CH2O of ethanol at δ 3.40 confirmed the structure and purity of the product. ([[2015-musa|Musa, 2015, p. 38]])

- Used for confirming the identity of compounds 1c, 2c, and 3c. ([[2015-hassan|Hassan, 2015, p. 60]])
