# Nuclear Magnetic Resonance

**Summary**: A logging technique that measures the distribution of relaxation times of protons to determine porosity and fluid types.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- NMR porosity is independent of lithology, but its T-2 distribution shape depends on the fluids contained in the pore space. ([[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010, p. 101]])

- NMR provides a more accurate representation of total porosity and fluid distribution compared to standard logs in complex lithologies. ([[2013-abdullah|Abdullah, 2013, p. 17]])

- Signals of CH2P- of 2 at δ 3.11 versus CH2O of ethanol at δ 3.40 confirmed the structure and purity of the product. ([[2015-musa|Musa, 2015, p. 38]])

- Used for confirming the identity of compounds 1c, 2c, and 3c. ([[2015-hassan|Hassan, 2015, p. 60]])

- NMR parameters were used to classify pores into D1, D2, and D3 based on T2 relaxation times. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 109]])

- The most abundant porosities were found to be microporosities (clay bound water and capillary trapped water). ([[2021-ahmed|Ahmed, 2021, p. 102]])

- Used alongside core data and well logs to evaluate the lower unit (J1) of the Jubaila Formation. ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 51]])

- NMR data showed bimodal distributions in proximal/distal lithofacies and unimodal in medial lithofacies. ([[2022-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2022, p. 24]])

- Used as part of the petrophysical suite to quantify the contribution of different pore types to total porosity. ([[2022-salih|Salih, 2022, p. 41]])

- NMR was used to determine water saturation distribution at different stages of the coreflood experiment. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 12]])

- NMR was utilized to confirm porosity values across different sample types (sand and shale). ([[2024-abdrabo|Abdrabo, 2024, p. 33]])

- CF stromatolites exhibited a bimodal T2 distribution (mesopores and macropores), while LL forms showed more varied distributions. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 31]])

- The study found that drilling fluid bulk relaxation significantly affects the NMR T2 values, particularly in macropores. ([[2024-akomolafe|Akomolafe, 2024, p. 12]])
