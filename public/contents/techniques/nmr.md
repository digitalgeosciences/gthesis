# Nuclear Magnetic Resonance

**Summary**: Nuclear Magnetic Resonance measures the hydrogen content in fluids to determine porosity independent of lithology.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2013-al-kharraa|Al-Kharraa, 2013]] used NMR to evaluate porosity accuracy in carbonate reservoirs compared to conventional tools.

- [[2013-abdullah|Abdullah, 2013]] used NMR to distinguish free fluid from bound fluid volumes in thinly bedded reservoirs.

- [[2014-al-duailej|Al-Duailej, 2014]] used NMR to determine pore size distribution and identify changes in interconnectivity between different pore systems.

- [[2014-alao|Alao, 2014]] used 1H and 13C NMR to characterize functional groups in the surfactant samples.

- [[2015-adebayo|Adebayo, 2015]] used NMR to characterize the petrophysical properties of carbonate rocks before and after CO2 exposure.

- [[2015-musa|Musa, 2015]] used NMR to confirm the chemical structure and purity of synthesized monomers.

- [[2015-hassan|Hassan, 2015]] used NMR to confirm the chemical structures of products 1c, 2c, 3c, and 4c.

- [[2016-jad|Jad, 2016]] used NMR to quantify formation damage and identify adsorption locations in carbonate cores.

## Key Results and Findings

- In clean limestone, NMR provided the highest correlation (0.966) and lowest AARE (9.74%) compared to core data. ([[2013-al-kharraa|Al-Kharraa, 2013, p. 47]])

- NMR results showed a producible zone between 55 ft and 30 ft that was underestimated by conventional logs. ([[2013-abdullah|Abdullah, 2013, p. 75]])

- NMR was able to accurately analyze, in terms of pore size distribution, different characteristics of the wormholing process of an acid and was consistent with and complementing the CT scan analysis. ([[2014-al-duailej|Al-Duailej, 2014, p. 13]])

- NMR data revealed a marked chemical-shift band distinguishing compositions at different temperatures, identifying alkylic character vs. unsaturated bonds. ([[2014-alao|Alao, 2014, p. 18]])

- NMR measurements showed that all core samples are of bimodal pore type and are very homogeneous. ([[2015-adebayo|Adebayo, 2015, p. 16]])

- 1H- and 13C-NMR spectra were measured respectively. ... confirm how pure the product is. ([[2015-musa|Musa, 2015, p. 42]])

- 1H NMR was performed at 10,000 Hz and 13C NMR at 34013.6 Hz for structure confirmation. ([[2015-hassan|Hassan, 2015, p. 79]])

- Not stated in source ([[2015-barri|Barri, 2015]])

- The inclusion of NMR logs helped identify specific parameters like 'Integrated Permeability' and 'T2 Logarithmic Mean' that were selected by feature selection algorithms. ([[2015-alsahaf|Alsahaf, 2015, p. 50]])

- NMR results showed that a significant gap between T2 profiles before and after test indicated residual oil in Experiment-1, while close profiles in Experiment-3 suggested minor dissolution. ([[2016-adam|ADAM, 2016, p. 101]])

- NMR results showed a clear change in the pore size distribution after the injection of HCl. ([[2016-jad|Jad, 2016, p. 16]])

- 13C NMR showed that carbon peaks were in the aliphatic region, with intensity changes indicating potential degradation or concentration differences. ([[2017-janjua|Janjua, 2017, p. 141]])

- NMR was used to generate T2 relaxation time profiles to evaluate the diffusion process of water into the shale. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 35]])

- NMR was used to understand porosity, saturation profile, and the homogeneity of the core samples. ([[2017-muqtadir|Muqtadir, 2017, p. 50]])

- New peaks appeared at 5.80, 6.02, and 6.41 ppm due to new bonds with acrylic acid. ([[2019-mohammad|Mohammad, 2019, p. 61]])

- NMR confirmed that porosity increased without damaging original pore connectivity and verified the creation of micro-fractures. ([[2019-gomaa|Gomaa, 2019, p. 19]])

- NMR provided a way to distinguish between water-wet and oil-wet states by analyzing surface relaxation effects in T2 and T1T2 maps. ([[2019-al-garadi|AL-GARADI, 2019, p. 14]])

- The 13-interval Bipolar Gradients pulse sequence (BG-PFGSTE) was used to mitigate internal magnetic field gradients in carbonates. ([[2019-elsayed|Elsayed, 2019, p. 41]])

- NMR measurements confirmed the creation of multiple fractures due to the thermochemical injection. ([[2020-mohammed|Mohammed, 2020, p. 22]])

- The PSD revealed trimodal and bimodal distributions, with very few unimodal distributions. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 19]])

- NMR T2 relaxation time is proportional to the pore size, allowing for the identification of macropores, mesopores, and micropores. ([[2020-isah|Isah, 2020, p. 36]])

- NMR results showed that the emulsifier did not cause any plugging in the pore throats after the reaction was complete. ([[2020-aly|Aly, 2020, p. 163]])

- NMR T2 diffusion showed that the oil saturation is almost uniform in the two hemicylindrical plugs in Experiment 3. ([[2021-al-shuqayyiqi|Al-Shuqayyiqi, 2021, p. 46]])

- NMR was used to identify that rock porosity decreased due to mud solids invasion as exposure time increased. ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 54]])

- Not stated in source ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 91]])

- Used to compare different types of amines and latex for their effectiveness on Qusaiba shale. ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 43]])

- NMR was performed to evaluate the performance of ionic liquids as clay swelling inhibitors in completion fluids. ([[2021-khan|KHAN, 2021, p. 21]])

- NMR data showed bimodal porosity distribution curves in proximal and distal shelf lithofacies, while medial lithofacies were unimodal. ([[2022-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2022, p. 41]])

- The full T2 spectrum was used for the first time to benefit from all available information, improving model accuracy over single-parameter methods. ([[2022-suleiman|Suleiman, 2022, p. 16]])

- The area under the curve (average 1020 ms*p.u. for limestone) indicated high variation in pore structure. ([[2022-aljaberi|AlJaberi, 2022, p. 157]])

- NMR was used to observe that a resin-free asphaltene relaxes faster than one with resins, supporting the colloidal theory. ([[2023-suleiman|Suleiman, 2023, p. 66]])

- NMR T2 measurement is a primary tool for determining pore size distribution and permeability in both lab and field settings. ([[2023-al-boghail|AL-BOGHAIL, 2023, p. 40]])

- The study utilized NMR-based indicators to validate the empirical relationship between dielectric measurements and rock wettability. ([[2023-abdulwahab|Abdulwahab, 2023, p. 14]])

- NMR T2 results showed a dual porosity system with 97% of samples having large pores or fractures (relaxation times near 1000 ms). ([[2023-dafaalla|Dafaalla, 2023, p. 107]])

- NMR confirmed that even after the flowback stage, a slightly higher amount of water remained in the larger pores compared to the first drainage stage. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 43]])

- NMR results showed no deviation window for improved systems but large deviations for barite, ilmenite, and hematite based cements. ([[2023-ahmed-2|AHMED, 2023, p. 30]])

- NMR was used to correlate T2 relaxation time with pore radius to determine pore size distribution. ([[2023-pratama|PRATAMA, 2023, p. 54]])

- NMR confirmed that dynamic retention was highest in the lowest permeability rocks (10 mD). ([[2024-al-azani|Al-Azani, 2024, p. 23]])

- Not stated in source ([[2024-suleiman-2|Suleiman, 2024, p. 53]])

- T2 measurement showed that the peak for micropores shifted significantly toward the bulk-free nano-silica value after treatment. ([[2024-suleiman-4|Suleiman, 2024, p. 78]])

- Used for characterizing the pore network in tight rocks. ([[2024-isah|Isah, 2024, p. 58]])

- NMR provided detailed information about the pore structure and fluid content, allowing for a comparison of porosity before and after aging. ([[2024-almukainah|Almukainah, 2024, p. 45]])

- NMR is identified as a superior tool for carbonate rocks because it is independent of rock mineralogy. ([[2024-alhamood|ALHAMOOD, 2024, p. 36]])

- NMR data for CF stromatolites showed a bimodal distribution, while LL forms exhibited a mix of bimodal and unimodal distributions. ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 50]])

- The study found that T2 bulk values of drilling fluids are significantly affected by additives like XC polymer and hematite, impacting the interpretation of macropores. ([[2024-akomolafe|Akomolafe, 2024, p. 12]])

- NMR results confirmed that viscoelastic polymer flooding enhances recovery by targeting both macropores and micropores. ([[2024-jameel|Jameel, 2024, p. 87]])
