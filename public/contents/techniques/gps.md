## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2016-yasin|Yasin, 2016]] used GPS to facilitate the identification of breeding sites.

- [[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017]] used GPS to georeference electrode locations across multiple survey lines.

- [[2018-putra|Putra, 2018]] used DGPS to measure the position and elevation of 22 receivers on a beach survey with accuracy of 0.5 to 1 cm.

- [[2018-hanif|Hanif, 2018]] applied gps to provide accurate elevation measurements for gravity data correction.

## Key Results and Findings

- The use of GIS with GPS facilitated the mapping of larval breeding sites in the study area. ([[2016-yasin|Yasin, 2016, p. 16]])

- Provided accurate positioning for 72 electrodes per line in the Duba region. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 132]])

- DGPS measurements confirmed that while positions were mostly accurate, some variations existed in the actual distances between elements compared to the nominal 5m spacing. ([[2018-putra|Putra, 2018, p. 75]])

- Leica 1200+ GPS was used to achieve accuracy of ±1 cm for station elevations. ([[2018-hanif|Hanif, 2018, p. 41]])
