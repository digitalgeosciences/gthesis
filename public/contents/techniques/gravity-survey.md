# Gravity Survey

**Summary**: A geophysical method measuring variations in Earth's gravity to map density differences.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2012-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2012]] used gravity data to interpret regional faults and the general orientation of lineaments.

- [[2014-saeed|Saeed, 2014]] used gravity-survey to detect and characterize subsurface cavities based on density contrasts.

- [[2018-hanif|Hanif, 2018]] used gravity_survey to identify fault structures and potential cavities in the Dammam Dome.

## Key Results and Findings

- Provided information on both deep sources (basement structure) and shallow sources (faults, dykes). ([[2012-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2012, p. 20]])

- The method provides useful information about the depth, shape, and geometry of cavities with a resolution as small as 10 $\mu$Gal. ([[2014-saeed|Saeed, 2014, p. 28]])

- The survey identified 67 dominant fault structures with specific strike directions (N 39° W and N 48° E). ([[2018-hanif|Hanif, 2018, p. 15]])
