# Mathematical Modeling

**Summary**: The use of mathematical equations to model and predict physical properties like compressibility and pseudo-critical points.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- The newly developed correlations were found to be the most accurate across all ranges of pressure and density compared to existing models. ([[1990-al-jarri|Al-Jarri, 1990, p. 109]])

- Analytical models showed that the critical rate for a horizontal well is higher than that of a vertical well. ([[1993-kandil|Kandil, 1993, p. 35]])

- Dranchuk and Abou-Kassem correlation was found to give the least average absolute relative error with respect to original data. ([[1995-al-khamis|Al-Khamis, 1995, p. 14]])

- Not stated in source ([[1998-khan|Khan, 1998]])

- The developed program achieved an accuracy of less than 0.1% in determining permeability when slippage was the dominant effect. ([[2000-khan|Khan, 2000, p. 90]])

- Newer models developed in this study outperformed existing correlations across various pressure and temperature ranges. ([[1996-mehmood|Mehmood, 1996, p. 53]])

- A 29x29 grid system was found sufficient to reproduce the analytical solution accurately. ([[1990-mimoune|Mimoune, 1990, p. 47]])

- Second-order polynomial equations were introduced to keep relative errors within acceptable limits (10%). ([[1994-muhammad|Muhammad, 1994, p. 127]])

- Beggs and Brill provided the best overall prediction for Saudi Arabian field conditions. ([[1989-al-muraikhi|Al-Muraikhi, 1989, p. 19]])
