# Ozonation

**Summary**: The process of treating water with ozone to oxidize and remove organic contaminants.

---

## How It Is Used in the Thesis Collection

- [[2009-zubair|Zubair, 2009]] used ozonation to evaluate the degradation of phenol and the subsequent formation of bromate in water samples.

- [[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011]] used ozonation to evaluate bromate formation in bromide-containing waters.

## Key Results and Findings

- Continuous Ozonation (1 liter per minute) was found to be the most effective technique for complete phenol degradation within five minutes. ([[2009-zubair|Zubair, 2009, p. 13]])

- Ozonation of raw water containing as low as 50 μg/l of bromide produces bromate at concentrations in excess of the regulatory threshold. ([[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011, p. 33]])
