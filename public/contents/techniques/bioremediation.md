# Bioremediation

**Summary**: The use of microorganisms to detoxify or remove pollutants from the environment.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- Biodegradation was most effective at pH 7.0 and a temperature of 35°C using an ammonium chloride nutrient source. ([[2011-salamah|Salamah, 2011, p. 12]])

- Bioremediation is used as an interim treatment or disposal step, which reduces the overall level of hydrocarbon contamination prior to final dispose. ([[2012-attyah|Attyah, 2012, p. 35]])

- Identified several strains with high potential for bioremediation in the Arabian Gulf. ([[2015-oyehan|Oyehan, 2015, p. 18]])

- Four distinct bacterial colonies (Enterobacter, Staphylococcus, Pseudomonas, Bacillus) were identified. ([[2015-bello|Bello, 2015, p. 14]])

- Bioremediation represents an effective and environmentally benign technique for cleaning hydrocarbon contaminated sites. ([[2015-malik|Malik, 2015, p. 15]])

- The degradation rate of pyrene by Halomonas smyrnensis 20PY1A was the highest at 0.0495 day-1. ([[2017-budiyanto|Budiyanto, 2017, p. 84]])

- Prosopis juliflora showed more ability to absorb these chemicals from soils than Conocarpus erectus. ([[2019-alhaiqi|Alhaiqi, 2019, p. 67]])

- Indigenous bacteria were more efficient than microbial consortia, achieving removal rates of 69.3% to 73.6%. ([[2024-almutawa|AlMutawa, 2024, p. 22]])
