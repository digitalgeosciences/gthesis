# BET

**Summary**: A method for measuring the specific surface area and pore volume of a material by nitrogen adsorption.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- Used to understand the surface morphology, microstructure and other physical and chemical properties of the adsorbent materials. ([[2014-adebayo|Adebayo, 2014, p. 44]])

- Specific surface area was calculated by the Brunauer-Emmett-Teller (BET) method, and pore volumes were determined at the P/P0 = 0.974 single point. ([[2015-musa|Musa, 2015, p. 43]])

- The optimum activation temperature for enhancement of porosity was 900°C. ([[2015-danmaliki|Danmaliki, 2015, p. 19]])

- Fe2O3 had a specific surface area of 14.76 m2/g and nZVI had 15.17 m2/g. ([[2016-salawu|Salawu, 2016, p. 92]])

- The BET model was found more representative than Langmuir for describing adsorption behavior on studied rocks. ([[2016-elnaeem|Elnaeem, 2016, p. 97]])

- The surface area values for 3K-700-30, 2Z-700-90, 2K-500-90, and 2Z-500-30 were 451 m2/g, 385 m2/g, 53 m2/g, and 38 m2/g respectively. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 12]])

- GPDADMAC showed a Type II isotherm and H3 hysteresis loop, indicating micropores, mesopores, and macropores. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 50]])

- The surface area of the ground Saudi Arabian limestone was 13.2 m2/g, while pure calcite was 2.074 m2/g. ([[1987-ahmed|Ahmed, 1987, p. 44]])
