# Saturation Height Modeling

**Summary**: A method to calculate hydrocarbon saturation as a function of depth and rock property.

---

## Key Results and Findings

- The saturation height model results compared favorably with resistivity-derived saturations, confirming its accuracy for volume estimation. ([[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010, p. 176]])

- Water saturation was calculated using hydraulic unit parameters, showing a 5.3% difference in Reservoir D compared to average well methods. ([[2011-al-nasser|Al-Nasser, 2011, p. 67]])

- The presence of magnesium ions requires an increase in the capillary pressure to rupture the water film as temperature increases. ([[2012-jabbar|JABBAR, 2012, p. 31]])

- Capillary forces are dispersive on the fine scale but can lead to self-sharpening behavior on the coarse scale. ([[2013-al-mutairi|AL-MUTAIRI, 2013, p. 33]])

- The J-function was used to convert all capillary curves to a universal one. ([[2015-al-amri|Al-Amri, 2015, p. 26]])

- Capillary pressure is the primary driver for initial imbibition in both sandstones and shales. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 39]])

- Capillary pressure values in the database ranged from zero to 60,000 psi. ([[2017-busaleh|BuSaleh, 2017, p. 48]])

- Thermochemical treatment reduced capillary pressure by 47% (or 55.57% in specific samples). ([[2020-mohammed|Mohammed, 2020, p. 22]])

- The Pc curve can be broken down into components for macropores and micropores to better understand the contribution of different pore systems. ([[2020-isah|Isah, 2020, p. 15]])

- Capillary pressure is influenced by pore size, surface tension, and contact angle. ([[2023-pratama|PRATAMA, 2023, p. 117]])

- Thermochemical treatment was found to decrease capillary pressure by 57%. ([[2024-abdallah|Abdallah, 2024, p. 37]])

- The application of oleic acid resulted in a notable transformation in the rock sample's characteristics, resulting in a significant reduction in water saturation at 20 psi (50% for carbonate). ([[2024-suleiman-2|Suleiman, 2024, p. 57]])

- Capillary pressure is a function of the relationship between capillary pressure and brine saturation. ([[2024-suleiman-3|Suleiman, 2024, p. 35]])

- Effective storage is achieved when Pce is greater than the buoyancy pressure exerted by the fluids in the underlying layer. ([[2024-muhammed|Muhammed, 2024, p. 93]])

- Thermochemical treatment resulted in... a drop in the capillary pressure (Pc). ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024, p. 43]])

- Lowering IFT reduces the capillary forces that trap oil in the pore spaces of the reservoir rock. ([[2024-jameel|Jameel, 2024, p. 30]])

- Capillary pressure curves are insensitive to sample size in sandstone but highly dependent on it in carbonate rocks. ([[1991-ali|Ali, 1991, p. 22]])

- Capillary pressure curves were found to be insensitive to sample size in sandstone but sensitive to it in carbonate rocks. ([[1991-ali-2|Ali, 1991, p. 22]])

- Including capillary pressure end effects results in higher recovery for composites and lower calculated relative permeabilities compared to cases without these effects. ([[1995-hussain|Hussain, 1995, p. 104]])
