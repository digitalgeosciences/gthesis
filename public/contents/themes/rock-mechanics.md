## Key Findings Across Sources

- Used elastic constants (Young's modulus, Poisson's ratio) as primary indicators for brittle behavior. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 56]])

- Borehole logs showed R.Q.D. values as low as 0% to 14% in several sections. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 126]])

- Young's modulus (E*) from impulse hammer provides a reliable proxy for rock strength. ([[2020-gbiyanju|Gbiyanju, 2020, p. 31]])

- Chemical treatments significantly improved the indentation hardness of chalk and limestone. ([[2022-samarkin|Samarkin, 2022, p. 13]])

- Acidizing decreases the stiffness and elastic modulus of the rocks to various degrees. ([[2023-al-garni|Al-Garni, 2023, p. 2]])

- Young's modulus and Poisson's ratio were reduced after acid treatment in limestone. ([[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023, p. 40]])

- Young's modulus is a key indicator for predicting unconfined strength. ([[2002-elhassan|ElHassan, 2002, p. 40]])
