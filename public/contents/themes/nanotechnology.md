## Key Findings Across Sources

- MWCNTs produced by CVD were confirmed as high-purity materials with a diameter of 20-40 nm. ([[2009-al-khaldi|Al-Khaldi, 2009, p. 70]])

- Nano silica was found more effective than ordinary silica fume in enhancing compressive strength. ([[2012-amer|Amer, 2012, p. 36]])

- Referenced in this thesis. ([[2013-murtaza|Murtaza, 2013]])

- CNT-included samples showed superior thermal endurance compared to standard formulations. ([[2014-alao|Alao, 2014, p. 18]])

- Mg-MOF-74 showed distinct adsorption characteristics compared to traditional adsorbents like activated carbon and zeolite 13X. ([[2014-jamil|JAMIL, 2014, p. 68]])

- Synthesized AC loaded with metals showed significant improvement in the adsorption of the refractory sulfur compounds. ([[2015-danmaliki|Danmaliki, 2015, p. 19]])

- Referenced in this thesis. ([[2015-thalab|Thalab, 2015]])

- Nanoparticles provide enhanced mechanical, optical, transport, and magnetic properties due to high surface area-to-volume ratios. ([[2015-khan-2|Khan, 2015, p. 45]])

- Nanoparticles offer unique properties like surface plasmon resonance and high reactivity. ([[2016-salawu|Salawu, 2016, p. 51]])

- Nanoparticles smaller than 100 nm can easily penetrate through porous media due to their size relative to pore throats. ([[2016-alhamad|AlHamad, 2016, p. 18]])

- Nanosilica was found to improve gel strength at 200 , while nanoclay did not provide similar benefits for rheology. ([[2017-alakbari|Alakbari, 2017, p. 11]])

- Nano zeolite can serve as an effective additive for Oil-well cement as it enhances early compressive strength with improvement in durability properties. ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 20]])

- GPDADMAC and GPHM were successfully synthesized and characterized as effective adsorbents. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 15]])

- Modified graphene (PG) showed a maximum adsorption capacity of 161.29 mg/g, significantly higher than other tested variants. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 69]])
