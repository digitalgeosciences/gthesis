## Key Findings Across Sources

- Reservoir quality was found to be highly dependent on matrix content and cementation types. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 32]])

- Peloid coated-grain grainstone/packstone has the best quality due to interparticle porosity, while others have poor quality due to microporosity. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 94]])

- Reservoir quality is influenced by both depositional and diagenetic controls. ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 19]])

- Cross-bedded oolitic skeletal grainstone facies has the highest flow units quality where moldic and vuggy porosities are dominant. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 18]])

- Reservoir quality was assessed across three units (A, B, and C) using specific measurement keys. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 138]])

- Categorized into high (9-14% porosity, >1 mD), moderate (1-12% porosity, 0.1-1 mD), and poor (<11% porosity, <0.1 mD). ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 16]])

- Heterogeneity in porosity and permeability is controlled by both depositional and post-depositional processes. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 45]])

- High porosity (>17%) in open marine environments was attributed to partial dolomitization in burrows. ([[2018-ismanto|Ismanto, 2018, p. 103]])

- Kaolinite, compaction, and cementation reduced quality; feldspar dissolution enhanced it. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 19]])

- Reservoir quality was found to be low overall, with some improvements in specific intervals due to feldspar dissolution. ([[2021-alqarni|Alqarni, 2021, p. 123]])

- The study found that burrowing styles (e.g., pipe framework) significantly influence permeability and porosity distribution. ([[2022-syahputra|Syahputra, 2022, p. 14]])

- Three Reservoir Rock Types (RRT) were identified with varying qualities from good to poor. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 77]])

- Sandstone bodies have medium to high permeabilities, while conglomerate deposits have low to medium range permeabilities. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 79]])

- Sandy peloidal grainstone had mean porosity of 13.3% and permeability of 1.01 mD. ([[2024-allam|Allam, 2024, p. 11]])

- FA1 showed high quality (25-30% porosity) while FA4 showed low quality (0.1-11% porosity). ([[2024-yusmananto|Yusmananto, 2024, p. 60]])

- Porosity was relatively consistent across forms, but permeability varied significantly (up to two orders of magnitude). ([[2024-muharrag|Muharrag, 2024, p. 37]])

- Bioturbation can change the physical properties of rocks... changing the original porosity and permeability. These changes can be either an enhancement or reduction in the reservoir quality. ([[2024-allehyani|allehyani, 2024, p. 3]])
