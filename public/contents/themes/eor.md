## Key Findings Across Sources

- Gas preconditioning improves the effectiveness of mud acid treatment by preventing sludge formation. ([[1999-al-muhareb|Al-Muhareb, 1999, p. 46]])

- Oil recovery increases as the injected water salinity increases up to 100,000 mg/l. ([[1990-al-mumen|Al-Mumen, 1990, p. 22]])

- The combination of solvent and hot water was found to be an effective method for improving mobility in tarmat reservoirs. ([[1995-okasha|OKASHA, 1995, p. 10]])

- The proposed chelating agent method aims to reduce residual oil saturation (Sor) by increasing solution pH and altering wettability. ([[2013-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2013, p. 21]])

- Maximum recovery of 47.7% of residual oil in place in low permeability was observed compared to 43% of residual oil in place on high permeability. ([[2013-geri|Geri, 2013, p. 17]])

- Chelating agents were shown to provide up to 30% additional recovery over sea water flooding. ([[2013-elsayed|Elsayed, 2013, p. 14]])

- CO2 flooding was identified as a key EOR method to reduce residual oil saturation in mature fields. ([[2013-sibaweihi|Sibaweihi, 2013, p. 17]])

- CO2 flooding is one of the most applied EOR processes, contributing significantly to global production. ([[2013-al-mutairi|AL-MUTAIRI, 2013, p. 27]])

- Surfactant-Polymer (SP) flooding combines mobility control by polymer and interfacial reduction by surfactant. ([[2013-ahmed|AHMED, 2013, p. 25]])

- The study aimed to provide a viable option for high salinity carbonate reservoirs in Saudi Arabia. ([[2014-jangda|Jangda, 2014, p. 13]])

- Chemical EOR is a promising technique for carbonate reservoirs due to its ability to provide both displacement and sweep efficiency. ([[2014-azad|Azad, 2014, p. 23]])

- Chemicals like Tallowamine Acetate and polymers are commonly used in EOR but complicate downstream water treatment. ([[2015-he|He, 2015, p. 61]])

- Carbon dioxide flooding is a proven technique which can be employed in order to enhance oil recovery from such reservoirs. ([[2014-shakeel|SHAKEEL, 2014, p. 13]])

- 5wt% EDTA in seawater was found to be the optimal concentration for maximizing recovery without damaging rock integrity. ([[2015-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2015, p. 23]])

- ES-SAGD is one of the most promising thermal techniques to recover heavy oil and extra heavy-oil reservoirs. ([[2015-moussa|Moussa, 2015, p. 16]])

- SAGD and VAPEX were identified as primary EOR methods for heavy oil, with the latter utilizing solvent injection to reduce viscosity. ([[2015-khan|KHAN, 2015, p. 46]])

- DTPA-K5 showed significant promise in increasing both oil recovery and permeability in Berea sandstone. ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 48]])

- The best sequential scenario for enhancing the oil recovery can be achieved by flooding with 3wt. % followed by 5wt% for both chelants GLDA and EDTA. ([[2015-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2015, p. 16]])

- Foam has proved to significantly increase the oil recovery because foam increases the apparent viscosity of the system. ([[2016-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2016, p. 19]])

- Smart water-flooding offers an opportunity to gain extra oil recovery with lower costs compared to other EOR methods. ([[2016-adam|ADAM, 2016, p. 17]])

- The study confirmed that while VES is an EOR fluid, its adsorption in pore throats leads to significant permeability loss. ([[2016-jad|Jad, 2016, p. 106]])

- The mixture of Al2O3 and SiO2 nanoparticles showed significant improvement over standard water flooding. ([[2016-alhamad|AlHamad, 2016, p. 24]])

- The study aimed to find a low IFT system using surfactants like FS-50, Alfoterra, and Armovis. ([[2016-mahboob-2|Mahboob, 2016, p. 72]])

- In-situ CO2 generation is considered a potential EOR method because it addresses issues like gravity override and poor foam stability. ([[2014-alam|ALAM, 2014, p. 18]])

- Carbonates have higher affinity to adsorb CO2 than methane, making them good candidates for EGR. ([[2016-elnaeem|Elnaeem, 2016, p. 55]])

- CO2-EOR is an efficient method for recovering oil from depleted reservoirs, but requires mobility control to prevent early gas breakthrough. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 17]])

- The main goal of EOR process is to alter the mobility and to increase the capillary number in the reservoir. ([[2017-janjua|Janjua, 2017, p. 21]])

- The core-flooding experiments support the importance of optimizing the design of CEOR processes taking into consideration the type of chemicals, concentrations, slug sizes, and flooding sequence. ([[2017-ahmed-3|Ahmed, 2017, p. 19]])
