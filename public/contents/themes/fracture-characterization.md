## Key Findings Across Sources

- Characterized fractures based on orientation, length, and intensity across multiple scales. ([[2012-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2012, p. 11]])

- Fracture intensity is higher in Lithofacies-III due to its higher silica content. ([[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015, p. 36]])

- Identified three sets of fractures in Upper Jubaila and five sets in Arab-D member. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 14]])

- No correlation was found between curvature, coherence, and AVOA for fracture identification. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 12]])

- Identified three types of fracture modes (opening, sliding, shear) with varying fill materials. ([[2016-babker|Babker, 2016, p. 13]])

- Most are mineralized Mode-I fractures associated with bedding-parallel stylolites. ([[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016, p. 19]])

- Two major directions (NE-SW and NW-SE) were identified for the primary fracture sets. ([[2017-mahgoub|Mahgoub, 2017, p. 67]])

- Two primary fracture sets (N165° and N075°) were identified in the Sanamah Formation. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 13]])

- Fractures act as primary conduits for fluid flow in rocks with low matrix permeability. ([[2020-mansouri|Mansouri, 2020, p. 51]])

- Identified a variety of fracture types including shear fractures and silica veins. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 48]])

- FFD is a critical metric for identifying high-permeability zones in non-volcanic systems. ([[2023-arrofi|Arrofi, 2023]])

- Higher fracture density was observed in areas where burrows were affected by dissolution. ([[2024-saraih|Saraih, 2024, p. 60]])
