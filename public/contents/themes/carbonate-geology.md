## Key Findings Across Sources

- Identified specific carbonate lithofacies (oolitic, skeletal) as high permeability units. ([[2002-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2002, p. 22]])

- The succession represents a steadily shallowing upward cycle from Late Eocene to Early Oligocene. ([[1992-el-sahn|El-Sahn, 1992, p. 78]])

- Carbonate reservoirs present unique complexities in petrophysical and geological interpretation. ([[2003-al-shridi|Al-Shridi, 2003]])

- The first shallowing upward carbonate cycle, incorporating of Sulaiy and Yamama Formations was initiated at the beginning of the Cretaceous. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 104]])

- Carbonate sediments dominate the shallow water along the Arabian side of the Gulf. ([[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988, p. 25]])

- Identified five distinct facies including grainstone, wackestone, and mudstone types. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 13]])

- Identified various facies such as ooid grainstones and thrombolites in a shallow marine shelf environment. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 30]])

- Identified distinct carbonate lithofacies such as stromatolite boundstone and oolitic grainstone. ([[2015-koeshidayatullah|Koeshidayatullah, 2015, p. 39]])

- The Ulayyah Reservoir Equivalent is a carbonate system with diverse lithofacies reflecting various marine environments. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 95]])

- Carbonate rocks are highly variable and heterogeneous in terms of facies stacking pattern, architecture, and diagenesis. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 17]])

- The study reveals a dominance of carbonate lithofacies over siliciclastics in the area. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 119]])

- The Dam Formation contains diverse carbonate facies like stromatolites and oolitic grainstones. ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 53]])

- The study identified distinct microfacies within the carbonate system. ([[2016-radwan|Radwan, 2016, p. 36]])

- Identified various oolitic grainstones and diagenetic impacts on reservoir quality. ([[2016-makkawi|Makkawi, 2016, p. 31]])

- Carbonate lithofacies account for approximately 85% of the Dam Formation. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 58]])

- Large benthic foraminifera might be responsible for 150 g year-1 m-2 in carbonate production. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 43]])

- TMF is a significant carbonate reservoir in Saudi Arabia. ([[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017, p. 20]])

- Identified 7 distinct environmental settings within the Upper Khartam Member. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 100]])

- The Dhruma Formation is a prime example of an ancient carbonate ramp system. ([[2018-ismanto|Ismanto, 2018, p. 16]])
