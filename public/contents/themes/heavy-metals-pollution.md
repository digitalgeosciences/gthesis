# Heavy Metals Pollution

**Summary**: The study of pollutants caused by the presence of heavy metals in soil and water.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Identified that industrial and residential activities contribute significantly to elevated trace metal levels. ([[2014-adedapo|Adedapo, 2014, p. 13]])

- Trace metals are highly persistent and pose risks to human health through bioaccumulation in the food chain. ([[2015-jawad|Jawad, 2015, p. 19]])

- Mercury is highly persistent and can bioaccumulate in the food web, posing significant risks to human health. ([[2016-salawu|Salawu, 2016, p. 38]])

- Cadmium is a significant threat to human health and the environment due/to its lack of biodegradability. ([[2017-olabemiwo|Olabemiwo, 2017, p. 19]])

- GPHM removed more than 60% of 0.5 ppm cadmium within 10 min. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 15]])

- Copper concentrations reached up to 70.0 mg/kg in Gurmah Island bark samples. ([[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019, p. 92]])
