## Key Findings Across Sources

- Several pesticides were found at concentrations exceeding local safety concerns, necessitating a baseline for future management. ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013, p. 32]])

- Identified sites with varying levels of PAH contamination (9.09 to 13.42 mg/Kg). ([[2015-oyehan|Oyehan, 2015, p. 57]])

- Organotins were found at varying levels, with distance from shore being a significant factor. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 11]])

- Sewage effluents were found to significantly impact nutrient and microbial levels in coastal waters. ([[2015-yusuf|Yusuf, 2015, p. 68]])

- Impacts were assessed specifically regarding hydrocarbonds and heavy metals along the Saudi coastline. ([[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015, p. 124]])

- The average concentrations of PM10 were 126.00 µg/m3, 177.00 µg/m3, and 379.85 µg/m3 in Dammam, Dhahran, and Khobar respectively. ([[2016-lawal-2|Lawal, 2016, p. 14]])

- Mercury is a significant pollutant requiring specialized removal and detection techniques. ([[2016-salawu|Salawu, 2016, p. 17]])

- Identified high toxicity in Brine Shrimp exposed to SPP of OBM. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 16]])

- The production process successfully eliminated microorganisms from the raw sludge samples. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 74]])

- Identified significant concentrations of trace metals in PM10 samples from industrial areas. ([[2019-alotaibi|Alotaibi, 2019, p. 144]])

- Over 40% of the shorelines have been industrialized, leading to significant heavy metal and PAH contamination. ([[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019, p. 27]])

- Saudi Arabia is a major emitter of Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), which poses significant health challenges. ([[2022-mustapha|Mustapha, 2022, p. 25]])

- Microplastics are introduced to marine environments via land-based and sea-based sources, including waste management failures and industrial activities. ([[2022-alshaikhali|Alshaikhali, 2022, p. 16]])

- Illegal dumping leads to potential contamination of groundwater, soil, and air. ([[2022-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2022, p. 17]])
