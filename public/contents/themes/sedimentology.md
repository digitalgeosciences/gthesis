## Key Findings Across Sources

- Petrographic analysis revealed a quartz-dominant framework with significant diagenetic alterations. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 80]])

- Determined that the deposits formed in a restricted shelf environment. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 26]])

- Identified a complex depositional history involving alluvial fan and playa environments. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 35]])

- Two major shallowing-upward cycles were identified in the Lower Cretaceous sequence. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 15]])

- The study identified a transition from subtidal lagoonal environments to subaerial facies during the Pleistocene. ([[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988, p. 108]])

- The Dam Formation is characterized by various carbonate lithofacies including grainstones, packstones, and argillaceous limestones. ([[2006-al-enezi|Al-Enezi, 2006, p. 30]])

- Identified distinct eolian facies including dunes, interdunes, and sand sheets. ([[2011-al-masrahy|AL-MASRAHY, 2011, p. 37]])

- Identified a transition from low energy (tidal flat) to high energy (shoal) environments. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 54]])

- Sedimentological analysis identified the Hofuf Formation as representing continental facies with varying porosity (0-40%). ([[2013-yousif|Yousif, 2013, p. 26]])

- Identified distinct lithofacies including trough cross-bedded, horizontally stratified, and ferruginous sandstone. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 47]])

- Identified distinct facies types, geometries, and architectural elements across five paleovalleys. ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 15]])

- Identified a variety of sedimentary structures including trough cross-bedding, mud clasts, and syneresis cracks. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 100]])

- Lithofacies such as foraminiferal grainstone show distinct textures like crinkly laminations. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 68]])

- Lithofacies investigation revealed that Dam Formation is composed of mixed carbonate-siliciclastic deposits. ([[2016-hassan-2|Hassan, 2016, p. 13]])

- Identified five distinct lithofacies including diamictite, siltstone, and fluvial sandstone. ([[2016-babker|Babker, 2016, p. 13]])

- Identified diverse sedimentary structures and paleocurrent directions. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 41]])

- Identified distinct lithofacies associations (e.g., alluvial fan, fluvial, deltaic). ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 17]])

- Ten distinct lithofacies were identified, ranging from oolitic grainstones to mudstones. ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 7]])

- The Dam Formation is characterized by sandstone, marl, sandy limestone, clay, conglomerate, and thin gypsum layers. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 25]])

- The Qusaiba member was deposited in offshore, lower shoreface, middle shoreface, and upper shoreface environments. ([[2017-mahgoub|Mahgoub, 2017, p. 61]])

- Identified 17 lithofacies representing mid-ramp to inner ramp environments. ([[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017, p. 16]])

- Identified four distinct lithofacies associations (FA1-FA4) representing different environments. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 65]])

- Identified various carbonate environments including lagoonal settings and high-energy shoals. ([[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017, p. 89]])

- Identified 12 lithofacies in a similar study, grouped into 5-4 associations. ([[2017-yousif|Yousif, 2017, p. 50]])

- Identified four facies associations and thirteen sub-lithofacies in the Nutaysh member. ([[2018-butt|Butt, 2018, p. 2]])

- Dhruma is a good example of an ancient carbonate ramp. ([[2018-ismanto|Ismanto, 2018, p. 83]])

- The system includes dunes, interdunes, sand sheets, and sabkhas influenced by wind and water. ([[2022-al-alawi|Al-Alawi, 2022, p. 60]])

- Sedimentary features like cross-bedding, ripples, and burrowing were used to interpret depositional environments. ([[2020-al-zoukani|Al-Zoukani, 2020, p. 70]])
