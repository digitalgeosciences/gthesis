## Key Findings Across Sources

- contributed to the theme of Seismic Interpretation ([[1997-al-marhoun|Al-Marhoun, 1997]])

- Interpretation data provided a basis for identifying consistent horizon picks across various locations. ([[2010-al-moqbel|Al-Moqbel, 2010, p. 32]])

- Faults detected from seismic data showed consistent trends with lineaments from satellite imagery and gravity data. ([[2012-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2012, p. 11]])

- The method can serve as a complementary tool to other existing seismic attributes for identifying unconformity, sequence boundary, and structural deformation. ([[2015-adetokunbo|Adetokunbo, 2015, p. 13]])

- Identified four major sequence boundaries as erosional unconformities resulting from the structural collapse of the clastic wedge. ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 73]])

- Identified four continuous reflectors corresponding to major stratigraphic surfaces including the MFS and Samma boundary. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 17]])

- Seismic attributes (RMS) were used to delineate the outer configuration of delta systems. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 117]])

- Seismically derived chimneys were successful in highlighting hydrocarbon migration pathways. ([[2020-abdallah|Abdallah, 2020, p. 27]])

- Identified a robust sub-salt structure by iteratively testing salt flood velocities between 3925 and 4925 m/s. ([[2020-abdallah-2|Abdallah, 2020, p. 73]])

- The success of the rock physics work determines the feasibility of future seismic interpretation. ([[2020-almumtin|Almumtin, 2020, p. 22]])

- The 2D tomogram showed a low velocity area (650-800 m/s) below the deformation zone. ([[2023-alshaibani|AlShaibani, 2023, p. 11]])

- Quantitatively interpreted porosity and kerogen content using a multi-step workflow involving upscaled variables. ([[2024-alabbad|Alabbad, 2024, p. 123]])

- A 5.4m receiver line was used for high-resolution data, while a 73 shot gather survey provided broader coverage. ([[2024-tarchi|Tarchi, 2024, p. 43]])

- Seismic data provided P-wave velocity values used in conjunction with GPR data to estimate water saturation. ([[2024-alkhunaizi|Alkhunaizi, 2024, p. 12]])

- Seismic data provide the seismic velocity variations in the subsurface and aid in the characterization of different geological formations. ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 48]])

- Errors in depth calculation were found to be significantly higher when using 'all available' offsets compared to restricted offsets near the critical angle. ([[2024-felemban|Felemban, 2024, p. 11]])

- Identified five key horizons: Upper Manganui, Moki A, Middle Manganui, Moki B, and Lower Manganui. ([[2024-maigana|Maigana, 2024, p. 8]])

- Inverted water saturation values fell within the range of (0.0882 - 0.1514) with a mean of 0.1035. ([[2025-oyekan|Oyekan, 2025, p. 12]])
