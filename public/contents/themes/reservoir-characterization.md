## Key Findings Across Sources

- Integrated seismic, well, and core data to identify high permeability zones. ([[2002-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2002, p. 13]])

- Integrating facies and seismic data improved the spatial description of the reservoir compared to well-only models. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 17]])

- Multi-attribute analysis provided more reliable results than single-attribute methods. ([[1997-al-marhoun|Al-Marhoun, 1997, p. 16]])

- Reservoir characterization can yield major improvements in reservoir performance prediction and reservoir development and management. ([[1997-al-qassab|Al-Qassab, 1997, p. 18]])

- Determined that chlorite and illite significantly impacted permeability, while quartz overgrowths reduced both porosity and permeability. ([[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001, p. 18]])

- Geostatistical techniques are very useful tools to meet the challenges of reservoir characterization and management. ([[1996-al-salem|Al-Salem, 1996, p. 20]])

- Lithofacies and porosity were successfully predicted using neural networks. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 23]])

- Porosity, permeability, and lithofacies are key factors in reservoir characterizations. ([[2009-sitouah|Sitouah, 2009, p. 15]])

- Saturation-height modeling is a key component for characterizing oil and gas-capped reservoirs. ([[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010, p. 1]])

- Identified that peloid coated-grain grainstone and packstone has the best reservoir quality due to connected interparticle porosity. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 15]])

- Included identifying orientation, distribution, and geometry of fractures using multi-source data. ([[2012-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2012, p. 20]])

- Laminated Sand Analysis (LSA) provides superior characterization for thinly bedded reservoirs compared to standard methods. ([[2013-abdullah|Abdullah, 2013, p. 16]])

- Multi-attribute analysis helped identify sand bodies and their distribution in the Aradeiba-D zone. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 14]])

- Not stated in source ([[2013-atig|atig, 2013]])

- Integration of SGR, geochemistry, and sedimentology improved the lithofacies model. ([[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013, p. 189]])

- Characterized the formation as an unconventional tight gas reservoir with significant heterogeneity. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 34]])

- Outcrop analogs help predict the distribution of potential reservoir facies and their petrophysical properties. ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 84]])

- Characterized porosity types and ranked flow units to determine reservoir quality. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 124]])

- Characterization included sedimentology, DOM, and petrophysical analysis to determine reservoir quality. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 135]])

- Referenced in this thesis. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015]])

- A 3D geocellular model was used to characterize lithofacies heterogeneity and reservoir architecture. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 13]])

- Arab-D member can be differentiated from Upper Jubaila through their strength-permeability correlation more clearly than using strength-porosity correlation. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 90]])

- Identified high-quality zones in sand shoal grainstones with significant porosity. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 13]])

- Models showed small scale heterogeneity in porosity and permeability even within the same lithofacies. ([[2016-hassan-2|Hassan, 2016, p. 14]])
