**Summary**: The process of removing contaminants from water to make it safe for human consumption.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Continuous Ozonation (1 liter per min) was found most effective for phenol degradation in 5 minutes. ([[2009-zubair|Zubair, 2009, p. 13]])

- MWCNTs and modified versions (MWCNT-Fe) were effective at removing As(III) and Hg(II). ([[2009-al-khaldi|Al-Khaldi, 2009, p. 11]])

- Different disinfectants (chlorine, chlorine dioxide, ozone) produced different levels of DBPs based on dosage, temperature, and time. ([[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011, p. 9]])

- Advanced oxidation processes (AOPs) are a promising treatment option for water contaminated by MTBE. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 15]])

- Biological techniques include natural attenuation, phytoremediation, and bioremediation. ([[2014-abdallah|Abdallah, 2014, p. 35]])

- Silver oxide impregnated fly ash was identified as an effective adsorbent for MTBE removal. ([[2014-adebayo|Adebayo, 2014, p. 12]])

- The study evaluated the removal of Ni, Co, Cd, and Cr from water using three types of synthesized resins. ([[2015-musa|Musa, 2015, p. 13]])

- Desulfurization is a key process in refining and environmental protection. ([[2015-danmaliki|Danmaliki, 2015, p. 10]])

- Maximum phenol removal reached 86.6% using a combination of graphene oxide and organic dyes. ([[2015-hassan|Hassan, 2015, p. 16]])

- Both Phragmites australis and Bolboschoenus maritimus were effective in treating cadmium and lead. ([[2015-bello|Bello, 2015, p. 59]])

- The combination of nanoparticles and membranes provided an effective method for mercury removal. ([[2016-salawu|Salawu, 2016, p. 117]])

- UV-H2O2 was found effective, with 97% removal efficiency under optimal conditions. ([[2016-sameh|Sameh, 2016, p. 15]])

- S1 showed 96, 98 and 97% nitrate removal at N1, N2 and N3 respectively when incubated at 35°C. ([[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016, p. 76]])

- Modified fly ash (PEFA) showed high efficiency in cadmium removal, reaching 98.6% at optimal conditions. ([[2017-olabemiwo|Olabemiwo, 2017, p. 14]])

- SDAC 2Z-700-90 was found effective for removing MTBE from contaminated aqueous solutions. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 12]])

- GPDADMAC achieved 80% removal of phenol while GPHM removed over 60% of cadmium. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 15]])

- Electrochemical oxidation was found to be an efficient and environmentally friendly process for nitrogen-based pollutants. ([[2020-ghazwani|Ghazwani, 2020, p. 15]])

- PG was found effective in both batch and dynamic modes for treating methylene blue and metals. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 19]])

- Fe-modified biochar achieved 99% arsenic removal in batch studies. ([[2021-siddiq|Siddiq, 2021, p. 13]])

- Chelating agents can serve as multi-purpose additives to simplify fluid design while managing high hardness. ([[2021-alkhowaildi|Alkhowaildi, 2021, p. 37]])

- Referenced in this thesis. ([[2023-al-aqeel|Al-Aqeel, 2023]])

- The optimum treatment conditions for MTBE were: CAC dosage of 2 g, initial concentrations of 5000 μg/L, flow rate of 1 mL/min, using sandstone bed type and bed height of 20 cm at a conductivity of 33850 μS/cm. ([[2025-almousa|Almousa, 2025, p. 13]])
