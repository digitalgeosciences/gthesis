## Key Findings Across Sources

- The study highlighted the impact of structural relief and diagenesis (dolomitization) on carbonate deposition and reservoir quality. ([[2002-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2002, p. 91]])

- The specific surface area for the crushed rock sample was 13.2 m²/g (reported) or 0.317 m²/g (measured). ([[1992-rodriguez|Rodriguez, 1992, p. 43]])

- The study area is located in a productive basin in a carbonate reservoir of upper Jurassic. ([[2003-al-shridi|Al-Shridi, 2003, p. 54]])

- The carbonate surface became more water-wet with increasing concentration of Mg2+ ions. ([[2012-jabbar|JABBAR, 2012, p. 100]])

- More than 50% of proven oil resources are trapped in these rocks, but average recovery is less than 30%. ([[2012-kassim|KASSIM, 2012, p. 22]])

- Carbonate reservoirs are often neutral to oil-wet, hindering standard waterflooding efficiency. ([[2013-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2013, p. 15]])

- Maximum recovery of 47.7% of residual oil in place in low permeability was observed compared to 43% of residual oil in place on high permeability. ([[2013-geri|Geri, 2013, p. 17]])

- Exposing carbonate rock to brine containing CO2 causes alteration of the rock wettibility from an oil-wet to an intermediate-wet state. ([[2013-al-mutairi|AL-MUTAIRI, 2013, p. 132]])

- The study focused on a carbonate anticline capped by an anhydrite seal. ([[2014-al-julaih|Al-Julaih, 2014, p. 31]])

- The study used limestone rock to simulate Saudi carbonate reservoir conditions. ([[2014-jangda|Jangda, 2014, p. 35]])

- Different carbonate types showed varying responses to brine chemistry regarding surface roughness and charge. ([[2014-kasha|Kasha, 2014, p. 123]])

- Carbonate rocks have a very complex pore system because of the co-existence of interparticle porosity and highly variable secondary system of dissolution voids. ([[2014-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2014, p. 8]])

- Used Indiana limestone, Austin chalk, and pink Desert as model carbonates. ([[2015-barri|Barri, 2015, p. 52]])

- The study highlights that carbonate reservoirs pose significant challenges due to their inherent spatial heterogeneities. ([[2015-alsahaf|Alsahaf, 2015, p. 21]])

- The ultimate recovery in the second experiment (using GLDA) is 83.76% while it is 65.5% in the first experiment (using EDTA). ([[2015-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2015, p. 112]])

- Carbonate reservoirs often exhibit complex behavior due to mineral dissolution and multi-ion exchange. ([[2016-adam|ADAM, 2016, p. 31]])

- Hydrothermal dolomitization significantly altered the rock fabric, impacting mechanical properties in Khuff C. ([[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016, p. 71]])

- Not stated in source ([[2014-alam|ALAM, 2014]])

- Carbonate reservoirs are naturally fractured and have dual-porosity and dual-permeability. ([[2017-khan|Khan, 2017, p. 32]])

- The study addressed the prevalent bimodality of carbonate rock. ([[2017-busaleh|BuSaleh, 2017, p. 19]])

- Carbonate rocks are often oil-wet or mixed-wet, requiring specialized chemical treatments for wettibility modification. ([[2022-xiao|Xiao, 2022, p. 95]])

- Carbonate rocks showed low adsorption values (0.89-1.11 mg/g-rock) for the surfactant GS12. ([[2022-kalam|Kalam, 2022, p. 75]])

- Carbonate systems often exhibit complex pore structures that lead to significant diffusion coupling. ([[2023-al-boghail|AL-BOGHAIL, 2023, p. 25]])

- The study focused on non-water wet heterogeneous carbonate core samples. ([[2023-dafaalla|Dafaalla, 2023, p. 120]])

- Carbonates typically exhibit intermediate-wet to oil-wet properties. ([[2024-al-azani|Al-Azani, 2024, p. 47]])

- Carbonate reservoirs are known for higher chemical reactivity compared to clastic reservoirs, impacting their response to CO2 injection. ([[2024-yusmananto|Yusmananto, 2024, p. 36]])

- Carbonate reservoirs are characterized by high complexity in pore size and geometry due to diagenesis. ([[2024-alhamood|ALHAMOOD, 2024, p. 28]])
