- Petrographic data can be used to estimate porosity, permeability, and hydraulic conductivity. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 17]])

- Porosity, water saturation, and volume of silt were the primary petrophysical properties studied. ([[1997-al-marhoun|Al-Marhoun, 1997, p. 16]])

- Established relationships between acoustic, electrical, and lithological parameters. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 14]])

- Referenced in this thesis. ([[1996-al-salem|Al-Salem, 1996]])

- Well log analysis provides a standard method for determining porosity and permeability. ([[2009-sitouah|Sitouah, 2009, p. 21]])

- Petrophysical models include porosity, permeability, and water saturation calculations. ([[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010, p. 76]])

- Pore pressure is directly related to the appropriate drilling mud weight for reservoir safety. ([[2013-mark|Mark, 2013, p. 16]])

- Standard petrophysical models fail in thinly bedded formations because they cannot resolve individual sand/shale layers. ([[2013-abdullah|Abdullah, 2013, p. 16]])

- Petrophysical parameters including porosity, permeability, and water saturation were modeled using multi-attribute transforms. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 85]])

- TAVO provides a way to estimate S-wave/P-wave velocity and density differences more accurately than some reflection methods. ([[2014-kola|Kola, 2014, p. 15]])

- Porosity and permeability are influenced by mineral composition, texture, and diagenetic processes. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 106]])

- Porosity and permeability are influenced by grain size, sorting, cement, and matrix content. ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 19]])

- Petrophysical models like CRIM and the porosity-density relationship are essential for multi-modal data integration. ([[2014-saeed|Saeed, 2014, p. 34]])

- Incorporating the Q-factor into the PSPI algorithm significantly improved image clarity in complex zones. ([[2015-mohammed|Mohammed, 2015, p. 63]])

- Integration of petrophysics and 3D seismic data allowed for more accurate rock characterization. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 19]])

- Shales are highly anisotropic due to their intrinsic layered texture. ([[2015-own|Own, 2015, p. 95]])

- Petrophysical properties (porosity, permeability, and water saturation) were found to be correlated with specific lithofacies. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 13]])

- Fourteen sand reservoirs were identified within the Agbada formation. ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 65]])

- Porosity values for the central outcrop averaged 13.5% with permeability averaging 68 mD. ([[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016, p. 160]])

- Petrophysical properties like porosity and permeability are critical for unconventional plays. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 17]])

- Petrophysical properties are essential for calibrating seismic responses. ([[2016-ali|Ali, 2016]])

- Petrophysical properties were derived from conditioned logs to support rock physics modeling. ([[2016-houssain|Houssain, 2016, p. 17]])

- Established relationships between lithofacies and petrophysical values. ([[2016-makkawi|Makkawi, 2016, p. 62]])

- Used to determine porosity, permeability, and hydrocarbon saturation to distinguish between lithofacies. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 30]])

- Porosity and permeability were measured for all core plugs across different lithofacies. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 47]])

- The highest values of Young's Modulus (E) were observed in Juayl Formation (48.28 GPa). ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 100]])

- Petrophysical reservoir properties such as porosity and used for 3D modeling were calculated using wireline logs (GR, RHOB, NPHI). ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 36]])
