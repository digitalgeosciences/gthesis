## Key Findings Across Sources

- The succession represents a shallowing upward carbonate cycle moving from open marine shelf to shelf edge conditions. ([[1992-el-sahn|El-Sahn, 1992, p. 13]])

- Evidence of pluvial periods and sea-level fluctuations (80,000-6000 y.b.p.) was identified in the sediment record. ([[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988, p. 121]])

- Identified shoaling-upward cycles and high salinity conditions during deposition. ([[2006-al-enezi|Al-Enezi, 2006, p. 13]])

- Identified a dominant paleowind direction toward east-northeast with a mean vector of 72 degrees. ([[2011-al-masrahy|AL-MASRAHY, 2011, p. 10]])

- The presence of Cruziana trace fossils indicates a marine shelf environment. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 54]])

- Foraminiferal distribution was linked to seasonal variations and pollution levels. ([[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015, p. 90]])

- Depositional environments show a gradual change from deep parts of the platform eastwards towards shallower conditions westwards. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 146]])

- Determined a restricted carbonate platform with hypersaline lagoon and inner ramp features. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 16]])

- Determined diversity and distribution across various marine habitats from intertidal to offshore. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 140]])

- Identified four distinct lithofacies associations (FA1-FA4) representing nearshore, glaciolacustrine delta, subglacial tillite, and glaciofluvial environments. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 65]])

- Lithofacies were grouped into five major carbonate environments ranging from distal-to-proximal carbonate ramp settings. ([[2017-yousif|Yousif, 2017, p. 18]])

- Determined a transition from dysoxic offshore conditions to oxic lower shoreface environments. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 14]])
