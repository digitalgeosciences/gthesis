## Key Findings Across Sources

- The Sarah Formation is considered a potential tight gas sand reservoir in the Rub’ Al-Khali Basin. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 20]])

- Defined by Saudi Aramco as having <12% porosity and <1mD permeability. ([[2016-ejaz|Ejaz, 2016, p. 17]])

- Tight gas reservoirs are responsible for 57-59% of global unconventional resources. ([[2017-mohammed-3|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 20]])

- Tight gas reservoirs are characterized by permeability less than 0.1 mD and often require advanced drilling technologies like horizontal or multi-lateral wells. ([[2017-jafarov|Jafarov, 2017, p. 30]])

- Shale gas reservoirs are characterized by high volumes but significant technical challenges in extraction. ([[2018-aleid|Aleid, 2018, p. 19]])

- In these reservoirs, liquid banking causes extreme damage to hydrocarbon production. ([[2020-mohammed|Mohammed, 2020, p. 21]])

- The formation is considered a target unconventional tight gas sand reservoir in Saudi Arabia. ([[2020-islam|Islam, 2020, p. 19]])

- The study highlights that ignoring adsorption effects can lead to overestimating permeability by up to 16.5 times at low pressures. ([[2023-afagwu|AFAGWU, 2023, p. 151]])

- The use of ionic liquids improved regained permeability by 25% compared to the base case. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 48]])

- Breakdown pressure reduction of up to 82.58% was achieved in these reservoirs. ([[2024-suleiman-6|Suleiman, 2024, p. 17]])
