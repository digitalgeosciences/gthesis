# Signor Lipps Effect

**Summary**: A phenomenon where the fossil record makes a sudden extinction appear gradual due to incomplete sampling.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024]] applied calculations based on the Signor-Lipps effect to determine accurate extinction rates of DWAF.

## Scientific Context

- [[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024]] Used to correct the perceived magnitude of mass extinctions at the K/Pg boundary.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024]] calculated by varying sample sizes in multiples of 5.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The apparent extinction rate dropped from 84% to 53% when accounting for the Signor-Lipps effect by increasing sample size to 44 samples. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 117]])
