# Relative Permeability

**Summary**: The ratio of the effective permeability of a fluid to its absolute permeability.

---

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The relative permeability ratio (Kro/Krw) increased as the injected water salinity increased. ([[1990-al-mumen|Al-Mumen, 1990, p. 153]])


- Relative permeability to oil increases and relative permeability to water decreases with temperature increase. ([[1995-okasha|OKASHA, 1995, p. 37]])

- The reservoir dip angle had the most significant influence on pseudo relative permeability curves among all factors studied. ([[1992-al-otaibi|Al-Otaibi, 1992, p. 86]])

- Relative permeability curves are strongly influenced by wettibility, with oil permeability increasing as wettibility moves toward water-wet. ([[2013-al-mutairi|AL-MUTAIRI, 2013, p. 24]])

- A lack of reliable relative permeability data is a major concern for history matching smart water flooding simulations. ([[2016-adam|ADAM, 2016, p. 19]])

- Gas flow productivity is reduced as critical condensate saturation increases. ([[1999-fawumi|Fawumi, 1999, p. 18]])

- New models were found to outperform all previous empirical equations in both development and validation sets. ([[1994-al-fattah|Al-Fattah, 1994, p. 16]])

- Relative permeabilities for the composite were found to be higher or lower than the individual core plugs, meaning they did not represent an average of the individual plugs. ([[1995-hussain|Hussain, 1995, p. 67]])
