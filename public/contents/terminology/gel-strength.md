# Gel Strength

**Summary**: The ability of the drilling fluid to suspend solids when circulation is stopped.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2014-wajheeuddin|Wajheeuddin, 2014]] evaluated gel strength to ensure mud stability during pump shutdowns.

- [[2014-mohamed|MOHAMED, 2014]] measured gel-strength as an indicator of the barrier capability for water control.

- [[2017-alakbari|Alakbari, 2017]] measured gel strength to evaluate the thixotropic properties of the drilling mud.

- [[1990-ali|Ali, 1990]] measured the initial and 10-minute gel strengths as functions of temperature and aging.

## Scientific Context

- [[2014-wajheeuddin|Wajheeuddin, 2014]] Ensures that cuttings do not settle in the wellbore during pauses in circulation.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2014-wajheeuddin|Wajheeuddin, 2014]] measured at 10 seconds and 10 minutes.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Gel strengths were observed to increase with temperature for all tested formulations. ([[2014-wajheeuddin|Wajheeuddin, 2014, p. 113]])


- The elastic modulus (G') increased from 703 Pa to 1035 Pa when temperature was raised from 120°C to 150°C. ([[2014-mohamed|MOHAMED, 2014, p. 131]])

- At 200°F, the base drilling fluid had zero gel strength after 30 minutes. ([[2017-alakbari|Alakbari, 2017, p. 47]])

- Gel strength decreased with increasing temperature but increased with aging time, showing diminishing effects at higher ages. ([[1990-ali|Ali, 1990, p. 61]])
