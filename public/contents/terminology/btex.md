# BTEX

**Summary**: A group of volatile organic compounds (benzene, toluene, ethylbenzene, and xylene) commonly found in petroleum products.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2016-sameh|Sameh, 2016]] identified BTEX as the primary contaminants in diesel-based drilling fluids.

- [[2025-almousa|Almousa, 2025]] investigated the removal of BTEX from groundwater.

## Scientific Context

- [[2016-sameh|Sameh, 2016]] BTEX compounds are common pollutants in groundwater near oil fields.

- [[2025-almousa|Almousa, 2025]] investigated the removal of BTEX from groundwater.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2016-sameh|Sameh, 2016]] quantified via GC-MS

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Toluene was selected as the target pollutant because it has higher solubility in water compared to other BTEX compounds. ([[2016-sameh|Sameh, 2016, p. 20]])


- Ethylbenzene and Xylenes were often not detected or at very low levels even at higher concentrations. ([[2025-almousa|Almousa, 2025, p. 59]])
