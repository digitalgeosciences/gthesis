# Architectural Element

**Summary**: A component of a depositional system larger than a facies but smaller than or equal to a channel fill.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014]] identified 7 architectural elements (Channel, SB, LA, DA, FF, HO) in the Sarah Formation.

## Scientific Context

- [[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014]] Used to understand the geometry and relationship of rock bodies in the subsurface.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014]] Defined by distinctive facies assemblage, internal geometry, external form, and vertical profile.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Architectural elements include Channel (CH), Sandy bedforms (SB), Lateral-accretion (LA), Downstream-accretion (DA), Overbank fines (FF), and Scour hollows (HO). ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 83]])
