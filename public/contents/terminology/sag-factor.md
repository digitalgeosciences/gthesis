# Sag Factor

**Summary**: A measure of the settling behavior of solids in a drilling fluid.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2017-jafarov|Jafarov, 2017]] calculated sag factor to ensure that barite remained suspended in the fluid.

- [[2022-aljaberi|AlJaberi, 2022]] measured sag factor to determine if perlite could stabilize high-density muds.

## Scientific Context

- [[2017-jafarov|Jafarov, 2017]] Ensures that weighting agents do not settle at the bottom of the wellbore.

- [[2022-aljaberi|AlJaberi, 2022]] measured sag factor to determine if perlite could stabilize high-density muds.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2017-jafarov|Jafarov, 2017]] Measured using a static sag test unit.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The sag factor for the fluid must be between 0.5 and 0.53 for having a good performance. ([[2017-jafarov|Jafarov, 2017, p. 61]])


- The base hematite mud showed a high sagging tendency with a sag factor of 0.54 for vertical wells. ([[2022-aljaberi|AlJaberi, 2022, p. 100]])
