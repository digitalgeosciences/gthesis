# Adhesion Tension

**Summary**: A parameter derived from contact angle and interfacial tension to provide a more robust measure of surface wettibility.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2021-alqam|ALQAM, 2021]] calculated adhesion tension using the Thomas Young equation as an alternative approach for defining surface wettibility.

## Scientific Context

- [[2021-alqam|ALQAM, 2021]] Used to overcome limitations of using only one parameter (contact angle) for wettibility assessment.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2021-alqam|ALQAM, 2021]] Calculated from IFT and contact angle.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Adhesion tension values shifted from negative to positive as water wetness increased, with Type-V oil showing higher values due to high asphaltene content. ([[2021-alqam|ALQAM, 2021, p. 95]])
- [[2021-alqam|ALQAM, 2021]] Values ranged from -9.13 to +14.40 dynes/cm.
