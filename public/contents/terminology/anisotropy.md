# Anisotropy

**Summary**: The property of a geological variable having different properties (like range or sill) in different directions.

---

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Horizon 1 showed geometric anisotropy, while Horizon 3 showed slight zonal anisotropy. ([[1996-jarad|Jarad, 1996, p. 155]])
- [[1996-jarad|Jarad, 1996]] Anisotropy ratio of 4 for Horizon 1.

- No significant directionality (anisotropy) was found in the horizontal semivariograms for well-log porosity. ([[1996-al-salem|Al-Salem, 1996, p. 103]])

- The study confirmed that the pressure diffusion technique can distinguish between different directional permeabilities (kx, ky, kz). ([[2009-mansur|Mansur, 2009, p. 40]])

- Resistivity anisotropy was highest in the thin-bedded pay zone, reaching values of 2-3. ([[2013-abdullah|Abdullah, 2013, p. 93]])

- Not stated in source ([[2013-khan-2|Khan, 2013]])

- Qusaiba Shale shows strong transversely isotropic nature, especially for S-wave anisotropy (38%). ([[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015, p. 123]])

- Transverse Isotropy (TI) models are essential for correcting errors caused by layered or fractured rock structures. ([[2015-own|Own, 2015, p. 20]])

- Anisotropy was found to be very weak in both reservoirs, with an ellipticity ratio of less than 1 in most locations. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 12]])

- Brittleness is a tensorial quantity when anisotropy is considered, varying with orientation angles. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 61]])

- The study confirmed that the sand dune behaves as a weakly anisotropic TTI medium (epsilon=0.035, delta=0.005). ([[2024-makki|Makki, 2024, p. 13]])

- The model accounts for anisotropy by using directional permeability values (kx, ky) and corresponding dimensions. ([[2006-adebowale|ADEBOWALE, 2006, p. 23]])

- For horizontal wells, the critical rate is an increasing function of the anisotropy ratio only when 0.5 < a < 1.0. ([[1994-hakim|Hakim, 1994, p. 150]])

- When k_y/k_x exceeds 0.75, single fractures outperform multiple fractures at large times. ([[1996-al-hajji|Al-Hajji, 1996, p. 58]])

- Permeability anisotropy influences the shape of the damage zone and its impact on production. ([[1999-hasan|Hasan, 1999, p. 50]])
