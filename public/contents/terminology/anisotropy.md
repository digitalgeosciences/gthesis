# Anisotropy

**Summary**: The property of a variable having different properties (like range or sill) in different directions.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[1996-jarad|Jarad, 1996]] identified geometric and zonal anisotropies using semivariogram analysis.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Horizon 1 showed strong geometric anisotropy with an anisotropy ratio of 4. ([[1996-jarad|Jarad, 1996, p. 113]])

- Marker surface semivariograms showed marked anisotropy along the northeast/southwest direction (N 50° E). ([[1996-al-salem|Al-Salem, 1996, p. 120]])

- Analyzed Transversely Isotropic (TI) properties for the Qusaiba Shale. ([[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015, p. 97]])

- Anisotropic models (VTI, HTI, TTI) significantly improved perforation shot location accuracy compared to isotropic models. ([[2015-own|Own, 2015, p. 95]])

- Anisotropy was found to be very weak in both reservoirs, with an ellipticity ratio of less than 1. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 12]])
