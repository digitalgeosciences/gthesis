# Micritization

**Summary**: The transformation of carbonate grains into fine-grained calcite by endolithic organisms.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2016-salih|Salih, 2016]] evaluated micritization to understand its role in preserving grain shape and porosity.

- [[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017]] identified microbial micritization as a key early diagenetic process in the TMF.

## Scientific Context

- [[2016-salih|Salih, 2016]] Micritization can preserve primary porosity in high-energy environments like beach and upper shoreface.

- [[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017]] identified microbial micritization as a key early diagenetic process in the TMF.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2016-salih|Salih, 2016]] identified by the presence of micrite envelopes on grain margins.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Micrite envelopes preserved the original shape of dissolved bivalve fragments and protected them from compaction. ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 86]])

- Microbial micritization is observed throughout the entire TMF succession in all microfacies and occurs in both grain-dominated and mud-dominated facies. ([[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017, p. 142]])
