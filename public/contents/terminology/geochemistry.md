# Geochemistry

**Summary**: The study of chemical composition and distribution in rocks.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[1996-matter|Matter, 1996]] used geochemistry to determine the concentration of uranium and other elements.

- [[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013]] applied geochemistry to analyze the outcrop succession and identify reservoir facies.

- [[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016]] used geochemistry to determine major elements, trace elements, and TOC in the Sharawra Formation.

- [[2023-al-eid|Al-Eid, 2023]] performed a geochemical investigation to assess the impact of preservation on pyrolysis results.

- [[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023]] used geochemistry to evaluate reservoir temperature and fluid chemistry.

- [[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024]] applied geochemistry to identify mercury (Hg) enrichment as a proxy for Deccan Trap volcanism.

- [[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024]] used geochemistry to differentiate between A-type and I-type granitoids.

- [[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025]] applied geochemistry to characterize the mineralogical and organic changes in carbonate mudrocks.

- [[1999-babalola|Babalola, 1999]] used geochemistry to differentiate between Red and Grey units.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Uranium content was found to be up to 300 ppm in some samples. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 26]])

- Geochemical results show a strong correlation between major/trace elements and the reservoir facies. ([[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013, p. 18]])

- Geochemical data provided insights into paleoclimate, weathering intensity, and provenance. ([[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016, p. 166]])

- Poor preservation leads to significant drops in S1, S2, and TOC values. ([[2023-al-eid|Al-Eid, 2023, p. 17]])

- Determined a reservoir temperature of 136°C for the Ain Al Harrah spring. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 28]])

- Identified Hg/TOC spikes in the Bottaccione section corresponding to Phase-2 of Deccan volcanic eruptions. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 24]])

- A-types were metaluminous and enriched in LREE, while I-types were metaluminous-peraluminous. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 17]])

- Strontium (87Sr/86Sr) results were found to be low compared with the global range. ([[2024-malik|Malik, 2024, p. 24]])

- Identified specific geochemical shifts resulting from CO2-carbonate interactions. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 75]])

- Concentrations of metals like Mg, Ca, Fe increased over 100 times after CO2 injection. ([[2025-alghawi|Alghawi, 2025, p. 37]])

- Trace element ratios (Cr/Ni, V/Cr) were used as provenance indicators. ([[1999-babalola|Babalola, 1999, p. 119]])

- Determined the chemical signatures of halite, gypsum, and dolomite within the sabkha system. ([[1994-al-guwaizani|Al-Guwaizani, 1994]])
