# Hole Cleaning

**Summary**: The process of removing drilled solids from the wellbore to prevent stuck pipe and ensure smooth drilling.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2017-al-rubaii|AL-RUBAII, 2017]] studied the impact of hole cleaning on ROP.

- [[2019-elzenary|Elzenary, 2019]] evaluated hole cleaning conditions using a new Hole Cleaning Index (HCI).

- [[2021-al-malki|Al-Malki, 2021]] analyzed hole cleaning efficiency in both vertical and deviated wells.

- [[2023-murif|Murif, 2023]] evaluated hole-cleaning efficiency using various indices like HCR and CCI.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Inadequate hole cleaning is responsible for a large portion of all stuck pipe problems (approximately 33%). ([[2017-al-rubaii|AL-RUBAII, 2017, p. 21]])


- Hole cleaning is most difficult at an inclination of approximately 60 degrees. ([[2019-elzenary|Elzenary, 2019, p. 32]])

- Inefficient hole cleaning leads to high nonproductive time (NPT) due to stuck pipe and lost circulation. ([[2021-al-malki|Al-Malki, 2021, p. 13]])

- A hole cleaning ratio (HCR) greater than 1.1 is generally sufficient to avoid stuck pipe incidents. ([[2023-murif|Murif, 2023, p. 73]])
