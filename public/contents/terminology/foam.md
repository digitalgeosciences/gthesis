# Foam

**Summary**: A collection of gas bubbles stabilized by surfactants or other agents.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2013-geri|Geri, 2013]] evaluated the stability and volume of bulk foam to determine optimal surfactant concentrations.

- [[2016-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2016]] studied foam stability and texture as an EOR method to overcome issues like viscous fingering and gravity override.

- [[2017-adam|Adam, 2017]] evaluated foam as a mobility control agent to reduce the mobility of injected CO2.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2013-geri|Geri, 2013]] measured via initial volume (Vi) and half-time for dewatering (t1/2).

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Tergitol NP-9 and Triton X-100 showed stable foam for over 1 hour at 1.0 wt%. ([[2013-geri|Geri, 2013, p. 71]])
- [[2013-geri|Geri, 2013]] Tergitol NP-9 at 1.0 wt% produced 750 ml of foam with a t1/2 of 235 sec.

- Foam is essential in EOR projects where the main aim is to recover trapped oil from the reservoirs. ([[2016-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2016, p. 19]])

- Foam significantly reduces the mobility of the gas phase, which is essential for improving the volumetric sweep efficiency during gas flooding. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 18]])

- Foam quality of 80% resulted in faster recovery than 70% in some scenarios. ([[2021-al-shuqayyiqi|Al-Shuqayyiqi, 2021, p. 16]])

- The HPHT foam analyzer showed that Duomeen TTM had higher foamability at high pressure due to its ability to reduce liquid drainage. ([[2024-al-drwish|AL-DRWISH, 2024, p. 101]])
