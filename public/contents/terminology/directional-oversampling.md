# Directional Oversampling

**Summary**: A technique to improve the accuracy of local downscaling by extending the area over which the solution is calculated.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2022-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2022]] applied directional_oversampling in EDMM and ETMM to reduce the impact of boundary conditions.

## Scientific Context

- [[2022-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2022]] crucial for ensuring consistency between different mesh levels.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2022-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2022]] compared results of standard vs. extended methods.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- EDMM and ETMM, using DO, were significantly more accurate than DMM and TMM because they reduced local boundary artifacts. ([[2022-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2022, p. 84]])
