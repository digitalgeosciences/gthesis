# Total Petroleum Hydrocarbons

**Summary**: The total weight of all petroleum hydrocarbons in a sample.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2011-salamah|Salamah, 2011]] measured total-petroleum-hydrocarbons (TPH) to evaluate the effectiveness of biodegradation.

- [[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019]] measured TPH to assess hydrocarbon contamination and its removal by Avicennia marina.

- [[2024-almutawa|AlMutawa, 2024]] measured TPHs to assess the extent of soil contamination from industrial activities.

## Scientific Context

- [[2011-salamah|Salamah, 2011]] TPH is the standard metric for assessing the extent of hydrocarbon contamination and the success of remediation.

- [[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019]] measured TPH to assess hydrocarbon contamination and its removal by Avicennia marina.

- [[2024-almutawa|AlMutawa, 2024]] measured TPHs to assess the extent of soil contamination from industrial activities.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2011-salamah|Salamah, 2011]] Gravimetric analysis after extraction with n-hexane.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Initial TPH levels were approximately 0.19g per 10g of soil, and removal was tracked over 20 days. ([[2011-salamah|Salamah, 2011, p. 42]])


- The average soil TPH removal percentage for diesel contaminated soils with Avicennia marina were 76%, 80% and 91%. ([[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019, p. 103]])

- Soil TPH concentrations at industrial sites were reported to range from 5,000 mg/kg on average. ([[2024-almutawa|AlMutawa, 2024, p. 18]])
