**Summary**: The measure of the tortuous path for current flow.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2009-al-gathe-2|AL-GATHE, 2009]] Determined via the 'a' parameter in Archie's equation.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- A value other than one is sometimes appropriate for 'a' to compensate for variation in cementation, pore structure, and grain size. ([[2009-al-gathe-2|AL-GATHE, 2009, p. 21]])
- [[2009-al-gathe-2|AL-GATHE, 2009]] Typically between 0.6 and 1.0

- Sandstone tortuosity values were found to be lower than carbonate values, ranging from 1.94 to 2.06 for sandstones versus 2.64 to 3.07 for carbonates. ([[2019-elsayed|Elsayed, 2019, p. 95]])

- The inclusion of tortuosity and sorbed gas interaction significantly impacts flow in micropores. ([[2023-afagwu|AFAGWU, 2023, p. 26]])

- Increased tortuosity helped distribute stress more evenly across the rock, mitigating potential weak points. ([[2024-almukainah|Almukainah, 2024, p. 70]])
