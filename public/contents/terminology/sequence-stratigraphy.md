## Key Findings Across Sources

- Defined two composite sequences (LFC1 and LFC2) each containing four high-frequency sequences. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 13]])

- Used to identify six high-frequency sequences and multiple fining-upward small-scale cycles. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 12]])

- A total of 44 cycles were identified, with cycle thicknesses ranging from 15.2 cm to 255 cm. ([[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013, p. 18]])

- The stratigraphic horizons were accurately picked from the digital model along with the bed thicknesses. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 180]])

- Identified aggradational-progradational parasequence stacking patterns in specific platform types. ([[2015-koeshidayatullah|Koeshidayatullah, 2015, p. 19]])

- Identified three sequences based on LST, FSST, TST, and HST components. ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 18]])

- Defined four sequences based on cyclicity and stacking patterns; CS2 was subdivided into HFS1 and HFS2. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 14]])

- Two sequence boundaries characterized by subaerial exposure were delineated, defining LST, TST, and HST. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 15]])

- Defined three levels: second order super sequence, second order, and third order. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 17]])

- A sequence stratigraphic framework was established for the D5 member based on lithofacies distribution. ([[2016-malik|Malik, 2016, p. 109]])

- The Dam Formation is interpreted as a 3rd order sequence with three major 4th order sequences (DLS 1, DLS 2, DLS 3). ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 123]])

- Identified TST, HST, and LST within three to five composite sequences depending on location. ([[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017, p. 102]])

- Identified several HFS units within the Upper Khartam Member. ([[2017-adam|Adam, 2017, p. 8]])

- Analysis revealed approximately 53 cycles and cycle sets of 5th to 6th order magnitude. ([[2017-yousif|Yousif, 2017, p. 19]])

- Used to define third-order sequences and parasequence sets. ([[2017-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2017, p. 90]])
