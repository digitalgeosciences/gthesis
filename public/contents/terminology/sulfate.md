# Sulfate

**Summary**: Sulfate ions often resulting from gypsum dissolution or fertilizer use.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011]] analyzed sulfate levels to identify potential contamination and lithological influence.

## Scientific Context

- [[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011]] High sulfate levels can indicate gypsum dissolution or fertilizer impact.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Sulfate concentrations in the deep groundwater samples range between 395 ppm and 1494 ppm, with an average of 760 ppm. ([[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011, p. 120]])
