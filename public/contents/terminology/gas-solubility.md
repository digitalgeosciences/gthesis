# Gas Solubility

**Summary**: The amount of gas that can be dissolved in a liquid under specific temperature and pressure conditions.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[1996-mehmood|Mehmood, 1996]] evaluated gas solubility to determine the volume of gas in solution within reservoir fluids.

- [[2019-suleiman-2|Suleiman, 2019]] modeled the gas solubility using SAFT-VR Mie coupled with M-DH.

## Scientific Context

- [[1996-mehmood|Mehmood, 1996]] Important for determining the amount of water and gas in reservoir systems.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[1996-mehmood|Mehmood, 1996]] Empirical correlation based on temperature, pressure, and salinity.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Gas solubility in brine decreases as total solid content increases; for example, it was found to be significantly lower than in pure water at high salinities. ([[1996-mehmood|Mehmood, 1996, p. 33]])


- For the gas solubility, nineteen systems were analyzed... and the highest AADP was less than 5%. ([[2019-suleiman-2|Suleiman, 2019, p. 18]])
