# Hazard Quotient

**Summary**: A ratio used to determine the potential for human health risk from non-carcinogenic pollutants.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025]] calculated hazard quotients (HQ) and a hazard index (HI) to assess risks of systemic toxicity.

## Scientific Context

- [[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025]] Used in human health risk assessment (HHRA) to determine if exposure levels are safe.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025]] Calculated as Average Daily Dose divided by Reference Dose.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The hazard quotient is considered safe if it is less than 1.0; values above 1.0 indicate potential risk. ([[2025-al-afif|Al-Afif, 2025, p. 26]])
