# Recovery Factor

**Summary**: The efficiency with which one fluid displaces another in a porous medium.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[1995-okasha|OKASHA, 1995]] evaluated the displacement efficiency of hot water and solvent on tar.

- [[2012-baarimah|BAARIMAH, 2012]] used recovery factors (free gas, gross gas, and oil) to compare well performance.

- [[2012-kamal|Kamal, 2012]] calculated recovery factor (R.F.) as a primary metric for comparing well performance.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The combination of solvent and hot water was found to be an effective method for improving mobility in tarmat reservoirs. ([[1995-okasha|OKASHA, 1995, p. 10]])


- Horizontal wells showed a 10% higher free-gas recovery factor than vertical wells in low-permeability cases with thin pay zones. ([[2012-baarimah|BAARIMAH, 2012, p. 70]])

- HFVW achieved higher R.F. values than HW and VW in low permeability reservoirs. ([[2012-kamal|Kamal, 2012, p. 95]])
