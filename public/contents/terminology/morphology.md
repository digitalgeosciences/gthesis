**Summary**: The study of the form and structure of organisms.

- [[2016-lawal-2|Lawal, 2016]] performed morphology analysis to determine the physical characteristics of PM10.

- [[2017-amao|Amao, 2017]] analyzed morphological abnormalities to distinguish between environmental stress and epibiont interaction.

- [[2022-al-alawi|Al-Alawi, 2022]] analyzed the morphology of dunes to determine wind regimes.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Morphological description of Ammonia tepida included a test with a biconvex shape and 3-4 whorls. ([[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015, p. 62]])


- Morphology showed that location affects the processes the particles underwent, resulting in different shapes and sizes. ([[2016-lawal-2|Lawal, 2016, p. 78]])

- Morphological test abnormalities caused by an epibiont in an unpolluted environment have implications for pollution biomonitoring. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 47]])

- Dune types (Barchans, Parabolic) are directly related to wind direction and vegetation presence. ([[2022-al-alawi|Al-Alawi, 2022, p. 16]])

- The three sections showed distinct shifts in morphotype proportions, indicating changes from well-oxygenated environments to shallower settings with higher oxygen levels. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 62]])

- Deformities were most prevalent in adult stages, particularly in LBF like Peneroplis. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 70]])

- M1 (suspension feeding) showed an immediate decline after the K/Pg event, while M2 and M3 increased. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 168]])
