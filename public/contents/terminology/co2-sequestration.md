# CO2 Sequestration

**Summary**: The process of capturing and transporting waste carbon dioxide and and injecting it into suitable underground geological storage sites.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Identified cut-off values for favorable CO2 sequestration locations based on lithology, permeability, and trap geometry. ([[2020-abdallah|Abdallah, 2020, p. 96]])

- Saline aquifers are the most desirable option for sequestration due to size, distribution, and safety. ([[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023, p. 21]])

- Different trapping mechanisms (structural, residual, and dissolution) vary significantly based on depositional facies. ([[2024-yusmananto|Yusmananto, 2024, p. 16]])

- Submarine channels offer promising features such as geological stability and well-defined traps for containment. ([[2024-maigana|Maigana, 2024, p. 17]])

- The saline nature of the aquifer makes it a viable candidate for industrial use such as CO2 sequestration. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 17]])

- Carbonate mudrocks were studied as a medium for CO2 interaction, relevant to sequestration studies. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 5]])

- Maximum uptake reached 5.63 g/l under optimal conditions (1000 psi, pH 3, Mg-rich basalt). ([[2025-alghawi|Alghawi, 2025, p. 14]])
