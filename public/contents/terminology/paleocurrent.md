# Paleocurrent

**Summary**: The direction of past currents in a sedimentary system.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-ali|Ali, 2015]] determined paleocurrent directions using sedimentological indicators like cross-beds and oolitic grainstone.

- [[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016]] applied paleocurrent-analysis to determine the primary direction of sediment transport in the Al Lidam area.

- [[2023-butt|Butt, 2023]] applied paleocurrent-analysis to 636 measurements to determine paleoflow direction in the Midyan Basin.

## Scientific Context

- [[2015-ali|Ali, 2015]] Paleocurrent analysis helped reconstruct the paleogeography and shoreline variability.

- [[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016]] applied paleocurrent-analysis to determine the primary direction of sediment transport in the Al Lidam area.

- [[2023-butt|Butt, 2023]] applied paleocurrent-analysis to 636 measurements to determine paleoflow direction in the Midyan Basin.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2015-ali|Ali, 2015]] Measured from oriented sedimentary structures like cross-beds.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Paleocurrent data indicates a general seaward direction to the southeast and landward direction to the northwest. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 155]])


- Analysis showed a general eastward trend with a major SE direction, consistent with the basin's E-W orientation. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 121]])

- Paleocurrent data from Sukk subbasin showed a mean azimuth of 155° towards the southeast. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 89]])
