## Key Findings Across Sources

- Porosity values are critical for determining flow units; high-permeability zones have >14% intergranular porosity. ([[2002-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2002, p. 18]])

- Not stated in source ([[1996-jarad|Jarad, 1996]])

- Not stated in source ([[1993-kaka|Kaka, 1993]])

- Porosity is essential for development drilling, reserve estimation, and fluid flow simulation. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 25]])

- Porosity was used as a primary input for calculating hydraulic conductivity and transmissivity. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 53]])

- Porosity was estimated using multi-variant regression from nine different seismic attributes. ([[1997-al-marhoun|Al-Marhoun, 1997, p. 16]])

- Storage coefficients were calculated for several aquifers to determine their ability to supply water. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 66]])

- The Khobar aquifer generally has a porosity of more than 3%. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 30]])

- Statistical analysis of the physical properties of SFNC Sequence such as porosity and facies based porosity was done. ([[1997-al-qassab|Al-Qassab, 1997, p. 16]])

- Porosity can be enhanced or reduced by diagenetic processes, including compaction, cementation, and dissolution. ([[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001, p. 20]])

- Well-log derived porosity values from 44 vertical wells were used to construct geostatistical models. ([[1996-al-salem|Al-Salem, 1996, p. 19]])

- Secondary porosity from dissolution is an important diagenetic event for the formation. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 23]])

- Primary porosity is determined by texture; secondary porosity in carbonates results from dissolution, recrystallization, and dolomitization. ([[2003-al-shridi|Al-Shridi, 2003, p. 27]])

- Not stated in source ([[2009-al-lehyani|Al-Lehyani, 2009]])

- Porosity is a key factor for reservoir modeling and was predicted using both MLP and GRNN models. ([[2009-sitouah|Sitouah, 2009, p. 15]])

- Total porosity measured by density or neutron logs includes micro, small, large-connected, and isolated vugs. ([[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010, p. 106]])

- Not stated in source ([[2011-salamah|Salamah, 2011]])

- Not stated in source ([[2011-al-masrahy|AL-MASRAHY, 2011]])

- Muddy stromatolites were found to have low porosity compared to ooid grainstones. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 51]])

- Not stated in source ([[2012-amao|Amao, 2012]])

- Porosity and permeability in basement reservoirs are controlled by the fracture network. ([[2012-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2012, p. 11]])

- Not stated in source ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013]])

- Porosity values ranged from 0% to 40% across the sampled lithofacies. ([[2013-yousif|Yousif, 2013, p. 34]])

- Standard methods underestimate porosity in thin-bedded formations due to the averaging effect of low-resolution tools. ([[2013-abdullah|Abdullah, 2013, p. 16]])

- Porosity distribution maps were generated for the Aradeiba-D sand zone. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 41]])

- Porosity values were assigned based on correlation between outcrop and subsurface data from three fields. ([[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013, p. 40]])

- Not stated in source ([[2013-mohammed|Mohammed, 2013]])

- Not stated in source ([[2014-kola|Kola, 2014]])

- Porosity values for the proximal part are between 15% and 25%, with an average of 20%. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 126]])

- Porosity was preserved by authigenic kaolinite coatings preventing quartz overgrowth. ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 16]])

- Porosity types included moldic, vuggy, and intercrystalline, with different distributions based on pore size. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 74]])

- The joint inversion method accurately retrieved porosity values within 2.5% error. ([[2014-saeed|Saeed, 2014, p. 17]])
