# Seawater_Intrusion

**Summary**: The movement of saline water into freshwater aquifers from nearby bodies of water.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992]] investigated seawater_intrusion in the northern sections of both aquifers.

## Scientific Context

- [[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992]] Seawater intrusion significantly alters the chemical signature and mineral saturation status of coastal aquifers.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992]] Analyzed via chloride/sulfate ratios and comparison with seawater composition.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Seawater intrusion is the main process controlling hydrogeochemistry north of Jubayl, evidenced by high chloride and sulfate concentrations. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 97]])
