# Sequence Boundary

**Summary**: A surface that marks a significant change in the depositional environment or sea level.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010]] identified two types of sequence boundaries (Type 1 and Type 2) based on sea-level fall magnitude.

- [[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015]] identified sequence-boundary surfaces to define stratigraphic units.

## Scientific Context

- [[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010]] Used to identify major and minor changes in the depositional environment.

- [[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015]] identified sequence-boundary surfaces to define stratigraphic units.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010]] identified via core photos, petrography, and gamma-ray logs.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Type 1 are unconformities with karst features; Type 2 are conformable hardgrounds. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 85]])


- Four sequence boundaries (SB1 to SB4) were identified as erosional unconformities. ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 17]])
