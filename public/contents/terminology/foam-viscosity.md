# Foam Viscosity

**Summary**: The resistance of a foam to flow, influenced by bubble size, density, and surfactant chemistry.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2024-al-drwish|AL-DRWISH, 2024]] evaluated foam viscosity as a critical factor for controlling fluid loss and improving acid diversion.

## Scientific Context

- [[2024-al-drwish|AL-DRWISH, 2024]] Critical for determining the effectiveness of acid fracturing fluids in carbonate reservoirs.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2024-al-drwish|AL-DRWISH, 2024]] Measured using an HPHT foam rheometer.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Synergistic surfactants (Duomeen TTM & Armovis VES) produced the highest viscosity at 150°C. ([[2024-al-drwish|AL-DRWISH, 2024, p. 187]])
