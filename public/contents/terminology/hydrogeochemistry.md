# Hydrogeochemistry

**Summary**: The study of the chemical composition of groundwater and its interaction with geological materials.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011]] used hydrogeochemistry to identify sources of contamination like agricultural runoff and industrial waste.

- [[2012-kassim|KASSIM, 2012]] analyzed hydrogeochemistry to understand how specific ions in seawater affect carbonate rock surfaces.

- [[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023]] applied hydrogeochemistry to assess reservoir temperature, fluid properties, and hydrological processes.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- High chloride levels (average 2206 ppm) were identified as indicators of sewage system pollution. ([[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011, p. 115]])


- Specific ions (Ca2+, Mg2+, SO4 2-) act as potential determining ions for surface charge alteration. ([[2012-kassim|KASSIM, 2012, p. 30]])

- Thermal water is affected by a combination of several factors, including water-rock interactions, mixing, and dilution. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 16]])

- Seawater Mg concentration averaged 2126 ppm, while foraminifera showed varying concentrations based on location. ([[2024-alsadah|Alsadah, 2024, p. 36]])

- Both aquifers follow Chebotarev's sequence, indicating typical mineral dissolution/precipitation processes. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 19]])
