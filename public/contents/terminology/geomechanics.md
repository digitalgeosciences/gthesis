# Geomechanics

**Summary**: The study of the mechanical properties and behavior of rocks and soils.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Determined Young's modulus, Poisson's ratio, and UCS for the Qusaiba Shale. ([[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015, p. 63]])

- Rock mechanics are fundamental to understanding hydraulic fracturing success. ([[2015-own|Own, 2015, p. 72]])

- Three distinct geomechanical units were established based on variations in strength index, porosity, and wave velocity. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 14]])

- Identified five geomechanical units with varying strength levels linked to specific lithofacies. ([[2016-babker|Babker, 2016, p. 13]])

- Average values of Young's modulus and Poisson's ratio indicate high stiffness and brittle nature of the Sarah Formation. ([[2016-ejaz|Ejaz, 2016, p. 14]])

- Lithology is the main control on brittleness in the Khuff Formation. ([[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016, p. 19]])

- Lithological units were classified based on point load index and uniaxial compressive strength. ([[2017-mahgoub|Mahgoub, 2017, p. 126]])

- Correlation was established between grain size and geomechanical properties like UCS. ([[2017-benaafi|Benaafi, 2017, p. 14]])
