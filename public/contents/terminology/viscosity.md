# Viscosity

**Summary**: A measure of a fluid's resistance to flow.

---

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Viscosity was identified as the most important oil-related parameter controlling demulsification in electrostatic fields. ([[1995-al-jaziri|Al-Jaziri, 1995, p. 24]])


- Brine viscosity is primarily correlated with temperature, while pressure effects are often negligible in practical calculations. ([[1996-mehmood|Mehmood, 1996, p. 28]])

- The gel point is defined as the point of maximum rate of increase of viscosity. ([[1994-muhammad|Muhammad, 1994, p. 42]])

- The gel viscosity increased with time and was influenced by temperature and shear rate. ([[1997-al-nashmi|Al-Nashmi, 1997, p. 16]])

- Tar viscosity was significantly reduced by the addition of solvents (reformate and naphtha). ([[1995-okasha|OKASHA, 1995, p. 40]])

- Effective and plastic viscosities were found to decrease with increasing temperature and aging duration. ([[1994-shokoya|Shokoya, 1994, p. 86]])

- The canola-based OBM showed comparable rheological behavior to synthetic systems across various tests. ([[2011-sanmi|Sanmi, 2011, p. 11]])

- Apparent viscosity and plastic viscosity increased by 20% and 18% respectively along the section. ([[2012-geri|Geri, 2012, p. 126]])

- Viscosity is defined as the ratio of shear stress to shear rate, measured in Pascal-seconds (Pa·s). ([[2012-omer|Omer, 2012, p. 41]])

- Found that 3% NC increased the yield point to 16.4 lbf/100ft2. ([[2013-murtaza|Murtaza, 2013, p. 101]])

- The addition of nanoparticles resulted in a manifold increase in the shear viscosity at a strain of 5%. ([[2014-alao|Alao, 2014, p. 34]])

- Determined rheological properties including yield stress and viscosity under various conditions. ([[2014-elhag|ELHAG, 2014]])

- The shear thinning nature of VES allows it to penetrate low permeability regions while maintaining high viscosity at lower flow rates. ([[2014-azad|Azad, 2014, p. 46]])

- All three materials with varying particle sizes and concentrations exhibited improved rheology. ([[2014-wajheeuddin|Wajheeuddin, 2014, p. 18]])

- The lowest value of viscosity was observed with 2.71 g/L of HEDTA. ([[2015-he|He, 2015, p. 117]])

- Not stated in source ([[2014-hashmat|Hashmat, 2014]])

- The zero-shear viscosity reduced by three to five orders of magnitude at 60°C with increasing concentrations of oils. ([[2015-fogang|FOGANG, 2015, p. 15]])

- Oil viscosity reduced significantly (up to 98.5%) when saturated with CO2 at high pressures. ([[2014-alam|ALAM, 2014, p. 50]])

- Apparent viscosity decreased with increasing temperature in both diesel and waste oil systems. ([[2016-sidaoui|Sidaoui, 2016, p. 41]])

- The new formulation... can be controlled with the concentrations of the constituents of the fluid (GLDA or polymer). ([[2017-mohammed-3|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 17]])

- Increasing the concentration of nanosilica was decreasing the plastic viscosity of the drilling fluid. ([[2017-alakbari|Alakbari, 2017, p. 82]])

- Water viscosity is modeled using correlation 1.595T^-1.182 (where T is in F). ([[2016-david|DAVID, 2016, p. 52]])

- Viscoelastic surfactants (VES) provide better mobility control than standard surfactants due to their non-Newtonian behavior. ([[2017-janjua|Janjua, 2017, p. 35]])

- The copolymer had only a minor effect on the plastic viscosity. ([[2018-basfar|BASFAR, 2018, p. 50]])

- Dead oil viscosity was 2.69 cP at 100 °C. ([[2019-al-azani|AL-AZANI, 2019, p. 63]])

- Crude oil viscosity decreased from 19.8 cP to 8.2 cP when temperature increased from 25°C to 100% (sic, text says 52°C). ([[2019-alghamdi|AlGhamdi, 2019, p. 77]])

- Modified xanthan showed significantly higher viscosity (2,200 mPa.S) than standard xanthan (578 mPa.S) at 1,500 ppm concentration. ([[2019-mohammad|Mohammad, 2019, p. 13]])

- The hydrocarbon viscosity can be reduced 17 times using thermochemical treatment. ([[2020-mohammed|Mohammed, 2020, p. 206]])

- A 10 wt. % GLDA concentration resulted in an apparent viscosity of 93 cP, a 27% increase over the 68 cP observed without chelating agents. ([[2021-alkhowaildi|Alkhowaildi, 2021, p. 50]])

- A 20 C change in temperature can decrease viscosity from 30,000 cp to 1,000 cp (Athabasca oil). ([[2022-alnasser|Alnasser, 2022, p. 20]])

- Plastic viscosity increased from 26.21 to 38.98 cP as barite dosage rose from 250g to 450g. ([[2022-aljaberi|AlJaberi, 2022, p. 46]])

- Adding 25 wt.% ilmenite increased the YP/PV ratio from 0.94 to 1.34, improving stability. ([[2023-basfar|Basfar, 2023, p. 86]])

- Daqing field had the highest recovery due to its high polymer-to-oil viscosity ratio (30). ([[2023-alameer|Alameer, 2023, p. 72]])

- The rheology of pure VES at 1.25% showed zero shear viscosity of 1953 cP. ([[2023-ahmed|Ahmed, 2023, p. 23]])

- Viscosity was updated at each time step to reflect the impact of hot water injection. ([[2023-suleiman-2|Suleiman, 2023, p. 15]])

- Adding CNDs reduced the viscosity of the commercial formulation. ([[2023-al-dogail|AL-DOGAIL, 2023, p. 18]])

- Viscous polymers improve mobility control, but their primary impact on recovery is often linked to elasticity at high flow rates. ([[2023-dafaalla|Dafaalla, 2023, p. 31]])

- The addition of 10 wt.% BL reduced Crude 3 viscosity from 29.2 cP to 16 cP at 50°C (44.9% reduction). ([[2023-al-hakami|Al-Hakami, 2023, p. 100]])

- Reduction in viscosity is a primary indicator of successful polymer breakdown. ([[2024-abdallah|Abdallah, 2024, p. 39]])
