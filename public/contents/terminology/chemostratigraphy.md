# Chemostratigraphy

**Summary**: The study of chemical variations in sedimentary sequences to identify lithological changes or environmental shifts.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015]] applied chemostratigraphy to characterize and sub-divide geochemically distinct units.

- [[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017]] applied chemostratigraphic tools for reservoir zonation in the Dam Formation.

## Scientific Context

- [[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015]] Used to detect subtle differences in depositional environments and mineralogy.

- [[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017]] applied chemostratigraphic tools for reservoir zonation in the Dam Formation.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015]] Analysis of major elements (CaO, MgO, SrO) and trace elements (U, Th).

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Chemostratigraphy identified two distinct geochemical zones separated at the mid-point of the section, reflecting different mineralogical contents. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 101]])

- Chemostratigraphic tools were found successful for reservoir zonation. ([[2017-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2017, p. 148]])
