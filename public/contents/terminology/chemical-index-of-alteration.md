# Chemical Index Of Alteration

**Summary**: A geochemical index used to quantify the extent of chemical weathering.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[1999-babalola|Babalola, 1999]] calculated the chemical-index-of-alteration (CIA) to assess the degree of weathering at the source.

## Scientific Context

- [[1999-babalola|Babalola, 1999]] High CIA values indicate significant weathering of minerals like feldspar and mica.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[1999-babalola|Babalola, 1999]] Calculation based on major element concentrations.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- CIA values ranged from 51 to 98, indicating extensive chemical weathering. ([[1999-babalola|Babalola, 1999, p. 167]])
