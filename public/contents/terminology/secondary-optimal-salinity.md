# Secondary Optimal Salinity

**Summary**: A second region of low interfacial tension occurring at higher salt concentrations due to a thin interfacial layer.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[1987-fuseni|Fuseni, 1987]] identified the secondary optimal salinity (SOS) as a key finding for high-salinity reservoir design.

## Scientific Context

- [[1987-fuseni|Fuseni, 1987]] SOS is critical for designing surfactants for high-salinity, high-temperature (HSHT) reservoirs.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[1987-fuseni|Fuseni, 1987]] Identified via salinity scans of IFT measurements.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The SOS was found to be approximately 20% NaCl for B1083 in crude oil systems. ([[1987-fuseni|Fuseni, 1987, p. 79]])
- [[1987-fuseni|Fuseni, 1987]] Found at 14% in dodecane and 20% in crude oil.
