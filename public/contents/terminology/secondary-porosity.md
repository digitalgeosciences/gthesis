# Secondary Porosity

**Summary**: Pore space created after the initial deposition of sediment.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001]] evaluated secondary-porosity to determine its contribution to reservoir quality.

## Scientific Context

- [[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001]] Secondary porosity is critical for maintaining reservoir quality in deeply buried sandstones.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001]] quantified via thin-section analysis and identification of features like corroded grains.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Secondary porosity in many samples was primarily generated by the dissolution of feldspar and carbonate minerals (especially calcite). ([[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001, p. 120]])
