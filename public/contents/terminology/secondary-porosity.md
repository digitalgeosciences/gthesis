# Secondary Porosity

**Summary**: Porosity created after initial deposition through processes like dissolution or fracturing.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001]] evaluated the impact of secondary porosity on reservoir quality.

- [[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002]] evaluated secondary porosity in the Unayzah sandstone as a major component of total porosity.

## Scientific Context

- [[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001]] Secondary porosity is critical for maintaining permeability in deeply buried reservoirs.

- [[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002]] evaluated secondary porosity in the Unayzah sandstone as a major component of total porosity.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001]] Identification of features such as honeycomb grains, oversized pores, and elongated pores.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Secondary porosity was primarily generated by the dissolution of feldspar and calcite cement. ([[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001, p. 100]])

- Secondary porosity is the main constituent of the total porosity in the Unayzah sandstone. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 76]])
