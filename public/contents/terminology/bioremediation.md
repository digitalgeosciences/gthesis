# Bioremediation

**Summary**: The use of microorganisms to detoxify or remove pollutants from the environment.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2011-salamah|Salamah, 2011]] investigated bioremediation as a non-invasive and cost-effective method for removing petroleum hydrocarbons.

- [[2012-attyah|Attyah, 2012]] evaluated bioremediation as an interim treatment or disposal step for reducing hydrocarbon contamination.

- [[2014-abdallah|Abdallah, 2014]] used bioremediation to treat groundwater contaminated with EDC and oil.

- [[2015-oyehan|Oyehan, 2015]] evaluated bioremediation as an eco-friendly approach for hydrocarbon contamination.

- [[2015-malik|Malik, 2015]] studied bioremediation as an effective and environmentally benign technique for cleaning hydrocarbon-contaminated sites.

- [[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016]] investigated bioremediation potential through nutrient removal by microalgae.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Bioremediation was identified as an effective method for treating soil contaminated with crude oil. ([[2011-salamah|Salamah, 2011, p. 15]])

- Bioremediation is used as an interim treatment or disposal step, which reduces the overall level of hydrocarbon contamination prior to final dispose. ([[2012-attyah|Attyah, 2012, p. 45]])

- Bioremediation is a well-developed way of cleaning up different contaminants, including petroleum hydrocarbons and chlorinated organic compounds. ([[2014-abdallah|Abdallah, 2014, p. 38]])

- Bacterial communities in the Arabian Gulf are rich in oil-degrading strains that can be explored for bioremediation. ([[2015-oyehan|Oyehan, 2015, p. 18]])

- Identified several bacterial strains as potential candidates for the remediation of environments contaminated with PAHs. ([[2015-malik|Malik, 2015, p. 15]])

- All three strains showed high efficiency in removing nitrate and phosphate, regardless of initial concentration. ([[2016-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2016, p. 63]])

- Biological treatment was identified as an environmentally friendly and cost-effective alternative to physical/chemical methods. ([[2017-budiyanto|Budiyanto, 2017, p. 17]])

- Removal efficiencies for PAHs were 79-92% over a 90-day period. ([[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019, p. 102]])
