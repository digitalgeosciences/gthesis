# Chelating Agents

**Summary**: Molecules that can form multiple bonds with a metal ion to create a stable complex.

---

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Citric acid showed the highest formation constant (11.7) for aluminum. ([[2012-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2012, p. 38]])
- [[2012-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2012]] Citric: 11.7, Formic: 3.1, Acetate: 1.51

- Chelating agents like EDTA can effectively form wormholes in limestone at non-acidic pH (4-13). ([[2013-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2013, p. 33]])

- EDTA at pH 12.2 was found to be the most effective agent for chelation and oil recovery. ([[2013-elsayed|Elsayed, 2013, p. 14]])

- DTPA and HEDTA were found to be more efficient than HCl when injected at low rates. ([[2015-barri|Barri, 2015, p. 37]])

- GLDA was found to be the most effective chelant for stimulating both Berea and Bandera cores at low pH. ([[2015-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2015, p. 59]])

- GLDA was found to be more stable at lower pH values than EDTA. ([[2015-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2015, p. 64]])

- The optimum concentration of chelating agents was found to be 20 wt. % at pH 11. ([[2016-hamza|HAMZA, 2016, p. 19]])

- DTPA and EDTA were found to be more effective than other tested agents. ([[2016-geri|GERI, 2016, p. 67]])

- DTPA is identified as the strongest chelating agent among those tested. ([[2017-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2017, p. 41]])

- The addition of a chelating agent prevented premature crosslinking and enabled further hydration of the polymer. ([[2021-alkhowaildi|Alkhowaildi, 2021, p. 33]])

- GLDA was found to be more effective than EDTA and DTPA in terms of fracture conductivity improvement. ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023, p. 43]])

- GLDA was identified as a highly effective chelating agent for maintaining stability in high-temperature, high-salinity environments. ([[2023-othman|Othman, 2023, p. 31]])
