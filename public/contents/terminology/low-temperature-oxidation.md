# Low Temperature Oxidation

**Summary**: The oxidation of crude oil at relatively low temperatures, producing a coke-like residue.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[1999-iddris|Iddris, 1999]] investigated LTO as a method for sand consolidation and its risk of causing spontaneous ignition.

## Scientific Context

- [[1999-iddris|Iddris, 1999]] Used for sand consolidation; if it leads to ignition, it is a liability.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[1999-iddris|Iddris, 1999]] Measured via TGA and DTA analysis of oil samples.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- LTO begins at 170 °C and peaks at 270 °C in the studied oil. ([[1999-iddris|Iddris, 1999, p. 15]])
