# Gradual Deformation Method

**Summary**: A parameterization technique that reduces the dimensionality of an inverse problem while preserving the spatial variability and geological structure of a model.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-adeniji|Adeniji, 2015]] used gradual deformation method (GDM) to reduce the dimensionality of the inverse problem from 60,000 to 60.

## Scientific Context

- [[2015-adeniji|Adeniji, 2015]] Addresses the problem of high-dimensional inverse problems in reservoir modeling.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2015-adeniji|Adeniji, 2015]] Linear combination of prior model realizations.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- GDM ensures that the spatial variability (mean, variance and histogram) of the prior and posterior models are the same. ([[2015-adeniji|Adeniji, 2015, p. 31]])
