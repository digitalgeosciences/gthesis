# Asphaltenes

**Summary**: Heavy, complex molecules in crude oil that contribute to viscosity and oxidation behavior.

---

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The low asphaltene content of Oil #1 resulted in lower heat generation during LTO. ([[1999-iddris|Iddris, 1999, p. 15]])
- [[1999-iddris|Iddris, 1999]] Oil #2 had 4.1% weight of asphaltenes.

- Higher concentration of asphaltene material is a primary cause for the promotion of emulsion formation in Well-B. ([[1995-al-jaziri|Al-Jaziri, 1995, p. 85]])

- Only a certain, equal amount of asphaltenes were adsorbed on rock surfaces initially, regardless of oil type. ([[2014-shakeel|SHAKEEL, 2014, p. 55]])

- Asphaltenes are stabilized by resins and form micelles with dual affinity to oil and water, making them effective emulsifying agents. ([[2021-altowilib|Altowilib, 2021, p. 26]])

- Asphaltenes with larger poly-aromatic rings tended to precipitate easier and altered the wettability at high pressure. ([[2021-alqam|ALQAM, 2021, p. 37]])

- Asphaltenes are defined as a solubility class insoluble in alkanes but soluble in aromatic fluids, with an average H/C ratio of 1.2. ([[2023-suleiman|Suleiman, 2023, p. 48]])
