# Breakdown Pressure

**Summary**: The pressure at which a fracture is created in a rock formation.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2017-muqtadir|Muqtadir, 2017]] investigated the breakdown pressure of tight sandstone rocks under different fluid conditions.

- [[2024-suleiman-6|Suleiman, 2024]] measured breakdown pressure to evaluate the effectiveness of thermochemical treatments.

## Scientific Context

- [[2017-muqtadir|Muqtadir, 2017]] Critical for determining well safety limits and designing hydraulic fracturing treatments.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The study highlights that breakdown pressure can be influenced by both the type of fracturing fluid and the saturation level of the reservoir. ([[2017-muqtadir|Muqtadir, 2017, p. 57]])


- Breakdown pressure was reduced by 79.28% and 82.58% in shale and organic-rich carbonate samples respectively under cyclic treatment. ([[2024-suleiman-6|Suleiman, 2024, p. 17]])
