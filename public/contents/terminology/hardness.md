# Hardness

**Summary**: The resistance of a material to localized plastic deformation.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2022-samarkin|Samarkin, 2022]] measured the hardness of carbonate rocks as a primary indicator of durability for hydraulic fracturing.

- [[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023]] measured rock hardness using an auto-scan system.

## Scientific Context

- [[2022-samarkin|Samarkin, 2022]] Harder surfaces are less prone to proppant embedment and maintain fracture conductivity.

- [[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023]] measured rock hardness using an auto-scan system.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2022-samarkin|Samarkin, 2022]] Measured via Brinell, scratch test, and impulse hammering techniques.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Hardness was improved by 3.8% (NaF) and 21% (ZnSO4) in limestone/chalk samples. ([[2022-samarkin|Samarkin, 2022, p. 13]])
- [[2022-samarkin|Samarkin, 2022]] Improved by up to 30% with DAP treatment.

- Hardness reduction was highest in low salinity cases due to higher acidity. ([[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023, p. 91]])
