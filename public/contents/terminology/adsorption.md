# Adsorption

**Summary**: The process by which molecules or ions from a gas or liquid are attached to the surface of a solid.

---

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Adsorption capacity increased with agitation rate and higher pH levels. ([[2009-al-khaldi|Al-Khaldi, 2009, p. 11]])


- Confirmed that carbonate rock is negatively charged in Arabian Gulf seawater, with values varying by ion concentration and temperature. ([[2012-kassim|KASSIM, 2012, p. 19]])

- Chelating agents shifted the zeta potential of Berea sandstone toward more negative values, improving its water-wet state. ([[2013-elsayed|Elsayed, 2013, p. 84]])

- Silver oxide impregnated-FA showed 24% removal, while other materials were <10%. ([[2014-adebayo|Adebayo, 2014, p. 12]])

- The system measured average effective diameters of 2,104 nm (calcite), 677 nm (dolomite), and 994 nm (MEC). ([[2014-kasha|Kasha, 2014, p. 68]])

- Mg-MOF-74 showed higher adsorption capacity compared to zeolite and activated carbon. ([[2014-jamil|JAMIL, 2014, p. 19]])

- Adsorption is one of the key toxins-removing method receiving attention today with great potential. ([[2015-musa|Musa, 2015, p. 32]])

- Zeta potential values were found to be negative for both oil-wet and intermediate-wet particles. ([[2015-he|He, 2015, p. 35]])

- AC treated with HNO3 yielded carbons with higher surface area and better adsorptive properties. ([[2015-danmaliki|Danmaliki, 2015, p. 19]])

- EDTA increased the negative magnitude of zeta-potential, indicating successful chelation of cations from the mineral surfaces. ([[2015-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2015, p. 89]])

- More negative zeta potential values were observed after DTPA-K5 conditioning, indicating a shift toward more water-wet states. ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 58]])

- The Barnett Shale data used had a Langmuir pressure of 650 psi and a Langmuir volume of 96 scf/ton. ([[2015-rammay|RAMMAY, 2015, p. 50]])

- Using the chelating agent solutions prepared using synthetic Arabian Gulf seawater change the limestone surface charge to more negative zeta potential values leading to alteration of limestone rock wettability to a more water-wet condition. ([[2015-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2015, p. 15]])

- Adsorption efficiency increased with time and agitation speed, peaking at pH 7. ([[2016-salawu|Salawu, 2016, p. 21]])

- Nanoparticles in 1.2 wt% NaCl showed a zeta potential of -13.50 mV, approaching the zone of rapid coagulation. ([[2016-alhamad|AlHamad, 2016, p. 60]])

- CO2 adsorption on carbonate was significantly higher than CH4 at all temperatures due to high affinity for carbonate rocks. ([[2016-elnaeem|Elnaeem, 2016, p. 39]])

- The isoelectric point for nanosilica was found at pH=1.47, with a stable range typically being above 30 mV or below -30 mV. ([[2017-alakbari|Alakbari, 2017, p. 29]])

- A 98.6% removal efficiency was achieved using polyelectrolyte-coated fly ash at pH 9. ([[2017-olabemiwo|Olabemiwo, 2017, p. 14]])

- The magnitude of negative zeta potential is higher in carbonate outcrop compared to calcite due to the presence of silica content. ([[2017-sadeed|SADEED, 2017, p. 18]])

- Zeta potential measurements for both VES and VES/CA systems are evaluated for supporting the results of adsorption. ([[2017-janjua|Janjua, 2017, p. 18]])

- The adsorption behavior of SDAC fitted better into pseudo-second-order reactions. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 12]])

- Adsorption processes in both cases were best described by the pseudo-second order kinetic model. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 15]])

- Not stated in source ([[2019-suleiman-2|Suleiman, 2019]])

- The adsorption process on PG followed a two-step mechanism: fast adsorption (first 20 min) and slow adsorption (after 20 min). ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 53]])

- Fe-modified biochar achieved sorption capacity in less time due to greater porosity. ([[2021-siddiq|Siddiq, 2021, p. 48]])

- Kerogen showed significantly higher adsorption capacity for methane, nitrogen, and carbon dioxide compared to asphaltene. ([[2021-aslanov|Aslanov, 2021, p. 58]])

- Zeta potential values between -5 mV and 5 mV indicate high possibility of instability and particle precipitation. ([[2021-gasimli|Gasimli, 2021, p. 128]])

- The addition of alkali can modify the zeta potential of the rock surface. ([[2022-xiao|Xiao, 2022, p. 98]])

- Adsorption was found significantly higher in sandstone than carbonates due to the high density of negative charges. ([[2022-kalam|Kalam, 2022, p. 56]])

- Sorbed phase flux contributes significantly to production when organic matter has high sorption capacity. ([[2023-afagwu|AFAGWU, 2023, p. 27]])

- Emulsifier-B was found to be positively charged, while Emulsifier-C was negatively charged. ([[2024-abdallah|Abdallah, 2024, p. 61]])

- Zeta potential is independent of the technique used, but results can vary if inappropriate models are used for conversion. ([[2024-isah|Isah, 2024, p. 46]])

- Adsorption capacity increased with higher CAC dosage, reaching 735 μg/g at optimal conditions. ([[2025-almousa|Almousa, 2025, p. 49]])

- The isoelectric point was found to be 4.2 in deionized water. ([[1992-rodriguez-2|Rodriguez, 1992, p. 50]])

- Adsorption increased significantly with temperature and was influenced by pH, salinity, and the presence of crude oil. ([[1987-ahmed|Ahmed, 1987, p. 22]])

- Alkali reduced surfactant adsorption by over 85% for initial concentrations below 0.4%. ([[1994-eme|Eme, 1994, p. 110]])
