# Adsorption

**Summary**: The physical or chemical bonding of molecules (solutes) to a surface.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- MWCNTs are good adsorbents for removing Hg(II) and As(III) due to high surface area. ([[2009-al-khaldi|Al-Khaldi, 2009, p. 11]])

- By-products were below detectable levels when adsorption was the primary mechanism rather than photocatalysis. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 54]])

- Silver oxide impregnated-FA showed significant adsorption (24% in English summary, 42% in Arabic) compared to other materials. ([[2014-adebayo|Adebayo, 2014, p. 12]])

- The experimental data fitted Langmuir, Freundlich, and Temkin isotherms to describe the adsorption process. ([[2015-musa|Musa, 2015, p. 13]])

- Adsorption was evaluated using various models including Langmuir and Freundlich isotherms. ([[2015-danmaliki|Danmaliki, 2015, p. 117]])

- Adsorption efficiency was found to be dependent on pH, contact time, and adsorbent dosage. ([[2016-salawu|Salawu, 2016, p. 21]])

- Adsorption was found to be highly dependent on pH, with optimal removal at pH 9. ([[2017-olabemiwo|Olabemiwo, 2017, p. 14]])

- Adsorbent 2Z-700-90 achieved a removal efficiency of 70% under optimized conditions. ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 12]])

- Removal efficiency was influenced by pH, agitation speed, temperature, dosage, concentration, and time. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 42]])
