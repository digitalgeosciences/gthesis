# Thixotropy

**Summary**: The property of a fluid to become thicker or more viscous over time when left at rest.

---

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Higher concentrations of Nano silica led to higher thixotropy, meaning the slurry developed high gel faster during static periods. ([[2012-amer|Amer, 2012, p. 90]])


- The upward and downward speed schedule is to assess the thixotropic behavior of drilling fluid. ([[2014-elhag|ELHAG, 2014, p. 60]])

- The LWS slurry exhibited intrinsic thixotropic behavior without the addition of specific thixotropic agents, likely due to GGBFS content. ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 80]])

- The viscosity of the EAS decreased with time under constant shear rates, indicating thixotropic behavior. ([[2023-al-dogail|AL-DOGAIL, 2023, p. 148]])

- Claytone-IMG 400 enhanced the thixotropic behavior, allowing it to thin under shear stress and recover its initial viscosity once the stress was removed. ([[2024-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2024, p. 78]])
