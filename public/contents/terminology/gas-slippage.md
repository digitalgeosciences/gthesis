# Gas Slippage

**Summary**: The phenomenon where gas molecules slide along the pore walls, occurring when pore sizes are comparable to the mean free path of the gas.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2000-hamid|Hamid, 2000]] accounted for gas slippage in the mathematical model and data analysis.

## Scientific Context

- [[2000-hamid|Hamid, 2000]] Significant in low-permeability rocks where pore sizes are small.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2000-hamid|Hamid, 2000]] Modeled using the Klinkenberg equation.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- A mathematical model incorporating slippage effects was developed to solve the problem of transient flow through whole cores. ([[2000-hamid|Hamid, 2000, p. 16]])
