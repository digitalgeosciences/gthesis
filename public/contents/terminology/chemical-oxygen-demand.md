# Chemical Oxygen Demand

**Summary**: A measure of the amount of oxygen required by microorganisms to decompose organic matter in water.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-he|He, 2015]] measured COD to assess the purity of separated water.

- [[2023-al-aqeel|Al-Aqeel, 2023]] measured chemical-oxygen-demand (COD) as the primary indicator of treatment success.

## Scientific Context

- [[2015-he|He, 2015]] Used to ensure the treated water meets environmental discharge standards.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2015-he|He, 2015]] Measured via titration after heating samples at 150°C.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Used as a metric to evaluate the effectiveness of PAM in improving the quality of the treated water. ([[2015-he|He, 2015, p. 54]])
- [[2015-he|He, 2015]] initial values around 63,258 ppm reduced significantly by demulsifiers.

- The target for safe discharge was a COD level below 50 mg/L. ([[2023-al-aqeel|Al-Aqeel, 2023, p. 13]])
