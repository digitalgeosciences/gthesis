# Ground Motion Prediction

**Summary**: Empirical equations used to predict the ground motion of an earthquake based on its magnitude, distance, and site conditions.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-latif|Latif, 2015]] developed GMPEs for subduction-zone earthquakes in Java Island.

- [[2025-rachmadan|Rachmadan, 2025]] developed GMPMs for three tectonic categories in West Java.

## Scientific Context

- [[2015-latif|Latif, 2015]] Essential for probabilistic seismic hazard analysis to estimate risk in densely populated areas.

- [[2025-rachmadan|Rachmadan, 2025]] developed GMPMs for three tectonic categories in West Java.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2015-latif|Latif, 2015]] Regression of strong-motion records against magnitude, distance, and site class.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The developed GMPEs are intended to be more accurate than existing models for moderate events (M ≤ 6.0). ([[2015-latif|Latif, 2015, p. 21]])

- A highly accurate GMPM is essential for predicting the ground motion values generated by an Earthquake. ([[2025-rachmadan|Rachmadan, 2025, p. 19]])
