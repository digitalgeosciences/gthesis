**Summary**: A sedimentary rock containing significant amounts of phosphate minerals, primarily apatite.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The main constituent of the phosphorite deposit is carbonate-apatite (francolite or dahillite). ([[1996-jarad|Jarad, 1996, p. 47]])
- [[1996-jarad|Jarad, 1996]] Grades ranging from 9.72% to 20.03% across three horizons.

- The ore is mainly composed of microcrystalline apatite (46.35%), dolomite (36.69%), and calcite (13.14%). ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 26]])

- The primary phosphate minerals are carbonate-fluorapatite (collophane) and wavellite. ([[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987, p. 39]])
