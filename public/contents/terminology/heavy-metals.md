# Heavy Metals

**Summary**: Metallic elements that can be toxic to humans at high concentrations.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The average concentration of manganese (Mn) in shallow ground water was 0.37 ppm, significantly higher than the MCL of 0.05 ppm. ([[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011, p. 90]])

- The study recommends assessing effects of heavy metals associated with drilling wastes. ([[2012-attyah|Attyah, 2012, p. 134]])

- Cd, Cr, Cu, and Zn were found to exceed allowable limits in industrial and residential areas. ([[2014-adedapo|Adedapo, 2014, p. 12]])

- Heavy metals include Ni, Co, Cd, and Cr, which are non-biodegradable and pose significant health risks. ([[2015-musa|Musa, 2015, p. 15]])

- Concentrations of Cd, Pb, and Ni were tested at 5ppm. ([[2015-bello|Bello, 2015, p. 19]])

- All concentration levels of V, Cr, Co, Ni, Cu, Zn, Mo, Ba, and Pb were within permissible limits. ([[2015-jawad|Jawad, 2015, p. 14]])

- Several metals (Ba, Zn, Cr, V) were found at high or exceeding limits, while others (As, Cd, Pb) remained within safe ranges. ([[2016-lawal-2|Lawal, 2016, p. 104]])

- Mercury (II) was identified as a critical contaminant requiring specific detection and removal techniques. ([[2016-salawu|Salawu, 2016, p. 24]])

- Maximum concentration of Cu was 102.5 mg/kg in bark tissue. ([[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019, p. 15]])
