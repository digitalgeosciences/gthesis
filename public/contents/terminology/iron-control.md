# Iron Control

**Summary**: The process of preventing iron precipitation in acid stimulation fluids.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-barri|Barri, 2015]] investigated chelating agents as iron control agents to prevent damage from ferric precipitates.

## Scientific Context

- [[2015-barri|Barri, 2015]] Crucial for preventing the plugging of pores by ferric salts in carbonate reservoirs.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2015-barri|Barri, 2015]] Measured by monitoring the pH at which iron precipitates.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- HEDTA and DTPA successfully kept 3000 ppm of ferric ions in solution up to pH 4.35. ([[2015-barri|Barri, 2015, p. 73]])
