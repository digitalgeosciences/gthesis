## Key Findings Across Sources

- Identified four lithofacies associations: massive bioturbated sandstone, grayish massive sandstone, diamictites, and partially deformed, graded to massive sandstone. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 20]])


- Seismic facies maps were used to identify zones of high impedance, such as fully dolomitized limestones. ([[2002-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2002, p. 91]])

- The study identified 11 distinct facies, with 9 being reservoir-quality units. ([[2001-al-khalifa|Al-Khalifa, 2001, p. 36]])

- Eight distinct facies were used to build the 3D geological architecture using SIS. ([[1997-al-qassab|Al-Qassab, 1997, p. 17]])

- Lithofacies are a very important variable in porosity estimations; removing them from the training set resulted in averaged values. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 142]])

- Lithology varied from lime mudstone (Sulaiy) to packstone/grainstone (Yamama) and carbonate-ramp sediments. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 50]])

- Identified 10 distinct lithofacies across two mineralogical units (dolomitic and non-dolomitic). ([[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988, p. 44]])

- Basal lithofacies commenced in a hypersaline environment. ([[2006-al-enezi|Al-Enezi, 2006, p. 95]])

- Lithofacies are determined by a combination of petrophysical and petrographical properties. ([[2009-sitouah|Sitouah, 2009, p. 19]])

- Identified five primary facies: upper shoreface, lower shoreface, inner lagoon, outer lagoon, and transgressive lagoon. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 46]])

- Dune facies (46%) were identified as the most productive lithofacies. ([[2011-al-masrahy|AL-MASRAHY, 2011, p. 127]])

- Depositional facies is the dominant control of reservoir quality and behavior. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 87]])

- The facies model showed mudstone as the dominant component, indicating a fluvial red mudstone and sandstone system. ([[2013-yousif|Yousif, 2013, p. 60]])

- Not stated in source ([[2013-abdullah|Abdullah, 2013]])
