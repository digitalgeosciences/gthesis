# Young's Modulus

**Summary**: A measure of the stiffness of a material.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015]] determined Young's modulus to assess the brittleness and geomechanical behavior of Qusaiba Shale.

- [[2015-barri|Barri, 2015]] evaluated changes in Young's modulus as an indicator of rock mechanical integrity.

- [[2016-tariq|Tariq, 2016]] calculated static Young's modulus (Estatic) using both core data and ANN-derived empirical models.

- [[2019-suleiman-3|Suleiman, 2019]] used Young's modulus to assess the mechanical strength and durability of rock surfaces.

- [[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021]] calculated Young's modulus to assess changes in rock geomechanics due to mud exposure.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Lithofacies-III showed higher Young's Modulus (32 GPa) compared to Lithofacies-I (26 GPa). ([[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015, p. 125]])


- Young's Modulus for Austin Chalk decreased by over 55% while Indiana Limestone only decreased by about 5%. ([[2015-barri|Barri, 2015, p. 182]])

- The proposed ANN model for Estatic yielded an R2 of 0.96 and AAPE of 6.2, outperforming several industry standard correlations. ([[2016-tariq|Tariq, 2016, p. 107]])

- The average Young's modulus for Khuff sample 5.1 was 6 GPa before treatment and 5.7 GPa after. ([[2019-suleiman-3|Suleiman, 2019, p. 68]])

- Young's modulus increased from 7.72 GPa (saturated) to 9.52 GPa after five days of mud interaction. ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 55]])

- The higher Young's Modulus in Indiana limestone resulted in more stable wormhole structures under high stress. ([[2023-al-garni|Al-Garni, 2023, p. 53]])

- Young's modulus decreased significantly in cases where less acidic brine (higher salinity/temperature) required more time to create wormholes. ([[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023, p. 103]])

- The highest reduction in YM occurred at 4000 psi, indicating significant damage to rock strength. ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 86]])

- Cyclic thermochemical treatment reduced Young's modulus by 29.38% in Eagle Ford samples. ([[2024-suleiman-6|Suleiman, 2024, p. 89]])

- The relationship between grain size parameters and mechanical properties (Young's modulus and Poisson's ratio) were not significant. ([[2002-elhassan|ElHassan, 2002, p. 19]])
