## Key Findings Across Sources

- Taleh aquifer water in the southeast is poor quality due to high chloride and sulfate concentrations from anhydrite dissolution. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 180]])

- Deep groundwater is not suitable for drinking due to high concentrations of certain elements. ([[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011, p. 12]])

- Average TDS values were 4200 mg/L in 2010, with a range of 3900 to 4700 mg/L. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 129]])

- Wasia groundwater quality in the study area generally ranges from poor to unsuitable for drinking according to WQI. ([[2019-ahmed|Ahmed, 2019, p. 13]])

- 67% of samples showed high nitrate levels, requiring treatment before drinking. ([[2020-mansouri|Mansouri, 2020, p. 13]])

- Not stated in source ([[2025-almousa|Almousa, 2025]])

- Nitrate concentrations were found to be between 0 and 80 mg/L in all samples. ([[2026-fatima|Fatima, 2026, p. 117]])

- TDS levels exceed recommended limits for most uses, but are generally suitable for municipal, stock farming, and some industrial use. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 19]])
