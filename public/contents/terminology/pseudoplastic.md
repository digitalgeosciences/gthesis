# Pseudoplastic

**Summary**: A non-Newtonian fluid whose viscosity decreases as the shear rate increases.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[1990-ali|Ali, 1990]] confirmed that the drilling fluids followed a pseudoplastic model based on power law index values of n < 1.

## Scientific Context

- [[1990-ali|Ali, 1990]] Standard behavior for drilling fluids to ensure ease of flow during circulation while maintaining suspension when static.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[1990-ali|Ali, 1990]] Determined from the power law equation.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The calculated power-law indices (n) were between 0.525 and 0.536, confirming pseudoplastic behavior. ([[1990-ali|Ali, 1990, p. 88]])
