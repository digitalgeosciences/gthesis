# Pore Volume To Breakthrough

**Summary**: A measure of the amount of fluid required to achieve a connection between two points in a porous medium.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2020-aly|Aly, 2020]] used pore-volume-to-breakthrough (PVBT) as a primary metric for evaluating acid efficiency.

- [[2022-aboluhom|Aboluhom, 2022]] used PVBT as a key metric to evaluate the efficiency of different injection rates and mineralogies.

- [[2022-alkathim|Alkathim, 2022]] used PVBT as the primary metric for evaluating the efficiency of matrix acidizing treatments.

## Scientific Context

- [[2020-aly|Aly, 2020]] Lower PVBT values indicate more efficient acid usage for creating connected channels.

- [[2022-aboluhom|Aboluhom, 2022]] used PVBT as a key metric to evaluate the efficiency of different injection rates and mineralogies.

- [[2022-alkathim|Alkathim, 2022]] used PVBT as the primary metric for evaluating the efficiency of matrix acidizing treatments.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2020-aly|Aly, 2020]] Calculated from the volume of fluid injected until a pressure drop is observed.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- PVBT values for radial tests were much lower compared to those of the linear tests. ([[2020-aly|Aly, 2020, p. 56]])


- The model confirmed the existence of an optimum injection rate where PVBT is minimized. ([[2022-aboluhom|Aboluhom, 2022, p. 58]])

- Optimal PVBT is achieved at an intermediate injection rate where wormholes become dominant and long-thin, rather than just face or conical. ([[2022-alkathim|Alkathim, 2022, p. 24]])
