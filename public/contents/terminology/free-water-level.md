# Free Water Level

**Summary**: The depth at which the pressure of oil/gas equals the pressure of water.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010]] determined the Free Water Level (FWL) as the primary datum for saturation height calculations.

## Scientific Context

- [[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010]] It defines the base level for calculating buoyancy and is critical for determining the transition zone height.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010]] Determined from pressure gradients of water and oil measured by formation testers.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The FWL is the level where capillary pressure is zero and is essential for calculating buoyancy forces. ([[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010, p. 72]])
