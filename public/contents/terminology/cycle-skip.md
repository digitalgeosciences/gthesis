# Cycle Skip

**Summary**: A discontinuity in seismic data caused by incorrect static corrections or velocity errors.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2011-toma|Toma, 2011]] identified cycle_skip issues caused by multi-layer models due to horizontal ray paths.

- [[2018-mohamed|Mohamed, 2018]] addressed the cycle skipping problem by using a multi-scale approach and surface wave inversion.

## Scientific Context

- [[2011-toma|Toma, 2011]] Cycle skips can lead to false subsurface images and are often difficult to resolve with standard methods.

- [[2018-mohamed|Mohamed, 2018]] addressed the cycle skipping problem by using a multi-scale approach and surface wave inversion.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Cycle skips were clearly visible in cross-line sections and time slices at 1400 ms. ([[2011-toma|Toma, 2011, p. 63]])


- Cycle skipping increases with increasing frequency, making low-frequency content essential for stable inversion. ([[2018-mohamed|Mohamed, 2018, p. 34]])
