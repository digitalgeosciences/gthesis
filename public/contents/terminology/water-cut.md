# Water Cut

**Summary**: The ratio of water to the total volume of fluids produced by a well.

---

## Key Findings Across Sources

- At a water cut of 95%, the amount of water injected was 0.64 pv for UHW compared to 1.08 pv for UVW. ([[1993-kandil|Kandil, 1993, p. 112]])


- At higher production rates, the increase in water-cut was much sharper compared to lower rates, leading to reduced overall oil recovery. ([[1998-khan|Khan, 1998, p. 109]])

- The system was designed to determine the water cut accurately in remote unmanned locations. ([[1997-al-taweel|Al-Taweel, 1997, p. 15]])

- A good water-cut match was achieved from all the fractions of wavelets in both 16x16 and 32x32 systems. ([[2013-mehmood|Mehmood, 2013, p. 104]])

- Well-B in L-3 would not be able to sustain flow due to excessive water production (79% initial). ([[2014-al-julaih|Al-Julaih, 2014, p. 76]])

- Inaccurate water-cut measurement in high GOR wells leads to incorrect decisions regarding well performance and potential unnecessary workover costs. ([[2018-al-kadem|Al-Kadem, 2018, p. 40]])

- EDMM was significantly more accurate than DMM in predicting water cut across all three models. ([[2022-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2022, p. 46]])
