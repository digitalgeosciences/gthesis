# Solidification and Stabilization

**Summary**: A combined process to physically encapsulate and chemically stabilize waste materials.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2012-attyah|Attyah, 2012]] discussed solidification and stabilization as methods for treating drilling cuttings.

## Scientific Context

- [[2012-attyah|Attyah, 2012]] Used to manage the hazardous components of drilling fluids like heavy metals and salts.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2012-attyah|Attyah, 2012]] Evaluated based on waste characterization.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Solidification refers to techniques that encapsulate the waste in a monolithic solid of high structural integrity. Stabilization refers to those techniques that reduce the hazard potential of a waste by converting the contaminants into their least soluble, mobile, or toxic form. ([[2012-attyah|Attyah, 2012, p. 36]])
