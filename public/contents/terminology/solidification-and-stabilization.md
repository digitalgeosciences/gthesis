# Solidification And Stabilization

**Summary**: Techniques to encapsulate waste or reduce its hazard potential by converting contaminants into less mobile forms.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2012-attyah|Attyah, 2012]] evaluated solidification and stabilization as methods for preparing waste for disposal.

## Scientific Context

- [[2012-attyah|Attyah, 2012]] Used when waste is not amenable to direct chemical fixation or requires stabilization for road foundations.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2012-attyah|Attyah, 2012]] Laboratory tests to determine the proper blend of additives.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Solidification refers to techniques that encapsulate the waste in a monolithic solid of high structural integrity. Stabilization refers to those techniques that reduce the hazard potential of a waste by converting the contaminants into their least soluble, mobile, or toxic form. ([[2012-attyah|Attyah, 2012, p. 46]])
