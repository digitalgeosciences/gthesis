# Heavy Metals Pollution

**Summary**: Naturally occurring metallic elements that can be toxic at high concentrations.

---

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Lead was detected in sample 6, reaching 0.1 ppm, which is near the limit for drinking water. ([[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011, p. 107]])


- There is concern for trace heavy metals within barite, such as mercury and cadmium. ([[2012-attyah|Attyah, 2012, p. 28]])

- Identified specific areas where industrial activity led to elevated levels of Ba, Cr, and Zn. ([[2014-adedapo|Adedapo, 2014, p. 74]])

- Cadmium (Cd), lead (Pb) and nickel (Ni) were used as target inorganic pollutants. ([[2015-bello|Bello, 2015, p. 14]])

- The average concentrations of Cr, Cu, Ni, and Hg were higher in the mangrove sediments than the non-mangrove sediments. ([[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019, p. 64]])

- Concentrations of Co, Al, Cr, Pb, Ni, As, Cu, Cd, and Hg were within normal ranges compared to EPA standards. ([[2022-al-harbi|Al-Harbi, 2022, p. 54]])

- Several metals (Pb, Hg, As, Zn) exceeded the reference criteria of 2.0 in both sites. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 49]])

- Cadmium (Cd) was identified as a significant risk factor, particularly in Jeddah. ([[2024-alenazi|Alenazi, 2024, p. 19]])
