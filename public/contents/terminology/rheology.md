## Key Findings Across Sources

- Polyacrylamide solutions are non-Newtonian fluids exhibiting pseudoplastic behavior (shear thinning). ([[1994-muhammad|Muhammad, 1994, p. 34]])


- Gel viscosity increased with time and increased if the temperature was increased; it decreased if shear rate was increased. ([[1997-al-nashmi|Al-Nashmi, 1997, p. 16]])

- Effective viscosity decreased as temperature increased due to the aggregation of clay platelets. ([[1994-shokoya|Shokoya, 1994, p. 86]])

- Canola oil based OBM compared favorably with a synthetic OBM system in terms of rheology. ([[2011-sanmi|Sanmi, 2011, p. 11]])

- Nano-silica was shown to improve rheological properties including consistency and stability. ([[2012-amer|Amer, 2012, p. 45]])

- The Hybrid Rheometer DH3 with a Peltier Concentric cylinder arrangement was used to measure viscosity over a wide range of shear rates. ([[2012-omer|Omer, 2012, p. 40]])

- Rheology determines the performance of cement and helps in determining the pumpability of cement. ([[2013-murtaza|Murtaza, 2013, p. 57]])

- Addition of nanoparticles significantly increased the low shear rate viscosity and elastic modulus of the surfactant. ([[2014-alao|Alao, 2014, p. 34]])

- Rheological properties were enhanced and achieved the target of standard API specifications after treatment. ([[2014-elhag|ELHAG, 2014, p. 18]])

- Rheological characterization indicated that VES could combat complexities in carbonate reservoirs and outperform polymer as a mobility control agent. ([[2014-azad|Azad, 2014, p. 24]])

- All three materials with varying particle sizes and concentrations exhibited improved rheology. ([[2014-wajheeuddin|Wajheeuddin, 2014, p. 18]])

- Emulsification was found to slow down cross-linking rate and reduce elastic modulus by approximately 54%. ([[2014-mohamed|MOHAMED, 2014, p. 14]])

- The addition of Nano-clay resulted in significant improvement in mechanical and rheological properties. ([[2015-thalab|Thalab, 2015, p. 63]])

- Rheology determines the performance of cement and helps in determining pumpability. ([[2015-khan-2|Khan, 2015, p. 78]])
