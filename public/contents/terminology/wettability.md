# Wettability

**Summary**: The preference of a solid surface to be in contact with one fluid over another.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Wettability alteration toward water-wet is considered a primary mechanism for additional oil recovery in carbonate systems. ([[2013-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2013, p. 46]])


- Contact angles decreased from 101° to 83.9° in Run 1, indicating a shift toward intermediate-wet state. ([[2013-al-mutairi|AL-MUTAIRI, 2013, p. 100]])

- Flotation tests confirmed that modified powders of all rocks were successfully made oil-wet. ([[2014-kasha|Kasha, 2014, p. 59]])

- Carbonate rocks are more inclined to oil wetness than water wetness. ([[2015-adebayo|Adebayo, 2015, p. 48]])

- DTPA-K5 enhanced the wettibility toward more water wet, which reduced the contact angle and decreased the residual oil saturation (ROS). ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 50]])

- Using the chelating agent solutions prepared using synthetic Arabian Gulf seawater change the limestone surface charge to more negative zeta potential values leading to alteration of limestone rock wettability to a more water-wet condition. ([[2015-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2015, p. 15]])

- The neutral-wettibility system produced the largest movable oil recovery in high mobility ratio cases. ([[2016-kalam|Kalam, 2016, p. 81]])

- The addition of PVP-stabilized zeolite nanoparticles altered the rock's wettability toward more water-wet. ([[2016-alhamad|AlHamad, 2016, p. 15]])

- Contact angles were measured at 60°C and pressures from 1500 to 5500 psi for various oils. ([[2016-mahboob-2|Mahboob, 2016, p. 74]])

- Release of adsorbed oil from the rock surface alters the rock surface from strong oil-wet to less oil-wet, leading to incremental oil recovery. ([[2017-sadeed|SADEED, 2017, p. 19]])

- Al3+ concentration showed a significant role in rock wettability (R2=0.8981). ([[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017, p. 154]])

- Two weeks of thermal aging was sufficient to restore the original wettibility for calcite rock discs. ([[2017-ahmed-3|Ahmed, 2017, p. 110]])

- Salicylic acid successfully turned the calcite from water-wet to oil-wet, while NaI brine restored it to water-wet. ([[2020-opoku|Opoku, 2020, p. 121]])

- Sacrificial fluids were tested for their ability to provide long-term protection against wettibility changes caused by asphaltenes. ([[2023-suleiman|Suleiman, 2023, p. 33]])

- The best surfactant to be selected is the one which can alter the wettability besides decreasing the IFT. ([[2023-ahmed|Ahmed, 2023, p. 43]])

- The core's oil-wet nature and heterogeneity made it resistant to mobilization by water at varying rates. ([[2023-dafaalla|Dafaalla, 2023, p. 108]])

- Contact angle measurements showed a reduction from 45° to 33° when ionic liquid was added, indicating a shift toward more water-wet. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 39]])

- Acid treatment changed the carbonate matrix from oil-wet to neutral-wet. ([[2023-pratama|PRATAMA, 2023, p. 20]])

- Surfactant-mediated wettibility alteration was confirmed by contact angle and IFT measurements. ([[2024-al-azani|Al-Azani, 2024, p. 22]])

- Anhydrite-rich rocks (S-1 to S-3) were water-wet, while dolomite-rich samples (S-4 to S-6) were oil-wet. ([[2024-isah|Isah, 2024, p. 115]])

- Contact angles were found in the range of 20° to 41°, indicating strong water-wet conditions for all gas mixtures. ([[2024-muhammed|Muhammed, 2024, p. 125]])

- An increase in oil wetness resulted in an increase in water relative permeability and a decrease in oil relative permeability. ([[1994-al-fattah|Al-Fattah, 1994, p. 52]])
