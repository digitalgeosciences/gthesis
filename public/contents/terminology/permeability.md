# Permeability

**Summary**: The measure of a rock's ability to transmit fluids.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- High permeability (>1 Darcy) is restricted to specific lithofacies and dolomitized zones. ([[2002-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2002, p. 18]])

- Estimated permeability from point-count data was compared against measured values to validate the petrographic approach. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 55]])

- Not stated in source (specific values not provided for permeability in summary). ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000]])

- Hairy illite bridging between grains strongly reduce permeability. ([[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001, p. 121]])

- Permeability depends on the statistics of pore throat diameters and is related to effective porosity. ([[2009-sitouah|Sitouah, 2009, p. 19]])

- Petrophysical rock types are differentiated by their distinct relationships between permeability and porosity. ([[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010, p. 126]])

- Not stated in source ([[2011-al-masrahy|AL-MASRAHY, 2011]])

- Permeability distribution maps were generated for the Aradeiba-D sand zone. ([[2013-el-kheir|El-Kheir, 2013, p. 43]])

- Permeability shows high variability, with values ranging from 53 mD to 4948 mD in the distal part. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 127]])

- Permeability was preserved by kaolinite coatings and influenced by grain size, sorting, and cement content. ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 16]])

- Permeability in KM-1 reached 13 mD, while in KM-2 it did not exceed 1 mD. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 80]])

- Siltstone facies showed relatively good permeability, with LF 6 reaching approximately 45 mD (liquid). ([[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016, p. 157]])

- Porosity-permeability relationship exhibits poor correlation for these lithofacies. ([[2016-hassan-2|Hassan, 2016, p. 13]])

- Permeability values were analyzed for different lithofacies and used in 3D modeling. ([[2016-makkawi|Makkawi, 2016, p. 61]])

- Permeability was highly variable, with values up to 56 mD in Well-C due to fractures. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 143]])

- Permeability was found to be below 1 mD in most cases across both mud and grain-dominated facies. ([[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017, p. 21]])

- Permeability distribution is positively skewed with a mean value of 0.54 mD and standard deviation of 1.4. ([[2017-yousif|Yousif, 2017, p. 138]])

- Highest permeability was found in burrowed wackestone due to dedolomitization. ([[2018-ismanto|Ismanto, 2018, p. 17]])
