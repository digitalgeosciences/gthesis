# Sensitivity Coefficients

**Summary**: Values representing how much a specific output (e.g., pressure) changes with respect to a small change in an input parameter (e.g., permeability).

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-zhang|Zhang, 2015]] calculated sensitivity coefficients using the adjoint method to facilitate gradient-based optimization.

## Scientific Context

- [[2015-zhang|Zhang, 2015]] They help identify trust regions and ensure optimization stays within physical limits.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2015-zhang|Zhang, 2015]] calculated via adjoint method, finite difference, or forward sensitivity.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The efficiency of gradient-based Newton algorithms largely depends on the computation of sensitivity coefficients. ([[2015-zhang|Zhang, 2015, p. 29]])
