# Residual Trapping

**Summary**: The immobilization of a fluid (usually CO2) in pores due to capillary forces.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2024-suleiman-3|Suleiman, 2024]] evaluated residual trapping of CO2 in Berea sandstone samples under different conditions.

- [[2024-yusmananto|Yusmananto, 2024]] evaluated residual_trapping as a primary mechanism for immobilizing CO2 along its migration route.

## Scientific Context

- [[2024-suleiman-3|Suleiman, 2024]] One of the primary mechanisms for long-term storage of CO2 in geological formations.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Residual trapping is a function of the relationship between capillary pressure and brine saturation. ([[2024-suleiman-3|Suleiman, 2024, p. 35]])


- Heterogeneous reservoirs provide a larger volume of residual trapping compared to homogeneous ones. ([[2024-yusmananto|Yusmananto, 2024, p. 119]])
