# Mobility Ratio

**Summary**: The ratio of the mobility of the displacing fluid to the mobility of the displaced fluid.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2014-azad|Azad, 2014]] calculated mobility ratio to assess the risk of fingering and ensure good sweep efficiency.

- [[2016-mahboob|MAHBOOB, 2016]] evaluated the impact of the mobility ratio (M) on water flood performance and its role in determining flood patterns.

- [[2016-kalam|Kalam, 2016]] evaluated the impact of the mobility ratio (M) ranging from 0.1 to 4 on water flood performance.

- [[2017-janjua|Janjua, 2017]] analyzed mobility ratio to ensure a favorable displacement of oil.

- [[2023-dafaalla|Dafaalla, 2023]] used the mobility ratio to determine the stability of the displacement process.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- A mobility ratio of less than 1 is desired to provide better sweep efficiency in carbonate reservoirs. ([[2014-azad|Azad, 2014, p. 37]])


- Mobility ratio is the controlling factor of the water flooding performance in multi-layered composite linear reservoirs. ([[2016-mahboob|MAHBOOB, 2016, p. 43]])

- Mobility ratios greater than 1 result in less efficient displacement, requiring more water to recover the same amount of oil. ([[2016-kalam|Kalam, 2016, p. 28]])

- A favorable mobility ratio occurs when we have M ≤ 1; this means that the displacing phase has a less mobility than the displaced phase. ([[2017-janjua|Janjua, 2017, p. 31]])

- A favorable mobility ratio (below 1) indicates a stable displacement process and improved sweep efficiency. ([[2023-dafaalla|Dafaalla, 2023, p. 31]])

- For horizontal wells, the critical rate increases as Mp rises from 0.2 to 1.0 but decreases for Mp > 1. ([[1994-hakim|Hakim, 1994, p. 134]])
