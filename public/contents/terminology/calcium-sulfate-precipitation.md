# Calcium Sulfate Precipitation

**Summary**: The precipitation of calcium sulfate (CaSO4) from the aqueous phase onto the rock surface.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2013-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2013]] addressed calcium-sulfate-precipitation as a major cause of formation damage in carbonate reservoirs.

## Scientific Context

- [[2013-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2013]] A primary challenge in low-salinity waterflooding for carbonate rocks.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2013-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2013]] Monitored via pressure drop and ion concentration analysis.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Diluting seawater leads to lower solubility of CaSO4, increasing its precipitation rate and causing significant pressure drops. ([[2013-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2013, p. 53]])
