# Marine Bioerosion

**Summary**: The erosion of hard ocean substrates by living organisms.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2012-amao|Amao, 2012]] investigated marine-bioerosion as a process driven by the boring activity of cyanobacteria.

- [[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015]] investigated the impact of pollution on marine organisms (foraminifera) in the Arabian Gulf.

## Scientific Context

- [[2012-amao|Amao, 2012]] It impacts the structural integrity of reefs and carbonate sediments.

- [[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015]] investigated the impact of pollution on marine organisms (foraminifera) in the Arabian Gulf.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2012-amao|Amao, 2012]] Observation of boring tunnels in carbonate samples.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Boring activity of euendoliths results in biological corrosion and disintegration of carbonate surfaces. ([[2012-amao|Amao, 2012, p. 25]])


- Pollution led to a reduction in diversity and specific population shifts in foraminiferal groups. ([[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015, p. 146]])
