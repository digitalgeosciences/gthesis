# Yield Point

**Summary**: The stress required to initiate flow in a non-Newtonian fluid.

---

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Yield point of modified starch was nearly twice that of grass mud at higher concentrations. ([[2014-wajheeuddin|Wajheeuddin, 2014, p. 108]])


- The yield point decreased from 13.85 lb/100 ft2 to 10.16 lb/100 ft2 when temperature increased from 120°F to 150°F. ([[2017-alakbari|Alakbari, 2017, p. 47]])

- A greater yield point raises the transport capacity of the mud and builds up the circulating pressure drop in the annulus. ([[2017-al-rubaii|AL-RUBAII, 2017, p. 49]])

- The YP model showed R of 0.91 for both training and testing data sets with AAPE under 5%. ([[2021-alsabaa|Alsabaa, 2021, p. 78]])

- Yield point increased with the addition of ilmenite, reaching 70.8 lb/100ft2 for Mud-3. ([[2022-aljaberi|AlJaberi, 2022, p. 74]])

- Laponite at 0.3% BWOC increased the yield point of ilmenite-weighted cement to 60.6 lb/100 ft2. ([[2023-ahmed-2|AHMED, 2023, p. 168]])

- Yield point decreased with increasing temperature but increased with aging time. ([[1990-ali|Ali, 1990, p. 61]])
