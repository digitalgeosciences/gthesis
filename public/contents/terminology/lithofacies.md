## Key Findings Across Sources

- The non parametric Sequential Indicator Simulation was then used to build geological architecture of lithofacies using the results obtained from indicator kriging of lithofacies. ([[1997-al-qassab|Al-Qassab, 1997, p. 16]])

- Lithofacies are defined by three criteria: similar textural features, similar diagenetic histories, and similar petrophysical properties. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 118]])

- The Sulaiy is made up of finer grained lime mudstone, while Yamama is calcarenitic limestone. ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992, p. 49]])

- The distribution of dolomitic lithofacies suggests a series of persistent paleoshoals or barriers. ([[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988, p. 99]])

- Lithofacies identification is a primary task in reservoir characterization. ([[2009-sitouah|Sitouah, 2009, p. 19]])

- The relationship of petrophysical rocks with sedimentological facies is used to distribute petrophysical rocks in three dimensions. ([[2010-alroudhan|AlRoudhan, 2010, p. 70]])

- Identified five distinct facies: upper shoreface, lower shoreface, inner lagoon, outer lagoon, and transgressive lagoon. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 13]])

- Five distinct lithofacies were identified in the study area. ([[2011-al-masrahy|AL-MASRAHY, 2011, p. 48]])

- Identified 9 distinct sedimentary facies including ooid grainstones and thrombolites. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 45]])

- Not stated in source ([[2012-amao|Amao, 2012]])

- Three main facies were identified: fluvial channel, bay, and marine. ([[2013-atig|atig, 2013, p. 44]])

- The study identified several lithofacies associations interpreted as a gentle slope platform. ([[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013, p. 18]])

- Identified several facies including Sp (planar cross-bedded), St (trough cross-bedded), and ferruginous sandstone. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 35]])

- Lithofacies include Sh (horizontally bedded), St (trough cross-bedded), Sm (massive), Sl (low-angle cross-bedded), Ss (scour fills), Sp (planar cross-bedded), and Sb (bioturbated). ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 76]])

- Four main facies were identified: well-sorted oolitic grainstone, cross-bedded oolitic fossiliferous grainstone, graded mudstone to packstone, and dolostone. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 95]])

- Four main lithofacies associations were identified: poorly sorted intraclasts, graded mudstones, shoal complexes, and thin marl limestones. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 70]])

- Identified Lithofacies-I (micaceous), Lithofacies-II (clay-rich), and Lithofacies-III (siliceous). ([[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015, p. 19]])

- Lithology was quantified as a fractional log where values between 1 and 3 indicated mixtures of sand, limestone, and dolomite. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 37]])

- Identified facies including stromatolite boundstone, oolitic grainstone, and foraminiferal packstone. ([[2015-koeshidayatullah|Koeshidayatullah, 2015, p. 39]])

- Three distinct lithofacies were identified: sand, shaly-sand, and shale. ([[2015-jabir|Jabir, 2015, p. 13]])

- Identified distinct associations such as skeletal bank and tidal flat lithofacies in the Arab-D member. ([[2015-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2015, p. 77]])

- Identified 10 distinct lithofacies, including oolitic and grainstone types. ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 7]])

- Facies One (Shaly Sandstone) was identified as channel point bar deposits. ([[2015-sanuade|Sanuade, 2015, p. 47]])
