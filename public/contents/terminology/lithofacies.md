# Lithofacies

**Summary**: The classification of rocks based on their petrophysical properties like porosity and permeability.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2022-suleiman|Suleiman, 2022]] used as a target for machine learning models to identify reservoir zones.

- [[2023-butt|Butt, 2023]] used lithofacies-analysis to identify 11 distinct lithofacies (F1-F11) in the Al Wajh Formation.

- [[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023]] performed lithofacies-analysis to classify the xenoliths as lherzolites, harzburgites, and wehrlites.

- [[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023]] performed lithofacies analysis to characterize the rock types in the study area.

- [[2024-almomen|Almomen, 2024]] used lithology to determine the impact of formation changes on ROP.

- [[2024-ahmed|Ahmed, 2024]] used lithofacies analysis to interpret the sedimentary sequence in the Al-Hassa area.

- [[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024]] performed petrographic analysis to identify mineral textures and alterations.

- [[2025-kesumastuti|Kesumastuti, 2025]] interpreted low-velocity anomalies near the Palu Koro Fault as sedimentary layers with high porosity.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The rock typing model reached an accuracy of 90% of accurate Petrophysical rock types. ([[2022-suleiman|Suleiman, 2022, p. 17]])


- Identified eleven lithofacies grouped into conglomerate, sandstone, and mudstone categories based on grain size. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 75]])

- Classified 7 peridotites as lherzolites, 4 as harzburgites, and 2 as wehrlites. ([[2023-iskandar|Iskandar, 2023, p. 63]])

- Rocks are primarily composed of granitic and felsic igneous and low-grade metamorphic rocks. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 16]])

- AI tools were used for lithology identification and monitoring complex lithological properties. ([[2024-almomen|Almomen, 2024, p. 40]])

- The sedimentary part is divided into distinct tectono-sedimentary units including the Hofuf, Dam, and Hadrukh formations. ([[2024-ahmed|Ahmed, 2024, p. 102]])

- Identified several alteration phases: albitization, sericitization, muscovitization, silicification, and hematitization. ([[2024-al-hakimi|Al-Hakimi, 2024, p. 58]])

- A low-velocity anomaly was detected at depths from 5 to 25 km, interpreted as sedimentary layers. ([[2025-kesumastuti|Kesumastuti, 2025, p. 47]])
