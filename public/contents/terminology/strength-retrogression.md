# Strength Retrogression

**Summary**: The loss of compressive strength in cement due to the conversion of C-S-H to alpha-C2SH at high temperatures.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2017-baig|Baig, 2017]] addressed strength retrogression in HPHT wells.

- [[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022]] investigated strength retrogression in systems containing various pozzolans.

## Scientific Context

- [[2017-baig|Baig, 2017]] Critical for ensuring the integrity of well casings in high-temperature environments.

- [[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022]] investigated strength retrogression in systems containing various pozzolans.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2017-baig|Baig, 2017]] Monitored via XRD and ultrasonic analysis.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Additions like silica flour or Nano Zeolite are used to prevent this transition. ([[2017-baig|Baig, 2017, p. 61]])


- The addition of SCMs like fly ash and GGBFS resulted in more stable C-S-H minerals, which enhanced mechanical properties at high temperatures. ([[2022-adjei|Adjei, 2022, p. 171]])
