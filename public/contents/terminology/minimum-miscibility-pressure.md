# Minimum Miscibility Pressure

**Summary**: The pressure at which two fluids become miscible and the interface between them disappears.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2016-mahboob-2|Mahboob, 2016]] calculated minimum-miscibility-pressure to determine the conditions under which CO2 and brines would mix completely.

- [[2019-al-azani|AL-AZANI, 2019]] estimated the minimum-miscibility-pressure for CO2 with dead crude oil.

## Scientific Context

- [[2016-mahboob-2|Mahboob, 2016]] MMP is a critical parameter in CO2-EOR design.

- [[2019-al-azani|AL-AZANI, 2019]] estimated the minimum-miscibility-pressure for CO2 with dead crude oil.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2016-mahboob-2|Mahboob, 2016]] Extrapolation of IFT vs. Pressure curves.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- MMP for Brine 1 was found to be 17,707 psi at 30°C and 46,990 psi at 60°C. ([[2016-mahboob-2|Mahboob, 2016, p. 106]])


- Estimated MMP was approximately 3,098 psia at 100 °C. ([[2019-al-azani|AL-AZANI, 2019, p. 86]])
