# Phytoremediation

**Summary**: The use of plants to clean up contaminated soil, water, or air.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-bello|Bello, 2015]] evaluated the phytoremediation potential of Bolboschoenus maritimus and Phragmites australis.

- [[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019]] evaluated the sequestration potential of Avicennia marina for hydrocarbons.

## Scientific Context

- [[2015-bello|Bello, 2015]] Phytoremediation is a promising method for treating water contaminated with heavy metals and PAHs.

- [[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019]] evaluated the sequestration potential of Avicennia marina for hydrocarbons.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2015-bello|Bello, 2015]] Measured by comparing initial and final concentrations of pollutants in the hydroponic system.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Both species showed high removal rates for heavy metals (over 80%) and naphthalene (over 90%). ([[2015-bello|Bello, 2015, p. 14]])

- Avicennia marina was found to be an efficient species for hydrocarbon sequestration. ([[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019, p. 16]])
