# Fractured Reservoirs

**Summary**: Reservoirs where porosity and permeability are primarily controlled by a network of fractures.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2012-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2012]] analyzed to determine the impact of fracture distribution on hydrocarbon potential.

- [[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016]] evaluated the potential for fractured reservoirs in the Unayzah-C and Unayzah-A units.

- [[2016-babker|Babker, 2016]] studied the Sarah Formation as a fractured reservoir.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- No matrix porosity is present in fresh basement; fractures are the main defined porosity and permeability. ([[2012-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2012, p. 14]])

- The overall impression is that the Unayzah-A reservoir is not a fractured reservoir due to its friable nature. ([[2016-hassan|Hassan, 2016, p. 34]])

- Mode I (opening) fractures increase reservoir quality, while Mode III (shearing) acts as a barrier. ([[2016-babker|Babker, 2016, p. 83]])

- Fractures are categorized into three modes (I, II, and III) based on displacement vectors. ([[2017-mahgoub|Mahgoub, 2017, p. 34]])
