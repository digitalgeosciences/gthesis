# Fractured Reservoirs

**Summary**: Reservoirs containing natural or induced fractures that enhance fluid flow.

---

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Fractured horizontal wells were found to be superior to non-fractured horizontal wells in terms of recovery and breakthrough delay. ([[1993-kandil|Kandil, 1993, p. 10]])


- The study highlights that non-Darcy flow significantly impacts the perceived conductivity of these reservoirs. ([[1990-mimoune|Mimoune, 1990, p. 26]])

- The 3D method and CAPE (a, m, n) showed higher correlation factors for carbonate samples. ([[2009-al-gathe-2|AL-GATHE, 2009, p. 108]])

- Fractured systems require specific models (like dual-porosity) to accurately predict production performance. ([[2009-bajaalah|BAJAALAH, 2009, p. 28]])

- In these systems, porosity and permeability are controlled by the fracture network. ([[2012-abdelgader|Abdelgader, 2012, p. 11]])

- The pressure derivative in dual-porosity systems typically shows three periods: fracture response, transition period, and total system response. ([[2012-al-abdulmohsin|Al-Abdulmohsin, 2012, p. 30]])

- The dual porosity model is used to describe naturally fractured reservoirs where two different media are involved in the flow process. ([[2013-al-nemer|Al-Nemer, 2013, p. 27]])

- The existence of natural fractures play a major role in well performance. ([[2014-al-julaih|Al-Julaih, 2014, p. 14]])

- Fracture presence leads to dual-porosity and dual-permeability characteristics. ([[2017-khan|Khan, 2017, p. 32]])

- The Brinkman model provides a more accurate representation of velocity profiles in free-flow regions compared to the standard Darcy model. ([[2018-jamal|Jamal, 2018, p. 102]])

- Natural fractures provide preferential flow paths for mud invasion, increasing risk of loss circulation. ([[2019-al-nutaifi|Al-Nutaifi, 2019, p. 25]])

- Natural fractures can either enhance or reduce well productivity depending on their intensity and the resulting hydraulic fracture length. ([[2022-alsulaiman|AlSulaiman, 2022, p. 18]])

- The study confirmed that fractures significantly contribute to water flow, with pre-treatment permeability being 184.73 mD compared to 70 mD for non-fractured sections. ([[2024-suleiman-4|Suleiman, 2024, p. 116]])

- The more conductive formations indicate the fractured basement, potentially hosting high heat-generating rocks. ([[2024-chavanidis|Chavanidis, 2024, p. 100]])
