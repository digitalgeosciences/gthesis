# Acoustic Impedance

**Summary**: A physical property of rock that is the product of density and velocity.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Impedance is an ideal basis for the application of modern volume interpretation methods because it is a layer property. ([[2002-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2002, p. 39]])

- Acoustic impedance was used as a primary indicator for lithology and porosity in the study. ([[2015-fuad|Fuad, 2015, p. 29]])

- Inversion of AI, P-wave velocity, density, and compressional modulus resulted in a percent error of almost 0% for gas, live oil, and brine. ([[2016-ali|Ali, 2016, p. 14]])

- Not stated in source ([[2018-mohamed|Mohamed, 2018]])

- Hydrocarbon-filled reservoirs are characterized by lower acoustic impedance than surrounding water-filled rocks. ([[2019-al-dawood|Al-Dawood, 2019, p. 19]])

- P-wave impedance was used as a primary indicator for mineralogy and porosity. ([[2020-almumtin|Almumtin, 2020, p. 43]])

- A 100 Hz Ricker wavelet was convolved with an impedance model derived from velocity and density data. ([[2024-allehyani|allehyani, 2024, p. 65]])
