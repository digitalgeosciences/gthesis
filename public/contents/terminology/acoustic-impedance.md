# Acoustic Impedance

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Used as a basis for seismic refraction interpretation. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 39]])
- [[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000]] 1800 m/s to 3970 m/s (field)

- Trace envelope is proportional to the acoustic impedance contrast. ([[2009-sitouah|Sitouah, 2009, p. 64]])

- P-wave and S-wave velocities are key indicators for evaluating formation porosity and lithology. ([[2012-elhaj|Elhaj, 2012, p. 15]])

- P-wave velocity decreased significantly after CO2 flooding, while S-wave velocity showed variable behavior. ([[2014-al-ameri|AL-AMERI, 2014, p. 38]])

- Not specifically measured, but used in the context of wave propagation equations. ([[2015-mohammed|Mohammed, 2015, p. 30]])

- Not stated in source ([[2015-own|Own, 2015]])

- A sudden change in AI at 1.07s indicated the boundary between different pore fluids. ([[2016-ali|Ali, 2016, p. 52]])

- A correlation with R^2 = 0.9 was established between porosity and acoustic impedance. ([[2016-houssain|Houssain, 2016, p. 89]])

- Compressional and shear wave travel times are critical for determining rock elastic parameters like Young's modulus and Poisson's ratio. ([[2016-tariq|Tariq, 2016, p. 31]])

- High impedance contrast between the sand dunes and underlying formations results in an amplification of seismic waves. ([[2017-almarzooq|Almarzooq, 2017, p. 14]])

- Not stated in source ([[2018-mohamed|Mohamed, 2018]])

- Acoustic impedance inversion for well A followed the same trend as the AI log at top target. ([[2019-al-dawood|Al-Dawood, 2019, p. 85]])

- A value of 13 km/s g/cc was identified as a cut-off for identifying porous sand. ([[2020-almumtin|Almumtin, 2020, p. 60]])

- Acoustic impedance is often meaningless to geologists until transformed into reservoir properties like porosity and lithology. ([[2021-suleymanov|Suleymanov, 2021, p. 21]])

- Established that variations in velocity are the primary driver for impedance contrast due to low mineral density differences. ([[2022-salih|Salih, 2022, p. 43]])

- Impedance showed a clear distinction between clean and contaminated calcite for concentrations as low as 1%. ([[2023-badhafere|Badhafere, 2023, p. 61]])

- Inversion for acoustic impedance is significantly less convoluted than direct inversion for velocity or density. ([[2023-almarzooq|Almarzooq, 2023, p. 43]])

- Strata with Ip below 9 km/s g/cc contained oil, while those above were brine-saturated. ([[2024-alabbad|Alabbad, 2024, p. 49]])

- High contrast in acoustic impedance leads to energy being trapped within the dune (Page 20). ([[2024-jahlan|Jahlan, 2024, p. 20]])

- Not specifically quantified as a standalone value, but used within the Gassmann-Hertz-Mindlin model for VP calculation. ([[2024-tarchi|Tarchi, 2024, p. 30]])

- Factors like fractures, stylolites, and organic matter significantly impacted acoustic velocities. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 14]])

- Model was populated with seismic impedance (Vp * density) values based on bioturbation style and intensity. ([[2024-allehyani|allehyani, 2024, p. 30]])

- Not specifically quantified in this thesis, but used as a basis for rock physics models. ([[2025-oyekan|Oyekan, 2025, p. 38]])
