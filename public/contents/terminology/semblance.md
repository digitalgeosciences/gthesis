# Semblance

**Summary**: A measure of the similarity between traces in a seismic survey, used to identify coherent reflections.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2010-al-moqbel|Al-Moqbel, 2010]] used semblance analysis to identify potential problems with multiple picks and poor coherency.

- [[2020-alzahrani|Alzahrani, 2020]] calculated velocity semblance to map time-space data to a velocity-semblance plane.

## Scientific Context

- [[2010-al-moqbel|Al-Moqbel, 2010]] Used to determine the most likely path of a seismic wave through the subsurface.

- [[2020-alzahrani|Alzahrani, 2020]] calculated velocity semblance to map time-space data to a velocity-semblance plane.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2010-al-moqbel|Al-Moqbel, 2010]] Analyzed as a function of time and offset.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Semblance showed maximum coherency at wide ranges of offset (e.g., 650ms and 820ms), creating ambiguity in horizon picking. ([[2010-al-moqbel|Al-Moqbel, 2010, p. 129]])


- The vertical resolution for the linear semblance was improved compared with that of the conventional semblance. ([[2020-alzahrani|Alzahrani, 2020, p. 49]])
