# Hydrogen Index

**Summary**: A measure of the hydrogen content in kerogen, indicating its potential to generate hydrocarbons.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2020-usman|Usman, 2020]] calculated hydrogen index from pyrolysis data to determine generation potential.

## Scientific Context

- [[2020-usman|Usman, 2020]] HI indicates whether a rock is likely to produce oil or gas.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2020-usman|Usman, 2020]] Calculated from S1 and S2 pyrolysis values.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Average HI values for high-potential lithofacies ranged from 150 to 200 mgHC/gTOC. ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 77]])
- [[2020-usman|Usman, 2020]] Values up to 200 mgHC/gTOC were found in several lithofacies.
