# Corrosion

**Summary**: The destructive attack of a metal by chemical or electrochemical reaction with its environment.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[1994-shokoya|Shokoya, 1994]] investigated the corrosion behavior of mild steel in water-based drilling fluids.

- [[2014-al-hussain|Al-Hussain, 2014]] used corrosion surveys to determine the condition of 4-1/2'' tubing and 7'' casing with metal loss associated.

- [[2017-al-ajmi|Al-Ajmi, 2017]] quantified corrosion using EMIT and calculated corrosion rates in mpy.

- [[2023-khalid|Khalid, 2023]] evaluated the impact of internal and external corrosion on pipeline integrity.

## Scientific Context

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Corrosion rates were found to be highly dependent on temperature and pH levels. ([[1994-shokoya|Shokoya, 1994, p. 19]])


- 53% corrosion factor for 4-1/2'' tubing... and 39% corrosion factor for 7'' casing were used as thresholds for identifying potential leaks or corrosion. ([[2014-al-hussain|Al-Hussain, 2014, p. 35]])

- Maximum likely corrosion rate was 23.27 mpy for the first 900 feet. ([[2017-al-ajmi|Al-Ajmi, 2017, p. 76]])

- Internal corrosion is driven by water coupled with acid gases (CO2 or H2S) or sulfate-reducing bacteria. ([[2023-khalid|Khalid, 2023, p. 43]])
