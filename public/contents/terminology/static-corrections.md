# Static Corrections

**Summary**: Corrections applied to seismic data to account for variations in the near-surface velocity.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The difference between multi-layer and single-layer low frequency statics ranged from -11 to +120 ms. ([[2011-toma|Toma, 2011, p. 43]])


- Static correction accounts for elevation differences and lateral variations in velocity/thickness of the weathering layer. ([[2021-almashhor|AlMashhor, 2021, p. 17]])

- A two-step solution is required: first removing high-frequency delays from dunes, then medium-wavelength issues from the underlying weathered layer. ([[2022-aljishi|AlJishi, 2022, p. 46]])

- Statics can lead to a 35% error in estimated velocity if they are not correctly accounted for. ([[2023-muhammad|Muhammad, 2023, p. 18]])

- Accurate knowledge of velocity from surface to a reference datum is essential for accurate statics. ([[2002-al-ali|Al-Ali, 2002, p. 108]])
