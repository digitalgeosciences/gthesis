# Microfacies

**Summary**: A group of fossils that occur together and indicate a specific environment.

---

- [[2006-al-enezi|Al-Enezi, 2006]] defined foraminiferal biofacies to interpret the depositional history of the Dam Formation.

- [[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017]] identified 8 distinct microfacies to determine depositional environments.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Identified 14 distinct biofacies in the Dam Formation, such as the 'Miliolid-Rotalia' and 'Stromatolite' facies. ([[2006-al-enezi|Al-Enezi, 2006, p. 87]])


- Eight types were identified, including coral rudstone (MF1) and peloidal grainstone (MF8). ([[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017, p. 19]])

- Lithofacies LFT 1 (coarse-grained oolitic grainstone) shows high-angle cross-lamination and is interpreted as intertidal channels. ([[2017-adam-2|Adam, 2017, p. 95]])

- Identified eight distinct microfacies including peloidal skeletal wackestone, burrowed wackestone, and mudstone. ([[2018-ismanto|Ismanto, 2018, p. 17]])

- Identified 11 distinct microfacies based on mineralogy, texture, and fossil content. ([[2021-alabyadh|Alabyadh, 2021, p. 58]])

- Ten carbonate microfacies were identified, ranging from mudstone to grainstone and floatstone. ([[2024-hassan|Hassan, 2024, p. 14]])

- Morphogroup analysis revealed that variations in test shapes correlate with specific habitat preferences. ([[2024-jalloh|Jalloh, 2024, p. 38]])

- Identified two distinct burrowing styles: pipe framework (high energy) and bio-retexturing (mixed sediment). ([[2025-refaat|Refaat, 2025, p. 65]])

- DINOv2 + SVM achieved 83.1% accuracy on a lithofacies dataset with only 20% training data. ([[2025-ferreira|Ferreira, 2025, p. 48]])

- Identified 10 distinct microfacies types in the section. ([[1989-abdou|Abdou, 1989, p. 12]])

- Two main microfacies were identified: Nautiloculina/Kurnubia Grainstone and Pseudocyclammina/Kurnubia Dolomitic Wackestone. ([[1992-abdulghani|Abdulghani, 1992, p. 108]])
