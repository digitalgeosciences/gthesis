# In-Situ Stress

**Summary**: The pressure of fluids within the pores of a rock.

---

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Pore pressure is defined as the pressure of pore fluids in excess of the hydrostatic pressure. ([[2013-mark|Mark, 2013, p. 11]])


- Overburden stress averaged 1.0 psi/ft; pore pressure gradient averaged 0.48 psi/ft. ([[2014-al-haidary|Al-Haidary, 2014, p. 63]])

- Injection of carbon dioxide was shown to cause an increase in the reservoir pore pressure. ([[2017-khan|Khan, 2017, p. 21]])

- Pore pressure was predicted to drop from 6,600-7,800 psi in 2017 to 3,300-3,900 psi by 2038. ([[2018-aldossary|AlDossary, 2018, p. 61]])

- Pore pressure values for Well-3 ranged from 4254 to 6496 psi. ([[2023-alzayer|Alzayer, 2023, p. 23]])

- The azimuths of the minimum and maximum horizontal stress are approximately northwest and northeast, respectively. ([[2002-elhassan|ElHassan, 2002, p. 19]])
