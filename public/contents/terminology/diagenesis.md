# Diagenesis

**Summary**: The chemical, physical, and biological changes in sediment or rock after deposition.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Diagenesis included reworking, leaching, and recrystallization. ([[1996-jarad|Jarad, 1996, p. 53]])

- Suggested a biochemical origin for the deposits rather than purely abiogenic processes. ([[1996-matter|Matter, 1996, p. 26]])

- Diagenetic processes include compaction, cementation, and dissolution (secondary pore generation). ([[2001-al-ramadan|Al-Ramadan, 2001, p. 18]])

- Key stages included compaction, cementation (calcite, clay, quartz), and dissolution-induced secondary porosity. ([[2002-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2002, p. 23]])

- Not stated in source ([[1992-al-sulami|Al-Sulami, 1992]])

- Diagenetic processes included cementation, micritization, and dissolution of aragonitic components. ([[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988, p. 13]])

- Not stated in source ([[2006-al-enezi|Al-Enezi, 2006]])

- Meteoric cements and karst features were used as primary indicators of subaerial exposure and unconformities. ([[2010-al-mojel|Al-Mojel, 2010, p. 85]])

- Not stated in source ([[2011-al-masrahy|AL-MASRAHY, 2011]])

- Identified Dolomitic Breccia as a result of dissolution of interbedded evaporites during transgressions. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 53]])

- Not stated in source ([[2012-ghazwani|Ghazwani, 2012]])

- Identified multiple stages of diagenesis including early and late dolomitization. ([[2013-abdalla|Abdalla, 2013, p. 74]])

- Iron cementation and kaolinite formation were key diagenetic factors impacting petrophysical properties. ([[2014-waseem|Waseem, 2014, p. 78]])

- Kaolinite cementation and quartz overgrowth are the primary diagenetic factors affecting reservoir quality. ([[2014-el-deek|El-Deek, 2014, p. 109]])

- Dolomitization significantly enhanced the overall porosity in the graded mudstone to packstone facies. ([[2014-mohammed|Mohammed, 2014, p. 132]])

- Diagenesis is one of the two main controls on reservoir heterogeneity at the outcrop scale. ([[2014-sami|Sami, 2014, p. 18]])

- Original calcite cement was replaced by microcrystalline quartz in the Siq Sandstone. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 83]])

- Diagenetic processes included micritization, cementation, dissolution, neomorphism, and dolomitization. ([[2015-koeshidayatullah|Koeshidayatullah, 2015, p. 67]])

- Reservoir quality is driven by both depositional facies and diagenesis (e.g., dolomitization). ([[2015-elzain|Elzain, 2015, p. 45]])

- Diagenetic controls are present and contribute to the variability of porosity and permeability. ([[2015-ali|Ali, 2015, p. 25]])

- Cementation by calcite and iron oxide were primary factors affecting porosity and permeability. ([[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016, p. 14]])

- Dolomite was found to be the main mineral content in mudstone when interbedded with evaporites due to Mg-rich fluids. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 122]])

- Key early-stage processes include dolomitization, selective dissolution, and anhydrite-calcite cementation. ([[2016-saleh|Saleh, 2016, p. 47]])

- Dolomitization was identified as a key process that occurred in grainstone facies underlying mudstone-evaporite beds. ([[2016-salih|Salih, 2016, p. 52]])
