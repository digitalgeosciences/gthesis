## Key Findings Across Sources

- The optimization goal was to maximize NPV, which incorporates costs like gas purchase, recycling, and surfactant injection. ([[2013-sibaweihi|Sibaweihi, 2013, p. 37]])


- CMAES produced the highest NPV values, but IWO provided more stable well configurations. ([[2013-ahmed|AHMED, 2013, p. 91]])

- The highest NPV in Example 1, Scenario 3 was achieved using the Pareto-based approach (11.3 x 10^9). ([[2014-ahmed-2|Ahmed, 2014, p. 82]])

- Optimizing for ST-NPV resulted in a more rapid production decline than MT or LT optimization. ([[2014-ali|Ali, 2014, p. 72]])

- Maximizing NPV is the standard industry goal, but it must be balanced against environmental constraints. ([[2014-al-ismael|AL-ISMAEL, 2014, p. 32]])

- Net Present Value (NPV) is a better representation of project profitability and recovery capability than cSOR. ([[2015-moussa|Moussa, 2015, p. 13]])

- NPV was chosen because it accounts for the time value of money and includes capital costs like drilling, steam generation, and solvent processing. ([[2015-khan|KHAN, 2015, p. 58]])

- At 200 md-ft conductivity, NPV was lower than at other high conductivities due to higher proppant costs. ([[2015-rammay|RAMMAY, 2015, p. 76]])

- The NPV calculation included costs for facilities, drilling, and trajectory complexity (TFD), providing a realistic economic evaluation of the field development plan. ([[2023-alharbi|Alharbi, 2023, p. 47]])
