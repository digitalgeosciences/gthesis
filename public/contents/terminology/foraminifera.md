# Foraminifera

**Summary**: Single-celled protists with shells (tests) that are widely used as indicators of marine environments.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2006-al-enezi|Al-Enezi, 2006]] analyzed foraminifera to interpret the paleoenvironments of the Miocene Dam Formation.

- [[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015]] studied benthic foraminifera as primary subjects to assess environmental impacts and seasonal dynamics.

- [[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016]] used foraminifera to reconstruct the depositional environment of the Dam Formation.

- [[2017-amao|Amao, 2017]] studied benthic foraminifera as primary indicators of marine environments in the Arabian Gulf.

- [[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018]] analyzed foraminifera to reconstruct the oxygenation and depth of the Qusaiba Formation.

## Scientific Context

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2006-al-enezi|Al-Enezi, 2006]] Identified through microscopy of thin sections and processed sediment samples.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- A total of 47 species were identified in the Arabian Gulf samples. ([[2006-al-enezi|Al-Enezi, 2006, p. 1]])

- Several species including Ammonia, Murrayinella, and Glabratellina were identified in the Arabian Gulf. ([[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015, p. 33]])

- The assemblage was dominated by calcareous porcellaneous Miliolina (Quinqueloculina, Peneroplis). ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 15]])

- Identified numerous species including Pseudonubeculina arabica and Pseudotriloculina hottingeri. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 36]])

- Identified 24 genera and 77 species, including a first discovery of agglutinated forms in the Middle East. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 13]])
