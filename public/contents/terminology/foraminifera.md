## Key Findings Across Sources

- Identified 47 species from the Gulf (2 agglutinated, 21 miliolid, 24 rotalid) and 51 from the Dam Formation. ([[2006-al-enezi|Al-Enezi, 2006, p. 13]])


- Benthic foraminifera are key carbonate producers in the Arabian Gulf and serve as bio-indicators for pollution. ([[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015, p. 17]])

- The assemblage was dominated by calcareous porcellaneous Miliolina, indicating a restricted carbonate platform. ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 16]])

- Benthic foraminiferal species were dominated by porcelaneous taxa (85.1%) in Salwa Bay. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 20]])

- Identified 77 species of agglutinated foraminifera, the first such record in Gondwana clastic deposits. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 54]])

- 63 species were identified, including 10 new ones, showing high diversity in the lower Dhruma. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 10]])

- Quinqueloculina and Ammonia were identified as the most salt-tolerant species in the hypersaline environment. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 14]])

- Over 109 species were identified, with LBF showing higher deformity rates (>40%). ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 66]])

- The abundance and diversity of DWAF provide information on oxygenation and productivity changes. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 22]])

- Identified 59 taxa, including several new species like Bulbobaculites sp. 1. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 33]])

- The study identifies 33 foraminiferal species from 21 genera. ([[2024-jalloh|Jalloh, 2024, p. 85]])
