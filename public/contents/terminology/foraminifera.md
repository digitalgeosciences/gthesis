# Foraminifera

**Summary**: Single-celled protists with shells (tests) that are widely used as indicators of marine environments.

---

## Key Findings Across Sources

- A total of 47 species were identified in the Arabian Gulf samples. ([[2006-al-enezi|Al-Enezi, 2006, p. 1]])

- Several species including Ammonia, Murrayinella, and Glabratellina were identified in the Arabian Gulf. ([[2015-arslan|Arslan, 2015, p. 33]])

- The assemblage was dominated by calcareous porcellaneous Miliolina (Quinqueloculina, Peneroplis). ([[2016-septriandi|Septriandi, 2016, p. 15]])

- Identified numerous species including Pseudonubeculina arabica and Pseudotriloculina hottingeri. ([[2017-amao|Amao, 2017, p. 36]])

- Identified 24 genera and 77 species, including a first discovery of agglutinated forms in the Middle East. ([[2018-perdana|Perdana, 2018, p. 13]])

- 63 species were identified, including several endemic ones. ([[2022-khamsin|Khamsin, 2022, p. 10]])

- Quinqueloculina and Ammonia were the most salt-tolerant species identified. ([[2023-nasir|Nasir, 2023, p. 14]])

- Approximately one-quarter of foraminifera were alive at sampling, with high deformity rates above 40 PSU. ([[2023-prayudi|Prayudi, 2023, p. 56]])

- Identified several new species including Arenoturrispirillina waskowskae and Ammopemphix contessaensis. ([[2024-hikmahtiar|Hikmahtiar, 2024, p. 40]])

- 59 taxa were identified, including 42 at the species level. ([[2024-alhilali|Alhilali, 2024, p. 13]])

- Benthic foraminifera were found to be more resistant to dissolution than planktonic varieties. ([[2024-alsadah|Alsadah, 2024, p. 21]])

- 33 species representing 19 genera were documented in the D7 unit. ([[2024-jalloh|Jalloh, 2024, p. 15]])
