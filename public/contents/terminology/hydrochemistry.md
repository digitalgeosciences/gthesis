# Hydrochemistry

**Summary**: The study of the chemical composition of waters and its interaction with minerals.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2014-kasha|Kasha, 2014]] analyzed hydrochemistry to determine how ions in Arabian Gulf Seawater affect carbonate rocks.

- [[2017-sadeed|SADEED, 2017]] analyzed the hydrochemistry of various brines to determine their effect on carbonate minerals.

- [[2020-mansouri|Mansouri, 2020]] performed a hydrochemical assessment of the aquifer.

- [[1994-al-guwaizani|Al-Guwaizani, 1994]] analyzed hydrochemistry to determine the influence of evaporation and groundwater on brine composition.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Magnesium ions were found to have a higher affinity for carbonate surfaces than calcium ions. ([[2014-kasha|Kasha, 2014, p. 93]])


- 5000 ppm NaI was found to be the most effective brine for altering wettability toward less oil-wet. ([[2017-sadeed|SADEED, 2017, p. 89]])

- 67% of samples showed elevated nitrate levels due to fertilizer use. ([[2020-mansouri|Mansouri, 2020, p. 13]])

- Brine shows enrichment of all stable isotopes except deuterium, indicating strong evaporation of Neogene groundwater. ([[1994-al-guwaizani|Al-Guwaizani, 1994, p. 12]])
