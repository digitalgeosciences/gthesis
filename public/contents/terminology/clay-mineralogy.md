# Clay Mineralogy

**Summary**: The study of clay minerals, which are hydrous aluminum phyllosilicates that significantly impact reservoir properties.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Kaolinite can cause pore filling, while illite leads to pore bridging, both reducing permeability. ([[2015-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2015, p. 39]])


- Kaolinite is the dominant clay mineral throughout the formation, often acting as a pore lining. ([[2016-abbas|Abbas, 2016, p. 106]])

- Rectorite (illite-smectite) is a common mixed layer in shale formations. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 66]])

- Clay content was used as a proxy for mineralogy in the constant-cement model. ([[2021-suleymanov|Suleymanov, 2021, p. 59]])

- Smectite (montmorillonite) has high CEC (80-150 m2/g) and is highly sensitive to water. ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 29]])

- Smectite group (2:1 type clays) are considered most prone to swelling, with Montmorillonite being the primary member. ([[2021-khan|KHAN, 2021, p. 36]])

- OMMT is the most popular commercial rheology additive used in OBDF due to its hydrophobicity and large basal spacing. ([[2024-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2024, p. 30]])
