# Micro Emulsion

**Summary**: A mixture of oil, water, and surfactant forming a stable dispersion.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2013-geri|Geri, 2013]] determined the phase behavior as an oil-in-water micro-emulsion (Winsor Type I).

- [[2016-mahboob-2|Mahboob, 2016]] observed micro-emulsions formed by fluorosurfactants inside the system.

## Scientific Context

- [[2013-geri|Geri, 2013]] Determining the correct micro-emulsion type is critical for selecting the right surfactant concentration.

- [[2016-mahboob-2|Mahboob, 2016]] observed micro-emulsions formed by fluorosurfactants inside the system.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

## Key Findings Across Sources

- It is believed that low IFT can be obtained at high solubility ratio for the optimum salinity concentration. ([[2013-geri|Geri, 2013, p. 16]])


- Fluorosurfactant caused formation of micro-emulsions, leading to a transition from gas-wet to oil-wet behavior near the PIP. ([[2016-mahboob-2|Mahboob, 2016, p. 144]])
