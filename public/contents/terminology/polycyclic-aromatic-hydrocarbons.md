# Polycyclic Aromatic Hydrocarbons

**Summary**: A class of organic compounds with two or more fused benzene rings, known for being persistent and toxic.

---

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Identified as particularly difficult to degrade due to high boiling points and hydrophobicity. ([[2015-oyehan|Oyehan, 2015, p. 22]])

- Naphthalene is a common PAH with an octanol-water partition coefficient (LK) of 3.37. ([[2015-bello|Bello, 2015, p. 22]])

- Naphthalene was completely mineralized in several consortia and isolates under optimal conditions (37°C). ([[2015-malik|Malik, 2015, p. 15]])

- Pyrene was identified as a particularly recalcitrant PAH in hypersaline environments. ([[2017-budiyanto|Budiyanto, 2017, p. 17]])

- The total concentration of PAHs in Arabian Light crude oil was 27,377.7 ng/ml. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 63]])

- HMW PAHs (4+ rings) are more prevalent in mangrove sediments than LMW PAHs due to the latter's susceptibility to microbial degradation. ([[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019, p. 34]])

- PAHs are persistent, non-volatile, and hydrophobic pollutants that require remediation in contaminated sites. ([[2019-alhaiqi|Alhaiqi, 2019, p. 22]])
