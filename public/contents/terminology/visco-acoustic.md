# Visco Acoustic

**Summary**: A model of the earth that accounts for both wave velocity and attenuation (absorption) due to rock friction.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-mohammed|Mohammed, 2015]] modeled the subsurface as a visco-acoustic medium to account for energy loss.

## Scientific Context

- [[2015-mohammed|Mohammed, 2015]] Real-world earth materials are rarely purely acoustic; visco-acoustic models provide more realistic imaging results.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2015-mohammed|Mohammed, 2015]] Incorporated into the wavefield extrapolator in the f-k domain.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Incorporating the Q-factor into the model allows for compensation of absorption and dispersion. ([[2015-mohammed|Mohammed, 2015, p. 13]])
