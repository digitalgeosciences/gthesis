# Loss Circulation

**Summary**: The escape of drilling fluids into adjacent formations or fractures.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2012-omer|Omer, 2012]] investigated the use of polymeric materials as loss circulation agents (LCM).

- [[2014-hashmat|Hashmat, 2014]] addressed the problem of lost circulation by evaluating cross-linked polymer gels as potential LCMs.

- [[2019-al-nutaifi|Al-Nutaifi, 2019]] quantified 32 loss circulation events in the study area.

## Scientific Context

- [[2012-omer|Omer, 2012]] Critical in wellbore stability and completion; requires materials that can plug leaks effectively.

- [[2014-hashmat|Hashmat, 2014]] addressed the problem of lost circulation by evaluating cross-linked polymer gels as potential LCMs.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Manganese is being used for the first time in this research thesis for the preparation of LCM to make the gel formed behave elastic than viscous. ([[2012-omer|Omer, 2012, p. 109]])


- Losses exceeding 10 bbl/hr but less than 50 bbl/hr are defined as partial loss, while over 500 bbl/hr is considered complete loss. ([[2014-hashmat|Hashmat, 2014, p. 20]])

- Loss circulation was identified as the primary wellbore instability problem, leading to significant costs. ([[2019-al-nutaifi|Al-Nutaifi, 2019, p. 60]])
