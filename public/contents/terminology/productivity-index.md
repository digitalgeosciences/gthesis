# Productivity Index

**Summary**: A measure of the flow rate of a well per unit of pressure drop.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2020-mohammed|Mohammed, 2020]] calculated productivity_index to quantify the improvement in core conductivity.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2020-mohammed|Mohammed, 2020]] Calculated from coreflooding pressure and flow rate data.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Thermochemical treatment improved the productivity index by a factor of 1.94 for tight sandstone samples. ([[2020-mohammed|Mohammed, 2020, p. 109]])


- The well productivity index dropped from approximately 23 MSCF/D/psia to 2.1 MSCF/D/psia upon reaching the dew point. ([[1999-fawumi|Fawumi, 1999, p. 98]])

- Hydraulic fracturing increased the PI up to 3.6 times below the dew point pressure after 10 years. ([[2000-hashmi|Hashmi, 2000, p. 22]])
