# Point Of Zero Charge

**Summary**: The pH value at which the net surface charge of a material is zero.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019]] determined the PZC for GPDADMAC to understand its interaction with phenol.

## Scientific Context

- [[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019]] Determining PZC helps predict how the adsorbent will interact with charged or uncharged ions.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019]] A titration method involving boiling water to remove CO2 followed by adding a known amount of adsorbent.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The PZC of GPDADMAC was measured to be 3.5. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 40]])
