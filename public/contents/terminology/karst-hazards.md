# Karst Hazards

**Summary**: Geological hazards associated with the formation and collapse of underground cavities.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Characterizing cavity content helps engineers assess risks related to ground surface subsidence. ([[2014-saeed|Saeed, 2014, p. 21]])


- The existence of natural voids and cavities in subsurface limestone poses severe hazard for civil engineering activities. ([[2017-tayyab|Tayyab, 2017, p. 15]])

- Karst systems are highly heterogeneous and can contain significant volumes of water or oil in large conduits. ([[2018-elsaieed|ElSaieed, 2018, p. 23]])

- Karst structures leave an imprint on seismic gathers, including undulations in first arrivals. ([[2018-mohamed|Mohamed, 2018, p. 55]])

- The presence of karst is indicated by synforms and potentially hazardous sinkholes or caverns. ([[2018-hanif|Hanif, 2018, p. 28]])

- Karst aquifers cover 12% of the terrestrial land and provide a source of drinking water to almost a quarter of the world's population. ([[2018-jamal|Jamal, 2018, p. 23]])
