# Spectral Entropy

**Summary**: A measure of information based on the probability distribution of discrete cosine transform (DCT) coefficients.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-mohammed|Mohammed, 2015]] used spectral entropy to distinguish noise and smoothness more effectively than spatial entropy.

## Scientific Context

- [[2015-mohammed|Mohammed, 2015]] Used to capture texture variations and distinguish noise from resolution loss.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2015-mohammed|Mohammed, 2015]] Computed on 8x8 blocks using the Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT).

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Spectral entropy is a more accurate descriptor of image energy spectrum and emphasizes main frequency/orientations. ([[2015-mohammed|Mohammed, 2015, p. 89]])
