# Diffusion Coefficient

**Summary**: A measure of the rate at which ions move through a fluid.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2014-khalid|Khalid, 2014]] calculated the diffusion coefficient to evaluate the impact of reaction products on acid/rock interaction.

- [[2017-ahmed-2|AHMED, 2017]] calculated the diffusion coefficient (De) for both emulsified systems at HPHT.

- [[2022-alkathim|Alkathim, 2022]] investigated how higher diffusion coefficients affected acid consumption and PVBT.

## Scientific Context

- [[2014-khalid|Khalid, 2014]] Critical for predicting wormhole growth in carbonate reservoirs.

- [[2017-ahmed-2|AHMED, 2017]] calculated the diffusion coefficient (De) for both emulsified systems at HPHT.

- [[2022-alkathim|Alkathim, 2022]] investigated how higher diffusion coefficients affected acid consumption and PVBT.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2014-khalid|Khalid, 2014]] Calculated from the 'F' function vs. square root of rotation speed.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The diffusion coefficient obtained was 2.83 x 10^-6 cm^2/sec. ([[2014-khalid|Khalid, 2014, p. 83]])


- De values are significantly lower at high pressure and temperature compared to low-pressure conditions. ([[2017-ahmed-2|AHMED, 2017, p. 14]])

- Higher diffusion coefficient leads to higher acid consumption at low injection rates but lower PVBT at high injection rates. ([[2022-alkathim|Alkathim, 2022, p. 97]])
