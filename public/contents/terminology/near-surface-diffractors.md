# Near Surface Diffractors

**Summary**: Geological features near the surface that scatter seismic waves, creating complex signals.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2009-al-lehyani|Al-Lehyani, 2009]] studied the impact of near-surface diffractors on surface wave energy and signal quality.

## Scientific Context

- [[2009-al-lehyani|Al-Lehyani, 2009]] They are a primary source of noise in land seismic exploration, complicating wave suppression schemes.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2009-al-lehyani|Al-Lehyani, 2009]] Identified via T-X relations and semblance maps.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Near-surface diffractors manifest as strong hyperbolic events (diffractions) that mask weak reflected body waves. ([[2009-al-lehyani|Al-Lehyani, 2009, p. 83]])
