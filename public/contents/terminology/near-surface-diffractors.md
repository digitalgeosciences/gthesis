# Near-Surface Diffractors

**Summary**: Seismic signals produced by waves hitting objects or boundaries in the shallow subsurface.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2009-al-lehyani|Al-Lehyani, 2009]] studied near-surface diffractors that mask deeper reflections in land seismic surveys.

## Scientific Context

- [[2009-al-lehyani|Al-Lehyani, 2009]] They create complex noise patterns that can mask primary reflections from deeper targets.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2009-al-lehyani|Al-Lehyani, 2009]] Identified via semblance mapping and attenuated via least-squares fitting.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- These features manifest as strong hyperbolic events on shot gathers, complicating surface wave suppression. ([[2009-al-lehyani|Al-Lehyani, 2009, p. 10]])
