# Transverse Isotropy

**Summary**: A type of anisotropy where properties are symmetric about a specific axis, common in layered sedimentary rocks.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-own|Own, 2015]] evaluated transverse-isotropy to correct for velocity discrepancies caused by rock layering and fracturing.

## Scientific Context

- [[2015-own|Own, 2015]] Essential for accurate microseismic location in shales and other layered formations.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2015-own|Own, 2015]] Determined via VSP and advanced sonic tools to calculate Thomsen parameters.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The use of an anisotropic initial velocity model reduced the misfit between observed and modeled times significantly compared to isotropic models. ([[2015-own|Own, 2015, p. 94]])
