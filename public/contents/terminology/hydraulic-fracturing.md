# Hydraulic Fracturing

**Summary**: The process of injecting high-pressure fluids to create fractures in rock.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Hydraulic fracturing is a key technology for developing unconventional reservoirs like the Qusaiba Member. ([[2015-own|Own, 2015, p. 7]])

- Accurate estimation of these properties prevents 'hit or miss' fractures, making fracking more cost-effective. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 19]])

- Microseismic data was used to estimate fracture geometry and impact on drainage. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 210]])

- The addition of chelating agents can mitigate the negative effects of high-salinity and high-hardness content on frack fluids. ([[2021-alkhowaildi|Alkhowaildi, 2021, p. 34]])

- Hardening surfaces can mitigate proppant embedment and acid-related damage. ([[2022-samarkin|Samarkin, 2022, p. 12]])

- The study highlights the use of ionic liquids as robust alternatives to surfactants in fracking fluids under harsh conditions. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 14]])
