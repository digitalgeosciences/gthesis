# Hydraulic Fracturing

**Summary**: The process of injecting high-pressure fluids to create fractures in rock.

---

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Hydraulic fracturing creates a complex network that enhances connectivity between pores, but its effectiveness must be validated via microseismic monitoring. ([[2015-own|Own, 2015, p. 20]])


- Fracture half-length was the most influential parameter (33%) on cumulative production. ([[2015-rammay|RAMMAY, 2015, p. 31]])

- Frackability is more useful than brittleness for determining fracture gradient and suitability for multi-stage fracking. ([[2016-lawal|Lawal, 2016, p. 44]])

- Hydraulic Fracturing is considered one of the most important stimulation methods. ([[2017-mohammed-3|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 20]])

- The study highlights several types of fluids, including slickwater, linear gel, and cross-linked gels, each with specific advantages for different reservoir conditions. ([[2017-muqtadir|Muqtadir, 2017, p. 40]])

- Fracture half-length was found to be a primary driver for production volume compared to other factors. ([[2018-aleid|Aleid, 2018, p. 66]])

- Stress contrast between pay zones and adjacent layers determines if fractures are contained or propagate into other zones. ([[2018-aldossary|AlDossary, 2018, p. 67]])

- Hardening the fracture surface can mitigate proppant embedment and ensure long-term conductivity. ([[2022-samarkin|Samarkin, 2022, p. 15]])

- Referenced in this thesis. ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023]])

- The study highlights that x3 is a promising candidate for reducing IFT and improving flowback in high-temperature/salinity conditions. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 40]])

- Developed a system that overcomes high salinity issues, including scale, compatibility, and stability. ([[2023-othman|Othman, 2023, p. 19]])

- Identifying two distinct phases at the nanoscale is crucial for understanding response to forces in hydraulic fracturing. ([[2024-alvayed|Alvayed, 2024, p. 52]])

- Resin coating is a cost-effective method to improve the performance of sand proppants by reducing crushing and ensuring better stress distribution. ([[2024-suleiman-5|Suleiman, 2024, p. 44]])

- Hydraulic fracturing increased the PI by up to 3.6 times compared to non-fractured wells below the dew point pressure. ([[2000-hashmi|Hashmi, 2000, p. 22]])
