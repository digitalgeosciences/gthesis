# Transmissity

**Summary**: A measure of the ability of an aquifer to transmit water, expressed in m^2/sec.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993]] analyzed transmissivity values to assess the productivity of various wells within the Baidoa aquifer.

## Scientific Context

- [[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993]] Transmissivity is critical for determining the volume of water that can be extracted from an aquifer and its response to pumping.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993]] Calculated using Jacob's equation from drawdown data during a pumping test.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The well at Sarman Dheere (Well 10) exhibited a high transmissivity of 2.52 x 10^7 m^2/sec due to secondary permeability from karstification. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 88]])
