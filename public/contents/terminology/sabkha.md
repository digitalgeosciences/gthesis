# Sabkha

**Summary**: Salty playas or closed evaporation pans that act as natural discharge zones for groundwater.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[1995-hasan|Hasan, 1995]] identified sabkhahs in the study area as critical indicators of groundwater discharge from the Neogene aquifer.

## Scientific Context

- [[1995-hasan|Hasan, 1995]] They are used to estimate the amount of discharge from the Neogene aquifer and represent a significant loss of water volume.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[1995-hasan|Hasan, 1995]] Identified via remote sensing (LANDSAT-TM) based on unique spectral characteristics.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Sabkhahs are formed in elongated depressions of structural origin, trending north to northeast, and serve as natural discharge zones. ([[1995-hasan|Hasan, 1995, p. 45]])
