# Immiscible Displacement

**Summary**: A displacement process where the two fluids remain as separate phases.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2013-al-mutairi|AL-MUTAIRI, 2013]] modeled immiscible-displacement of CO2 and oil in carbonate reservoirs.

## Scientific Context

- [[2013-al-mutairi|AL-MUTAIRI, 2013]] Occurs when the Minimum Miscibility Pressure (MMP) is above the reservoir pressure.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2013-al-mutairi|AL-MUTAIRI, 2013]] determined by pressure and temperature conditions.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Immiscible CO2 flooding has potential for recovering moderately viscous oils in deep or thin formations. ([[2013-al-mutairi|AL-MUTAIRI, 2013, p. 31]])
