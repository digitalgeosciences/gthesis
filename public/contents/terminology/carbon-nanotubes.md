# Carbon Nanotubes

**Summary**: Cylindrical molecules composed of rolled-up sheets of graphene.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2009-al-khaldi|Al-Khaldi, 2009]] used multi-walled carbon nanotubes as the primary adsorbent for heavy metal removal.

- [[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013]] used carbon-nanotubes as a support for TiO2 to create a composite material.

- [[2015-khan-2|Khan, 2015]] investigated the effect of Carbon Nanotubes (CNT) on cement performance in HPHT environments.

## Scientific Context

## Key Findings Across Sources

- MWCNTs have a diameter range of 2 to 30 nm and a spacing between graphene sheets of 0.34 nm. ([[2009-al-khaldi|Al-Khaldi, 2009, p. 40]])


- MWCNTs showed higher removal rates than the TiO2-MWCNT composite, likely due to their inherent adsorption properties. ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013, p. 48]])

- CNTs are reported to have moduli of elasticity exceeding 1 TPa. ([[2015-khan-2|Khan, 2015, p. 51]])
