# Fracture Conductivity

**Summary**: The product of fracture width and permeability.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023]] measured fracture conductivity to evaluate the effectiveness of acid treatments.

## Scientific Context

- [[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023]] It is a key metric for determining if an acid treatment successfully created a durable flow path.

- [[2024-al-drwish|AL-DRWISH, 2024]] measured fracture conductivity to compare foamed acid against conventional treatments.

- [[1996-al-hajji|Al-Hajji, 1996]] evaluated the impact of finite vs. infinite fracture conductivity on well performance.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023]] Calculated from pressure drop and flow rate data using the formula kwf = (q * mu * deltaP) / (mu * deltaP)

## Key Findings Across Sources

- GLDA treatment resulted in a fracture conductivity approximately 20 times higher than before stimulation. ([[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023, p. 43]])
- [[2023-alabdrabalnabi|ALABDRABALNABI, 2023]] Varies by treatment; GLDA showed significant improvement.

- Foamed acid significantly improved fracture conductivity compared to conventional acid treatments. ([[2024-al-drwish|AL-DRWISH, 2024, p. 30]])

- For low values (F_c < 10), finite conductivity multiple fractures are preferred over single ones if anisotropy is present. ([[1996-al-hajji|Al-Hajji, 1996, p. 15]])
