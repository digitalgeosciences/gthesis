# Surface Tension

**Summary**: The property of a liquid surface that allows it to resist tension; in this context, the interface between gas and brine.

---

- [[2024-muhammed|Muhammed, 2024]] measured surface tension to evaluate the impact of cushion gas on fluid-fluid interaction.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Surface tension was found to be more sensitive to pressure and temperature than contact angle due to its dependence on density differences. ([[2024-muhammed|Muhammed, 2024, p. 114]])
- [[2024-muhammed|Muhammed, 2024]] 62.8 to 45.3 dynes/cm (example)

- Surface tension for produced water was reduced from 68.66 mN/m to 30 mN/m when Duomeen TTM was added. ([[2024-al-drwish|AL-DRWISH, 2024, p. 79]])

- The CMC values were 0.021 g/l for V2880 and 0.043 g/l for B1083. ([[1987-fuseni|Fuseni, 1987, p. 71]])
