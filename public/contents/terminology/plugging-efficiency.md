# Plugging Efficiency

**Summary**: A measure of how effectively a treatment material blocks flow in a specific zone.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2024-suleiman-4|Suleiman, 2024]] measured plugging efficiency to compare the performance of nano-silica and PAM/PEI gels.

## Scientific Context

- [[2024-suleiman-4|Suleiman, 2024]] Crucial for determining the success of water shut-off treatments in high permeability zones.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2024-suleiman-4|Suleiman, 2024]] Calculated from coreflooding data comparing pre-treatment and post-treatment flow rates.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Nano-silica gel achieved 99.98% to 100% plugging efficiency across different sample types after 6 months. ([[2024-suleiman-4|Suleiman, 2024, p. 20]])
