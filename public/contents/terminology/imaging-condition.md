# Imaging Condition

**Summary**: A mathematical operation used to extract the reflectivity from the wavefield at a specific depth.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-mohammed|Mohammed, 2015]] applied cross-correlation and deconvolution imaging conditions to improve resolution in prestack migration.

## Scientific Context

- [[2015-mohammed|Mohammed, 2015]] Essential for prestack imaging to ensure accurate amplitude representation.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2015-mohammed|Mohammed, 2015]] Implemented as cross-correlation or deconvolution in the frequency domain.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Deconvolution imaging condition overcomes the lack of accuracy in cross-correlation by using an illumination weighting function. ([[2015-mohammed|Mohammed, 2015, p. 53]])
