# Critical Salt Concentration

**Summary**: The concentration of salt at which a colloidal system becomes unstable and begins to aggregate.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2016-alhamad|AlHamad, 2016]] used the concept of critical salt concentration (CSC) to explain why divalent ions caused rapid precipitation.

## Scientific Context

- [[2016-alhamad|AlHamad, 2016]] Explains why different ions have varying impacts on nanoparticle stability.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2016-alhamad|AlHamad, 2016]] determined by observing the point of rapid aggregation in various salt solutions

## Key Findings Across Sources

- NaCl has a CSC of 15 kppm, while MgCl2 and CaCl2 have much lower CSCs (0.125 and 0.25 kppm). ([[2016-alhamad|AlHamad, 2016, p. 55]])
