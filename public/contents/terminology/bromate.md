# Bromate

**Summary**: A chemical compound that is a potential carcinogen formed during the oxidation of bromide-containing water.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2009-zubair|Zubair, 2009]] studied bromate formation as a critical byproduct in ozone-based AOP treatment of Saudi Arabian groundwater.

- [[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011]] monitored bromate formation as a result of ozone treatment.

## Scientific Context

- [[2009-zubair|Zubair, 2009]] Bromate is a major concern in drinking water treatment when bromide levels are high.

- [[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011]] monitored bromate formation as a result of ozone treatment.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2009-zubair|Zubair, 2009]] Analytical measurement following ozonation processes.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- A pH level of 6.0 and an ammonia dosage of 1.5 ppm were found to be the optimum conditions to prevent its formation. ([[2009-zubair|Zubair, 2009, p. 13]])

- Bromate levels increased with higher ozone dosage, temperature, and contact time. ([[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011, p. 9]])

## Contrasting Values and Interpretations

- [[2009-zubair|Zubair, 2009]] Concentrations can range from less than 1 ppb to over 150 ppb.
