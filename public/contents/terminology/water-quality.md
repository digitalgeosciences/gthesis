## Key Findings Across Sources

- Focused on removing contaminants and by-products from limited water resources. ([[2009-zubair|Zubair, 2009, p. 120]])

- Removal of Arsenic (As) and Mercury (Hg) is essential to ensure safe drinking water. ([[2009-al-khaldi|Al-Khaldi, 2009, p. 14]])

- Saudi Arabian groundwater is high in bromide content, making the potential for forming bromate during treatment a significant concern for water quality. ([[2011-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2011, p. 21]])

- The study found that salinity is the principal concern for groundwater quality in the area. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 16]])

- Not stated in source ([[2013-muhyedeen|Muhyedeen, 2013]])

- Bentonite can form a viscous gel in fresh water, which kills fish by inhibiting gill action. ([[2012-attyah|Attyah, 2012, p. 87]])

- Water quality is a critical concern in EDC accidents due to long-term impacts on human health. ([[2014-abdallah|Abdallah, 2014, p. 48]])

- MTBE is a significant contaminant that makes water undrinkable due to taste and odor. ([[2014-adebayo|Adebayo, 2014, p. 13]])

- Heavy metals like Ni, Co, Cd, and Cr were identified as significant pollutants affecting water quality. ([[2015-musa|Musa, 2015, p. 15]])

- Salinity (TDS) levels of 0, 1823, and 3645 mg/L were tested to determine their effect on plant performance. ([[2015-bello|Bello, 2015, p. 73]])

- Chlorophyll a concentrations were significantly high only in Dammam at levels indicative of poor water quality. ([[2015-yusuf|Yusuf, 2015, p. 14]])

- The determination of the detection limit of Hg (II) ions using unmodified AgNPs was performed to ensure selectivity. ([[2016-salawu|Salawu, 2016, p. 71]])

- Salinity levels from 1250 to 5000 ppm significantly impacted the removal efficiency of Toluene. ([[2016-sameh|Sameh, 2016, p. 17]])

- MTBE removal efficiency reached 70% under optimal conditions (pH 6, 150 rpm). ([[2018-adeyemi|Adeyemi, 2018, p. 12]])
