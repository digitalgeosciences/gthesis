## Key Findings Across Sources

- Sea water was found to be incompatible with connate water due to high sulfate content leading to scale. ([[1990-al-mumen|Al-Mumen, 1990, p. 103]])


- Saudi Arabian groundwater is high in bromide content, making it susceptible to bromate formation during treatment. ([[2009-zubair|Zubair, 2009, p. 13]])

- Removal of heavy metals is a critical step in ensuring safe drinking water. ([[2009-al-khaldi|Al-Khaldi, 2009, p. 55]])

- Deep groundwater is not suitable for drinking purposes due to high concentrations of several contaminants. ([[2011-alsadeq|Alsadeq, 2011, p. 100]])

- Removal efficiency was highest at pH 3 for Ag2O-FA, but study focused on groundwater conditions (pH 6). ([[2014-adebayo|Adebayo, 2014, p. 99]])

- The resin successfully removed arsenic (As) from industrial wastewater to below detection limits. ([[2015-musa|Musa, 2015, p. 100]])

- Naphthalene was used as a target organic pollutant while cadmium, lead, and nickel were used as inorganic pollutants. ([[2015-bello|Bello, 2015, p. 14]])

- Chlorophyll a concentrations were significantly high only in Dammam at levels indicative of poor water quality. ([[2015-yusuf|Yusuf, 2015, p. 14]])

- Mercury is highly toxic even at low concentrations (US EPA limit of 2 ppb). ([[2016-salawu|Salawu, 2016, p. 44]])

- Cadmium levels in drinking water are strictly regulated (3-5 ppb) due to toxicity. ([[2017-olabemiwo|Olabemiwo, 2017, p. 20]])

- In real groundwater samples, adsorption efficiency dropped from 80% to 49% for phenol. ([[2019-salawudeen|Salawudeen, 2019, p. 16]])

- 93.6% of samples were classified as poor, very poor, or unsuitable for drinking purposes based on WQI. ([[2019-ahmed-2|Ahmed, 2019, p. 45]])

- High electrical conductivities (21,700 μS/cm and 43,300 μS/cm) drastically influenced the removal of ammonia. ([[2020-ghazwani|Ghazwani, 2020, p. 37]])

- 22% of samples were very poor and 36% were unsuitable for drinking due to high concentrations of various parameters. ([[2020-mansouri|Mansouri, 2020, p. 124]])

- PG was found to be highly effective in removing methylene blue and metals simultaneously across a wide range of conditions. ([[2021-alnasser|AlNasser, 2021, p. 19]])

- Arsenic is a Class-I carcinogen with threshold limits set to 10 μg L⁻¹ for drinking water. ([[2021-siddiq|Siddiq, 2021, p. 14]])

- The process achieved a 93% COD removal rate after 4 hours of electrolysis. ([[2023-al-aqeel|Al-Aqeel, 2023, p. 76]])

- Optimal removal was achieved at a conductivity of 33850 μS/cm for MTBE. ([[2025-almousa|Almousa, 2025, p. 53]])
