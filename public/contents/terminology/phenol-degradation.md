# Phenol Degradation

**Summary**: The chemical breakdown of phenol molecules into simpler compounds.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2009-zubair|Zubair, 2009]] evaluated the degradation of phenol using various ozone-based AOP techniques.

- [[2015-hassan|Hassan, 2015]] studied the degradation of phenol in water using organic dye photocatalysts.

## Scientific Context

- [[2009-zubair|Zubair, 2009]] Phenol is a common organic contaminant that must be removed from drinking water.

- [[2015-hassan|Hassan, 2015]] studied the degradation of phenol in water using organic dye photocatalysts.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2009-zubair|Zubair, 2009]] Measured via HPLC analysis of samples taken at specific time intervals.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Continuous ozonation at 1 L/min achieved 100% phenol removal in less than 5 minutes. ([[2009-zubair|Zubair, 2009, p. 60]])

- Phenol is a priority pollutant requiring removal from water sources due to its toxicity and endocrine disruption. ([[2015-hassan|Hassan, 2015, p. 61]])
