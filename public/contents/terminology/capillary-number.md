# Capillary Number

**Summary**: A dimensionless number representing the ratio of viscous forces to capillary forces.

---

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[1990-al-mumen|Al-Mumen, 1990]] Calculated from flow velocity, viscosity, and IFT.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Sor decreased as the Capillary Number increased, which was achieved by lowering IFT or increasing velocity. ([[1990-al-mumen|Al-Mumen, 1990, p. 78]])


- An increase in Capillary number results in lowering of residual oil saturation. ([[2014-azad|Azad, 2014, p. 38]])

- Reducing the oil viscosity or decreasing the interfacial tension (IFT) increases the capillary number, and decreases the residual oil saturation. ([[2017-janjua|Janjua, 2017, p. 32]])

- Increasing the capillary number by reducing IFT allows for the mobilization of trapped oil. ([[2023-hajiyev|Hajiyev, 2023, p. 18]])

- Residual oil saturation typically only drops significantly above a critical threshold (10^-4 to 10^-6). ([[2023-dafaalla|Dafaalla, 2023, p. 32]])
