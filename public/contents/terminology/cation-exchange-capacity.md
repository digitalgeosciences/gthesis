# Cation Exchange Capacity

**Summary**: A measure of the ability of certain ions to adsorb onto and exchange with neighboring ions between layers of silicate-group minerals.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017]] measured CEC to quantify the reactivity of the shale samples.

## Scientific Context

- [[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017]] High CEC is directly linked to the presence of swelling clays like smectite.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017]] CEC test using various salt solutions.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Samples B and D had high CEC values (15 and 13.5 meq/100g), indicating higher reactivity due/to high clay content. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 125]])
