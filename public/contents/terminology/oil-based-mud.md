# Oil Based Mud

**Summary**: A drilling fluid that uses oil as the continuous phase, often used in high-temperature and high-pressure environments.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2021-alsabaa|Alsabaa, 2021]] focused on five types of oil-based mud (OBM) including all-oil, invert emulsion, high-overbalance, flat-rheology, and high-density.

## Scientific Context

- [[2021-alsabaa|Alsabaa, 2021]] Used for drilling in unstable formations like shale and in high-pressure/high-temperature (HPHT) wells.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2021-alsabaa|Alsabaa, 2021]] Analyzed via rheological properties such as PV, YP, and n.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Oil-based mud is divided into two main classes: all-oil and invert emulsion; the latter can contain up to 60% water. ([[2021-alsabaa|Alsabaa, 2021, p. 21]])
