# Gelation

**Summary**: The chemical process of forming a gel, where a liquid becomes a solid-like material.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[1994-muhammad|Muhammad, 1994]] studied the gelation process to determine how concentration and temperature affect the time required for transition.

- [[1997-al-nashmi|Al-Nashmi, 1997]] studied the gelation time as the primary parameter for determining the suitability of the treatment.

- [[2024-alabdrabalnabi|Alabdrabalnabi, 2024]] modeled the gelation kinetics using an Arrhenius-based equation to predict the time required for the fluid to solidify.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Gelation is defined as the transformation from a liquid state to a solid-like state. ([[1994-muhammad|Muhammad, 1994, p. 43]])


- Gelation is defined as the point where an abrupt increase in viscosity occurs due. to chemical reaction between polymer, crosslinker, and retarder. ([[1997-al-nashmi|Al-Nashmi, 1997, p. 84]])

- At 20 wt.% activator, gelation was poor due to insufficient concentration; at 30% and 40%, it was successful but sensitive to temperature drops. ([[2024-alabdrabalnabi|Alabdrabalnabi, 2024, p. 103]])
