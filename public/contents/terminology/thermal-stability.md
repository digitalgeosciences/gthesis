# Thermal Stability

**Summary**: The ability of an emulsion to remain intact without phase separation when subjected to high temperatures.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2016-sidaoui|Sidaoui, 2016]] evaluated the thermal stability of waste oil emulsions at 120°C for a period of 6 hours.

## Scientific Context

- [[2016-sidaoui|Sidaoui, 2016]] Crucial for ensuring the acid remains in an emulsified state during deep well treatment.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2016-sidaoui|Sidaoui, 2016]] Monitored volume of three phases (emulsion, water, oil) over 6 hours at 120°C.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Emulsions with 1.5 vol% emulsifier were the most stable, but all tested formulations broke down within 3 hours at 120°C. ([[2016-sidaoui|Sidaoui, 2016, p. 123]])
