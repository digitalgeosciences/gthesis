# Surfactant

**Summary**: A substance that reduces surface tension between two liquids or a liquid and a solid.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2019-abdelaal|Abdelaal, 2019]] used AOS as a surfactant to stabilize the CO2/N2 foam.

- [[2021-altowilib|Altowilib, 2021]] evaluated different surfactant types (non-ionic vs. anionic) in demulsifier formulations.

## Scientific Context

- [[2019-abdelaal|Abdelaal, 2019]] Essential for creating and stabilizing foam in the presence of oil and brine.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- AOS was found to be capable of generating stable foam in high-salinity environments. ([[2019-abdelaal|Abdelaal, 2019, p. 108]])


- Non-ionic surfactants showed better resistance to salinity, polarity, and water hardness compared to anionic ones. ([[2021-altowilib|Altowilib, 2021, p. 55]])
