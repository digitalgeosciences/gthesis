# Ground Roll

**Summary**: A type of coherent surface wave that travels horizontally and is often undesirable in seismic data.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2018-putra|Putra, 2018]] identified ground-roll as a primary noise source to be attenuated by the array.

## Scientific Context

- [[2018-putra|Putra, 2018]] A common noise source in land seismic surveys that masks deeper reflections.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2018-putra|Putra, 2018]] Not measured directly, but attenuated by array geometry.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Arrays are used specifically to attenuate these waves because they have lower horizontal velocity than reflections. ([[2018-putra|Putra, 2018, p. 21]])
