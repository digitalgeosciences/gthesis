# Carbonation

**Summary**: The chemical reaction between CO2 and minerals in cement, leading to the formation of calcium carbonate.

---

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Carbonation leads to the dissolution of portlandite (CH) and conversion of C-S-H into amorphous silica gel, increasing porosity. ([[2020-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2020, p. 36]])


- The precipitation of carbonate minerals is a significant aspect of CO2 sequestration as it can contribute to the long-term immobilization of CO2. ([[2024-badhafere|BADHAFERE, 2024, p. 31]])

- Carbonate mudrocks undergo significant mineralogical changes during interaction with CO2. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 10]])

- Optimal conditions for carbonation included high pressure, low pH, and magnesium-rich rock. ([[2025-alghawi|Alghawi, 2025, p. 16]])
