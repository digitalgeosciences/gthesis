# Pressure Derivative

**Summary**: The derivative of pressure with respect to time, used as a primary tool for diagnosing well-test models.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2001-sultan|SULTAN, 2001]] analyzed the pressure-derivative to identify flow regimes and reservoir characteristics.

## Scientific Context

- [[2001-sultan|SULTAN, 2001]] It is the primary tool for diagnosing well-test models and identifying flow regimes.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2001-sultan|SULTAN, 2001]] Calculated from pressure and production rate data.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Pressure derivative plots are more sensitive than log-log or semilog graphs for identifying events like skin or boundaries. ([[2001-sultan|SULTAN, 2001, p. 38]])
