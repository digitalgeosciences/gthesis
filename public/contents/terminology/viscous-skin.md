# Viscous Skin

**Summary**: A term used to describe the additional pressure drop caused by the difference in viscosity between two fluids (e.g., acid and gas).

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2019-alawi|ALAWI, 2019]] quantified a viscous skin to subtract from total skin to determine true treatment performance.

## Scientific Context

- [[2019-alawi|ALAWI, 2019]] Used to differentiate between effects of acid volume and the physical properties of the fluid mixture.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2019-alawi|ALAWI, 2019]] Calculated using the ratio of viscosities and log-ratios of radii.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The viscous skin factor was calculated using equations derived from fluid properties and geometry. ([[2019-alawi|ALAWI, 2019, p. 30]])
