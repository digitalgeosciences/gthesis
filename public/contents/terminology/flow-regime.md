# Flow Regime

**Summary**: A specific type of flow behavior (e.g., radial, linear, bilinear) occurring in a well during a test.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2001-sultan|SULTAN, 2001]] identified various flow regimes to determine the reservoir's physical characteristics.

- [[2018-al-dogail|Al-Dogail, 2018]] analyzed flow regimes to determine how fluid properties influence boundary transitions.

- [[2006-adebowale|ADEBOWALE, 2006]] investigated the appearance of flow regimes in both wells as a function of well length.

## Scientific Context

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2001-sultan|SULTAN, 2001]] Identified via analysis of the derivative plot.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Identifying and marking the position of these regions on the derivative plot is critical for accurate parameter estimation. ([[2001-sultan|SULTAN, 2001, p. 29]])


- Identified five main categories: stratified, intermittent, annular, dispersed bubble, and mixed flow. ([[2018-al-dogail|Al-Dogail, 2018, p. 21]])

- Flow regimes are influenced by the geometry and dimensions of the horizontal wells. ([[2006-adebowale|ADEBOWALE, 2006, p. 57]])
