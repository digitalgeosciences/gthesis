# Rock Mechanics

**Summary**: The mechanical strength of rock, often measured as Unconfined Compressive Strength.

---

- [[2016-tariq|Tariq, 2016]] evaluated rock mechanical parameters to mitigate risks in drilling and maximize production.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Rock strengths in the AG formation ranged from 8000 psi to 18000 psi. ([[2014-ahmed|Ahmed, 2014, p. 59]])


- Reported a reduction in Young's modulus and tensile strength post-CO2 treatment. ([[2014-al-ameri|AL-AMERI, 2014, p. 51]])

- Mechanical parameters include static Young's modulus (elastic) and unconfined compressive strength (failure). ([[2016-tariq|Tariq, 2016, p. 22]])

- Elastic moduli were found to be independent of maturity level but sensitive to fluid type. ([[2021-aslanov|Aslanov, 2021, p. 39]])

- Rock compressive strength exceeding 20 kips was noted in abrasive sandstone. ([[2022-al-dahmoush|Al-Dahmoush, 2022, p. 27]])

- Referenced in this thesis. ([[2023-al-garni|Al-Garni, 2023]])

- The injection of live brine creates a wormhole, which weakens the rock strength. ([[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023, p. 15]])
