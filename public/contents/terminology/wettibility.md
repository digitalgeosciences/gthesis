## Key Findings Across Sources

- Wettability is determined by rock mineralogy, pore surface conditions, and the presence of polar components like carboxylic acids. ([[2012-jabbar|JABBAR, 2012, p. 23]])


- Wettibility is the primary factor determining the success of waterflooding in carbonate reservoirs, which are often naturally oil-wet. ([[2012-kassim|KASSIM, 2012, p. 27]])

- A shift from oil-wet (115-180°) to water-wet (0-75°) was observed when using high pH chelating agents. ([[2013-elsayed|Elsayed, 2013, p. 45]])

- Wettibility alteration from oil-wet to intermediate-wet was observed when the system was exposed to carbonated brine. ([[2013-al-mutairi|AL-MUTAIRI, 2013, p. 19]])

- Carbonate rocks are usually oil-wet; therefore, considerable amount of oil remains adhered to the rock surface. ([[2014-shakeel|SHAKEEL, 2014, p. 12]])

- Increasing the pH of injected water results in increasing the stability of the water film, moving toward more water-wet conditions. ([[2015-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2015, p. 36]])

- Zeta potential value for sandstone rocks with seawater becomes more negative after the sandstone being treated with DTPA-K5 – indicating a more water wet surface. ([[2015-alarifi|Alarifi, 2015, p. 103]])

- Wettibility alteration can be triggered by double layer expansion, multi-ion exchange, or pH-induced desorption. ([[2016-adam|ADAM, 2016, p. 26]])

- A Wittensity Indicator (WI) was introduced; values less than 1 indicate oil-wet systems and greater than 1 indicate water-wet systems. ([[2016-mahboob|MAHBOOB, 2016, p. 21]])

- Two weeks thermal aging is enough to restore the original wettibility for calcite rock discs while one week was too short. ([[2017-ahmed-3|Ahmed, 2017, p. 18]])

- Wettability determines key parameters like relative permeability, residual oil saturation, and capillary pressure. ([[2019-al-garadi|AL-GARADI, 2019, p. 19]])

- Contact angle measurements confirmed that the addition of specific ions like Iodide significantly promoted the transition from oil-wet back to water-wet. ([[2020-opoku|Opoku, 2020, p. 18]])

- Carbonated water injection caused the contact angle to change from weakly oil-wet to intermediate or water-wet. ([[2021-alqam|ALQAM, 2021, p. 42]])

- Gemini surfactants have been proven to be effective in changing oil-wet surface to water-wet in both sandstone and carbonate rocks. ([[2022-xiao|Xiao, 2022, p. 72]])

- Wettibility controls capillary forces and the relative movement of fluids, significantly impacting oil recovery. ([[2023-abdulwahab|Abdulwahab, 2023, p. 17]])

- AU sample transitioned from neutral-wet to water-wet after acid treatment. ([[2023-pratama|PRATAMA, 2023, p. 75]])

- The shift from an initial oil-wet state to an intermediate or water-wet condition was evidenced by changes in contact angle and T2 distribution. ([[2024-al-azani|Al-Azani, 2024, p. 185]])

- Even minimal concentrations of organic acid can significantly increase the hydrophobicity of rock surfaces, leading to higher gas retention. ([[2024-suleiman-2|Suleiman, 2024, p. 39]])

- Wettability plays a vital role in containment, trapping, and capillary pressure characteristics of CO2 in the storage reservoir. ([[2024-suleiman-3|Suleiman, 2024, p. 20]])

- Anhydrite influences the surface charge at the rock/fluid interface and increases water wetness in carbonates. ([[2024-isah|Isah, 2024, p. 17]])

- Both the surfactant-glycerol solution and the viscoelastic polymer solution demonstrated superior wettibility alteration compared to DI water. ([[2024-jameel|Jameel, 2024, p. 80]])

- All five core samples were found to have neutral (intermediate) wettibility. ([[1995-hussain|Hussain, 1995, p. 57]])
