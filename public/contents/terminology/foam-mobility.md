# Foam Mobility

**Summary**: The use of foam to reduce the mobility of gas or water in a reservoir.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2013-geri|Geri, 2013]] evaluated foam as an alternative to polymer for mobility control in carbonate reservoirs.

- [[2013-sibaweihi|Sibaweihi, 2013]] evaluated foam mobility as a function of surfactant amount to balance oil recovery and CO2 storage.

## Scientific Context

- [[2013-geri|Geri, 2013]] Foam provides resistance to gas flow by increasing apparent viscosity and creating lamellae.

- [[2013-sibaweihi|Sibaweihi, 2013]] evaluated foam mobility as a function of surfactant amount to balance oil recovery and CO2 storage.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2013-geri|Geri, 2013]] measured via pressure drop across core samples during injection.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Lower gas velocity results in more stable foam and thus better foam propagation. ([[2013-geri|Geri, 2013, p. 31]])


- Higher surfactant amounts reduced gas mobility, which was necessary for CO2 storage but increased costs; 0.005 lb/stb was the optimal balance. ([[2013-sibaweihi|Sibaweihi, 2013, p. 67]])
