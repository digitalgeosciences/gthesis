# Archies Equation

**Summary**: An empirical equation used to relate the resistivity of a rock to its porosity and water saturation.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2009-al-gathe|AL-GATHE, 2009]] used Archie's equation as the basis for determining m, n, and a parameters.

- [[2009-al-gathe-2|AL-GATHE, 2009]] applied archies-equation to determine water saturation in carbonate rocks.

## Scientific Context

- [[2009-al-gathe|AL-GATHE, 2009]] Standard model for calculating water saturation in and carbonate and clastic reservoirs.

- [[2009-al-gathe-2|AL-GATHE, 2009]] applied archies-equation to determine water saturation in carbonate rocks.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2009-al-gathe|AL-GATHE, 2009]] used to derive m, n, and a parameters from core data.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The accuracy of the equation depends heavily on the correct determination of m, n, and a. ([[2009-al-gathe|AL-GATHE, 2009, p. 57]])


- Archie's equation is not easy to apply to carbonate rocks because parameters (a, m, n) are functions of pore geometry and clay content. ([[2009-al-gathe-2|AL-GATHE, 2009, p. 20]])
