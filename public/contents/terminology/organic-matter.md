## Key Findings Across Sources

- Jubail harbour had a moderate positive correlation (r = 0.65) between TOC and organotins. ([[2015-akere|Akere, 2015, p. 54]])


- Tuwaiq Mountain Formation average TOC is 4.9%, with local peaks up to 13.17%. ([[2017-mohammed|Mohammed, 2017, p. 58]])

- Lithofacies 3, 5, and 9 showed TOC values between 1.5% and 3.0%. ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 77]])

- The measured TOC was 12% for the carbonate source rocks. ([[2021-lawal|LAWAL, 2021, p. 41]])

- High TOC values were found in lithofacies 4, 7, and 9 under anoxic/dysoxic conditions. ([[2022-septriandi|Septriandi, 2022, p. 24]])

- TOC values showed a 38% to 62% drop in unpreserved high-maturity cores compared to preserved ones. ([[2023-al-eid|Al-Eid, 2023, p. 92]])

- A reduction in TOC was observed over time, indicating active biodegradation by indigenous bacteria. ([[2024-almutawa|AlMutawa, 2024, p. 41]])

- Thermochemical-assisted maturation resulted in a decrease in TOC by 18-42% in the shale samples. ([[2024-moh-ali|Moh-Ali, 2024, p. 13]])

- Eagle Ford shale had ~3% TOC, while Jordan Carbonate had ~16% TOC. ([[2024-suleiman-6|Suleiman, 2024, p. 44]])

- Variations in organic content significantly influence the isotopic and petrophysical responses of mudrocks to CO2. ([[2025-alhubail|Alhubail, 2025, p. 10]])
