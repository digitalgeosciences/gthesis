# Mobility

**Summary**: The ratio of permeability to the dynamic viscosity of a fluid, representing its ability to flow through porous media.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2011-al-dhufairi|AL-DHUFAIRI, 2011]] analyzed mobility profiles to improve geosteering in horizontal wells.

- [[2013-sibaweihi|Sibaweihi, 2013]] used mobility reduction factor (MRF) to evaluate the effectiveness of foam in reducing gas mobility.

## Scientific Context

- [[2011-al-dhufairi|AL-DHUFAIRI, 2011]] Essential for determining well productivity and planning field development in areas with high viscosity variations.

- [[2013-sibaweihi|Sibaweihi, 2013]] used mobility reduction factor (MRF) to evaluate the effectiveness of foam in reducing gas mobility.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2011-al-dhufairi|AL-DHUFAIRI, 2011]] Measured using formation testing tools to provide real-time data on permeability and fluid properties.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Mobility is expressed as k/μ (permeability divided by viscosity) and is critical for identifying reservoir fluids and characterizing tar mats. ([[2011-al-dhufairi|AL-DHUFAIRI, 2011, p. 28]])
- [[2011-al-dhufairi|AL-DHUFAIRI, 2011]] Values ranged from approximately -150 to 175 in some cases.

- High MRF indicates significant pressure drop and successful reduction of gas mobility by the foam. ([[2013-sibaweihi|Sibaweihi, 2013, p. 29]])
