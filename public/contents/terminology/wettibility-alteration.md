# Wettibility Alteration

**Summary**: The change in the degree to which a solid surface is preferentially wetted by one of two immiscible liquids.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2013-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2013]] investigated wettibility-alteration as a result of rock dissolution and chemical treatment.

- [[2013-elsayed|Elsayed, 2013]] investigated wettibility alteration caused by rock dissolution during chelating agent flooding.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Rock dissolution leads to more water-wet conditions, helping to unlock trapped oil in the pore space. ([[2013-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2013, p. 50]])


- Oil recovery increased due to rock dissolution leading to more water-wet conditions. ([[2013-elsayed|Elsayed, 2013, p. 89]])

- Exposing carbonate rock to brine containing CO2 causes alteration of the rock wettibility from an oil-wet to an intermediate-wet state. ([[2013-al-mutairi|AL-MUTAIRI, 2013, p. 132]])

- Wettibility alteration was attributed to changes in electrical charge at the oil/brine and brine/rock interfaces. ([[2015-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2015, p. 54]])

- More wettability alteration can be obtained with minimum cost by using chelating agent solutions instead of low salinity water. ([[2015-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2015, p. 111]])

- Surfactants with one phenyl group in the spacer performed the best that changed oil-wet rock to water-wet conditions. ([[2022-xiao|Xiao, 2022, p. 21]])

- ZIS treatment resulted in a contact angle of approximately 75.7°, shifting the rock surface to intermediate-wet. ([[2024-al-azani|Al-Azani, 2024, p. 95]])

- Anhydrite presence tends to shift the system toward more water-wet states. ([[2024-isah|Isah, 2024, p. 70]])
