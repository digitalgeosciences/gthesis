# Pore Systems

**Summary**: The network of pores and throats within a rock.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Wormhole formation increased connectivity in regions away from the main wormhole. ([[2014-al-ameri|AL-AMERI, 2014, p. 54]])


- Kaolinite can cause pore filling at the entrance to the pore throat, potentially damaging it entirely. ([[2015-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2015, p. 39]])

- Micro-pores were found to be more interconnected than macro-pores in the shale samples. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 136]])

- Some dissolution occurred in the rock, resulting in pore enlargement in certain areas. ([[2017-ahmed-3|Ahmed, 2017, p. 98]])

- Not specifically quantified in this thesis. ([[2019-khan|Khan, 2019]])

- Carbonate rocks have more complicated pore systems than sandstone, making petrophysical correlation difficult. ([[2019-al-garadi|AL-GARADI, 2019, p. 21]])

- Five pore types were identified: intercrystal non-fabric preserved (BC-nfp), intercrystal fabric preserved (BC-fp), intraparticle (WP), fracture, and microporosity. ([[2022-stiawan|Stiawan, 2022, p. 18]])

- Two main types of pore systems were identified: interparticle and intraparticle. ([[2022-abdlmutalib|Abdlmutalib, 2022, p. 24]])

- Dielectric measurements are sensitive to both the pore geometry and the fluid distribution within those pores. ([[2023-abdulwahab|Abdulwahab, 2023, p. 24]])

- In carbonate, 20 psi was sufficient to displace all brine from macropores (> 200 ms T2). ([[2024-suleiman-2|Suleiman, 2024, p. 61]])

- NMR T2 measurements showed that the core exhibits bi-modal pore size distribution. ([[2024-suleiman-4|Suleiman, 2024, p. 120]])

- Micro-CT is used to assess pore geometry on the electrical behavior of rock during fluid interaction. ([[2024-isah|Isah, 2024, p. 42]])

- The pore system predominantly consists of isolated pores with vertical variations in volume, surface area, and equivalent diameter. ([[2025-refaat|Refaat, 2025, p. 20]])

- The sample exhibited a significant heterogeneity characterized by dual pore systems (macro and micropores). ([[2024-jibrin|Jibrin, 2024, p. 42]])
