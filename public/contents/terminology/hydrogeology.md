## Key Findings Across Sources

- Not stated in source ([[1995-al-jaziri|Al-Jaziri, 1995]])


- Petrographic methods provide a viable shortcut for preliminary assessment of hydrostratigraphic units. ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 17]])

- Assessment included recharge, movement, occurrence, discharge, and storage. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 49]])

- Identified the Khobar aquifer as a significant but heavily pumped resource in the Eastern Province. ([[2000-mohammad|Mohammad, 2000, p. 31]])

- The UER is a karstified fractured bedrock aquifer with significant heterogeneity in permeability. ([[2012-iwalewa|Iwalewa, 2012, p. 46]])

- Flow is modeled using modified Darcy equations to account for memory effects. ([[2016-david|DAVID, 2016, p. 49]])

- Karst aquifers are often associated with significant thermal energy sources. ([[2018-elsaieed|ElSaieed, 2018, p. 18]])

- Karst aquifers provide a source of drinking water to almost a quarter of the world's population. ([[2018-jamal|Jamal, 2018, p. 23]])

- Identified UER, Rus, Dammam, Hadrukh, and Hofuf as key aquifers/aquitards. ([[2019-prasetyo|Prasetyo, 2019, p. 22]])

- Groundwater flow is generally towards the east, away from the outcrop area. ([[2019-ahmed-2|Ahmed, 2019, p. 58]])

- The aquifer is a major source for water supply to Al-Madinah Al-Munawwarah. ([[2020-mansouri|Mansouri, 2020, p. 13]])

- The Saq Aquifer is a primary aquifer with excellent porosity and permeability, but shows significant hydraulic head decline due to extraction. ([[2022-karami|Karami, 2022, p. 26]])

- Thermal waters were found to be of meteoric origin, circulating through fault-controlled systems. ([[2023-rafiq|Rafiq, 2023, p. 108]])

- Determined that both inverted and measured values indicated a dry sand dune, suitable for practical application. ([[2024-tarchi|Tarchi, 2024, p. 14]])

- Groundwater in the region is used for irrigation but faces threats from salinity and over-extraction. ([[2024-ahmad|Ahmad, 2024, p. 15]])

- Groundwater salinity ranges between 1,000 and 2,000 mg/l across the study area. ([[2024-ahmed|Ahmed, 2024, p. 39]])

- Soil evaporation was identified as the primary loss mechanism, while deep percolation remained low. ([[2024-prasetyo|Prasetyo, 2024, p. 84]])

- The aquifer is a restricted system with transmissivity values ranging from 0.001 to 0.0062 m2/sec. ([[2025-ahmed|Ahmed, 2025, p. 24]])

- Identified three main aquifers (Neogene, Alat, Khobar) with distinct hydraulic properties. ([[1995-hasan|Hasan, 1995, p. 54]])

- Both aquifers are impacted by seawater intrusion in the northern regions. ([[1992-hassan|Hassan, 1992, p. 116]])
