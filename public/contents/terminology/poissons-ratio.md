# Poisson's Ratio

**Summary**: The ratio of transverse strain to axial strain.

---

- [[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015]] Derived from P and S wave velocities or uniaxial compression tests.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Lithofacies-III had a lower Poisson's ratio (0.25) compared to Lithofacies-I (0.34). ([[2015-mustafa|Mustafa, 2015, p. 125]])


- Austin Chalk samples showed an increase in Poisson's ratio, suggesting they became more ductile after stimulation. ([[2015-barri|Barri, 2015, p. 183]])

- The ANN model for predicting Poisson's ratio achieved an R2 of 0.96 and an AAPE of 1.4 during testing. ([[2016-tariq|Tariq, 2016, p. 108]])

- Poisson's ratio increased from 0.24 (saturated) and stabilized at 0.3 after one day of interaction. ([[2021-serag|SERAG, 2021, p. 55]])

- Poisson's ratio increased for all samples after wormhole generation. ([[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023, p. 88]])

- The relationship between quartz percentage and grain contact percentage for Young's modulus was not significant whilst it was significant with Poisson's ratio. ([[2002-elhassan|ElHassan, 2002, p. 19]])
