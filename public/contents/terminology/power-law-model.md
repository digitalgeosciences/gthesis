# Power Law Model

**Summary**: A mathematical model used to describe the behavior of non-Newtonian fluids.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2024-abdallah|Abdallah, 2024]] applied the power-law model to characterize the shear-thinning behavior of TCF-in-diesel emulsions.

## Scientific Context

- [[2024-abdallah|Abdallah, 2024]] Used to quantify how viscosity changes with flow rates in wellbore applications.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2024-abdallah|Abdallah, 2024]] Determined by fitting experimental data to the equation mu = K * gamma_dot^(n-1).

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The consistency index (K) decreased as temperature increased, which is consistent with the behavior of non-Newtonian fluids. ([[2024-abdallah|Abdallah, 2024, p. 72]])
