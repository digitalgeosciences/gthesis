# Interfacial Tension

**Summary**: The force per unit length existing at the interface between two immiscible liquids.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The increase in oil recovery was found to be supported by a decrease in the IFT as a function of water salinity up to 100,000 mg/l. ([[1990-al-mumen|Al-Mumen, 1990, p. 22]])


- Temperature has a significant effect on the interfacial tension between the aqueous and oleic phases. ([[1995-okasha|OKASHA, 1995, p. 40]])

- Lower IFT can be obtained at high solubility ratio for the optimum salinity concentration. ([[2013-geri|Geri, 2013, p. 16]])

- IFT was observed to decrease as surfactant concentration increased, facilitating oil mobility. ([[2014-jangda|Jangda, 2014, p. 27]])

- Reduction in IFT is a primary goal for chemical EOR to lower the capillary pressure and mobilize trapped oil. ([[2014-azad|Azad, 2014, p. 40]])

- Interfacial tension decreased with the increase in the crude oil API gravity for a given solution. ([[2015-barri|Barri, 2015, p. 113]])

- Addition of 5wt% EDTA in seawater reduced IFT from 20.58 mN/m to 5.29 mN/m. ([[2015-mahmoud|Mahmoud, 2015, p. 23]])

- Increasing the chelating agent concentration leads to lower IFT values. ([[2015-mohammed-2|Mohammed, 2015, p. 15]])

- Determined CMC for FS-51, AOS, and Witcolate to ensure surfactants were injected at their optimal concentrations. ([[2016-siddiqui|Siddiqui, 2016, p. 18]])

- The addition of PVP-stabilized zeolite nanoparticles reduced IFT values compared to standard seawater injection. ([[2016-alhamad|AlHamad, 2016, p. 15]])

- Brine 4 showed the lowest IFT with CO2, making it the preferred base for surfactant studies. ([[2016-mahboob-2|Mahboob, 2016, p. 78]])

- Spent citric acid resulted in 8% more recovery than sea water, confirming IFT reduction due to calcium citrate. ([[2014-alam|ALAM, 2014, p. 18]])

- GLDA is a low IFT fluid, which will reduce the IFT eliminating the Water-Blockage effect. ([[2017-mohammed-3|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 21]])

- Reduction of IFT from 20-30 mN/m to 0.001-0.01 mN/m leads to the lowest residual oil saturation. ([[2017-janjua|Janjua, 2017, p. 43]])

- Rhamnolipid was shown to reduce surface tension from 72 mN/m to 29 mN/m or less. ([[2019-alghamdi|AlGhamdi, 2019, p. 30]])

- Condensate and flue gas systems had lower interfacial tension than crude oil with flue gas. ([[2021-almarri|AlMarri, 2021, p. 16]])

- Spontaneous imbibition results indicate that for oil-wet carbonate rock when wettibility alteration happens, both enhancing wettibility alteration, and alleviating IFT reduction, can increase and accelerate the oil recovery. ([[2022-xiao|Xiao, 2022, p. 21]])

- Lower IFT values (below 10^-3 mN/m) are required to significantly reduce residual oil saturation. ([[2023-hajiyev|Hajiyev, 2023, p. 16]])

- VES reduced the IFT by two orders of magnitude (100 times) compared to waterflooding. ([[2023-ahmed|Ahmed, 2023, p. 24]])

- In the fracturing-based fluid, IFT for x3 stabilized at 6.2 mN/m at 50°C, 7 mN/m at 100°C, and 8.2 mN/m at 90°C. ([[2024-mohamed|Mohamed, 2024, p. 36]])

- The presence of emulsifiers significantly reduced the IFT, indicating their suitability for forming stable emulsions. ([[2024-abdallah|Abdallah, 2024, p. 63]])

- Surfactants can reduce IFT to values below 0.01 dyne/cm. ([[2024-al-azani|Al-Azani, 2024, p. 45]])

- GS's superior ability to reduce interfacial tension enabled rapid nucleation of hydrates. ([[2024-habiburrahman|HABIBURRAHMAN, 2024, p. 152]])

- Surfactant-glycerol solutions were designed to reduce IFT without exhibiting viscoelasticity, while polymer solutions provided no IFT reduction but offered viscoelasticity. ([[2024-jameel|Jameel, 2024, p. 17]])

- A properly formulated ASP slug can reduce the interfacial tension (IFT) by over a hundred fold. ([[1994-eme|Eme, 1994, p. 27]])

- IFT values for ethoxylated sulfonates were significantly lower in the secondary optimal salinity region. ([[1987-fuseni|Fuseni, 1987, p. 23]])
