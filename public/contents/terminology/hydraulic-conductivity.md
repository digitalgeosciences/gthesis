# Hydraulic Conductivity

**Summary**: The ease with which water can move through soil or rock.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995]] estimated hydraulic conductivity from both grain size and pore size data derived from thin sections.

- [[2020-mansouri|Mansouri, 2020]] assessed hydraulic conductivity across the aquifer.

- [[2023-desouky|Desouky, 2023]] measured hydraulic conductivity in both intact and acidized fractures using an API conductivity cell.

- [[2026-fatima|Fatima, 2026]] measured hydraulic conductivity to evaluate the impact of clogging on aquifer performance.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995]] calculated from grain size, pore size, and porosity using empirical coefficients.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Hydraulic conductivity was estimated using various empirical equations, including those by Younger (1992) and Chilingarian (1986). ([[1995-magaji|Magaji, 1995, p. 57]])


- Hydraulic conductivity increases westward, influenced by fracture aperture size and quantity. ([[2020-mansouri|Mansouri, 2020, p. 134]])

- DAP-stiffened samples showed at least a twofold increase in hydraulic conductivity compared to untreated ones. ([[2023-desouky|Desouky, 2023, p. 16]])

- Material D showed higher and more stable hydraulic conductivity compared to Material L under various pumping conditions. ([[2026-fatima|Fatima, 2026, p. 78]])
