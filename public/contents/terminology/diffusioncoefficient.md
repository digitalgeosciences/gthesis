# Diffusioncoefficient

**Summary**: A measure of how quickly ions move through a fluid toward the rock surface.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2014-khalid|Khalid, 2014]] calculated diffusion coefficients for fresh and spent acids at high pressures.

- [[2017-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2017]] calculated diffusion coefficients for GLDA in both fresh and seawater systems.

## Scientific Context

- [[2014-khalid|Khalid, 2014]] Determines the speed and profile of wormhole growth during acidizing.

- [[2017-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2017]] calculated diffusion coefficients for GLDA in both fresh and seawater systems.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2014-khalid|Khalid, 2014]] Calculated from dissolution rates at varying rotation speeds using rotating disk theory.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Diffusion coefficients decreased significantly at higher pressure because CO2 remained in the aqueous phase. ([[2014-khalid|Khalid, 2014, p. 15]])
- [[2014-khalid|Khalid, 2014]] Decreased by 30% in spent systems compared to fresh systems.

- The diffusion coefficient for GLDA/SW was significantly lower than GLDA/DI due to salt-induced resistance. ([[2017-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2017, p. 100]])
