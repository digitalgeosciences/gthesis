# Q Factor

**Summary**: A parameter representing the inverse of the energy loss per cycle in a medium.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-mohammed|Mohammed, 2015]] incorporated the Q-factor into the PSPI extrapolator to compensate for wave attenuation.

## Scientific Context

- [[2015-mohammed|Mohammed, 2015]] Used to quantify and correct for seismic signal degradation due to absorption.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2015-mohammed|Mohammed, 2015]] derived from velocity models where Q = sqrt(velocity) for values > 1500 m/s.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The Q-model was derived where values above 1500 m/s were treated as square roots of velocity, and water layers were assigned infinite Q. ([[2015-mohammed|Mohammed, 2015, p. 71]])
