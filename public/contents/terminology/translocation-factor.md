# Translocation Factor

**Summary**: The ratio of the concentration of a substance in the aerial parts of a plant to its concentration in the roots.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-bello|Bello, 2015]] calculated TF for cadmium, nickel, and lead to assess movement from roots to shoots.

- [[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019]] calculated TF to determine the movement of contaminants from roots to other tissues.

## Scientific Context

- [[2015-bello|Bello, 2015]] Determines if the plant moves contaminants to harvestable parts or keeps them in the root zone.

- [[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019]] calculated TF to determine the movement of contaminants from roots to other tissues.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2015-bello|Bello, 2015]] Calculated as (metal concentration in shoot) / (metal concentration in root).

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Low TF values (0.36-0.43) for all metals indicated that plants primarily sequestered contaminants in their root systems. ([[2015-bello|Bello, 2015, p. 95]])

- The maximum translocation factor (TF) was 7.15 for leaf tissue. ([[2019-alamran|AlAmran, 2019, p. 16]])
