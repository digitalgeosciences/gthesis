# Velocity Modeling

**Summary**: The process of creating a model of the earth's seismic velocity to improve imaging.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2018-mohamed|Mohamed, 2018]] performed velocity modeling using FDelmodc and SWI to refine the starting model for FWI.

- [[2020-abdallah-2|Abdallah, 2020]] used velocity modeling to determine the impact of salt and sedimentary layers on image quality.

- [[2023-alsahwan|AlSahwan, 2023]] used velocity-modeling to create a relationship between P-wave velocity and water saturation.

- [[2023-almarzooq|Almarzooq, 2023]] performed velocity modeling to predict depth and identify hazards ahead of the bit.

## Scientific Context

- [[2018-mohamed|Mohamed, 2018]] Accurate velocity models are critical for overcoming signal degradation caused by complex near-surface geology.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2018-mohamed|Mohamed, 2018]] Iterative inversion and manual picking.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- SWI provided more accurate velocity at top 200m, leading to improved RTM images. ([[2018-mohamed|Mohamed, 2018, p. 46]])

- A high-quality, geologically consistent velocity model is critical for producing accurate images in complex environments like sub-salt areas. ([[2020-abdallah-2|Abdallah, 2020, p. 21]])

- Successfully modeled the expected velocity profile for various saturation levels. ([[2023-alsahwan|AlSahwan, 2023, p. 26]])

- Neural networks can directly map corridor stacks to velocity profiles, bypassing wavelet selection issues. ([[2023-almarzooq|Almarzooq, 2023, p. 19]])
