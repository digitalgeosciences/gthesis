# PAH

**Summary**: Hydrocarbons with two or more fused benzene rings, known for being toxic and persistent in the environment.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-oyehan|Oyehan, 2015]] studied PAH degradation by isolated bacterial strains.

- [[2015-malik|Malik, 2015]] studied the biodegradation of three specific PAHs: naphthalene, phenanthrene, and pyrene.

- [[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018]] measured PAH concentrations in various fractions of Arabian Light Crude Oil and Oil-Based Mud.

## Scientific Context

- [[2015-oyehan|Oyehan, 2015]] PAHs are a major concern due to their toxicity and persistence in the environment.

- [[2015-malik|Malik, 2015]] studied the biodegradation of three specific PAHs: naphthalene, phenanthrene, and pyrene.

- [[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018]] measured PAH concentrations in various fractions of Arabian Light Crude Oil and Oil-Based Mud.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2015-oyehan|Oyehan, 2015]] quantified via GC-MS after SPME extraction

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Isolates BC5 and LB were excellent phenanthrene degraders (95% reduction). ([[2015-oyehan|Oyehan, 2015, p. 18]])


- Naphthalene, phenanthrene, and pyrene are classified as highly persistent, mutagenic, and carcinogenic organic pollutants by the USEPA. ([[2015-malik|Malik, 2015, p. 28]])

- The concentration of total PAHs in WSF was 39.55 ng/ml and in SPP was 345.58 ng/ml. ([[2018-amiruddin|Amiruddin, 2018, p. 17]])
