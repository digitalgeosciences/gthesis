# Surface Active Compound

**Summary**: Compounds that accumulate at the interface between two phases, such as oil and rock.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2020-opoku|Opoku, 2020]] used surface-active model compounds (SAMC) to simulate the behavior of Asphaltenes and Carboxylic Acids in carbonate rocks.

## Scientific Context

- [[2020-opoku|Opoku, 2020]] These compounds are primarily responsible for making carbonate rocks oil-wet, hindering oil recovery.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2020-opoku|Opoku, 2020]] Quantified via HPLC and UV-vis detection of the effluent.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The release of SAMCs from the rock surface was used as a proxy for determining the effectiveness of different brine formulations on wettibility alteration. ([[2020-opoku|Opoku, 2020, p. 18]])
