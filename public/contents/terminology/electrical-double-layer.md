# Electrical Double Layer

**Summary**: A layer of charge that forms at the interface between a conductor and an electrolyte.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2023-abdulwahab|Abdulwahab, 2023]] used the presence or absence of the EDL to distinguish water-wet from oil-wet media.

## Scientific Context

- [[2023-abdulwahab|Abdulwahab, 2023]] The EDL is a primary driver for dielectric dispersion in water-wet rocks.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2023-abdulwahab|Abdulwahab, 2023]] Identified via low-frequency dielectric measurements.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The EDL typically disappears in polar oil-wetting conditions, making it a key indicator for wettibility. ([[2023-abdulwahab|Abdulwahab, 2023, p. 20]])
