# Depositional Environments

**Summary**: The physical setting in which sediments are deposited.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Identified five distinct settings: Sabkha, Tidal flat, Lagoon, Shoal, and Foreshoal. ([[2011-busubait|Busubait, 2011, p. 56]])

- The Saq Sandstone shows a transition from fluviatile to tidal and shallow marine environments. ([[2014-mohammedfaiz|Mohammedfaiz, 2014, p. 51]])

- Identified environments ranging from sabkha to inner-mid ramp skeletal mounds. ([[2016-bashri|Bashri, 2016, p. 97]])

- Identified a range of environments including fluvial, lacustrine shoreface, and fluvio deltaic systems. ([[2016-awad|Awad, 2016, p. 201]])

- Four environments were identified: nearshore lake, glaciolacustrine delta, subglacial tillite, and glaciofluvial outwash. ([[2017-alqubalee|Alqubalee, 2017, p. 20]])

- Evidence points to an open-marine, anoxic to suboxic environment of deposition. ([[2020-usman|Usman, 2020, p. 83]])

- Identified various environments from proximal coarse sand to pro-glacial outwash fan deposits to distal diamictites. ([[2021-alqarni|Alqarni, 2021, p. 31]])

- The Al Wajh Formation is interpreted as an alluvial-braided fluvial fan system. ([[2023-butt|Butt, 2023, p. 64]])

- Identified high-energy fluvial or beach settings for the Red Unit based on cross-stratification. ([[1999-babalola|Babalola, 1999, p. 156]])
