# Non Darcy Flow

**Summary**: Flow behavior in porous media where the pressure drop is not linearly proportional to velocity due to inertial effects.

---

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Non-Darcy flow results in a pressure drop that exceeds Darcy's law at high flow rates. ([[2000-khan|Khan, 2000, p. 22]])


- Non-Darcy effects can cause a 85% difference between apparent and true conductivity. ([[1990-mimoune|Mimoune, 1990, p. 26]])

- Non-Darcy flow is dominant at higher gas flow rates and requires the Forchheimer equation for accurate modeling. ([[2012-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2012, p. 29]])

- Large pulse sizes (100 psi) can lead to non-Darcy effects, causing an underestimation of permeability. ([[2000-hamid|Hamid, 2000, p. 82]])
