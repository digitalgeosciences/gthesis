# Capillary Imbibition

**Summary**: The spontaneous displacement of a non-wetting fluid (oil) by a wetting fluid (water) due to capillary pressure.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[1993-kandil|Kandil, 1993]] evaluated capillary-imbibition as the primary mechanism for oil recovery in fractured systems.

## Scientific Context

- [[1993-kandil|Kandil, 1993]] Capillary imbibition is the dominant mechanism for oil recovery in fractured reservoirs with high fracture permeability.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[1993-kandil|Kandil, 1993]] Analyzed via the difference in capillary pressure between matrix and fracture.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Capillary forces are much higher in the matrix than in the fracture, causing water to be 'sucked' into the matrix while oil is expelled into the fractures. ([[1993-kandil|Kandil, 1993, p. 204]])
