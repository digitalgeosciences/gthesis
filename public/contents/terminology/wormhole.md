# Wormhole

**Summary**: Enhanced flow paths created by the dissolution of carbonate minerals.

---

## Key Findings Across Sources

- A dominating wormhole was generated in the core due to interaction between low pH EDTA and the calcite matrix. ([[2013-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2013, p. 79]])


- Wormholes are formed from the largest pores or surface defects; their morphology is significantly influenced by the phase state of CO2. ([[2013-khan|Khan, 2013, p. 23]])

- Wormholes are results of bulk of injecting acid passing through and connecting a limited number of existing pores. ([[2013-kareem|Kareem, 2013, p. 16]])

- The size and path of generated wormholes are very distinctive in the CT scan images and can be measured qualitatively. ([[2014-al-duailej|Al-Duailej, 2014, p. 23]])

- The gelled acid/NC mix indicated wormholes that traveled halfway, showing good diversion potential at 65°C. ([[2014-alao|Alao, 2014, p. 19]])

- Wormhole growth is controlled by injection rate and the diffusion coefficient of the fluid. ([[2014-khalid|Khalid, 2014, p. 14]])

- The optimum injection rate for the chelating agents was in the range of 1.5 – 2.0 cc/m when both minimum chelant volume and just enough time were used. ([[2015-barri|Barri, 2015, p. 22]])

- The full-speciation model successfully captured different dissolution patterns (face, wormhole, uniform) reported experimentally. ([[2017-mahrous|Mahrous, 2017, p. 12]])

- The waste oil system produced effective wormhole development compared to other fluids. ([[2017-ahmed-2|AHMED, 2017, p. 14]])

- An optimum injection rate of 0.5 cm3/min was determined for GLDA/SW at 250°F. ([[2017-ibrahim|Ibrahim, 2017, p. 20]])

- Wormholes were successfully generated in both the 10 mD and 100 mD samples after thermochemical treatment. ([[2019-alawi|ALAWI, 2019, p. 77]])

- The study aimed to establish a relation between linear and radial wormhole behavior to predict radial results from linear data. ([[2020-aly|Aly, 2020, p. 63]])

- Wormhole growth direction in perforated casing showed an interesting behavior. ([[2022-aboluhom|Aboluhom, 2022, p. 15]])

- Wormholes preferentially propagate in the direction of larger pores as acid flows, and their shape is influenced by both reaction kinetics and transport mechanisms. ([[2022-alkathim|Alkathim, 2022, p. 23]])

- Wormholes in Austin chalk were more susceptible to closure than those in Indiana limestone due to differences in rock rigidity. ([[2023-al-garni|Al-Garni, 2023, p. 53]])

- The time to generate wormholes has a positive relationship with both salinity and temperature values. ([[2023-aldhafeeri|Aldhafeeri, 2023, p. 15]])

- The largest wormhole volume was observed at an injection rate of 5 cm3/min, but the thickest occurred at 1 cm3/min. ([[2024-suleiman|Suleiman, 2024, p. 52]])

- Wormhole formation showed a slight increase but remained minor enough to have minimal impact on overall rock integrity. ([[2024-almukainah|Almukainah, 2024, p. 14]])
