# Calcitization

**Summary**: The replacement of dolomite by calcite.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988]] investigated calcitization in the green mudstone.

- [[2016-radwan|Radwan, 2016]] analyzed calcitization to determine the role of evaporite dissolution as a driver.

- [[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017]] identified dolomite calcitization as a result of meteoric-driven dissolution and precipitation.

- [[2023-omarov|Omarov, 2023]] investigated the calcitization process via EICP to consolidate sand.

- [[1988-darwish-2|Darwish, 1988]] analyzed calcitization as a secondary diagenetic process caused by fresh water seepage.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Calcitization was caused by the seepage of Ca-rich fresh water through the dolomite during pluvial periods. ([[1988-darwish|Darwish, 1988, p. 98]])


- Vanished evaporites are the primary source for the Ca-rich solution required for calcitization. ([[2016-radwan|Radwan, 2016, p. 77]])

- Dolomite calcitization in the TMF is suggested to be of meteoric origin due to equant calcite crystals filling rhombohedral pores. ([[2017-elyamani|ElYamani, 2017, p. 147]])

- EICP was found to be an effective method for producing carbonate precipitates to bind sand grains. ([[2023-omarov|Omarov, 2023, p. 69]])

- Calcitization was evidenced by the presence of large, euhedral to subhedral crystals in the green mudstone. ([[1988-darwish-2|Darwish, 1988, p. 95]])
