# Cement Segregation

**Summary**: The non-uniform distribution of particles within a cement slurry, often caused by differences in specific gravity and size.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2023-ahmed-2|AHMED, 2023]] investigated the cement segregation problem using three different methods.

## Scientific Context

- [[2023-ahmed-2|AHMED, 2023]] Segregation leads to non-uniform density and porosity, compromising wellbore integrity.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2023-ahmed-2|AHMED, 2023]] measured as a percentage of difference between top, middle, and bottom sections.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Barite-weighted cement showed the highest settling propensity with a density variation of 24.6%. ([[2023-ahmed-2|AHMED, 2023, p. 146]])
- [[2023-ahmed-2|AHMED, 2023]] Variation values: Barite (24.6%), Hematite (20.3%), Ilmenite (17.5%), Micromax (1.4%).
