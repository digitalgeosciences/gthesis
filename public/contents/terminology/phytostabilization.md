# Phytostabilization

**Summary**: The use of plants to immobilize contaminants in the soil or water.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2015-bello|Bello, 2015]] concluded that P. australis could be used for phytostabilization of cadmium and lead due to high BCF and low TF values.

## Scientific Context

- [[2015-bello|Bello, 2015]] A strategy for managing heavy metals where translocation is not desired.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2015-bello|Bello, 2015]] Determined by comparing BCF and TF values.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- P. australis showed suitability for phytostabilization because it sequestered metals in the roots rather than translocating them to shoots. ([[2015-bello|Bello, 2015, p. 95]])
