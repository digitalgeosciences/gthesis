# Spherical Model

**Summary**: A common mathematical model used to fit experimental variograms.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987]] fitted spherical models to the experimental variograms of P2O5, thickness, and accumulation.

## Scientific Context

- [[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987]] Used to characterize the spatial continuity of geological variables.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987]] Fitting mathematical equations to experimental variogram data points.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Spherical models with zero nugget variances were successfully fitted to all vertical and horizontal variograms. ([[1987-abdul-latif|Abdul-Latif, 1987, p. 85]])
