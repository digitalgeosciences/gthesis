# Swelling

**Summary**: The increase in volume of clay minerals when they come into contact with water or other fluids.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017]] investigated the swelling behavior of illite-smedite mixed layers.

- [[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022]] measured the linear swelling of Qusaiba shale and API bentonite pellets to evaluate inhibitor effectiveness.

## Scientific Context

- [[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017]] Swelling is a primary cause of wellbore instability in shale formations.

- [[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022]] measured the linear swelling of Qusaiba shale and API bentonite pellets to evaluate inhibitor effectiveness.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017]] measured via XRD d-spacing changes and NMR T2 distribution analysis.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Illite-smectite showed high levels of swelling compared to pure illite. ([[2017-al-arfaj|Al-Arfaj, 2017, p. 14]])


- Latex 2000 showed the lowest swelling percentage on Qusaiba shale at 23.2%. ([[2022-alshalan|Alshalan, 2022, p. 92]])
