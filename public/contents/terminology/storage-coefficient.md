# Storage Coefficient

**Summary**: A dimensionless coefficient representing the volume of water released from storage per unit decline in hydraulic head.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993]] calculated the storage coefficient for the Baidoa aquifer to assess its capacity to supply water.

## Scientific Context

- [[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993]] Storage coefficient is essential for understanding the aquifer's ability to sustain long-term extraction.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993]] Calculated using Jacob's method based on aquifer thickness and porosity.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The storage coefficient for the Baidoa aquifer was calculated as 7.50 x 10^-3, which is consistent with typical values for confined aquifers. ([[1993-moallim|Moallim, 1993, p. 93]])
