# Near Surface Diffraction

**Summary**: Seismic signals produced by objects near the surface that scatter energy and create hyperbolic events.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2009-al-lehyani|Al-Lehyani, 2009]] studied the impact of near-surface diffractions on land seismic exploration data.

## Scientific Context

- [[2009-al-lehyani|Al-Lehyani, 2009]] These are a primary source of noise and complexity in land seismic surveys.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2009-al-lehyani|Al-Lehyani, 2009]] identified via semblance mapping and removed via least-squares fitting.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Near-surface diffractions mask weak reflected body waves and complicate surface wave suppression. ([[2009-al-lehyani|Al-Lehyani, 2009, p. 20]])

## Contrasting Values and Interpretations

- [[2009-al-lehyani|Al-Lehyani, 2009]] Not specified as a value, but identified as high-amplitude hyperbolic events.
