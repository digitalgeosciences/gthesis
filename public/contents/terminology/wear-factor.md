# Wear Factor

**Summary**: A metric used to quantify the degradation or wear of a drilling bit over time/distance.

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[2014-ahmed|Ahmed, 2014]] analyzed the wear factor (Wf) to determine optimal bit replacement points and minimize total time.

## Scientific Context

- [[2014-ahmed|Ahmed, 2014]] Used to balance ROP against the cost and time of bit replacement.

## Measurement and Characterization Methods

- [[2014-ahmed|Ahmed, 2014]] Plotting Wf vs. depth/time.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The optimal range for Wf at which bits should be pulled was determined as [0.25-0.4] in the intermediate section and [0.2-0.3] in the main section. ([[2014-ahmed|Ahmed, 2014, p. 139]])
