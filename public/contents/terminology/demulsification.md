# Demulsification

**Summary**: The process of breaking an emulsion into its constituent parts (oil and water).

---

## How It Appears in the Thesis Collection

- [[1995-al-jaziri|Al-Jaziri, 1995]] investigated the demulsification of water-in-oil emulsions.

- [[2015-he|He, 2015]] investigated various demulsifiers including HEDTA and different polyacrylamides to break stable emulsions.

- [[2021-altowilib|Altowilib, 2021]] studied demulsification mechanisms including flocculation, coalescence, and sedimentation.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- Champion demulsifier was found to be the most effective for resolving tight emulsions. ([[1995-al-jaziri|Al-Jaziri, 1995, p. 14]])


- HEDTA was confirmed as a demulsifier that could seize cations in emulsion solutions, increasing oil mobilization at high pH (12.2). ([[2015-he|He, 2015, p. 38]])

- Demulsification is considered effective if it results in a fast separation rate, low water content, and low cost. ([[2021-altowilib|Altowilib, 2021, p. 31]])
