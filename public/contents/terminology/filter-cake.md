# Filter Cake

**Summary**: A layer of solids deposited by drilling fluid on the borehole wall.

## Key Findings Across Sources

- The absence of a well-established, low permeable filter cake in the first 4 hours led to higher invasion rates. ([[2001-mohammed|Mohammed, 2001, p. 89]])


- The sand percentage in the filter cake can reach up to 50% in long horizontal sections, making it difficult to remove with standard acids. ([[2012-geri|Geri, 2012, p. 13]])

- The maximum thickness of the filter cake was 1.3 mm. ([[2016-geri|GERI, 2016, p. 50]])

- The new fluid system was tested and evaluated in terms of... filter cake integrity against a low permeability sandstone core sample. ([[2017-mohammed-3|MOHAMMED, 2017, p. 17]])

- The 0.075 wt% concentration resulted in a thin filter cake of 0.7 mm. ([[2017-jafarov|Jafarov, 2017, p. 100]])

- The GBRF solution was able to remove up to 96% of the filter cake. ([[2018-alqahtani|AlQahtani, 2018, p. 73]])

- Filter cake thickness increased from 2.45 mm to 5.02 mm as barite dosage increased from 250g to 450g. ([[2022-aljaberi|AlJaberi, 2022, p. 44]])

- Filter cakes formed with perlite were thicker than those without, leading to more pronounced signal changes in the T2 distribution. ([[2024-akomolafe|Akomolafe, 2024, p. 53]])
